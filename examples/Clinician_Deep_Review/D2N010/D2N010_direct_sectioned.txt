Chief Complaint
Reports uncontrolled blood sugar levels.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that his blood sugar levels have been creeping up slightly over the past few weeks, with readings consistently in the 300s. He states that his diet has not changed significantly, though he primarily consumes fruit for sugars. He reports experiencing occasional nausea a couple of days ago, attributed to car travel. He has a history of reflux, which he reports is currently better with protonix. He also notes some joint pain in his right knee, which he discovered incidentally. The patient denies fever or chills, and reports no burning with urination. He prefers to sleep on his side. He has a history of congestive heart failure, but denies recent weight gain or fluid retention. He reports his right knee pain started after a minor insect bite, and he has experienced some erythema and fluctuants. He has been experiencing some lower extremity edema on the right side.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild erythema and fluctuants in the right knee. The thyroid is slightly enlarged. The lungs are clear to auscultation. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. The right knee shows edema. Vital signs are stable. The nurse reported a lyme titer of 12, elevated. A rapid strep test was positive. Dragon shows a lyme titer of 12 and a positive rapid strep test.

Assessment
The patient presents with hyperglycemia, likely related to an inflammatory response, potentially exacerbated by an elevated lyme titer and a positive rapid strep test. He also has a history of congestive heart failure and chronic right knee pain.

Plan
Order a Western blot PCR for lyme and amoxicillin 500 mg three times daily for 10 days. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. The nurse will schedule follow-up testing and provide patient education regarding medication adherence and symptom monitoring.
