Chief Complaint
High blood sugar and concerns about blood sugar control.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that his blood sugars have been running in the 300s and that he's concerned about not having them under control. He states that he's been taking his blood sugars at home and has noticed they've been creeping up slightly. He reports having had some mild knee pain, and reports experiencing nausea a couple of days ago, but attributes it to sitting in the back of a car. He also reports difficulty sleeping. The patient reports that he's been taking protonix for reflux, which has helped somewhat. He has a history of reflux and reports no recent weight gain or fluid retention.

Findings
The physical exam revealed mild knee pain with palpation and range of motion limitations. Examination showed no fever or chills. The patient's vital signs were stable. The nurse reported elevated lyme titers and a positive strep test. The thyroid was slightly enlarged.

Assessment
The patient's primary assessment is hyperglycemia, likely related to an underlying infection, as evidenced by the elevated lyme titer and positive strep test. He also has a history of reflux and mild knee pain.

Plan
The doctor ordered amoxicillin 500 milligrams three times a day for 10 days. The doctor also ordered a western blot pcr for lyme. The nurse will set up the patient with the necessary tests and follow-up. The patient was instructed to call if he experiences any worsening symptoms. ,
