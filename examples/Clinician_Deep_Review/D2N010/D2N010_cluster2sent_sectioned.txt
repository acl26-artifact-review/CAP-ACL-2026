Chief Complaint
The patient reports high blood sugar and concerns about his blood sugar control.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent nausea in the past few days, primarily related to sitting in a car. He reports improved reflux symptoms since starting protonix. He also reports some joint pain and lower extremity edema on the right side. The patient confirms a history of congestive heart failure and reports no nighttime awakenings due to reflux. He denies burning with urination. He is certain he does not have alpha-gal syndrome. The patient reports a positive rapid strep test and was treated with amoxicillin.

Findings
The patient's blood sugar is not under control. A physical exam reveals erythema and fluctuants on the right knee, consistent with an insect bite. Vital signs are stable. The lyme titer is elevated. A rapid strep test was positive.

Assessment
The patient presents with hyperglycemia, likely related to an underlying infection, potentially exacerbated by a lyme infection.

Plan
Order a Western blot and PCR for lyme disease. Order amoxicillin 500 milligrams three times a day for 10 days. The nurse will set up laboratory tests and provide further instructions.
