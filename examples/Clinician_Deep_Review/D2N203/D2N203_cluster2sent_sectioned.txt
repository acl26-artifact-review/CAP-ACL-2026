Chief Complaint
The patient reports vision changes and difficulty with reading, particularly in dim light.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports vision changes, particularly difficulty reading in dim light, and reports that straight lines appear distorted. He has attempted self-management strategies including hypnosis, lozenges, and chewing gum, but has struggled to quit smoking and has inconsistently taken his blood pressure medication. He reports a history of needle stick to the eye.

Findings
The patient's anterior segment examination reveals posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes. The right eye cornea is clear with no detectable nuclear vortex or cup-to-disc ratio of greater than 0.2. The left eye cornea is clear with no detectable nuclear vortex or cup-to-disc ratio of greater than 0.2. Fundus examination of the right eye reveals the retina attached at 360 degrees, with no evidence of retinal holes or tears, and mild retinal pigment epithelium changes in the fovea. The posterior segment examination reveals drusen in the macula, retinal thickening, and subretinal fluid and exudate accumulation, consistent with a hemorrhage. The left eye shows retinal attachment at 360 degrees, mild retinal pigment epithelium changes in the fovea, and no hemorrhage or subretinal fluid. Diagnostic testing reveals drusen deposits under the retinal pigment epithelium and subretinal fluid accumulation. Fluorescein angiography confirms focal leakage in the right eye consistent with neurovascular age-related macular degeneration.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with neurovascular age-related macular degeneration, advanced to a stage that typically leads to more severe vision loss.

Plan
A monthly injection of lucent will be administered into the vitreous to slow the progression of vision loss. The nurse will prepare the patient for the injection today, and a follow-up appointment will be scheduled. The patient should continue to take his blood pressure medication as prescribed and monitor his blood pressure regularly. A physical exam of the eyes will be performed before scheduling the injection.
