Chief Complaint
Vision changes and trouble reading, particularly in dim light.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports vision changes over the past three to four months, particularly when reading in dim environments like at an Italian restaurant. He notes difficulty adjusting to dim lighting, such as in the basement of a building. He also mentions that straight lines, like those in a door frame, appear wavy. He has tried to quit smoking but has struggled with relapse. He is currently taking medication for high blood pressure but has recently stopped taking it. He reports experiencing discomfort with eye injections.

Findings
The otoscopic examination revealed drusen deposits under the retinal pigment epithelium in both eyes. The fluorescein angiography showed a focal area of leakage consistent with neurovascular age-related macular degeneration. The retina is attached appropriately in both eyes. There is subretinal fluid and hemorrhage noted in the right eye.

Assessment
The diagnostic test and eye exams are consistent with neurovascular age-related macular degeneration, a stage that usually leads to more severe vision loss.

Plan
Schedule monthly injections of lucent into the vitreous portion of the eye to slow the progression of the disease. The injections will be administered after numbing the eye to minimize discomfort. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
