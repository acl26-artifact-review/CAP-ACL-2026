The examination reveals drusen deposits under the retinal pigment epithelium and subretinal fluid accumulation in the right eye, consistent with neovascular age-related macular degeneration. The left eye shows no hemorrhage or subretinal fluid. The anterior segment examination reveals posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes.

A:
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with neovascular age-related macular degeneration (wet macular degeneration).

P:
I will initiate a monthly intravitreal injection of lucent in the vitreous to slow the progression of the macular degeneration. I will also schedule a follow-up appointment to monitor the response to treatment and discuss potential vision-preserving strategies.
