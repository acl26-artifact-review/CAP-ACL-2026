Chief Complaint
Vision changes and trouble adjusting in dim environments.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty adjusting in dim environments, starting approximately three to four months ago. He notes that lines appear wavy to him, particularly in the paneling of his office and the door frame. He and his wife have been attending fancy restaurants recently, and he experienced difficulty seeing in the dim lighting at one such restaurant, describing the scene as resembling a 'saprano family' scene. He also reports difficulty quitting smoking, having tried hypnosis, lozenges, and chewing gum, though he often relapses. He has a history of high blood pressure, which he admits he hasn't been taking medication for recently.

Findings
The otoscopic examination revealed posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes. The cornea is clear in both eyes. The right eye shows no peripheral vision impairment (PVI). The left eye shows a slightly larger PVI. The right eye fundus examination reveals drusen in the macula, retinal thickening, and subretinal fluid and hemorrhage. The left eye fundus examination reveals drusen under the retinal pigment epithelium (RPE) and subretinal fluid.

Assessment
The diagnostic test and eye exams are consistent with neovascular age-related macular degeneration (wet macular degeneration).

Plan
The doctor plans to administer monthly lucent injections in the vitreous portion of the eye, after numbing the eye to minimize discomfort. The doctor orders a physical exam on the patient's eyes and a pill bottle to help the patient remember to take his blood pressure medication. The doctor recommends taking blood pressure medication as prescribed and wants to schedule an injection for the patient today.
