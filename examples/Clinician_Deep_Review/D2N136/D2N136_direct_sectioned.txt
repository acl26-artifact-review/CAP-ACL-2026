Chief Complaint
Left knee pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left knee pain and swelling, primarily in both knees, which has worsened with stair workouts. He attributes this to increased activity. He has a prior right shoulder rotator cuff repair two years ago with no subsequent issues. He reports no other joint pain and denies fever, chills, or nausea. He takes norvasc 5 mg daily for hypertension and monitors his blood sugars, which have been well-controlled. He has been taking medication for hypertension and the joint pain has not elevated.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild erythema and edema in the left knee with a small effusion. Range of motion is decreased in the left knee. The patient's blood pressure is elevated today. The neck is supple, thyroid is not enlarged, lungs are clear, and the abdomen is soft. White blood cell count is normal. The a1c is slightly elevated.

Assessment
Left knee pain and swelling, likely secondary to a strain.

Plan
Order autoimmune labs to evaluate for underlying inflammatory conditions. Order a physical therapy referral. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain relief. Order a lipid profile. Continue metformin 500 mg twice daily. Instruct the patient to monitor blood pressure at home and report any changes. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
