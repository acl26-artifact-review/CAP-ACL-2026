Chief Complaint
Left knee pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left knee pain, primarily exacerbated by stair workouts over the past few months. He attributes this to building muscle, but notes significant discomfort. He denies any recent trauma. He mentions a prior rotator cuff repair two years ago, but reports no issues since. He reports no pins and needles or swelling in his legs. He does, however, mention a family history of diabetes and monitors his blood sugars regularly. He currently takes norvasc 5mg daily for hypertension, which he monitors at home and reports is well-controlled. He denies fever, chills, nausea, or shortness of breath.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild erythema and edema in the left knee. There is slight effusion noted. Range of motion is decreased, with limited flexion and extension. Lower extremity strength is intact. Blood pressure is elevated today. White blood cell count is normal. A1c is slightly elevated.

Assessment
The patient presents with left knee pain and swelling, likely secondary to overuse and potentially exacerbated by recent activity. No acute inflammatory findings are present. The slightly elevated A1c suggests a need for continued diabetes management.

Plan
Order an autoimmune profile to evaluate for underlying inflammatory conditions. Order a physical therapy referral to address strength and range of motion. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain management. Continue metformin 500 mg twice daily for diabetes management. Schedule a follow-up in two weeks to assess response to treatment and monitor blood pressure.
