Chief Complaint
Left knee pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness

He attributes this to increased stair workouts, believing they were building muscle. However, the pain has persisted and worsened. He has a prior rotator cuff repair two years ago with no subsequent issues. He notes no pins and needles or swelling in other joints. He monitors his blood sugars and reports that his blood pressure has been elevated today, which is not uncommon. He takes norvasc 5mg daily and metformin 500mg twice daily.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild erythema and edema in the left knee, with slight decrease in flexion and extension. Strength in the lower extremities is intact. White blood cell count is normal. A1c is slightly elevated. Blood pressure is elevated today.

Assessment
The patient presents with left knee pain and swelling, likely secondary to a strain or effusion. Further investigation is warranted to rule out other etiologies.

Plan
Order autoimmune labs to work up the left knee pain. Order physical therapy referral. Order meloxicam 15mg once daily. Order a lipid profile. Order a blood pressure cuff for home monitoring and request a blood pressure report in two weeks. Schedule follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
