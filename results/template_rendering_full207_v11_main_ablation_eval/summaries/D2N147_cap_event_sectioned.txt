Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain following a football injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that approximately four days ago, while playing football, he landed awkwardly on his right shoulder. He describes a 'weird' feeling at the time. Over the past few days, he's experienced increasing pain and difficulty lifting objects, particularly heavier items like a box of chips. He's been taking Tylenol, which provides minimal relief. He denies any fever or chills. He reports a mild headache. He also reports pain in his left hand and left shoulder. He denies any prior orthopedic injuries.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the acromioclavicular joint. Range of motion is limited, with decreased abduction and adduction (approximately 90 degrees). Radial pulse is palpable. Cervical spine exam is unremarkable. Vital signs are within normal limits: blood pressure 120/76 mmHg, heart rate 82 bpm, oxygen saturation 95% on room air. X-ray confirms an acute acromioclavicular joint fracture.

Assessment
The patient presents with an acute acromioclavicular joint fracture. He is experiencing right shoulder pain.

Plan
I'm going to order a CT scan of the right shoulder to get a better look at the fracture. I'm also going to prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain management. Finally, I'm referring you to physical therapy to help strengthen your shoulder and avoid surgery in the future. Please avoid tackle football for now.
