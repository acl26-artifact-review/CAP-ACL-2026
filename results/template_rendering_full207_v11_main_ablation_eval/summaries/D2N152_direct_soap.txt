S:
The patient reports back pain for approximately one week, with occasional relief and recurrence. He attempted over-the-counter medications without sustained relief. He describes a twisting injury approximately one week prior to the onset of pain, and reports occasional left eye irritation coinciding with the back pain. He reports occasional visits to the bakery, consuming sweets, and acknowledges this intake is monitored. He reports checking his blood sugar in the mornings most days, but occasionally skips this practice, and occasionally goes skiing. His blood sugars have trended towards the higher side when he does check them. He takes his metformin twice daily.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the lumbar spine, particularly at the central region. Range of motion is limited. Neurological exam is intact. Lung sounds are clear. Heart rhythm is regular. Blood pressure is slightly low. No edema is noted. Glucose levels have been elevated recently.

A:
The patient's presentation is consistent with a musculoskeletal low back strain, likely secondary to a recent injury. His diabetes is currently under control, but requires ongoing monitoring.

P:
Order a low back x-ray for outpatient evaluation. Order physical therapy twice per week to address pain and strengthen back muscles. Prescribe ibuprofen 400 mg twice daily as needed with tylenol up to three times daily. Prescribe flexeril 30 mg at bedtime as needed for muscle relaxation. Order an A1C test to assess diabetes control. Schedule a follow-up in 4-6 weeks to review x-ray results and assess response to physical therapy. Advise patient to monitor blood sugars daily and keep a record. Advise patient to limit carbohydrate intake, particularly from bakery items.
