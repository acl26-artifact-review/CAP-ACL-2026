S:
The patient reports experiencing pain in his right flank for the past two weeks, radiating down to his groin. He describes the pain as intermittent and exacerbated by increased fluid intake and physical activity. He also reports a previous episode of hematuria and has a history of kidney stones.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation in the right flank. Blood pressure is slightly elevated. No signs of acute distress are noted. The patient reports using an ibuprofen.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a kidney stone, likely causing the flank pain and hematuria.

P:
Rescribe ibuprofen for pain management. Order a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks to assess progress and discuss potential lithotripsy if needed. Advise the patient to continue using a strainer when urinating.
