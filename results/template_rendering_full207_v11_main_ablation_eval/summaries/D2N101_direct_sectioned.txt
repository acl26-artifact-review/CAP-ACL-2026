Chief Complaint
Discussion of a breast reduction.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a consideration of breast reduction for approximately six to seven years, with ongoing discomfort related to her breasts, particularly when working at her desk all day. She has been attending chiropractic care for several years to address back and neck pain, attributing a portion of this pain to her breasts. She primarily wears a 34E sports bra due to comfort and expense considerations. The patient reports occasional nipple numbness and has no current children planned. She is currently studying psychology and drinks wine occasionally. She anticipates being on her new insurance plan in July. She has a family history of breast cancer, with her mother diagnosed at age 40.

Findings
Examination reveals asymmetrical breasts with significant ptosis bilaterally. The patient is wearing a C cup bra. No nipple retraction or discharge is observed. No breast tenderness, masses, or palpable axillary lymphadenopathy is present. Measurements indicate a difference in breast size of approximately 1.5 cm.

Assessment
The patient presents with symptomatic macromastia, consistent with a need for breast reduction.

Plan
Schedule a consultation for a breast reduction, obtain photographs for insurance review, and discuss surgical details. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Advise the patient to notify the office upon obtaining new insurance information.
