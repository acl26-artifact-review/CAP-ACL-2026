Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for the last three weeks. He states it's not getting better and he's been doing a lot of work in his basement, including putting in a new ceiling, which he suspects may be contributing. He denies any memory of a specific injury or fall. He has not tried any medications for the pain, but reports that Tylenol usually helps. He also reports some pain in his left hand. He has not iced the shoulder recently. He describes the pain as constant and notes it's worse when he sleeps. He's been able to move the arm, but has difficulty reaching for things and lifting anything, and doesn't want to put his hands over his head because it causes so much pain. He's been having trouble sleeping because of the pain. He has not had any previous shoulder pain.

Findings
Examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder. There is tenderness to palpation of the greater tuberosity of the humerus. The capillary refill is less than three seconds in the left hand. Sensation is intact to light touch in the left arm. No tenderness is palpated at the sternoclavicular or acromioclavicular joints.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to a rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order an MRI of the left shoulder to further evaluate the rotator cuff. Refer the patient to physical therapy for a six to eight week course of treatment. Continue Tylenol as needed. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms do not improve. ,
