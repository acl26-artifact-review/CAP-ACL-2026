Chief Complaint
Dennis is a 57-year-old male today complaining of shortness of breath, and he has also got a history of diabetes, high blood pressure, and asthma.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports waking up with shortness of breath, and he is short of breath when he goes home and when he returns. He has been coughing up blood and has noticed swelling in his legs. He reports that his blood sugar has been running high and low, and his blood pressure has been running somewhat low. He has had a knee problem but no swelling, and he has not been to the hospital or emergency room for his coughing or coughing up blood. He wears a Dexcom.

Findings
The patient's oxygen level is approximately 94% on pulse oximetry. There is no fever, no swelling in the neck, no carotid bruits, and no edema on his lower extremities. Lung exam reveals rails and rhonchi on the right side, with diminished breath sounds on the right side. The heart exam shows a two-over-successorial ejection murmur. The belly exam is soft, and extremity exam reveals one plus nonpitting edema on both ankles with no calf tenderness.

Assessment
Dennis is a 57-year-old male presenting with shortness of breath, a history of diabetes, high blood pressure, and asthma, and is currently experiencing a possible infection in his lungs.

Plan
Order a chest x-ray and a CT scan of the chest for blood testing, and refer him to the emergency department for evaluation. Increase his metformin dose from 500 mg twice a day to 750 mg twice a day. Order a hemoglobin A1c today. Hold the norvasc medication until further evaluation of his blood pressure.
