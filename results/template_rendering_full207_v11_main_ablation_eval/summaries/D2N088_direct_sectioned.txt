Chief Complaint
Upper respiratory infection and fatigue.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling tired and short of breath over the past week, particularly with exertion such as carrying heavy bags of soil during yard work. He attributes this to recent outdoor activities and suspects a common cold. He denies fever, nausea, or vomiting. He reports his depression has been manageable with barre classes and medication, and his diabetes is generally well-controlled with diet and medication, though he occasionally misses medication when busy. He has not been exposed to anyone with COVID-19.

Findings
Physical exam reveals scattered rhonchi bilaterally in the lung exam, which clears with cough. Lower extremity edema is noted. Elbows are tender to palpation bilaterally. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 132/78 mmHg, heart rate 78 bpm. Chest x-ray demonstrates no acute findings. Hemoglobin A1c is elevated at 8.0%.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with a viral upper respiratory infection. The patient's depression is currently well-managed. His type 2 diabetes is being monitored.

Plan
Order a COVID-19 test. Prescribe robitussin for cough and ibuprofen for fever. Increase metformin to 1000 mg twice daily. Order a hemoglobin A1c in four months. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress. The nurse will assist with medication refills.
