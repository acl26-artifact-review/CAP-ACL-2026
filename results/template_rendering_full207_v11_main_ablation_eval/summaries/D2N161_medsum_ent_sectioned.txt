Chief Complaint
The patient reports shortness of breath and ankle swelling, following an acute on chronic heart failure exacerbation. He also reports feeling better since starting new medications and dietary changes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing increasing shortness of breath over the past few weeks, exacerbated by dietary indiscretions and salt intake. He admits to enjoying pizza and chicken wings frequently. He reports having missed a few doses of his medications, but has been trying to take them consistently. He reports feeling significantly better since starting bumex and following dietary recommendations. He denies chest pain or recent trauma. He reports fatigue for the past two months. He denies blood in stool.

Findings
The patient's vital signs reveal a blood pressure of 128/72 mmHg, heart rate of 79 bpm, respiratory rate of 26 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 99% on room air. Jugular venous distention is absent. A grade II/VI systolic ejection murmur is present. The lungs are clear bilaterally. Lower extremities demonstrate trace edema. Echocardiogram shows a preserved ejection fraction of 55%, abnormal diastolic filling, and mild to moderate mitral regurgitation. Weights have been monitored daily, with a reported gain of approximately three pounds over two days.

Assessment
The patient presents with congestive heart failure, likely exacerbated by uncontrolled hypertension and dietary indiscretion. He also has chronic kidney disease and mild mitral regurgitation.

Plan
Continue bumex 2 mg daily. Order a renal artery ultrasound. Order more blood work to assess renal function. Refer the patient to a nutritionist for dietary counseling. Instruct the patient to weigh themselves every other day and call if they gain three pounds in two days. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months. Monitor for any worsening symptoms or changes in medication tolerance.
