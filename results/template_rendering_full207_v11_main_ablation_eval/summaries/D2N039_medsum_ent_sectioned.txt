Chief Complaint
Patient reports experiencing elevated blood pressure and seeks evaluation and management.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing elevated blood pressure, which she has been actively managing with medication adjustments as directed by her primary care physician. She reports consistently monitoring her blood pressure at home, noting readings as high as 170/90 mmHg. She has been taking prescribed medications as directed, but reports continued difficulty in controlling her blood pressure. She reports a fondness for pizza, wings, and subs, particularly during football season, and admits to struggling to limit these foods, especially during game days. She has a history of mild headaches when her blood pressure is elevated. She denies any chest pain or shortness of breath. She reports some mild edema in her lower extremities, particularly after prolonged standing. Her family history includes high blood pressure and kidney disease in both parents.

Findings
Vitals reveal a blood pressure of 169/74 mmHg. Heart rate is 88 bpm. Oxygen saturation is 98%. Physical exam reveals no jugular venous distension or carotid bruits. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. A two over six systolic ejection murmur is auscultated. One plus pitting edema is noted bilaterally in the lower extremities.

Assessment
Uncontrolled hypertension. Possible multifactorial etiology.

Plan
Order a renal artery ultrasound to evaluate for potential kidney disease. Order a 24-hour urine collection to assess aldosterone and renin levels. Refer to a registered dietitian for guidance on dietary modifications, particularly regarding sodium intake and limiting processed foods. Prescribe Cardura 4 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to review blood pressure monitoring data and assess treatment response. Advise patient to continue monitoring blood pressure at home and to record readings daily. Strongly encourage reduction in alcohol consumption, starting with limiting intake to one or two beers per week.
