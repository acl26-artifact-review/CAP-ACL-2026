Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right shoulder pain that began approximately three weeks ago following a fall while painting. He reports that the pain was exacerbated by reaching overhead. He initially treated the pain with ice and ibuprofen. He denies any prior shoulder injuries or significant medical history. He rates his pain as a 6 without medication and a 4 with ibuprofen. He denies numbness or tingling.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the right shoulder. Tenderness is noted to the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Neurovascular exam is intact with brisk capillary refill and equal sensation. Right shoulder x-ray demonstrates no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy, likely secondary to the recent fall and subsequent strain.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI of the right shoulder to further evaluate the rotator cuff tendons. I am also referring you to physical therapy for a six to eight week course. Continue taking ibuprofen as needed for pain relief. Please schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess your progress.
