Chief Complaint
Numbness and tingling in the hands.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports numbness and tingling in both hands, which began approximately four weeks ago following a car accident last December. He describes the symptoms as more pronounced on the right hand, with tingling extending from the middle and ring fingers up the forearm and from the pinky and ring fingers on the left. He attributes the symptoms to the car wreck and a subsequent break in both wrists, which were treated with splints. He has been taking nerve pain medication but has not been consistent with the regimen. He also reports mild neck stiffness, related to a contusion sustained during the accident. He denies any history of thyroid issues.

Findings
Neurological examination reveals diminished pinprick sensation in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand and decreased sensation in the fourth and fifth fingers of the left hand. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric bilaterally. Tinel signs are positive at both wrists and both elbows. The patient reports a zing sensation and radiating pain up the wrist and elbow on both sides with palpation.

Assessment
Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome is suspected, potentially secondary to the past trauma. Cubital tunnel syndrome should also be considered.

Plan
Order an EMG to evaluate nerve conduction studies and pinpoint the source of the nerve irritation. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks after the EMG results are available. Consider referral to a hand clinic for potential carpal tunnel release surgery if symptoms persist despite conservative management. Continue wearing splints as instructed.
