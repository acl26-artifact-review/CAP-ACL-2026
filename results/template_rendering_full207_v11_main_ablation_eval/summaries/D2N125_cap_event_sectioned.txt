Chief Complaint
Facial pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports facial pain for approximately two to three months, primarily in the right cheekbone area, with occasional sharp, stabbing pain. The pain is triggered by certain temperatures and cold exposure. Ibuprofen and tiger balm have not provided relief. The patient reports no sensory loss or weakness on the affected side. He occasionally experiences headaches, but they are different from his usual migraines. He takes occasional Tylenol for headaches. He reports a history of multiple sclerosis and brain tumors, but denies any current symptoms.

Findings
Physical exam reveals facial sensation is diminished on the right side of the face, particularly when using the cotton tape. The patient follows the finger in both directions, and the smile appears symmetrical. The patient reports a dull sensation on the left side of the face and a sharp sensation on the right side of the face.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with trigeminal neuralgia.

Plan
Prescribe gabapentin to start, and schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to assess the effectiveness of the medication. Continue to monitor the patient's symptoms and consider surgical options if medication is not effective.
