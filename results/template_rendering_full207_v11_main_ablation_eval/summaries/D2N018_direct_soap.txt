S:
The patient reports severe right upper arm pain following a volleyball injury, describing a fall and landing on her arm. She states the pain started approximately 18 hours ago after spiking the ball and being kicked by an opponent. She has been taking ibuprofen every six hours, but reports it is not providing adequate relief. She rates the pain as a 9 out of 10. She denies numbness or tingling in the right arm.

O:
The patient has swelling and erythema on her right shoulder with tenderness over the right shoulder. Pulses are intact. Examination of the remainder of the body is normal. X-ray reveals a proximal humerus fracture.

A:
The patient has a proximal humerus fracture. The injury is likely due to a fall during a volleyball game.

P:
Rescribe lortab 500mg every 6 hours as needed for pain, quantity 20. Order a long arm cast and sling. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess fracture alignment.
