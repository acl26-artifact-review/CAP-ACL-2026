Chief Complaint
Left elbow pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the inside of his elbow, which he rates as 7 on a 1-10 scale. He describes the pain as radiating down his arm. The pain wakes him up at night. He also reports pain with resisted pronation and wrist flexion. The patient has a sweet tooth and makes senna rolls. He tried ice and bakes cakes in the past.

Findings
Examination reveals pain with wrist extension and resisted pronation. The patient is taking ibuprofen, metformin, and lisinopril.

Assessment
The patient has a history of hypertension and is being treated for diabetes. He presents with left elbow pain, likely secondary to tendinitis.

Plan
The doctor orders a sling to be worn during waking hours. The doctor recommends contacting a dietitian. The doctor orders ibuprofen at 600 milligrams every six hours with food for one week. The patient is taking ibuprofen.
