S:
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall, with initial inability to walk and current limping. He describes using ice, elevation, and ibuprofen for the pain. His pain level is 6/10 without ibuprofen and 1/10 with ibuprofen. He reports no numbness or tingling in the right foot.

O:
The patient's vitals are within normal limits. A physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the anterior lateral soft tissue of the right ankle and no laxity on anterior drawer or inversion stress. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds and dorsalis pedis pulse is strong. X-ray of the right ankle showed no fracture.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a right ankle sprain, likely involving the anterior talofibular ligament.

P:
The plan includes keeping the leg elevated, applying ice, using an air cast, and prescribing crutches. The patient should start walking on the leg in a few days as tolerated. Follow-up is scheduled in 14 days, and the patient is instructed to continue taking ibuprofen as needed.
