Chief Complaint
Headaches

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of headaches for years, initially associated with menstruation, but now occurring more frequently and with greater severity. The headaches are predominantly on the right side and described as a pulsating sensation behind the eye. The patient reports worsening headaches with exercise and has noticed ringing in the ears and blurred vision intermittently. He has been trying to lose weight through exercise but finds it difficult due to the headaches. He previously used New Balance and Asics running shoes.

Findings
Neurological exam reveals papilledema on funduscopic examination. Cranial nerves I-XII are intact. Gait is normal. Heart exam reveals normal sinus rhythm with no murmurs, rubs, or gallops. Blood pressure was within normal limits.

Assessment
Idiopathic intracranial hypertension is concerning, given the patient's symptoms and findings.

Plan
Order an MRI of the brain to evaluate for other contributing factors. Schedule a consultation with a neuro-ophthalmologist for a complete eye exam. Initiate a referral to neurology for further evaluation and consideration of a lumbar puncture to assess intracranial pressure. Advise the patient that medication may be considered to manage intracranial pressure if the MRI and lumbar puncture results confirm the diagnosis.
