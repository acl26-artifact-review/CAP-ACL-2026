Chief Complaint
The patient reports back pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain following golfing, with an immediate need to hit the ground after the pop. The patient reports the back pain loosened up a little bit by Monday morning, but is still pretty sore. The patient reports struggling with his right foot and right leg. The patient has a history of surgery on his lower back. He reports congestive heart failure and is currently taking his medications for heart failure, avoiding salty foods, and reports weight gain of five pounds over the summer. He denies problems laying flat while he goes to bed. The patient reports a sweet tooth. He monitors his blood sugars at home and is currently taking metformin.

Findings
The nurse reviewed symptoms of back pain and weakness in the right leg. The doctor's examination revealed a 2 out of 6 systolic ejection murmur. The doctor's examination revealed pain to palpation of the lumbar 5 or lumbar spine at the level of L5. The doctor's examination revealed decreased range of motion with flexion and extension. The doctor's examination revealed a positive straight leg raise. The doctor's examination revealed a strength of 4 out of 5 on the right and 5 out of 5 on the left. The x-ray shows normal lumbar spine alignment. The diabetic lab shows a hemoglobin A1c of 8.

Assessment
The patient presents with lumbar pain, potentially consistent with lumbar strain, given the reported pop and physical exam findings.

Plan
The doctor prescribes meloxicam 15 milligrams once a day. The doctor orders a physical therapy referral. The doctor orders a lumbar MRI if symptoms persist. The doctor increases metformin to 1,000 milligrams twice daily. The doctor orders a hemoglobin A1c in a couple of months. The doctor continues the patient on lisinopril 20 milligrams once a day. The doctor continues the patient on lasix.
