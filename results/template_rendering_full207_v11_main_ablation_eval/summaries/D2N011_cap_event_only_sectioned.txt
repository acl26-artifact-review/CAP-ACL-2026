Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent chest pain for the past week, described as a dull ache, occasionally sharp, radiating to his left arm. The pain is triggered by exertion and relieved by rest. He denies shortness of breath, palpitations, or nausea. He reports a family history of coronary artery disease.

Findings
Blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, regular rhythm. Oxygen saturation 98% on room air. Auscultation reveals normal heart sounds. No murmurs appreciated.

Assessment
Possible angina pectoris. Rule out acute coronary syndrome.

Plan
Order serial cardiac enzymes, obtain a 12-lead ECG, and initiate aspirin 81 mg daily. Recommend cardiac stress test if symptoms persist.
