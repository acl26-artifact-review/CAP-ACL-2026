Chief Complaint
Patient reports a new heart murmur and concerns about it.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a new, worrisome heart murmur that she discovered during a routine checkup. She is unsure of the severity or nature of the murmur. She reports experiencing leg pain for approximately one month, which has been worsening. She also reports experiencing lightheadedness and dizziness, particularly when walking. She takes meclizine for this. She has no known allergies. The patient reports that her leg pain has been worsening over the past six months. She has experienced fatigue and reports feeling overly tired. She notes that her weight has decreased slightly over the past year. She denies any fever or chills. She reports that her brother had a stroke and her eldest sister had a heart problem.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade 4/6 systolic murmur at the right upper sternal border with radiation to the carotids. Blood pressure is 130/80 mmHg. Heart rate is 78 bpm with regular rhythm. Neurological exam is normal. The patient reports swelling in her ankles.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are concerning for aortic stenosis and possible peripheral neuropathy. The murmur suggests aortic stenosis, and the leg pain and lightheadedness could be related to this condition. Further evaluation is needed to confirm the diagnosis and assess the severity of the stenosis.

Plan
Order an echocardiogram to evaluate the patient's heart function and aortic valve. Discuss the possibility of a Tavr procedure with the patient. Continue meclizine as needed for lightheadedness. Refer to a physical therapist for evaluation and treatment of leg pain and mobility issues. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to discuss the echocardiogram results and further management.
