Chief Complaint
Headache and elevated blood pressure readings.

History of Present Illness
Donna is a 37-year-old female presenting with a worsening headache over the past few weeks, accompanied by elevated blood pressure readings. She reports forgetting to take her lisinopril medication occasionally, particularly on weekends. Her blood sugar has been running between 120 and 120 mg/dL. She describes the headaches as a 7/10 initially, which decreases to a 4-5 with Tylenol. She has experienced headaches before, but not of this severity or duration. She denies congestion, cough, or runny nose. She reports tenderness to palpation over the forehead and neck.

Findings
Blood pressure readings have been elevated, with readings of 160/92 and 175/92 mmHg. Her blood sugar levels have been between 120 and 120 mg/dL. Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the forehead and neck, but no lymphadenopathy. No tenderness to palpation of maxillary sinuses.

Assessment
The patient presents with a persistent headache and hypertension, requiring further evaluation and management.

Plan
Increase lisinopril to 40 mg once daily. Order routine blood work including a hemoglobin, A1c, and laboratory tests. Provide diabetic education. Schedule a follow-up appointment with Dr. Ruth to review blood test results and assess ongoing management. Encourage continued regular exercise, such as walking.
