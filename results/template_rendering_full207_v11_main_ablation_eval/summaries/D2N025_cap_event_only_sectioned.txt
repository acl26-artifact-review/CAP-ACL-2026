Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent chest pain for the past month, described as a dull ache, occasionally sharp, and rated 5/10. The pain is typically triggered by exertion and relieved by rest. He denies shortness of breath, palpitations, or nausea. He reports a family history of coronary artery disease. He currently takes aspirin daily.

Findings
Blood pressure 138/82, heart rate 72, regular. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Cardiac auscultation reveals normal rhythm and no murmurs. EKG shows no acute ST-segment changes.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and EKG findings are concerning for possible angina pectoris. Further evaluation is warranted to rule out coronary artery disease.

Plan
Order cardiac stress test. Continue aspirin daily. Advise patient to avoid strenuous activity until further evaluation is complete. Schedule follow-up in one week to discuss test results.
