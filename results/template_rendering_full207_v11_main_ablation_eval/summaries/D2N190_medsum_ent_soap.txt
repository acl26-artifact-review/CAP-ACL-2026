S:
The patient reports a right knee injury following a bicycle fall approximately two weeks ago. He describes the pain as persistent and 'holding on,' exacerbated by walking up hills. He reports using ibuprofen, but is not supposed to take it for more than two weeks. He states his left knee is unaffected and symmetric. He describes the injury as occurring when he hit the curb while his partner was distracted, seeing a squirrel.

O:
Physical examination reveals mild posterior drawer instability in the right knee, with a grade 1 laxity. Patellar tracking is full and without crepitus. The patient reports a slight feeling of instability when walking. X-rays demonstrate no acute fractures or bony abnormalities, but suggest a possible sprain of the posterior cruciate ligament.

A:
The clinician suspects a sprained or injured posterior cruciate ligament in the right knee. An MRI scan is recommended to evaluate the ligament and potentially meniscus. The clinician will order a brace for temporary support and refer the patient to physical therapy.

P:
The clinician recommends continuing ibuprofen as needed for pain relief. A prescription for a different medication with potentially fewer doses is offered. A referral to physical therapy is arranged, with a follow-up appointment scheduled in 4-6 weeks. The clinician will monitor the patient's progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.
