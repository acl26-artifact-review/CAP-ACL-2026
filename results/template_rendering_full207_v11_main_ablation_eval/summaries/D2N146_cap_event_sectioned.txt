Chief Complaint
Right-sided belly pain for several weeks.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right-sided belly pain for several weeks. He describes the pain as a severe, squeezing sensation on the right hand side of his belly, located under his ribs. The pain comes and goes, but when it does, it's very severe, and he becomes nauseated and sick to his stomach. He rates the pain as an 8 on a scale of 1 to 10. The pain is often triggered by eating, particularly within a couple of hours after meals, but it can also occur sporadically. He has tried cutting out fatty foods, as recommended by his previous doctor, but the pain persists. He has a history of type 2 diabetes, managed with diet and exercise, and has lost 25 pounds over the past three months by following a keto diet. He reports that his symptoms improved with the keto diet, but the pain still persists. He has experienced low-grade fevers and has occasionally vomited. He has a family history of gallbladder disease, with both his parents having had their gallbladders removed. He has not been exercising regularly, but enjoys nature photography and hiking, especially in the new park behind the Rex Center.

Findings
Physical exam reveals positive bowel sounds in the abdomen. Tenderness to palpation is noted in the right upper quadrant, but no rebound tenderness is present. Vital signs are normal: blood pressure 128/88, respiratory rate 16, pulse rate 68, oxygen saturation 99% on room air. Murphy's sign is positive.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with cholecystitis secondary to gallstones. Further investigation is warranted to confirm the diagnosis.

Plan
I recommend a laparoscopic cholecystectomy to remove the gallbladder. I will order abdominal radiographs and a follow-up appointment in approximately one week to monitor the patient's progress. The patient should continue to follow up with their physician to monitor their diabetes. I will also schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist.
