Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right knee pain for the past three months, worsening with activity and stairs. He denies a specific injury. He describes the pain as a deep ache, with occasional sharp shooting sensations. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with limited relief. He rates his pain as a 6/10 at rest and 8/10 with exertion.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the medial joint line, limited range of motion, and a positive drawer test. No effusion is palpable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with medial meniscus injury.

Plan
Order MRI of the right knee to evaluate for meniscal tears. Recommend physical therapy to improve range of motion and strengthen surrounding muscles. Advise the patient to avoid high-impact activities until further evaluation.
