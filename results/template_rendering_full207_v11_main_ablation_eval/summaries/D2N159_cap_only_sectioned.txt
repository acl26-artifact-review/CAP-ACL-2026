Chief Complaint
Right hand pain and numbness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right hand pain and numbness for the past month, primarily with mouse use. He describes the pain as sharp and aching, and the numbness as tingling and cold. He reports decreased grip strength. He denies any recent trauma. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain. He states the pain is worse with prolonged mouse use. He reports tingling and numbness in his right hand.

Findings
The doctor observes a positive Tinel's sign in the right hand. Grip strength is decreased. The patient reports pain on extension of the wrist.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
A nerve conduction study will be ordered. The doctor orders a wrist splint to be worn at night. The doctor prescribes ibuprofen. Physical therapy may be considered if symptoms persist.
