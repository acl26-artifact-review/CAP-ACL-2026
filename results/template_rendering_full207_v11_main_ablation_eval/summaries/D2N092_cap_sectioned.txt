Chief Complaint
Pain in the arm following hitting it on the net.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his arm following hitting it on the net. He describes the injury as occurring when he hit a backhand and the pole part of the net, resulting in a jar to his arm. He reports some swelling, but not significant. He also reports some numbness in his hand when moving his wrist, and some pain when flexing his thumb. He has a history of allergies and takes flonase. He previously sustained an injury from a fall while using a rake, and also a stabbing injury. He reports this current injury occurred while playing tennis.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal radius of the arm. Pain is elicited with palpation, flexion, and extension of the wrist and thumb. No fracture is identified on the x-ray.

Assessment
The patient presents with a strain and possible contusion of the distal radius muscle.

Plan
Start ice packs to the affected area. Order motrin 800 mg three times a day with food. Follow up in one week if symptoms do not improve. Dragon, please order the medications and procedures as discussed.
