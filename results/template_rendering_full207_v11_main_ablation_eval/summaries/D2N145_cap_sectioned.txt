Chief Complaint
A small rash leftover from doxycycline.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a small rash leftover from doxycycline. She started using doxycycline only one a day because the pharmacist said if it was getting better, to just limit it to once a day. She was taking it twice a day and noticed improvement, so she switched to once a day, and it seems to have plateaued. She is not breastfeeding and should not be breastfeeding while on the medication. She uses metro cream after her face wash and no other lotions. She stopped using elidel because she read about the risks associated with the oral form and not the topical form. She is feeling sun sensitive and takes precautions when going out. She reports hand pain. She was taking doxycycline twice a day and now only once a day. She is feeling moisture getting back in. She is worried about elidel.

Findings
Examination reveals Fitzpatrick skin type IV, with faint erythematous papules and mild redness around and underneath the nostrils on the bilateral medial cheeks. The patient reports a small rash. The patient reports feeling dry.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with rosacea and perioral dermatitis.

Plan
The doctor recommends gently washing eyelids once a day with gentle cleanser such as vanicream. The doctor recommends using a bruder mask as needed. The doctor recommends restarting doxycycline to twice a day until the rash is gone, and then once a day for another week before stopping. The doctor recommends using metro cream. The doctor recommends vanicream as a moisturizer. The doctor recommends increasing doxycycline to twice a day. The doctor recommends a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist. The patient should continue using the face wash and metro cream. The patient should reduce to once per day if starting to get dry.
