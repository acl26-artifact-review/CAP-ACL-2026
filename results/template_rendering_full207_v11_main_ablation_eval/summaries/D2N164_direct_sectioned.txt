Chief Complaint
Elizabeth is a 53-year-old female today complaining of chest pain and she has a past medical history of diabetes and high blood pressure so elizabeth tell me what's going on with your with your chest pain

History of Present Illness
Elizabeth reports chest pain that started approximately three months ago, occurring with walking. The pain is described as dull and pressure-like, not radiating. She has been trying to maintain her activity level but has been limited by the pain. Her blood sugars have been slightly higher due to decreased activity, and she is trying to reduce her soda intake. She has an ophthalmology appointment scheduled in two weeks. She denies nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. She reports no prior diagnosis of high blood pressure, although previous clinicians have noted elevated readings. She has been taking metformin 500mg twice daily. She is addicted to diet coke.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a two out of six systolic ejection murmur. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable, including clear lung sounds, regular heart rhythm, equal pulses, soft abdomen, and no lower extremity edema. The EKG shows no signs of acute myocardial infarction.

Assessment
Unstable angina is suspected, considering her history of diabetes, hypertension, and chest pain.

Plan
I am going to order aspirin daily, eighty one milligrams, and nitroglycerin for use as needed for chest pain. I am also going to order a hemoglobin A1c and start hydrochlorothiazide for blood pressure control and edema management. I will refer her to cardiology for a stress test. Continue metformin 500mg twice daily and ophthalmology appointment. Schedule a follow-up in two weeks to monitor blood pressure and adjust medications as needed.
