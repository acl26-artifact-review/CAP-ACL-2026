Chief Complaint
Back pain, primarily in the lower back and radiating down the left leg.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain that began four days ago after bending over to lift an object, accompanied by pain radiating down his left leg. He describes occasional tingling but primarily experiences pain, particularly with certain positions. He has a history of occasional back pain over the years and has previously tried ibuprofen with some relief. He reports being approximately 15 pounds heavier and acknowledges a need for weight loss. He is currently taking norvasc 5mg daily for blood pressure and reports some ankle swelling. He has not had any prior back surgeries or significant past back problems.

Findings
The patient is a 43-year-old male with a history of diabetes, hypertension, and high cholesterol. Examination reveals no jugular venous distension or bruits. Lung sounds are clear, and the heart exam shows a two over six systolic ejection murmur, which is stable. The abdomen is soft, and the lower back examination reveals tenderness in the left paraspinal area. Straight leg raise is negative. Neurological exam is normal. There is one plus edema of the lower extremities, primarily in the ankles. Hemoglobin A1c was 7.0%. The patient's blood pressure in the clinic is 120/70 mmHg. The patient reports checking his blood sugar approximately two to three times a week, with readings typically in the range of 120-140 mg/dL.

Assessment
The patient presents with musculoskeletal back pain, likely a muscular sprain, secondary to his reported activity. He also continues to have uncontrolled diabetes and hypertension with associated edema.

Plan
I am ordering a hemoglobin A1c to further assess his diabetes control and will continue the metformin 500 mg twice daily. I will also order some blood work to evaluate his kidney function and electrolytes. I am prescribing naproxen 500 mg twice daily and flexeril 10 mg twice daily for pain and muscle relaxation. I am referring the patient to physical therapy to address his back pain and strengthen his lower back muscles. I will also prescribe hydrochlorothiazide 10 mg once daily to manage the ankle edema. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to review the A1c results and assess treatment response. We will continue to monitor his blood pressure and edema levels.
