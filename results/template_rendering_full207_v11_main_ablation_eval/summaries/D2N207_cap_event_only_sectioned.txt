Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies any recent trauma. He reports partial relief with acetaminophen. He states, 'The pain is constant, but it gets worse when I try to lift things.'

Findings
The patient's left shoulder exhibits limited active abduction and demonstrates pain with impingement testing. Vitals are within normal limits. No acute distress noted.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs to evaluate for rotator cuff pathology. Initiate physical therapy for targeted strengthening and range of motion. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
