Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right elbow pain for approximately four days, exacerbated by pottery creation. The pain is located on the medial aspect of the elbow and radiates down the arm. Ibuprofen provides minimal relief. The patient reports a history of hypertension and diabetes mellitus and takes lisinopril 10 mg daily and metformin 500 mg twice daily. The patient also enjoys baking and consuming sweets, occasionally experiencing elevated blood sugars. He has a history of diabetic counseling but would like a refresher.

Findings
Physical exam reveals moderate tenderness to palpation over the medial epicondyle of the right elbow. Resistance pronation elicits pain. Wrist flexion elicits pain. Pulse and blood pressure are normal. Respiratory rate is 18 breaths per minute. Oxygen saturation is 100%. The patient's lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. The patient's x-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormality. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is consistent with medial epicondylitis. The patient also has longstanding hypertension and diabetes mellitus.

Plan
Order a sling for right elbow support. Prescribe ice packs for 20 minutes, three times daily. Prescribe ibuprofen 600 mg every six hours as needed for one week. Increase metformin to 1000 mg twice daily. Order an A1C test. Refer to diabetic counseling. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress and adjust management. Consider physical therapy referral if symptoms persist beyond two weeks.
