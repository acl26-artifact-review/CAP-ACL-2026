Chief Complaint
Right wrist injury sustained during laundry activities, accompanied by significant pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports injuring his right wrist yesterday morning while doing laundry. He describes the injury as severe, rating the pain a nine on a scale of one to ten. He denies prior wrist injuries. He reports some numbness and tingling in his fingers, which he attributes to swelling. He has attempted to manage the pain with ice, an ACE wrap, and ibuprofen, with some modest relief. He states the pain is exacerbated by movement and relieved somewhat by keeping his arm in a slightly elevated position. He reports a history of a cold falling out of a basket, which he attempted to catch, resulting in the injury. He describes the injury as persistent and significantly impacting his ability to perform daily activities.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the dorsum of the right wrist, with crepitus upon palpation. Observation reveals significant ecchymosis and swelling. The patient's vital signs demonstrate a slightly elevated blood pressure, likely secondary to pain. Heart rate is elevated at 80 beats per minute. Respiratory rate is normal at 20 breaths per minute. Temperature is 99.7°F. The patient's lower extremities are without edema. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Dorsal flexion and lateral deviation elicit significant pain. Radial deviation also causes pain. The patient is able to make a fist, but with difficulty and reduced strength.

Assessment
The patient presents with a confirmed Colles' fracture of the right wrist. The clinical findings are consistent with a traumatic injury, and the examination reveals significant pain and swelling. The presence of dorsal angulation and ulnar styloid fracture further supports the diagnosis.

Plan
We are providing a thumb spica brace for six weeks to immobilize the wrist and promote healing. We will initiate physical therapy sessions in approximately three weeks to restore range of motion and strength. A course of ultram 50mg every six hours has been prescribed for pain management. We recommend surgical intervention to stabilize the fracture and prevent malunion. The patient will undergo an overnight hospital stay following surgery. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess healing and adjust the treatment plan as needed. The patient should postpone any non-essential travel until the fracture has adequately stabilized. Further imaging may be considered to monitor fracture healing.
