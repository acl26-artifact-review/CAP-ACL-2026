S:
The patient reports right knee pain following a fall while changing a light bulb, describing the pain as primarily in the inside of his knee. He states the pain worsened when bending his knee forward and twisting it. He has been applying ice and taking ibuprofen, but reports minimal relief. He denies any other injuries. He reports a history of afib and has been taking digoxin.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the right knee, particularly when applying valgus stress. No fractures are identified on x-ray. There is a small amount of fluid in the knee, but no dislocation. Sensation is intact. The patient's knee range of motion is limited by pain.

A:
The patient has an acute medial meniscus sprain or strain, likely secondary to trauma. Examination findings support this diagnosis.

P:
Rescribe a knee brace and instruct the patient to use crutches for two days. Recommend motrin 800 mg every six hours as needed for pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to reassess and consider an MRI if symptoms do not improve.
