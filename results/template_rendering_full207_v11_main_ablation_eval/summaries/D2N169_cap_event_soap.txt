S:
The patient reports a sudden onset of shortness of breath following a three-hour bike ride over the weekend, accompanied by lightheadedness and a mild chest pain that resolved within an hour. He denies fever, chest pain, nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. He reports a mild cough in the past year and a half, now resolved. He states his cataract surgery from four and a half months ago has been successful. He is currently taking imitrex for migraines and protonix for reflux, both of which are well-managed.

O:
Xygenation is normal. Jugular venous distension is absent. Heart rhythm is regular, with no murmurs. Lung auscultation reveals expiratory wheezing bilaterally. Lower extremity edema is present. Chest x-ray is normal, showing no pulmonary edema or pneumonia. Pulmonary function tests are within normal limits. Labs are unremarkable.

A:
The patient's symptoms suggest a possible asthma exacerbation triggered by exercise and allergies. A differential diagnosis includes cardiac etiology, though less likely given the normal exam and chest x-ray.

P:
Rescribe albuterol inhaler, two puffs every four hours as needed for wheezing or shortness of breath. Order a pulmonary referral. Continue current imitrex regimen as needed for migraines. Follow up in two weeks to assess symptom resolution.
