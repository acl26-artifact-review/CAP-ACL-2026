Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing accident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a knee injury sustained while skiing over the weekend. He describes a 'pop' sensation and immediate pain following a cartwheel-like fall. He has been managing the pain with Advil and icing. He denies prior knee injuries. He reports some bruising and tenderness to palpation. The patient reports a mild shoulder bruise.

Findings
The patient's vitals are within normal limits: temperature 97.9°F, heart rate 64 bpm, respirations 16 breaths per minute, blood pressure 110/74 mmHg, and oxygen saturation 98%. Physical examination reveals edema and ecchymosis around the right knee. Range of motion is limited, with some pain on flexion and extension. Pain is reported with range of motion. A quick physical exam was performed. The patient reports tenderness to palpation. An x-ray of the right knee revealed no fracture or bony abnormalities. A quick x-ray was ordered for the right shoulder.

Assessment
The patient has a knee contusion with associated edema and pain. There is no evidence of fracture.

Plan
We will recommend rest, ice application (two to three times daily for 20 minutes), and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory medication such as ibuprofen every six to eight hours as needed. We will order an x-ray of the right shoulder to rule out further injury. I recommend follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist, or sooner if there is no improvement with conservative management. Schedule a follow-up appointment to assess physical therapy referral if needed.
