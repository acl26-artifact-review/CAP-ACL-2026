Chief Complaint
Right knee pain and limp.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain and a limp for the past 3 days following a fall. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, exacerbated by weight-bearing. He denies any prior knee injuries. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain, which provides minimal relief. He also reports some stiffness in the knee.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Lachman's test is negative. McMurray's test is negative. The patient demonstrates a noticeable limp.

Assessment
Based on the patient's report and physical exam findings, the most likely diagnosis is a lateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
Order an x-ray of the right knee to rule out fracture. Prescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for pain management. Refer to physical therapy for evaluation and treatment. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess progress.
