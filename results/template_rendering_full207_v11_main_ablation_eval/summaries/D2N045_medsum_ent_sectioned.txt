Chief Complaint
Knee pain, primarily on the right side, exacerbated by running.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing knee pain primarily in the right knee, particularly when running. He describes the pain as starting during runs and intensifying to the point where he needs to stop. Ice and ibuprofen provide temporary relief. He has been running for years, including 5ks and marathons, and has not previously experienced this level of discomfort. He denies any recent trauma. He reports a family history of immune diseases but is unsure if there is a history of arthritis.

Findings
Blood pressure was 120/60 mmHg. Heart rate was 58 bpm. Respirations were 14 breaths per minute. Pedal pulse was strong and rhythmic. Physical examination revealed no redness or swelling in the right knee. A small amount of effusion was appreciated. Knee movement elicited pain with pushing and pulling, but no pain with passive range of motion. The right knee caused pain with external pressure. No numbness or tingling was noted in the feet.

Assessment
The patient has a knee sprain from overuse, likely exacerbated by recent running activity. There is no evidence of fracture or arthritis.

Plan
I am ordering physical therapy for your right knee to strengthen the lower extremities and recommend good running exercises. I would like you to stay on two tylenol five hundred milligrams and two ibuprofen two hundred milligram tablets, taking them three times a day. I would like to schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess your progress. Please refrain from running for two weeks and consult with the physical therapist for appropriate exercises. Do you have any questions?
