Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports falling yesterday, striking his right knee. He describes immediate pain and swelling. He denies any loss of consciousness. He reports being unable to bear weight on the leg. He states he has taken ibuprofen for the pain, but it hasn't provided significant relief. He denies any prior knee injuries.

Findings
The patient presents with significant right knee swelling and tenderness to palpation. Range of motion is limited due to pain. The right knee demonstrates mild ecchymosis. Vitals are unremarkable. No signs of neurovascular compromise.

Assessment
Right knee contusion with possible ligament sprain. Clinical exam suggests a possible MCL injury.

Plan
Order right knee X-ray to rule out fracture. Prescribe ice packs and elevation. Advise weight-bearing as tolerated. Follow up in 2 weeks or sooner if symptoms worsen.
