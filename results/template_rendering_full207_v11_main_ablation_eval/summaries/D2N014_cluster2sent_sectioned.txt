Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath and concern for heart failure exacerbation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath approximately 30 minutes after arriving in the emergency department, accompanied by feelings of anxiety and concern for a possible heart attack. He also describes episodes of lightheadedness and dizziness six hours after returning home, followed by feeling fine on Sunday. He takes medication for heart failure and has had a skin cancer removed five months ago with no complications. He denies vision problems. Lung exam reveals fine crackles bilaterally and trace lower extremity edema.

Findings
Blood pressure was elevated, consistent with previous emergency department findings. The patient's heart rate and rhythm were regular with no murmurs. An ECG showed stable left ventricular hypertrophy. Echocardiogram revealed stable but slightly reduced left ventricular ejection fraction. Skin biopsy results are pending review.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with an exacerbation of heart failure, likely secondary to underlying left ventricular hypertrophy.

Plan
Prescribe lasix 80 milligrams once daily and carvedilol 25 milligrams twice daily. Order lasix 80 milligrams, once daily. Arrange follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
