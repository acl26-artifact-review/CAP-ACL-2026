Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath and chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing increasing shortness of breath over the past month, particularly with exertion. He describes the shortness of breath as a feeling of being 'winded.' He also reports intermittent chest pain, described as a tightness, rated 4 out of 10, and occurring primarily with activity. The patient denies any recent trauma. He reports a history of smoking 2 packs per day for 20 years, which he quit 4 years ago. He has a history of a heart attack in 2018. He currently takes aspirin daily and insulin. He also reports occasional numbness and tingling in his feet, and reports mild pain in his right foot. He denies pain in his foot.

Findings
The patient's blood sugar has been running higher than normal. Examination reveals sensation taking longer to warm up. The patient has granulation tissue and purulent discharge on the right foot. The patient reports tension headaches.

Assessment
The patient has coronary artery disease, diabetes, neuropathy, and a history of heart attack. The patient has an ulcer on the right foot.

Plan
A lower extremity arterial ultrasound is ordered. A podiatry consult is ordered. The patient should continue with a statin and aspirin. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Arrange follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
