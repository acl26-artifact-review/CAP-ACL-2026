Chief Complaint
Newly diagnosed diabetes and elevated blood sugar levels following an emergency room visit.

History of Present Illness
John, a 55-year-old male with a history of anxiety and epilepsy, presented to the emergency room after experiencing high blood sugar levels. He reports a period of increased stress and strain over the past couple of weeks, coupled with inconsistent sugar intake, which he attributes to frequent travel and visitors. He was subsequently treated in the ER and placed on metformin. He reports feeling somewhat better now, but remains concerned about his diabetes and anxiety. He is currently taking keppra for his epilepsy and has not experienced any recent seizures.

Findings
Physical exam reveals trace pitting edema to the lower extremities. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, respiratory rate 16 breaths per minute. Glucose level was 162 mg/dL. Hemoglobin A1c is 8%.

Assessment
The patient presents with newly diagnosed diabetes, likely related to recent stress and inconsistent dietary habits. His elevated blood sugar levels and HbA1c warrant further investigation and management.

Plan
Continue the patient on metformin 500 mg twice daily. Order a follow-up hemoglobin A1c to monitor glycemic control. Schedule a neurology consultation to discuss epilepsy management. Arrange a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
