Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain, which began approximately three weeks ago following increased tennis activity. He describes the pain as sharp and stabbing, present with movement and rated as 8/10 at worst, and 3/10 at rest. He has not experienced any trauma or popping sensation. He has tried tylenol and ibuprofen, but finds relief is temporary. He also attempted ice, but did not notice improvement. The patient reports Easter candy consumption which may be contributing to elevated blood sugars.

Findings
Physical exam reveals moderate lateral epicondylar tenderness of the right elbow. Passive range of motion elicits pain. There is no palpable joint effusion. Right heart exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur. Right wrist exam reveals mild pain with resisted extension. The patient has an allergy to penicillin and a surgical history of a previous gallbladder removal ten years ago. Right elbow x-ray demonstrates no acute fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
Acute lateral epicondylitis of the right elbow.

Plan
Increase metformin to 1000 mg twice daily. Order a hemoglobin A1c and CBC blood test and a chem-12 blood test. Prescribe ultram 50 mg once daily for pain. Provide a forearm brace. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to reevaluate. Order a refill of albuterol. Continue flovent and albuterol as needed.
