Chief Complaint
Acne follow-up.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports acne, stating that he has been using the benzoyl peroxide wash and tretinoin cream as prescribed. He reports no redness, peeling, or dryness with the medications. He states he washes his face with the benzoyl peroxide wash and applies the tretinoin cream at night. He reports that he has not been applying the benzoyl peroxide wash to his chest and back. He reports that the acne on his face is improving, but there is a mild flare on his back.

Findings
Examination reveals scattered inflammatory papules and pustules across the upper back and posterior shoulders. There are less than 5 inflammatory papules on the forehead, cheeks, and chin. The patient's skin appears normal. The patient's mask comes down easily.

Assessment
Acne vulgaris, mild to moderate, with a mild flare on the back. The patient's acne is improving on the face.

Plan
Continue current treatment plan for the face. Initiate treatment with PanOxyl 10% foaming wash to the back in the shower daily. Send a one-year prescription for clindamycin with 11 refills and a tretinoin cream with three refills. Send samples and coupons for PanOxyl 10%.
