Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and when lying on his left side. He denies any recent trauma. He has been taking two extra-strength acetaminophen tablets every six to eight hours for pain relief. He reports a pain level of 7 without medication and a 2-3 with acetaminophen. He experiences numbness and tingling in his arm and hand. The patient believes the pain will eventually resolve and references successful outcomes for other patients with physical therapy.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder, tenderness to palpation of the greater tuberosity of the humerus, and no tenderness at the sternoclavicular or clavicular joints. Hand grip strength is normal bilaterally. Cap refill is brisk in all digits. Neurovascular exam is intact. X-ray of the left shoulder shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order an MRI of the left shoulder to evaluate for additional pathology. Refer the patient for physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. Continue acetaminophen PRN for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
