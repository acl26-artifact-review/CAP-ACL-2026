Chief Complaint
Reports kidney stone pain and seeks evaluation.

History of Present Illness
Angela reports experiencing intermittent kidney stone pain for several years, with the most recent episode occurring last week. She presented to the emergency room and reports that the stone passed approximately three days ago. She denies further blood in her urine, but does report some pain. She has been taking percocet for the pain. She reports difficulty with fast food and has been struggling to maintain a healthy diet. She has been taking metformin 500mg daily. She has a history of diabetes and hypertension. She has been taking norvasc 2.5mg daily. She has been taking melatonin as needed for insomnia. She reports worrying about things and staying awake at night. She has a blood pressure cuff at home and has been taking readings two to three times a week, with readings around 130/80. She has an A1C of 6.7%.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure was measured at 150/90. The patient's heart rate was 78 bpm. The patient's lungs were clear bilaterally. The patient's heart murmur was grade 2/6. The patient's abdomen was non-tender. The patient's back was non-tender. The patient's left shoulder range of motion was limited. The patient reported pain with impingement testing.

Assessment
The patient has a history of recurrent kidney stones. The patient has hypertension and diabetes.

Plan
Order a referral to urology for further evaluation of the recurrent kidney stones. Order a urinalysis. Order blood sugar monitoring before meals. Continue metformin 500mg daily. Continue norvasc 2.5mg daily. Prescribe melatonin 10mg as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months. Recommend practicing proper sleep hygiene, including turning off electronics before bed.
