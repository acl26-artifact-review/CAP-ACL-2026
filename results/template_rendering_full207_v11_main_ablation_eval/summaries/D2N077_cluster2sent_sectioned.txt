Chief Complaint
Right wrist pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right wrist pain that began yesterday morning after bumping his wrist while walking up and down the stairs doing laundry. He slipped and tried to catch himself putting his arms out. The pain is described as severe, rated 9/10, and is exacerbated by raising his arm. Ibuprofen provides some relief. He has been using ice and an ace wrap. The patient feels tingling, unsure if this is due to swelling. He denies blood in stool.

Findings
The clinician palpates the wrist and finds tenderness. Observation reveals obvious swelling and bruising. Range of motion is normal with the right wrist. Positive abduction adduction pain on movement. Blood pressure is elevated, likely due to pain. Heart rate is approximately 80 beats per minute, and respiratory rate is 20 breaths per minute. A positive colles' fracture was identified. Dorsal angulation of the distal fracture fragment is present. Ulnar styloid fracture is present.

Assessment
The patient has a colles' fracture and ulnar styloid fracture of the right wrist.

Plan
The patient will receive a thumb spica brace for today. The clinician will prescribe fifty milligrams of ultram every six hours. Physical therapy will be prescribed a few weeks after surgery. Surgery will be performed as quickly as possible. The patient will stay overnight after surgery. The patient will wear the brace for approximately six weeks. The patient will attend physical therapy three times a week. The fracture appears extra-articular and proximal to the radial ulnar joint.
