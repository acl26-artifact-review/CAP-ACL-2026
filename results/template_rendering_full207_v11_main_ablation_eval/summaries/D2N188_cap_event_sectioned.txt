Chief Complaint
Right ankle injury following a fall during mountain biking.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining an ankle injury after a fall while mountain biking. He states he was doing something he didn't particularly want to be doing and fell, resulting in pain. He describes the pain as being primarily on the outer ankle and mentions tingling and numbness in his feet. He reports a fever of 100 degrees Fahrenheit and describes hearing a crackling sound at the time of the injury. He has been mountain biking for approximately three years and enjoys riding in Colorado, though he's unsure of his favorite location. He has taken Tylenol for the pain, but it provided no relief. He reports limping and difficulty walking.

Findings
Physical exam reveals edema and erythema on the lateral malleolus of the right ankle, along with ecchymosis and excoriations. Palpation of the right lateral malleolus elicits pain. Range of motion is limited, with decreased flexion and extension and internal/external rotation. Vital signs include a temperature of 98.6°F, blood pressure of 118/77 mmHg, and heart rate of 77 beats per minute. X-ray findings show no acute fracture or bony abnormality, but a soft tissue injury is present.

Assessment
Acute right ankle sprain.

Plan
Apply an air cast and provide crutches. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily. Instruct the patient to ice the area for 20 minutes five times a day. Advise weight-bearing as tolerated. Follow up in two weeks if symptoms persist, considering physical therapy or further imaging if needed.
