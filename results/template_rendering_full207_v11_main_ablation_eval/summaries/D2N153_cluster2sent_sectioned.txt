Chief Complaint
Right knee pain and clicking sensation with activity.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining a right knee injury, initially thought to be related to his ankle, with subsequent clicking sensation primarily during deep squatting activities. He has taken ibuprofen, which provides limited relief. He reports numbness and tingling in his right foot related to shoe choice, a chronic issue stemming from previous foot fractures. He describes a threshold for pain, noting that symptoms are relatively stable over the past two weeks, except during exertion like yard work.

Findings
Physical examination reveals slight edema and effusion in the right knee. The varus test is positive, and Lachman's test is negative. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibialis pulses are palpable. X-ray findings demonstrate no acute fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
Based on the examination findings and reported symptoms, the patient likely has a lateral collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam fifteen milligrams once daily and referring the patient to physical therapy to strengthen the surrounding muscles and prevent future injuries. If pain persists after four weeks, an MRI will be considered.
