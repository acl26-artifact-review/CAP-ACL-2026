S:
The patient reports intermittent knee pain related to running, with pain isolated to the right knee and a history of ice and ibuprofen use. He denies redness or swelling. The patient reports a family history of arthritis.

O:
Blood pressure was 120/60 mmHg. Heart rate was 58 bpm. Respiratory rate was 14 breaths per minute. Pedal pulse was present. No numbness or tingling was noted. A small amount of effusion was appreciated in the right knee.

A:
The patient has a probable knee sprain due to overuse, with no evidence of fracture or arthritis. Physical therapy is indicated to strengthen the lower extremities and recommend appropriate running exercises.

P:
Rescribe two tablets of Tylenol 500 mg and two tablets of ibuprofen 200 mg three times daily. Order physical therapy for the right knee. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress and adjust activity levels. Advise the patient to avoid running for two weeks and to consult with physical therapy for appropriate exercise recommendations.
