S:
The patient reports a right knee pain exacerbated by running, particularly towards the end of the day. He describes the pain as isolated to the outside of the knee. He has been icing and taking ibuprofen for the pain, and reports that this provides some relief. He denies any family history of arthritis. He states he has been running regularly, including 5k races and marathons, and has experienced knee pain limiting his ability to continue longer distances.

O:
Blood pressure was 120/60 mmHg, heart rate 58 bpm, respirations 14. The knee examination revealed no redness or swelling, but a small effusion was noted. Pedal pulses were present and intact. The knee moved through a full range of motion with no increased pain. Pedal pulses were normal.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a knee sprain, likely secondary to overuse. There is no evidence of fracture or arthritis.

P:
Rescribe two tablets of Tylenol 500 mg every 4 hours and two tablets of Ibuprofen 200 mg every 4 hours. Order physical therapy for the right knee to address strength and balance. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress.
