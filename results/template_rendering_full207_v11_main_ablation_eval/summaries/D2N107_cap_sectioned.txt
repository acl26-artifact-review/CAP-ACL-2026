Chief Complaint
Back pain following raking leaves.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain that began approximately two hours after raking leaves yesterday. He describes the pain as tightening and pins and needles in his right foot. He previously experienced similar back pain three years ago, which resolved after a day. He tried taking two Advils, but it did not help. He reports that sitting makes the pain slightly better, but bending over causes it to worsen. His partner reports that he experiences pain when standing, particularly when he stands up. He is concerned about a long-term injury.

Findings
Physical exam reveals pain to palpation around the L5 vertebra. Dorsiflexion is normal. Pulses are equal bilaterally. The patient reports high pain in his right hand. Examination does not reveal any acute findings.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a low back sprain. He has a history of similar episodes.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam for pain relief. I am also prescribing physical therapy to improve strength and flexibility. We will schedule a follow-up in two weeks to assess improvement. If symptoms persist, we will consider ordering an MRI.
