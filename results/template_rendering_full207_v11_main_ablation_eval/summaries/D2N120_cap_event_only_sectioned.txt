Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and decreased sensation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand that has been present for approximately two weeks. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, exacerbated by gripping and lifting objects. He also reports decreased sensation to light touch in his left hand, particularly in his fingers. He denies any recent trauma. He reports taking ibuprofen as needed for the pain, which provides some relief. He states, "It's hard to grip things without it hurting."

Findings
Physical exam reveals decreased sensation to light touch in the patient's left hand, particularly in the fingers. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Strength is intact. There are no signs of inflammation.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are most consistent with a lumbar radiculopathy, specifically an L4-L5 herniated disc with radiculopathy.

Plan
A spinal injection is ordered. A spinal injection with a strong inflammatory medication called a corticosteroid is recommended. The patient will be monitored. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
