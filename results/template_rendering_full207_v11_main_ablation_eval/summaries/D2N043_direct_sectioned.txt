Chief Complaint
Patient reports a desire to quit smoking and discusses his history of nicotine dependence.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a 44-year history of smoking, starting in high school and progressing to a pack and a half daily. He attempted to quit cold turkey a year ago, successfully for almost a year, but experienced increased work-related stress leading to a return to smoking. He reports learning meditation and joining a gym to manage stress. He is about to become a father and is motivated to be present for his daughter's growth.

Findings
Vitals were stable, including oxygen saturation 98% on room air, blood pressure 128/88, heart rate 68, and respirations 16. Physical exam revealed a regular and rhythmic heart rate, a two over six systolic murmur, clear and equal bilateral lung sounds with no wheezes or rales, and no palpable lymphadenopathy. No carotid bruits were appreciated.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is nicotine dependence, with a history of type 2 diabetes and gout.

Plan
I will initiate nicotine replacement therapy with a 21 mg transdermal patch, starting next Monday, his birthday, and will decrease the dosage over time. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to re-evaluate the patch dosage and address any concerns. I will order a hemoglobin A1c for his next blood draw. I will continue allopurinol for his gout. I will provide handouts on smoking cessation strategies and stress management techniques. I encourage continued exercise and mindfulness practices to support his overall well-being.
