S:
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall yesterday, describing a pop sensation. He initially could not walk on it but can now put some weight on it with limping. He rates his pain as 6/10 without ibuprofen and 4/10 with ibuprofen. He denies numbness or tingling. He has a history of playing soccer and has not played since the injury. He has had previous ankle injuries but not this one.

O:
There is ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus with associated edema. Tenderness to palpation is present over the anterior and lateral soft tissue. No laxity is noted on anterior drawer or inversion stress. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong and brisk. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds. X-ray shows no fracture.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a right ankle sprain, likely involving the anterior talofibular ligament. No fracture is present.

P:
I am ordering an air cast for the right ankle. Prescribing crutches for weight-bearing. Advise elevation and ice. Prescribe ibuprofen as needed for pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 14 days to assess progress. Advise patient to continue taking ibuprofen as needed.
