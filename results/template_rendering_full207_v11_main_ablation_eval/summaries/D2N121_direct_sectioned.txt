Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a twisting injury sustained while skiing last weekend. He describes the injury as feeling like a stretch followed by swelling. He was able to get out of the water but found it difficult to climb up and squat. He denies prior knee injuries. He has been taking over-the-counter ibuprofen and applying cold packs to the knee, which has provided some relief. He is a frequent water skier and enjoys weekend activities.

Findings
Physical examination reveals ecchymosis and edema in the right knee. Palpation elicits tenderness. Range of motion is decreased. Negative varus and valgus tests, negative Lachman's test, and palpable dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses. Vital signs are stable with blood pressure at 120/77 mmHg. The patient reports no numbness or tingling in the foot.

Assessment
The patient has a medial collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
Order a right knee X-ray to rule out fracture. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain and inflammation. Refer to physical therapy for rehabilitation. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain. Order a lipid panel. Schedule a tetanus shot for preventative health maintenance. Follow up in four weeks to assess healing and response to treatment.
