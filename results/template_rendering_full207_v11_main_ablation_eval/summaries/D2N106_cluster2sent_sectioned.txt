Chief Complaint
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing sudden episodes of shortness of breath, often associated with a feeling of being unwell. He suspects allergies are a contributing factor, having experienced similar symptoms in the past, including lip and throat swelling. He reports diarrhea following heavy drinking and notes that symptoms are often triggered by exposure to cats or being outdoors. He currently takes a daily medication to decrease asthma occurrences. He received a nebulizer at the urgent care for a previous episode.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no rash or angioedema. Pulse oximetry demonstrates normal oxygen saturation. Lung auscultation reveals faint expiratory wheezing bilaterally. Chest x-ray and pulmonary function tests were previously normal. Allergy testing will be performed via skin testing.

Assessment
Newly diagnosed allergic asthma.

Plan
Prescribe albuterol inhaler for symptomatic relief. Prescribe singulair ten milligrams. Schedule allergy testing for next week. Advise patient to avoid potential triggers and continue current medication regimen.
