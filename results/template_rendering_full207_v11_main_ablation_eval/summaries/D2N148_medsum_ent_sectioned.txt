Chief Complaint
Left lower back pain following a back injury four months ago.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of lower back pain following a back injury approximately four months ago, sustained while moving boxes with his son. Initially, he experienced sharp pain with certain movements, but this has subsided somewhat. However, he continues to experience pain when turning or lying on his side, waking him up at night. He tried physical therapy at his gym but found it ineffective. He has been taking ibuprofen and Tylenol, with the ibuprofen providing some, albeit temporary, relief. He denies any bladder or bowel symptoms. He has not had any imaging performed previously, citing concerns about insurance coverage, but is now seeking further evaluation.

Findings
Physical exam reveals decreased flexion and extension of the lower back, a positive straight leg raise, and spasm of the paraspinal muscles. Vital signs are normal: temperature normal, blood pressure 137/85, heart rate 68 bpm, and oxygen saturation 98% on room air. The neck exam reveals bony protuberance of the C5-C6 without pain on palpation. Lower extremity strength is equal bilaterally. There is no acute bony abnormality on lumbar spine x-ray.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and physical examination, an acute disk herniation is suspected.

Plan
I am ordering a lumbar spine MRI to further evaluate the extent of the herniation. I will also refer the patient to physical therapy for rehabilitation. Additionally, I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain management. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and discuss further treatment options, including potential epidural steroid injection if needed.
