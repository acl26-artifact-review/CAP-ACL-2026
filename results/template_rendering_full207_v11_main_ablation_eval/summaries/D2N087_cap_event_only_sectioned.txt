Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and sugar avoidance.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, which he attributes to sugar. He states he tries to avoid pie because of sugar. He also reports occasional headaches and dizziness, though he doesn't find them consistently bothersome. He denies recent trauma. He reports body aches and chills, but these are intermittent. The patient reports taking metformin 'most of the time' and is currently taking lisinopril. He states he is not taking doxycycline.

Findings
Examination reveals edema in the right knee. The patient reports dizziness.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is concerning for Lyme disease, given the reported symptoms and the physician's suspicion. He also has hypertension and diabetes.

Plan
Order a basic metabolic panel and a lipid panel. Order Doxycycline. Continue the patient on lisinopril and metformin.
