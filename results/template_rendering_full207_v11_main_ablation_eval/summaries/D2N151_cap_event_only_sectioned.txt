Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right knee pain for the past three months, worsening with activity. He denies a specific injury. He describes the pain as a deep ache, with occasional sharp twinges. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with limited relief. He states, 'It's hard to walk long distances'.

Findings
The patient demonstrates mild tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited by pain. Lachman's test is negative. McMurray's test is negative. No effusion is palpable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with medial collateral ligament sprain.

Plan
Prescribe ice packs to the right knee three times daily. Recommend physical therapy for range of motion and strengthening exercises. Advise the patient to avoid activities that exacerbate the pain. Follow up in two weeks to assess progress.
