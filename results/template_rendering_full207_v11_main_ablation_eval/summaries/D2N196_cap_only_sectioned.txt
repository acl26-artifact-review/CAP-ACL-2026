Chief Complaint
Lightheadedness and chest heaviness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling lightheaded, particularly when standing up quickly. He describes this as a 'dizzy' sensation. He also reports occasional chest heaviness, which he describes as a 'little heaviness in my chest.' He denies chest pain, palpitations, or shortness of breath. He rates the lightheadedness as mild, and the chest heaviness as occasional. He denies recent trauma. He reports taking lisinopril and atorvastatin as prescribed. He states he has been taking his medications as directed.

Findings
Blood pressure is elevated at 152/92 mmHg. The patient reports feeling lightheaded. Examination reveals no acute distress. Cardiovascular exam is unremarkable. Neurological exam is normal.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms of lightheadedness and chest heaviness, combined with elevated blood pressure, suggest a need to evaluate for potential cardiovascular issues. Hyperlipidemia and osteoarthritis are also present.

Plan
Order a CBC with differential, comprehensive metabolic panel, and cholesterol panel. The doctor wants the patient to check blood pressure measurements twice a day for two weeks. The doctor is increasing the lisinopril dosage to 60 milligrams twice per day. The doctor is adding hydrochlorothiazide at a dosage of 25 milligrams per day. The doctor recommends the patient to stay active as best as possible. Arrange follow-up in four weeks.
