Chief Complaint
Left shoulder and elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
Mr. James presents with constant pain in his left shoulder and left elbow for approximately five months. He reports the pain began five months ago after falling down a flight of icy stairs at home. He describes the fall as landing on his left side. He has continued to lift weights at the gym twice a week, but has had to reduce the weight due to the pain. He reports that the pain is worse when lifting and has stopped lifting more than 15 or 20 pounds. He continues to walk approximately 30 minutes almost every day with his wife since retiring last year. He denies any numbness or tingling. He has tried icing the area, but has not taken any pain medication. He reports that the pain is constant and frustrating. He fell on icy stairs five months ago. He has been lifting weights and has a weight lifting routine. He has been lifting weights and has been to the gym.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the left shoulder and elbow. Hawkins-Kennedy and Neer's tests are positive. Proximal and distal upper extremity strength is intact. Median and radial-ulnar sensation are intact. Abduction is limited to 90 degrees. Empty can test is normal.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with impingement syndrome of the left shoulder. The history of the fall and subsequent pain is concerning for a possible rotator cuff injury, but further evaluation is needed to rule out other causes.

Plan
A referral has been requested for Dr. Martha Rivera to provide physical therapy two days per week. The patient will start physical therapy and continue icing the area and resting. A cortisone injection will be considered if physical therapy does not improve symptoms. The patient should call or email us if symptoms worsen or do not improve with therapy.
