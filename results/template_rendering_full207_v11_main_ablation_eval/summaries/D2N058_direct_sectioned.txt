Chief Complaint
Follow-up after anterior stemi and ongoing management of coronary artery disease.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports having experienced an anterior stemi followed by drug-eluting stent placement in 2018. He has a history of hypertension and diabetes mellitus. He reports feeling tired occasionally but overall feels pretty well since his heart attack. He has been able to enjoy some spring weather and is hoping to get back to walking the trails at the Rex Center. He enjoys gardening and photography. He denies chest pain or shortness of breath. He has been taking his medications as prescribed and reports that the co-pay is not too bad. He has been sleeping with two pillows to avoid laying flat on his back. He reports no swelling in his legs. He is aware of the need to reduce his salt intake due to his heart's reduced ejection fraction.

Findings
Examination reveals a three out of six systolic ejection murmur heard at the left base. Lung sounds are clear. Extremities are unremarkable, with no edema or signs of infection at the cath site. Vitals are stable. The EKG shows normal sinus rhythm with expected changes.

Assessment
Coronary artery disease, newly reduced left ventricular dysfunction, moderate mitral regurgitation, hypertension, and diabetes mellitus.

Plan
Continue aspirin 81 milligrams daily, brilinta 90 milligrams twice daily, atorvastatin 80 milligrams daily, and toprol 50 milligrams daily. Refer to cardiac rehabilitation. Initiate lisinopril 20 milligrams daily and add aldactone 12.5 milligrams daily. Order repeat echocardiogram in approximately two months. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to monitor progress and adjust medications as needed.
