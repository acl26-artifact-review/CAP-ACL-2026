Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a trampoline injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain that began approximately four days ago after slipping off a trampoline in his backyard. He describes a popping sensation at the time of injury followed by significant swelling. He has taken ibuprofen and applied ice to the knee, which provided some relief but he continues to have difficulty with weight-bearing and range of motion. He reports some pain on the inside of the knee and limited range of motion with flexion and extension. He has been managing his diabetes with diet and reports forgetting to check his blood sugar frequently.

Findings
Physical examination reveals significant right knee swelling. Pain is elicited with palpation on the medial aspect of the knee and with range of motion testing, including flexion and extension. The McMurray's test is negative. Vitals are unremarkable. The patient's heart and lungs are clear to auscultation.

Assessment
Suspected medial collateral ligament (MCL) sprain with associated swelling and pain.

Plan
Prescribe a straight leg brace for right knee. Prescribe mobic for pain relief and to reduce inflammation. Order an MRI of the right knee to evaluate for further injury. Obtain a hemoglobin A1c to assess glycemic control. Refill metformin 500 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
