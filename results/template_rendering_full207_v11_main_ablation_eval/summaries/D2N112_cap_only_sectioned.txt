Chief Complaint
Left leg pain and difficulty walking.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports worsening left leg pain, primarily in the calf, that has been present for approximately two weeks. He describes the pain as sharp and radiating, and states it worsens with standing and walking. He denies any recent trauma. He reports difficulty tying his shoes due to weakness in his legs. He has a history of back pain managed with over-the-counter analgesics. He fell off a roof thirty years ago and reports a previous nerve injury.

Findings
The patient exhibits weakness in his lower extremities with a decreased ability to dorsiflex the left foot. Neurological exam reveals decreased sensation in the left lower extremity. Examination reveals pain with palpation of the left calf muscle.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a lumbar radiculopathy, likely secondary to a disk herniation or nerve root impingement. The history of a previous fall and nerve injury supports this diagnosis.

Plan
Order lumbar MRI to evaluate for disk herniation. Recommend an epidural steroid injection to manage nerve root impingement. Continue current NSAIDs. Schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately one month to assess response to treatment.
