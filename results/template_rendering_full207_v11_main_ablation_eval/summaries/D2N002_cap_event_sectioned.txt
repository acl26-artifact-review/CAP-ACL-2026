Chief Complaint
Right knee pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
Andrew reports that his right knee pain began over the weekend following moving boxes in his basements. He describes the pain as constant and reports equal pain in both knees. He denies any recent injury but notes the repetitive nature of the activity. He has been using Tylenol and icing the knees with limited relief. He reports no fever, chills, or nausea. He has been taking synthroid regularly and reports no fatigue or weight gain. He has a history of polycystic kidney disease and hypothyroidism, both well-managed. He has previously experienced knee problems but attributes this episode to the recent exertion. He has been taking acetaminophen for the pain, but it has not provided significant relief.

Findings
Physical exam reveals moderate right knee edema and erythema. Palpation elicits tenderness. Range of motion is limited due to pain. A systolic ejection murmur is present, stable from previous examinations. Vital signs are within normal limits. Lab results show a normal white blood cell count and good kidney function. Right knee X-ray demonstrates normal bony alignment without fracture.

Assessment
Acute exacerbation of arthritis, likely secondary to repetitive strain and exacerbated by recent activity. Ruling out other causes with autoimmune panel.

Plan
Prescribe ultram 50 mg every six hours as needed for pain. Order an autoimmune panel. Continue synthroid as prescribed. Recommend rest and activity modification. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and consider referral to physical therapy if needed.
