Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right knee pain for the past three months, worsening with activity and stairs. He denies a specific injury. He reports using ibuprofen with limited relief. He states the pain is most noticeable when walking on uneven surfaces and during prolonged standing. He denies any swelling or warmth.

Findings
The patient demonstrates mild tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited by pain, with decreased flexion and extension compared to the left knee. Lachman's test is negative. McMurray's test is negative. No effusion is palpable.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with medial knee osteoarthritis.

Plan
Prescribe NSAIDs for pain management. Recommend physical therapy to improve range of motion and strength. Schedule follow-up in six weeks to assess response to treatment.
