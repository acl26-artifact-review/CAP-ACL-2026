Chief Complaint
Roger reports chest pain following yard work and heat exposure.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing chest discomfort approximately one hour during yard work on a hot weekend, accompanied by shortness of breath. He has never experienced this before. He denies any prior cardiac events or family history of heart disease, though his cousin has a history of cardiovascular issues. He reports feeling fine since the episode but sought evaluation. He denies any other symptoms since the initial episode. He had knee surgery approximately two months ago and reports doing well in physical therapy. He reports no chest pain during physical therapy exercises. His blood pressure was elevated at the emergency room, around 180/95, but seemed to regulate after treatment. He has been generally managing his high blood pressure with lisinopril 10mg daily.

Findings
Physical exam reveals an enlarged thyroid gland, not tender. A carotid bruit is present on the right side. Lung sounds are clear. Heart rate is regular and at 72 beats per minute. A systolic ejection murmur of 3/6 is heard. The abdomen is soft. There is a healed scar from a prior right knee surgery, with no lower extremity edema. Blood pressure was 180/95 at presentation. An EKG shows normal sinus rhythm. An echocardiogram reveals a low ejection fraction, which requires further evaluation.

Assessment
The patient presents with a concerning episode of chest pain, potentially related to hypertension or underlying cardiac disease. A low ejection fraction was noted on echocardiogram.

Plan
We will initiate carvedilol 25 mg twice daily to manage blood pressure and improve cardiac function. A cardiac catheterization will be ordered to evaluate for coronary artery disease. Follow-up will be scheduled in four weeks to assess response to treatment and further investigate the low ejection fraction.
