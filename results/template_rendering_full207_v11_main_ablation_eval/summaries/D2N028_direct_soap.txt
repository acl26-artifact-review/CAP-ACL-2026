S:
The patient reports chronic abdominal pain, primarily in the mid-abdomen, for approximately two years. He describes alternating constipation and explosive diarrhea, with the pain being more prominent during constipation. The pain is intermittent, varying in intensity. He reports that certain foods, such as creamy pasta sauces, trigger diarrhea. He has not experienced nausea or vomiting. He reports a stable weight, having lost approximately 25 pounds. He has a sister with irritable bowel syndrome. He denies any family history of gastrointestinal cancers or liver disease. He takes Crestor and olmesartan daily, occasionally uses Tylenol.

O:
Auscultation reveals normal bowel sounds in all four quadrants. The patient reports slight abdominal tenderness to palpation in the left lower quadrant. Liver palpation reveals a slight enlargement. Vital signs are within normal limits. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable. No jaundice is observed. Heart and lung sounds are clear.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a possible irritable bowel syndrome or other gastrointestinal disorder. Liver enlargement warrants further investigation. The patient's history of slightly elevated liver enzymes requires follow-up.

P:
Order laboratory tests including a comprehensive metabolic panel and liver function tests. Schedule a liver ultrasound. Counsel the patient to discuss Crestor with his primary care physician and explore alternative medications. Recommend a fiber supplement to manage constipation. Advise the patient to increase water intake to at least 48 ounces daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to review test results and assess symptom improvement.
