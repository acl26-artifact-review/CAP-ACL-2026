S:
The patient reports ankle pain following a basketball injury, describing a twisting motion and subsequent bruising and swelling. He states he's been having trouble walking and that the ankle doesn't feel stable. He's also been having difficulty walking and reports it doesn't feel solid. He reports returning to a good rhythm with working out and doesn't want to disrupt that. He's been volunteering to coach starting in April. He also reports working from home and can elevate his ankle.

O:
The patient's right ankle is visibly swollen and bruised. Capillary refill is brisk. Examination reveals exquisite tenderness to palpation over the lateral aspect of the ankle. The patient can stand on the ankle with some difficulty. An AP lateral oblique x-ray demonstrates a displaced lateral malleolus at the Weber C level with no evidence of medial or posterior malleolar fractures.

A:
Lateral malleolar fracture is diagnosed. Surgical intervention is recommended due to the location of the fracture. The patient will likely require a period of immobilization and will need to sit out of activities for approximately three months.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam for pain and inflammation. Instruct the patient to ice and elevate the ankle regularly. Order crutches for weight-bearing. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months to assess healing and rehabilitation progress. Advise the patient to continue coaching and to gradually resume exercise as tolerated.
