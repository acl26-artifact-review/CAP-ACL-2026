Chief Complaint
Anxiety and recent breast changes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing anxiety, which was significant in November and December of last year, and has since improved with buspar. She notes that her anxiety often spiked around her menstrual periods, correlating with the progesterone she was taking. She describes a pattern of anxiety episodes beginning during the day, often at work. She attributes this to the stress of delivering COVID-19 test results in the early pandemic, noting the delays and the feeling of being unable to help adequately. She reports feeling selfish because she wasn't on the front lines, but acknowledges the overall stress and pressure on the healthcare system. She stopped taking the camila birth control recently, and has experienced increased hunger and a sense of being 'off.' She has also noticed changes in her breasts, specifically the appearance of small, persistent lumps, which she is concerned about given her family history of breast cancer and her mother's diagnosis.

Findings
Physical exam reveals palpable, smaller lumps in the left breast, which are tender to palpation. The patient's face appears somewhat thinner, likely due to weight loss over the past six months. Her vital signs are within normal limits. The pap smear from 2019 was negative.

Assessment
The patient presents with anxiety, likely exacerbated by pandemic-related stress and recent hormonal changes. She also has palpable breast changes, warranting further investigation to rule out malignancy.

Plan
A referral to a breast specialist will be arranged for a consultation and potential diagnostic workup, including a breast ultrasound. The patient will be advised to continue with buspar as prescribed. The patient's medication list will be reviewed, and the camila birth control refill will be ordered. Follow-up will be scheduled in four weeks to assess the response to treatment and monitor the breast changes. The patient will be educated on the importance of self-examination and reporting any new or concerning changes to her healthcare provider.
