Chief Complaint
Severe pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports severe pain, primarily in the abdomen, for the past several weeks. He describes the pain as constant and throbbing, and rates it as 8 out of 10. He also reports weight loss and constipation. The patient denies fever or chills. He has a history of ovarian cancer diagnosed 3 years ago. He reports feeling fatigued and weak. He states he has experienced some nausea but denies vomiting.

Findings
Physical exam reveals the patient to be pale and appears fatigued. Vital signs are: blood pressure 110/70 mmHg, heart rate 88 bpm, respiratory rate 18 breaths per minute. Abdominal exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the lower right quadrant. Neurological exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient presents with ovarian cancer, likely metastatic, complicated by pain, weight loss, and constipation.

Plan
Order CT scan of the abdomen and pelvis to evaluate for metastatic disease. Initiate palliative care consultation. Prescribe morphine for pain management. Schedule follow-up with oncologist in two weeks.
