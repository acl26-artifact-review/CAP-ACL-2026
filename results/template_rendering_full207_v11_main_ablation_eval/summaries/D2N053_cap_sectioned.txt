Chief Complaint
The patient reports itchy scalp and dandruff.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent itchy scalp and dandruff problem for approximately six months, with worsening over time. He has tried over-the-counter treatments such as Head and Shoulders and castor oil, but none provided significant relief. He denies any recent scalp trauma or new chemical exposures. He reports that his sister has similar symptoms. The patient denies any other symptoms, including joint pain or fever.

Findings
Physical examination reveals demarcated, scaly, erythematous plaques on the scalp, consistent with dandruff. Vital signs are within normal limits: blood pressure 130/86 mmHg, heart rate 98 bpm, respiratory rate 18 breaths per minute, and temperature 37.2°C.

Assessment
The patient has a scalp psoriasis.

Plan
I recommend clobetasol solution 0.05% twice daily to the affected areas of the scalp. Continue using t-gel shampoo. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in three months or sooner if symptoms worsen. Please do not hesitate to call if you experience a flare-up.
