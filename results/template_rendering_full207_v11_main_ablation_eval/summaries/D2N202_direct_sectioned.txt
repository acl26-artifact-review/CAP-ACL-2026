Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right shoulder pain that began approximately three weeks ago following a fall while painting. He reports pain with overhead reaching and lifting. He iced the arm and took Advil initially. He denies prior shoulder pain or numbness/tingling. He has not yet visited the new Lowe's store.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the right shoulder and tenderness to the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Neurovascular examination shows brisk capillary refill and equal and bilateral sensation. Right shoulder x-ray demonstrates no fracture or bony abnormality. Vitals are stable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order an MRI of the right shoulder. Refer to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. Continue taking an NSAID as tolerated, and consider Tylenol if Advil is not tolerated. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to evaluate progress and adjust the treatment plan.
