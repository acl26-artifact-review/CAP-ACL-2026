S:
The patient reports numbness and tingling in both hands, beginning approximately one month ago and worsening over the past month. He describes the sensation as 'sore' and 'weaker,' particularly at night. He also reports mild stiffness in his neck, related to a contusion sustained during a car accident last December. He mentions experiencing occasional floaters in his vision. The patient reports fatigue and weight gain, and excessive appetite and bloating. He also states he is always hungry. He describes nail bed dryness and itching. He reports shortness of breath and leg swelling.

O:
Physical exam reveals pin prick sensation diminished in the third and fourth fingers on the right hand and the fourth and fifth fingers on the left hand. Reflexes are one plus and symmetric bilaterally. Tinel's sign is positive at both wrists and both elbows. Blood pressure is mildly low. Heart rate is 78 bpm. Temperature is 98.6°F.

A:
The primary problem is bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome, with possible cubital tunnel syndrome. Differential diagnosis includes peripheral neuropathy. The patient's history of chronic kidney disease, gout, hypertension, anxiety, and diabetes are relevant to this presentation.

P:
I will order an EMG and nerve conduction study to evaluate the bilateral carpal tunnel and cubital tunnel syndromes. I will schedule a follow-up in two weeks to discuss the results. I will recommend continued use of splints. I will also recommend a referral to hand clinic for possible carpal tunnel release surgery if symptoms do not resolve.
