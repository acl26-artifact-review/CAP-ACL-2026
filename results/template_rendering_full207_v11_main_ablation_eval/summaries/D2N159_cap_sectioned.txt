Chief Complaint
Numbness and tingling in the right hand, particularly with typing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing numbness and tingling in his right hand for approximately six months, with intermittent symptoms. The symptoms have become more persistent over the past month, particularly when typing on the keyboard, leading to pain. Ibuprofen and avoiding keyboard use provide some relief. The patient denies any recent trauma or other neurological symptoms. He reports the numbness and tingling is most prominent with the thumb, index, and middle fingers, and that it is not affecting his sleep.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the wrist, with pain reproduced upon wrist extension and flexion. Grip strength is decreased in the right hand. A positive Tinel's sign was elicited over the wrist. The patient grimaces when the doctor taps on the wrist.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Prescribe a wrist splint to wear at night. Continue ibuprofen to 600 mg four times daily. Order an EMG to evaluate nerve conduction studies. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and discuss potential surgical options if conservative management fails.
