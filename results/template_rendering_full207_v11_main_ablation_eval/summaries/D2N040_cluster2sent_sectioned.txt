Chief Complaint
Carolyn is a 34-year-old female with a history of diabetes mellitus type two who is here today with a headache.

History of Present Illness
Carolyn reports that her headache started about a week ago, presenting as a dull pain in the back of her head accompanied by flushing in her ears and occasional dizziness. She has been taking Tylenol and Advil, but the headaches persist and recur. She describes experiencing tightness in her neck and shoulders, potentially linked to stress, and reports that the headaches have interfered with her exercise routine. She denies fever or visual changes, and states this is not the worst headache she has experienced. The headaches began around the time she increased her stress levels.

Findings
A physical examination revealed unremarkable findings, including intact extraocular movements, no papilledema on funduscopic examination, and a grade three out of six systolic ejection murmur. Posterior mild posterior paraspinal muscular tenderness was noted in the cervical spine and bilateral trapezius musculature, with tightness in those muscles. Carolyn has not been checking her blood sugars.

Assessment
Carolyn's presentation is most consistent with a tension-type headache, likely exacerbated by musculoskeletal tension and stress.

Plan
I am ordering a hemoglobin A1c and a chem-12 to assess her blood tests. I will also prescribe flexeril 5 milligrams three times daily to help relax her neck muscles. We will continue her metformin at 500 milligrams twice daily. Please monitor your blood sugars daily and return in one month with those numbers. I recommend returning for a follow-up in four weeks if the headaches do not improve within the next few days, or contacting us sooner if they worsen. Please continue to prioritize your exercise routine to help manage your blood sugars.
