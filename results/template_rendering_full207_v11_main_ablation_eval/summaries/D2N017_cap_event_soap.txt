The patient reports a painful contusion to the lateral aspect of his right upper leg following a bowling incident, where he hit the ball into the lane and subsequently fell. He describes the pain as significant, especially when walking, and reports it is exacerbated when going upstairs or downstairs. He has been taking ibuprofen and tylenol for the pain.

O:
Examination reveals tenderness to the lateral aspect of the right upper leg. The x-ray demonstrates no fracture or swelling. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The primary problem is a contusion of the right upper leg.

P:
The doctor recommends the patient use ice for pain relief. The doctor will start the patient on mobic 15 milligrams once a day. The doctor recommends follow-up if symptoms do not improve within one week.
