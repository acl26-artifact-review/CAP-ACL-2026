Chief Complaint
Abnormal lab findings, including low hemoglobin and gastritis.

History of Present Illness
Vincent is a 56-year-old male presenting with dizziness and lightheadedness, reported in the emergency room. He has noted slight decreased appetite and some nausea, particularly when sitting in a car. He denies any recent trauma or bleeding from gastrointestinal sources. He reports having had a previous scare with a possible lung cancer, which was later determined to be benign. He has had knee replacement surgery last year, which is currently doing okay. He admits to a fondness for coffee, consuming approximately one 8-ounce cup daily.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to the right lower quadrant upon palpation of the abdomen. The patient's heart rhythm is regular, and his lungs are clear. Lower extremities show no edema. Endoscopy revealed gastritis and a mild polyp, which was biopsied. The complete blood count (CBC) shows a hemoglobin of 8.2, significantly low for his height and weight.

Assessment
The patient presents with anemia, likely secondary to gastritis and a mild polyp.

Plan
Prescribe proton pump inhibitor (PPI) - protonix 40 mg daily. Advise the patient to reduce caffeine intake and avoid ibuprofen and alcohol. Order a complete blood count (CBC) for follow-up. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
