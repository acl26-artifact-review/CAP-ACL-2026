Chief Complaint
Left knee pain and weakness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of a knee injury sustained three weeks ago during a sporting event. He describes a 'dime-size' abrasion and reports tenderness to palpation. He denies any significant swelling or instability. The patient reports feeling weak, particularly with weight-bearing activities. He also reports clicking and full extension of the joint. He denies crepitus. He reports walking the dog and reports tenderness. He denies any prior knee injuries.

Findings
Examination reveals mild laxity of the left knee with gentle valgus stress. Palpation elicits tenderness along the medial collateral ligament. Range of motion is limited to 0-130 degrees. No effusion is palpable. The patient demonstrates mild weakness with quadriceps testing.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a medial collateral ligament sprain with secondary weakness.

Plan
Order knee radiographs to evaluate for fracture. Initiate physical therapy for strengthening and range of motion. Recommend ice and elevation. Follow up in 4-6 weeks to assess progress.
