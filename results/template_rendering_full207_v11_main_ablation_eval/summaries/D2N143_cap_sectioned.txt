Chief Complaint
Concern about potential STD exposure and symptoms of vaginal discomfort.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being concerned about potential STD exposure due to her boyfriend's infidelity, which she discovered approximately three months ago. She describes experiencing vaginal discomfort, including a change in vaginal discharge and odor, which began last week. She also notes pain on the right side of her vagina. She denies fever or chills. She previously had an irregular pap smear approximately 20 years ago, but no subsequent screenings. Her last pap smear was normal approximately six months ago. She reports having been with her boyfriend for three years.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no external lesions. The vaginal exam demonstrates normal labia and a physiological discharge. The cervix is normal, with no cervical motion tenderness or adnexal tenderness.

Assessment
The patient presents with symptoms suggestive of a possible STD infection, including vaginal discharge, odor, and pain. She is considered high risk due to her recent discovery of infidelity and multiple sexual partners.

Plan
The patient will be tested for gonorrhea, chlamydia, trichomoniasis, syphilis, HIV, and hepatitis C. Follow-up will be scheduled in three days to discuss test results. The patient is advised to continue with an annual checkup.
