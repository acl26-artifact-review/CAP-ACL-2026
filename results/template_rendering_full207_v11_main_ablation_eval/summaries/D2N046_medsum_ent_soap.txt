S:
The patient reports feeling depressed and is experiencing severe pain and bleeding. She denies vomiting but reports constipation and weight loss. She states she has no children and was not pregnant. She reports no oral hormone replacement therapy and denies a history of endometriosis. Her period started at age 13 and menopause occurred at age 28. She does not take any medications.

O:
Physical exam reveals a palpable left adnexal mass. Abdominal exam reveals slight tenderness to palpation in the left lower quadrant. The vaginal exam shows no external lesions, a normal cervix, and a within-normal-limits vaginal vault. CT scan shows a three centimeter left ovarian mass with associated localized lymph node involvement. There is no evidence of gross peritoneal or metastatic disease.

A:
Based on the CT scan findings, a stage three A ovarian cancer is suspected. I recommend genetic counseling and testing to assess for a genetic predisposition.

P:
I order a CA-125, HCG, AFP, and LDH blood test. I recommend genetic counseling and testing. I recommend a hysterectomy, bilateral oophorectomy, and lymph node dissection. I recommend adjuvant chemotherapy with cisplatin and taxol. I will consider intraperitoneal chemotherapy if appropriate based on surgical findings. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four to six weeks to review pathology results and discuss further treatment options. I counsel the patient to report any new symptoms immediately.
