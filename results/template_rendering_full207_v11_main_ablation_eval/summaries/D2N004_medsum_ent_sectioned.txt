Chief Complaint
James presents today with back pain following a golf injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain following a golf injury, specifically a pop and immediate ground contact during his backswing on Saturday morning. He initially took tylenol and ibuprofen for the pain, but found little relief. He reports feeling a dropped foot in his right foot and some struggling with his right leg, which improved slightly this morning. He has a 10-year history of lower back surgery. He reports avoiding salty foods and a weight gain of 5 pounds over the summer. He denies problems laying flat. He monitors his blood sugars at home and is currently taking metformin. He reports fatigue for the past two months.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a 2 out of 6 systolic ejection murmur, and decreased range of motion with flexion and extension of the lumbar spine. Straight leg raise elicited a positive response on the right side. Strength testing revealed a 4 out of 5 on the right and a 5 out of 5 on the left. The x-ray demonstrates normal lumbar spine alignment. Diabetic lab results show a hemoglobin A1c of 8%.

Assessment
The patient has an acute lumbar strain, likely secondary to the recent injury. He also continues to manage his type 2 diabetes and congestive heart failure.

Plan
I have prescribed meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily and have referred the patient to physical therapy. I have ordered an MRI to rule out disc herniation. I have increased the metformin dosage to 1000 milligrams twice daily and ordered a hemoglobin A1c in approximately two months. I have ordered a refill of lisinopril 20 milligrams once daily. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately two weeks.
