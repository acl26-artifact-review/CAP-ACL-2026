Chief Complaint
Yearly routine exam.

History of Present Illness
Christine is a 63-year-old female patient here for her routine yearly exam. She has a history of high blood pressure and depression, and she had a carpal tunnel release done approximately two years ago. She reports that the depression is doing well with medication and therapy, and that her carpal tunnel release has been doing well. She's been sleeping well at night. She has not had a normal blood pressure reading in the last couple of weeks, fluctuating between high and low. She reports that her diet has been all over the place due to increased work hours, leading to reliance on frozen meals. She denies any thoughts of self-harm. She reports headaches, attributing them to computer screen use.

Findings
Physical exam reveals clear lungs, normal heart sounds without murmurs, equal grips, intact neurological function, and good pulses. Her blood pressure is elevated in the office today. Lab results are all normal. No lymphadenopathy noted.

Assessment
The patient has hypertension and requires further management. Her depression is currently well-controlled. The carpal tunnel release appears to be resolving well.

Plan
Increase lisinopril to 10 milligrams daily, starting today. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to reassess blood pressure control. Continue current depression medication and therapy. Advise the patient to monitor for any recurrence of carpal tunnel symptoms and to report them promptly.
