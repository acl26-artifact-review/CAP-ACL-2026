Chief Complaint
Left knee pain following a fall while assisting with her niece's bicycle training.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that she injured her left knee while helping her five-year-old niece learn to ride a bicycle with training wheels. She fell and believes she twisted her knee in an unusual way. She has experienced left knee pain for approximately one week, which has progressively worsened. She rates the pain as initially four out of ten, but now estimates it as a throbbing pain, particularly on the medial aspect of the knee. She has been limping and reports that the pain has not improved. She has been taking Tylenol and kinesiology tape, but finds the ibuprofen unavailable due to a recent ulcer. The Tylenol provides some relief, but the effect is limited and wears off after approximately two hours.

Findings
Examination revealed moderate edema and effusion in the left knee. Range of motion is limited, but not severely restricted. Pain was elicited with flexion and extension. Negative Lachman's, valgus, and varus tests were performed. A chest x-ray was unremarkable, demonstrating no fractures or bony abnormalities. There was no evidence of a torn meniscus or ligamentous injury on physical examination.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a medial collateral ligament strain or MCL injury.

Plan
I am prescribing tramadol 50 mg twice daily as needed for breakthrough pain, along with Tylenol as needed. A knee immobilizer will be provided for one week to restrict movement and reduce inflammation. I recommend ice application three to four times daily for 20-30 minutes. Follow-up in approximately one month is scheduled to assess healing and function. The patient should avoid strenuous activity, including running, for approximately three weeks. If symptoms persist or worsen, a musculoskeletal MRI should be considered.
