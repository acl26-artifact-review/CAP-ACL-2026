Chief Complaint
Annual exam and management of hypertension and depression.

History of Present Illness
Ralph is a 62-year-old male with a past medical history significant for depression and prior lobectomy as well as hypertension, who presents for his annual exam. He reports experiencing symptoms related to pollen, including nasal congestion. He also reports some chest pain when running, which usually resolves. He occasionally forgets to take his medications, particularly on weekends, and reports difficulty managing hypertension. He's been staying on top of his medications with the prozac, and hasn't had any incidents in a while. He also reports feeling a bit chaotic with his daughter moving into college.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 120/80 mmHg. A slight two out of six systolic ejection murmur is auscultated. Lung sounds are clear. No lower extremity edema is present. The patient's EKG is completely normal. A chest x-ray shows no acute abnormalities and is consistent with his prior lobectomy.

Assessment
The patient's primary problems include hypertension and depression. He is currently managing his hypertension effectively.

Plan
The doctor orders a refill of prozac 20 milligrams daily. The doctor orders an echocardiogram to evaluate the murmur. The doctor orders routine blood work. The doctor will follow up with the patient through the patient portal.
