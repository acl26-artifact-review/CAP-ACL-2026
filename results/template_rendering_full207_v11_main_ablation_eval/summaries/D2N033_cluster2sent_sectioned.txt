Chief Complaint
The patient reports a painful right knee injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a recent injury to his right knee, characterized by a popping sensation and subsequent pain. He attempted self-treatment with ibuprofen and ice. He admits to struggling with avoiding sugary foods and regularly consuming ice cream.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited range of motion in the right knee, with pain on both flexion and extension. Palpation of the inner knee reveals tenderness. The McMurray's test is negative. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are normal with no murmurs or gallops. The knee exhibits significant swelling.

Assessment
The patient likely has a torn or injured medial collateral ligament (MCL) in the right knee, based on the reported mechanism of injury and physical exam findings.

Plan
A straight leg brace will be applied, and Mobic will be prescribed for pain relief and to reduce swelling. An MRI will be ordered to evaluate the extent of the injury. The patient should ice the knee for 15 minutes once an hour.
