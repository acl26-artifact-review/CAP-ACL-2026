Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a basketball injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a basketball injury where he fell to the ground. He states he landed on his side and the inside of his knee hurt. He received ice and an ace wrap and reports decreased range of motion with movement. He denies a popping sensation. He is currently managing the pain with over-the-counter medications and exercises. He is concerned about returning to basketball.

Findings
Examination reveals right knee effusion and medial joint tenderness. Negative varus and valgus stress test. The x-ray shows no fractures but demonstrates a medial collateral ligament strain and effusion. The patient's blood sugars are being monitored daily.

Assessment
Right knee medial collateral ligament strain.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. Order a right knee brace. Refer to physical therapy. Schedule a follow-up appointment in seven days to reassess and consider further imaging if needed. Advise patient to continue monitoring blood sugars and to avoid basketball activity until cleared by physical therapy.
