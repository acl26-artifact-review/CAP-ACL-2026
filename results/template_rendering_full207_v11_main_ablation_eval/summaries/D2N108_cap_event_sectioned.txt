Chief Complaint
Nonhealing foot ulcer

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a nonhealing foot ulcer that has been present for approximately two months. He describes it as becoming red and draining, prompting concern. He attributes the ulcer to walking barefoot on the beach. He previously received antibiotics from his PCP but has not yet filled the prescription. He recalls taking a similar medication previously. He states that the beach he visited was Dewey Beach. He describes the pain as throbbing and reports increased pressure but not distinct pain. The patient's diabetes is not well controlled, as he does not regularly check his blood sugar. His last A1C reading was approximately ten.

Findings
A one by two inch circular wound is present on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot, proximal to the fifth metatarsal head. The wound is red and has drainage. There is some edema around the wound. No necrosis or odor is present. Bone is not exposed. A right foot x-ray revealed no evidence of osteomyelitis.

Assessment
The patient has a diabetic foot ulcer with signs of infection.

Plan
Order an ABI to assess blood supply to the foot. Perform debridement of the ulcer. Prescribe clindamycin 400mg twice daily for seven days. Obtain a surgical shoe for the patient to wear while the ulcer heals. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess healing progress.
