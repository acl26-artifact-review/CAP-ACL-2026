Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing injury two weeks ago.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right knee pain following a skiing injury two weeks ago, sustained during a fall while skiing down a black diamond run. He describes attempting to speed past another skier and subsequently colliding with a tree. He reports a pop sensation at the time of injury. The patient reports that he has been wearing a brace and using THC cream for pain relief. He denies any other recent injuries. He reports occasional pain to the degree of eleven on a scale, particularly when walking his dog. He has not resumed skiing since the injury. He reports that his pain tolerance is high.

Findings
Examination reveals mild swelling and bruising of the right knee. Gait reveals a slight limp. Range of motion is generally preserved, although with some stiffness in the morning. Dorsiflexion causes pain, while plantar flexion does not. Pain is elicited with knee extension.

Assessment
The patient has a strain of the posterior cruciate ligament in the right knee, likely secondary to the prior injury sustained during skiing. The mild arthritis observed on examination may contribute to the reported pain.

Plan
I recommend continuing bracing for the right knee to provide support and reduce stress. I recommend physical therapy three times per week to improve strength and range of motion. I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain and inflammation, and ibuprofen 800 mg twice daily as needed for the left knee. I recommend avoiding skiing and other strenuous activities for now. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
