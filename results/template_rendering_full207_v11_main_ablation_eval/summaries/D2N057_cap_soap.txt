The patient reports pain in his big toe, describing it as throbbing with occasional sharp, stabbing pain, especially when moving it. He mentions that the pain is exacerbated by pressure on the toe, particularly at night when the sheets are on it. The patient also reports feeling a bone spur and experiencing pain on the left side of his big toe. He reports taking ibuprofen twice a day but finds it doesn't help. He also reports heartburn when taking the medication.

O:
The examination reveals redness and warmth in the patient's big toe. Range of motion reveals limited plantar flexion and increased pain with further movement. Palpation elicits tenderness on the right side of the big toe joint and between the big toe and second toe. The patient demonstrates a limited range of motion with the big toe, exhibiting a degree of plantar flexion of approximately ten degrees and a degree of pain with twenty degrees of movement. The x-ray shows a large spur on the top of the big toe joint and cartilage loss.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with hallux rigidus with secondary osteoarthritis. The patient has foot pain and joint pain.

P:
The patient will receive an orthotic insert for his shoe. The patient will be prescribed meloxicam to take once daily. The patient should follow up in two weeks to assess treatment efficacy. If symptoms do not improve, a shot or surgery may be considered. The patient will have an x-ray.
