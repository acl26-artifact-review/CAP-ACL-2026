Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right knee pain for the past three months, worsening with activity and stairs. He denies a specific injury. He reports using ibuprofen with limited relief. He states the pain is most noticeable when walking uphill or prolonged standing.

Findings
The patient demonstrates mild right knee effusion. Range of motion is limited by pain. Lachman's test is negative. McMurray's test is negative. Palpation reveals tenderness over the medial joint line.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with medial meniscus injury.

Plan
Order MRI of the right knee to evaluate for meniscal tear. Recommend physical therapy to improve range of motion and strength. Advise patient to avoid high-impact activities until further evaluation.
