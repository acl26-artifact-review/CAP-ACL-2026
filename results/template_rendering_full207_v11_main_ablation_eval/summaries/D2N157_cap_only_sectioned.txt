Chief Complaint
Left hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent left hand pain, which began approximately two weeks ago following a minor fall. He describes the pain as sharp and intermittent, and rates it as 6/10. He denies any numbness or tingling. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain, with some relief. The patient also reports feeling down and having difficulty adjusting after a recent heart attack.

Findings
The patient's left hand exhibits mild tenderness to palpation over the metacarpal bones. Range of motion is limited by pain. Grip strength is reduced. The patient reports dizziness.

Assessment
The patient has chronic pain in the left hand, likely secondary to the recent fall. He also reports feeling down and experiencing symptoms consistent with depression.

Plan
Order a musculoskeletal ultrasound of the left hand to evaluate for possible carpal tunnel syndrome. Refer the patient to psychiatry for evaluation and management of depressive symptoms. Continue ibuprofen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress.
