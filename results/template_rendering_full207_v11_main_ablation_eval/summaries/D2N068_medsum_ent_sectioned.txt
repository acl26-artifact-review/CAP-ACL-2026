Chief Complaint
Feeling out of sorts lately, lack of energy, and lightheadedness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling out of sorts lately, describing a lack of energy and lightheadedness, which began approximately five weeks ago around Labor Day. He attributes this to a combination of seasonal changes and ongoing construction projects at his home, including kitchen renovations. He admits to struggling with consistent healthy eating due to the construction, frequently relying on takeout food. He denies fever or chills. He reports occasional slight cramps that resolve within an hour. He is taking lisinopril 20mg daily and monitors his blood pressure regularly with his wife's assistance. He has been doing relatively well with his heart failure management over the past year, but acknowledges a period of chaos. He denies nausea or vomiting.

Findings
Physical exam reveals jugular venous distension to 8 cm. A three out of six systolic ejection murmur is auscultated. Fine crackles are present in the bases bilaterally. Lower extremities demonstrate 1+ pitting edema. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 132/82 mmHg, heart rate 78 bpm, oxygen saturation 98% on room air. Chest x-ray shows some pulmonary edema. Echocardiogram reveals a reduced ejection fraction of 45% and mitral regurgitation.

Assessment
The patient presents with congestive heart failure, likely exacerbated by recent lifestyle changes and compounded by mitral regurgitation. He also exhibits symptoms consistent with fatigue and lightheadedness, potentially related to the underlying heart failure and volume depletion.

Plan
Increase lasix dosage to 80 mg daily. Continue monitoring weight and reporting any significant changes. Order a repeat echocardiogram to assess cardiac function. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and address any ongoing concerns. Advise patient to continue monitoring blood pressure at home and reporting any elevations.
