Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began with increased tennis activity, describing it as sharp and aching, rated 6/10. He reports that over-the-counter analgesics provide limited relief. He also mentions a history of asthma and diabetes type 2, and reports that his asthma is generally well-controlled.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate lateral epicondylitis tenderness of the right elbow, with mild pain on resisted wrist extension. The patient's cardiovascular exam shows a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is unchanged from prior examination. X-ray of the right elbow demonstrates no acute fracture or bony abnormality. Blood pressure is 135/85 mmHg, heart rate 78 bpm.

Assessment
Acute lateral epicondylitis of the right elbow is the primary diagnosis. The patient's diabetes type 2 is being monitored, and his asthma remains under control.

Plan
Prescribe ultram 50mg once daily for pain relief. Instruct the patient to rest his right arm, avoid repetitive stress, and apply ice three times daily. Provide a forearm brace. Increase metformin to 1000mg twice daily. Order a hemoglobin A1c and CBC blood test. Prescribe a refill of albuterol inhaler. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to reevaluate the elbow and adjust treatment as needed.
