Chief Complaint
Severe acid reflux and heartburn.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a several-year history of worsening acid reflux, with symptoms occurring on a variable basis. He currently takes 40 milligrams of omeprazole twice daily, but reports that it is not consistently effective, experiencing heartburn despite medication. He identifies several trigger foods, including spicy foods, carbonated beverages, citrus products, and tomato-based products. He reports decreased appetite over the past two months and attributes this to work stress. He notes that his grandmother had esophageal cancer and his mother had acid reflux and took medication to coat the lining of her stomach. He denies difficulty swallowing and reports no abdominal pain or nausea.

Findings
Physical examination is unremarkable except for well-healed abdominal surgical scars from prior surgery at an unknown age. Vital signs are within normal limits. The patient denies dysphagia.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD).

Plan
I will prescribe nexium 40 milligrams twice daily to replace the omeprazole. I recommend an upper endoscopy to evaluate for potential causes of the GERD. We will schedule this for you. Please follow up with me in the office for the results and to discuss further management. I will also recommend a pH-impedance study to help determine the etiology of the GERD.
