Chief Complaint
Reports knee pain following moving boxes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports knee pain that began over the weekend following repetitive movements, such as moving boxes down basement stairs. He describes equal pain in both knees and denies any injury. He has previously experienced knee problems, attributing them to the repetition and weight of the boxes. He has taken Tylenol and icing with limited relief. He denies fever, chills, or nausea. He reports no other systemic symptoms. He is currently taking Synthroid for hypothyroidism and immunosuppressants for his kidney transplant, both of which are being managed as expected.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild edema and erythema in the right knee, with decreased range of motion upon palpation. The left knee appears normal. A two out of six systolic ejection murmur is auscultated, which is stable. White blood cell count is within normal limits. Kidney function is preserved. Right knee X-ray demonstrates good bony alignment and no acute fracture.

Assessment
The patient's knee pain is most likely an acute exacerbation of his underlying arthritis, complicated by repetitive strain. His hypothyroidism and kidney transplant are currently well-managed.

Plan
I have prescribed Ultram 50 milligrams every six hours as needed for pain relief. I have ordered an autoimmune panel to evaluate for potential inflammatory processes. I recommend the patient take it easy and will discuss referral to physical therapy if symptoms persist. I have ordered thyroid labs to monitor his Synthroid levels. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
