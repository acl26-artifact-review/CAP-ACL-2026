Chief Complaint
Right index finger pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right index finger pain following a skiing injury over the weekend. He sustained a strap caught on his finger while jumping moguls, resulting in a bend in the finger. He initially tried ice and ibuprofen, but the pain persists. He describes the pain as worsening with movement and reports tenderness at the distal phalanx and the joint itself. He has no prior surgeries and reports constipation treated with miralax.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness over the distal phalanx and the joint of the right index finger. Range of motion is limited, with pain on flexion. X-rays demonstrate no fractures and normal alignment of the finger joints.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with a right hand index finger contusion, likely involving the distal phalanx and joint.

Plan
Prescribe an aluminum foam splint and recommend 600 mg ibuprofen every six hours for one week. Advise the patient to call if symptoms do not improve within one week. Finalize report.
