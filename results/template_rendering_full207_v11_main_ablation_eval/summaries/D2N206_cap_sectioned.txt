Chief Complaint
Reports pain in the right flank radiating to the groin for the past two weeks.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the right flank radiating to the groin for the past two weeks, which starts in the right flank and radiates down to his groin. He describes it as under control but with bouts where it gets a little out of control, depending on how much he's been drinking and the activities he's been doing. He reports no pain when urinating and denies nausea or vomiting. He felt like he might have had chills in the past, but denies fever. He has a history of kidney stones and previously passed some on his own, and he's had surgery twice. He's been taking ibuprofen, which provides some relief. His family has a history of kidney stones.

Findings
Physical exam reveals elevated blood pressure, tenderness to palpation in the right flank, and cVA tenderness. CT scan confirms a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter, without evidence of high-flow nephrosis. The patient denies any other significant findings.

Assessment
The patient has a history of kidney stones with a current 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter.

Plan
Continue the patient on ibuprofen as needed for breakthrough pain. Prescribe tramadol 50 mg if pain persists. Instruct the patient to continue using a strainer when urinating to catch the stone. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks to assess symptoms and discuss potential lithotripsy if needed. Order a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture.
