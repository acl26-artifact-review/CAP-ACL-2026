S:
The patient reports a history of a skiing injury two weeks ago, resulting in pain and instability in his left knee. He describes persistent pain, stiffness, and swelling, particularly in the morning. He also reports radiating pain down his ankle. The patient reports using a brace and THC cream for pain relief and taking THC gummies. He has had previous knee surgery related to a back injury and a pop sensation.

O:
Physical examination reveals mild swelling and bruising in both knees. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Dorsiflexion causes pain in the right knee, but not plantar flexion. X-rays demonstrate the development of arthritis and no fracture.

A:
The patient has a probable posterior cruciate ligament strain in the right knee and mild pain in the left knee. The patient's history of prior knee surgery and the reported pop sensation suggest a possible history of instability.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for right knee pain and ibuprofen 800 mg twice daily as needed for left knee pain. Recommend physical therapy three times per week to improve strength and stability in the right knee. Advise against skiing or gymnastics for now. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to assess progress.
