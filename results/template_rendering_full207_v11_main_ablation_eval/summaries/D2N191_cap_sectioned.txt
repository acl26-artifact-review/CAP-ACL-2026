Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain and chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports waking up with right elbow pain yesterday and describes it as aching and heavy, extending up to the neck. He denies any recent trauma or injury. He reports occasional chest pain, which he describes as coming and going, often associated with the elbow pain. He states he has been mowing the grass recently and feels it contributed to the elbow pain. He reports difficulty stopping pooping and anxiety. He has been taking motrin for the pain, but it doesn't fully relieve it. He denies shortness of breath. He is currently taking norvasc 2.5mg and metformin 500mg.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a slight murmur with cardiac auscultation. The patient reports chest pain and reports pain radiating to the neck. The patient reports elbow pain and arm pain.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is concerning for possible cardiac chest pain, potentially exacerbated by musculoskeletal pain in the left arm. He has a history of hypertension and diabetes mellitus.

Plan
Order an EKG and aspirin. Administer nitroglycerin as needed. Order shoulder radiographs. Continue metformin 500mg and norvasc 2.5mg. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist. If the EKG reveals concerning findings, we will consult with cardiology and consider transfer to the hospital for further evaluation.
