Chief Complaint
Right foot pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of prior ankle injuries twenty years ago, including a cast for an ankle fracture, and occasional soreness in both ankles. He denies recent trauma but describes persistent right foot soreness and swelling following a fall. He has not taken any medications for the pain. He reports numbness in the entire right foot since the incident.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bruising on the right foot, tenderness to palpation, and swelling. The right dorsalis pedis pulse is strong and bounding. Capillary refill is brisk bilaterally. An x-ray demonstrates dorsal displacement of the base of the second metatarsal with a three millimeter separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and presence of bony fragments. The patient's vitals are stable.

Assessment
The patient's right foot pain is due to a Lisfranc fracture with associated ligament injuries.

Plan
An MRI has been ordered to assess ligament injuries. An outpatient mri will be performed this afternoon. Surgical intervention is likely indicated to align the bones and ligaments with plates and screws to promote healing. The patient will undergo same-day outpatient surgery tomorrow, followed by a 24-hour post-procedure follow-up and a two-week follow-up. The patient will receive a cast and crutches for six to eight weeks, with ambulation advanced gradually based on healing and tolerance. Follow-up will be scheduled to monitor progress and adjust the treatment plan.
