Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports unbearable pain in the shoulder, exacerbated by overhead reaching and with pressure. He reports difficulty reaching for objects and left shoulder pain. The patient reports left shoulder pain. He denies numbness. The patient is currently taking two extra strength tylenol every six to eight hours and recommends the patient continue taking Tylenol.

Findings
Examination reveals reduced active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most likely due to a rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
The doctor is referring the patient to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. A steroid injection for the shoulder may be considered if symptoms do not improve. A cervical MRI is ordered for the left shoulder. The doctor schedules a follow-up visit with the patient after they complete physical therapy.
