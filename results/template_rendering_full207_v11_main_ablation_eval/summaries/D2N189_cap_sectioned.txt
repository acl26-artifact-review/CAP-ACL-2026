Chief Complaint
Reports shortness of breath and difficulty with exertion.

History of Present Illness
Betty reports experiencing chest pain following a heart attack six weeks ago, during which a stent was placed in her right coronary artery. She was hospitalized for 48 hours. Since returning home, she's been advised to take it easy and has been doing light work. She reports some soreness at the surgical site but it's improving. She continues to experience shortness of breath when walking or climbing stairs. She is currently taking brilinta twice daily and aspirin nightly. She reports taking baby aspirin as well. She has been eating a healthy diet and is taking her medications as prescribed. She is a big fan of Denver Nuggets basketball and enjoys watching them play, but has not been able to swim since her surgery.

Findings
Blood pressure is 127/70 mmHg. Jugular venous pulse is not palpable. Carotid bruits are absent. Heart sounds reveal a systolic ejection murmur of 3/6 at the left base. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. Radial pulse is palpable and intact. Wrist exam reveals no signs of infection and the surgical site is clean and dry. The patient reports no chest pain since the stent placement.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is an inferior myocardial infarction with a stent in place. She also has mild mitral regurgitation as evidenced by the echocardiogram.

Plan
Continue the patient on brilinta twice daily and aspirin nightly. Schedule a follow-up echocardiogram in six months. Recommend enrollment in cardiac rehabilitation. Advise the patient to resume swimming after a few laps, monitoring for any symptoms. Order liver function studies and a lipid panel for follow-up. Schedule a follow-up appointment in six weeks.
