Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath during tennis match.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing sudden shortness of breath while playing tennis approximately six hours after the match. He describes the breathlessness as severe, with associated lightheadedness and dizziness. He attributes this episode to the hot and humid conditions during the match. Prior to this, he has experienced similar episodes after strenuous activity, but this was more sudden and severe. He has been diligent with his heart failure medications and has been watching his diet, avoiding salty foods. He had a skin cancer removed five months ago and reports no issues with that. He also had a cataract surgery approximately three months ago, which has been fine. He denies any recent trauma or chest pain.

Findings
Blood pressure is elevated. The patient's lungs exhibit fine crackles bilaterally. There is trace lower extremity edema. The ECG shows a stable rhythm with left ventricular hypertrophy. The echocardiogram reveals a stable ejection fraction.

Assessment
The patient's acute shortness of breath is most likely an exacerbation of his congestive heart failure, likely triggered by dietary indiscretion and exacerbated by the hot and humid conditions. The elevated blood pressure and lower extremity edema support this diagnosis.

Plan
Prescribe an additional dose of lasix 80 mg orally once daily. Order carvedilol 25 mg twice daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess response to treatment. Advise the patient to continue monitoring his weight and fluid intake.
