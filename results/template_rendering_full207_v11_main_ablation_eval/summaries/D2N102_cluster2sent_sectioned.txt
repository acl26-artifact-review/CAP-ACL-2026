Chief Complaint
Hematuria and recurrent episodes of bright red urine.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports passing two small stones approximately one week prior to this appointment, followed by several episodes of bright red urine, resembling cherry kool-aid, over the past six weeks. These episodes are typically associated with zero flank pain and have improved since passing the stones. The patient is currently taking Alavert 10 milligrams daily and reports no drug allergies. He desires strategies to prevent recurrence of these stone episodes. He states he will avoid food rich in sodium oxalate and animal protein.

Findings
Renal ultrasound revealed a six millimeter left renal stone, non-obstructing. Urine dipstick showed trace amounts of blood, with a specific gravity of 1.020 and urobilinogen 0.2. The urine is yellow and clear, with a pH of 5.5. An anti-Gu test was normal. A negative vaginal exam was performed.

Assessment
Primary diagnosis is hematuria. Secondary diagnosis is chronic urolithiasis.

Plan
Recommend dietary modifications to reduce sodium oxalate and animal protein intake to minimize stone recurrence. Schedule follow-up in one year, unless the patient experiences further episodes of bleeding or pain, in which case they should contact the office sooner. Advise the patient to seek emergency room care for severe pain or significant hematuria.
