Chief Complaint
Patient reports a non-healing wound on their right foot.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports the wound began approximately two to three weeks ago and was initially caused by Hoka shoes. The patient reports discomfort with the shoes. The patient's previous HbA1c was over 8.0.

Findings
Examination reveals a one by two inch circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot with yellow slough and minimal granulation tissue. The doctor observes no surrounding erythema or cellulitis, nor evidence of fluid collection or necrosis, bony exposure, or odor. Palpable bilateral femoral and popliteal pulses were noted, with Doppler signs present. An x-ray showed no evidence of osteomyelitis.

Assessment
The patient has a chronic, non-healing right foot wound.

Plan
The doctor plans to perform a debridement in the office and prescribes a collagenase ointment. A surgical shoe will be worn. An ankle brachial index (ABI) will be ordered to assess blood supply. The doctor plans to see the patient again in two weeks. The doctor may refer the patient to a vascular specialist if the ABI indicates suboptimal blood supply and plans to consult with a diabetes specialist to modify the patient's diet.
