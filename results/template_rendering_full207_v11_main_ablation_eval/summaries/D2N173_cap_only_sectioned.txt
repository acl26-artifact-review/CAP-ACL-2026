Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient presents with progressive left shoulder pain over six weeks, worse with overhead activity and when lying on the affected side. He denies recent trauma. Acetaminophen provides partial relief. The patient traveled over the last week or so. The patient reports a cold.

Findings
Examination reveals reduced active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. The patient has cervical lymphadenopathy. The patient has bilateral ronchi. The patient has wheezing. The patient reports chest pain.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs and a stress test in six months. The doctor orders a lipid panel in six months. The patient should continue taking crestor 20 milligrams a day. The doctor advises the patient to quarantine for 10 days.
