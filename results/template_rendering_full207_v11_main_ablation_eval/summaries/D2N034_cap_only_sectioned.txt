Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain, describing it as constant. He states the pain began approximately three weeks ago and has been worsening. The patient reports the pain is exacerbated by overhead activity and when lying on the affected side. He denies any recent trauma. He reports that acetaminophen provides partial relief. The patient has been doing work in the basement recently.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness of the greater tuberosity of the humerus. The patient demonstrates limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder. There is no tenderness at the sternoclavicular or acromioclavicular joints. The patient reports left hand pain.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy and left hand pain.

Plan
The doctor orders an MRI of the left shoulder. The doctor is referring the patient to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. The doctor may consider a steroid injection of the shoulder if the patient's symptoms do not improve. The patient has not taken anything for the pain recently.
