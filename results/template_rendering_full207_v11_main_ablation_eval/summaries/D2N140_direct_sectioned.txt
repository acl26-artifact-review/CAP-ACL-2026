Chief Complaint
Irritable bowel syndrome with functional abdominal pain and nausea.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing recurrent episodes of abdominal pain, nausea, and vomiting, often triggered by spicy foods and alcohol consumption, particularly when dining out. She reports that these episodes have been increasing in frequency and severity over the past few months. She has previously been diagnosed with irritable bowel syndrome and treated with bentyl. She has also used reglan as needed for nausea. She reports that she has been using citrucel daily and probiotics to help with gas and bloating. She has been avoiding heavy foods to help keep her regular.

Findings
The patient is alert and cooperative. Physical examination reveals soft, non-tender abdomen with normal active bowel sounds. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
Irritable bowel syndrome.

Plan
Start elavil 25 milligrams at bedtime for management of IBS symptoms. Continue bentyl 20 milligrams as needed, up to four times daily. Encourage dietary modifications, including limiting spicy foods, fatty foods, and alcohol. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one month via mychart to assess response to treatment and address any concerns. Dispense prescriptions for elavil and bentyl.
