Chief Complaint
Recurrent chest pain, experienced three times per week.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing recurrent chest pain, described as a burning sensation, approximately three times per week, primarily when walking. He denies any associated symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or shortness of breath. He reports that the pain is located centrally in the chest and has been present for approximately one month. He has a history of coronary artery disease and prior stenting, and reports no lower extremity edema or muscle aches. He also reports some nasal congestion due to allergies. He denies blood in stool. Hemoglobin is 8.2. The nurse reviewed a systems check with him regarding these symptoms.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a systolic ejection murmur of 3/6 in the left ventricle. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. No carotid bruits are appreciated. The patient has healed surgical scars on the right wrist. The echocardiogram from last year showed a slightly low pumping function of 45% and mitral regurgitation. The ECG is stable and reflects prior coronary artery disease. Vital signs are stable, including blood pressure under good control and normal oxygenation.

Assessment
The patient's recurrent chest pain, coupled with his history of coronary artery disease and the echocardiogram findings, suggests ongoing cardiac ischemia. The murmur and stable ECG do not preclude the possibility of intermittent ischemia.

Plan
I have ordered a cardiac stress test to further evaluate the extent of coronary artery disease. I am continuing the patient on aspirin and toprol. I am also ordering a lipid panel and initiating Crestor 20 mg daily. A Lasix prescription of 40 mg daily has been ordered. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess the results of the stress test and adjust the management plan as needed. The nurse will assist with scheduling these tests and ensuring appropriate follow-up.
