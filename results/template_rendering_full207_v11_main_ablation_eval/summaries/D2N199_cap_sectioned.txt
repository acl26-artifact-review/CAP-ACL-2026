Chief Complaint
Cough and feeling unwell.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a cough that started approximately one week ago, accompanied by yellow sputum production. He describes feeling progressively worse over the last two days, with some aches and occasional sinus pressure. He denies fever or chills. He reports a right knee injury sustained when he fell down four or five stairs this morning, resulting in pain and decreased range of motion. He has no shortness of breath. He is a teacher and reports that he has been taking over-the-counter Dayquil for his cough and cold symptoms. He does not check his blood pressure or take his blood sugars.

Findings
Physical examination reveals coarse rhonchi bilaterally, erythema and edema of the nasal turbinates bilaterally with associated yellow discharge, bilateral cervical lymphadenopathy, and pain to palpation of the right knee with decreased flexion and extension and ecchymosis. Vital signs are stable: temperature 98.4°F, blood pressure 120/62 mmHg, heart rate 80 bpm, and oxygen saturation 97% on room air. The patient reports a slight limp.

Assessment
The patient presents with a likely viral syndrome with mild bronchitis, accompanied by a right medial collateral ligament strain and hypertension. He also has diabetes mellitus.

Plan
I recommend guaifenesin 600 mg twice daily for symptomatic relief. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for the knee pain. Order a right knee X-ray to evaluate the injury. Refer to physical therapy for rehabilitation. Schedule a lipid panel and hemoglobin A1C to monitor cardiovascular risk. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to treatment. Advise the patient to monitor his blood sugars and report any changes to me through the patient portal.
