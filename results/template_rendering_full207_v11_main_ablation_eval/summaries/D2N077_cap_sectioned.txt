Chief Complaint
Patient reports a left wrist injury sustained during laundry activities.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he was doing laundry and slipped while trying to catch himself, resulting in immediate wrist pain and swelling. He describes the pain as severe, particularly with movement. He has taken ibuprofen, which provides some relief, but the pain persists. He denies prior wrist injuries. He reports a cold and tingling in his wrist. The pain is exacerbated when the arm is raised overhead. He reports that the pain is better when the arm is held at a neutral level.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the right wrist, with crepitance upon palpation. The wrist exhibits significant swelling and ecchymosis. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Radial deviation is painful. Lateral deviation is painful. Wrist abduction and adduction are painful. The patient reports difficulty making a fist. Vital signs reveal a slightly elevated blood pressure and a heart rate of 80 beats per minute. The patient's temperature is 99.7°F, and respiratory rate is 20 breaths per minute. A right wrist X-ray confirms a Colles' fracture.

Assessment
The patient presents with a Colles' fracture of the right wrist. The elevated blood pressure and heart rate are likely secondary to pain.

Plan
The patient will be placed in a thumb spica brace for six weeks. The doctor will prescribe ultram at 50 milligrams every six hours for pain management. Physical therapy will be ordered in a few weeks after the surgery. The patient will follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. A follow-up appointment will be scheduled to assess the fracture healing and adjust the treatment plan as needed.
