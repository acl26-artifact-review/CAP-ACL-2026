S:
The patient reports experiencing left shoulder pain for several weeks, exacerbated by reaching and overhead movements. He denies any history of trauma. He describes a pain level of 7 without medication and 2-3 with Tylenol. He reports numbness and tingling in his arm and hand. He has been taking Tylenol every 6-8 hours.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder, tenderness to palpation of the greater tuberosity, and brisk cap refill. X-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy. A rotator cuff injury is suspected.

P:
I am ordering an MRI of the left shoulder to evaluate for rotator cuff pathology. I am also referring the patient for physical therapy for approximately 6-8 weeks. Continue taking Tylenol PRN as needed. We will consider a steroid injection if symptoms do not improve.
