Chief Complaint
Right knee pain for approximately six months.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain for approximately six months, initially without a specific injury. He describes the pain as a deep, achy sensation, particularly exacerbated by sitting and standing for extended periods, as well as stair climbing. He reports some relief with ibuprofen and Aleve, but not complete pain relief. He denies any recent trauma. He is a runner who has significantly reduced his mileage due to the pain. He also enjoys cycling and reports that this activity is less painful. He works from home, with a desk setup that is accessible via stairs.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal gait. Lower extremity strength is rated as 3/5 for abduction bilaterally. Patellar grind test is positive upon standing. Lachman's, anterior and posterior drawer, and McMurray tests are negative bilaterally. Neurological exam is intact. Radiographs show no fractures or osteoarthritis. MRI prior to presentation revealed no acute findings.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome.

Plan
Recommend physical therapy to address lower extremity strength and running mechanics. Continue ibuprofen or Aleve as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
