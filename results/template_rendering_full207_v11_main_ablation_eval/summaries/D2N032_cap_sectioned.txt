Chief Complaint
Right hip pain.

History of Present Illness
Ms. Reyes reports right hip pain, primarily in the groin area, for approximately two years. The pain began after walking around the infusion room and has progressively worsened. She describes the pain as exacerbated by movement, particularly pivoting, and rates it 2-7 out of 10. She reports occasional falls related to the pain. She has had previous surgeries including a pcl and a gall bladder removal. She has also experienced infertility treatment and has had several bulging discs. She denies fever or chills, tingling, or bowel/bladder changes. She reports that staying still provides relief but she doesn't want to stay still. She has lost 110 pounds through weight watchers, starting in 2018.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a height of 5'6", weight of 169 pounds, and blood pressure of 115/75. Pulse rate is 67. No ankle edema is noted. Range of motion is decreased in exertion with lateral flexion. The sacroiliac signs are negative. The trochanteric region is non-tender. Range of motion is decreased in right hip with pain in the groin and internal and external rotation. A slight discomfort is noted with knee lift. The patient reports a catching sensation when walking on her heels and toes. Deep tendon reflexes are normal throughout.

Assessment
Right hip degenerative joint disease is the most likely diagnosis. The patient's reported pain and limited range of motion are consistent with this diagnosis.

Plan
We will initiate a low-impact exercise program and prescribe NSAIDs as needed. A cortisone injection into the right hip joint will be performed today. The patient will follow up in three months for another injection if needed. A cane will be used to offload the right side.
