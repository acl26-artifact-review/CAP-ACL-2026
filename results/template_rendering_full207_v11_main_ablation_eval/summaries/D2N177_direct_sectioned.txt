Chief Complaint
Follow-up of chronic depression, type 2 diabetes, and hypertension.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing increased anxiety and pressure related to the holiday season approximately a year and a half ago. He reports that his anxiety was exacerbated by the demands of holiday preparations. He reports that he took a vacation and enjoyed the warm weather and activities. He reports that his diabetes control has been somewhat impacted during the holiday season, with increased sugar intake. He is currently taking metformin 1000mg twice daily and lisinopril 20mg daily. He reports no chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, or blood in the stool. He denies any weight loss. He reports no current suicidal ideation.

Findings
Blood pressure is 132/82 mmHg. Heart rate is 78 bpm. The patient reports no carotid bruits. A grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur is heard at the left base. Lower extremity edema is 1+ bilaterally. An EKG shows no acute abnormalities. Hemoglobin A1c is 8.0%.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with chronic depression, type 2 diabetes mellitus, and hypertension.

Plan
Refer the patient to psychiatry for ongoing talk therapy. Increase metformin to 1000 mg twice daily. Order a lipid panel and a hemoglobin A1c in two months. Order an echocardiogram to evaluate the murmur. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust management as needed.
