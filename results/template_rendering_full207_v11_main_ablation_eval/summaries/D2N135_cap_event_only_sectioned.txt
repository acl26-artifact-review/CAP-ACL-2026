Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent left shoulder pain for the past six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies any recent injuries. He has been taking acetaminophen for relief, but finds it only provides partial symptom improvement. He describes the pain as a dull ache, with occasional sharp spikes. He rates the pain as a 6/10 on a pain scale.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and positive impingement signs. Range of motion is reduced. No signs of acute inflammation are present.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a rotator cuff tendinopathy, likely involving the supraspinatus tendon.

Plan
I have ordered radiographs of the left shoulder to evaluate for rotator cuff tears and other structural abnormalities. I have also prescribed a course of physical therapy to address the pain and improve range of motion. The patient will continue taking acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust the treatment plan as necessary.
