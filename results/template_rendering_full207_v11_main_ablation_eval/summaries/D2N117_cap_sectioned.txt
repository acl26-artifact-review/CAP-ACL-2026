Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain for three weeks, related to increased tennis activity. He describes the pain as sharp and stabbing, with a pain level of 8/10 with use and 3/10 at rest. He has tried Tylenol and ibuprofen, but finds no lasting relief. He denies any recent trauma or popping sensation. He reports ice provides minimal relief. He has a history of playing tennis and was a state champion in high school. He also mentions playing the Masters and enjoying the tournament. He denies fever. He is currently taking metformin 500 mg twice daily and flovent twice daily with albuterol as needed.

Findings
Physical exam reveals moderate lateral epicondylar tenderness in the right elbow with pain on passive range of motion. Palpation of the wrist elicits pain. Right elbow x-ray shows no fracture or effusion. Hemoglobin A1c and CBC blood tests ordered. Chem-12 blood test ordered.

Assessment
The patient has acute lateral epicondylitis of the right elbow.

Plan
Prescribe ultram 50 mg once daily for pain management. Order a right elbow x-ray. Order a hemoglobin a1c test, a cbc blood test, and a chem-12 blood test. Prescribe a brace for the right forearm. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to reevaluate symptoms. Increase metformin dosage to 1000 mg twice daily. Continue flovent and albuterol as needed. Ensure a refill of albuterol is available.
