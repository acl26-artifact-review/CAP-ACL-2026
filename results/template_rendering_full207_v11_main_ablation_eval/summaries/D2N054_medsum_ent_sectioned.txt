Chief Complaint
Right foot pain following a drop injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining a right foot injury after dropping a landscape brick on it yesterday. He initially felt some soreness but continued working, putting weight on the foot. Today, the pain is more significant, accompanied by swelling and numbness. He previously injured his ankle twenty years ago, which resolved, but occasionally experiences soreness and swelling. He has also had surgery on his other ankle. He denies taking any medications for the pain. He reports having been to a new landscape warehouse recently with his wife.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bruising on the bottom and top of the right foot, along with significant swelling. Capillary refill is brisk. The dorsalis pedis pulse is strong and bounding. Neurovascular exam is intact. A radiograph reveals dorsal displacement of the base of the second metatarsal with a three millimeter separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and presence of bony fragments. The patient reports complete numbness of the entire foot.

Assessment
The patient presents with a Lisfranc fracture, characterized by dorsal displacement of the second metatarsal and separation of the first and second metatarsal bases, accompanied by bony fragments. This injury requires immediate attention to prevent further complications.

Plan
An MRI will be ordered to assess ligament injuries. Due to the severity of the Lisfranc fracture, surgical intervention is likely necessary to realign the bones and stabilize the fracture with plates and screws. The patient will be scheduled for outpatient surgery tomorrow morning, following which he will be discharged home that evening. A follow-up appointment will be scheduled in 24 hours post-procedure, and again in two weeks. The patient will be placed on crutches and will be restricted from weight-bearing on the affected foot for six to eight weeks. We will advance ambulation gradually based on healing and tolerance.
