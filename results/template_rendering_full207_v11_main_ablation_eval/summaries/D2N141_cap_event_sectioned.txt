Chief Complaint
Follow-up for adult hydrocephalus and gait impairment.

History of Present Illness
Mr. Watson is an 86-year-old male returning for follow-up regarding adult hydrocephalus and gait impairment. He reports progressive gait impairment and cognitive impairment over the past eight to ten years, with decreased bladder control. He states he is able to walk to the dining room but needs assistance. His bladder control has improved since shunt surgery but now seems to have leveled out, requiring diaper use. He reports no issues with sores from wearing diapers and no pain with urination. He denies headaches or recent falls. He reports pain in his left hand. He denies any recent hospitalizations. He reports feeling like he can have a normal conversation with others again. His daughter states that he feels like he can have a normal conversation with her again.

Findings
Examination reveals gait scoring of 6-8 out of 12 and balance scoring of 12 out of 16. CT scan from 10/15/2020 shows a frontal horn span at the level of foramen of monro of 4.6 centimeters with a third ventricular contour that is flat with a span of 10 millimeters. There is a tiny amount of blood in the right front region with a tiny subdermal collection. The shunt sight was clean, dry, and intact with a confirmed setting of 2.0. Blood pressure is 124/80, heart rate 64, respirations 18, weight 174 pounds, and pain zero out of 10. Cranial nerves are intact. Pupils are equal. Eoms are intact. Face is symmetric. No disarthria.

Assessment
Adult hydrocephalus, gait impairment, urinary incontinence and urgency, and cognitive impairment.

Plan
Maintain shunt setting at 2.0. Follow up in three months to reassess symptoms and consider further intervention if necessary. Recommend a driver safety evaluation if Mr. Watson continues to improve. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months.
