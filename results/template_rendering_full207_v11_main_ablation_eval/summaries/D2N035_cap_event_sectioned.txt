Chief Complaint
Patient reports experiencing ankle swelling and difficulty breathing, leading to an ambulance ride and ER visit.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing ankle swelling that was not resolving even with rest and reports difficulty breathing, prompting a call to 911. She describes an ambulance ride and subsequent ER visit, noting the experience was quite unsettling. She admits to enjoying potato chips and salt and vinegar chips, and eating them frequently at home. She has been prescribed medications for heart failure and hypertension but is unsure if she is consistently taking them. She reports feeling better now with the hospital's care, including a water pill, and has been sleeping well. She denies chest pain or shortness of breath since returning home.

Findings
Vital signs reveal a blood pressure of 128/72 mmHg, heart rate of 72 bpm, respirations of 18 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 96%. The patient exhibits trace edema in the lower extremities. A two over six systolic ejection murmur is heard. The echocardiogram shows a preserved ejection fraction of 55% and mild-to-moderate mitral regurgitation. No jugular venous distension is present.

Assessment
The patient presents with congestive heart failure secondary to uncontrolled hypertension and likely dietary indiscretion. She also has hypertension and mild mitral regurgitation.

Plan
Continue the patient on bumex 2 mg daily. Advise the patient to monitor weight daily and call if she gains three pounds in two days. Continue cozaar 100 mg daily and norvasc 5 mg daily. Order a renal artery ultrasound. Refer the patient to a nutritionist for dietary education. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months. Encourage the patient to use a blood pressure cuff at home and keep a log of her readings. Consider a pillbox or alarm to aid in medication adherence.
