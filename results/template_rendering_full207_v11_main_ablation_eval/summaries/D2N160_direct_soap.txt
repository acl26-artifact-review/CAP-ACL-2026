S:
The patient reports right elbow pain approximately one week ago due to playing lacrosse with his children. He states the pain started suddenly when throwing the ball. He recalls no fall but mentions his son was rough with him and checked him several times with a sick. The patient describes the pain as sharp and radiating down the forearm. He has tried taking Advil, finding it somewhat helpful. He typically forgets to take it regularly. He reports blood sugars between 80 and 100 when checked, and he does not take insulin daily.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the olecranon area of the posterior elbow. Range of motion is normal. A grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur is present. Blood pressure is 150/90. Lung exam is clear bilaterally. Heart exam is normal. X-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
The patient's right elbow pain is consistent with a contusion. His blood pressure is elevated. His diabetes is well-controlled.

P:
Rescribe 800 mg ibuprofen twice daily for pain and inflammation. Recommend icing the elbow three times daily for 20 minutes. Advise against playing lacrosse for two weeks. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Order an A1C test. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks.
