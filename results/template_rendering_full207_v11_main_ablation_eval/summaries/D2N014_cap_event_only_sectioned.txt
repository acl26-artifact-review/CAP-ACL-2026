Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his left hand, which began approximately two weeks ago. He describes the pain as sharp and intermittent, and it worsens with movement. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is impacting his ability to perform daily activities, such as gripping and holding objects. He denies any numbness or tingling. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain, which provides minimal relief.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the volar aspect of the left wrist. Range of motion is limited due to pain. There is no swelling or erythema. Neurological exam is intact, with full strength and sensation in the hand and fingers.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Order carpal tunnel ultrasound to evaluate for nerve compression. Recommend wrist splinting and activity modification. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
