Chief Complaint
New patient evaluation and management of elevated blood pressure.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that she noticed her blood pressure was elevated when she started seeing her new primary care physician last year. She has been taking medications to manage her blood pressure, but she continues to have difficulty controlling it. She measures her blood pressure at home several times a week and has observed it increasing, with a recent reading as high as 170 over 90. She has been taking ten milligrams of norvasc daily and carvedilol twenty five milligrams twice a day and lisinopril before. She occasionally takes advil for knee pain and back pain. She reports that she enjoys watching football games and frequently consumes pizza, wings, and subs, particularly during games. She has been trying to cut back on these foods, but it has been difficult. She reports mild headaches when her blood pressure is high. She denies chest pain or shortness of breath.

Findings
The patient's vital signs reveal a blood pressure of 169/74 mmHg and a heart rate of 88 bpm with an oxygen saturation of 98%. Physical examination reveals no jugular venous distension or carotid bruits. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. There is no edema noted. A small, one plus pitting edema is present bilaterally. A two over six systolic ejection murmur is heard. The patient reports knee pain and mild headaches when her blood pressure is high.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is uncontrolled hypertension. The patient's history and examination findings suggest a multifactorial etiology, potentially including dietary factors, medication adherence, and underlying renal vascular disease.

Plan
Order a renal artery ultrasound to rule out renal artery stenosis. Order a 24-hour urine collection to assess aldosterone and renin levels. Prescribe cardura 4 milligrams once daily. Refer the patient to a registered dietitian for dietary counseling. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to assess response to treatment and review laboratory results. Advise the patient to reduce alcohol intake and decrease salt intake. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain.
