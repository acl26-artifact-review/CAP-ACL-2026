The patient reports pain in the outer ankle, with a crackling sound heard. He also reports hand pain and numbness and tingling. The patient reports limping. He states he took Tylenol.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation of the right lateral malleolus. Neurological exam is unremarkable. Vital signs are stable.

A:
The patient has an acute right ankle sprain. The differential includes possible nerve entrapment contributing to the numbness and tingling.

P:
Order an air cast. Prescribe meloxicam fifteen milligrams once daily. Recommend icing the area for twenty minutes five times a day. Order crutches.
