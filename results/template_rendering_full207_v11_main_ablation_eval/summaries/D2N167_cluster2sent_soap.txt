S:
The patient reports a left knee injury following a fall and twisting, experiencing limping and persistent pain for approximately one week. He has been taking Tylenol and kinesiology tape for the pain, but finds it provides only temporary relief. He reports he cannot take ibuprofen due to liver concerns. He rates his pain as 6/10 and has been taking Tylenol as needed.

O:
Physical exam reveals moderate range of motion in the left knee, edema, and effusion. The left knee x-ray is normal with no fractures or bony abnormalities. Lachman's and valgus/varus tests are negative, indicating no ligamentous instability.

A:
The patient likely has an MCL strain secondary to the reported fall and twisting injury. There is no evidence of fracture or significant ligamentous injury based on the physical exam and x-ray.

P:
Rescribe tramadol 50mg twice daily for breakthrough pain, along with Tylenol as needed. Order a knee immobilizer for one week. Recommend icing the knee three to four times daily for 20-30 minutes. Schedule a follow-up in approximately one month to assess healing and function.
