Chief Complaint
Fall and chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports falling down while walking and subsequently experiencing chest pain. He describes the pain as sudden and intense, rated 8 out of 10. He initially dismissed it as heartburn but it persisted. He reports some shortness of breath and mild ankle swelling. He has been taking his hypertension medications consistently, though he admits to missing a few doses recently. He reports eating salty foods and chips frequently, especially over the holidays. He denies any recent trauma or other medical issues. He reports sleeping well and denies any recent chest pain episodes.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 128/72 mmHg. Pulse is 78 bpm, regular. Respiratory rate is 16 breaths per minute. Temperature is 98.6°F. Examination reveals mild ankle edema bilaterally. Lung sounds are clear. Heart sounds are normal. No jugular venous distension is noted. A systolic ejection murmur of II/VI is heard at the right upper sternal border.

Assessment
The patient presents with a history of heart failure, likely exacerbated by uncontrolled hypertension and dietary indiscretion. The recent fall is concerning for potential injury.

Plan
Prescribe bumex 2 mg once daily. Order a renal artery ultrasound to rule out obstructive causes of heart failure. Refer to nutrition for dietary counseling. Schedule follow-up in one week to assess response to medications and monitor symptoms. Advise the patient to monitor weight daily and report any gain of more than three pounds in two days. Continue current medications.
