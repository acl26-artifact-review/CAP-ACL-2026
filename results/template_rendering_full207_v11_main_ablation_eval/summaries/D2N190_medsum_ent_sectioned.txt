Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a bicycle fall approximately two weeks ago.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports the pain began approximately two weeks ago after a fall while riding a tandem bicycle with his partner. He describes the fall as hitting the curb and experiencing immediate pain. He took ibuprofen and applied a bandage, but the pain has persisted. He states the pain is described as 'holding on' and he has been taking ibuprofen as directed, but the pain keeps returning. He denies any prior knee problems and reports his knee was normal before the injury. He describes difficulty walking up hills, stating it causes pain. He denies weakness. He has been swimming since the injury, but is unsure if that is appropriate.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation along the medial joint line of the right knee. Range of motion is full, with mild posterior drawer instability (1+), and no anterior instability. Lachman's test is negative. McMurray's tests are negative bilaterally. Patellar tracking is full and without crepitance. The skin is intact, with a dime-sized abrasion approximately one week old over the medial patella. The left knee examination reveals full range of motion with no instability or tenderness. The patient reports a slight feeling of instability when walking up a hill.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a sprained or injured posterior cruciate ligament, possibly accompanied by a small abrasion. An MRI is indicated to evaluate the ligament and potentially the meniscus.

Plan
I am recommending an MRI scan to evaluate the ligament and meniscus. We will also fit a temporary brace to provide support. I am prescribing ibuprofen 600mg every 6 hours as needed for pain relief. I will also send in a prescription for a different medication with potentially fewer doses. I am referring you to physical therapy for rehabilitation and strength training. Please schedule a follow-up appointment in 4-6 weeks to reassess your symptoms and adjust the treatment plan as needed.
