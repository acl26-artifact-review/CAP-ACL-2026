S:
The patient reports pain and swelling in his right knee following a skiing injury. He states he felt a pop during the incident. He describes the pain as exacerbated with flexion and extension. He reports mild bruising to his left shoulder following the fall. He denies any other injuries.

O:
Vitals: Temperature 97.9°F, Heart Rate 64 bpm, Respiratory Rate 16 breaths/min, Blood Pressure 140/90 mmHg, Oxygen Saturation 98%. Examination reveals moderate edema and ecchymosis around the right knee. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Left shoulder demonstrates mild bruising. X-ray of the right knee reveals no fracture or bony abnormalities.

A:
The patient has a right knee contusion. He reports pain with flexion and extension. There is no evidence of fracture.

P:
I recommend rest, ice, compression, and elevation for the right knee. Prescribe ibuprofen 650mg every 6-8 hours as needed for pain. Order a left shoulder X-ray to rule out further injury. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 7 days to assess progress and consider physical therapy if needed.
