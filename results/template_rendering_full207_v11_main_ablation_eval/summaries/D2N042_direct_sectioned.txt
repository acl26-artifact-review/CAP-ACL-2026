Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall yesterday while taking out the trash. She initially reported inability to walk on the ankle, but now can tolerate some weight-bearing with limping. She iced and elevated the ankle last night and took ibuprofen. She rates her pain as 6/10 without ibuprofen and 4/10 with ibuprofen. She has a history of ankle injuries but this is the first time she has injured this specific ankle. She plays inner-marathon soccer and has not been able to participate since the injury.

Findings
Physical examination reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus with associated edema. Tenderness to palpation is present over the anterior lateral soft tissue. There is no laxity on anterior drawer or inversion stress. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong and brisk. Capillary refill is less than three seconds. X-ray of the right ankle shows no fracture.

Assessment
Right ankle sprain, likely involving the anterior talofibular ligament.

Plan
Prescribe an air cast and crutches to limit weight-bearing. Advise elevation and ice. Continue ibuprofen as needed for pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 14 days to assess healing. Recommend physical therapy as appropriate.
