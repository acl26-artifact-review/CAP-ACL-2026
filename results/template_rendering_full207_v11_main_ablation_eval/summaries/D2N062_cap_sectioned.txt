Chief Complaint
Difficulty swallowing and chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty swallowing over the past several weeks, particularly with larger food pieces, and experiences pain when swallowing. He notes this has been exacerbated by increased consumption of pizza and burgers over the past couple of months due to stress related to a recent move from the West Coast to the East Coast. The patient reports gaining approximately ten pounds over the last month. He expresses concern about putting his dog in the car for travel, citing negative stories of dogs injured in plane cargo holds. He also reports experiencing epigastric pain. He denies any blood in the stool.

Findings
Physical exam reveals blood pressure of 130/70 mmHg, heart rate of 72 bpm, oxygen saturation of 100%, and respiratory rate of 18 breaths per minute. The patient's abdomen is nondistended with active bowel sounds. Palpation reveals tenderness to palpation in the epigastric region and a negative Murphy's and rebound tenderness. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Neurological exam is unremarkable. Laboratory results show a barium swallow revealing two areas of mild esophageal narrowing.

Assessment
The patient has acute esophagitis.

Plan
Prescribe protonix 40 mg daily and carafate 1 gram four times daily for one month. Schedule an upper endoscopy for follow-up in one week. Advise the patient to modify their diet, avoiding citrus fruits, spicy foods, and caffeine, especially during the initial recovery period. Recommend eating slowly and chewing food thoroughly. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week.
