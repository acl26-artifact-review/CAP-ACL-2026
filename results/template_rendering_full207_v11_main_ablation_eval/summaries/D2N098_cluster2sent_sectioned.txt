Chief Complaint
Left shoulder and elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
James is a new patient presenting with pain in his left shoulder and elbow, reported to have started approximately five months ago following a fall down stairs. He describes constant pain in the left shoulder and left elbow for about five months, exacerbated by lifting and limiting his lifting to 15-20 pounds, which is frustrating. He has stopped going to the gym due to the pain. He has tried icing the area but has not used any other pain medication. He denies any numbness or tingling. Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation in the left shoulder and elbow, with no warmth, erythema, or deformity. The patient suspects impingement syndrome of his left shoulder.

Findings
Positive Hawkins-Kennedy and Neer's tests were noted. There is no additional injury or issue with the wrist, but pain in the wrist is present due to the shoulder.

Assessment
Impingement syndrome of the left shoulder.

Plan
Start with physical therapy and consider a cortisone injection if physical therapy does not improve symptoms. Refer to Dr. Martha Rivera for physical therapy two days per week. Advise the patient to call or email if symptoms worsen or do not improve with therapy.
