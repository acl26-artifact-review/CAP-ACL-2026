S:
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his hands, primarily on the right and left, starting from the fingers and extending up the forearms. He describes the sensation as 'sore' and 'weaker,' particularly when using his hands. He also reports mild stiffness in his neck, stemming from a contusion sustained during the car accident last December. The patient reports experiencing these symptoms more frequently at night and throughout the day. He states that the tingling and numbness are often dull, but can be sharp when the pain is present. He also reports experiencing pain in both hands.

O:
Physical exam reveals decreased sensation to pinprick in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand and the fourth and fifth fingers of the left hand. Tinel's signs are positive at both wrists and elbows. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric bilaterally. Blood pressure is mildly low. Heart rate is 78 bpm. Neurological exam is nonfocal.

A:
The primary problem is bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome, potentially complicated by cubital tunnel syndrome. The patient's history of trauma and current symptoms strongly suggest carpal tunnel syndrome. Further investigation with an EMG is warranted to confirm the diagnosis and rule out other causes.

P:
A nerve test, called an EMG test, is scheduled. The doctor orders an EMG test. A follow-up visit will be scheduled if symptoms persist. The patient should continue wearing splints. The doctor plans to schedule a nerve test. A referral to an orthopedist may be considered.
