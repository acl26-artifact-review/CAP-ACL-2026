Chief Complaint
Elevated and fluctuating blood pressures.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing elevated and fluctuating blood pressures over the past three to four weeks, occasionally exceeding 180 mm Hg. He has experienced periods of lightheadedness and slight headaches concurrent with these elevated readings. He denies recent trauma or chest pain. He has been taking lisinopril twice daily as prescribed and reports rarely missing doses, utilizing a pillbox and recently experimenting with a mobile app to aid in medication adherence. He reports occasional dizziness, described as a 'fuzzy' sensation, particularly during episodes of lightheadedness. He denies fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. He has been managing mild osteoarthritis with over-the-counter acetaminophen and modifying his activity levels, particularly golf, to avoid overexertion.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is unchanged from previous examinations. Lung exam shows trace bilateral lower extremity edema. The heart exam is otherwise unremarkable. No wheezes or rhonchi are present. The patient reports no respiratory difficulty. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's elevated and fluctuating blood pressures require further management. His current blood pressure control is suboptimal.

Plan
I am increasing the lisinopril dosage to 60 mg twice daily and adding 25 mg of hydrochlorothiazide daily to help regulate his blood pressure. I have ordered a cholesterol panel, a CBC with differential, a comprehensive metabolic panel, and a urinalysis to assess his overall health. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to monitor his blood pressure and assess the effectiveness of the medication adjustments. Please continue to monitor your blood pressure twice daily and report any significant changes. I recommend staying well hydrated, approximately six to eight glasses of water per day, especially with increased activity levels. Finally, please limit alcohol consumption to no more than a couple of drinks per week.
