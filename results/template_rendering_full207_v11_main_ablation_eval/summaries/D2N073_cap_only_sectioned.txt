Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and chills.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing left hand pain and chills for the past 24 hours. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, and rates it as 6 out of 10. He denies any recent trauma. He also reports feeling cold easily and experiencing chills. He denies fever, cough, or congestion. He reports no nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. He takes no regular medications.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild pallor of the left hand. Vital signs are: temperature 98.6°F, pulse 72, respirations 16, blood pressure 120/80. The patient denies tenderness to palpation in the left hand.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms of left hand pain, chills, and pallor are concerning for anemia. Given the prolonged duration of symptoms, further investigation is warranted.

Plan
Order a complete blood count (CBC) with differential and iron studies. Start ferrous sulfate 80mg daily with food. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to review the results and assess response to treatment. Advise the patient to monitor for worsening symptoms and to report any new symptoms promptly.
