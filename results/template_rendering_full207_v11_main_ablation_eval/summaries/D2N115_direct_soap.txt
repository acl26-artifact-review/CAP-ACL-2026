S:
The patient reports a car accident approximately one week prior, describing forceful forward movement of his neck and head. He states the pain is approximately 7/10 in severity and is not constant, but worsens with head movement. He reports ibuprofen provided some relief, approximately 5/10. He describes headaches occurring early on and recently. He denies any history of sports injuries or previous neck problems. He reports no swelling or bruising on his neck. He states he did not go to the emergency room for the accident due to cost concerns.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the cervical muscles with neck flexion and rotation. Range of motion is limited with neck flexion and extension. No signs of acute instability. Neurological exam is unremarkable. X-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormalities.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a cervical strain, likely secondary to the reported car accident. The mechanism of injury suggests whiplash-associated disorder.

P:
I am prescribing ibuprofen 600 mg every 6-8 hours as needed. I am also prescribing flexeril 10 mg every 12 hours as needed. I am ordering an MRI to rule out concussion or other serious injury. I am referring the patient to physical rehabilitation or a chiropractor following the MRI results. I recommend conservative management, including avoiding neck strain and promoting relaxation.
