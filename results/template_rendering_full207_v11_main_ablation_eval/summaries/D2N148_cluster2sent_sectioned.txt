Chief Complaint
Acute lower back pain with radiculopathy.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of back injury approximately three months ago, followed by intermittent lower back pain, exacerbated by certain movements and positions, particularly when lying on his side. He describes sharp pain that subsides with positional changes. He has been taking over-the-counter ibuprofen and acetaminophen with limited relief. He reports a previous total knee replacement in 2018 and a prior knee surgery two years ago. He denies fever or chills. He reports no bladder or bowel symptoms.

Findings
Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 68 bpm, oxygen saturation 95% on room air. The patient reports decreased lumbar flexion and extension with some spasm of the paraspinal muscles. Straight leg raise test is positive on the right. Lumbar spine X-rays reveal no acute bony abnormalities. The patient's right toe exhibited tingling, which resolved within an hour. Examination reveals no tenderness to palpation of the lumbar spine.

Assessment
The patient has an acute disk herniation, likely related to the previous injury.

Plan
I am ordering a lumbar spine MRI to further evaluate the findings. I will also refer the patient to physical therapy for continued management. I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain control. Follow up in four weeks or sooner if symptoms worsen.
