S:
The patient reports a history of headaches for years, starting in adolescence, often associated with menstruation, and sometimes stress. He has recently experienced more frequent and severe headaches. He reports exercising more to lose weight, but finds it triggers headaches. He also reports ringing in his ears and blurred vision in the side of his vision. He denies numbness or tingling. He used to run a lot, preferring New Balance and Asics shoes, and now uses Nike shoes. He has had a previous MRI from a few years ago but no recent one.

O:
Blood pressure is within normal limits. Heart rate is within normal limits. Neurological examination reveals cranial nerves I-XII intact, normal cranial reflexes, and a normal gait. Funduscopic exam shows papilledema. Musculoskeletal exam is normal.

A:
The patient's symptoms are concerning for idiopathic intracranial hypertension. A recent MRI is needed to rule out other causes. A spinal tap is also indicated to evaluate intracranial pressure. Referral to a neuro-ophthalmologist is recommended for a complete eye exam.

P:
I will order a preapproval for an MRI. I will also schedule a follow-up with a neuro-ophthalmologist. I will discuss medication options to manage intracranial pressure, such as those that decrease the amount of fluid in the brain. I will recommend weight loss and increased oral hydration, and advise the patient to make slow positional changes to avoid worsening symptoms.
