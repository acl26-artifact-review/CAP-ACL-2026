Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a skiing accident, describing a pop sensation and subsequent pain after a cartwheel maneuver. He reports swelling and pain, and has been using Advil and icing the knee. He denies prior knee injuries and reports no other injuries sustained during the incident. He also inquired about Washington state and its maple syrup production.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate right knee swelling and ecchymosis. Vital signs are within normal limits; oxygen saturation is 98% on room air. The x-ray confirms no fracture or bony abnormalities. There is mild tenderness to palpation, particularly with range of motion.

Assessment
The patient presents with a right knee contusion and associated pain.

Plan
The patient should rest the knee, apply ice for 20 minutes every 2-3 hours, and take ibuprofen 625 mg every 6-8 hours as needed. A follow-up x-ray will be obtained of the right shoulder to rule out any injury. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in one week if symptoms do not improve.
