S:
The patient reports experiencing discomfort, a change in vaginal discharge, and an odor in her vaginal region. She also states that the right side of her vagina is starting to become painful. She denies any fevers or chills. She reports learning her partner has been in sexual encounters with other people and wants to be evaluated for STDs.

O:
A physical exam reveals no external lesions, normal labia majora and labia minora, and normal physiological vaginal discharge with normal color and smell. Cervix is normal. Vaginal wall is normal. No cervical motion tenderness or adnexal tenderness or masses noted.

A:
The patient presents with symptoms suggestive of a possible sexually transmitted infection and concerns regarding a recent change in sexual activity.

P:
The provider ordered swabs for gonorrhea, chlamydia, trichomoniasis, and blood for syphilis, HIV, and hepatitis C. The provider advised the patient that results will be available in approximately three days and instructed her to call if she has not heard from the office by that time. The provider scheduled a follow-up appointment for an annual checkup.
