Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath, which began approximately one week ago. Prior to this, she was active, regularly walking her dog for mile-long walks. However, over the weekend, she was only able to walk down to the end of the street, requiring her husband to take the dog for a walk. She describes a dry cough and reports pain when swallowing, attributing it to irritation in her chest. She has been taking ensure and has been able to tolerate liquids, though she experienced some nausea and fatigue during the initial chemotherapy infusions, which were managed with zofran. She reports feeling somewhat better now. She has been on a combination of cisplatin and topotecan chemotherapy and is currently undergoing radiation therapy.

Findings
Physical exam reveals crackles in both lung fields bilaterally. Examination of the skin reveals erythema on the anterior chest wall on the left side, likely due to radiation exposure. Vital signs are within normal limits. Cervical lymphadenopathy is not present. No subclavicular adenopathy is noted. Extremities are clear of edema.

Assessment
The patient's presenting symptoms, including shortness of breath, dry cough, and dysphagia, are consistent with radiation pneumonitis secondary to her lung cancer treatment. She also continues to have lung cancer and is receiving ongoing chemotherapy and radiation.

Plan
Prescribe a low-dose prednisone (40 mg daily for 5 days) to manage radiation pneumonitis. Continue the current chemotherapy and radiation regimen. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to assess response and address any ongoing concerns. Advise the patient to continue taking ensure and to monitor for any worsening symptoms, particularly difficulty swallowing or increased shortness of breath. Provide instructions for lidocaine swabs for swallowing discomfort.
