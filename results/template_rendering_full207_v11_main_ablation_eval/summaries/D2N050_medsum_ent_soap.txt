The patient presents with a history of kidney stones, confirmed by CT scan. There is tenderness and inflammation over the right kidney, suggesting a current kidney stone. No evidence of hydronephrosis or obstruction.

P:
I recommend pushing fluids to facilitate stone passage. Prescribing oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours for pain. Continue using the urine strainer. Ordering a BMP and urinalysis with urine culture. Scheduling a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks to assess stone passage. If the stone has not passed within two weeks, we will discuss further treatment options, including lithotripsy.
