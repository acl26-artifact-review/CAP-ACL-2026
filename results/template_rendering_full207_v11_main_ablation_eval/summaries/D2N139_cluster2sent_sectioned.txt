Chief Complaint
The patient presents with resistant hypertension and palpitations.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of hypertension and palpitations since a young age, with a family history of high blood pressure. He is currently taking benicar hct, hydrochlorothiazide, and amlodipine 10 milligrams daily. He was recently in the emergency room with high blood pressure and palpitations, which he attributed to a panic attack. He reports being on three medications at maximum dose, but his blood pressure remains elevated. The patient is interested in exploring dietary interventions, specifically the DASH eating plan, to manage his blood pressure. He also mentions potential side effects of spironolactone, including gynecomastia and electrolyte disturbances. He has experienced dizziness when on hydrochlorothiazide.

Findings
The patient's potassium level was 4.0 one week ago. He reports no swelling. Thyroid profile from March 1, 2021, has been forwarded. The patient is checking his blood pressure at home, but the numbers are not documented. The patient has had issues with his blood pressure since he was young.

Assessment
The patient is a 49-year-old with hypertension and palpitations, presenting with resistant hypertension. He may have secondary hypertension due to hormonal influences or adrenal gland overproduction.

Plan
We will add spironolactone to his regimen and monitor his potassium and sodium levels. He will have a metabolic panel and blood pressure check in two weeks. We will continue to monitor for potential side effects of spironolactone, including gynecomastia and electrolyte disturbances. If spironolactone is not effective, we will consider referral to a resistant hypertension clinic. Order a follow-up in two weeks to assess blood pressure and electrolyte levels. Consider referral to a cardiologist for evaluation of potential heart failure risk given the history of hypertension and potential for left ventricular hypertrophy.
