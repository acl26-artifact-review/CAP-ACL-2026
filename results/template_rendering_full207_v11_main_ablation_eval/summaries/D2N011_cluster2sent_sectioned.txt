Chief Complaint
The patient reports episodes of chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing chest discomfort while performing yard work during a hot weekend, accompanied by shortness of breath and chest pain lasting approximately one hour. He denies other symptoms since the ER visit. He has no history of knee surgery or rehabilitation, and reports no chest pain during physical therapy exercises. He reports a history of high blood pressure and notes that his blood pressure was elevated at the time of the chest pain. He has a healed scar from a prior knee surgery and no lower extremity edema. He has an enlarged, non-tender thyroid and a carotid bruit on the right side.

Findings
The patient's heart rate is regular and at 72 beats per minute. The lungs are clear to auscultation. A three out of six systolic ejection murmur is heard. Blood pressure is elevated at 160/95 mmHg. The echocardiogram reveals a mild low pumping function of the heart.

Assessment
The patient has an episode of chest pain, likely related to severe hypertension or underlying heart disease.

Plan
Order a cardiac catheterization to evaluate for coronary artery blockages. Initiate carvedilol at 25 mg twice daily and continue lisinopril at 10 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess blood pressure response to medication and to discuss the results of the cardiac catheterization.
