S:
The patient reports left knee pain following a fall while helping his niece learn to ride a bike approximately one week ago. He describes the pain as throbbing, rated 4/10 initially, but worsening, and reports it is more prominent on the medial aspect of the knee. He reports taking Tylenol and kinesiology tape, but cannot take ibuprofen due to a recent ulcer. He states the pain has not improved and he has been limping. He reports a pop sensation during the injury.

O:
The patient's left knee exhibits moderate edema and effusion. Range of motion is limited, particularly with flexion and extension. Lachman's and valgus/varus tests are negative. A chest x-ray revealed no fractures or bony abnormalities. The left knee exam reveals moderate edema and effusion.

A:
The patient likely has an MCL strain or medial collateral ligament injury based on the mechanism of injury and physical exam findings. There is no evidence of fracture or other structural injury.

P:
Rescribe tramadol 50 mg twice daily as needed for breakthrough pain. Order a knee immobilizer for one week. Advise icing the knee three to four times daily for 20-30 minutes. Schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately one month to assess healing and return to activity. Advise the patient to avoid running for three weeks to allow for healing.
