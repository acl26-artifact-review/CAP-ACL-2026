Chief Complaint
Christine Hernandez is here today to discuss the price of her thyroid medication and to explore options for managing her weight gain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing weight gain over the past several months, which she attributes to her previous birth control medication. She has been diligently following a low-carbohydrate diet and has been working out at the gym, but her weight has not decreased. She also reports experiencing some swelling in her feet. She has not been able to take her thyroid medication due to the high cost of the new prescription. She has completed the COVID vaccine and has not experienced any adverse effects. She has been taking the medications that you recommended, and has not had any issues with them. She is also interested in exploring options for managing her acne and hair growth. Her cousin suggested that she try CLA and amino acids, and she is asking about their potential benefits. She also mentioned a coworker who has a thyroid issue and is considering chromium for weight loss.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no acute distress. The patient reports swelling in her feet. Examination of the left shoulder reveals reduced active abduction and pain with impingement testing. Liver enzymes are improved since discontinuation of birth control. Cholesterol levels are elevated, with a total cholesterol of 222 mg/dL and a non-HDL cholesterol of 178 mg/dL. Vitamin D levels are in a decent range. ALT levels are at 128 U/L, but were at 254 U/L in January. The patient denies any other significant findings.

Assessment
The patient's primary concerns include weight gain, thyroid issues, and acne. Clinical findings suggest possible rotator cuff tendinopathy and a vitamin D deficiency. Ruling out other endocrine causes is important.

Plan
I will order a prescription for unithroid and provide a discount card to help with the cost. I will also order lab tests to monitor her vitamin D levels and liver enzymes. We will explore options for managing her weight gain, including a referral to a weight loss study. I recommend a low-carbohydrate diet and avoiding fatty foods. I will also prescribe benzoyl peroxide and clindamycin for her acne. Finally, I will provide instructions for applying the spermicide before intercourse. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. I will also provide information on CLA and chromium, but caution that they are unlikely to significantly aid in weight loss.
