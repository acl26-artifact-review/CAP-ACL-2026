Chief Complaint
Back pain for approximately one week.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing back pain for approximately one week, which began after twisting his lower back while picking up an object. He initially attempted over-the-counter medications, but found no relief. The pain has persisted and occasionally returns, exacerbated by sudden movements, particularly to the left. He reports occasional visits to a bakery, consuming sweets and carbohydrates, and notes his blood sugar levels have been trending higher. He takes metformin twice daily and attempts to exercise regularly. He tests his blood sugar in the mornings but sometimes skips it when skiing. His family owns a bakery.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lumbar spine, primarily in the center and right side. Range of motion is limited with lateral flexion and rotation. Neurological examination is intact, with no reported numbness or tingling in the lower extremities. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart rhythm is regular and auscultation reveals no murmurs. Blood pressure readings have been elevated recently. The patient's ears appear normal.

Assessment
Musculoskeletal low back strain.

Plan
Order a low back x-ray to rule out structural abnormalities. Order physical therapy to address pain and strengthen back muscles. Prescribe ibuprofen 400 mg as needed with tylenol up to 3 times daily for pain relief. Prescribe flexeril 5 mg at bedtime for muscle relaxation, cautioning about potential drowsiness. Order an A1C to assess diabetes control. Continue metformin twice daily. Schedule a follow-up in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed. Advise the patient to monitor blood sugar levels daily and maintain a consistent exercise routine. Refer the patient to a pain management specialist if symptoms persist despite conservative management.
