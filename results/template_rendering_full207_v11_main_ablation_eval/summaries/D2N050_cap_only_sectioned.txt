Chief Complaint
Right flank pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right flank pain, which began approximately 3 days ago. He describes the pain as sharp and intermittent, with radiation to his groin. He denies fever, chills, nausea, or vomiting. He reports urinary straining and dark urine. He states he has a history of kidney stones and reports having had them in the past. He also reports that he was moving hay.

Findings
The patient reports tenderness to palpation over the right kidney. Examination reveals mild urinary straining. Dark urine is noted.

Assessment
The patient has a kidney stone with associated urinary symptoms.

Plan
Order a urine culture and BMP. Prescribe oxycodone 5mg as needed for pain and Tylenol 500mg every 4-6 hours as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one to two weeks.
