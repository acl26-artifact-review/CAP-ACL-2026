Chief Complaint
Left knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports intermittent left knee pain, which he describes as aching and sharp. He states the pain is worse with activity and better with rest. He denies any recent trauma. He reports that the pain has been present for approximately two weeks. The patient states, "It's a dull ache, but sometimes it's sharp when I move it."

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the left knee. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No effusion is palpable. Vitals are unremarkable. The patient has a knee.

Assessment
The patient has knee pain.

Plan
The doctor recommends physical therapy. The doctor orders an echocardiogram. The doctor prescribes meloxicam at 15 mg daily. The doctor orders a lipid panel.
