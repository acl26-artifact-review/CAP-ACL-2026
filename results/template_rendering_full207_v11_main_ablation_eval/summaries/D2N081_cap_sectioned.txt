Chief Complaint
Follow-up for stage three non-small cell lung cancer and management of chemotherapy side effects.

History of Present Illness
Beverly is a 53-year-old female with a recent diagnosis of stage three non-small cell lung cancer. She presents for follow-up during neoadjuvant chemotherapy. She was diagnosed with a 4.4 centimeter left upper lobe nodule. A biopsy was positive for adenocarcinoma. Molecular testing is pending at this time. She reports minimal cough and sore throat, but some fatigue. She has been able to tolerate nausea and fatigue, and reports no nausea or vomiting currently. She's been eating less than usual. She reports a lingering, light dry cough and a persistent sore throat. She denies feeling stuck. She's been able to turn to family and friends for support and feels mentally strong. She is currently receiving a combination of cisplatin and epirubicin and has been tolerating the treatment well. She reports no difficulty swallowing.

Findings
Oxygen saturation is good. Lung exam reveals bilateral crackles. Cervical and supraclavicular lymph nodes are unremarkable. Chest x-ray shows mild radiation pneumonitis with erythema on the anterior chest wall of the left side. Vitals are stable. No edema is noted on the lower extremities.

Assessment
1. Stage three non-small cell lung cancer. 2. Radiation pneumonitis.

Plan
Continue current chemotherapy regimen of cisplatin and epirubicin. Continue radiation therapy at 45 Gy. Prescribe a low dose of prednisone (40 mg daily for 5 days) for symptom management, specifically for the sore throat and inflammation. Prescribe lidocaine swish and swallow (four times daily) for symptomatic relief of dysphagia. Schedule follow-up imaging in four weeks to assess tumor response.
