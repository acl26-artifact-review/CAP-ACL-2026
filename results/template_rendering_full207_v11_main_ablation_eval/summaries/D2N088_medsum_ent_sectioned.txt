Chief Complaint
Upper respiratory infection and shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue and shortness of breath over the past week or so, associated with carrying heavy bags of soil. He denies any current cough, but reports a temperature of feeling warm but no fever. He also reports elbow pain bilaterally and knee pain, describing tension in his knees. The patient's wife has been encouraging him to attend barre classes to help with relaxation. He has been monitoring his blood pressure at home when going to work, but not consistently on weekends. He reports elevated hemoglobin A1c at 8.2 and has been taking metformin 1000mg twice daily. He is currently taking lisinopril 20mg daily.

Findings
Physical examination reveals scattered rhonchi bilaterally in the lung exam, which clears with cough. There is some edema of the lower extremities and pain to palpation of the elbows bilaterally. Chest x-ray is unremarkable, showing no airspace disease or pneumonia. The COVID test is pending.

Assessment
The patient presents with a likely viral syndrome, likely exacerbated by exertion and underlying musculoskeletal issues. His elevated hemoglobin A1c suggests suboptimal glycemic control with his current diabetes management.

Plan
I am ordering a COVID test to rule out infection. I will prescribe robitussin for cough relief and recommend ibuprofen for fever. I will increase his metformin dosage to 1000 mg twice daily. I will order a follow-up hemoglobin A1c in four months. I will also order a lipid panel. I will continue lisinopril at 20 mg daily and will schedule a follow-up appointment in four months. The nurse will assist with scheduling and will provide further instructions.
