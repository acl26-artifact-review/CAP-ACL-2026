Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and neck pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left hand pain that started approximately two weeks ago, initially with minor discomfort while typing, but has now become more persistent and painful. He describes the pain as a dull ache, exacerbated by prolonged use of his hands. The patient also reports neck pain, which began approximately one week ago, and is described as a sharp pain radiating down his left arm. He denies any recent trauma. He reports that the pain builds up throughout the day. He attributes the pain to working at his desk all day. The patient reports pain in his neck and upper back. The patient reports breast pain. The patient reports nipple pain. The patient reports pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation in the left hand and neck. Range of motion is limited in the left hand and neck. No swelling or redness is noted.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with musculoskeletal pain, likely involving the cervical and upper extremity regions. Further evaluation is needed to determine the underlying etiology.

Plan
Order musculoskeletal ultrasound of the neck and hand. Recommend physical therapy for cervical and upper extremity pain. Advise patient to modify work habits to reduce strain on the hands and neck. Follow up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
