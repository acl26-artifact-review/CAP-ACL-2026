Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath and lightheadedness following a recent tennis match.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath and lightheadedness approximately six hours after playing tennis on Saturday, which he attributes to the heat and humidity. He describes feeling short of breath and struggling to breathe during the match. He reported similar symptoms a few days prior, but this episode was more pronounced. He has been diligently taking his prescribed heart failure medications and avoiding salty foods. He reports no recent changes in his diet or medications. He had a cataract surgery three months ago and a skin cancer removed five months ago, both of which have resolved without complications.

Findings
Blood pressure is elevated, consistent with previous emergency room findings. The neck is supple, with no jugular venous distention. Heart rate is regular and 72 beats per minute. Lung sounds reveal fine crackles bilaterally. Lower extremity edema is trace. The ECG demonstrates stable left ventricular hypertrophy. The echocardiogram shows stable but slightly reduced left ventricular ejection fraction. Skin biopsy results confirm complete resolution of the skin cancer.

Assessment
Exacerbation of congestive heart failure, likely secondary to dietary indiscretion and volume overload. Left ventricular hypertrophy is present.

Plan
Prescribe lasix 80 milligrams once daily and carvedilol 25 milligrams twice daily. Order finalizes the note.
