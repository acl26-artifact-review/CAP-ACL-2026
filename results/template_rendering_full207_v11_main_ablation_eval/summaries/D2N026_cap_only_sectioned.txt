Chief Complaint
Hand pain and weakness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports hand pain, primarily in the left hand, for approximately two weeks. He describes the pain as 'awful' and notes difficulty gripping objects. He suspects a pinched nerve, based on previous experiences. The pain began approximately two weeks ago and has been gradually worsening. He reports weakness in his hand. He has taken numbing medicine and oral steroids. He is currently taking eliquis and gabapentin. He previously injured his hand and is one percent disabled. He has a history of leg pain and a previous hand injury.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the left hand. The patient demonstrates weakness with hand grip strength testing.

Assessment
The patient has nerve pain and neuropathy. He has leg issues, knee issues, and foot issues.

Plan
Order nerve conduction studies to evaluate for nerve compression. Continue current medications: eliquis, gabapentin, and numbing medicine. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
