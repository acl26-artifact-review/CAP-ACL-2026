Chief Complaint
Follow-up after anterior stemi and LAD stent.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports having had a prior myocardial infarction and subsequent placement of a drug-eluting stent in the LAD. He states he's been doing alright, but has experienced some fatigue and reports feeling tired every now and then. He's been able to walk around the house and enjoys gardening and photography, and hopes to get back to those activities. He denies any recent chest pain or shortness of breath. He's been taking his medications as prescribed and reports that his blood pressures have been well-controlled. He has always slept with two pillows. He notes that he likes pizza and is aware that he needs to watch his salt intake. He's been able to afford his medications and is taking them regularly.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur at the left base. Vital signs are stable. The cath site is clean and dry. Extremities are not swollen. Jugular venous distension is not present. Carotid bruits are absent. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally.

Assessment
Clinical assessment is consistent with coronary artery disease, newly reduced left ventricular ejection fraction, and moderate mitral regurgitation. Hypertension is also present.

Plan
Continue aspirin 81 mg daily, brilinta 90 mg twice daily, atorvastatin 80 mg daily, and toprol 50 mg daily. Refer to cardiac rehabilitation. Order echocardiogram in two months. Prescribe aldactone 12.5 mg daily. Schedule follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
