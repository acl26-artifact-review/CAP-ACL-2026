Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began approximately four days ago, following increased pottery activity. He denies any trauma. Ibuprofen provides mild relief. He reports pain primarily in the inner aspect of the elbow and some radiation into the forearm, but denies shoulder involvement. He has not previously injured this elbow.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate tenderness to palpation of the medial epicondyle. Pain with resisted wrist pronation and flexion of the wrist. Radiographs of the right elbow demonstrate no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with medial epicondylitis.

Plan
Order a sling to be worn while awake. Instruct the patient to apply ice to the elbow for 20 minutes, three times daily. Prescribe ibuprofen 600 mg q6h with food for one week. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess progress. The nurse will provide the sling and instructions.
