S:
The patient reports pain for the last two weeks, with a pain score of 7 out of 10. He describes the pain as constant and reports it is slightly tender to palpation. He denies nausea, vomiting, or fever. He reports passing small stones previously and has had prior surgeries. He reports the pain radiated to one testicle, which resolved. He has been taking ibuprofen for pain relief.

O:
Blood pressure is slightly elevated. There is cVA tenderness on the right side. A CT scan reveals a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter, without evidence of high-flow nephrosis. The abdomen is not tender upon palpation.

A:
The patient has a kidney stone, likely causing his current pain. The CT scan confirms the diagnosis and rules out obstruction.

P:
Rescribe tramadol 50mg for breakthrough pain. Continue ibuprofen as needed. Order a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks if symptoms persist or if the stone is not passed. Refer to a urologist for consideration of lithotripsy if needed.
