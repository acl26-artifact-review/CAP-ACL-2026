Chief Complaint
Evaluation of numbness and tingling in both hands.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports numbness and tingling in both hands for approximately four weeks. He attributes this to a car accident he sustained last December, during which he sustained a neck contusion. He describes the tingling as primarily occurring in the fingers, with the right hand presenting with numbness from the middle finger through the wrist and the left hand from the pinky finger through the wrist. He reports occasional throbbing and weakness, particularly with use. He also notes mild stiffness in his neck, related to the contusion sustained during the accident. He has been taking nerve pain medication, but has not been consistent with his medication regimen. He also reports experiencing excessive appetite and bloating, and has been taking coreg for hypertension and lexapro for anxiety. He denies any history of thyroid issues. He reports nail bed changes and dryness/itching of the skin.

Findings
Physical examination reveals decreased sensation to pinprick in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand and decreased sensation to pinprick in the fourth and fifth fingers of the left hand. Tinel's signs are positive at both wrists and both elbows. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric bilaterally. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome, likely secondary to the trauma sustained in the car accident. Cubital tunnel syndrome should also be considered, although the current symptoms are more suggestive of carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
I am ordering an EMG test to further evaluate the nerve conduction and identify the specific nerve affected. I would like to schedule a follow-up visit in four weeks to assess the results of the EMG and discuss further management options, which may include referral to an orthopedist for consideration of carpal tunnel release surgery or injection therapy. Please continue wearing your splints as instructed. I will see you after those studies are complete. ,
