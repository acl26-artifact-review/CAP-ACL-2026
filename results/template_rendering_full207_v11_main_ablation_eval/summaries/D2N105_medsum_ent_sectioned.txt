Chief Complaint
The patient reports a new worrisome heart murmur and seeks evaluation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a new heart murmur, which she discovered during a routine check-up. She describes feeling fatigued, particularly with exertion, and notes leg pain that has been present for approximately one month. She also reports occasional episodes of lightheadedness, managed with meclizine as needed. She has been taking simvastatin 20 mg daily and levothyroxine. She reports a family history of heart problems, including a brother with a heart attack and a sister with a heart condition. She denies any recent trauma or fever. She has lost approximately 10 pounds over the past year. The patient states that her symptoms have worsened over the past six months.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade 4/6 systolic murmur at the right upper sternal border with radiation to the carotids. Blood pressure is 130/80 mmHg. Heart rhythm is regular with frequent PVCs on the EKG. Neurological exam is intact. Ankle edema is present bilaterally.

Assessment
The patient presents with a likely aortic stenosis, evidenced by the new systolic murmur and family history. Additionally, the patient exhibits frequent PVCs and possible diastolic dysfunction, as suggested by the EKG.

Plan
I am ordering an echocardiogram to assess cardiac structure and function, including the severity of aortic stenosis. I will also discuss the possibility of statin cessation with the patient's PCP following the echocardiogram results. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in three to four weeks to discuss the results and determine the next steps, including potential consideration of a transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR) if indicated. Continue current medications, simvastatin 20 mg and levothyroxine.
