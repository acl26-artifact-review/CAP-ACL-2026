Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a fall resulting in right knee pain seven days ago. He received ice and an ACE wrap at the scene. He denies hearing or feeling a pop during the injury. He currently manages pain with ibuprofen and meloxicam, and exercises with a brace. He reports decreased range of motion in the right knee.

Findings
Examination reveals right knee swelling and effusion, with pain to palpation on the medial aspect. Range of motion is limited. The varus and valgus stress tests are negative. The x-ray demonstrates no fractures but shows surrounding effusion. Blood sugars are monitored daily at home.

Assessment
The patient likely has a medial collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain and swelling. Provide a knee brace and instruct the patient to continue using it. Refer the patient for physical therapy. Schedule a follow-up appointment in seven days to reassess the knee and consider imaging if symptoms persist. Advise the patient to discontinue nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs and monitor blood sugars closely. Refer the patient to their primary care physician for diabetes management.
