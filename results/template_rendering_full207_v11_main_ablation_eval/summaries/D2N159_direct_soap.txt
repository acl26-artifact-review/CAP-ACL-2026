S:
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his right hand, primarily affecting the thumb, index, and middle fingers, which began approximately six months ago and has become more persistent over the past month. He experiences some pain with typing and when using his mouse, and the pain is relieved by rest. Ibuprofen and avoiding keyboard use help manage the pain. He denies any pain or tingling at night or any impact on sleep. He reports using ibuprofen for the pain and states that using his mouse causes pain.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild weakness in grip strength on the right hand. Tinel's sign is positive on the right wrist. Wrist extension and flexion elicit pain. Fingertips are sensitive to palpation. The patient grimaces when the physician taps the wrist.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome. The findings support a diagnosis of carpal tunnel syndrome.

P:
Rescribe a wrist splint to wear at night. Increase ibuprofen dosage to 600 mg four times daily. Order an EMG to evaluate nerve conduction studies in the right wrist. Schedule a follow-up to discuss test results and consider physical therapy or surgical intervention if conservative measures are insufficient.
