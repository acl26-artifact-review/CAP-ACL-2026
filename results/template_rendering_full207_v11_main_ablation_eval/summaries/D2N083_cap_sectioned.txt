Chief Complaint
Evaluation of right elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began approximately four days ago following a period of intensive pottery making. He describes the pain as aching and localized to the inside of his elbow, with some radiation down the forearm. The pain is exacerbated by activity and is present at rest. He has tried ibuprofen, which provides minimal relief. He denies any recent trauma or injury to the elbow.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the medial epicondyle of the right elbow. Range of motion is limited due to pain. The patient reports pain with resisted wrist flexion and pronation. The x-ray demonstrates no fracture or bony abnormality. Vitals are stable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with medial epicondylitis.

Plan
The doctor orders a sling to be worn during wakefulness and instructs the patient to apply ice to the elbow three times daily for 20 minutes. The patient is prescribed ibuprofen 600 mg every 6 hours with food for one week. A follow-up appointment is scheduled in one week to assess progress.
