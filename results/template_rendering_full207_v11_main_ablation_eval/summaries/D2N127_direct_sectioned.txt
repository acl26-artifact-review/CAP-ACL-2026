Chief Complaint
Neck pain following multiple car crashes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports neck pain following multiple car crashes, with the most recent crash occurring in February. He previously received therapy for a few months after the second crash and underwent surgery after the third crash. He was previously prescribed fentanyl for pain management, but the prescription has recently lapsed. He reports experiencing stiffness and pain with neck movement, particularly with forward flexion. He also reports headaches and occasional dizziness. He has noted numbness in his left arm and right leg. He describes the pain as sharp and incapacitating, preventing him from working. He has been experiencing fatigue since the accident.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate range of motion in the neck with pain on palpation of the bony process and muscle. There is no bruising or swelling noted. Vital signs are within normal limits. X-ray findings show no fracture.

Assessment
Neck sprain, secondary to multiple car crashes and associated injuries.

Plan
Order a CT scan to further evaluate the neck and surrounding tissues. Initiate treatment with robaxin 1500 mg every 6-8 hours. Recommend ice application to affected areas as needed. Schedule physical therapy evaluation following CT scan results. Consider local injections if symptoms persist. Arrange for a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed. Offer time off work until symptoms improve.
