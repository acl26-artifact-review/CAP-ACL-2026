Chief Complaint
Left ankle pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his left ankle following a fall yesterday while walking. He describes a feeling of 'extension and stretch' and reports ongoing pain for the past few days. He has been elevating and icing the ankle and taking ibuprofen. He denies any prior ankle injuries. He reports walking with some difficulty but was able to get home. He has been taking ibuprofen and ice. He mentions going on a walk with his wife behind the new Rex Center trails.

Findings
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus and swelling of the left ankle. Tenderness to palpation is present on the lateral side. Neurovascular exam is intact with brisk capillary refill and strong dorsalis pedis pulses. X-rays demonstrate no fracture or bony abnormalities. The exam is consistent bilaterally.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with an ankle sprain of the lateral ligament complex.

Plan
Prescribe an air cast for immobilization. Order crutches for weight-bearing restriction. Recommend continued elevation, ice, and ibuprofen. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess healing and function. Advise the patient to continue taking NSAIDs as needed for pain and to contact the office if symptoms worsen or if there is any concern about stability.
