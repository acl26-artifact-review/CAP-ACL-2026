Chief Complaint
Bilateral knee pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a six-month history of bilateral knee pain, with a deep aching pain located behind the kneecaps and occasional creaking. Pain is exacerbated by rising from a seated position, watching television, and climbing stairs. The patient recalls no specific inciting event and reports that rest provides some relief. He has used ice packs and applied ACE bandages, and occasionally takes ibuprofen or aleve. He denies any recent trauma.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal gait. Knee examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the bilateral retinacular retinaculum and a positive patellar grind test. Strength is 3/5 for abduction bilaterally. X-rays of both knees show no fractures or osteoarthritis. Vitals are within normal limits. The patient reports no swelling or redness.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history, physical examination, and radiographic findings, the patient's symptoms are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome.

Plan
Recommend physical therapy to improve lower extremity strength, mobility, and correct running mechanics. Continue ibuprofen or aleve as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up visit in four weeks to assess progress and continue management.
