Chief Complaint
Right lower extremity pain following activity.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his right lower extremity after activity, initially present upon waking and exacerbated by activity. He describes pain relief after periods of rest. He is currently taking ibuprofen or Advil, three tablets every six hours.

Findings
The Thompson test was negative. Palpation of the right heel elicits no significant pain. Range of motion is within normal limits. No swelling or erythema is observed.

Assessment
Diagnosis is Achilles tendinitis, likely secondary to overuse.

Plan
I have prescribed ibuprofen or Advil three tablets every six hours. I have ordered physical therapy visits to address strength and stretching, and will follow up in seven to ten days after the first appointment. I will discharge the patient and instruct them to call the office to schedule their next physical therapy appointment.
