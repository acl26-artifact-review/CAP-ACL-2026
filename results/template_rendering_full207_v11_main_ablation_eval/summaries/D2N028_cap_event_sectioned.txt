Chief Complaint
Chronic abdominal pain and diarrhea.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports having experienced chronic abdominal pain and intermittent diarrhea for approximately two years. The pain is typically located in the mid-abdomen and fluctuates in intensity. He describes periods of constipation followed by episodes of explosive diarrhea, often triggered by specific foods, such as creamy pasta sauces. The diarrhea has been more pronounced over the past couple of months. He previously sought care at an urgent care facility a few months ago, where a CT scan revealed no abnormalities. He has not had any prior surgeries. He takes Crestor and olmesartan daily and occasionally uses Tylenol for pain. He reports a family history of irritable bowel syndrome in his sister. He has had slightly elevated liver enzymes in a previous physical exam four months ago. He denies any history of heavy alcohol use.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a slight enlargement of the liver on the upper right side. The patient reports an increased stool burden. Lung sounds are clear, and heart sounds are normal. The patient reports hepatomegaly. Examination reveals a slight tenderness to palpation in the left hand.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with chronic abdominal pain and diarrhea, possibly related to a hepatic abnormality. Further investigation is warranted to rule out other potential causes.

Plan
Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks. Recommend a fiber supplement to manage constipation. Discuss the possibility of adjusting medications, particularly Crestor, with the primary care physician. Arrange for a liver ultrasound to evaluate the liver enlargement.
