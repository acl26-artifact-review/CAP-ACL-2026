Chief Complaint
High blood pressure check and management.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a longstanding history of high blood pressure, which has recently become more difficult to control. She reports experiencing headaches, possibly related to stress at work and the elevated blood pressure. She has had a kidney transplant approximately a few years ago, which is currently stable and well-managed with immunosuppressant medications. She takes all of her immunosuppression medications as prescribed. She denies chest pain or shortness of breath. The patient reports some stress at work.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slight systolic ejection murmur, irregular heart rate and rhythm, and no lower extremity edema. The scar from her kidney transplant is healed. Her creatinine is within normal limits, and her EKG is normal. Blood pressure is elevated at 158/92. Dragon shows a creatinine of 0.8 and an EKG with normal intervals.

Assessment
The patient presents with uncontrolled hypertension, requiring further management.

Plan
Prescribe Coreg 25 milligrams twice daily and Carvedilol 25 milligrams twice daily. Order a lipid panel and a complete metabolic panel. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess blood pressure response to medication and arrange for a nurse visit to schedule a longer-term follow-up appointment.
