Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a fall yesterday while attempting to change a light bulb on a ladder. He states he experienced a stumble and attempted to catch himself, resulting in a twist of the knee. He denies any other injuries. He has been applying ice to the knee and taking ibuprofen, but reports minimal relief. He has a history of afib and underwent a nose job. He is currently taking digoxin.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the right knee, particularly with valgus stress and flexion. Range of motion is limited by pain. X-rays demonstrate no fractures or dislocations, but reveal some fluid accumulation. There is no sensation deficit noted.

Assessment
The patient presents with an acute medial meniscus sprain or strain of the right knee.

Plan
I recommend a knee brace and crutches for the next couple of days. Prescribe 800 mg of ibuprofen every six hours as needed for pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess progress. If symptoms do not improve, an MRI will be ordered.
