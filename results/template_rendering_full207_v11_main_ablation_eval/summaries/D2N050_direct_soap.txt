S:
The patient reported a right flank pain approximately one week prior, initially attributed to hay fever but later confirmed as a kidney stone following a CT scan. He described the pain radiating to his groin. He has been straining to urinate and has not observed any blood in his urine. He reports intermittent pain, which has lessened since the initial presentation.

O:
Blood pressure and heart rate are within normal limits. Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation on the right flank. No fever is present. Abdominal exam is unremarkable.

A:
The patient has a confirmed kidney stone, likely causing the flank pain. There is no evidence of hydronephrosis or acute kidney injury.

P:
Recommend increasing fluid intake to facilitate stone passage. Prescribe oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours for pain. Continue Tylenol as needed for breakthrough pain. Order BMP and urinalysis with urine culture. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks to assess stone passage and consider further management if necessary, including potential lithotripsy if the stone has not passed within two weeks.
