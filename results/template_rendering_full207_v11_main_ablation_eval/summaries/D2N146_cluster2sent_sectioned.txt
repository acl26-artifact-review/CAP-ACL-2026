Chief Complaint
The patient reports pain in the right hand side of the belly.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue for the past two months, and pain that feels like a squeezing, severe pain. The pain comes and goes, but when it comes it's severe. The patient rates the pain as an eight. The patient states the pain comes immediately after eating or two hours after eating. The patient states he likes to eat. The patient reports his symptoms improved when he cut back and went more of a keto diet. The patient reports the fatty food was difficult to cut out. The patient reports he still gets pain. The patient states he has a family history of gallbladder disease, and both his parents had their gallbladders taken out. The patient states he is not exercising much. The patient enjoys photography and nature and mentions he has nature pictures.

Findings
The doctor notes positive bowel sounds. The doctor notes slight guarding to the right upper quadrant. The doctor notes no rebound tenderness. The doctor notes Murphy's signs. The ultrasound shows multiple gallstones in the gallbladder. The common bile duct size is within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient presents with symptoms consistent with acute cholecystitis.

Plan
The doctor recommends a laparoscopic cholecystectomy, which will take approximately one hour to one and a half hours. The patient will likely spend the night in the hospital. The patient should avoid high fat foods after surgery. The patient will need to follow up with his physician regarding his diabetes.
