Chief Complaint
Difficulty swallowing and chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty swallowing certain foods for the past several weeks, accompanied by pain in his chest when swallowing. He notes that this has been worse over the last couple of months due to stress related to a recent move. He reports drinking more and eating foods like pizza and burgers. The patient also reports some epigastric pain and has gained approximately ten pounds over the past month. He denies any blood in his stool.

Findings
Vital signs include blood pressure of 130/70 mmHg, heart rate of 70 beats per minute, oxygen saturation of 100%, and respiratory rate of 18 breaths per minute. Physical examination reveals nondistended bowel sounds, no tenderness to palpation of the epigastric area, and a negative Murphy's sign. The barium swallow revealed two areas of mild esophageal narrowing in the mid and lower portions of the esophagus, consistent with esophagitis.

Assessment
Acute esophagitis.

Plan
Prescribe proton pump inhibitor (PPI) - 40 mg daily, and carafate - 1 gram four times daily for one month. Schedule an upper endoscopy for follow-up in one week. Advise the patient to change his diet, decrease alcohol and caffeine intake, and eat slowly with thorough chewing. Recommend avoiding citrus foods, fruits, and spicy foods until symptoms improve. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week for endoscopy results and further management.
