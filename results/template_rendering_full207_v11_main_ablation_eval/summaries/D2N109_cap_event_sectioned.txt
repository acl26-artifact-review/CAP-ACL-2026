Chief Complaint
Left ankle pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a slip and fall two days ago, twisting her left ankle on the outside. She describes significant pain and swelling, making it difficult to walk. She has been using ibuprofen and ice, which provided some relief. She denies any bleeding or fractures.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral malleolus and the fifth metatarsal. There is some swelling and redness noted. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are intact and equal. Sensation is intact. No signs of fracture appreciated on visual inspection. Neck exam is normal. Lungs are clear. Heart sounds are normal. The patient has a two over six systolic ejection murmur.

Assessment
Likely ankle sprain. Rule out fracture.

Plan
I'm going to order an x-ray of the ankle to rule out fracture. I'll place you in an air splint and provide crutches. We'll also administer naproxen 500 mg twice daily for pain control. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. I'll order a hemoglobin a1c today as well.
