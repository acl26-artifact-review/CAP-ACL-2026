Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports bilateral knee pain, with the right knee being the primary source of concern. He describes the pain as aching and sharp, rated 6/10, and reports it worsening with weight-bearing activities. He states the pain has been present for approximately two weeks and has not improved with over-the-counter pain relievers. He also reports decreased range of motion in the right knee, particularly with squatting. He denies any recent trauma. The patient reports pain to palpation of the right knee and observes edema and erythema. He also reports occasional elbow pain.

Findings
The doctor observes edema and erythema in the right knee. Palpation reveals tenderness to the right knee. Range of motion is limited in the right knee, with decreased active abduction. The patient reports pain with impingement testing of the right knee. The patient's pain is in the left hand. The patient reports occasional elbow pain.

Assessment
The patient has arthritis and right knee pain. The patient has hyperthyroidism and occasional elbow pain.

Plan
The doctor orders an autoimmune panel. The patient should ice the right knee. The doctor prescribes ultram 50 milligrams every six hours as needed. The patient is taking synthroid. The doctor advises the patient to take it easy.
