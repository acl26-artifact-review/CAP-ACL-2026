Chief Complaint
Patient reports a desire to quit smoking and manage related health concerns.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of gout and type 2 diabetes. He stopped smoking abruptly one day after reflecting on the negative health impacts and has been able to maintain abstinence for approximately a year. He reports increased stress due to work-related layoffs and the responsibilities associated with becoming a new father, leading to a resumption of smoking. He reports engaging in regular exercise to manage his type 2 diabetes and reduce stress.

Findings
The patient's vital signs are stable, including blood pressure of 128/88 mmHg and oxygen saturation of 98% on room air. A regular and rhythmic heart rate of 68 bpm was noted. Lung auscultation revealed clear and equal bilateral lung sounds with no wheezes or rales. Examination of the neck revealed no palpable lymphadenopathy or carotid bruits. A systolic murmur of 2+ over 6 was noted in the past.

Assessment
The patient presents with nicotine dependence, type 2 diabetes, and a history of gout.

Plan
The patient will initiate nicotine replacement therapy with a 21 mg nicotine patch, starting next Monday. The patch location will be rotated daily to minimize skin irritation. Follow-up will occur in two weeks to reassess the nicotine patch dosage and address any challenges. A hemoglobin A1c will be ordered for the next blood draw. Allopurinol will be continued for gout management. The patient is advised to monitor stress levels and explore strategies such as meditation or yoga to manage stress related to work and the upcoming birth of his child. Continue exercise and monitor blood glucose levels.
