Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain and swelling following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he slipped and fell on an icy patch yesterday, resulting in right ankle pain and swelling. He initially could not walk and required assistance from a friend. Since then, he has been elevating his leg, icing it, and taking ibuprofen. Today, he reports some improvement in his ability to put weight on the ankle, but he is still limping. He denies any numbness in his foot. He has a history of multiple sports injuries, including a previous soccer injury, but cannot recall if he ever injured this ankle specifically. He plays in a community soccer league and his children are starting soccer lessons. The patient reports taking ibuprofen for the pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals ecchymosis and swelling over the lateral malleolus. Palpation of the ankle is tender. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds. Neurological exam is intact. Right ankle x-ray shows no fracture.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a right ankle sprain.

Plan
The doctor recommends keeping the leg elevated and icing it. An air cast will be applied. Crutches will be provided for two to three days. The patient will continue to take NSAIDs as needed. A follow-up visit is scheduled in two weeks to assess progress. The nurse will check on the patient.
