S:
The patient reports experiencing back pain after raking leaves yesterday, with pins and needles in his right foot. He previously had similar pain three years ago, but it resolved after a day. He reports that a hot shower provides some relief when sitting. He reports his partner has similar pain when standing. He states he normally does not engage in physical activity. He describes his partner's pain tolerance as high and that he would not be saying anything if it were truly a problem. He is worried about his partner's pain and believes it is more of a long-term injury.

O:
Physical exam reveals normal dorsiflexion. Pulses are equal bilaterally. There is some tingling sensation in the right leg. An x-ray of the low back is normal.

A:
The patient likely has a low back sprain. The patient's symptoms are consistent with a musculoskeletal injury exacerbated by recent activity.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam. Order physical therapy referral. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to reassess and consider an MRI if symptoms persist. Advise the patient to contact the office if symptoms do not improve after two weeks.
