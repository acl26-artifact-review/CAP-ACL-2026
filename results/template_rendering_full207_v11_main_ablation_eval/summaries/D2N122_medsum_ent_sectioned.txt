Chief Complaint
The patient reports a cough that began approximately three months ago, initially triggered by loading bills and exacerbated by a subsequent cold.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent cough for approximately three months, initially noticed around the time of loading approximately 400 bills daily. He describes the onset of the cough coinciding with a subsequent cold. He reports regular exercise, specifically biking, and enjoys riding on the old rail road trails. The patient has a history of rheumatoid arthritis, currently managed with methotrexate 7.5 milligrams weekly, which he reports helps maintain joint function. He reports stopping smoking approximately seven years ago after recognizing the detrimental effects on his health. His mother had breast cancer, but is currently doing well. He denies any prior history of lung infections. The patient reports no current pain or shortness of breath.

Findings
Physical examination revealed blood pressure of 120/76 mmHg, heart rate of 70 beats per minute, respiration rate of 16 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 98% on room air. The patient is afebrile. Lung auscultation revealed clear and equal bilateral lung sounds. Extremity examination revealed some swelling to the knuckles on the third digit of each hand, consistent with rheumatoid arthritis. Pulses are intact, and capillary refill is brisk. A chest CT prior to the visit revealed a solitary 2 centimeter nodule in the lateral aspect of the right upper lobe, which appears smooth and lacks evidence of emphysematous disease.

Assessment
The patient presents with an incidentally discovered solitary 2 centimeter lung nodule in the right upper lobe, requiring further evaluation to determine its nature. Given the patient's smoking history and rheumatoid arthritis, a thorough investigation is warranted to rule out malignancy and optimize management of his underlying conditions.

Plan
I am ordering a pulmonary function test to assess lung function. I am also scheduling a PET CT scan to determine if the nodule is metabolically active. I will proceed with video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery (VATS) to remove the nodule, utilizing three small incisions. The patient will be under general anesthesia and admitted for one night. A chest tube will be inserted and likely removed at bedside. I will recommend follow-up with the rheumatologist and continuation of methotrexate therapy as previously outlined. Please schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and address any ongoing concerns.
