Chief Complaint
Tick bite and associated symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a tick bite approximately one week ago, acquired while working in the woods and yard. He describes the area around his knee as burning and annoying. He denies fever or chills. He reports typical arthritic pain, but no other joint pain. He reports going hunting two weeks ago and has no other relevant history. He is taking lisinopril for hypertension and metformin for diabetes. He has been checking his blood pressure periodically and reports it is doing okay. He has no side effects from lisinopril.

Findings
Physical exam reveals erythema and edema over the right patella, with fluctuance. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 120/70 mmHg, heart rate 67 bpm, temperature 98.4°F. The right knee demonstrates mild tenderness to palpation and full range of motion. Bull's-eye rash is present over the right knee. Blood sugars are running below 150 mg/dL.

Assessment
Possible Lyme disease based on the presence of a bull's-eye rash and a recent tick bite. Controlled hypertension with lisinopril. Controlled diabetes with metformin.

Plan
Start doxycycline 100 mg twice daily for three weeks. Order a Lyme titer and Western blot. Order a lipid panel. Order a basic metabolic panel to assess kidney function. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Continue metformin 1000 mg twice daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
