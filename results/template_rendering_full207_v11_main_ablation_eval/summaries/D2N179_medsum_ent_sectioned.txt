Chief Complaint
Evaluation of numbness and tingling in both hands.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing numbness and tingling in both hands for several weeks, initially noticed after a car wreck last December. He describes the symptoms as exacerbated by cold weather and prolonged use of his hands. He notes that the tingling and numbness has worsened over the past month. He also reports mild neck stiffness following the accident and has been taking nerve pain medicine without consistent adherence. He has a history of prediabetes, which he controlled through dietary changes. He denies any history of thyroid issues. The patient reports experiencing fatigue and weight gain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals diminished pinprick sensation in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand and decreased sensation in the fourth and fifth fingers of the left hand. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric bilaterally. Tinel's signs are positive at both wrists and both elbows. The patient reports a 'zing' sensation in his wrist and into his elbow with palpation. Examination of the cardiovascular system reveals no signs of leg swelling.

Assessment
Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome is likely, given the patient's history of trauma and current symptoms. Cubital tunnel syndrome should also be considered.

Plan
Schedule an EMG test to confirm the diagnosis and determine the specific nerve involvement. Advise the patient to continue wearing splints. Follow up in four weeks after the EMG results are available, or sooner if symptoms worsen. Consider referral to a hand clinic for potential carpal tunnel release surgery if conservative management fails.
