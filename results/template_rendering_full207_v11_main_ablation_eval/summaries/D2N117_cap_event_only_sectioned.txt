Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right knee pain for the past three months, initially following a twisting injury during recreational soccer. He denies any significant improvement with rest and ice. He describes the pain as aching and sharp, exacerbated by stairs and prolonged walking. He reports some stiffness in the morning. He denies any fever or warmth. He reports taking ibuprofen as needed, with some relief.

Findings
The patient's right knee demonstrates mild effusion. Range of motion is limited by pain. Lachman's test is negative. McMurray's test is negative. Palpation reveals tenderness over the medial joint line.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with medial collateral ligament sprain.

Plan
Prescribe ice packs and elevate the leg. Recommend over-the-counter ibuprofen as needed for pain. Instruct the patient to avoid activities that exacerbate the pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress.
