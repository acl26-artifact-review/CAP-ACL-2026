S:
The patient reports a two-week history of throbbing pain in his big toe, exacerbated by movement and pressure, particularly at night. He describes sharp, stabbing pain with movement and reports bone spurs. He denies any injury and reports no previous problems. He reports taking ibuprofen daily without relief. He also reports a preference for riding bikes and enjoys fishing, and he's currently out of work due to his toe. He states he'd like to get a couple weeks off to go fishing.

O:
Physical exam reveals redness and warmth in the right big toe joint. Range of motion is limited due to pain. A large bony spur is palpated on the top of the joint. Neurological exam is unremarkable. Blood pressure is slightly low.

A:
The patient's presentation is consistent with hallux rigidus with secondary osteoarthritis and a large bony spur.

P:
Rescribe an orthotic insert for the shoe. Prescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess treatment efficacy and discuss potential further interventions, including joint cleaning or fusion if conservative measures fail. Advise the patient to continue with regular activities and monitor for worsening symptoms.
