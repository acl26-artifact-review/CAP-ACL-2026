Chief Complaint
Emergency room follow-up for some chest pain.

History of Present Illness
Roger is a 62-year-old male here for emergency room follow-up for some chest pain. He reported experiencing chest discomfort for about an hour or so during yard work, which was particularly strenuous due to the heat over the weekend. He felt short of breath and experienced some chest pain. He has never had this before. Since then, he has felt fine, but he sought a review. He denies any other symptoms. He has a history of knee surgery a couple of months ago and has been feeling well since then. He reports no chest pain while exercising in physical therapy. He has generally normal blood pressure, although it was elevated at the emergency room visit. He denies any family history of heart disease.

Findings
Physical exam reveals an enlarged thyroid gland, non-tender. A carotid bruit is present on the right side. Lung sounds are clear. Heart sounds reveal a systolic ejection murmur, grade 3/6. The abdomen is soft. The healed scar from his prior knee surgery is noted. There is no lower extremity edema. The EKG is normal. The echocardiogram shows a low pumping function of the heart. Blood pressure is 180/95.

Assessment
The patient presents with chest pain, potentially related to hypertension or underlying heart disease. The low ejection fraction on echocardiogram warrants further investigation.

Plan
The doctor will prescribe carvedilol 25 milligrams twice a day to help with coronary disease and improve heart pumping function. The doctor will also prescribe lisinopril 10 milligrams a day. A cardiac catheterization will be ordered to evaluate for coronary artery blockages. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
