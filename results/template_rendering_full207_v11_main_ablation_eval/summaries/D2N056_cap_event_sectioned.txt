Chief Complaint
Recurrent lung infections and concerns about pneumonia.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of frequent lower respiratory infections, approximately monthly or every other month over the past six months, particularly associated with unloading hay. Symptoms include cough, mucus production, shortness of breath, and low-grade fever. He has sought treatment from his primary care physician and urgent care multiple times, resulting in antibiotic prescriptions. He describes symptoms starting the days he is unloading hay. He has ten siblings and denies any personal history of allergies. He does not smoke and does not live with someone who smokes. He has been helping his brother with extra work load. He reports no family history of allergies.

Findings
Vitals reveal a respiratory rate of 20 and oxygen saturation of 99% on room air. Lung exam reveals fine rales but no wheezes. Chest x-ray demonstrates round glass opacities. Pulmonary function testing is pending.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history, symptoms, and chest x-ray findings, hypersensitivity pneumonitis (farmer's lung) is suspected. This is likely triggered by exposure to mold and bacteria present in hay.

Plan
Prescribe an oral course of prednisone to reduce inflammation in the lungs. Order a CT scan of the lungs to confirm the diagnosis and assess the extent of lung damage. Order a pulmonary function test to assess respiratory impairment. Strongly recommend wearing a respirator when unloading hay to minimize exposure. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and adjust management as needed. Encourage siblings to assist with work load to prevent further exposure.
