Chief Complaint
Left knee pain following a fall while helping his niece learn to ride a bike.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a left knee injury approximately one week ago after falling while helping his niece with her training wheels. He describes the initial pain as a 'funky' twist and reports limping since the injury. The pain has been worsening, now rated as a 4/10 initially, but is now a throbbing pain on both medial and lateral aspects of the knee. He has been taking Tylenol and kinesiology tape, but the pain has not significantly improved. He was previously prescribed ibuprofen, but cannot take it due to liver issues. He reports taking Tylenol as needed, which provides some relief, but the pain returns after a few hours. He denies any popping sounds at the time of the injury.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate edema and effusion in the left knee. Range of motion is limited, with pain on flexion and extension. Lachman's and valgus/varus tests are negative. A standard X-ray of the left knee is normal, showing no fractures or bony abnormalities. The patient's vital signs are stable.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a medial collateral ligament strain, likely secondary to the twisting injury sustained during the fall.

Plan
I am going to prescribe tramadol 50 mg twice daily for breakthrough pain, along with Tylenol as needed. I will also provide a knee immobilizer to wear for one week to reduce inflammation and promote healing. The patient should ice the knee three to four times daily for 20-30 minutes. Follow up in approximately one month to assess healing and re-evaluate symptoms. If symptoms persist, we will consider an MRI to further evaluate the ligament.
