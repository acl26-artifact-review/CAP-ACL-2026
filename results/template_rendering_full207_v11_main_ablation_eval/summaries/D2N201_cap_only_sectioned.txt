Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports progressive left shoulder pain over the past six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and when lying on the affected side. He denies any recent trauma. He reports that acetaminophen provides partial relief. The patient also reports a hard impact approximately one year ago. He has participated in physical therapy in the past. The patient's fourth cousin has scoliosis. The patient reports a fall approximately ten years ago. The patient played basketball in high school.

Findings
Examination reveals reduced active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. Right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness is noted. The patient reports midline tenderness.

Assessment
The patient has a herniated disk and radiculopathy. He has chronic lumbar back pain.

Plan
A prescription for pain medication is ordered. A spinal injection is ordered. Lying on the side makes the patient feel better. A corticosteroid will be injected. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
