S:
The patient reports right knee pain following a basketball injury, describing it as achy and exacerbated by weight-bearing. He reports swelling and favors his right leg. He denies blood in stool. His blood pressure was approximately 140/80, and his A1c was 7.2. He takes metformin 500mg daily but skips doses due to stomach upset, resulting in blood sugars around 150 when taking the medication. He reports eating anything he wants and expresses uncertainty about the effect of strawberries on blood pressure.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild edema and ecchymosis in the right knee. The right knee exam shows pain to palpation of the medial aspect. A grade 2 systolic ejection murmur is present. Blood pressure is 140/80. Heart rate is within normal limits. Neurological examination is nonfocal. An X-ray of the right knee is normal, showing no fractures or bony abnormalities.

A:
The patient's right knee pain is consistent with a musculoskeletal injury, likely an MCL strain. His blood pressure is elevated, and his A1c is above target. He is experiencing suboptimal glycemic control despite medication adherence.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. Prescribe a knee immobilizer and instruct the patient to stay off the injured leg for two weeks. Order an MRI of the right knee to evaluate for MCL injury. Increase norvasc dosage. Order a consultation with a nutritionist to address dietary habits and improve diabetes management. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to monitor progress and adjust medications. Provide a logbook for daily blood sugar monitoring and return with the logbook at the follow-up appointment.
