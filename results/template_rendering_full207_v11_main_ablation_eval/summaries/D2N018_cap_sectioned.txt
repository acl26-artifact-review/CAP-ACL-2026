Chief Complaint
Severe right upper arm pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports severe right upper arm pain that began approximately 18 hours ago following a volleyball game. He states that he spiked the ball and, due to a dirty ball and a kick to his legs from an opposing player, he fell and landed on his arm. The patient describes the pain as a nine out of ten, and reports that ibuprofen provides minimal relief. He has a history of gallstones and takes Pepcid for them, and he had a lumbar fusion six years ago. He denies numbness or tingling in the arm.

Findings
Examination reveals swelling and erythema on the right shoulder, tenderness over the right shoulder, and normal pulses. The patient can straighten their arm as long as they do not move their shoulder. The rest of the physical exam is normal.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a proximal humerus fracture.

Plan
Prescribe lortab 500 mg, one to two tablets every six hours as needed for pain. Order a long arm cast and sling. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess healing and adjust treatment as needed. ,
