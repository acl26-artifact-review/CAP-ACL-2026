Chief Complaint
Right foot pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right foot pain following a tennis injury. He describes falling on his foot while volleying a ball, resulting in immediate pain and difficulty bearing weight. He wrapped the foot with ace wraps and iced it last night. He took ibuprofen, which provided some relief but wore off over time. He has previously injured his left leg.

Findings
Vitals are stable: blood pressure 120/75, respiratory rate 18, temperature 97.1°F, pulse 60 bpm. Physical exam reveals bruising of the plantar and dorsal aspects of the right foot. There is associated swelling in the midfoot. Palpation of the midfoot elicits tenderness. The piano key test of the first and second metatarsals is positive. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the right foot. Neurological exam reveals intact sensation and strong dorsalis pedis pulse. The left foot exam is normal. A small amount of stridor was noted on lung exam. No other abnormalities were detected on abdominal or head/neck exam.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a Lisfranc fracture, a fracture to one of the second metatarsal bones near the top of the foot.

Plan
I am recommending surgery for the Lisfranc fracture to help place the bones in their proper positions using plates and screws to prevent further complications. An MRI will be ordered to further assess the fracture and any injury to the ligaments. The patient will be discharged the same day with a cast and crutches and will follow up in approximately one week. Physical therapy will be recommended post-surgery to strengthen the muscles and ensure optimal recovery. The patient should expect a six to eight week recovery period before resuming weight-bearing activities. I will provide the patient with documentation regarding the risks associated with surgical procedures, including bleeding, loss of sensation, and nerve damage. Please contact us if you have any questions.
