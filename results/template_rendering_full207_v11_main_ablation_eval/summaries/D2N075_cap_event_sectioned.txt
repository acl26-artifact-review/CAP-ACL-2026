Chief Complaint
New patient referral from the ER for esophagitis, experiencing chest pain and difficulty swallowing.

History of Present Illness
The patient presents with a new referral from the ER regarding esophagitis, initially experiencing chest pain approximately three months ago. The chest pain, which was described as sharp and felt like a heart attack, resolved within four days without intervention. Since then, the patient has not experienced any recurrence of the chest pain or difficulty swallowing. She reports experiencing occasional heartburn, though she is unsure if this is the correct term. The patient attributes the initial episode to a stressful job transition. She denies any recent trauma or other significant medical history. She has had her gall bladder removed several years ago.

Findings
Examination reveals mild tenderness to light palpation in the upper abdominal quadrants. No specific findings were noted during the physical exam.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with esophagitis, though musculoskeletal etiologies and anxiety related to the job transition should be considered. Rule out cardiac etiologies given the initial ER evaluation.

Plan
The patient has elected to self-monitor for any additional symptoms. She will contact the office if she experiences any recurrence of chest pain or difficulty swallowing. We will discuss the option of an esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EDG) if symptoms persist. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
