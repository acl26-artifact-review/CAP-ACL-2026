Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain following a box-moving incident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began yesterday after moving boxes, describing a popping sensation and strain while lifting a box onto a shelf. He has been using ice and taking ibuprofen, which provides some relief, but the pain persists and throbs upon removal of the ice. He reports no prior injuries to the elbow. He also reports a history of anaphylaxis managed with an epinephrine auto-injector.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness over the lateral epicondyle of the right elbow, associated swelling and mild redness. Range of motion is limited with flexion and extension, and there is pain on dorsal forearm palpation. X-ray of the right elbow demonstrates normal bony alignment with mild lateral epicondyle swelling, ruling out fracture.

Assessment
Lateral epicondylitis, likely secondary to the reported incident of box-moving.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 800 milligrams every six hours for pain management. Provide a sling for comfort and support. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week if symptoms do not improve. Order medications and procedures.
