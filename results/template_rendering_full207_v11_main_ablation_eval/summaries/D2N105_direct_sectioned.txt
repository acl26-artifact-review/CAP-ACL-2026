Chief Complaint
Patient reports a new heart murmur as noted by her primary care physician.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a new, worrisome heart murmur as identified by her primary care physician. She denies any current chest pain or shortness of breath. She reports experiencing leg pain for approximately one month, which prompted a recent chest x-ray. She has been taking pain medication for her legs. She reports occasional episodes of lightheadedness, treated with meclizine as needed. She notes that her symptoms have improved approximately 70-80% compared to six months ago. She has a family history of heart problems, including a son with a heart attack and a sister with a heart problem.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a grade 4/6 systolic murmur at the right upper sternal border with mild radiation to the carotids. Blood pressure is 128/78 mmHg. Heart rate is 72 bpm with regular rhythm. Neurological examination is intact. No peripheral edema is noted.

Assessment
The patient presents with a new systolic heart murmur, likely representing aortic stenosis. She also exhibits symptoms consistent with peripheral vascular disease, including leg pain. Her EKG demonstrates frequent premature ventricular contractions.

Plan
Order an echocardiogram to evaluate for aortic stenosis and assess overall cardiac function. Continue current medications, including amlodipine and lisinopril. Consider a statin trial based on the leg pain and potential for aortic stenosis. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three to four weeks to discuss echocardiogram results and further management strategies.
