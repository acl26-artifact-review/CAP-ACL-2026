Chief Complaint
Chronic abdominal pain and alternating constipation and diarrhea.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports chronic abdominal pain localized to the mid-abdomen, just below the belly button, for approximately two years, with recent exacerbations over the past couple of months. He describes alternating episodes of constipation and explosive diarrhea, often triggered by specific foods, particularly creamy pasta dishes. Prior bowel movements were reported as normal. The pain is often a nagging feeling and varies in intensity. He denies blood in the stool and reports no nausea or vomiting. He notes a possible family history of irritable bowel syndrome in his sister.

Findings
Physical examination revealed increased stool burden in the colon and a slight enlargement of the liver. Bowel sounds were present in all four quadrants. The patient's liver enzymes were slightly elevated (ALT, AST, ALP). Vital signs were within normal limits. The patient reports no recent bruising or swelling.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a complex gastrointestinal disorder, potentially involving constipation, diarrhea, and subtle liver dysfunction. Further investigation is warranted to determine the underlying etiology.

Plan
I have ordered a liver ultrasound and a comprehensive anemia profile. I recommend discussing Crestor alternatives with the patient's primary care physician. We will also initiate a fiber supplement to manage constipation and recommend increasing daily water intake to 48 ounces. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to review test results and adjust the management plan as needed. I will also contact the radiology department to schedule the ultrasound.
