Chief Complaint
Right upper quadrant abdominal pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right upper quadrant abdominal pain for the past 3 days, worsening with movement and eating. He describes the pain as sharp and constant. He denies fever, nausea, or vomiting. He reports a history of gallstones and takes lisinopril for hypertension. He also takes sumatriptan as needed for migraines. He reports chills and a fever.

Findings
The patient exhibits right upper quadrant tenderness to palpation. A grade 3 systolic ejection murmur is heard. Murphy's sign is equivocal. The patient reports chills.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are concerning for possible gallbladder infection and gallstones. Given the right upper quadrant pain and fever, infection is a primary concern.

Plan
Order a complete blood count. A refill of lisinopril is requested. Follow-up is scheduled in one week to assess response to treatment. The doctor ordered a complete blood count.
