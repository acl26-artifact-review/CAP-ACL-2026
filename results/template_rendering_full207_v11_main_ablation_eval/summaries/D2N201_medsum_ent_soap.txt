S:
The patient reports back pain for approximately two years, initially following a basketball injury. He has tried a couple of things but nothing seems to work. The pain is around a seven on a scale of one to ten and is often sharp and throbbing. It radiates down his left leg, and he occasionally experiences numbness in his toes. He also reports some weakness when standing for prolonged periods. He describes the pain as being worse when bending forward and slightly better when lying on his side. He has experienced one instance of incontinence, related to prolonged standing.

O:
Blood pressure is 120/80 mmHg. Heart rate is 72 bpm, regular. Neurological exam reveals decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at L4 dermatome. Straight leg test is positive. No palpable tenderness to palpation of the spine. Inflammatory markers are within normal limits.

A:
Based on the patient's reported symptoms and physical exam findings, a herniated disc at L4-L5 with radiculopathy is highly suspected. The pain and neurological deficits are consistent with nerve root compression.

P:
I recommend a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and pain. I will prescribe a medication to help with anxiety before the injection. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess the response to the injection and discuss further management options, including physical therapy if needed. I will also recommend monitoring for any worsening of symptoms and to contact the office immediately if they occur.
