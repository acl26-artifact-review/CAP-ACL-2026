Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following swimming.

History of Present Illness
Rebecca reports that she has been swimming more regularly over the past month. She started swimming with breaststroke, and she has been feeling some right knee pain after a couple of laps. This pain started about a month ago and has been increasing. She denies any injury, such as a fall or twist, that might have caused the pain. She reports that her chronic back pain has been doing well with current medication and therapy, and she is sleeping and eating well. She has a history of carpal tunnel syndrome, which has been well-managed with release a couple of years ago. She is currently taking medication for depression, which has been stable.

Findings
Examination reveals pain with palpation of the patella and with patellar inhibition. The patient's heart rhythm is regular, lungs are clear to auscultation, and there is no palpable adenopathy. The thyroid is normal. Grip strength is equal bilaterally. X-ray of the right knee is normal, showing no acute findings. There is some pain with range of motion of the knee.

Assessment
The patient has chondromalacia of the patella, likely secondary to overuse from swimming.

Plan
Start ibuprofen 600 mg four times daily with food. Instruct the patient to perform straight leg raises to strengthen the quadriceps. Advise the patient to avoid lunges and breaststroke swimming until the pain subsides. Recommend alternative swimming strokes that maintain a straight knee. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks if symptoms persist. Continue current medication for depression.
