S:
The patient reports a pop in his right knee during a soccer game three days ago, followed by swelling. He states the swelling has decreased somewhat, but the knee feels limited in its range of motion and is not feeling normal. He reports pain between 6 and 7 on a scale of 1 to 10. He has been taking ibuprofen regularly to relieve the pain. He can bend his leg but reports pain at the end of the range of motion and difficulty straightening it fully, requiring assistance. He can bear weight on it but feels it is loose. The patient reports neurovascularly his lower extremities are intact and has a muscle strength of 5/5.

O:
A significant effusion is noted in the right knee with mild ecchymosis. Passive range of motion reveals a limitation of terminal extension to 115 degrees and a limitation of terminal flexion to 20-30 degrees secondary to pain. A positive Lachman exam is performed. The patient demonstrates a marked limp upon ambulation.

A:
Based on the examination, the patient's right knee injury is highly suggestive of an anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) tear. An MRI is ordered to evaluate the integrity of the ACL. Management will initially focus on stabilizing the knee with a knee immobilizer and crutches to minimize stress.

P:
Order an MRI of the right knee. Prescribe meloxicam 50 mg daily for pain and inflammation. Advise the patient to ice the knee as needed. Instruct the patient to wear a knee immobilizer and use crutches for weight-bearing. Recommend slowing down jogging until further evaluation. Schedule follow-up to discuss MRI results and determine the next steps in treatment.
