Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following running.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of knee pain exacerbated by running, initially treated with ice and ibuprofen. He currently uses ibuprofen and Tylenol for pain management. He denies recent trauma. Family history is unknown.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure was 120/60 mmHg and heart rate 58 bpm. Examination revealed no redness, swelling, or effusion. Palpation elicited pain localized to the medial aspect of the right knee. Pedal pulses were present and intact. No sensory deficits were noted.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with a right knee sprain secondary to overuse. There is no evidence of fracture or arthritis.

Plan
The patient will take two Tylenol 500 mg and two ibuprofen 200 mg tablets three times daily. Physical therapy referral was ordered to address lower extremity strengthening and recommend appropriate running exercises. Follow-up is scheduled in two weeks to assess progress and adjust activity levels. A two-week period of no running is recommended.
