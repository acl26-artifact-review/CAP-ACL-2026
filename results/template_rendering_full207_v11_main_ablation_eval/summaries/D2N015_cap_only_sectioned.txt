Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing left hand pain for approximately one month, which he describes as sharp and intermittent. He states the pain is exacerbated by gripping and lifting objects. The patient also reports experiencing shortness of breath, particularly with exertion, for the past three weeks. He describes it as a feeling of being winded. He denies any recent trauma. He reports a history of smoking two packs of cigarettes per day for 30 years and reports feeling extremely low. The patient states he gained weight during the COVID-19 pandemic. He also reports experiencing moments of depression.

Findings
The patient's diaphragm is flattened. Examination reveals mild wheezes. The patient reports palpitations. The patient's left hand exhibits tenderness to palpation. Vitals are stable.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy and COPD. The patient's symptoms are also consistent with depression.

Plan
Order pulmonary function tests. Prescribe albuterol and combivent. Order shoulder radiographs. Initiate physical therapy. Continue effexor. Prescribe prednisone taper pack. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
