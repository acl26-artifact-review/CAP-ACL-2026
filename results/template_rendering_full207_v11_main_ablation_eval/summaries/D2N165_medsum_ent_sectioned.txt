Chief Complaint
Knee pain for approximately six months.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a deep, achy pain in both knees that has persisted for approximately six months. He recalls no specific inciting event, but describes the pain as being exacerbated by getting from a seated to standing position, as well as during stair climbing. He reports using ice packs and ace bandages in the past and has taken ibuprofen and occasionally aleve. He enjoys running, currently able to manage only half a mile before experiencing significant leg pain. He primarily works from home and spends a lot of time sitting at a desk. He has been running on a treadmill during the winter months and enjoys outdoor running when the weather permits. He has never broken a bone, though he did break his arm in sixth grade.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal gait. Strength testing demonstrates 3/5 abduction bilaterally. Knee exam reveals no erythema, effusion, or warmth. Lachman's anterior and posterior drawer tests are negative bilaterally, and McMurray's tests are also negative. A positive patellar grind test was noted. X-rays of both knees demonstrate no fractures or osteoarthritis. Vital signs are stable.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history, physical examination, and review of the MRI, the patient's symptoms are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome. This condition is common in active young people and involves the way the kneecap moves along the groove of the thigh bone.

Plan
I recommend physical therapy to address lower extremity strength, mobility, and correct any incorrect running mechanics. Continue taking ibuprofen or anti-inflammatory medications as needed for pain relief. We will continue to monitor your progress with each visit and adjust the plan as necessary. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess your progress.
