Chief Complaint
Patient reports a fall and subsequent chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports falling down while walking and experiencing chest pain. He initially dismissed the pain but sought medical attention after experiencing shortness of breath. He denies any recent trauma or cardiac events. He reports eating 'anything honestly' and frequently consuming salty foods, including chips and pizza. He has been taking his medications inconsistently, taking one dose per week. He reports feeling slightly out of breath but states it's nothing he can't handle. He denies any sleep problems or chest pain other than the recent episode. He has quit smoking two years ago and has been compliant with that.

Findings
Physical exam reveals blood pressure of 128/72 mmHg. Vital signs are stable. Neck exam is unremarkable, with no jugular venous distention. Lung exam is clear bilaterally. A grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur is auscultated. Lower extremities show trace edema. Echocardiogram reveals preserved ejection fraction of 55% and mild to moderate mitral regurgitation. Weight: Unknown.

Assessment
The patient presents with chest pain and shortness of breath, likely secondary to musculoskeletal injury and exacerbated by uncontrolled hypertension and mild mitral regurgitation. His history of inconsistent medication adherence and dietary indiscretion contributes to his current condition.

Plan
Order a renal artery ultrasound to rule out renal artery stenosis. Continue bumex 2 mg daily, cozaar 100 mg daily, and norvasc 5 mg daily. Schedule a consultation with a nutritionist to address dietary concerns and monitor weight daily, aiming for no more than a three-pound gain in two days. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed. Advise the patient to obtain a blood pressure cuff for home monitoring and track his readings daily.
