S:
The patient reports a basketball injury, specifically a fall while playing that resulted in pain and instability in his right ankle. He describes increased aggression in his shooting and a feeling of instability when attempting shots. He states he has been trying to work out and is back in a good rhythm at the gym. He reports difficulty walking and a feeling that his ankle is not stable. He has been trying to elevate his ankle.

O:
Examination reveals tenderness over the medial deltoid region and swelling on the lateral side of the ankle. Capillary refill is brisk. There is no evidence of malalignment. The x-ray demonstrates a displaced lateral malleolus at the Weber C level with no medial or posterior malleolar fractures.

A:
The patient has a lateral malleolar fracture, which is unstable due to its position. This is contributing to his reported difficulty walking and instability.

P:
I recommend surgical fixation with a plate and screws to stabilize the fracture. The patient will likely require a three-month period of immobilization and will need crutches. I am prescribing meloxicam for pain and swelling and instructing the patient to ice and elevate the ankle regularly. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in three months to assess healing and progress towards returning to coaching and exercise.
