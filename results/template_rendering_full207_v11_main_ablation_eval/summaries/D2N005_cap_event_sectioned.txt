Chief Complaint
Pain in the right middle finger after a motor vehicle accident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the right middle finger four days after a motor vehicle accident. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, exacerbated by pressure. He states the pain started immediately after the accident and has not improved with rest or ice. He also reports a small bump on his forehead from the car. He is embarrassed to discuss the circumstances surrounding the accident, stating he 'flipped the bird' at the other driver. He denies any other injuries or symptoms. He is currently taking digoxin for atrial fibrillation.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal phalanx of the right middle finger. The right middle finger demonstrates limited range of motion. No other significant findings on physical exam.

Assessment
Distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger.

Plan
Prescribe a finger splint and tramadol 50 mg every 6 hours as needed for pain for two days. Schedule a follow-up x-ray in two weeks to assess healing.
