Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain following a fall during mountain biking.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing pain in his right ankle following a fall while mountain biking. He states he was doing something he didn't really want to be doing and fell, resulting in pain. He describes the pain as being in the outer ankle and reports some numbness and tingling. He took Tylenol for the pain, but it did not provide relief. He reports hearing a crack and a subsequent pain. He has been mountain biking for approximately three years, though he doesn't recall his favorite place to go. He also reports a fever.

Findings
Physical examination reveals edema and erythema on the lateral malleolus of the right ankle, with associated ecchymosis and excoriations. Palpation of the right lateral malleolus elicits pain. Range of motion is limited due to decreased flexion and extension, and internal/external rotation. Vital signs include a temperature of 98.6°F, blood pressure of 118/77 mmHg, and heart rate of 77 beats per minute. The patient is limping.

Assessment
The patient has an acute right ankle sprain.

Plan
The doctor orders an air cast and prescribes meloxicam fifteen milligrams once daily. The doctor recommends icing the area for twenty minutes five times a day. The doctor orders crutches. Follow up in two weeks if symptoms persist.
