Chief Complaint
Establish care for a history of DCIS.

History of Present Illness
The patient presents to establish care for a history of DCIS. She reports having undergone a right breast lumpectomy in January 2020 and states she's feeling good since then, regularly performing self-breast exams and not detecting any abnormalities. She confirms a calcification was found in her right breast during a mammogram in October 2019, which led to an ultrasound-guided core needle biopsy in December 2019. Pathology revealed grade two, ER positive, PR positive DCIS. She underwent the lumpectomy in January 2020, with negative margins and five lymph nodes removed, all negative for malignancy. Her last mammogram was in January 2021, which was normal. She has had tubes tied after her last baby and denies any other pregnancies that were miscarriages or terminations. She reports no family history of breast cancer, but her mother had non-Hodgkin's lymphoma and her father had prostate cancer and heart disease. She also reports having experienced miscarriages and terminations.

Findings
Physical examination reveals skin thickening at the medial inferior aspect of the right breast, which may be radiation skin changes. The patient's ECOG performance status is zero. Examination of the left shoulder reveals reduced active abduction and pain with impingement testing. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient has a history of stage zero, ER positive, PR positive DCIS. She is post-lumpectomy with negative margins and lymph nodes.

Plan
The patient should continue to be observed. Order shoulder radiographs. Initiate physical therapy. Continue acetaminophen as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one year, with a mammogram in April 2022. The nurse will discharge the patient.
