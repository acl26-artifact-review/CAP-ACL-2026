Chief Complaint
Discussing a breast reduction.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that she's been considering a breast reduction for approximately six to seven years. She states that the pain has been present for several years, attributing it to working at her desk all day and to her shoulders. She describes the pain as building up and reports pain in her neck and upper back. She also reports nipple pain and pain in her left hand. She has attempted nonsurgical treatments but has experienced minimal relief. She currently wears a 34E sports bra due to the expense of larger sizes. She is currently studying psychology and drinks a little wine on occasion. She is planning to have children in the future. She reports a family history of breast cancer in her mother, who was diagnosed at age 40.

Findings
Examination reveals asymmetrical breasts with severe ptosis bilaterally. No nipple retraction or discharge is palpable. No breast tenderness or axillary lymphadenopathy is present. The patient reports shoulder pain and hand pain. She also reports sweating underneath her breasts.

Assessment
The patient is a suitable candidate for a breast reduction, given her symptoms and desire for improvement.

Plan
I recommend a breast reduction to alleviate the patient's pain and improve her quality of life. We will schedule a consultation to discuss the procedure in detail and address any remaining questions. I will order shoulder radiographs and initiate physical therapy. We will continue acetaminophen as needed. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
