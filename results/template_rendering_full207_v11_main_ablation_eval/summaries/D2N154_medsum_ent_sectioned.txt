Chief Complaint
Pain in the heel and calf after workouts.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent pain in the heel and calf following workouts, typically after a workout or when first getting up in the morning. The pain is described as tight and almost tearing. It eases slightly after walking for about 15 minutes, but returns when sitting for extended periods and then attempting to stand again. The pain also worsens after significant activity. He reports no stretching routine and does not stretch at all. He trains five days a week, alternating between a long run and a long bike ride, with no swimming. He runs approximately 10 kilometers and bikes for 45-60 minutes.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the right heel and calf. The Thompson test is negative. Range of motion is full without pain. Vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
Achilles tendinitis is diagnosed, likely secondary to overuse and lack of stretching.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 320 mg every 6 hours as needed for pain and inflammation. Order physical therapy evaluation and initiate a course of treatment focusing on stretching and strengthening exercises. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 7-10 days to assess response to treatment. Advise patient to modify training schedule to allow for adequate recovery.
