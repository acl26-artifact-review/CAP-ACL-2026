O:
Physical exam reveals a grade two out of six systolic ejection murmur appreciated on lung exam. Vital signs are stable. Neurological exam is unremarkable. Liver panel shows an elevated AST of 39, with normal ALT and total bilirubin. The patient appears in no distress and exhibits no jaundice.

A:
The patient has been diagnosed with hepatitis C and requires further investigation to assess the severity of liver disease. He has a history of substance use and smoking, which contribute to his risk profile.

P:
Order hepatitis C RNA and HCV genotype testing. Order an ultrasound elastography to assess liver fibrosis. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to review test results and discuss treatment options. Refer the patient to his family physician for continued care and support.
