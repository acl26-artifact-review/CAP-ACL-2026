S:
The patient reports experiencing pain primarily when typing on the keyboard, accompanied by numbness and tingling in his right hand. He describes this numbness and tingling as constant and reports pain exacerbated by wrist flexion and extension. He uses ibuprofen to manage the pain and reports reduced grip strength in his right hand. He also reports experiencing pain that wakes him up at night. He states that the numbness and tingling is most pronounced in his thumb, pointer, and middle finger, differentiating it from the other hand.

O:
Physical exam reveals pain with wrist extension and flexion, and positive Tinel's sign. Grip strength is reduced in the right hand. The patient grimaces when the examiner taps the wrist and forearm.

A:
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

P:
I will order an EMG to evaluate nerve conduction studies in the wrist. I will prescribe a wrist splint to wear at night. I will also increase the ibuprofen dosage to 600 mg four times daily. We will monitor the patient's response to conservative treatment and consider surgery if symptoms persist or do not improve following the EMG results.
