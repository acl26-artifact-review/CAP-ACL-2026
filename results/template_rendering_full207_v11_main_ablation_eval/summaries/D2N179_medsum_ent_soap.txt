S:
The patient reports intermittent numbness and tingling in both hands for the past few weeks, initially noticed after a car wreck last December. He describes the tingling and numbness as more pronounced at night and fluctuating throughout the day. He also reports mild stiffness in his neck following the accident and has been taking nerve pain medicine inconsistently. He denies loss of sensation, but notes reduced sensitivity compared to normal. He reports bloating and excessive appetite, and states he has been taking coreg for hypertension and lexapro for anxiety. He reports fatigue and weight gain.

O:
Physical exam reveals diminished pinprick sensation in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand and the fourth and fifth fingers of the left hand. Tinel's signs are positive at both wrists and elbows. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric bilaterally. Vital signs are within normal limits. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable.

A:
Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome is suspected, potentially exacerbated by the past trauma. The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with this diagnosis. Further investigation with an EMG is warranted to confirm the diagnosis and determine the specific nerve involvement.

P:
Schedule an EMG test to evaluate nerve function. Advise the patient to continue wearing splints. Follow up in 2 weeks to discuss EMG results and consider referral to a hand clinic or orthopedist for potential carpal tunnel release surgery if symptoms persist despite conservative management.
