S:
The patient reports a right knee injury seven days ago, with ongoing swelling and pain. He describes pain with palpation on the medial aspect of the right knee and decreased range of motion. He reports hearing or feeling a pop in his knee during the injury. The patient used ice and an ACE wrap following the injury. He is currently taking 500 mg of metformin twice daily and checks his blood sugars every morning at home. He reports using motrin or advil for pain and swelling.

O:
The right knee x-ray shows a small amount of effusion but no fractures. Physical exam reveals ecchymosis and swelling on the inside of the right knee and fluid in the knee. Palpation elicits pain on the medial aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited. The varus and valgus stress tests are negative.

A:
The patient likely has a medial collateral ligament strain based on the mechanism of injury and examination findings. The effusion and medial pain suggest ligamentous injury.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain and swelling. Provide a right knee brace for a few days. Refer for physical therapy. Schedule a follow-up appointment in seven days for reevaluation and consider a CT scan or MRI if swelling persists or symptoms worsen. Advise the patient to discontinue nonsteroidal medications. Instruct the patient to continue monitoring blood sugars daily and to contact their PCP if they experience significant increases due to inactivity. Schedule a return visit in seven days for reevaluation.
