Chief Complaint
Back pain following a refrigerator move.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain that began five days ago after pushing a refrigerator through a room. He describes the pain as being on the lower right side of his back. He has a prior discectomy and has been taking ibuprofen and occasionally Tylenol with ibuprofen for the pain. He denies any neurological symptoms such as numbness or tingling in his legs, or any chest, abdominal, or other systemic symptoms. He reports that bending over is difficult.

Findings
Physical examination reveals pain to palpation of the right lumbar spine, decreased flexion and extension, and a positive straight leg raise on the right. Vital signs are normal. X-rays show normal bony alignment. Labs are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a lumbar strain, likely secondary to the recent injury. The prior discectomy is not concerning at this time.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI to rule out other pathology. I will prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily and ultram 50 mg every four hours as needed for pain. I am also referring the patient to physical therapy once the MRI results are available. Continue Tylenol as needed.
