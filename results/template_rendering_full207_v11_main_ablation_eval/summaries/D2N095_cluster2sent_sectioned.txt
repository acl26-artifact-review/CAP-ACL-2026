Chief Complaint
The patient reports back pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing back pain, initially described as a spasm, and has been taking ibuprofen for pain management. He denies any recent trauma or numbness/tingling in his legs or feet. The patient reports a history of hypertension and is currently taking lisinopril. He reports seasonal allergies with some nasal congestion. The nurse provided a review of systems sheet, and the patient endorses the back pain. There is no elevated white blood cell count.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is well controlled. A physical exam reveals slight systolic ejection murmur, but this is chronic. Examination of the lumbar spine reveals pain to palpation on the right lateral aspect and pain with flexion and extension, with a negative straight leg raise. An x-ray of the lumbar spine shows no fracture. Lab results are normal with no signs of infection.

Assessment
The patient has a lumbar strain, likely related to activity.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily. Refer the patient to physical therapy for back strengthening exercises. Continue the patient on lisinopril 10 milligrams daily. Order lisinopril 10 milligrams po daily.
