Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and tingling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left hand pain and tingling for approximately two years, initially with carpal tunnel release. He states the release has been doing well. He now reports increased pain and tingling, particularly with cold weather and prolonged use of the hands. He denies numbness. He also reports eating frozen meals frequently. The patient reports feeling stressed.

Findings
Examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the volar aspect of the left wrist. Grip strength is equal bilaterally. Neurological exam is intact. The doctor does not find any adenopathy in the patient's neck. The patient's heart is regular without murmurs. The doctor finds the patient's neurological exam to be intact. The doctor finds the patient's grips to be equal. The patient's pulses are good. The patient's lungs are clear.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with chronic peripheral neuropathy, possibly exacerbated by cold exposure and stress. A history of carpal tunnel release is present.

Plan
Start a trial of gabapentin 300mg twice daily for neuropathic pain. Recommend warm hand therapy and stress management techniques. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to medication and review symptoms.
