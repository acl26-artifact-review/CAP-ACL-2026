Chief Complaint
Gloria reports shortness of breath and is concerned about her current congestive heart failure and COPD.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty breathing when lying flat, exacerbated by exertion, and reports recent weight gain and swelling in her legs and ankles. She admits to struggling with dietary control and managing her blood sugars, and reports enjoying salty foods recently. She is currently taking lasix and metformin.

Findings
Examination reveals neck swelling, mild JVD, crackles in both lung bases and intermittent wheezing, a two-over-six systolic ejection murmur, and one-and-a-half plus pitting edema in her ankles. Her oxygen saturation is 92% in the office. Hemoglobin A1c was 7.0%.

Assessment
Gloria is a 46-year-old female with a history of diabetes, congestive heart failure, and COPD, presenting with an exacerbation of her CHF symptoms.

Plan
Increase Gloria's lasix dose to 60 mg daily, order a repeat hemoglobin A1c, order blood tests to assess blood sugars, provide albuterol and atrovent inhalers as needed, and schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately two days to reassess her condition. Additionally, we will initiate naproxen and flexeril for her back pain and provide exercises to improve mobility.
