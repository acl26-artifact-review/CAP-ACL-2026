Chief Complaint
Headaches

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of headaches for years, dating back to adolescence. Initially, headaches were associated with menstruation, but have since become more frequent and severe, occurring regardless of menstrual cycle. The patient reports that exercise exacerbates the headaches, specifically jogging. Additionally, the patient reports experiencing ringing in the ears for several months and has recently noticed blurred vision in the side of their vision. The patient denies numbness or tingling. The patient reports using New Balance and Asics running shoes.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal sinus rhythm with no murmurs, rubs, or gallops. Funduscopic exam demonstrates papilledema. Neurological exam is intact, including cranial nerves and gait.

Assessment
Idiopathic intracranial hypertension is suspected based on the patient's symptoms of headaches, blurred vision, and papilledema.

Plan
Order an MRI of the brain to rule out other causes of the patient's symptoms. Schedule a follow-up appointment with a neuro-ophthalmologist for a complete eye exam. I will also work with the front office to obtain pre-authorization for the MRI. We will discuss medication options to manage the increased intracranial pressure if the MRI confirms the diagnosis.
