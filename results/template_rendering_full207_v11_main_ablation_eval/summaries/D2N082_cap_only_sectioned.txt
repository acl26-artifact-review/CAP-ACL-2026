Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand that began approximately two weeks ago. The pain is constant and is exacerbated by gripping and lifting objects. The patient denies any recent trauma. He reports that the pain has not significantly impacted his daily activities, but he has difficulty opening jars. He reports a history of iga deficiency and frequent infections. The patient's PCP reported a low immunoglobulin A level. The patient believes their cold symptoms are unusual. The patient reports frequent infections and severe diarrhea. The patient's family owns a donut shop.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the distal interphalangeal joint of the left hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No swelling or erythema is noted.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a distal interphalangeal joint sprain. Given the patient's history of iga deficiency, secondary infection should be considered.

Plan
Order X-rays of the left hand to rule out fracture. Prescribe ice packs and elevate the hand. Advise the patient to avoid activities that exacerbate the pain. Follow up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
