Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand. He states the pain started approximately one month ago and has been gradually worsening. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is exacerbated by gripping and lifting objects. He denies any numbness or tingling. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain, which provides some relief but not complete resolution. He also reports experiencing coughing and wheezing monthly or every other month for the past six months.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the distal volar aspect of the left hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No swelling or erythema is noted. Vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with moderate to intermediate asthma. The patient is diagnosed with moderate intermittent asthma. The patient has a history of asthma.

Plan
The doctor will create an asthma action plan for the patient. The patient will take an inhaled steroid inhaler as prescribed. The patient will use an albuterol inhaler as needed. The patient uses an inhaler.
