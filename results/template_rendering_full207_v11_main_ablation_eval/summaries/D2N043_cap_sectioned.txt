Chief Complaint
Quit smoking and manage type 2 diabetes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a 20 pack-year smoking history, starting in adolescence and continuing daily. He states he's been trying to quit smoking for several times, with a successful attempt lasting approximately one year. This attempt was followed by increased work-related stress, leading to a resumption of smoking. The patient is currently smoking approximately one and a half packs per day. He reports experiencing pain in his left hand, which he attributes to gout. He is also motivated to quit smoking due to the impending arrival of his daughter. He has been trying to manage his type 2 diabetes through diet and exercise. He reports feeling stressed and is considering meditation and joining a gym to manage his stress levels.

Findings
Vitals: Blood pressure 128/88 mmHg, heart rate 68 bpm, oxygen saturation 98% on room air. Lung exam reveals clear and equal bilateral lung sounds with no wheezes or rales. Heart exam reveals a two out of six systolic murmur. Neck exam is unremarkable. The patient's skin is intact and without signs of rash or lesions.

Assessment
The patient's primary problems include nicotine dependence, type 2 diabetes, and a history of gout. He is also experiencing stress related to the impending arrival of his daughter.

Plan
Start nicotine patch therapy with a 21 mg patch, changing location daily to minimize skin irritation. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress and adjust the nicotine patch dosage. Provide the patient with smoking cessation aids and handouts. Encourage the patient to continue his exercise routine and explore meditation or yoga for stress management. Order a hemoglobin A1c test for his next blood draw. Continue allopurinol for gout management.
