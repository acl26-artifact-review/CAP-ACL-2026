S:
The patient reports intermittent acid reflux since childhood, with worsening over the past few years. He describes the sensation as burning in his chest and throat. He denies difficulty swallowing or abdominal pain. He reports no nausea, vomiting, constipation, diarrhea, or rectal bleeding. He has experienced symptoms related to stress and caffeine consumption. He has not cut out spicy foods or citrus products completely. He reports a stable weight, though reduced appetite over the past two months. He has had a baby with intestinal surgery at one month old.

O:
Physical exam reveals well-healed abdominal surgical scars. Vital signs are within normal limits. Neurological exam is unremarkable.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a several-year history of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). The patient's symptoms are likely exacerbated by stress and caffeine consumption. A trial of proton pump inhibitors is warranted. An upper endoscopy is recommended to evaluate for underlying causes of GERD.

P:
Rescribe nexium 40 mg twice daily. Schedule an upper endoscopy. Recommend dietary modifications to avoid spicy foods, caffeine, and citrus products. Advise the patient to follow up in one week to assess response to medication and dietary changes. Schedule a follow-up appointment to discuss endoscopy results and further management strategies.
