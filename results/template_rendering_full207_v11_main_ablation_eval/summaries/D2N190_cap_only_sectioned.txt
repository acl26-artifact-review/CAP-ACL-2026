Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain that began approximately two weeks ago following a fall off a tandem bike. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, exacerbated by walking up inclines. He rates the pain as 6/10. He denies any prior knee injuries. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain. He states he needs to work harder. The patient feels weak.

Findings
The patient reports tenderness to palpation over the medial joint line of the right knee. Range of motion is limited by pain. The patient reports mild laxity. A dime-size abrasion is noted. The patient reports clicking. The patient denies crepitus.

Assessment
Right knee injury, likely involving medial joint line irritation. Persistent pain.

Plan
A brace will be fitted to the patient today. The doctor will examine the patient's knee. A prescription for ibuprofen is to be sent to the patient. The patient will follow up with physical therapy in 4-6 weeks. The doctor recommends leg removal.
