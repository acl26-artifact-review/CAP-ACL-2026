Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain following a lacrosse injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began approximately one week ago following a collision during a lacrosse game while playing with his children. He describes the pain as sharp and localized to the tip of the elbow, exacerbated by movement. He denies any specific mechanism of injury beyond being checked by his son. He took Advil for a couple of days, but it didn't provide significant relief. He does not recall falling, but his son was rough with him. He reports the pain radiates down the forearm. The patient's son was checking him several times with a sick look.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation at the olecranon process of the right elbow. Range of motion is limited with flexion and extension, producing pain. A grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur is present. The x-ray is normal, showing no fracture or bony abnormality. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. The heart exam is normal.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a right elbow contusion and associated inflammation.

Plan
I am prescribing 800 milligrams of ibuprofen to be taken twice daily for pain and inflammation. I also recommend icing the area three times a day for 20 minutes. For the next couple of weeks, I recommend you refrain from playing lacrosse with your son. We will obtain an A1C to assess his diabetes control and adjust metformin dosage as needed. Please follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
