Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain for approximately three weeks, primarily associated with increased tennis activity. He states he joined a country club and has been hitting the ball more frequently. The pain is located on the outside of the elbow and radiates to no distance. He has tried tylenol and ibuprofen, but finds no relief. Ice provided minimal benefit. He denies any recent trauma or popping sensation. He has been eating a lot of Easter candy recently, which may be contributing to his blood sugar levels. The patient also mentioned playing tennis in high school and enjoying the sport.

Findings
Physical exam reveals moderate tenderness to palpation of the lateral epicondyle of the right elbow. Range of motion is limited by pain. The patient reports pain with resisted extension of the right wrist. The heart exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur. Right elbow x-ray shows no acute fracture or malalignment. Vitals are stable. No fever noted.

Assessment
Acute lateral epicondylitis of the right elbow.

Plan
