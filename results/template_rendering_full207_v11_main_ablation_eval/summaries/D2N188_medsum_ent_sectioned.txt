Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain following a fall during mountain biking.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining an ankle injury while mountain biking, falling while performing a maneuver he did not want to be doing. He describes the injury as occurring three years ago. He reports taking Tylenol for the pain, which provided no relief. He describes hearing a 'crack crackle' during the fall. He reports tingling in his toes and numbness in his heart. He states he has had a fever, correlating with the pain. He reports limping and difficulty walking on the affected ankle. He reports a history of mountain biking for three years, but does not remember his favorite place to go.

Findings
Physical exam reveals edema and erythema on the lateral malleolus of the right ankle. Palpation of the right lateral malleolus elicits pain. Range of motion is limited, with decreased flexion and extension of the right ankle and internal/external rotation. The patient's temperature is 98.6°F, blood pressure is 118/77 mmHg, and heart rate is 77 beats per minute. An x-ray shows no acute fracture or bony abnormality, but reveals a soft tissue injury.

Assessment
Acute right ankle sprain.

Plan
I will place the patient in an air cast, provide crutches, and prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily. I will instruct the patient to ice the area for 20 minutes five times a day and to weight-bear as tolerated. I anticipate the patient will start feeling better in the next couple of weeks. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks if symptoms persist, or if the patient does not improve. We will consider physical therapy or further imaging if needed.
