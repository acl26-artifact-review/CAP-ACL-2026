Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath following a recent bike ride.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath and lightheadedness after a three-hour bike ride over the weekend. He describes this as a sudden onset, unlike his usual morning bike rides, which he has done regularly for years. He denies chest pain, but recalls feeling some discomfort that resolved within an hour. He reports a mild cough approximately a year and a half ago, which resolved. He is currently taking imitrex for migraines and protonix for reflux, both of which he reports are well-managed. He underwent cataract surgery four and a half months ago and reports good vision since then.

Findings
Oxygenation is normal. Neck examination reveals no jugular venous distension. Heart rhythm is regular, with no murmurs appreciated. Lung auscultation reveals expiratory wheezing bilaterally. Lower extremity edema is trace. Chest x-ray is normal, showing no pulmonary edema or pneumonia. Pulmonary function tests are within normal limits. Laboratory results are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient likely experienced an asthma exacerbation triggered by exercise and allergies.

Plan
Prescribe albuterol inhaler, two puffs every four hours as needed for wheezing or shortness of breath. Order a pulmonary referral. Continue imitrex as needed for migraines. Follow up in two weeks, and instruct the patient to contact the office immediately if shortness of breath recurs. Finalize note.
