S:
The patient reports a history of playing football and sustaining a shoulder injury four days ago. He describes pain on the posterior aspect of his right shoulder, particularly around the acromioclavicular joint. He reports difficulty reaching for objects like a box in the driveway. He denies fever or chills. He reports a slight headache. He states he has not taken any medication for the pain other than tylenol, which did not provide relief. He denies any neck problems or other joint problems. He reports no numbness or tingling in his fingers. He states he is right-handed. He denies any prior orthopedic injuries.

O:
The patient's blood pressure is 122/76 mmHg. His heart rate is 82 beats per minute. Oxygen saturation is 95% on room air. Cervical spine exam reveals no tenderness to palpation. Range of motion is full. There is no ecchymosis or bruising on the right shoulder. Physical exam reveals pain to palpation of the acromioclavicular joint. Abduction and adduction are limited to 90 degrees. Supination and pronation of the right arm elicit pain and are limited. Radial pulse is palpable. The nurse performed a right shoulder x-ray which revealed an acute acromioclavicular joint fracture. The cat scan order has been placed.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with an acute acromioclavicular joint fracture. There is no evidence of acute neurological deficits.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily. Order a cat scan of the right shoulder. Refer to physical therapy for shoulder strengthening. Advise the patient to wear a sling for support. Recommend avoiding tackle football until the fracture is healed. Advise the patient to avoid heavy lifting. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress.
