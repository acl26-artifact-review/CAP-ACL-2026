S:
The patient reports left elbow pain exacerbated by prolonged typing, accompanied by swelling and stiffness. He also reports nasal congestion due to allergies. He denies any numbness, tingling, or weakness in his arm, nor any chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, or vomiting. He reports a past seizure one year ago, but has not experienced any since.

O:
Physical examination reveals erythema and edema of the left elbow, with tenderness to palpation of the olecranon. The radial pulse is palpable. Vital signs are stable. The x-ray of the left arm shows no evidence of fracture.

A:
The primary problem is olecranon bursitis secondary to repetitive strain. The patient continues to manage epilepsy with Keppra and reports feeling well with therapy for depression. He has a history of peptic ulcer disease managed with protonix.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for left elbow pain. Order a refill of Keppra 500 mg twice daily. Initiate Lexapro 10 mg daily for depression. Continue protonix 40 mg daily before meals. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
