Chief Complaint
Left hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his left hand. He states the pain started approximately two weeks ago and has been gradually worsening. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, and it is exacerbated by gripping objects. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is interfering with his ability to perform daily activities, such as writing and opening jars. He denies any numbness or tingling. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain, which provides some relief but not complete resolution.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the distal volar aspect of the left hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. There is no swelling or erythema. Strength is intact. Tinel's sign is negative.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with lateral carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Order nerve conduction studies to evaluate for carpal tunnel syndrome. Recommend wrist splinting and activity modification. Counsel the patient on the importance of avoiding repetitive hand movements. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
