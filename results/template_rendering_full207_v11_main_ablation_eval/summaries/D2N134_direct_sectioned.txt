Chief Complaint
Abnormal lab finding, positive Lyme titer.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing fatigue and joint pain, particularly in the elbows, for several weeks. She attributes these symptoms to a recent hiking trip in wooded areas, where she believes she acquired a tick bite. She also reports nausea and decreased appetite. She has been taking metformin for type 2 diabetes and lisinopril for hypertension. She reports monitoring her blood pressure at home and has been limiting her salt intake. She notes occasional fluctuations in her blood sugar levels, sometimes experiencing both hyperglycemia and hypoglycemia.

Findings
Physical examination reveals swelling of the bilateral elbow joints, which are tender to palpation. A bull's-eye rash is observed on the left abdomen, which is warm to the touch but not painful. Vital signs are stable, with no fever detected. Laboratory results confirm a positive Lyme titer.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and laboratory findings are consistent with Lyme disease.

Plan
Prescribe doxycycline 100 mg twice daily for three weeks. Order a hemoglobin A1c to assess glycemic control. Order a lipid panel. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to monitor treatment response and address any further concerns. Refer to infectious disease for further management and to discuss additional testing.
