Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a fall yesterday while trying to change a light bulb on a ladder. He states he 'kinda stumbled and kinda twisted' his knee. He denies any other injuries. He has been applying ice and taking ibuprofen, but reports minimal relief. He has a history of afib and takes digoxin. He also had a nose job in the past. He's been doing okay, except for his knee.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the right knee, particularly when stressing the joint. Range of motion is limited by pain, with increased pain on forward flexion and valgus stress. Neurological exam reveals intact sensation. X-ray shows no fractures but demonstrates some fluid in the knee, with no dislocation or other abnormalities.

Assessment
Acute medial meniscus sprain or strain of the right knee.

Plan
Prescribe a knee brace and crutches for the next two weeks. Recommend ibuprofen 800 mg every six hours as needed. Order a two-week supply of ibuprofen. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to reassess symptoms and consider an MRI if symptoms do not improve.
