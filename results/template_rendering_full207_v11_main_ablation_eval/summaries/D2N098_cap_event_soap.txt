S:
The patient reports constant pain in his left shoulder and elbow for approximately five months, stemming from a fall five months ago. He states the pain is persistent and often severe, particularly when lifting weights. He also reports that he has stopped going to the gym due to the pain, finding it difficult to lift more than 15-20 pounds. The patient denies any numbness or tingling.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the left elbow and shoulder, with no warmth, erythema, or deformity. Hawkins-Kennedy and Neer's tests are positive. Proximal and distal upper extremity strength is intact. The empty can test is normal. Gait is stable.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with impingement syndrome of the left shoulder. The fall likely contributed to the injury.

P:
A referral is requested for Dr. Martha Rivera to provide physical therapy twice weekly. The doctor suggests a cortisone injection. The doctor advises the patient to ice and rest, and to follow up if symptoms worsen or do not improve.
