S:
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his hands, particularly in the right hand, for approximately one month following a car accident last December. He describes the tingling as 'dull' and the pain as 'sore,' with occasional throbbing. He also reports mild stiffness in his neck since the accident, attributing it to a contusion. He states that the symptoms are more pronounced at night and fluctuate throughout the day. He has been taking nerve pain medicine but hasn't been consistent with his medication regimen. He also reports mild bloating and excessive appetite.

O:
Physical exam reveals decreased sensation to light touch in the third and fourth digits of the right hand. Tinel's signs are positive at the wrists and elbows bilaterally. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric. Blood pressure is slightly low. Heart rate is 78 bpm and regular. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome, potentially complicated by cubital tunnel syndrome. The history of trauma supports a possible etiology.

P:
I have ordered an EMG to further evaluate the nerve conduction studies. I have also scheduled a referral to an orthopedist for consideration of a carpal tunnel release or other surgical interventions if conservative management fails. I have advised the patient to continue wearing his splints and will schedule a follow-up visit in two weeks to assess progress.
