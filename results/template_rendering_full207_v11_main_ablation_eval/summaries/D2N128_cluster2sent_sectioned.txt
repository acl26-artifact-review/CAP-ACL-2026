Chief Complaint
The patient reports increased osteoarthritis flares and seeks follow-up.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports increased osteoarthritis flares recently, attributing them to prolonged computer use and sedentary office work. He also reports experiencing numbness and tingling in his hands with prolonged typing, and has a history of gout that was previously well-controlled with medication. He has been using clobetasol cream for his scalp and reports no cervical lymphadenopathy. He denies systemic inflammatory processes.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a regular rate and rhythm, clear lung sounds, and no lower extremity edema. The right elbow demonstrates edema and inflammation of the olecranon with tenderness and effusion, and pain with flexion and extension. Palpation of the olecranon bursa elicits pain with pronation and supination. The abdomen is soft and non-tender.

Assessment
The patient's impression is stable gout, with ongoing osteoarthritis flares.

Plan
Continue the patient on colchicine 0.6 milligrams twice a day as needed for gout flares, and order a right elbow x-ray. Prescribe meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily for pain and swelling. Continue the steroid cream for the scalp. We will review the x-ray results before considering physical therapy referral.
