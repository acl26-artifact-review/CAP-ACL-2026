S:
The patient reports knee pain that has been increasing over the past few days, particularly after activity. He describes the pain as sharp and localized, and reports it is present bilaterally, though more pronounced on the right. He denies any recent injury. He reports stiffness in the mornings and some soreness by the end of the day. He denies fever, chills, nausea, or vomiting. He reports no chest pain or shortness of breath. He is currently taking Crestor and aspirin as prescribed.

O:
Blood pressure is 130/80 mmHg. Heart rate is 72 bpm. Temperature is 98.6°F. Right knee examination reveals mild edema and erythema, with slight effusion noted. Palpation of the right knee elicits tenderness. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are normal. Right knee X-ray shows no acute bony abnormalities. EKG shows normal sinus rhythm.

A:
The patient's primary concern is right knee pain, which is likely related to a flare of his underlying lupus. Further investigation with an autoimmune panel and echocardiogram is warranted to assess the extent of the inflammation and rule out cardiac complications.

P:
Order an autoimmune panel to evaluate for lupus activity. Order an echocardiogram to assess cardiac function. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to review the results and discuss further management strategies. Counsel the patient on the importance of adherence to his medications and lifestyle modifications, including avoiding overexertion and maintaining a healthy weight.
