Chief Complaint
Headache

History of Present Illness
Carolyn reports a headache that started approximately one week ago, described as a dull pain in the back of her head. She also experiences flushing in her ears and occasional dizziness with the headaches. She has taken Tylenol and Advil, but the headaches persist and return. The headache is worse in the evening. She attributes the onset to increased stress levels. She denies a history of trauma. She has had headaches before, but this one is not the worst she's experienced. She hasn't been checking her blood sugars regularly. She has not been eating well due to stress and hasn't been exercising for about a week, but generally she's been doing better. She reports no fever associated with the headaches.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no abnormalities except for some posterior mild paraspinal muscular tenderness in the cervical spine and bilateral trapezius musculature, with tightness in those muscles. Extraocular movements are intact. Funduscopic exam shows no papilledema. Heart exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, unchanged from prior exam. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. The patient is working on the computer for approximately 8 hours per day.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a tension-type headache, likely exacerbated by stress and musculoskeletal tension. Her history of diabetes mellitus type two is also present.

Plan
I am prescribing flexeril 5 mg three times daily for muscle relaxation. I am ordering an A1c and a CBC and a chem-12 to monitor her diabetes. I will continue her metformin 500 mg twice daily. I recommend she check her blood sugars daily. I recommend she return in one month for follow-up, or sooner if the headaches worsen or become more severe. I'd like to get her back into an exercise routine to help manage her blood sugars. Let's schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks. Please call if you have any questions or concerns. ,
