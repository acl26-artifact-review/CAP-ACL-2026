Chief Complaint
Right index finger pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right index finger pain following a skiing injury sustained last Saturday. He describes falling and jamming his finger while skiing at double diamonds and double black diamonds. He reported five days of pain and denies any other injuries. He took ibuprofen, which did not provide significant relief. He denies icing or splinting the finger.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the distal interphalangeal joint of the right index finger. Pain is elicited with flexion, extension, and palpation of the DIP joint. Radiographic examination demonstrates no fractures or bony abnormalities; the right hand appears normal.

Assessment
Sprain of the distal interphalangeal joint of the right index finger.

Plan
Prescribe a strong antiinflammatory medication, mobic, 15 milligrams once daily for 14 days. Order a splint for the right index finger. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess healing and consider referral to hand therapy if indicated. Dragon, please order the medications and procedures mentioned.
