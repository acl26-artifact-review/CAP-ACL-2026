The patient reports sudden visual distortions and blurring of vision. He has experienced a decrease in vision when reading and straight lines in doorframes have appeared crooked to him. He reports keeping up with his blood pressure medication.

The examination reveals posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes. The right eye cornea is clear, with no micro-vias, pigment clumping, or intraocular lenses. The cup to disc ratio is 0.2 in both eyes. The fundus examination of the right eye shows the retina attached without tears or holes or peripapillary changes. The fovea is unremarkable. The left eye cornea is clear, with no micro-vias. The cup to disc ratio is 0.3 in the left eye. There are no peripapillary changes. OCT shows drusen deposits and retinal pigment epithelium subretinal fluid accumulation. Fluorescein angiography reveals focal areas of leakage in the right eye consistent with neovascular age-related macular degeneration. The patient's blood pressure was measured at [value].

A:
The patient is diagnosed with neovascular age-related macular degeneration. The findings are indicative of advanced disease with potential for significant vision loss.

P:
The patient will receive monthly injections of Lucentis into the vitreous portion of the eye. The injections will be administered after numbing the eye. Follow-up will be scheduled to monitor disease progression and vision changes. The patient will be counselled on the potential for treatment to slow vision loss but also the possibility of continued disease progression despite treatment.
