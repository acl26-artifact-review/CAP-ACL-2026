Chief Complaint
Difficulty urinating and prostate concerns.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty urinating, with no reports of burning during urination. He denies prior episodes of urinary retention. He reports experiencing diarrhea last week, attributing it to a possible food sensitivity. He is currently taking metformin as prescribed and reports uncertainty about its effectiveness. He also reports a passion for watching sporting events, specifically needing to be able to sit comfortably while doing so.

Findings
A slight three out of six systolic ejection murmur is auscultated at the left base. The lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. The abdomen is nontender and nondistended. An enlarged prostate is palpated, without any masses or guarding.

Assessment
The patient presents with difficulty urinating and an enlarged prostate, requiring further evaluation and management.

Plan
Order PSA, flomax 0.4 mg daily, and a referral to a urologist. Order routine blood tests and a urinalysis/urine culture. Order an echocardiogram to follow up on the heart murmur. Continue current medications: lipitor 40 mg daily, aspirin, and metoprolol. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three to four weeks, encouraging the patient to contact the clinic or use the patient portal for any concerns.
