Chief Complaint
Right knee pain for approximately six months.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain that began six months ago, with no identifiable traumatic event. He describes the pain as a deep, achy sensation, particularly exacerbated by sitting and standing, and when going up and down stairs. Ibuprofen and acetaminophen provide some relief but do not eliminate the pain. The patient denies any prior injuries or fractures. He reports being a runner and biker, but has significantly reduced his running distance due to the pain. He works from home, with a desk setup upstairs, and commutes downstairs.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal gait and strength, with a three out of five abduction score bilaterally. Palpation elicits tenderness in the knees. The patellar grind test is positive upon standing. Vitals are within normal limits: blood pressure 120/70, heart rate 60, respiratory rate 14. Radiographs show no fractures or osteoarthritis. MRI prior to presentation is reviewed, showing no significant findings.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome.

Plan
Recommend physical therapy to address lower extremity strength and running mechanics. Continue ibuprofen or acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
