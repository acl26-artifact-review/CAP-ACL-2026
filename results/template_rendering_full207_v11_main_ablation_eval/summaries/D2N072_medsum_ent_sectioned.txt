Chief Complaint
Right finger pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right finger pain that began following a skiing accident over the weekend, where he got his strap caught on his finger while moguls jumping. He describes bending the finger back during the incident. He has tried applying ice and taking ibuprofen, but the pain persists. The patient reports the pain is exacerbated by movement. He has a history of constipation and reports taking miralax for it. He had his appendix removed at age 18.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness over the distal phalanx of the index finger, and tenderness over the joint itself. There is no tenderness over the proximal or metacarpophalangeal joints. The patient demonstrates pain with flexion. X-ray imaging reveals no fractures and appears normal.

Assessment
The patient has a right hand index finger contusion on the tip of the finger.

Plan
We recommend an aluminum foam splint and motrin 600 milligrams every six hours for approximately one week. If the pain does not improve after a week, we will schedule a follow-up appointment.
