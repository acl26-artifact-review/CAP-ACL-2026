Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent, dull chest pain for the past month, described as a tightness rather than sharp. The pain is not consistently triggered by exertion, but he does notice it more when he is stressed or lying down. He denies shortness of breath, palpitations, or recent trauma. He reports taking ibuprofen as needed for the pain, which provides minimal relief. He denies any history of heart disease or family history of cardiac issues.

Findings
Blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, regular rhythm. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Cardiac auscultation reveals no murmurs. The patient denies edema.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are concerning for musculoskeletal chest pain, but require evaluation to rule out cardiac etiologies.

Plan
Order ECG and cardiac enzymes (Troponin). Advise patient to continue ibuprofen as needed for pain, but to monitor for any worsening symptoms. Schedule follow-up in 24-48 hours to review results and assess stability.
