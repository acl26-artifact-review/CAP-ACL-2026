S:
The patient reports right elbow pain starting approximately one week ago due to playing lacrosse with his children. He describes the pain as sharp and present with movement, radiating down his forearm. He took Advil for a couple of days, which provided some relief. He denies any recent falls or direct impact to the elbow.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the olecranon process of the right elbow, mild pain with flexion and extension, and normal range of motion. An x-ray showed no fracture or bony abnormality. The patient's blood pressure was elevated at 150/90 mmHg.

A:
The patient's right elbow pain is most likely due to a contusion and inflammation, consistent with the reported mechanism of injury.

P:
Rescribe 800 mg ibuprofen twice daily for pain and inflammation. Recommend icing the elbow three times daily for 20 minutes. Advise limiting lacrosse activity for two weeks to allow for healing. Schedule an A1C test to assess diabetes control and consider adjusting metformin dosage based on results. Continue current lisinopril dosage.
