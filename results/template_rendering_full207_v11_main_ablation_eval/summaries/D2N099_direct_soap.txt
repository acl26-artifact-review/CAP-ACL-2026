S:
The patient reports a runny nose and stuffy nose starting around supper time last night. He also reports ear pulling and nasal rubbing. He has been irritable and has difficulty sleeping, fighting to go to sleep. He reports a rectal temperature of 100.3. He has been drinking approximately seven ounces of formula at one feeding and barely any at another this morning. His appetite has been variable. His older sister has been sick. He has been exposed to a playdate with a nephew who had a sinus infection.

O:
Physical exam reveals clear mucous. Examination of the ears reveals no acute findings. Vital signs are within normal limits except for the reported temperature of 100.3. The skin is warm to the touch.

A:
The patient is likely experiencing a viral upper respiratory infection, possibly secondary to exposure to a viral illness. The symptoms are consistent with a common cold or early stage of a sinus infection.

P:
Administer saline nasal mist. Provide Tylenol 65mg/5mL as needed for fever and discomfort. Recommend Zarbee's chest rub as needed for nasal congestion. Prescribe baby oil for cradle cap. Arrange follow-up in one week to reassess symptoms and monitor for progression. Advise to contact clinic if cough worsens to a barking cough, as this may indicate a bacterial infection. Continue to monitor for fever and irritability.
