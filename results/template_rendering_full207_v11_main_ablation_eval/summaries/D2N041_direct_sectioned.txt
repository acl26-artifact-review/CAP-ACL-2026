Chief Complaint
Sore throat and difficulty swallowing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a sore throat for four days, accompanied by difficulty swallowing and a fever last night. He denies chills but reports sweating. He also notes some nasal congestion but no other systemic symptoms. He reports that his tonsils appear swollen. He has no history of strep throat as an adult. He reports a recent COVID vaccine that made him feel sick for two days. He is currently taking lisinopril 20mg daily, metformin 1000mg twice daily, and vitamin D daily.

Findings
Vital signs reveal a low-grade fever of 100.4°F, blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, and heart rate 84 bpm. Physical exam reveals bilateral erythema and edema of the peritonsillar space with exudates, cervical lymphadenopathy on the right, and coarse rhonchi at the bases of the lungs. The patient reports no pain to palpation of the frontal or maxillary sinuses.

Assessment
The patient presents with signs of an upper respiratory infection, likely exacerbated by viral illness, with possible streptococcal involvement. Hypertension and diabetes are also present.

Plan
A rapid strep test will be performed. If positive, amoxicillin 500mg three times daily for ten days will be prescribed with lidocaine swish and swallow for symptomatic relief. Lisinopril will be increased to 40mg daily. A lipid panel and hemoglobin A1c will be ordered. Follow-up will be scheduled in four weeks if symptoms persist. Continue current medications as directed.
