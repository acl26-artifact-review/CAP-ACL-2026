Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain that began approximately one week ago following a gymnastics class incident, specifically during a maneuver that caused a fall. He denies taking any pain medications, having previously tried a Tylenol for two or three days without relief. He describes pain with any movement of the left shoulder, including elevation and rotation. He reports a history of left arm surgery, but believes this is unrelated to the current pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited range of motion in the left shoulder, with pain on active abduction and impingement testing. Radiographic examination demonstrates no evidence of fracture or acute bony injury.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with a musculoskeletal injury, likely involving the rotator cuff or subacromial space.

Plan
Recommend continued use of ibuprofen 600 mg three times daily for one week, with ice or heat as needed. Advise rest of the left arm for at least three weeks. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to assess progress or consider repeat imaging if symptoms persist. Counsel the patient to avoid heavy lifting or overhead activities during this period.
