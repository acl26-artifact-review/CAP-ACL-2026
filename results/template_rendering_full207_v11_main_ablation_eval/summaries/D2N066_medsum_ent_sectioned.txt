Chief Complaint
Back pain evaluation and left leg pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain following bending over to pick something up in the yard, accompanied by pain radiating down his left leg for approximately four days. He attributes this to working in the yard, specifically due to his wife's insistence on having him do yard work. He has experienced occasional back pain over the years but this episode is more acute. He reports some tingling in his legs but primarily pain in certain positions. He has tried ibuprofen with some relief. He is currently taking metformin 500mg twice daily for diabetes and hydrochlorothiazide for high blood pressure, reporting occasional ankle swelling.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation in the left paraspinal area. Straight leg raise is negative. Neurological exam is intact. One plus edema is noted in the lower extremities. Lung exam is clear. Heart exam reveals a two over six systolic ejection murmur, which is stable. No rales or wheezing present. Neck exam is unremarkable. Examination reveals no bruits.

Assessment
The patient's primary concern is a muscular strain of the lower back, likely exacerbated by the recent activity. He has a history of diabetes and hypertension, contributing to his overall clinical picture.

Plan
I'm ordering an HbA1c to assess his diabetes control. I'm also ordering a chest x-ray and a CMP to evaluate his kidney function and rule out other causes of the edema. I'm recommending physical therapy to address the back pain and strengthen the muscles. I'm prescribing naproxen 500mg twice daily and flexeril 10mg twice daily for pain and muscle relaxation. I'll also prescribe hydrochlorothiazide 10mg once daily for the edema. We will schedule a follow-up in two weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
