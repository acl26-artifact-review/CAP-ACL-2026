Chief Complaint
Irritable bowel syndrome with functional abdominal pain and nausea.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) with functional abdominal pain and nausea. She was previously diagnosed with IBS in 2019 and treated with bentyl. She reports a previous prescription for reglan that she used as needed for nausea. She has been eating out frequently, particularly on Friday and Saturday nights, and consuming spicy foods and spicy shrimp. She reports experiencing both lower belly cramps and upper belly nausea. She notes a change in bowel movement frequency, reporting it to be between two and three times a day. She has been using citrucel regularly, but feels it needs to be replaced. She has been avoiding heavy foods and greasy foods. She reports bloating. She prefers Patron tequila and dark liquor, often with sweet mixers.

Findings
The patient is alert and cooperative, appears stated age. Heart and lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. The abdomen is soft, non-tender, and has normal active bowel sounds. No masses or organomegaly are palpated.

Assessment
The patient presents with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) characterized by abdominal pain, nausea, and altered bowel habits. The symptoms are exacerbated by spicy foods, alcohol, and potentially portion sizes.

Plan
Start citrucel twice daily for regularity. Recommend bentyl 20mg as needed 30-45 minutes before eating out, and repeat up to four times daily. Advise patient to reduce greasy and fatty foods and alcohol consumption. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to assess response to treatment and discuss the possibility of initiating a long-term neuromodulator such as elavil if symptoms persist. Provide a prescription for bentyl and citrucel. Advise patient to monitor symptoms and report any side effects to mychart.
