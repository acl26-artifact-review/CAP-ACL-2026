Chief Complaint
Severe right upper arm pain following a volleyball injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right upper arm pain that began approximately 18 hours ago after spiking a volleyball. He describes falling while attempting the spike, landing directly on his arm, and experiencing immediate, severe pain. He has been taking ibuprofen every six hours, but reports minimal relief. He denies any prior injuries to the arm. He has gallstones and takes pepcid for it. He had a lumbar fusion six years ago.

Findings
Examination reveals swelling and erythema over the right shoulder, tenderness over the right shoulder, normal pulses, and no reported numbness or tingling in the right arm. X-ray demonstrates a proximal humerus fracture.

Assessment
Proximal humerus fracture.

Plan
Prescribe lortab 5mg every 6 hours as needed for pain, providing a prescription for 20 tablets. Order a long arm cast and sling. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess fracture alignment. Refer to physical therapy.
