Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain and swelling following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain and swelling following a fall. He has been icing and taking ibuprofen, which provides some relief. He denies prior ankle injuries and reports no numbness in his foot. He desires to return to soccer.

Findings
Physical examination reveals ecchymosis and swelling over the lateral malleolus. Palpation elicits tenderness. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong and capillary refill is less than three seconds. Right ankle x-ray demonstrates no fracture.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a right ankle sprain.

Plan
The patient will receive an aircast and crutches for the next two days. He should keep his leg elevated and continue icing. Follow up in two weeks to assess improvement, and continue NSAIDs as needed.
