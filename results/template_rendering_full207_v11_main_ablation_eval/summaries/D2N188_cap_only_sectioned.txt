Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall while mountain biking three years ago. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, rated 6/10. He denies any recent trauma. He reports some difficulty with weight-bearing and limping. The patient also reports numbness and tingling in his left foot, and a crackling sound when he moves his ankle. He takes Tylenol as needed for the pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the right lateral malleolus. The patient demonstrates mild limpness. Neurological exam reveals decreased sensation to light touch in the dorsal aspect of the left foot and decreased vibration sensation. Range of motion of the right ankle is limited due to pain.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with an acute right ankle sprain with associated neurovascular symptoms.

Plan
The doctor orders an air cast for immobilization. The doctor recommends icing the area for twenty minutes five times a day. The doctor prescribes meloxicam fifteen milligrams once a day. The doctor orders crutches for immediate weight-bearing. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
