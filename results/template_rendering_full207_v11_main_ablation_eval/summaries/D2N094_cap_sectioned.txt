Chief Complaint
Pain in the right index finger following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his right index finger following a skiing injury last Saturday. He describes falling and jamming his finger during the incident. He reports approximately five days of pain and rates it as a 7 out of 10. He denies any other injuries. He took ibuprofen, but it did not provide significant relief. He also reports chronic constipation and takes miralax, which helps. He had his appendix removed when he was seven years old.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the right index finger. The x-ray shows no fractures or other abnormalities in the right hand.

Assessment
The patient has a sprain of the distal interphalangeal joint (DIP) of the right index finger.

Plan
The doctor will apply a splint to the right index finger. The patient will take 15 milligrams of mobic once a day. The doctor will prescribe 14 tablets of mobic. The patient will return for follow-up in two weeks. Order the medications and procedures mentioned.
