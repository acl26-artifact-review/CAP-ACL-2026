S:
The patient reports a two-year history of back pain, initially following a weekend moving furniture. He has tried physical therapy and strong pain medications with limited success. He describes the pain as sharp and stabbing, radiating down his right leg to his big toe. He also reports numbness and tingling in his right leg and foot, and difficulty getting comfortable when sitting. He has gained some weight over the past years due to difficulty with activity with his back pain and previously enjoyed playing golf, but now finds it difficult to do so. He quit smoking twenty years ago.

O:
Vitals are stable. Musculoskeletal exam reveals no overlying erythema. Midline tenderness is present at L4-L5 with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness. Straight leg raise test is positive. Patellar and Achilles reflexes are symmetrical. MRI reveals a disk herniation with nerve impingement at L4-L5.

A:
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with an L4-L5 herniated disc with radiculopathy, causing nerve impingement and resulting in pain, numbness, and tingling in the leg and foot.

P:
I am recommending a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and alleviate pain. The injection will be performed under fluoroscopy to ensure accurate localization.
