S:
The patient reports experiencing neck pain following a car accident approximately one week ago. He describes the pain as a 7/10, constant but exacerbated by movement, particularly with head rotation and flexion. He reports some headaches early on, and the pain seems to radiate to the upper back and shoulders. He denies any swelling or bruising on his neck. He reports that he did not go to the emergency room after the accident due to the cost of a visit. He states he can mostly turn his neck, but experiences pain with the movement. He reports experiencing pain with lateral bending and with touching his chin to his chest. He denies any hearing problems or visual disturbances.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the neck muscles. Range of motion is limited with flexion and extension. Neurological exam is unremarkable. Blood pressure is slightly low.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with whiplash-associated neck pain. There are no signs of fracture or bony abnormalities on the x-ray. A potential concussion cannot be ruled out and warrants further investigation with an MRI.

P:
I am prescribing motrin 600mg every 6-8 hours and flexeril 10mg every 12 hours as needed. I have ordered an MRI to evaluate for potential concussion. I am referring the patient to physical rehabilitation or a chiropractor for further evaluation and treatment once the MRI results are available.
