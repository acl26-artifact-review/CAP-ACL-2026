Chief Complaint
Patient reports a history of kidney stones and seeks evaluation and management.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of recurrent kidney stones, with the most recent stone passing approximately three days ago. He previously presented to the emergency room last week with similar symptoms. He describes the pain as intermittent, with periods of improvement and worsening. He reports having taken percocet for pain relief at the hospital. He has a history of hypertension and diabetes mellitus. He reports difficulty with dietary adherence, particularly with fast food consumption. He has not seen a urologist in a while. He also reports insomnia and has been using meditation apps to help with sleep.

Findings
Blood pressure was elevated at 150/90 mmHg. The patient reports regular norvasc use at home, with blood pressures typically around 130-140/70-80 mmHg. A grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur was noted on heart exam. Physical exam revealed no abdominal tenderness or pain. Lung exam was clear bilaterally. The patient's A1c was 6.7% at the last visit, down from a previous value. The patient's blood sugar readings have been variable, with no consistent monitoring. Examination revealed no findings of note.

Assessment
The patient has a history of recurrent kidney stones, hypertension, and diabetes mellitus. Current symptoms suggest a recent stone passage, with no acute complications.

Plan
Order a urinalysis and urine culture to rule out infection. Refer the patient to urology for further evaluation and management of the recurrent kidney stones. Continue norvasc at 2.5 mg daily. Counsel the patient on dietary modifications, including reducing sodium intake and limiting fast food consumption; consider referral to a registered dietitian. Prescribe 10 mg melatonin as needed for insomnia, emphasizing proper sleep hygiene practices. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months to reassess symptoms and adjust management as needed.
