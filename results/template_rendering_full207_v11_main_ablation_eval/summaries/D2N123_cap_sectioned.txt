Chief Complaint
Difficulty swallowing and a feeling of food getting stuck in the throat.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a gradual onset of difficulty swallowing over the past four to five weeks. He describes a sensation of food getting stuck in his throat, particularly with solid foods, and occasionally feels a mild pain. He reports that he has never fully choked but has experienced a sense of needing to 'push' food down. He notes that liquids are generally easier to swallow, but still sometimes feel like they get stuck. He denies any recent trauma or significant changes in his swallowing pattern. He reports no nausea, vomiting, or blood in his stool or emesis. He has experienced no weight loss. He reports that his diabetes is well-controlled with metformin and his blood pressure is managed with norvasc.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no visible swelling or masses in the neck. The thyroid is normal in palpation. The patient's neck and oral cavity are examined, revealing no redness or abnormalities. Lung exam is clear. Heart exam is normal, with no murmurs. The patient's abdomen is soft and non-tender. Neurological exam is normal. Tenderness is noted to epigastric palpation. Blood pressure is 120/50 mmHg. The patient reports checking his blood sugars approximately 80% of the time, and the numbers are within target range.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms of difficulty swallowing are concerning for potential esophageal narrowing or stricture. Further investigation is warranted to rule out structural abnormalities and assess the underlying cause.

Plan
I am ordering a referral to gastroenterology for a scope to evaluate the esophagus. I am also recommending that you start taking prilosec 20 milligrams once a day to help with potential reflux. I recommend you chew your foods thoroughly, especially if they are hard, to prevent them from getting stuck. I will order a hemoglobin a1c test and some more blood work to assess kidney function. I am also ordering an eye referral to ophthalmology for a diabetic retinopathy screening. I will send a refill for your norvasc to your pharmacy. Please follow up with me in one month to discuss the results of the tests and assess your progress.
