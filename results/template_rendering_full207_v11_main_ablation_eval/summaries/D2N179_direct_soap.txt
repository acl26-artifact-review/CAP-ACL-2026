S:
The patient reports numbness and tingling in both hands, beginning approximately four weeks ago following a car accident last December. He describes the tingling and numbness as more pronounced since the accident, with worsening over the past month. He also reports mild neck stiffness following the head injury sustained during the accident. He denies loss of sensation. He reports experiencing the symptoms more frequently at night and throughout the day. He has been inconsistently adherent to his prescribed nerve pain medication.

O:
Physical exam reveals diminished pinprick sensation in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand and decreased sensation in the fourth and fifth fingers of the left hand. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric. Tinel signs are positive at both wrists and both elbows. The right wrist elicits a zing sensation up the wrist and into the elbow. The left wrist elicits a similar sensation.

A:
The patient's presentation is consistent with bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome, potentially complicated by cubital tunnel syndrome. The symptoms are likely related to the past trauma sustained in the car accident.

P:
Order an EMG to determine the specific nerve involvement and rule out cubital tunnel syndrome. Schedule a follow-up appointment after the EMG results are available. Consider referral to a hand clinic for potential carpal tunnel release surgery if conservative measures are unsuccessful. Continue wearing splints as instructed.
