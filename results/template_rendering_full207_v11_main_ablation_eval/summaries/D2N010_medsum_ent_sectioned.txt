Chief Complaint
High blood sugar and concerns about blood sugar control.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling his blood sugar is not under control, noting a slight, creeping increase over the past few weeks. He reports eating a similar diet, with no significant changes. He has experienced some joint pain, specifically in his right knee, which he noticed after mentioning the knee issue. He reports occasional nausea a couple of days ago, attributed to sitting in a car. He has a history of reflux, which he reports is currently better with protonix. He denies fever or chills. He reports no burning when urinating. He has a history of congestive heart failure and reports no recent weight gain or fluid retention. He prefers to sleep on his side, making it difficult to assess flat lying symptoms. He is concerned about alpha-gal syndrome, particularly given his recent meat consumption.

Findings
Physical exam reveals erythema and fluctuants in the right knee. Lower extremity edema is present on the right. The thyroid is slightly enlarged. The heart rhythm is regular. Lungs are clear to auscultation. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. The lyme titer is elevated. Rapid strep test is positive. Vital signs are stable.

Assessment
The patient presents with hyperglycemia, likely multifactorial in etiology, potentially exacerbated by an inflammatory response. Clinical findings suggest possible infection, as evidenced by the elevated lyme titer and positive rapid strep test.

Plan
Order a Western blot PCR for lyme and amoxicillin 500 mg three times daily for 10 days. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and further investigate the elevated lyme titer. The nurse will provide education regarding medication adherence and potential side effects. Arrange for a referral to physical therapy for targeted rehabilitation of the right knee.
