Chief Complaint
Ankle injury presenting with pain, swelling, and instability.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports an ankle injury sustained while playing basketball. He describes a fall resulting in a twisting injury, accompanied by immediate pain, swelling, and bruising. He has experienced difficulty walking and a sense of instability in the ankle. He had recently returned to a regular exercise routine, including gym workouts, and is concerned about disrupting this progress. He also volunteers as a coach and is scheduled to begin coaching in April.

Findings
Physical examination reveals significant swelling on the lateral aspect of the ankle. Palpation elicits tenderness over the lateral malleolus. Range of motion is limited due to pain. The ankle demonstrates instability upon weight-bearing. Capillary refill is brisk. An AP lateral x-ray demonstrates a displaced lateral malleolus at the Weber C level with no evidence of medial or posterior malleolar fractures.

Assessment
Lateral malleolar fracture.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam for pain and inflammation. Recommend ice and elevation of the ankle. Instruct the patient to use crutches for weight-bearing. Recommend surgical intervention for the lateral malleolar fracture. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months to assess healing and progress, and to continue coaching activities as tolerated.
