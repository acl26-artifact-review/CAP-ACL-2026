Chief Complaint
Follow-up for elevated blood pressure and associated symptoms, including headache and lightheadedness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent, elevated blood pressure readings over the past month, with the most recent reading in the emergency room reaching 190. He reports a headache and lightheadedness experienced yesterday during a walk. He attributes this to travel for work, noting that he does not typically bring his blood pressure cuff when traveling. He is currently taking lisinopril and reports attending weekly therapy sessions for the past year, supported by a strong social network including his boyfriend, mother, and brother. He denies any blood in stool or chest pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a slight two out of six systolic ejection murmur, trace pitting edema to the lower extremities bilaterally, and carotid bruits. The EKG demonstrates changes consistent with chronic high blood pressure. The echocardiogram shows a slightly leaky heart valve and stable murmur. Lung exam is clear bilaterally.

Assessment
The patient presents with hypertension and associated symptoms, including headache and lightheadedness. No acute cardiac abnormalities are identified on examination.

Plan
I am increasing the lisinopril dosage to 40 milligrams once daily. I recommend the patient monitor and report blood pressures through the patient portal. I will order an anemia profile to evaluate the reported fatigue. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and adjust as needed.
