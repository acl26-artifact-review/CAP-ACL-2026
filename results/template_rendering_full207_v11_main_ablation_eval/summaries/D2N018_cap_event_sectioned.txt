Chief Complaint
Severe right upper arm pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports severe right upper arm pain that began approximately 18 hours ago following a volleyball injury. He describes landing on his arm after being kicked during a spike attempt. He has been taking ibuprofen every six hours, but reports it is not providing significant relief. He rates his pain as a nine out of ten. He denies numbness. He has a history of lumbar fusion six years ago and reports having gallstones.

Findings
Examination reveals swelling and erythema on the right shoulder. Tenderness is noted over the right shoulder. Normal pulses. Elbow flexibility is limited, only able to straighten the arm as long as he does not move his shoulder.

Assessment
Clinical findings are consistent with a proximal humerus fracture.

Plan
Prescribe lortab 5mg/500mg tablets, to be taken every six hours as needed for pain. Order a long arm cast and sling. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess healing and adjust treatment as needed.
