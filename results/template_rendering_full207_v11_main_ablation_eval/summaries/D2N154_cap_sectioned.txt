Chief Complaint
Pain in the heel, typically after a workout or when first getting up in the morning.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the back of his heel, typically after a workout or when first getting up in the morning, describing it as a really tight sensation almost like tearing. This pain is present in the morning and eases up after about 10-15 minutes of walking, but it returns when sitting for extended periods and then trying to get up again. He also reports soreness in his leg and tightness in his leg. He does not stretch and does not work the lower legs to get them stretched out. The patient is training for a triathlon and cycles for approximately 45 minutes to an hour and swims for approximately 30 minutes to an hour. He runs approximately 10 kilometers five days a week, alternating between a long run and a long bike, and does not really do a long swim.

Findings
Examination reveals pain with palpation of the right heel and with dorsiflexion of the foot. The Thompson test is negative. The patient reports pain on the right side when pushing against the doctor's foot.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with Achilles tendinitis.

Plan
The doctor will order a physical therapy appointment and schedule a follow-up appointment in 7 to 10 days. The doctor recommends taking ibuprofen 3 tablets every 6 hours. The doctor recommends stopping training for two weeks and will discharge the patient.
