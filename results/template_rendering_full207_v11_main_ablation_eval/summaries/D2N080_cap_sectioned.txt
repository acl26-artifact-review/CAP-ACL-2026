Chief Complaint
Left knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports intermittent left knee pain that occurs occasionally, less than once a day, when walking. He describes a sensation of 'giving out' and usually catches himself. The patient often uses holding grocery carts to stabilize the knee. The pain lasts only a short time and does not interfere with daily activities. He has no history of knee injury. He is planning a trip to Georgia in the next month where he will be doing line dancing, and he does not think he will be able to participate due to the knee. The patient reports pain in the left knee.

Findings
Vital signs are stable: temperature 98.2°F, pulse 72, respirations 16, blood pressure 120/70. The patient's blood pressures are running around the same as before. The patient is taking farxiga and amlodipine and lisinopril and hydrochlorothiazide. His blood sugars have been running a little higher at about 130 in the morning. The patient reports pain upon palpation of the left knee. Examination reveals no ecchymosis or edema, no effusion, and no pain to palpation. Range of motion is full. A slight two over six systolic ejection murmur is heard at the left base. The patient denies pain with varus or valgus stress.

Assessment
The patient has arthritis in the left knee. The patient has diabetes mellitus. The patient has hypertension.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily. Recommend physical therapy. Order a hemoglobin A1C test. Order a lipid panel. Order an echocardiogram. Continue lisinopril at 20 mg daily. Schedule follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
