S:
The patient reports pain in the right middle finger for approximately five days following a rear-end motor vehicle accident. He describes the pain as 'very much so' and indicates it is exacerbated by palpation over the distal phalanx. He also reports a small amount of tenderness at the base of the middle finger. The patient states he was rear-ended while stopped at a four-way stop sign and that the other driver was texting.

O:
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal phalanx of the right middle finger. No other significant findings on physical exam. X-ray demonstrates a distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger.

A:
The patient has a distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger, likely secondary to blunt trauma.

P:
I am prescribing tramadol 50 mg every six hours as needed for pain for two days. I am also ordering a finger splint to be worn. The patient will have a follow-up x-ray in two weeks.
