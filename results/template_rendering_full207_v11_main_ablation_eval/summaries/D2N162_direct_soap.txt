S:
The patient reports right ankle pain and swelling following a fall on icy pavement yesterday. He describes slipping and being very careful, but still sustaining the injury. He has been icing and taking ibuprofen. He denies numbness in his foot. He has no recollection of prior ankle injuries but admits to playing soccer for many years, including currently for the community league. His wife observes he is unable to play currently due to his injury. He reports his oldest child is starting soccer soon.

O:
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis and swelling over the lateral malleolus. Capillary refill is less than three seconds. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong. Sensation is intact to light touch. Right ankle x-ray shows no fracture.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a right ankle sprain. There is no fracture.

P:
I recommend keeping the leg elevated, applying ice, and using an aircast for stability. Prescribe crutches for the next one to two days. Advise the patient to start walking as tolerated. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress. Continue NSAID use as needed for pain management.
