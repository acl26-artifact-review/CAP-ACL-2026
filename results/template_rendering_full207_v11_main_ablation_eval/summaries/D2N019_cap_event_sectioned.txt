Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began yesterday after moving boxes from a truck into the house. He states he felt a pop and strain while lifting a box and placing it on a shelf. He has been taking ibuprofen for the pain, which provides some relief, but the pain persists and throbs when the ice is removed. He denies any prior injuries to the elbow. He reports a history of anaphylaxis and has an epinephrine pen for that condition. He also had a carotid surgery.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral epicondyle of the right elbow. There is some swelling and redness present. The patient reports pain with flexion and extension of the elbow, and pain on the dorsal aspect of the forearm. Range of motion is limited due to pain. The x-ray shows some swelling but no fracture.

Assessment
The patient's presentation and examination findings are consistent with lateral epicondylitis.

Plan
The doctor orders a sling for comfort. The doctor orders motrin. Follow up in one week if symptoms do not improve.
