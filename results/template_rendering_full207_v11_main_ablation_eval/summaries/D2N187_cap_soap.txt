S:
The patient reports visual distortion, difficulty adjusting to dim lights, and blurred vision. He reports smoking a pack of cigarettes a day and reports remembering to check his blood pressure occasionally. The patient reports his favorite restaurant is McDonald's in Arlington, where he enjoys the eggplant parmesan.

O:
The examination reveals drusen in the macula, retinal pigment epithelium subretinal fluid accumulation, a choroidal neovascular membrane visualized on fluorescein angiography, and hemorrhage. The retina is attached bilaterally. The patient's visual fields are intact.

A:
The patient has neovascular age-related macular degeneration. The visual distortion and blurring are consistent with this diagnosis.

P:
Order an injection of lucentes once a month into the vitreous. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to assess treatment response and monitor for any changes in vision. Advise the patient to continue monitoring his blood pressure regularly and to follow up if symptoms worsen.
