Chief Complaint
Headache

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing worsening headaches over the past few weeks, with occasional episodes but increasingly frequent and severe. She reports blood pressure readings as high as 160/92 and 175/92 mmHg in the last three weeks. She has been taking acetaminophen for the headaches, but finds they do not fully resolve. She admits to occasionally forgetting to take her lisinopril medication, particularly on weekends. Her blood sugar has been running between 120 and 127 mg/dL. She denies urinary urgency or incontinence and reports no congestion, cough, or runny nose. She describes headaches as a seven out of ten initially, decreasing to a four or five with acetaminophen. She has experienced headaches before, but not of this severity or duration.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no tenderness to palpation over the frontal or maxillary sinuses. No palpable lymphadenopathy is noted. No tenderness to palpation over the forehead, cheekbones, or neck.

Assessment
Hypertension and headache.

Plan
Increase lisinopril to 40 mg daily. Order routine blood work including hemoglobin, A1c, and a comprehensive metabolic panel. Provide diabetic education and schedule a follow-up appointment with Dr. Ruth to review blood work results and assess blood pressure control. Encourage continued regular exercise, including walking, and advise the patient to continue taking acetaminophen as needed for headache relief.
