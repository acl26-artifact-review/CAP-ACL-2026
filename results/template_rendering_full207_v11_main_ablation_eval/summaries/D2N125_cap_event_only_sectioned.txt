Chief Complaint
Left facial pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sharp, shooting electrical pain on the left side of his face, primarily around his jaw and temple. He describes the pain as being triggered by chewing and cold temperatures. He denies any recent trauma or fever. He reports that the pain has been present for approximately 2 weeks and has been gradually worsening. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with minimal relief. He states, "It's like a jolt of electricity every time I bite down."

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild facial tenderness to palpation over the left jaw and temple. Neurological exam is intact. No signs of fever or inflammation.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with trigeminal neuralgia.

Plan
Start gabapentin 100mg twice daily. Schedule follow-up in 2 weeks to assess response to medication. Advise patient to keep a pain diary to monitor triggers and severity.
