Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain

History of Present Illness
Mark reports right elbow pain for three weeks, primarily related to tennis. The pain is exacerbated by movement and is described as sharp and shooting. He has tried tylenol and ibuprofen with limited relief. He denies any recent trauma or popping sensation. Ice provides minimal relief. He has a history of diabetes type 2 and mild intermittent asthma.

Findings
Examination reveals moderate lateral epicondylitis tenderness in the right elbow. Passive range of motion elicits pain. Palpation reveals no joint effusion. Right wrist demonstrates mild pain with resisted extension. Hemoglobin A1c and CBC blood tests are pending. A chest x-ray shows no acute fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
Acute lateral epicondylitis of the right elbow.

Plan
Increase metformin to 1000mg twice daily. Prescribe ultram 50mg once daily for pain. Provide an ice pack for right elbow. Order a follow-up appointment in four weeks to reevaluate. Provide a prescription for albuterol refill.
