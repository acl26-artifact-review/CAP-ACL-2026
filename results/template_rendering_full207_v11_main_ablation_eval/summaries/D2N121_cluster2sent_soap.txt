S:
The patient reports knee pain following a skiing injury, experiencing swelling and pain. He reports taking over-the-counter ibuprofen twice daily and applying ice packs. He denies numbness or tingling in his foot and reports no prior surgeries. He states he has not missed any blood pressure medication.

O:
The patient's blood pressure is 120/77 mmHg in the office. Examination reveals no palpable lymphadenopathy, a regular rate and rhythm heart, clear lung sounds, and ecchymosis and edema in the right knee with effusion. Pulse is palpable bilaterally.

A:
The patient has a medial collateral ligament strain in the right knee. The examination findings suggest a significant knee injury with effusion and inflammation.

P:
I am ordering an x-ray of the right knee to rule out fracture. I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain and inflammation. I am recommending physical therapy for strengthening and rehabilitation. I am ordering a tetanus shot. I advise the patient to rest his knee and avoid skiing until further evaluation.
