Chief Complaint
Back pain following a golf injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain that began four days ago after a golf swing. He describes a sudden pop and immediate inability to continue playing. He initially tried ice and heat, but found no relief. He reports some difficulty with his right foot and a struggle with his right leg, and has a sweet tooth. He's been taking Tylenol and ibuprofen. He reports some weight gain over the summer. He's currently taking metformin for diabetes and lisinopril for heart failure. He denies any recent trauma. He reports a history of lumbar surgery ten years ago.

Findings
Physical exam reveals lumbar pain to palpation at L5. Range of motion is limited with flexion and extension. Straight leg raise is positive for the right leg. Vitals are stable. The patient's hemoglobin a1c is 8. A review of symptoms revealed weakness in the right foot and leg, and a sweet tooth.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with an acute lumbar strain. He also has a history of diabetes and congestive heart failure.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily. Refer to physical therapy. Order a lumbar MRI to rule out disc herniation. Increase metformin to 1000 mg twice daily. Order a repeat hemoglobin a1c in two months. Continue lisinopril and lasix. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks.
