Chief Complaint
Right-sided abdominal pain for several weeks.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right-sided abdominal pain for several weeks, which he describes as a squeezing, severe pain that comes and goes. The pain is typically rated as 8/10 and is often accompanied by nausea. He notes that the pain is most prominent after eating and can also occur a couple of hours after meals. He has been managing his type 2 diabetes with diet and exercise, having cut back on sugar and started a keto diet, resulting in a 25-pound weight loss over the past three months. He enjoys eating, but has had to cut out pork rinds due to the keto diet. The patient reports a family history of gallbladder disease, with both his parents having had their gallbladders removed. He has no fever and has not vomited.

Findings
Physical examination reveals positive bowel sounds, a soft, nondistended abdomen, and slight guarding in the right upper quadrant. Murphy's signs are present. The patient's vital signs are stable, with a blood pressure of 128/88, respiratory rate of 16, pulse rate of 68, and oxygen saturation of 99% on room air. An abdominal ultrasound demonstrates multiple gallstones in the gallbladder and a common bile duct size within normal limits. The clinician notes mild guarding on examination.

Assessment
The patient presents with mild cholecystitis secondary to gallstones. The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with this diagnosis.

Plan
I recommend a laparoscopic cholecystectomy to remove the gallbladder. The patient will spend the night in the hospital post-procedure for monitoring. He should avoid high-fat foods after surgery and follow up with me in approximately one week. I will order a follow-up appointment to discuss the results of the surgery and to continue managing his diabetes.
