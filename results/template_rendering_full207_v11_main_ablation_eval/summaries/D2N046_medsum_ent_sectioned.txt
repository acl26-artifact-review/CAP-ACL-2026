Chief Complaint
Newly diagnosed ovarian cancer and related symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a recently diagnosed ovarian cancer and presents with symptoms including severe pain and bleeding. She also reports constipation and weight loss. She denies vomiting. She has no children and has never been pregnant. Her period started at age 13 and menopause occurred at age 28. She does not take any oral hormone replacement therapy. She has no history of endometriosis and was adopted. She reports no family history of gynecological cancers. The patient reports feeling depressed.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a left adnexal mass. There is slight tenderness to palpation of the left lower quadrant. The vaginal exam is unremarkable. Abdominal CT imaging shows a three centimeter left ovarian mass with associated local lymph node involvement, with no evidence of gross peritoneal or metastatic disease. Vital signs are stable.

Assessment
Based on the CT findings and physical exam, the patient is most likely suffering from stage three ovarian cancer with associated lymph node involvement.

Plan
I am ordering a ca-125, hcg, afp, and ldh blood test to help determine the type of tumor. I am also recommending genetic counseling and testing to assess for a genetic predisposition to ovarian cancer. I recommend a hysterectomy with removal of both ovaries and lymph node dissection. I will also recommend adjuvant chemotherapy with cisplatin and taxol, and potentially intraperitoneal chemotherapy if indicated. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and address any further questions.
