S:
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath and dizziness following a tennis match on Saturday due to high heat and humidity. He described feeling very short of breath and struggling to breathe, initially fearing a heart attack. He also reported lightheadedness and dizziness approximately six hours later, followed by feeling fine the next day. He admits to occasional dietary indiscretions, including salty foods. He reports being diligent with his heart failure medications and in contact with his cardiologist. He has had cataract surgery three months prior and skin cancer removal five months prior, both of which have healed well. He denies any vision problems or ongoing issues related to these procedures.

O:
Blood pressure is elevated. Heart rate is regular and within the normal range. Lung auscultation reveals fine crackles bilaterally. Lower extremity edema is present. ECG shows left ventricular hypertrophy. Echocardiogram reveals stable but slightly reduced left ventricular ejection fraction. Skin biopsy results are negative.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely an exacerbation of heart failure, likely triggered by dietary indiscretion and sodium retention. His history of left ventricular hypertrophy and reduced ejection fraction supports this assessment. The ECG and echocardiogram findings are consistent with this diagnosis.

P:
Rescribe lasix 80 mg orally once daily. Order carvedilol 25 mg orally twice daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess response to therapy. Advise patient to monitor for signs and symptoms of worsening heart failure and to continue with a low-sodium diet. Refer to nursing for medication reconciliation and patient education.
