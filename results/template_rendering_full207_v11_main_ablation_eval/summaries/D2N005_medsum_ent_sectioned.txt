Chief Complaint
Right middle finger pain following a motor vehicle accident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right middle finger pain that began four days after a motor vehicle accident, where he was rear-ended. He describes the pain as significant, particularly with palpation. He reports the accident involved the trunk crumpling and himself 'flipping the bird' at the other driver. The patient denies any other injuries. He is currently taking digoxin for atrial fibrillation. He reports no other relevant medical history.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal phalanx of the right middle finger. No other physical exam findings are noted.

Assessment
Distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger or third finger.

Plan
Prescribe tramadol 50 mg every six hours as needed for pain for two days. Order a finger splint. Schedule a follow-up x-ray in two weeks. Advise the patient to continue taking digoxin for atrial fibrillation. Counsel the patient on the importance of adhering to the follow-up appointment.
