Chief Complaint
Evaluation of newly diagnosed hypercholesterolemia.

History of Present Illness
Julie Cruz, a 17-year-old non-binary minor, presents for evaluation of newly diagnosed hypercholesterolemia. She reports being concerned about a recent annual checkup finding of elevated cholesterol, prompting her to seek medical attention. She enjoys cycling and outdoor activities, and her diet includes cookies. She recalls her grandparents mentioning a family history of heart issues, including a cousin who died of a heart attack at age 44. She has been living as non-binary for two years and reports feeling healthy. She denies smoking and reports no one around her smokes.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal vital signs, including blood pressure within the normal range. Examination of the neck reveals no thyroidomegaly. Lung exam is clear. Eye exam shows no papilledema. Abdominal exam is unremarkable, with no hepatomegaly or splenomegaly. Cholesterol level is elevated at 210 mg/dL.

Assessment
The patient presents with elevated cholesterol levels, requiring further investigation and management to mitigate cardiovascular risk.

Plan
Order a full cholesterol panel, complete metabolic panel, thyroid studies, liver panels, and genetic testing to determine the cause of the elevated cholesterol. Refer to a nutritionist for dietary counseling. Schedule a follow-up cholesterol level in 3-6 months after dietary changes are implemented. Continue with regular physical activity, including her bike riding.
