Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent chest pain for the past month, described as a dull ache, occasionally sharp, and radiating to his left arm. He rates the pain as 6/10 on a scale of 1 to 10. He denies shortness of breath, palpitations, or nausea. He reports a family history of heart disease. He currently takes aspirin daily.

Findings
Blood pressure 138/82 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, regular rhythm. Physical exam reveals no edema or tenderness in the legs. Auscultation reveals normal heart sounds.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and risk factors are concerning for possible angina pectoris. Further evaluation is warranted.

Plan
Order an EKG and cardiac enzymes. Recommend cardiac stress test. Continue aspirin daily as prescribed.
