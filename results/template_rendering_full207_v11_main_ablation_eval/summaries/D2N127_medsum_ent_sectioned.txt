Chief Complaint
Neck pain following multiple car crashes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports neck pain stemming from multiple car crashes, with the most recent occurring in February of this year. He previously experienced therapy for a few months following the second crash and underwent surgery after the third. Prior to this latest incident, he was reportedly fine until this recent crash. He was recently prescribed fentanyl for pain management, but the prescription has lapsed. He reports experiencing stiffness and pain with neck movement, particularly when bending forward. He also reports headaches and occasional dizziness. He has numbness in his left arm and right leg and experiences spasms throughout his body. He has been to a pain clinic and has a history of therapy. The patient reports seeing Pamela and Dr. Collins, but also mentions that his lawyer advised him to come here.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate range of motion in the neck, with increased pain upon palpation of the bony process and muscle tissue. Range of motion is limited when bending the neck forward. No bruising or swelling is observed. There is no laceration. The patient reports experiencing fatigue since the accident.

Assessment
Based on the history of multiple car crashes and current neck pain, a neck sprain is the most likely diagnosis. Imaging reveals no fracture, which is reassuring.

Plan
I recommend ordering a CT scan to further evaluate the soft tissues. We will start with Robaxin to help relax the muscles and reduce pain. I will also provide you with exercises to improve range of motion. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess your progress. If the pain persists, we will consider a referral to pain medicine for local injections. We will also arrange for physical therapy to help with rehabilitation. Please note that I am here to help you navigate this process, and I encourage you to communicate any concerns or questions you may have.
