Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall and describes it as a constant, throbbing pain with warmth. He reports icing and elevating the ankle last night and taking ibuprofen. He has previously sprained this ankle and states, "if you can't walk on it, we definitely good thing that you came in today and we were able to see you.", and denies numbness or tingling. He notes ecchymosis and bruising.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure was 120/80 mmHg and heart rate 80 bpm with a SpO2 of 98%. Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the anterior and lateral ankle, with no laxity on the anterior drawer or inversion stress. Capillary refill is less than three seconds at the dorsalis pedis pulse. The x-ray showed no fractures or bony abnormalities. The ankle appears swollen.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a right ankle sprain, likely grade two to three, based on the reported pain, swelling, and exam findings.

Plan
Keep the leg elevated when seated, ice the ankle five times a day for twenty minutes, and use an air cast to stabilize the ankle. Avoid weight-bearing on the leg for one to two days, then start walking as tolerated. Continue ibuprofen as needed for pain, but do not exceed the recommended dosage. Follow up in one to two weeks if symptoms persist or if there is no improvement. If pain continues after one week, contact the office for possible MRI evaluation.
