Chief Complaint
Right knee pain related to running.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain that began during running, exacerbated by activity and worsening towards the end of the day. He describes ice and ibuprofen providing temporary relief. He has been running for years, including 5k and marathon training, and has experienced similar episodes in the past. He reports no family history of arthritis or immune diseases. He previously consulted with Dr. Wood regarding this issue.

Findings
Blood pressure was 120/60 mmHg, heart rate 58 bpm, and respirations were 14. The left knee examination revealed no pain with palpation and normal pedal pulse. The right knee revealed mild effusion and pain to the outside with palpation. Examination findings were otherwise unremarkable.

Assessment
Right knee sprain secondary to overuse, likely exacerbated by running activity.

Plan
Prescribe two tablets of Tylenol 500 mg and two tablets of ibuprofen 200 mg to be taken three times daily. Order physical therapy for the right knee to address lower extremity strengthening and running-specific exercises. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress. Advise a two-week period of reduced running activity, with the option to resume light weight training and squats under physical therapy guidance.
