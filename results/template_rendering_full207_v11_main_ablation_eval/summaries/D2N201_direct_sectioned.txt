Chief Complaint
Reports back pain for approximately two years, with recent exacerbation following a basketball injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing back pain for approximately two years, initially following a basketball injury where he fell and hit his back. He tried a couple of things but nothing seemed to work. The pain is described as sharp and throbbing at times, and it radiates down his left leg, occasionally causing numbness. He reports occasional weakness, particularly when standing for prolonged periods. He has experienced one instance of incontinence, related to prolonged standing. He previously received injections, but is unsure of the details. He has been attempting to manage the pain with over-the-counter medications, but reports limited effectiveness. He has also engaged in home-based physical therapy exercises, but found them to be less effective than initially hoped. He currently sits at home watching television and has gained approximately eighty pounds over the past year.

Findings
Physical examination reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with right paravertebral tenderness upon palpation. Straight leg raise testing is positive on the right. Neurological examination demonstrates decreased sensation to light touch at the L4 dermatome. Vital signs are within normal limits. MRI findings demonstrate disk desiccation, disk bulge with paracentral disk herniation resulting in moderate right for neural foraminal stenosis.

Assessment
Clinical presentation and MRI findings are consistent with a herniated disk at L4-L5 with radiculopathy, resulting in lumbar radiculopathy and moderate right for neural foraminal stenosis.

Plan
Recommend a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and provide symptomatic relief. Schedule the injection for scheduling at the front desk. Continue to monitor symptoms and adjust treatment as needed. If symptoms persist after the injection, consider further imaging and potential surgical consultation.
