Chief Complaint
Pain in the distal radius of the right arm following a tennis injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the distal radius of the right arm following a tennis injury. He describes hitting a backhand shot, where the ball contacted the net pole, resulting in a direct impact to his arm. He experienced immediate swelling, which was moderate in extent. He reports mild pain with wrist movement and tenderness to palpation. He denies numbness or significant weakness in his hand. He has a past history of a fall while raking leaves, resulting in a stab wound to the forearm, which he describes as being treated with stitches. He also has a history of allergies and takes flonase for them. He has no prior surgeries.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal radius of the right arm. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No signs of fracture are observed on visual inspection. The patient reports mild pain with wrist flexion and extension.

Assessment
Strain and contusion of the right distal radius muscle.

Plan
Prescribe ice packs for 20 minutes every 2-3 hours. Recommend motrin 800 mg three times daily with food for pain and inflammation. Follow up in one week if symptoms do not improve. Order x-rays to rule out fracture.
