Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a fall while working on his property a week ago. He states he slipped and fell, and is still experiencing discomfort. He describes the pain as exacerbated by flexion and extension, particularly with pressure applied to the medial aspect of the knee. He reports some swelling and reports feeling a pop at the time of the injury. He has been taking ibuprofen for the swelling. He denies any other injuries. He reports a history of hypertension and is currently taking lisinopril 20mg daily.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited by pain with flexion and extension. Mild edema and effusion are noted. The patient's blood pressure is 150/70. A grade 2 systolic ejection murmur is auscultated. Lungs are clear bilaterally. The patient denies chest pain, abdominal pain, or shortness of breath. The patient reports pain at a 4/10 when stationary, increasing to a 5/10 with movement.

Assessment
The patient presents with a medial collateral ligament strain of the right knee, likely secondary to the recent fall. His hypertension remains a concern.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 800mg every 6 hours as needed for pain and swelling. Order a knee brace for support. Refer to nutrition for assistance with dietary changes to reduce sodium intake. Continue lisinopril 20mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress. Advise the patient to elevate his leg and stay off it as much as possible. Ensure the patient understands the importance of reporting any worsening symptoms.
