Chief Complaint
Acute shortness of breath and worsening edema.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a week of increasing shortness of breath, initially similar to six months ago when he experienced a similar episode requiring lasix dose adjustment. He notes prior episodes of shortness of breath and swelling in his legs, and reports using motrin for foot pain approximately a week ago. He takes lasix 40mg daily, divided, and reports difficulty adhering to a low-sodium diet. He has a history of gout and reports taking lasix and tylenol for pain.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 135/80 mmHg. Jugular venous distention is present to 9 cm. Lung exam reveals bibasilar crackles and occasional rhonchi, which resolve with coughing. Lower extremity exam reveals 1+ pitting edema bilaterally. A three out of six systolic ejection murmur is auscultated at the left base. Chest x-ray is pending.

Assessment
The patient presents with an acute heart failure exacerbation, likely secondary to underlying coronary artery disease and chronic kidney disease. Possible gout flare is also being considered.

Plan
Increase lasix dosage to 80mg daily, with close monitoring of weight and shortness of breath. Order a chest x-ray to evaluate for pulmonary congestion. Obtain an echocardiogram to assess left ventricular function and the murmur at the left base. Order a complete metabolic panel to assess renal function. Schedule a referral to a registered dietitian for dietary counseling and initiate a referral to a gout specialist. Advise the patient to monitor his weight daily and to contact the clinic immediately if shortness of breath worsens. Continue to monitor potassium levels and educate the patient on the importance of sodium restriction.
