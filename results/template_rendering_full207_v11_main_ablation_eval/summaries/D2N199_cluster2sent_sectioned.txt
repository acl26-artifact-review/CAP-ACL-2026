Chief Complaint
Fatigue and cough.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue for the past two months, accompanied by a cough that started approximately one week ago and produces yellow sputum. The patient feels worse over the last two days. He denies fever or chills. The patient reports congestion and drainage in the throat and sinus pressure. He reports illness in household members. The patient reports a knee injury from coughing and falling, involving four to five stairs. He reports pain in the inside of the right knee with full range of motion and no limping. He reports ecchymosis to the right medial knee and decreased flexion and extension of the right knee. He reports coarse rhonchi bilaterally and erythema and edema of the nasal turbinates bilaterally with yellow discharge. The patient reports bilateral cervical lymphadenopathy. He takes over-the-counter cough and cold medication, which partially relieves the cough. He reports no monitoring of diet.

Findings
The patient's heart rate is 88 bpm, blood pressure is 125/62 mmHg, temperature is 98.4°F, and oxygenation is 97% on room air. Examination reveals bilateral cervical lymphadenopathy and erythema and edema of the nasal turbinates bilaterally.

Assessment
The patient presents with fatigue, cough, and right knee injury. Clinical findings suggest possible secondary infection and cervical lymphadenopathy.

Plan
Order a right knee x-ray, lipid panel, and hemoglobin A1c. Prescribe guaifenesin 600mg twice daily and meloxicam 15mg daily. Advise the patient to monitor blood sugar intake and report blood sugars through the patient portal. Follow up in one week to assess response to medications and x-ray results.
