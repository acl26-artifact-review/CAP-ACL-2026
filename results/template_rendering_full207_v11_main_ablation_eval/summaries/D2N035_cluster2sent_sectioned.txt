Chief Complaint
Worsening shortness of breath and ankle edema.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports recent hospital admission for heart failure due to dietary indiscretion and uncontrolled hypertension. She experienced significant shortness of breath and ankle edema, requiring supplemental oxygen and bed elevation. She contacted emergency services due to difficulty speaking and sought medical evaluation. She reports attempting to adhere to a low-sodium diet and consistently taking her medications, but admits to occasional lapses. She reports experiencing urinary incontinence secondary to diuretic use.

Findings
Blood pressure is 128/72 mmHg. Temperature is 98.7°F. Heart rate is 72 bpm. Respirations are 18 breaths per minute. Oxygen saturation is 96% on room air. Jugular venous distention is absent. Heart sounds reveal a two over six systolic ejection murmur, which is stable. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. Lower extremities demonstrate trace edema. Echocardiogram shows a preserved ejection fraction of 55% and mild-to-moderate mitral regurgitation.

Assessment
The patient presents with congestive heart failure secondary to hypertension and mitral regurgitation, complicated by dietary indiscretion.

Plan
Continue bucindol 2 mg daily. Continue to monitor weight daily and call if weight increases by three pounds in two days. Order a renal artery ultrasound to evaluate for renal artery stenosis. Prescribe a nutritionist consultation to provide dietary education. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months.
