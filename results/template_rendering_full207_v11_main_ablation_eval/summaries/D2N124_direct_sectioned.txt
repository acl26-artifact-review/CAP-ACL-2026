Chief Complaint
Right ankle injury following a basketball-related incident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a right ankle injury sustained while playing basketball. He describes landing awkwardly after a jump shot, resulting in immediate pain and swelling. He has been experiencing difficulty walking and a sense of instability in the ankle. He reports a recent return to regular exercise and is concerned about disrupting his fitness routine. He also volunteers as a coach, starting in April.

Findings
Examination reveals significant lateral ankle tenderness with palpation, exquisite tenderness to movement, and swelling on the lateral aspect of the ankle. Brisk capillary refill is present. The patient reports difficulty standing on the ankle and a feeling of instability. An X-ray demonstrates a displaced lateral malleolus fracture at the Weber C level with no evidence of medial or posterior malleolar fractures.

Assessment
Lateral malleolar fracture.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam for pain and inflammation. Advise ice and elevation of the ankle. Order crutches for weight-bearing. Estimate a recovery period of approximately three months, with a potential need for surgical intervention. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and continue rehabilitation. Continue coaching activities as tolerated, with appropriate modifications.
