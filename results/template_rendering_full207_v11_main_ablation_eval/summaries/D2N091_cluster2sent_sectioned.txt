Chief Complaint
Insomnia and lingering right knee issues.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 54-year-old male with a history of osteoporosis and multiple sclerosis who presents for an annual exam. He reports adjusting to the fall season and experiencing persistent lack of sleep, which has been exacerbated by medication adjustments. He has tried various sleep aids, including melatonin, meditation, and stretching, with limited success. He reports no recent broken bones related to his osteoporosis. He continues to see his neurologist for his multiple sclerosis and is taking prescribed medications. He previously experienced weakness in his right leg, which has improved, but he still has lingering issues from his knee surgery. He also reports some lower extremity weakness and arthritic changes in his right knee. He is currently taking fosamax for osteoporosis.

Findings
Physical examination reveals no acute abnormalities. The right knee x-ray shows changes consistent with arthritis but no acute fracture. Vital signs are stable and within normal limits.

Assessment
Osteoporosis, multiple sclerosis, and chronic right knee osteoarthritis.

Plan
Order a refill of fosamax 1 tablet per week, 11 refills. Continue to encourage regular neurology visits and adherence to prescribed medications for multiple sclerosis. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess right knee symptoms and physical therapy progress.
