S:
The patient reports back pain following yard work, with pins and needles in his right foot. He describes the pain as tightening and sharp, exacerbated by bending over and relieved by a hot shower. He reports a previous episode three years ago that resolved in a day. He denies any prior pain with the back. His partner reports pain when he stands, mirroring his own.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation at L5. Dorsiflexion is normal, pulses are equal bilaterally. Neurologic exam shows tingling sensation in the right leg. An x-ray of the low back revealed no abnormalities.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a sprained low back. He reports a history of similar pain and his partner's report suggests a possible long-term issue. Further investigation is warranted to rule out other causes.

P:
I am prescribing meloxicam for pain relief. I am also prescribing physical therapy to help with rehabilitation and improve flexibility. I recommend a follow-up in two weeks to assess improvement, or sooner if symptoms persist or worsen. I will order an MRI if the pain does not improve after two weeks.
