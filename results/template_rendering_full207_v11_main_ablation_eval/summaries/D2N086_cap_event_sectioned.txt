Chief Complaint
Nonhealing right foot ulcer.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports the ulcer began approximately six weeks ago after a pair of shoes became too tight, causing blistering and stinging. He initially dismissed it but noticed a foul odor and his wife's observation of his socks smelling. He was started on antibiotics six days ago. He reports throbbing pain, described as a 'fire poker,' in the foot and cramping in his calf muscle. He's experiencing increased coughing and difficulty catching his breath. His blood sugars have been trending in the 3-400 range over the past two weeks, with a peak of 5-600. He previously managed diabetes with an insulin pump and has maintained an A1c of 6.7% for two years. He recently hiked to Charlie's Bunion trail, and has been wearing new diabetic shoes and inserts for about a week prior to the ulcer's development. He enjoys hiking and has been exploring the new trails at the park.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a necrotic wound on the right heel, approximately 2x2 cm, with sloughing tissue and surrounding cellulitis. Erythema is present. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable via Doppler, but not palpable directly. A slight fever is noted. The patient reports pain in the affected area, but no pain upon direct pressure. Vital signs are stable. No bony exposure is noted.

Assessment
The patient presents with a nonhealing diabetic foot ulcer secondary to trauma and likely exacerbated by neuropathy. There is evidence of cellulitis and potential for infection.

Plan
Order blood supply testing for the wound. Perform a debridement of the ulcer in the office. Obtain a wound culture and consider alternative antibiotic therapy based on culture results. Continue current antibiotic regimen. Schedule a follow-up appointment in seven days. Continue to monitor blood sugars closely and adjust medication as needed. Refer to endocrinology for ongoing diabetes management.
