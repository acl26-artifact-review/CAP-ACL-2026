Chief Complaint
Left elbow pain and chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left elbow pain and chest pain that began yesterday. He describes the elbow pain as aching and heavy, extending up to the neck, and the chest pain as intermittent. He denies any recent trauma or significant exertion. He reports taking motrin for the pain, but it hasn't provided significant relief. He also mentions a history of hypertension and diabetes. He reports that his blood pressure was elevated during the visit. He states that he has been taking norvasc for hypertension and metformin for diabetes. He does not regularly check his blood sugars. He reports mowing the grass the other day, which may have contributed to the elbow pain. He denies any recent job-related injuries or unusual activities.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slight murmur with cardiac auscultation. The patient's blood pressure was 150/90. An EKG is ordered to evaluate for possible cardiac issues. The patient reports intermittent chest pain.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms of left elbow pain and chest pain, along with the reported murmur, raise concern for possible cardiac issues. Further evaluation is needed to rule out musculoskeletal causes and assess for underlying cardiac pathology.

Plan
Order an EKG to evaluate for cardiac issues. Administer aspirin and nitroglycerin to alleviate chest pain. Continue norvasc at a dose of 2.5 mg. Continue metformin. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to review the EKG results and adjust the management plan as needed. Refer to cardiology for further evaluation if indicated.
