S:
The patient reports a history of back pain for approximately two years, initially following a weekend of furniture moving. He describes the pain as sharp and stabbing, radiating down his right leg to his toe. He reports numbness and tingling in his right leg and foot. He states he has tried physical therapy and strong pain medications with limited relief. He reports gaining weight over the past years and difficulty with activity due to the pain. He quit smoking twenty years ago. He denies any family history of spine conditions. He states he has no sensation loss in the genital or rectal area.

O:
Vitals are within normal limits. Musculoskeletal exam reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with right-sided lumbar paraverteal tenderness. Lumbar flexion elicits pain. Straight leg raise test is positive on the right. Neurological exam reveals decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at L4 dermatome. Patellar and Achilles reflexes are symmetrical. MRI reveals a disk herniation with nerve impingement at L4-L5.

A:
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with an L4-L5 herniated disk with radiculopathy, causing nerve impingement and resulting in radicular symptoms.

P:
I recommend a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and pain. The injection will be performed under fluoroscopy to ensure accurate localization. Follow-up will be scheduled to monitor response to the injection, with a maximum of four injections per year. The procedure is expected to be completed within an hour.
