Chief Complaint
Constant pain in the left back.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports constant pain in the left back, which began approximately two weeks ago. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, and rates it as a 7 out of 10. He denies any recent trauma. He reports that the pain is exacerbated by bending over and lifting. He has a history of kidney stones, but denies any current urinary symptoms. The patient reports a preference for french fries. He denies chest pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the left lumbar region. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Blood pressure readings were sometimes in the one forties. The patient denies chest pain.

Assessment
The patient presents with acute left lumbar pain, likely musculoskeletal in origin. He has a history of kidney stones. His blood pressure readings were sometimes in the one forties.

Plan
Start ibuprofen 640mg every 6 hours as needed for pain. Order lumbar x-rays to rule out fracture. Follow up in 2 weeks if symptoms persist. Advise patient to avoid heavy lifting and bending.
