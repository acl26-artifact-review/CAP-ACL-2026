Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath during tennis play.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of shortness of breath, initially experienced during a tennis match on Saturday, following exposure to hot and humid conditions. He describes feeling short of breath and struggling to breathe, prompting his wife to transport him to the emergency room. Since that event, he has experienced intermittent lightheadedness and dizziness, particularly on Sunday. He reports being diligent with his heart failure medications and avoiding salty foods. He also mentions a cataract surgery three months ago and a skin cancer removal five months ago. He has not experienced any vision problems or skin cancer recurrence. He admits to occasionally consuming salty foods.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure was elevated during the emergency room visit. Physical examination reveals neck mobility is normal, no jugular venous distension, heart rate is regular at 72 beats per minute with no murmurs, lung sounds exhibit fine crackles bilaterally, and there is trace lower extremity edema. An electrocardiogram shows stable left ventricular hypertrophy. An echocardiogram reveals a stable pumping function of the heart. The skin biopsy results are negative.

Assessment
The patient's current shortness of breath is most likely an exacerbation of heart failure, likely precipitated by dietary indiscretion and exacerbated by the hot and humid environment. He has a history of heart failure, left ventricular hypertrophy, and stable echocardiogram results. He has no acute findings to suggest a new cardiac event.

Plan
Prescribe a dose of Lasix 80 milligrams once daily and Carvedilol 25 milligrams twice daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment. The nurse will administer the medications and provide patient education on heart failure management, including dietary restrictions and medication adherence.
