Chief Complaint
Right-sided abdominal pain for several weeks.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right-sided abdominal pain for several weeks, initially intermittent and now more persistent. The pain is located on the right hand side of the abdomen, just beneath the ribs. He describes the pain as a squeezing, severe sensation that comes and goes, but when it does occur, it is quite severe. The patient reports nausea and sickness in his stomach when the pain is present. The pain began several weeks ago and has been worsening over time. He reports that the pain is often triggered by eating, specifically within a few hours after meals, and sometimes two hours after eating. He has attempted to limit his intake of high-fat foods, as recommended by his previous physician, and has been following a keto diet. He has lost approximately twenty-five pounds over the past three months due to this dietary change. The patient reports that his symptoms have improved somewhat with the dietary changes, but the pain persists. He denies fever or vomiting. He has a family history of gallbladder disease, with both his parents having had their gallbladders removed.

Findings
Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 128/88, respiratory rate 16, pulse 68, oxygen saturation 99% on room air. The patient is afebrile. Physical exam reveals mild guarding to palpation in the right upper quadrant. Bowel sounds are present and normophonetic bilaterally. Murphy's signs are present. An abdominal ultrasound reveals multiple gallstones in the gallbladder and mild thickening of the gallbladder wall. Laboratory results are pending.

Assessment
Mild cholecystitis with gallstones. The patient's symptoms are consistent with gallbladder inflammation and the presence of gallstones.

Plan
I recommend a laparoscopic cholecystectomy. I will order the necessary imaging and obtain informed consent. I will also schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to discuss the surgical plan and address any questions the patient may have. The patient should continue to avoid high-fat foods and follow a low-carbohydrate diet as tolerated. The nurse will obtain a signed consent form and schedule the surgical procedure. I will refer the patient to physical therapy for post-operative rehabilitation.
