Chief Complaint
Follow-up for hypertension.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 72-year-old woman with a history of significant alcohol use disorder and mild hypercholesterolemia. At her last visit on April 16th, her blood pressure was 130/90. After presenting at 150/100, she was prescribed hydrochlorothiazide 12.5 mg daily and lisinopril 20 mg daily. CMP and CBC were essentially unremarkable. The patient reports struggling with not drinking and discusses a recent drink, stating she last drank on Monday. She has been taking her blood pressure medications, but only half the lisinopril dose due to experiencing lethargy and headaches. She has been monitoring her blood pressure at home, noting readings ranging from 130/95 to 150/100, with lower readings in the morning and higher readings in the evening. She has been taking her medications in the morning. The patient has previously tried naltrexone.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no fever, cough, or shortness of breath. The patient's skin is moist and her trigger is negative. She is alert, pleasant, and in no acute distress. She denies recent travel or exposure to the coronavirus and has received her coronavirus vaccine on February 9th. Examination of the ankles and feet reveals no signs of swelling. Her blood pressure is within the target range of 130-140/70-80.

Assessment
Alcohol use disorder, hypertension.

Plan
Start naltrexone (vivitrol) as a monthly injection, scheduled for May 17th. Counsel the patient on the potential benefits of alcohol cessation for her hypertension. Continue current medications: hydrochlorothiazide 12.5 mg daily and lisinopril 20 mg daily. Recommend influenza vaccination and shingrix. Schedule a follow-up visit in approximately six weeks, around June 14th, to assess response to naltrexone and monitor blood pressure. Advise the patient to monitor her blood pressure at home and report any side effects or concerns.
