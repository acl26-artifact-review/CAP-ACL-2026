Chief Complaint
Kayla reports experiencing acne on her face, chest, and back over the past two years.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that acne began approximately two years ago and is present most days. She has been using persa-gel and washing regularly, which provides some relief. She denies associated symptoms such as itching, bleeding, or pain. She notes a significant flare-up when she started school sports in the fall due to sweating. Her skin care routine involves wiping her face with water in the morning and using the Elf super clarity cleanser, followed by a toner and persa-gel. At night, she washes with the same cleanser, allows it to dry, and applies adapalene 0.1% cream a few nights a week, gradually increasing frequency. She reports experiencing sensitivity and potential dryness or irritation from the retinoid. She has a regular menstrual cycle and reports no correlation between her periods and acne flares.

Findings
A full examination was performed, excluding the undergarment and bra areas. Multiple benign nevi were observed on the trunk and extremities. Scattered skin-colored papules, open and closed comedones, and erythrema papules were noted on the face, primarily on the forehead and central cheeks and chin. The chest and back are relatively spared. Physical exam findings are otherwise unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient presents with mild to moderate acne, primarily comedonal with a small inflammatory component, predominantly on the face.

Plan
I would like to start with a topical therapy. Every morning, you will wash your face with a mild cleanser and use a moisturizer labeled 'noncomedogenic' with sunscreen SPF 30 or higher. In the evening, wash your face with the same cleanser and allow it to dry, then apply adapalene 0.1% cream to the areas where you experience acne. Monitor for sensitivity and dryness. Follow up in six months to assess progress. Continue to wear sunscreen when outside.
