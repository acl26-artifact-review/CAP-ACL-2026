Chief Complaint
Ongoing management of rheumatoid arthritis and right knee pain.

History of Present Illness
Mr. Nelson reports ongoing rheumatoid arthritis, with his right knee being the primary area of concern. He states the injection he received two weeks ago provided some relief, but the swelling has returned and now extends to his entire leg. He reports pain around a 3/10 on a typical day, and up to 6 when it's really hurting. The pain has been present for approximately two months, initially just in the knee, but now affecting the entire leg. He has been wearing compression socks, but they haven't been providing significant relief. He is taking Xeljanz 11mg daily and Celebrex 200mg daily. He reports stiffness upon getting out of bed, requiring a cane for assistance. He states he's been eating a lot of salty snacks. He previously wore compression socks, but they haven't been helping.

Findings
The patient reports a range of motion diminished in his right knee. There is pitting edema noted around the knee and ankle. Capillary refill is normal in his toes. The knee is warm to the touch, indicating inflammation. He reports pain when straightening the knee, and reports a sore sensation, consistent with an old bruise.

Assessment
The patient presents with rheumatoid arthritis and significant inflammation in the right knee, likely contributing to his reported pain and swelling.

Plan
I will administer a steroid injection to the right knee. I will order an X-ray of the right knee to assess the extent of the inflammation and rule out other causes. I will schedule physical therapy for the patient. I will also check the patient's CMP levels every couple of months. I will instruct the patient to prop their feet up and reduce salt intake. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist. ,
