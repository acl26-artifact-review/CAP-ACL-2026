Chief Complaint
Neck pain following a car accident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports neck pain following a car accident approximately one week ago. He describes the injury as a forward head movement with significant neck flexion and rotation. The patient rates the pain as a 7/10, constant but exacerbated by movement. Ibuprofen provided minimal relief, approximately 5/10. He reports experiencing headaches intermittently. He has a history of playing football years ago, but denies any prior neck injuries. He did not seek emergency room care due to the cost of an initial visit, but reports no swelling or bruising to his neck. He reports mild left hand pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the neck. Range of motion is limited, particularly with flexion and extension, with pain reported. There is tenderness to palpation of the left hand. No neurological deficits are noted. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with whiplash-associated neck pain. There is no evidence of fracture on the X-ray.

Plan
I am prescribing motrin 600 mg every 6-8 hours and flexeril 10 mg every 12 hours as needed. I have ordered an MRI to rule out concussion. I am referring the patient to physical therapy or a chiropractor for further evaluation and management. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
