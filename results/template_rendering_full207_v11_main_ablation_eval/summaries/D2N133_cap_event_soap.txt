S:
The patient reports intermittent right knee pain exacerbated by swimming, particularly with the breaststroke. He describes pain with patellar inhibition. He reports stable chronic back pain and stable depression with medication and therapy. He denies any recent injuries or remembering them. Sleeping and eating patterns are reported as normal.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation of the right patella and pain with patellar inhibition. X-ray of the right knee shows normal findings. Heart and lungs are clear. Neurological exam is unremarkable. No signs of inflammation.

A:
The primary problem is chondromalacia of the patella secondary to overuse from swimming. Chronic back pain is stable. Depression is well-managed.

P:
Rescribe ibuprofen 600 mg four times daily with food. Recommend straight leg raising exercises. Advise avoiding the breaststroke and lunges for 2 weeks. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 2 weeks if symptoms persist or worsen. Continue current antidepressant medication and therapy.
