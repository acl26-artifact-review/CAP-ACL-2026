Chief Complaint
Reports fatigue and dizziness, secondary to a low hemoglobin level.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing significant fatigue over the past two months, progressively worsening over the last month, impacting her ability to maintain her usual exercise routine. She denies recent trauma or blood in her stools, and reports no other symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath, fever, or body aches. Her periods are normal in character. She has been diligently managing her congestive heart failure with dietary modifications and reports no swelling in her legs. She quit smoking several years ago and has remained smoke-free since. The patient also notes a slight weight loss, attributable to increased exercise.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a systolic ejection murmur at the cardiac auscultation. The abdomen is tender to palpation in the right lower quadrant. Vital signs are stable. Hemoglobin is 8.2.

Assessment
The patient presents with anemia, likely requiring further investigation to determine the underlying etiology.

Plan
I am ordering an endoscopy and a colonoscopy to evaluate for sources of bleeding. I have also ordered a referral to gastroenterology for these procedures. I will continue the patient on toprol, lisinopril, and lasix 20 milligrams daily. I will follow up with the patient in one week to discuss the results of the laboratory investigations and further management strategies.
