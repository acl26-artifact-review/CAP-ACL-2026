Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall last night, where he slipped on ice and heard a pop. He was unable to walk immediately after the fall and required assistance from a friend. Currently, he can tolerate a small amount of weight-bearing but is limping. He has iced and elevated the ankle and taken ibuprofen. He describes the pain as constant and throbbing, and it is exacerbated by touch. He denies numbness or tingling. He previously played soccer in high school but has no prior ankle injuries.

Findings
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus and swelling of the ankle. Palpation elicits tenderness in the anterior and lateral soft tissue. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong. Capillary refill is less than three seconds. The ankle demonstrates no laxity on anterior drawer or inversion stress. Blood pressure is 120/80 mmHg, heart rate 80 bpm, and SpO2 98%.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with a right ankle sprain, likely moderate in severity based on the reported pain and physical exam findings. There are no acute fractures identified on the pre-visit X-ray.

Plan
Keep the leg elevated and ice it five times a day for 20 minutes. Administer an air cast for ankle support. Instruct the patient to avoid weight-bearing on the affected leg for one to two days. Provide crutches for assistance with ambulation. Advise the patient to continue taking ibuprofen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two to three weeks to assess healing progress. If symptoms persist or worsen, an MRI should be considered.
