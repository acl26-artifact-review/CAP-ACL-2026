Chief Complaint
The patient reports experiencing pain related to a past history of kidney stones, currently experiencing intermittent pain and a recent episode at the emergency room.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent pain related to a past history of kidney stones, with a recent episode at the emergency room. He states the pain has been fluctuating in intensity. He reports passing one stone recently, but still experiences some discomfort. He describes having had approximately seven or eight kidney stones in the past. He reports no recent blood in his urine, but does recall some blood in the urine previously. He reports that the pain has now subsided. He has been taking percocet for the pain. He has a history of hypertension and diabetes. He reports difficulty with dietary changes, particularly with fast food, but is trying to improve his diet.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure was elevated at 150/90. The patient's heart rate was within normal limits. Lung sounds were clear bilaterally. The abdomen was non-tender upon palpation. A grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur was auscultated. The patient's A1c was 6.7%. The patient's blood sugar readings have been variable, but generally within acceptable ranges. There were no significant findings on physical exam.

Assessment
The patient presents with a history of recurrent kidney stones, hypertension, and type 2 diabetes. Current symptoms include intermittent pain and discomfort related to a recent stone passage.

Plan
A referral to urology is ordered to investigate recurrent kidney stones. A urinalysis will be performed to rule out infection. Continue metformin 500mg daily. Initiate a referral to nutrition counseling to assist with dietary modifications. The patient will follow up in three months. The patient should take blood pressure readings twice daily and record them. The patient should monitor blood sugars daily and record them. The patient should take 10mg of melatonin as needed for sleep.
