Chief Complaint
The patient reports chest pain following a fall and subsequent shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a fall while outside, resulting in chest pain which he initially dismissed. He subsequently developed shortness of breath and was evaluated in the emergency room for ankle swelling. He is currently taking bumex, cozaar, and norvasc. He reports difficulty keeping up with activities and occasional shortness of breath, but rates it as manageable. He has been trying to quit smoking two years ago and reports success. He has been using a blood pressure cuff at home and tracking his readings. He reports no sleep problems or chest pain other than occasional, mild heartburn.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 128/72 mmHg in the office. Jugular venous distention is absent. Heart sounds are clear with a grade II/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is stable. Lungs are clear bilaterally. Lower extremities demonstrate trace edema. Echocardiogram reveals a preserved ejection fraction of 55% and mild to moderate mitral regurgitation. The patient's weight is currently being monitored daily.

Assessment
The patient presents with hypertension, chronic heart failure, and mild mitral regurgitation.

Plan
The patient should continue bumex 2 mg daily and monitor weight daily, calling if they gain three pounds in two days. Order a renal artery ultrasound to rule out obstruction. Schedule a consultation with a nutritionist to address dietary modifications, particularly regarding sodium intake. Continue cozaar 100 mg daily and norvasc 5 mg daily. Arrange follow-up in four weeks.
