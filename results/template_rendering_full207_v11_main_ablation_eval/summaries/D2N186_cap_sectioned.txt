Chief Complaint
Left back pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports lower back pain, with pain occurring randomly and depending on the day. He reports that bending over makes his pain worse and that his significant other always tells him to stretch, which helps. The patient reports pain in his back and reports no pain with dorsiflexion. He denies using icing. The patient reports taking ibuprofen 800 milligrams a day.

Findings
Examination reveals pain with palpation to the left side around the L5. The patient reports pain with weightbearing. Dorsiflexion is normal. No pain with extension. No pain with palpation to the right side. The patient reports pain in the left hand. The patient reports no pain with palpation to the right side. The x-ray shows no fractures or abnormalities in the spinal bone.

Assessment
The patient has lower back pain, likely due to a muscle pull from misalignment in the spine.

Plan
A referral to pain management is ordered. The doctor prescribes meloxicam instead of ibuprofen. The doctor prescribes demerol. A referral to physical therapy is arranged.
