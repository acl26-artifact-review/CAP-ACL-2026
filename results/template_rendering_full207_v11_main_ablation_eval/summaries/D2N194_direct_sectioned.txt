Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on his left side. He denies any specific injury or trauma. He has been taking acetaminophen with varying degrees of relief. He has been actively renovating his basement and reports increased activity levels. He has been living in the house for some time and recently undertook renovations.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder, tenderness to palpation over the greater tuberosity of the humerus, and no tenderness in the sternoclavicular or acromioclavicular joints. Capillary refill is brisk in all digits of the left hand. Neurovascular examination is intact. Radiographs demonstrate no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order an MRI of the left shoulder to evaluate for additional pathology. Refer the patient for physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. Continue acetaminophen as needed for symptomatic relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately two weeks to assess progress.
