The patient reports intermittent left back pain, described as sharp or moving, and occasionally non-existent. He reports no current fever or other systemic symptoms. He denies recent trauma. He states he has not noticed any increased tiredness or appetite changes. He reports increased urine output, particularly at night, and has been drinking more water. He denies any sick contacts at home or school. His brother smokes and has been told not to smoke, but he does not listen. The patient reports four strep infections in the last five months, attributing them to his doctor's diagnosis.

O:
Physical exam reveals no tenderness to palpation of the back, no cervical lymphadenopathy, lungs are clear to auscultation, heart sounds are normal without murmurs, and abdominal exam is unremarkable. Throat exam shows no erythema or swelling, tonsils are midline and unremarkable. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms are concerning for possible glomerular nephritis, potentially related to recurrent strep infections. A CT scan revealed findings suggestive of glomerular nephritis. Referral to an ENT specialist is indicated to consider tonsillectomy. Further investigation is needed to determine the underlying cause of the recurrent strep infections.

P:
Order a lipid panel, UA, CMP, CBC, vitamin D, and TSH. Refer to an ENT specialist for evaluation and consideration of tonsillectomy. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to review lab results and discuss further management. Advise the patient to return if symptoms worsen or do not improve. Offer to discuss free food options at the restaurant if infection control is improved.
