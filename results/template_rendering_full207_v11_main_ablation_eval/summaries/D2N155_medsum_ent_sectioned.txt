Chief Complaint
A year-long nonhealing foot ulcer, accompanied by type 2 diabetes and associated neuropathy.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a nonhealing foot ulcer present for approximately one year, originating without trauma but developing after a small blister. He describes the ulcer as painless but with diminished sensation in the foot. He reports experiencing increased drainage and fatigue recently. He was previously on Keflex for approximately five months and notes that the ulcer did not resolve. The patient's blood sugars are currently in the 200s, with a recent A1c of approximately 10. He has been taking metformin and insulin as prescribed by his primary care physician.

Findings
A physical examination reveals a one by two centimeter circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot, proximal to the fifth metatarsal head. The wound demonstrates yellow slough and minimal granulation tissue. There is no surrounding erythema or cellulitis, and no evidence of fluid collection or necrosis. The patient reports no pain upon palpation. A right foot x-ray prior to the visit showed no evidence of osteomyelitis. Vitals are stable. The ABI will be ordered to assess blood flow.

Assessment
The patient presents with a chronic, nonhealing diabetic foot ulcer requiring further evaluation and management.

Plan
An ankle brachial index (ABI) will be ordered to assess blood flow to the foot. A debridement of the ulcer will be performed in the office today. A surgical shoe will be provided to reduce pressure on the ulcerated area. Colagenase ointment will be prescribed for topical application to the wound once daily, covered with a sterile dressing. Follow-up will be scheduled in two weeks to assess healing progress and adjust treatment as needed. The patient should return to his primary care physician for continued management of his diabetes and medication regimen.
