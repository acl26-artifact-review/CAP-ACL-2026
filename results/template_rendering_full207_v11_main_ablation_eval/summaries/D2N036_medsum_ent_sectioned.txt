Chief Complaint
Bilateral numbness and tingling in fingers, with pain in the wrist.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a gradual onset of numbness and tingling in his fingers over the past few months, primarily affecting the left hand. He describes this as worsening at night and often requiring shaking to alleviate the sensation. He notes that this is accompanied by wrist pain. The patient reports that the symptoms are most prominent when waking up. He performs yard work and landscaping daily, involving repetitive motions and heavy lifting. He denies any history of rheumatoid arthritis or similar conditions. He drinks approximately two beers on Friday and Saturday evenings.

Findings
Physical examination reveals diminished grip strength on the left hand and a notable weakness in the left hand. Tinel's signs are positive bilaterally. Palpation of the left wrist reveals tenderness. The patient reports dropping objects due to decreased sensation.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history, physical examination findings, and positive Tinel's signs, a diagnosis of bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome is highly suspected.

Plan
I am ordering an EMG to further evaluate the nerve conduction studies. I will also prescribe a wrist splint to be worn nightly and ibuprofen 600 mg every six hours as needed for pain. I recommend modifying his landscaping work to reduce repetitive motions and suggest exploring lighter work options. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess treatment response and discuss further management strategies, including potential surgical intervention if conservative measures are insufficient.
