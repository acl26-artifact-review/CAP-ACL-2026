Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain with radiation to the neck.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he woke up yesterday with pain in his left elbow, which has since spread up his arm to the neck. He denies any recent trauma or injury. The pain has been present since yesterday and is described as aching and heavy. He reports that the pain is exacerbated by movement. He has been taking Motrin for the pain, but it provides only minimal relief. He describes the pain as coming and going, occurring several times a day. He mowed the lawn yesterday and feels that may have contributed to the pain. He denies any recent job-related stressors or unusual activities. He reports that his brother is a heart doctor and he thought it might not be the heart, but he is concerned about the chest pain.

Findings
The patient reports chest pain that comes and goes, often associated with the elbow and arm pain. A slight murmur is auscultated. The blood pressure was 150/90 mmHg. The patient denies shortness of breath. An EKG is ordered to evaluate for possible cardiac ischemia. A glucose test will be performed to assess diabetes control.

Assessment
The patient presents with right elbow pain radiating to the neck, accompanied by chest pain. Clinical findings raise concern for possible cardiac ischemia, warranting further investigation with an EKG and cardiac evaluation.

Plan
An EKG will be obtained to evaluate for cardiac ischemia. Aspirin and nitroglycerin have been ordered to alleviate chest pain. A glucose test will be performed to assess diabetes control and to monitor blood sugar levels. The patient will continue on metformin 500mg twice daily for diabetes management. The patient will be monitored for any changes in symptoms and will be scheduled for a follow-up appointment in one week. If the EKG reveals concerning findings, the patient will be transferred to the emergency department for further evaluation and treatment.
