Chief Complaint
Pain in the right flank radiating to the groin.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing pain in the right flank for the past two weeks, which radiates down to his groin. The pain is intermittent and depends on his fluid intake and activity level, but is consistently present. He describes it as under control most of the time, but occasionally it becomes more intense. He notes an episode where the pain radiated to one of his testicles, which subsided. He has been taking ibuprofen, which provides some relief. He denies nausea, vomiting, or fever. He has a family history of kidney stones. He previously passed a stone on his own and has undergone surgery twice.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation in the right flank. Blood pressure is slightly elevated. The abdomen is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a kidney stone, likely located in the proximal right ureter. Imaging confirms a 0.5 cm stone.

Plan
Continue ibuprofen as needed for breakthrough pain. Prescribe tramadol 50mg for breakthrough pain. Order a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks to assess progress and discuss potential lithotripsy if symptoms persist. Advise the patient to continue using a strainer when urinating.
