Chief Complaint
Worsening headaches over the past few months.

History of Present Illness
Mr. Ward reports worsening headaches over the past few months, initially rated as 3/10 in severity, which have gradually increased to 6/10. The headaches are typically worse in the morning and last for several hours, with no specific alleviating factors. He denies any prior history of headaches and reports no recent trauma. He has noticed recent bumping into door frames. He denies any fever, rash, neck stiffness, numbness, tingling, or passing out. He has not taken any medications for the headaches and reports trying acetaminophen with limited relief. He was adopted and has no family history of relevant diseases. He works as a computer programmer and is married with a small house.

Findings
Physical exam reveals alert and oriented patient. Cranial nerve examination is grossly intact. Visual acuity and eye movements are normal. Pupils are equal and reactive. Visual field testing reveals bitemporal hemianopia. Color vision is normal. No focal motor or sensory deficits in the upper or lower extremities. The patient reports bumping into door frames.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with a pituitary adenoma, identified incidentally during workup for worsening headaches. Clinical and radiographic findings are consistent with compression of the optic chiasm.

Plan
I will refer Mr. Ward to the eye doctor for a full visual field exam prior to surgery. We will schedule a consultation with our surgical scheduler, Deborah, to arrange for a transsphenoidal approach to surgical excision of the adenoma. We will discuss the risks and benefits of surgery with the patient, including the potential for anesthesia-related complications, surgical complications such as infection and wound issues, and potential neurological sequelae. Follow-up will be scheduled to monitor his progress post-operatively.
