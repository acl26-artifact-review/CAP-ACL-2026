Chief Complaint
Symptoms of discomfort, a change in discharge, and odor in her vaginal region, along with right-sided vaginal pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing discomfort, a change in vaginal discharge, and an odor in her vaginal region over the past week. She notes that the right side of her vagina is starting to become painful. She denies fever or chills. She recently learned that her partner has been in sexual encounters with other people and desires evaluation for STDs. She reports having an irregular pap smear from approximately six months ago, which was normal. She has been dating her current partner for three years, and believes he has been cheating on her for approximately three months. She has not had any other sexually transmitted infections.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal external genitalia and labia. Physiological vaginal discharge is noted, with normal color and odor. No cervical motion tenderness or adnexal tenderness or masses are palpated. The cervix is normal.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is consistent with a possible sexually transmitted infection and requires further investigation.

Plan
Order testing for gonorrhea, chlamydia, trichomoniasis, syphilis, HIV, and hepatitis C. Follow up in three days to discuss test results and provide further counseling regarding safe sex practices and partner notification.
