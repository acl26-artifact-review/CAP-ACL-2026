The patient reports a knee pain following a fall while changing a light bulb, specifically in the inside of his knee. He describes pain with knee flexion and with twisting the knee. He has been using ice and ibuprofen with minimal relief. He takes digoxin for atrial fibrillation.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness over the medial aspect of the knee, particularly the medial meniscus area. There is mild swelling and tenderness with valgus stressing. Neurological exam is intact. X-rays show no fractures but some fluid in the knee.

A:
The patient has an acute medial meniscus sprain or strain.

P:
Order a knee brace and crutches for temporary use. Recommend Motrin 800mg every 6 hours as needed for pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to reassess and consider an MRI if symptoms persist.
