Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing left hand pain for the past month, worsening with activity. He describes it as a sharp, aching pain. He also reports experiencing shortness of breath, particularly with exertion. The patient denies any recent trauma. He asks if anyone in his family has a history of allergies. He reports recurrent lung infections over the past six months. He also reports coughing.

Findings
The patient reports pain in the left hand. Examination reveals no acute distress. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Oxygen saturation is 96% on room air.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and history are most consistent with hypersensitivity pneumonitis and farmer's lung. He also has a history of recurrent pneumonia episodes.

Plan
Order a cat scan of the lungs. Order a pulmonary function test. Recommend wearing a respirator. Prescribe an oral steroid. Schedule a follow-up in two weeks to assess response to treatment. ,
