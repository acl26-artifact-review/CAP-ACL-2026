Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and when lying on his left side. He denies any specific injury or trauma. He has been taking acetaminophen, two extra-strength tablets every six to eight hours, which provides some relief. He reports renovating his basement and doing a lot of physical labor, including using a power saw. He has been living in the house for several years and recently decided to undertake renovations. He denies any numbness or tingling in his arm or hand.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder, with tenderness to palpation over the greater tuberosity. Neurovascular examination demonstrates brisk cap refill in the left arm and intact motor and sensory function. X-ray of the left shoulder is unremarkable, showing no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI of the left shoulder to further evaluate the rotator cuff tendons. I am also referring the patient for physical therapy, starting with a six to eight week course. The patient should continue taking acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately two weeks to assess progress.
