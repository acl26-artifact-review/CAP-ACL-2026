Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain following a lacrosse injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began approximately one week ago after playing lacrosse with his children. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, exacerbated by movement. He denies a specific injury, but recalls being 'rough' with his son. He tried taking Advil, which provided some relief. He also reports getting hit while playing with his thirteen-year-old son. The patient's child checked him several times with a sick.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the olecranon area of the posterior elbow, mild pain with flexion and extension, and a normal range of motion. The patient's right elbow exam shows a grade 2 murmur. The patient's lungs are clear bilaterally, and his heart exam reveals a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur. Blood pressure is 150/90. Blood sugar levels have been between 80 and 100. The patient denies chest pain or abdominal pain.

Assessment
The patient's right elbow pain is most likely due to a contusion and inflammation, consistent with the reported mechanism of injury. He has hypertension.

Plan
Prescribe 800 mg ibuprofen twice daily for pain and inflammation. Advise icing the elbow three times daily for 20 minutes. Recommend avoiding lacrosse activity for two weeks. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Schedule an a1c test to assess diabetes control. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. A nurse will administer the prescriptions.
