Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing injury two weeks ago.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right knee pain following a skiing injury two weeks ago, sustained during a fall while skiing down a black diamond run. He describes attempting to speed past another skier and subsequently colliding with a tree, resulting in a twisting injury. He reports hearing a pop at the time of the injury. The patient has been wearing a brace and using THC cream for pain relief. He has experienced some swelling and stiffness, particularly in the morning, which improves with walking. He has had previous knee surgery at age 14 due to a sports injury, involving reconstruction. He describes a history of being a competitive gymnast and skier. He currently engages in regular skiing (three times per week) and occasionally participates in gym workouts. He denies any other significant medical history or current medications besides THC gummies.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild swelling and bruising on the right knee. The patient demonstrates a slight limp. Range of motion is limited, with pain on dorsiflexion and pain on palpation of the medial aspect of the knee. The patient reports pain with weight-bearing, particularly during prolonged walks. X-rays demonstrate mild osteoarthritis and no acute fracture.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are most consistent with a posterior cruciate ligament strain and underlying osteoarthritis, likely exacerbated by the previous knee surgery.

Plan
I am recommending physical therapy to improve strength and range of motion in the right knee. I will prescribe meloxicam 15mg once daily for pain and inflammation, and ibuprofen 800mg twice daily as needed. I recommend limiting skiing and strenuous gym activities for now. Follow up in four weeks to assess progress or adjust treatment as needed.
