Chief Complaint
Hypogonadism

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being referred for hypogonadism. He is currently taking 1 milliliter of testosterone every 10 days, along with finasteride and cialis. He reports feeling more vigorous and sleeping well since starting testosterone, with some improvements in bloodwork. He has been taking half a 5 milligram finasteride pill daily and 5 milligrams of cialis on workout days, otherwise 2.5 milligrams. His last PSA was 0.66 and his last testosterone was greater than 1,500. He is currently injecting testosterone every 8 days. He is considering indole-3-carbinol supplementation.

Findings
The patient's cholesterol and triglycerides were 265 milligrams per deciliter, which decreased to 145 milligrams per deciliter after starting testosterone. Red blood cell count has increased. Physical exam reveals normal heart and lung sounds. The patient's PSA today is 0.6, which was 0.5 last year.

Assessment
The patient presents with hypogonadism. His testosterone levels are elevated, but his peaks are greater than 1,500, increasing the risk of adverse effects.

Plan
Continue current testosterone regimen at 140 mg daily. Order cholesterol and triglyceride levels to monitor. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess response and adjust testosterone dose as needed. Counsel the patient on the importance of monitoring for excessive testosterone levels and potential side effects. Arrange for a repeat PSA test in one year.
