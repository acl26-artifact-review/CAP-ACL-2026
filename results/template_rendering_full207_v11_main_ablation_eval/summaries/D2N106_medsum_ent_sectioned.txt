Chief Complaint
New onset shortness of breath and wheezing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing new onset shortness of breath and wheezing for approximately two years, initially attributed to outdoor activities. He describes a noticeable increase in breathlessness recently, occurring in all three environments - indoors, outdoors, and with his cat. He reports experiencing eye watering, though not significant discomfort. He has used a nebulizer previously for relief and currently uses an inhaler when symptoms arise. He also reports some diarrhea after heavy drinking. He denies any prior history of allergies or asthma, and reports no current nausea, vomiting, or lip/throat swelling. He notes that his dog also experiences similar eye watering symptoms.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal vital signs, including pulse and oxygen saturation. No rash is observed. Lungs exhibit faint expiratory wheezing bilaterally. No stridor is audible. Examination does not reveal angioedema.

Assessment
Newly diagnosed allergic asthma.

Plan
Prescribe albuterol inhaler for symptomatic relief. Prescribe singulair ten milligrams daily to reduce asthma exacerbations. Order allergy testing (skin testing) to identify triggers. Schedule follow-up in one week to discuss allergy test results and further management strategies.
