S:
The patient reports discomfort, a change in vaginal discharge, and odor in her vaginal region. She states that the right side of her vagina is starting to become painful. She denies fevers or chills. She reports learning her partner has been in sexual encounters with other people and wants to be evaluated for STDs.

O:
Physical exam reveals normal labia majora and labia minora. Physiological vaginal discharge is noted, with normal color and smell. Cervix is normal. Vaginal wall is normal. No cervical motion tenderness or adnexal tenderness or masses are noted. Swabs were obtained for STD testing.

A:
The patient is concerned about potential STDs due to recent partner history and reported symptoms.

P:
Obtain swabs for gonorrhea, chlamydia, trichomoniasis testing. Order blood testing for syphilis, HIV, and hepatitis C. Counsel the patient that results will take variable amounts of time to return and to call the office in three days if results are not received. Schedule a follow-up appointment for annual gynecological care if testing results are negative.
