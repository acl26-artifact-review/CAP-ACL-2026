Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his left hand, which started approximately one week ago and has been gradually worsening. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, and rates it as 6 out of 10. He states the pain is exacerbated by movement. The patient also reports experiencing shortness of breath, particularly with exertion, for the past three weeks. He denies any recent trauma. He reports coughing up blood intermittently over the past week. He has a history of diabetes and hypertension, both managed with metformin. He also has a history of asthma, which he controls with an inhaler. He recently traveled to Southeast Asia. He denies leg swelling or bruising.

Findings
The patient's vital signs are as follows: blood pressure 140/90 mmHg, heart rate 88 bpm, respiratory rate 20 breaths per minute. Oxygen saturation is 96% on room air. Physical exam reveals decreased sensation in the left hand. Palpation of the left hand reveals mild tenderness. Lung auscultation reveals mild wheezing bilaterally.

Assessment
The patient presents with left hand pain and shortness of breath. Differential diagnoses include rotator cuff tendinopathy, pneumonia, and pulmonary embolism. Given the hemoptysis and shortness of breath, a pulmonary embolism is a significant concern. The patient's history of diabetes and hypertension are also relevant.

Plan
Order a chest x-ray and cat scan to evaluate for pneumonia and pulmonary embolism. Order blood testing to assess for infection and rule out pulmonary embolism. Continue the patient on metformin as prescribed. Increase the metformin dose from 500mg twice daily to 750mg twice daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess response to treatment and review imaging results.
