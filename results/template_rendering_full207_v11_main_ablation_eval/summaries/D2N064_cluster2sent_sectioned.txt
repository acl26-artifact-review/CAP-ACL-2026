Chief Complaint
Left ankle pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining a left ankle injury, requiring assistance to return home. He has been managing the pain with ibuprofen and elevation/ice. He denies prior ankle injuries. He reports a pain level of 0-10, with the current injury being a 7.

Findings
Examination revealed ecchymosis and swelling of the left lateral malleolus, tenderness to palpation, and strong dorsalis pedis pulses. Capillary refill was brisk bilaterally. X-rays showed no fracture or bony abnormalities. Vital signs were stable with mild elevation.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a lateral ankle sprain.

Plan
Prescribe an air cast and crutches, instructing the patient to avoid weight-bearing on the affected leg. Continue NSAIDs as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to monitor progress and assess continued symptoms.
