Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and cough.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, which has been present for approximately two weeks and is exacerbated by movement. He describes the pain as sharp and intermittent. The patient also reports a cough, productive of green phlegm, and nasal congestion. He denies fever or shortness of breath. He reports difficulty with sugar intake. He is currently taking cough medication that is not working. The patient reports a sore throat. He is drinking lots of water.

Findings
Examination reveals nasal congestion and a runny nose. The patient reports chest pain. The patient denies body aches.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with an upper respiratory infection and left hand pain, likely musculoskeletal in origin. His diabetes is currently being managed.

Plan
Continue cough medication. Order shoulder radiographs. Initiate physical therapy for the left hand. Recommend coricidin hbp. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. The patient is taking metformin at a lower dose of 500 milligrams and hydrochlorothiazide everyday.
