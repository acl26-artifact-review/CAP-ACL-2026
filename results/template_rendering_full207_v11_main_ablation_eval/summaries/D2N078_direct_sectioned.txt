Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a fall while working on his property a week ago. He describes a pop sensation at the time of the injury and subsequent pain with flexion and extension. He reports mild swelling and discomfort. He is currently taking ibuprofen for the pain and swelling. His diet has been somewhat inconsistent recently due to changes during the pandemic.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the medial aspect of the right knee, pain with flexion and extension, and mild edema with effusion. An x-ray previously revealed no fractures or bony abnormalities. The patient's blood pressure was elevated at 150/70 mmHg. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. A grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur is present.

Assessment
Right knee medial collateral ligament strain.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 800 mg twice daily for pain and swelling. Order a knee brace for support. Recommend ice therapy three to four times daily for 20 minutes. Refer the patient to a nutritionist to address dietary changes. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
