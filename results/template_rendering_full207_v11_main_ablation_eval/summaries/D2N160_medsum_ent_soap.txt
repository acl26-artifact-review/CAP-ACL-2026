S:
The patient reports right elbow pain approximately one week ago, sustained during lacrosse play with his children. He describes the pain as sharp and present with any movement. The pain radiates down the forearm. He reports taking Advil for a couple of days, finding it helpful. He states he often forgets to take medications. His blood sugar levels have been between 80 and 100 when checked. He denies any belly pain or chest pain. He does not recall a fall.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation of the olecranon area of the posterior elbow with pain during flexion and extension. Range of motion is normal. The heart exam reveals a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. An X-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
The patient's right elbow pain is likely due to a contusion sustained during lacrosse play. His blood pressure is elevated, and his diabetes is well-controlled. The musculoskeletal pain is the primary concern.

P:
Rescribe 800 mg ibuprofen twice daily for pain and inflammation. Advise icing the elbow three times daily for 20 minutes. Recommend avoiding lacrosse play for two weeks. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Obtain an A1c test to assess glycemic control. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to monitor progress.
