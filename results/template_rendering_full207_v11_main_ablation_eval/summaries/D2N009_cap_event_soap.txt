S:
The patient reports being a 55-year-old male with a past medical history of prior discectomy, and he is currently experiencing back pain that began five days ago after pushing a refrigerator. He reports that his wife made him push the refrigerator, and he feels something in his lower right back. He has been taking ibuprofen and occasionally tylenol and ibuprofen together for the pain, and he denies any neurological symptoms such as numbness or tingling, or any other symptoms like chest pain, shortness of breath, or abdominal pain.

O:
The patient's vital signs are within normal limits, including blood pressure and heart rate. A physical exam reveals pain to palpation of the right lumbar spine, decreased flexion and extension of the back, and a positive straight leg raise on the right. The lumbar spine x-ray shows good bony alignment without fracture. Lab results are unremarkable.

A:
The patient is diagnosed with a lumbar strain, likely secondary to the recent incident with the refrigerator. The MRI is ordered to rule out other potential causes.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain management. Prescribe ultram 50 mg every four hours as needed for pain. Refer to physical therapy after the MRI results are available. Advise the patient to discontinue ibuprofen and continue with tylenol if desired. Schedule a follow-up appointment to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
