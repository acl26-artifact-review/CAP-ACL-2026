Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain and swelling following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports falling on the ice yesterday, resulting in right ankle pain and swelling. He describes being very careful but still slipped. He has been elevating and icing the ankle. He has taken ibuprofen for the pain. He denies prior ankle injuries but admits to playing soccer for many years, including currently for the community league. His wife reports he enjoys watching him play. He denies any numbness in the foot.

Findings
Physical examination reveals ecchymosis and swelling over the lateral malleolus. Palpation elicits tenderness. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong and capillary refill is less than three seconds. Sensation is intact to light touch. Right ankle x-ray demonstrates no fracture.

Assessment
Right ankle sprain.

Plan
The patient should keep his leg elevated and continue icing the ankle. An aircast has been prescribed and crutches ordered for the next one to two days. Walking should be initiated as tolerated. Follow-up will be scheduled in two weeks to assess progress. Continue taking NSAIDs as needed for pain. The physician will contact the patient in two weeks to assess his progress and determine readiness to return to soccer.
