Chief Complaint
Right foot pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his right foot following a fall yesterday where he dropped a landscape brick on it. He initially tried to keep working but found it difficult. He called a few names and then sought medical attention. He has previously injured his ankle twenty years ago, which healed, but occasionally experiences soreness. He also has a history of surgery on that ankle. He has been using lucken tape for the current injury and has not taken any medications. He reports numbness in the entire foot since the incident. He has been avoiding weight-bearing.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bruising on the right foot, swelling, and tenderness to palpation at the midfoot. Capillary refill is brisk. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong and bilaterally equal. X-ray reveals dorsal displacement of the base of the second metatarsal with a 3mm separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and presence of bony fragments. MRI is pending.

Assessment
Lisfranc fracture with associated soft tissue injury. Risk of long-term complications including archal collapse and arthritis.

Plan
Order an MRI to assess ligament injuries. Anticipate same-day outpatient surgery to align the bones and stabilize with plates and screws. Provide a cast and crutches for weight-shifting. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 24 hours post-procedure and again in two weeks. Advise patient of the importance of adherence to the cast and crutch protocol.
