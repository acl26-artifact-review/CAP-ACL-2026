Chief Complaint

The patient reports a history of DCIS, confirmed by pathology following a core needle biopsy of a right breast mass in December 2019. She underwent a lumpectomy in January 2020, removing five benign lymph nodes. Subsequent pathology confirmed the DCIS with negative margins. The patient's last mammogram was in January 2021, which was negative for recurrence or malignancy. She reports no recent weight loss or gain, headaches, bone pain, urinary symptoms, or blood in the stools, but does experience age-related back and knee pain. She takes coq10, vitamin D, vitamin C, fish oil, and elderberry fruit. She has a penicillin allergy and no other reported allergies. She declined endocrine therapy after discussion. She had her tubes tied after her last baby.

Findings
The patient's exam reveals no palpable masses and skin thickening at the medial inferior aspect of the right breast, likely due to radiation. Her ECOG performance status is zero. Mammogram in January 2021 was negative for recurrence or malignancy. The patient had one polyp in the sigmoid colon in 2018, which was benign.

Assessment
The patient's post-lumpectomy status is stable with no evidence of recurrence or malignancy. Stage 0 DCIS with positive ER and PR, invasive ductal carcinoma of the right breast.

Plan
