Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies recent trauma and reports partial relief with acetaminophen. He also reports recently consuming a large number of holiday sweets and being surrounded by holiday treats.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy, likely exacerbated by recent dietary changes.

Plan
Order complete metabolic panel and shoulder radiographs. Initiate physical therapy and prescribe metformin, 500 milligrams twice daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
