Chief Complaint
Follow-up of chronic osteoarthritis and gout.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports ongoing osteoarthritis, primarily in her elbows, which has been exacerbated by prolonged computer use in the office. She also reports a recent gout flare-up approximately three months ago, treated with medication, and currently reports stable psoriasis and scalp psoriasis treated with topical steroid cream. She experiences occasional numbness in her hands when typing for extended periods, relieved by shaking her arms. She reports that she has been managing her gout effectively with current colchicine therapy.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild edema and inflammation in the right olecranon, with tenderness and effusion upon palpation. Range of motion in the right elbow is limited by pain and stiffness, with pain reported with flexion and extension. Cervical lymph nodes are unremarkable. Vital signs are within normal limits. Autoimmune panel results show no systemic inflammatory process. ESR is within normal limits. Uric acid levels are within the normal range.

Assessment
The patient's primary diagnoses are chronic osteoarthritis and stable gout. She is currently managing her psoriasis effectively.

Plan
Order a right elbow x-ray to evaluate the osteoarthritis. Prescribe meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily for pain and inflammation. Continue colchicine 0.6 milligrams twice daily as needed for gout flare-ups. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to review the x-ray results and assess treatment response. Refer to physical therapy for evaluation and management of the right elbow osteoarthritis.
