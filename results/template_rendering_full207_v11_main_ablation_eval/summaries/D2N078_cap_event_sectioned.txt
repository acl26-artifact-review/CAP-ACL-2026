Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a fall while working on his property a week ago. He describes a pop sensation at the time of the injury and subsequent pain and swelling. He has been taking ibuprofen for the swelling. He notes some discomfort with knee flexion and extension, and reports mild pain with palpation of the medial aspect of the knee. He reports a slight improvement in mobility over the past week, but still experiences discomfort.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate edema and effusion in the right knee. Palpation of the medial aspect elicits tenderness. Range of motion is limited due to pain, with decreased flexion and extension. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable. X-ray findings were negative for fracture, but revealed a medial collateral ligament strain.

Assessment
Right knee medial collateral ligament strain.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 800 mg every 6 hours as needed for pain and inflammation. Order a knee brace for support. Refer to nutrition counseling to address dietary sodium intake. Schedule follow-up in 2 weeks to assess healing and adjust treatment as needed.
