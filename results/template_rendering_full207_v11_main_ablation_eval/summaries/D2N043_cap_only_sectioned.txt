Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, which began approximately one week ago. The pain is constant and is described as aching. The patient denies any recent trauma. He reports that the pain is exacerbated by gripping objects. He denies any numbness or tingling. The patient reports increased stress levels and is planning to join a gym. He previously attempted to quit smoking but has struggled with cravings. The patient is going to be a new father.

Findings
Examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the distal interphalangeal joint of the left hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No swelling or erythema is noted. The patient smokes a pack-a-half a day.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are most consistent with osteoarthritis of the left hand. He also has a history of nicotine dependence and reports increased stress levels.

Plan
Start a nicotine patch dosage reduction over time. Recommend a smoking cessation aid. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress and adjust medication as needed. The doctor recommends setting a quit date. The clinician wants to discuss any handouts received today.
