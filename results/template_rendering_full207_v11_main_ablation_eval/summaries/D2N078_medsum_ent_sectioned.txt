Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a fall while working on his property. He states he slipped and fell, and is still experiencing issues with the knee. He reports some discomfort when flexing and extending the knee, particularly with pressure applied. He describes a slight swelling and reports taking ibuprofen for the swelling. He has a history of hypertension and is currently taking 20 milligrams of lisinopril. He reports needing assistance with his diet, specifically reducing sodium intake, as it was a concern during the pandemic. He denies chest pain, belly pain, or shortness of breath. He has been taking his medications regularly.

Findings
Physical examination reveals pain to palpation of the medial aspect of the right knee. There is pain with flexion and extension, and some edema and effusion around the knee. The x-ray did not reveal any fractures or bony abnormalities. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally with no wheezes or crackles. Heart exam reveals a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is consistent with his prior history. The knee brace is applied.

Assessment
The patient has a medial collateral ligament strain (mcl strain) secondary to the fall. He is currently managing his hypertension with lisinopril.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 800 milligrams twice daily for pain and swelling. A knee brace will be provided to support the knee. Referral to nutrition counseling will be arranged to assist with dietary changes, specifically reducing sodium intake. Continue lisinopril at 20 milligrams. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Advise the patient to elevate his leg and avoid activity if possible.
