Chief Complaint
Follow-up of chronic problems.

History of Present Illness
Bruce is a 60-year-old male with a past medical history of depression, type 2 diabetes, and hypertension. He presents for follow-up of his chronic problems. Over the summer, he tried something new - he started going for long walks outside every morning, enjoying the nice weather and the blooming plants. On weekends, he even got involved in some yard work. However, with the changing seasons and the increasing cold, he hasn't been able to maintain this routine and is looking for alternative options. He's not ready to try medication yet. He reports anxiety and pressure, and has experienced stress. He denies chest pain or blood in his stool. He's been managing his diabetes with metformin and is taking 20 mg of lisinopril daily. He reports his hemoglobin A1c is elevated at 8%. He's been through the holidays and didn't stick to his diet as much as he'd like, but is trying to get back on track. He's been monitoring his blood sugar. He's been doing well with his blood pressure, maintaining a blood pressure of around 130/80. He has one plus non-pitting edema bilaterally in his lower extremities. He denies any weight loss.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slight two out of six systolic ejection murmur heard at the left base. The lungs are clear. The patient has one plus non-pitting edema bilaterally in the lower extremities. The EKG shows no acute abnormalities. The patient's hemoglobin A1c is elevated at 8%.

Assessment
The patient has type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and anxiety. He is managing his chronic conditions with medication and lifestyle modifications.

Plan
I'm going to refer the patient to psychiatry for talk therapy. I'm going to order a lipid panel. I'm going to increase the patient's metformin dosage to 1000 mg twice a day. I'm going to order an echocardiogram to recheck the murmur. The nurse will be in soon to check him out. I'm going to schedule a follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist. I'm going to order a vacation for the patient.
