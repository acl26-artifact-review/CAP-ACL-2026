Chief Complaint
Right index finger pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right index finger pain that began five days ago after a skiing accident, where he reportedly jammed the finger. He took ibuprofen, but found it did not significantly alleviate the pain. He denies any other injuries sustained during the skiing incident. He has a history of chronic constipation managed with miralax.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the right index finger DIP joint, with pain reproduced with direct pressure and joint movement. The right hand x-ray demonstrates no fractures or bony abnormalities, indicating a sprain of the distal interphalangeal joint (DIP joint).
