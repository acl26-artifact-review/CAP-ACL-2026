Chief Complaint
The patient reports back pain for approximately one week, exacerbated by sudden movements and localized to the lower back.

History of Present Illness
The patient describes the onset of back pain approximately one week ago, initially sharp but recurring. He denies a specific injury, attributing the pain to a possible twist while reaching for something. Over-the-counter medications have not provided relief. The pain is primarily located in the center of his lower back, occasionally radiating to the left side, and worsens with sudden movements. He reports occasional episodes of left eye irritation, which he believes may be related to the back pain. The patient is currently managing his diabetes with metformin twice daily and self-testing his blood sugar in the mornings, though his readings have been trending higher. He admits to frequent visits to his family's bakery, though he attempts to limit his intake. The patient is currently taking four hundred milligrams of ibuprofen as needed with tylenol up to three times daily and a flexeril at night.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the lumbar region, particularly at the center of the lower back. Range of motion is limited. Neurological examination is unremarkable, with no reported numbness, tingling, or weakness in the lower extremities. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart rhythm is regular and auscultation reveals no murmurs. Blood pressure was measured at 138/82. The patient's blood sugar readings have been trending towards the higher side.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a musculoskeletal low back strain. His history of diabetes is also relevant to his overall health management.

Plan
I am ordering a low back x-ray to assess for any structural abnormalities. I am also ordering physical therapy to address the pain and strengthen the back muscles. I am prescribing 400 milligrams of ibuprofen as needed with tylenol up to three times daily. I am also prescribing flexeril at night. I recommend monitoring blood sugars in the mornings and will order an A1C test. I will consider referral to a pain specialist if symptoms persist despite conservative management. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
