Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports chest pain, describing it as a tightness and pressure sensation. He states the pain started approximately four days ago and has gradually worsened. He denies any recent trauma or strenuous activity. He reports experiencing similar chest discomfort three months ago, which resolved with rest and over-the-counter pain relievers. He also reports occasional heartburn, but not currently experiencing it. The patient reports difficulty swallowing. He is currently trying to lose weight.

Findings
The patient reports chest discomfort. The doctor observed mild tenderness to light palpation in the upper abdominal quadrants. An EKG was performed and blood tests were performed. Examination findings were normal.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are concerning for gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), esophagitis, and possible musculoskeletal etiology. Anxiety is a potential cause of the patient's condition.

Plan
The doctor recommends an endoscopy with biopsy (egd). The doctor recommends follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist. The patient should continue to monitor for symptoms and report any worsening.
