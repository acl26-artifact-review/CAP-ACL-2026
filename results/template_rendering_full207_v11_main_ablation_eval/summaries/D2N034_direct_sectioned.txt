Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for the past three weeks, initially attributed to basement remodeling activities. He denies a specific injury or trauma. He has been using acetaminophen with limited relief. He has not recently used icing. The patient reports that the pain is constant and worsens with overhead activities. He has not tried physical therapy previously.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder. Palpation of the greater tuberosity elicits tenderness. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the left hand. Sensation to light touch is intact. Radiographic examination demonstrates no fractures or bony abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order an MRI of the left shoulder to evaluate for further injury. Refer the patient to physical therapy for a six to eight week course of treatment. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
