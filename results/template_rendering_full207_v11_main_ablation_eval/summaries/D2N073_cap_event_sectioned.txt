Chief Complaint
Persistent iron deficiency anemia and related symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a long-standing history of iron deficiency anemia, dating back to age 13. He was unable to donate blood around December and subsequently received notification of anemia. He has experienced fatigue, feverishness with chills, headaches, and occasional wheezing with activity. He notes chilling sensations and increased sensitivity to cold. He reports worsening anxiety and depression. His last colonoscopy was approximately five to six years ago and revealed no polyps. He is currently taking ferrous sulfate 25mg twice daily with orange juice and vitamin B12. He denies any history of sedation issues.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no significant findings. The patient reports no tenderness to palpation of the abdomen.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and history are consistent with persistent iron deficiency anemia, complicated by possible underlying gastrointestinal pathology. Further investigation is warranted.

Plan
We will schedule an upper endoscopy and colonoscopy to evaluate for sources of blood loss. A referral to a hematologist has been sent. We will also initiate iron supplementation with ferrous sulfate, recommending concurrent vitamin C intake to enhance absorption. Follow-up in four weeks is scheduled to assess response to treatment.
