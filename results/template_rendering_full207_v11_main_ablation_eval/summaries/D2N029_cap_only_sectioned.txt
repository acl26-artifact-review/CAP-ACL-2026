Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, which began approximately one week ago. He describes the pain as sharp and intermittent, and rates it as 6/10. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is exacerbated by gripping and lifting objects. He denies any numbness or tingling. He reports no fever or other systemic symptoms. The patient reports that he has had a skin spot checked by his primary care physician, which was determined to be benign.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the volar aspect of the left wrist. Range of motion is limited by pain. Strength is 4/5 in all directions. There is no sensory deficit. The patient's skin is intact and unremarkable. No swelling or erythema is noted.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome. The history of a benign skin lesion is non-contributory.

Plan
Order a wrist ultrasound to evaluate for carpal tunnel syndrome. Recommend wrist splinting and activity modification. Follow up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
