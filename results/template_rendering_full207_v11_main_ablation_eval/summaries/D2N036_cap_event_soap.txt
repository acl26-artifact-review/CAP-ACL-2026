S:
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his fingers and wrist, with pain for several months, primarily at night and exacerbated by yard work. He describes having to shake his hands to relieve the symptoms and occasional numbness in his little finger. He reports dropping things due to decreased sensation. He drinks approximately two beers on Friday and Saturday nights. He denies any family history of rheumatoid arthritis. The patient states he feels weakness in his left hand.

O:
Physical exam reveals decreased grip strength on the left hand. Tinel's sign is positive bilaterally. Palpation of the right wrist reveals no abnormalities. Neurological exam is nonfocal. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome. Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome is suspected.

P:
Order an EMG study. Prescribe a wrist splint. Recommend ibuprofen 600 mg every six hours as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess treatment response and consider further interventions, including surgical consultation if necessary. Refer the patient to a landscape designer for potential backyard renovation projects.
