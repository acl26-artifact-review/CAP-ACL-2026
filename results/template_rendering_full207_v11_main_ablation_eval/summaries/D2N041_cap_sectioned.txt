Chief Complaint
Sore throat and difficulty swallowing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a sore throat for four days, accompanied by difficulty swallowing and a slightly stuffy nose. He reports chills and sweating last night and mentions swollen tonsils. He denies fever but reports feeling generally unwell. He reports that he drinks mostly water due to the difficulty swallowing. He received a COVID vaccine recently, which made him feel sick, and is hoping not to receive another one soon. He has had strep throat as a child but not as an adult. He works as a cashier at a supermarket. He takes lisinopril 20mg daily with his multivitamin and vitamin D, metformin 1000mg twice daily, and drinks wine at night.

Findings
The patient's temperature is 100.4°F. Blood pressure is 130/80 mmHg. Heart rate is 84 bpm. Physical exam reveals bilateral erythema and edema of the peritonsillar space with exudates present bilaterally. The throat is erythematous. Cervical lymphadenopathy is present on the right. Lung exam reveals coarse rhonchi at the bases that clear with cough. The patient reports no pain to palpation of the frontal or maxillary sinuses.

Assessment
The patient presents with an upper respiratory infection, likely complicated by possible strep throat. Hypertension is also present.

Plan
Order a rapid strep test. Prescribe amoxicillin 500mg three times a day for ten days. Prescribe lidocaine swish and swallow. Increase lisinopril to 40mg daily. Order a lipid panel. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Schedule a follow-up appointment for monitoring blood pressure and diabetes control.
