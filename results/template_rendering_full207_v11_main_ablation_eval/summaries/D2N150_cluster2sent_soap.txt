S:
The patient reports a right knee injury three days ago following a pop while playing soccer, with associated pain ranging from 4 to 5 on a 10-point scale. He describes some swelling that has decreased, and reports using ibuprofen with mild relief. He reports pain primarily along the medial joint line upon palpation. He denies significant pain or pain elsewhere.

O:
The patient exhibits a marked limp on the right leg upon ambulation. Physical examination reveals significant effusion in the right knee and ecchymosis. Neurovascular examination of the lower extremities is intact. Muscle strength is 5/5 bilaterally. Range of motion is limited, with approximately 115 degrees of terminal extension and 20-30 degrees of terminal flexion secondary to pain.

A:
The patient's presentation is highly suggestive of an anterior cruciate ligament tear, based on the physical exam findings and history.

P:
Order an MRI of the right knee to evaluate the ACL integrity. Prescribe meloxicam 50 mg daily for anti-inflammatory effects. Instruct the patient to ice the knee as needed. Recommend a knee immobilizer and crutches for weight-bearing. Schedule the patient for an MRI and follow-up to discuss results and potential surgical intervention. Advise the patient to reduce activity and avoid jogging until further evaluation.
