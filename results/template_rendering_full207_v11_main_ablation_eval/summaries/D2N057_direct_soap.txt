S:
The patient reports a two-week history of intermittent toe pain, exacerbated by walking. Last week, the pain became more significant, particularly during a trade show where he could not walk. He describes the pain as throbbing, occasionally sharp, especially with movement. He finds it hurts significantly when the sheets come down on it at night. He reports feeling a bone spur and has lost cartilage in the joint. He denies any injury and does not recall when the problem started. He has never had stomach problems and enjoys spicy food. The patient reports no difficulty with riding a bike, preferring to use the back of his feet on the pedals.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the great toe joint, with pain upon movement in multiple planes. Range of motion demonstrates pain with plantar flexion to 20 degrees. A large bony spur is palpated on the top of the great toe joint. The patient reports feeling grinding sensations when pushing the toe back and forth. There is mild tenderness to palpation along the lateral aspect of the great toe joint. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with hallux rigidus with secondary osteoarthritis. He has significant cartilage loss and a large bony spur, indicating chronic disease progression.

P:
Rescribe an orthotic insert for his shoe. Order meloxicam 15mg daily for one week. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess treatment response. Advise the patient to discontinue meloxicam and contact the clinic if he experiences significant stomach upset. Counsel the patient on the potential need for more invasive interventions, such as a joint injection or surgery, if symptoms persist despite conservative management. Recommend he continue with his regular activities, including fishing, and monitor his symptoms closely.
