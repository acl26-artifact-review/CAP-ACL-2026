Chief Complaint
Persistent coughing and wheezing related to asthma.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a long-standing history of asthma, characterized by intermittent symptoms. He describes a pattern of exacerbations, often triggered by colds and exacerbated by exposure to his aunt's cat. Over the past six months, he's experienced monthly or every other month episodes of coughing and wheezing, leading to frequent urgent care visits and primary care visits. He reports using an albuterol inhaler almost daily and occasionally needing to use it twice a day, particularly at night. He has been prescribed prednisone on several occasions when experiencing severe symptoms. He was hospitalized as a child with asthma but has not had a recurrence. He denies smoking or vaping. He occasionally visits bars where smoking occurs, which can irritate him. He denies any history of seasonal allergies.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 128/82 mmHg, respiratory rate is 16 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation is 99% on room air. Lung exam reveals diminished breath sounds bilaterally with occasional expiratory wheezing. The heart sounds are regular and without murmurs. Pulmonary function tests confirm a diagnosis of asthma. Examination reveals no palpable lymphadenopathy or edema.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with moderate to intermediate asthma. His symptoms are consistent with an active asthma condition.

Plan
The doctor will create an asthma action plan for the patient. The patient will take flovent one hundred ten micrograms one puff twice per day. The patient will use an albuterol inhaler as needed. Follow up in thirty days.
