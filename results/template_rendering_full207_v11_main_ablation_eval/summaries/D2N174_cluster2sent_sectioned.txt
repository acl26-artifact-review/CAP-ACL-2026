Chief Complaint
Joint pain, primarily in the knees.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 57-year-old male with a history of coronary artery disease and lupus who presents today with joint pain, specifically in his knees. He reports increased cycling mileage over the past few days, leading to more pronounced knee pain, particularly in the mornings and afternoons. He describes mild inflammation and slight redness in the knees. He is currently off Plavix but continues to take aspirin daily and reports good adherence to Crestor. He denies chest pain, shortness of breath, fever, abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting. He states that his lupus has been relatively well-controlled, with minimal recent activity. The patient reports no recent injuries.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bilateral edema and slight erythema of the knees, with a mild effusion noted on the right knee. Examination of the heart reveals a two out of six systolic ejection murmur. The patient's vital signs are stable and within normal limits. The most recent EKG demonstrates no acute abnormalities. Right knee x-ray shows no bony abnormalities or fractures. The autoimmune panel has been ordered.

Assessment
The patient's knee pain is concerning for possible lupus flare, given his history and the presence of inflammation. His coronary artery disease remains stable based on the EKG.

Plan
I am ordering an autoimmune panel to evaluate for lupus activity. I also ordered an echocardiogram and a lipid panel. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist or worsen.
