Chief Complaint
Low hemoglobin and fatigue.

History of Present Illness
Joyce is a 50-year-old female presenting for follow-up regarding an abnormal lab finding. She reports feeling really tired lately, noticing a significant decrease in energy over the past couple of months. She used to be very active, running and climbing regularly, about an hour or two a day, but over the past month, she's been so tired and exhausted that she hasn't been able to maintain that level of activity. She denies any chest pain, shortness of breath, fever, chills, or body aches. She reports no blood in her stools and her stools are not dark or charry. She also denies spotting a lot with her periods. She has noticed a slight weight loss, but attributes it to her increased exercise. She reports that she's been doing really well with her copd, stopping smoking several years ago, and watching her salt intake. She's also been feeling okay with her congestive heart failure, continuing her medication regimen. She denies any swelling in her legs.

Findings
The doctor assessed the patient's vital signs, noting they are within normal limits. The patient's hemoglobin level is 8.2. The doctor listened to the patient's heart and lungs, finding a slight two out of six systolic heart murmur and clear lung sounds. The doctor palpated the patient's belly and noted tenderness to palpation in the right lower quadrant. The patient's lab results are pending.

Assessment
The patient presents with anemia, likely secondary to an unidentified cause. The right lower quadrant tenderness warrants further investigation.

Plan
A referral to gastroenterology is ordered for an endoscopy and colonoscopy. The doctor will follow up with the patient in one week to discuss the lab results and further management. The patient will continue taking toprol, lisinopril, and lasix 20 milligrams daily.
