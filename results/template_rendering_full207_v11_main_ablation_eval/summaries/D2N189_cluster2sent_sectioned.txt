Chief Complaint
The patient reports a history of inferior myocardial infarction and subsequent stent placement, and asks about ongoing management.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing a myocardial infarction with subsequent stent placement in the right coronary artery. He denies further chest pain since the procedure, though he experienced some soreness post-surgery which has resolved. He reports experiencing shortness of breath with exertion, particularly when walking upstairs. He takes baby aspirin nightly and reports adhering to a regimen of light activity.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 127/70 mmHg. Examination reveals a slight 3/6 systolic ejection murmur at the left base. The radial artery cardiac catheter site is clean, dry, and intact with a palpable pulse. The lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. Echocardiography revealed mild mitral regurgitation.

Assessment
The patient has a history of inferior myocardial infarction with stent placement, mild mitral regurgitation, and currently remains stable.

Plan
The patient will continue aspirin and brilinta for lifelong and for at least one year, respectively. A repeat echocardiogram is ordered in six months. Enrollment in cardiac rehabilitation is recommended to improve confidence and provide dietary education and counseling. Liver function studies and a lipid panel will be ordered at the next follow-up appointment. The patient is advised to resume swimming after two weeks, with a limited number of laps, and is encouraged to follow up in six weeks.
