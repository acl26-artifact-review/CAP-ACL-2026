Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain and swelling following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports falling on icy pavement yesterday, resulting in right ankle pain and swelling. Initially, he was unable to walk, but last night he elevated and iced the ankle, and today it feels somewhat better, allowing him to put a little more weight on it, though he is still limping. He has experienced this pain since the fall and has taken ibuprofen, which has provided some relief. He denies prior ankle injuries, although he has had many sports-related injuries throughout his life, including a lot of soccer injuries, and is currently playing in a community league. His wife and children attend his soccer games. He has no numbness in his foot.

Findings
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis and swelling over the lateral malleolus. Palpation elicits tenderness. Capillary refill is less than three seconds. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong. Neurovascular exam is intact. Vital signs are stable.

Assessment
Right ankle sprain, consistent with the reported mechanism of injury and physical exam findings.

Plan
The patient should keep his leg elevated, continue icing the ankle, and use crutches for the next one to two days. An aircast will be provided. Follow up in two weeks to assess progress. Continue taking NSAIDs as needed for pain. If symptoms persist, contact the clinic.
