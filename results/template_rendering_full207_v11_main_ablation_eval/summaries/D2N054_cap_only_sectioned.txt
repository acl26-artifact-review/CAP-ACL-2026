Chief Complaint
Right foot pain and numbness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports significant right foot pain and numbness, which began approximately 3 days ago following a fall. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, with a rating of 9 out of 10. The numbness is intermittent and makes it difficult to feel temperature changes. He reports difficulty with weight-bearing and walking. He denies any recent trauma other than the fall. He reports a previous ankle injury 20 years ago and has had surgery on his other ankle. He currently takes acetaminophen as needed for pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate tenderness to palpation over the dorsometatarsal region of the right foot. Neurological exam reveals decreased sensation to light touch and pinprick in the plantar aspect of the foot. Range of motion is limited due to pain. A cervical MRI is ordered.

Assessment
The patient has a lisfranc fracture and a foot injury. He presents with right foot pain and numbness.

Plan
The patient will receive an MRI this afternoon. Surgery is planned for tomorrow. A follow-up appointment is scheduled in two weeks.
