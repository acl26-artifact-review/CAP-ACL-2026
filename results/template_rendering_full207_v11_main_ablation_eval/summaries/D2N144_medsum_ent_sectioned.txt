Chief Complaint
Ongoing management of rheumatoid arthritis and worsening right knee pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports ongoing pain in his right knee, which is still swollen. He states the whole leg is now swollen and reports a pain level of 3/10, with a typical pain level of 6/10. The swelling started approximately two months ago, and compression socks have not been effective. He reports pain when straightening the knee and describes a sore feeling, like an old bruise. The knee feels hot to the touch, and he notes pitting around the knee and ankle with normal capillary refill in the toes. He is currently taking xeljanz 11 milligrams daily and celebrex 200mg daily. He states that the medication has been helpful up until the swelling returned.

Findings
Examination reveals a diminished range of motion in the right knee and pain with impingement testing. The knee is warm to the touch, and pitting is noted around the knee and ankle. Capillary refill is normal in the toes.

Assessment
The patient presents with rheumatoid arthritis and acute right knee inflammation.

Plan
We will proceed with a steroid injection to the right knee, followed by an x-ray to assess the extent of the swelling. We will also schedule physical therapy and consider a surgery consult if conservative measures are insufficient. I will order CMP level checks every two months. I recommend reducing salt intake and elevating the feet as appropriate. Please follow up with your private care physician regarding this condition.
