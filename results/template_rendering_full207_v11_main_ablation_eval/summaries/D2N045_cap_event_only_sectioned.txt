Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent, dull chest pain for the past month, described as a tightness rather than sharp. The pain is not consistently triggered by exertion, but he does notice it more when he is stressed or lying down. He denies any recent trauma or fever. He reports taking ibuprofen as needed for the pain, with limited relief. He states he has a family history of heart disease.

Findings
Blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, regular rhythm. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. No murmurs auscultated. The patient appears in mild distress.

Assessment
Chest pain, etiology uncertain. Differential includes musculoskeletal pain, angina, and anxiety.

Plan
Order ECG and cardiac enzymes. Start aspirin 81 mg daily. Counsel the patient on recognizing symptoms of a heart attack and seeking immediate medical attention. Schedule follow-up in 24-48 hours to review test results and discuss management.
