Chief Complaint
Patient reports a new worrisome heart murmur.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a new worrisome heart murmur, stating her doctor identified it approximately one month ago. She denies chest pain but reports severe leg pain for the past month. She reports experiencing shortness of breath with exertion, particularly when walking too far or quickly. She takes pain pills for her legs and a pill for dizziness when she feels lightheaded. She has had multiple surgeries throughout her life and reports a family history of heart problems, including a heart attack in her son and a stroke in her brother. She has been losing weight gradually over the past year, currently weighing 120 pounds, and reports feeling fatigued. She denies fever or chills. She reports no known allergies.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade 4/6 systolic murmur at the right upper sternal border with radiation to the carotids. Blood pressure is 130/80 mmHg. Heart rhythm is regular with no palpitations noted. The patient reports swelling in the ankles. An EKG shows sinus rhythm with frequent PVCs and an anterior septum infarct pattern.

Assessment
The patient's primary concern is a new heart murmur, potentially indicative of aortic stenosis. She also presents with leg pain, fatigue, and reports feeling lightheaded. Further evaluation is needed to assess the severity of the murmur and rule out other contributing factors.

Plan
Schedule an echocardiogram to assess cardiac structure and function. Continue current medications, including amlodipine, lisinopril, and simvastatin. Discuss statin use with the PCP at the follow-up appointment. Arrange a follow-up appointment in four weeks to discuss the echocardiogram results and further management strategies. Consider a statin trial if appropriate based on the echocardiogram findings.
