Chief Complaint
Hospital follow-up after an acute on chronic CHF exacerbation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports shortness of breath and ankle swelling, which he noticed when he'd been watching his diet. He admits to frequent pizza and chicken wings consumption, particularly at social gatherings. He reports missing a couple of medication doses occasionally but strives to take them consistently. He states he feels much better since being hospitalized and taking a diuretic and new blood pressure medications, and reports no chest pain or shortness of breath at home. He has been consistently monitoring his weight and blood pressure at home.

Findings
The patient's vital signs reveal a blood pressure of 128/72 mmHg, heart rate of 79 bpm, respiratory rate of 26 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 99%. Physical exam reveals no jugular venous distension. Lung auscultation is clear bilaterally. A grade II/6 systolic ejection murmur is heard. Lower extremities demonstrate trace edema. The echocardiogram shows a preserved ejection fraction of 55% and mild to moderate mitral regurgitation.

Assessment
The patient's primary problems include congestive heart failure, hypertension, and chronic kidney disease. His CHF exacerbation was likely triggered by dietary indiscretion and uncontrolled hypertension.

Plan
Continue bumex 2 mg daily. Advise the patient to monitor his weight daily and call if he gains more than 3 pounds in two days. Order a renal artery ultrasound. Continue cozaar 100 mg daily and norvasc 5 mg daily. Schedule a consultation with a registered dietitian to provide dietary education. Follow up in three months.
