Chief Complaint
Follow-up of chronic problems and anxiety.

History of Present Illness
Bruce is a 60-year-old male with a past medical history of depression, type 2 diabetes, and hypertension. He presents for follow-up of his chronic problems. Over the summer, he tried going for long walks every morning and doing a little yard work to address his anxiety, but with the changing seasons and colder weather, he's found it difficult to maintain that routine. He reports feeling a lot of pressure related to Thanksgiving preparations and a general sense of stress. He's been managing his diabetes with metformin and is taking lisinopril for his hypertension. He denies chest pain, blood in his stool, or weight loss. He's been monitoring his blood pressure and reports it's been moderate. He reports feeling some pain in his left hand.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no carotid bruits. A slight two out of six systolic ejection murmur is appreciated at the left base. The patient has one plus non-pitting edema bilaterally in the lower extremities. The EKG shows no concerning findings. The patient's hemoglobin A1c is elevated at 8%.

Assessment
The patient's primary problems include depression, type 2 diabetes, and hypertension.

Plan
The doctor plans to refer the patient to psychiatry for talk therapy. The doctor plans to increase the patient's metformin dosage to 1000 milligrams twice a day. The doctor plans to order a lipid panel. The doctor plans to order an echocardiogram. The doctor plans to continue the patient's lisinopril dosage at 20 milligrams a day. The nurse will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks.
