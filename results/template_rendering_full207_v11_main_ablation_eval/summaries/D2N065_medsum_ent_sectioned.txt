Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain after a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall last night while taking out the trash. He slipped on ice and heard a pop. Initially, he was unable to walk, but now he can put some weight on it, though he is still limping. He describes the pain as constant and throbbing, and it hurts when touched. He iced and elevated his leg last night and took ibuprofen. He has not previously injured this ankle, though he played soccer in high school and knew what to do then. He denies numbness or tingling.

Findings
Physical examination reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus. Tenderness to palpation is noted over the anterior and lateral soft tissue. No laxity is observed on anterior drawer or inversion stress tests. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the dorsalis pedis pulse. The right ankle x-ray demonstrates no fractures or bony abnormalities. Blood pressure is 120/80 mmHg, heart rate 80 bpm, and SpO2 98%.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a right ankle sprain, likely grade 2, with no acute bony injuries.

Plan
I recommend keeping the leg elevated, continuing ice application five times a day for 20 minutes, and using an air cast to stabilize the ankle. Encourage offloading of the leg for one to two days. Prescribe crutches for support. Follow up in two to three weeks to assess healing and adjust treatment as needed. If symptoms persist, consider an MRI.
