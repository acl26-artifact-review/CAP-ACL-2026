Chief Complaint
Right flank pain secondary to suspected kidney stone.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports onset of right flank pain approximately one week prior, initially attributed to strenuous activity in the barn. The pain radiated towards the groin but not the entire length. He reports nausea and diaphoresis during the initial episode. He has not experienced recurrence since, but reports intermittent pain, primarily in the right flank. He has been straining to urinate and has used a strainer to collect urine, without observing blood. He denies prior history of kidney stones or family history of kidney stones.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the right flank. Vital signs are stable; blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, temperature 98.6°F. No fever detected. Abdominal exam is unremarkable, with no rebound tenderness or guarding. Urinalysis reveals no blood, but the urine is darker than usual.

Assessment
Right flank pain secondary to a probable kidney stone. No evidence of hydronephrosis.

Plan
Recommend oral fluids to facilitate stone passage. Prescribe oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours for pain, as needed. Continue acetaminophen as needed for breakthrough pain. Order BMP and urinalysis with urine culture. Schedule follow-up in one to two weeks to assess stone passage; consider lithotripsy if stone does not pass within two weeks.
