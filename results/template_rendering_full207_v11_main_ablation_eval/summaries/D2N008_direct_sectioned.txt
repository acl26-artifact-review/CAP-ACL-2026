Chief Complaint
Low hemoglobin levels.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue and dizziness over the past couple of months, exacerbated by recent travel. She notes increased swelling in her ankles and reports difficulty keeping up with her usual activity level. She had a colonoscopy three years ago that revealed mild diverticulosis with no complications. She previously experienced kidney stones two years ago, with no recent flares. She denies any recent blood in her stools or melena.

Findings
Physical examination reveals trace to one plus pitting edema in the ankles. The patient's blood pressure is within normal limits, and her heart rate is appropriate. A three out of six systolic ejection murmur is heard on cardiac auscultation. The echocardiogram demonstrates a left ventricular ejection fraction of 45% and moderate mitral regurgitation. Hemoglobin is 8.2 g/dL.

Assessment
The patient presents with anemia, likely secondary to chronic disease or other underlying causes. Her congestive heart failure and recent diuretic use may contribute to her symptoms.

Plan
I am ordering an anemia profile to further evaluate the cause of the anemia. I am also ordering a referral to gastroenterology for an endoscopy and repeat colonoscopy. I will initiate lasix 40 milligrams once daily, continue toprol 50 milligrams daily, and lisinopril 10 milligrams daily. I will instruct the patient to monitor her weight daily and call if there is any weight gain. I will also recommend a scale to assist with monitoring sodium intake.
