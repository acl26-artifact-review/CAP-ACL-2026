S:
The patient reports facial pain for approximately two to three months, described as stabbing and shooting, primarily in the right cheekbone area. He notes triggers such as certain temperatures and cold exposure. He also reports occasional headaches, but not a history of migraines. He has tried ibuprofen and tiger balm with no relief. He denies numbness or tingling in the affected face. He reports the pain is typically a ten on a pain scale, but can subside after a few minutes. The patient has tried gabapentin and tegretol for pain management.

O:
Physical exam reveals normal facial symmetry and symmetry of smile. Cotton tape test reveals numbness on the right side of the face, less on the left. MRI shows a small blood vessel abutting and potentially compressing the trigeminal nerve.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with trigeminal neuralgia, likely due to compression of the trigeminal nerve by a small blood vessel.

P:
Rescribe tegretol initially. Order genetic testing to assess potential side effects. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to assess response to medication and discuss surgical options if needed. Advise the patient to send the prescription to our pharmacy.
