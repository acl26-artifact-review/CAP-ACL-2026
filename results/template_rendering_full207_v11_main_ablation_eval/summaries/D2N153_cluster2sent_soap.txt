S:
The patient reports a knee injury following a fall, with initial ankle pain but subsequent knee pain and clicking during ambulation. He describes a fear of injury and reports a history of three prior toe fractures. He reports numbness and tingling in his foot related to shoe choice and experiences increased clicking with deep squatting, particularly when gardening or moving heavy objects. He states his symptoms have remained relatively stable over the past two weeks.

O:
Physical exam reveals slight edema and effusion in the patient's right knee, with a positive varus test and full range of motion. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibialis pulses are palpable. X-ray demonstrates no acute fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
The patient likely has a lateral collateral ligament strain based on the exam findings and reported mechanism of injury.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily. Refer the patient to physical therapy to strengthen surrounding muscles and prevent future injuries. Consider MRI if pain or symptoms persist despite physical therapy.
