Chief Complaint
The patient reports an ulcer on his right foot that has been present for approximately six to eight weeks and is not resolving.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that the ulcer developed after stepping on a nail in his backyard approximately six to eight weeks ago. He notes that his blood sugars have been running slightly higher than normal recently, and he occasionally experiences numbness and tingling in his feet, particularly in colder weather. He reports a history of smoking, having smoked at least two packs per day for twenty plus years, and quit approximately four years ago. He also reports occasional headaches, often described as tension headaches in the front of his head. The patient denies fever or chills. He has been feeling fatigued for the past two months.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a two by three centimeter ulcerated lesion on the right lateral foot near the fifth metatarsal-metatarsophalangeal joint, with associated granulation tissue and slight purulent discharge. Vital signs are unremarkable: blood pressure is 127/78 mmHg, heart rate is 62 bpm, and temperature is normal. A two out of six systolic ejection murmur is auscultated at the left base. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable. No jugular venous distension or carotid bruits are appreciated.

Assessment
The patient presents with a non-healing ulcer of the right foot, likely secondary to underlying diabetic neuropathy and impaired circulation. The ulceration is concerning for potential complications, including infection and impaired healing.

Plan
I am ordering an arterial ultrasound of the lower extremities to assess vascular supply to the foot. I am also ordering a podiatry consult to evaluate the ulcer and provide wound care management. I will continue the patient on aspirin as per current guidelines. I will schedule a follow-up with the primary care physician to discuss further optimization of diabetes management and address the underlying risk factors contributing to the ulceration. I will continue the patient on statin therapy as prescribed.
