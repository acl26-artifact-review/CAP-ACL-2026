Chief Complaint
Annual exam and concerns about blood pressure and heart failure.

History of Present Illness
Martha is a 50-year-old female with a past medical history significant for congestive heart failure, depression and hypertension who presents for her annual exam. Martha reports traveling a lot recently, particularly hiking in northern cascades, and has been doing a good job watching her sodium intake. She reports that her blood pressure has been difficult to manage, and she's been forgetting to take her medication. She's been attending therapy weekly since her last annual exam and reports feeling much better. She denies any chest pain or shortness of breath. She reports some nasal congestion from fall pollen and allergies. She reports that her blood pressure has been elevated during her work hours, which is related to increased stress.

Findings
The doctor finds the patient's blood pressure elevated. A three out of six systolic ejection murmur is heard on heart exam. One plus pitting edema is noted in the lower extremities. The patient's echocardiogram shows a low-ish pumping function of her heart at 45%. The patient's lipid panel from last year was slightly high.

Assessment
The patient has high blood pressure, congestive heart failure, and depression. The patient is currently managing her depression with therapy and is taking medication for her heart failure and hypertension.

Plan
The doctor wants the patient to continue on lisinopril 40mg daily and start lasix 20mg daily. The doctor wants the patient to record their blood pressures every day and send them to the doctor. The doctor wants the patient to take their blood pressure medication as directed. The doctor wants the patient to follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. An echocardiogram will be ordered. The doctor wants to schedule a mammogram.
