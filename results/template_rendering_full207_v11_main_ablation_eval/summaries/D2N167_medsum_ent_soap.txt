S:
The patient reports left knee pain following a fall while running with his niece, who is learning to ride a bike. He describes the pain as throbbing and rates it as 4/10 initially, but it's getting worse and feels like a throbbing pain. He's been limping since the injury and reports the pain is more pronounced on the medial aspect of the knee. He has been taking Tylenol and kinesiology tape, but it hasn't provided sufficient relief. He previously took ibuprofen, but it's not effective and he couldn't take it due to a previous ulcer. He reports taking Tylenol as needed for breakthrough pain.

O:
Examination reveals moderate range of motion in the left knee, some edema and effusion, and pain with flexion and extension. Lachman's and valgus/varus tests are negative. X-ray is normal, showing no fractures or bony abnormalities. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with an MCL strain. There are no signs of fracture or other structural damage.

P:
I'm prescribing tramadol 50mg twice daily for pain management and Tylenol as needed for breakthrough pain. I'm also prescribing a knee immobilizer to be worn for one week to reduce inflammation and promote healing. The patient should ice the knee three to four times a day for 20-30 minutes. I recommend avoiding running for approximately three weeks to allow the MCL to heal. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to assess progress.
