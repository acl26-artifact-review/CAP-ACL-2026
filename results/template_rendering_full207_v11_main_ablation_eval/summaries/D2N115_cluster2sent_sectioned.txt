Chief Complaint
Neck pain following a motor vehicle accident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports neck pain following a motor vehicle accident, characterized by forward head and neck movement with associated pain. He describes the pain as not constant, but exacerbated by head movement. He has attempted to manage the pain with ibuprofen with limited relief. He denies any recent trauma prior to the accident. He reports headaches and pain that radiates to his upper back and shoulders. He reports experiencing pain with neck flexion, extension, and rotation. He denies any hearing or visual disturbances. He did not seek emergency room care following the accident and reports no swelling or bruising to the neck.

Findings
Physical examination reveals positive tenderness to palpation of the soft tissues of the neck. Pain is elicited with neck flexion, extension, and rotation. Pain is also noted with lateral neck bending. There are no signs of fracture or bony abnormalities on radiographic examination.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with whiplash-associated neck pain.

Plan
I am prescribing motrin 600 mg every six to eight hours and flexeril 10 mg every twelve hours as needed. I am ordering an MRI to rule out concussion. I am referring the patient to physical rehabilitation or a chiropractor for evaluation and management once the MRI results are available. I am also recommending conservative measures, including neck relaxation techniques and avoiding activities that exacerbate the pain.
