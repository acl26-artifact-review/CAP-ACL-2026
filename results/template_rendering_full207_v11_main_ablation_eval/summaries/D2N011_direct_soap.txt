S:
The patient reports an episode of chest discomfort during yard work over the weekend, associated with shortness of breath and mild chest pain. He denies prior episodes of chest pain and reports no other current symptoms. He reports a previous knee surgery approximately two months ago and states he has been feeling well since then. He reports his blood pressure was elevated at the ER, around 180/95, but seemed to regulate after the visit. He has no family history of heart disease but does have a cousin with a history of heart disease. He denies chest pain with exercise since his knee surgery.

O:
Blood pressure measured 180/95 at ER presentation. Carotid bruit auscultated on the right side. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. Heart rate is 72 bpm and regular. Heart rhythm is regular. Thyroid is enlarged, but not tender. No lower extremity edema is present. EKG shows normal sinus rhythm. Echocardiogram reveals mild left ventricular dysfunction with a reduced ejection fraction.

A:
The patient's presentation suggests a possible cardiac etiology for the chest pain, potentially exacerbated by hypertension. The echocardiogram reveals mild left ventricular dysfunction, which requires further investigation.

P:
Start carvedilol 25 mg twice daily. Continue lisinopril 10 mg daily. Order a cardiac catheterization to evaluate for coronary artery disease. Schedule follow-up in one week to assess blood pressure response to the new regimen and discuss the results of the cardiac catheterization.
