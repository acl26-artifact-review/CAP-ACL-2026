S:
The patient reports that he was playing basketball and Jerry ran into him, causing pain on the inside of his knee. He fell to the ground and was unable to continue. He describes the pain as occurring after the injury. He reports icing and an ace wrap immediately following the injury and states it was seven days ago. He takes 500mg of metformin twice daily and checks his blood sugars every morning. He is currently taking some nonsteroidal medications, such as Advil or Motrin. The patient is experiencing decreased range of motion in his right knee and reports pain with palpation of the medial aspect of the knee. He denies hearing or feeling a pop in his knee during the injury.

O:
There is ecchymosis and swelling on the inside of the right knee. There is fluid in the knee (effusion). Palpation reveals tenderness on the medial aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited in the right knee.

A:
The patient likely has a medial collateral ligament strain. The effusion and medial tenderness support this diagnosis. Further evaluation with imaging is warranted to rule out other injuries.

P:
I'm prescribing meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. I'm providing a right knee brace for a few days. I'm sending the patient to physical therapy. I'm instructing the patient to discontinue any nonsteroidal medications. I'm ordering a follow-up appointment in seven days to reassess the knee and determine if further imaging is needed. I'm advising the patient to continue monitoring his blood sugars daily and to contact his PCP if they increase significantly. I will arrange for an assistant to provide discharge instructions and schedule a follow-up appointment.
