O:
The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the right flank and cVA near the flank tenderness. A CT scan revealed a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter with no evidence of hydronephrosis.

A:
The patient is diagnosed with a right-sided kidney stone. The stone is located in the proximal right ureter.

P:
The patient should drink as much as possible to facilitate stone passage. Prescribe ibuprofen 800 mg as needed for pain. Order a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture.
