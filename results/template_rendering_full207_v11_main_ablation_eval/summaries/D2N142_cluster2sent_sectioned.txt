Chief Complaint
Patient reports struggling with alcohol use.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of alcohol use, stating he drinks four to five times a week and last had a drink on Monday. He reports feeling lethargic and tired after taking lisinopril at half the usual dose, and experiences headaches and swooshing in his left ear while taking lisinopril. He denies chest tightness, pressure, or shortness of breath. He reports seeing lower numbers in the morning than in the evening and taking blood pressure medications in the morning, with readings between 130 and 145 or 150 over 95 and 100 at home. The patient reports receiving his coronavirus vaccine on February 9th and has had no recent travel or exposure to the coronavirus. He reports taking his blood pressure medications.

Findings
The patient reports naltrexone is available in pill form and he has taken it before. The clinician describes naltrexone as blocking brain receptors and tricking the brain into thinking it does not need alcohol. Naltrexone is being administered approximately once a month. The clinician describes potential side effects of naltrexone as nausea, headache, dizziness, anxiety, tiredness, and trouble sleeping.

Assessment
The patient presents with a history of alcohol use and reports experiencing side effects related to lisinopril use.

Plan
The clinician recommends upgrading influenza vaccination and shingrix. Order a cervical MRI.
