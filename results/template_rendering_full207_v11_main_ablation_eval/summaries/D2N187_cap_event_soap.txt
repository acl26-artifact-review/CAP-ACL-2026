S:
The patient reports sudden visual distortions and blurring of vision, stating it's difficult to adjust to dim lights at restaurants, and that he's been having trouble reading and seeing straight lines. He also reports a history of smoking a pack of cigarettes daily and difficulty quitting, and he occasionally checks his blood pressure but doesn't always remember to do so. He admits to having drusen in the macula and retinal thickening with subretinal fluid and hemorrhage.

O:
The technician noted decreased vision when reading and straight lines, and door frames appearing crooked. The anterior segment examination reveals posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes with a clear cornea and no microvascular or peripheral corneal issues. Cup to disc ratio is 0.2 in both eyes. Fundus examination reveals the retina attached with no tears or holes or pigment epithelial changes in the fovea. Left eye shows a clear cornea and no microvascular issues. Cup to disc ratio is 0.3 in the left eye. Fluorescein angiography reveals focal areas of leakage in the right eye consistent with neurovascular age-related macular degeneration. The patient's blood pressure was not immediately available for measurement.

A:
Neurovascular age-related macular degeneration is the primary diagnosis. The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with this condition.

P:
The plan is to initiate monthly intravitreal injections of Lucentis into the vitreous to slow the progression of the neurovascular age-related macular degeneration. Follow up will be scheduled to monitor the effectiveness of the treatment and assess any further vision loss.
