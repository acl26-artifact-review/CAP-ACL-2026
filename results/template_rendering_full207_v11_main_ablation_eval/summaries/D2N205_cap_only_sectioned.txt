Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and shortness of breath

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, which began approximately two weeks ago and has been gradually worsening. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, and rates it as 6/10. The patient also reports shortness of breath, particularly with exertion, and notes that it has been present for the past weekend. He denies any recent trauma. He reports a dry cough and difficulty walking. He reports nausea and fatigue. He states he can only walk down to the end of the street this weekend, and normally walked his dog for mile-long walks every day. The patient reports dysphagia. He reports that he has been feeling fatigued.

Findings
The clinician observes erythema on the anterior aspect of the patient's chest on the left side. The clinician detects faint crackles in the patient's lungs bilaterally.

Assessment
The patient has lung cancer and radiation pneumonitis.

Plan
The doctor prescribes prednisone. The doctor prescribes lidocaine. The clinician prescribes prednisone.
