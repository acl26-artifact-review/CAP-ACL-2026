He states that sitting is usually okay, but bending forward causes pain. He denies numbness, tingling, or weakness, and reports no loss of bladder or bowel control. He has tried ibuprofen and acetaminophen with some relief.

O:
Physical exam reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness. Range of motion is within normal limits. Straight leg raise test is positive on the left. Blood pressure is 130/75 mmHg. Heart rate is 18 bpm. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Neurological exam shows intact sensation and motor strength in the lower extremities.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a lumbar disc herniation with radiculopathy, likely at L4-L5.

P:
I recommend a lumbar spinal injection with a strong NSAID and corticosteroid under fluoroscopy to reduce inflammation and pain. I will schedule this procedure today. I will also provide a detailed explanation of the procedure and potential risks.
