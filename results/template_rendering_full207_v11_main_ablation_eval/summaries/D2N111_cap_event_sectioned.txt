Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a trampoline injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing pain following a fall while using a trampoline. He describes a 'snap' sensation and immediate pain. He states this is the first time he has ever experienced an injury like this. He reports using Tylenol for the pain, which provided minimal relief. He denies any regular exercise routine. He reports discomfort with walking, exhibiting a slight limp. He has been experiencing this pain for approximately one week. He reports reluctance to seek medical attention.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited due to pain. There is mild ecchymosis and edema noted on the lateral aspect of the right knee. The patient's blood pressure is 180/80 mmHg. The patient's heart rate is 72 bpm and regular. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. The right knee exhibits a slight limp during ambulation.

Assessment
The patient's clinical presentation and physical examination findings are most consistent with a lateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
I am ordering an X-ray to rule out fracture. I am also ordering a lipid panel and an echo to assess cardiovascular risk. I am referring the patient to physical therapy to facilitate rehabilitation and strengthen the surrounding muscles. I will prescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and inflammation. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.
