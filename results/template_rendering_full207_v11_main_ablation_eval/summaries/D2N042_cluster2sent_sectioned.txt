Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a right ankle sprain following a fall, initially unable to walk on it. He describes limping and has used ice, elevation, and ibuprofen for pain relief. Ibuprofen reduces pain from a 6/10 to a 1/10. The patient has a history of previous ankle injuries but not this specific ankle. He denies numbness or tingling.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the anterior lateral soft tissue and no laxity on anterior drawer or inversion stress. Capillary refill is less than three seconds and the dorsalis pedis pulse is strong. X-rays confirm no fracture.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a right ankle sprain, likely involving the anterior talofibular ligament.

Plan
The patient will receive an air cast and crutches to stabilize the ankle and limit weight-bearing. We will prescribe continued NSAIDs, such as ibuprofen, as needed for pain relief. Follow up in 14 days to assess progress and adjust treatment as necessary.
