Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent ache in his left shoulder for the past month, worsening with overhead activities like painting. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with limited relief. He states, 'It's just getting worse and I can't reach things properly.'

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the anterior shoulder, with a positive Neer test. Range of motion is limited, particularly active abduction. Vitals are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a rotator cuff strain.

Plan
I'm ordering an MRI of the left shoulder to assess the extent of the injury. We'll also start physical therapy to improve range of motion and strengthen the surrounding muscles. I'll prescribe a course of NSAIDs for pain management.
