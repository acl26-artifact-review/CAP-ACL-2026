Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in their right knee, describing it as a constant ache with occasional sharp twinges. He states the pain has been present for approximately three months and has been gradually worsening. He denies any recent injury. He reports that the pain is exacerbated by prolonged walking and stairs. The patient also reports feeling more and more discomfort. He has taken up swimming more regularly. He has a history of chronic back pain and carpal tunnel syndrome, both occurring several years ago. The patient has depression.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the right knee joint. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No swelling is noted. Neurological exam is intact.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with chronic knee pain, likely related to underlying musculoskeletal issues.

Plan
Start physical therapy to address the knee pain and improve range of motion. Continue current medication. Follow up in two weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
