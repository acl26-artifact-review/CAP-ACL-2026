Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain, describing a pop during a fall yesterday while taking out the trash. Initially, he was unable to walk on it, but today he can put a little weight on it, though he's still limping. He iced it last night, took ibuprofen, and reports a pain level of four out of ten with ibuprofen and six without. He has a history of ankle injuries, including a previous injury that prevented him from playing sports, and played soccer in college and inner marrow soccer. He has not been able to do that since the ankle injury. He reports taking ibuprofen this morning.

Findings
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus with edema, tenderness to palpation of the anterior lateral soft tissue, no laxity on anterior drawer or inversion stress, and brisk capillary refill of less than three seconds. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong.

Assessment
The doctor assesses an anterior talofibular ligament sprain and a right ankle sprain.

Plan
The doctor prescribes an air cast, recommends ice application, orders crutches, and prescribes ibuprofen. The doctor schedules a follow-up appointment in 14 days. The doctor recommends continuing to take NSAIDs as needed.
