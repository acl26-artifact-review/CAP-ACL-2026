Chief Complaint
Patient reports left hand pain and difficulty swallowing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a burning sensation in their throat and chest, along with difficulty swallowing, particularly with tomato-based products and citrus products. He also reports experiencing acid reflux and reduced appetite over the past two months. The patient eats spicy foods and drinks soda regularly. He reports experiencing work stress. The patient's mother had acid reflux. The patient previously took medication that coats the lining of their stomach. He is currently taking omeprazole twice a day.

Findings
Examination reveals pain in the left hand. The patient denies dysphagia. Upper endoscopy was performed in the past. The patient's weight is stable.

Assessment
The patient has GERD and left hand pain.

Plan
A pH-impedance study will be obtained. An EGD is scheduled. A trial of nexium may be considered. The patient will start taking nexium. A prescription for nexium will be sent to the patient's pharmacy. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
