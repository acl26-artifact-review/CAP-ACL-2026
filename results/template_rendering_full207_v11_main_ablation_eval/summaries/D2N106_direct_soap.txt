The patient reports intermittent shortness of breath, particularly when outdoors and around his cat, for approximately two years. He describes this as worsening over time. He notes eye irritation, which he attributes to allergies, and occasional diarrhea after heavy drinking. He denies any prior history of allergies or rash. He reports using an inhaler prescribed after a recent urgent care visit for symptom relief. He has a dog with similar eye irritation.

O:
Vital signs are within normal limits. Pulse oximetry is normal. Physical exam reveals faint expiratory wheezing bilaterally in all lung fields. No rash is observed. No stridor is audible.

A:
Newly diagnosed allergic asthma is suspected. The patient's reported symptoms and exam findings are consistent with this diagnosis.

P:
Rescribe albuterol inhaler for as-needed use. Prescribe singulair 10 mg daily. Order allergy testing (skin testing). Schedule follow-up appointment next week to discuss allergy test results and further management. Advise patient to avoid exposure to cat allergens if possible.
