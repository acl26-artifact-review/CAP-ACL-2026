Chief Complaint
Hypogonadism and concerns regarding gynecomastia.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 59-year-old HIV-positive gentleman presenting for hypogonadism. He is currently taking finasteride (half a 5mg pill) and cialis daily. He reports previously having high triglycerides (265 mg/dL), which improved after starting testosterone replacement on the 28th. He notes that his peak testosterone levels have been greater than 1500. The patient is also concerned about gynecomastia, which will be discussed today. He reports taking 2.5mg of cialis on non-workout days and 5mg on workout days, and has recently been out of his cialis supply.

Findings
His last PSA was 0.66 and his last testosterone was greater than 1500. Red blood cell count has increased. His current testosterone level is approximately 700. Cholesterol and triglycerides have been reviewed.

Assessment
The patient presents with hypogonadism and elevated peak testosterone levels.

Plan
Consider increasing testosterone dosage to 175 mg. Order cholesterol and triglyceride testing to assess the impact of testosterone replacement. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and monitor for potential cardiovascular risks associated with elevated testosterone levels.
