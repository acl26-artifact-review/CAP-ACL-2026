Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain, exacerbated by palpation. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, with some warmth. He reports difficulty moving his knee. The patient reports having received tick bites in the past. He reports chills and dizziness. The patient reports body aches. The patient reports pain. The patient reports a headache. The patient reports trying to avoid pie because of sugar. The patient reports taking metformin 'most of the time'. The patient is taking lisinopril.

Findings
Examination reveals edema in the right knee and pain to palpation. The patient reports warmth in the affected area.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is concerning for Lyme disease, given the history of tick bites and associated symptoms. He also presents with high blood pressure and arthritic pain.

Plan
The doctor orders a Hemoglobin A1c test and a basic metabolic panel. The doctor orders Lisinopril. The doctor orders Metformin. The doctor orders Doxycycline. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
