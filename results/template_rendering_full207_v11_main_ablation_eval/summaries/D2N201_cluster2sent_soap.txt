S:
The patient reports experiencing chronic lumbar back pain for several months, with pain exacerbated by flexion and extension. He describes occasional numbness in his toes and reports a single episode of urinary incontinence two years ago. He also reports a previous positive straight leg test. He took a white pill before, but is unsure of its name.

O:
Physical examination reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 on spinal palpation, with right paravertebral tenderness upon forward bending. Neurological examination demonstrates decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at L4 dermatome. Vital signs are within normal limits. MRI findings show disk desiccation, disk bulge with paracentral disk herniation resulting in moderate right for neural foraminal stenosis.

A:
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with a herniated disk at L4-L5 with radiculopathy, leading to moderate right for neural foraminal stenosis.

P:
I recommend a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to manage inflammation and pain. I will prescribe a strong anti-inflammatory medication to be taken three hours prior to the injection. If pain persists after the injection, we will explore options for pain management, including potential consideration of surgery. I will schedule a follow-up appointment to monitor progress and adjust treatment as needed.
