S:
The patient reports difficulty swallowing for four to five weeks, specifically with solid foods like sandwiches. He describes a feeling of something stuck in his throat when swallowing. He denies coughing, vomiting blood, or weight loss. The patient reports feeling 'pretty good' regarding his diabetes.

O:
The doctor observes no swelling in the neck, no masses in the neck, no redness in the throat, and no carotid bruit. Epigastric tenderness is noted. Blood pressure is 120/50 mmHg at the office and occasionally higher at home. The patient's blood sugar levels are within the target range.

A:
The doctor suspects a narrowing in the throat, possibly a stricture, given the reported dysphagia.

P:
The doctor recommends a referral to gastroenterology for a scope. The doctor recommends prescribing Prilosec 20mg once daily. The doctor recommends frequent chewing of food and chewing hard foods thoroughly. The doctor orders a hemoglobin A1c repeat and additional blood work including kidney function tests. The doctor orders an eye referral to ophthalmology. The doctor continues the patient on metformin. The doctor will schedule a follow-up appointment in one month.
