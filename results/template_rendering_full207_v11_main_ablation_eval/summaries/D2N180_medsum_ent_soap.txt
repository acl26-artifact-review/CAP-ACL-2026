S:
The patient reports fatigue and weight gain over the past few weeks. She also describes experiencing excessive appetite and bloating. She reports intermittent numbness and tingling in both hands, which began approximately four weeks ago following a car accident last December. The patient states the tingling and numbness has worsened over the last month and reports mild neck stiffness, likely related to the head injury sustained during the accident. She describes pain in her hands, reporting weakness and a dull ache, particularly when using her hands. The patient notes that the tingling is more pronounced at night and fluctuates throughout the day. She denies any significant loss of sensation, though she feels the hands may be less sensitive than expected. She reports nail bed trenches and dryness, and skin itching. The patient reports shortness of breath and leg swelling.

O:
Pin prick sensation is diminished in the third and fourth fingers on the right hand and the fourth and fifth fingers on the left hand. Reflexes are one plus bilaterally, two plus at the knees. Tinel's sign is positive at both wrists and both elbows. Blood pressure is within normal limits. Heart rate is within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome and potentially cubital tunnel syndrome. Further evaluation with EMG/NCV is indicated to confirm the diagnosis and rule out other causes of peripheral neuropathy. Given the history of trauma and chronic conditions, a thorough assessment of the patient's neurological status is warranted.

P:
Order EMG/NCV studies. Schedule follow-up for results. Encourage the patient to continue wearing her splints. Counsel the patient on the importance of adherence to her medication regimen, including carvedilol 25 mg twice daily and lexapro 10 mg daily. Recommend a referral to a hand clinic for potential symptomatic injections or carpal tunnel release surgery if symptoms do not resolve with conservative management. Arrange follow-up in 2 weeks to assess symptom resolution and discuss further management options.
