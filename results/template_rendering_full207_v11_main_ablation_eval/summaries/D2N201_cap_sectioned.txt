Chief Complaint
The patient is experiencing back pain for a while.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain for approximately a couple of years and has tried several things without success. He states that the pain began after a basketball injury where he fell hard on his back. The pain is described as sharp and throbbing at times. It radiates down his left leg and causes numbness in his toes. He reports difficulty standing for prolonged periods and weakness when standing. He mentions experiencing incontinence once, attributing it to prolonged standing. He previously received injections but is unsure of the details. He reports playing basketball before, and now spends most of the day watching television, leading to a weight gain of approximately eighty pounds over the past year. He mentions that his fourth cousin has scoliosis. The patient denies smoking but admits to having smoked in the past. He describes a hard impact from the fall.

Findings
Physical examination reveals midline tenderness on the L4-L5 disc space with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness upon palpation. Pain is elicited with lumbar flexion and extension. A straight leg raise test is positive. Neurological examination reveals decreased sensation to light touch at the L4 dermatome and diminished reflexes at the Achilles tendon. The patient reports numbness in his toes and weakness when standing.

Assessment
The patient has chronic lumbar back pain with radiculopathy, likely secondary to a herniated disk at the L4-L5 level. This is supported by the MRI findings and physical exam findings.

Plan
A spinal injection with a corticosteroid is recommended. A prescription for pain medication will be provided. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Schedule physical therapy.
