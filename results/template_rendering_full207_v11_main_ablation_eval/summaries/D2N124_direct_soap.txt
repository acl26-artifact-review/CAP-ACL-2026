S:
The patient reports an ankle injury following a basketball-related incident, describing a twisting injury and subsequent pain and swelling. He reports difficulty walking and a feeling of instability. He has been working to return to his regular exercise routine and is concerned about disrupting his progress. He also volunteers as a coach starting in April. He works from home and has difficulty with pet care.

O:
The patient presents with significant lateral ankle swelling, tenderness to palpation, and brisk capillary refill. Examination reveals exquisite tenderness over the lateral aspect of the ankle. AP radiograph demonstrates a displaced lateral malleolus fracture at the Weber C level, with no evidence of medial or posterior malleolar fractures.

A:
The patient has a lateral malleolar fracture. The instability of the fracture contributes to his reported difficulty walking.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam for pain and inflammation. Advise ice and elevation of the ankle. Order crutches for weight-bearing. Schedule surgical consultation for the lateral malleolar fracture. Advise the patient to anticipate a 3-month recovery period before returning to full activity, including coaching, with continued exercise progression as tolerated.
