Chief Complaint
To evaluate a suspicious skin lesion and discuss surgical options.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports noticing a lesion on her back approximately one month prior, initially observed by her mother during a visit. She sought evaluation with her dermatologist on April 10th, where a biopsy was performed, revealing melanoma. She has a family history of melanoma in her mother and sister. She denies any personal history of skin cancer. She currently works as an auto repair shop owner and reports occasional smoking. She has had previous surgeries for gall bladder and appendix.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a right inguinal lymph node with slightly atypical consistency. The biopsy site on the right inferior back measures 1 centimeter in diameter and shows no residual pigmentation or satellite lesions. No other suspicious moles were identified. Vital signs are within normal limits. The patient denies any unintentional weight changes, headaches, fatigue, nausea, vomiting, or vision changes.

Assessment
The patient presents with a confirmed diagnosis of invasive melanoma, stage 1 based on the biopsy findings. A suspicious inguinal lymph node was identified, warranting further investigation.

Plan
Arrange for an ultrasound of the right inguinal lymph node to evaluate for metastatic disease. Recommend a wide local excision with a 1-2 centimeter margin, followed by primary closure if possible, or skin graft closure if necessary. Schedule follow-up with the dermatologist for ongoing monitoring. Advise the patient to continue with sunscreen use and regular skin self-examinations, adhering to NCCN guidelines for melanoma management.
