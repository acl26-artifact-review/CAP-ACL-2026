Chief Complaint
Numbness and tingling in the fingers and wrist pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports ongoing numbness and tingling in his fingers and wrist for several months, primarily affecting the left hand. He describes the pain as gradually worsening over time, with symptoms most prominent at night and relieved by shaking his hands. He reports that the symptoms are associated with repetitive movements, such as raking and digging, as part of his landscaping work. He has noticed increased weakness in his left hand and occasionally drops objects.

Findings
Physical examination reveals decreased grip strength on the left hand and a positive Tinel's sign on the left wrist. The patient reports feeling a 'zing' in his fingertips with wrist dorsiflexion. Examination of the right hand reveals no abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient's clinical presentation and exam findings are consistent with bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Order an EMG to evaluate nerve conduction studies. I will also prescribe a wrist splint to be worn daily and ibuprofen 600 mg every six hours as needed. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess treatment response and consider further interventions, including surgical consultation if necessary. I recommend the patient explore options for modifying his landscaping work to reduce repetitive motions.
