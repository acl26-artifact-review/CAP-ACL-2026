S:
The patient reports intermittent abdominal pain localized to the mid-abdomen, just below the belly button, for approximately two years. He describes the pain as nagging and variable in intensity. He reports alternating episodes of constipation and explosive diarrhea, with a preference for constipation. The patient states bowel movements were previously normal. He reports a sensitive stomach, particularly to pasta with creamy sauces, leading to predictable diarrhea. He reports a bowel movement frequency of approximately three to four times per week when constipated, and the pain improves during bowel movements. He denies blood in the stool and reports no nausea or vomiting.

O:
Auscultation reveals normal bowel sounds in all four quadrants. The clinician observes an increased stool burden in the colon and a slight hepatomegaly. The patient's liver enzymes (ALT, AST, ALP) were slightly elevated on previous bloodwork. Vital signs are within normal limits. Physical exam reveals no signs of jaundice.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a possible gastrointestinal disorder, potentially involving constipation and diarrhea. The slightly elevated liver enzymes warrant further investigation to rule out underlying liver pathology. The hepatomegaly requires further evaluation to determine the cause.

P:
The clinician orders a liver ultrasound and recommends discussing Crestor alternatives with the patient's primary care physician. The clinician recommends a fiber supplement for constipation and advises the patient to increase water intake to 48 ounces daily. The clinician recommends avoiding foods that trigger diarrhea. A follow-up appointment is scheduled in four weeks to review test results and assess treatment response. The clinician recommends a call from radiology scheduling about setting up the ultrasound.
