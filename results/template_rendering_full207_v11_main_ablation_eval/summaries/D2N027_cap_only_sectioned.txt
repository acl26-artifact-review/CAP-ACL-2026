Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and fatigue.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, which he describes as sharp and intermittent. He states the pain is exacerbated by gripping and lifting. He also reports feeling more fatigued at the end of the day, particularly after his daily bicycle rides, which are approximately 20 to 30 miles. He denies any recent injuries or trauma. He reports sleeping six to seven hours per night. The patient reports pursed lips breathing when breathing. He also reports snoring.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the left hand. The patient's exhalation phase is low. An alkaline phosphatase level is elevated. The right testicular nodule is about two to three millimeters.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are concerning for possible testicular nodule and an elevated alkaline phosphatase level. The patient's fatigue and pursed lips breathing are also noted.

Plan
A referral to urology is placed. An echocardiogram is ordered. The doctor recommends the patient receive a shingles vaccine. The doctor recommends a wellness visit in approximately one year. The patient is currently taking clindamycin prior to dental procedures and vitamin D3.
