S:
The patient reports a non-healing foot ulcer present for approximately one year, which began as a blister and was treated with antibiotics approximately five months ago. He also reports some numbness in his foot and increased drainage and fatigue recently. He states he will be walking around at the zoo and eating out this weekend.

O:
The right foot exam reveals a one by two centimeter circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot, proximal to the fifth metatarsal head. There is some yellow slough and minimal granulation tissue present. There is no surrounding erythema or cellulitis, and no evidence of fluid collection or necrosis. An X-ray of the right foot showed no evidence of osteomyelitis. Vital signs are stable. Blood pressure is within normal limits. Heart rate is within normal limits.

A:
The patient has a one-year-old diabetic foot ulcer with neuropathy and decreased sensation. The ulcer is not healing despite previous antibiotic treatment. The patient's diabetes is not well controlled, with an A1c of approximately 10%.

P:
Order an ABI to assess blood supply to the foot. Perform a debridement of the ulcer in the office. Prescribe collagenase ointment for daily wound care and cover with a sterile dressing. Provide a surgical shoe for offloading and pressure relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks for re-evaluation and potential further debridement. Advise the patient to avoid excessive pressure on the foot, particularly during the weekend activities.
