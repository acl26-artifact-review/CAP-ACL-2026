Chief Complaint
Left shoulder and elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports constant left shoulder and elbow pain for approximately five months, following a fall five months ago while going up stairs. He describes the pain as constant and often severe, particularly with weight-bearing activities such as lifting. He has been maintaining a two-day-per-week weightlifting routine, but has found the pain makes it difficult to lift more than 15-20 pounds. He has stopped exercising due to the pain over the past two weeks. He denies numbness or tingling. He continues to walk approximately 30 minutes daily with his wife since retiring from his office job last year.

Findings
Left shoulder and elbow tenderness to palpation. Positive Hawkins-Kennedy and Neer's tests. Normal proximal and distal upper extremity strength. Intact median, radial, and ulnar sensation and abduction to 90 degrees. Normal empty tension test.

Assessment
Impingement syndrome of the left shoulder.

Plan
Order a referral to Dr. Martha Rivera for physical therapy, two sessions per week. Recommend ice and rest. Follow up if symptoms worsen or do not improve with physical therapy or if new symptoms develop.
