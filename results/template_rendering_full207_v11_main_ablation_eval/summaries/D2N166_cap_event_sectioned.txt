Chief Complaint
Low back pain with left leg radiation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports low back pain that began approximately two weeks ago following lifting a box of books. He describes the pain as both achy and sharp, and it radiates down his left leg. He admits to poor lifting technique and tried ibuprofen and acetaminophen with some relief. He denies any recent trauma. He has no prior history of spinal issues and reports no numbness or weakness. He has a sedentary job and inconsistent exercise habits. He plays soccer and was a midfielder. He does not smoke and has no family history of spine conditions.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness at L4-L5 upon palpation, with pain reproduced during lumbar flexion. Straight leg raise testing is positive for left leg radiation. Vital signs are within normal limits: blood pressure 130/75 mmHg, respiratory rate 18, regular heart rate. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Neurological exam shows intact sensation and motor function in the lower extremities. MRI reveals a disk desiccation, disk bulge with paracentral disk herniation resulting in moderate right neuroforaminal stenosis.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is consistent with lumbar radiculopathy secondary to L4-L5 disc herniation.

Plan
Administer a spinal injection with a strong NSAID and corticosteroid under fluoroscopy to target the L4-L5 level. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment. Continue physical therapy as appropriate. Advise the patient to avoid activities that exacerbate the pain.
