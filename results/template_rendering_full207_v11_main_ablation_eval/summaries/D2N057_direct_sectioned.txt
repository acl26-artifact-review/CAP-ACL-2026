Chief Complaint
Right big toe pain and redness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a two-week history of intermittent right big toe pain, which has become more pronounced last week. He describes the pain as throbbing, occasionally sharp, especially with movement. He experienced significant pain and inability to walk during a trade show convention last week. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He has been taking ibuprofen twice daily with limited relief. He reports that the toe becomes red and warm to the touch, particularly when it is compressed or when he is lying down.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the right great toe joint. Range of motion is limited due to pain. The patient reports a palpable bone spur on the dorsal aspect of the great toe. Dorsal flexion causes pain. Plantar flexion causes pain. The patient reports feeling grinding sensations with push-pull movements of the great toe. The patient reports some discomfort with pressure between the great toe and second toe. The x-ray demonstrates a large spur on the top of the great toe joint and significant cartilage loss.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with hallux rigidus with secondary osteoarthritis. The findings of a large spur and cartilage loss support this diagnosis.

Plan
Prescribe an orthotic insert for the patient's shoe. Initiate meloxicam, 1 tablet daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess treatment response. If significant improvement is not observed, consider a shot or surgical intervention to clean out the joint. Advise the patient to continue with regular activities and to monitor for any signs of medication-related side effects, such as stomach upset. Encourage the patient to continue fishing, but to take it easy for a couple of weeks.
