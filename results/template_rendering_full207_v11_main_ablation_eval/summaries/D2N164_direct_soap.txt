The patient reports intermittent chest pain, described as dull, occurring with walking, and lasting approximately three months. He states the pain is not currently present but occurs when walking and has been exacerbated by activity. He reports a preference for sodas and has been trying to reduce his intake. He reports his blood sugars have been higher since he has been less active. He has an ophthalmology appointment scheduled in two weeks. He reports no nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea related to his diabetes or medications.

O:
The EKG showed no signs of a heart attack. The patient has a two over six systolic ejection murmur. Physical exam reveals one plus edema in the bilateral lower extremities. Lung exam is clear with no crackles or rhonchi. Heart sounds are regular rate and rhythm. Pulses are equal bilaterally. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. Back exam is unremarkable. No calf tenderness is present.

A:
The patient's chest pain is concerning for possible unstable angina, considering his history of diabetes and suspected high blood pressure. The trend in his blood pressure readings warrants further investigation. A referral to cardiology is indicated to evaluate for potential coronary artery disease.

P:
Start aspirin 81 mg daily. Prescribe nitroglycerin for sublingual use if chest pain persists. Order a hemoglobin A1c. Start hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily. Schedule a cardiology stress test in one week. Counsel the patient on dietary modifications and exercise. Instruct the patient to monitor blood pressure at home and report findings to Epic MyChart. Arrange follow-up in two weeks to assess blood pressure trends and adjust medications as needed.
