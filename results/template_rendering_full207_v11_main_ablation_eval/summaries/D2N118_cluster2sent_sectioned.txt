Chief Complaint
Recurrent kidney stones and hypertension.

History of Present Illness

He has been trying to improve his diet, aiming for less than 2300 mg of sodium per day, but struggles with prepackaged foods. He reports experiencing insomnia and has been using melatonin as needed. He takes his metformin regularly and has been monitoring his blood sugars twice a day.

Findings
Blood pressure was elevated at 150/90 mmHg today. The patient's A1c was 6.1% at his last visit. Lung exam is clear bilaterally. Heart exam reveals a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is stable. Abdominal exam reveals no tenderness. The patient reports no current abdominal pain.

Assessment
The patient has a history of recurrent kidney stones and hypertension. His diabetes is currently well-controlled.

Plan
