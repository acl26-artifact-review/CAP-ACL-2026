Chief Complaint
Logan reports lumbar pain following a recent lifting incident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain following an incident where he lifted some heavy boxes, describing a 'pop' in his back and subsequent stiffness. He reports difficulty sleeping on his right side and has experienced tingling in his toes on his right foot approximately a week ago. He reports a history of type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and osteoarthritis in his knee. He admits to a less-than-ideal diet over the past few weeks due to frequent travel and infrequent home-cooked meals, and reports that he has been less diligent with his blood pressure monitoring. He has been taking Tylenol and Ibuprofen for the pain, but reports minimal relief. He also reports that his blood pressure has been running a little high, particularly on weekends.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a slight systolic heart murmur, grade 2/6. The lumbar spine examination reveals tenderness to palpation and pain with flexion and extension. A straight leg raise test is negative. A review of the back x-ray confirms normal bony alignment and no fracture. Laboratory results show an elevated hemoglobin A1c of 8%.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a lumbar strain secondary to mechanical injury. He also continues to have suboptimal control of his diabetes and hypertension.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg once daily for the lumbar pain. I recommend icing the affected area and recommend physical therapy referral. I am increasing his metformin dosage to 1000 mg twice daily to improve glycemic control. I am also increasing his lisinopril dosage to 20 mg daily to better manage his hypertension. I have ordered a hemoglobin A1c repeat in six months. The nurse will finalize the order for the hemoglobin A1c. I will follow up with the patient in four weeks if symptoms persist.
