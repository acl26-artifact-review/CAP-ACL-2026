Chief Complaint
Knee pain, secondary to arthritis exacerbation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports knee pain that began over the weekend following strenuous activity, including moving boxes. He describes equal pain in both knees and reports no injury. He has previously taken tylenol and ice with limited relief. He reports no fever, chills, or nausea. He has a history of polycystic kidney disease for which he is taking immunosuppressants and hypothyroidism for which he takes synthroid. His kidney transplant is stable, and his thyroid function is currently normal. He denies any other significant symptoms.

Findings
Physical examination reveals edema and erythema in the right knee, with decreased range of motion and tenderness to palpation. The left knee appears normal. Vital signs are stable, including a heart rate of 78 bpm and blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg. The right knee x-ray demonstrates good bony alignment without fracture. Laboratory results show a normal white blood cell count and stable kidney function.

Assessment
Acute exacerbation of arthritis, likely secondary to repetitive strain. The patient's history of polycystic kidney disease and immunosuppression requires careful medication management.

Plan
Prescribe ultram 50 mg every six hours as needed for knee pain. Order an autoimmune panel. Continue synthroid at current dose. Order thyroid labs. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and consider referral to physical therapy if symptoms persist.
