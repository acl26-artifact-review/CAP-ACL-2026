Chief Complaint
Right foot pain consistent with a Lisfranc fracture.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right foot pain following a twisting injury during a sporting event. She wrapped the injury with ice and ace wraps, and took ibuprofen. She denies any numbness or tingling. The patient is concerned about the potential for long-term complications, such as foot flatness and arthritis, if surgery is not performed.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bruising of the plantar and dorsal aspects of the right foot, associated swelling, tenderness to palpation of the midfoot, and a positive piano key test of the first and second metatarsals. The right foot is warm to the touch. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the right foot. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong. Sensation is intact to light touch. The left foot exam is normal with appropriate capillary refill and pedal pulses.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with a Lisfranc fracture, requiring surgical intervention to stabilize the injury and prevent long-term complications.

Plan
I am recommending surgical fixation of the Lisfranc fracture with plates and screws. An MRI will be ordered to assess ligamentous injury. The patient will undergo a day-surgery procedure and will be discharged the same day. Follow-up will be scheduled in one week with a cast and crutches. Gradual weight-bearing will be initiated after six to eight weeks, followed by physical therapy to strengthen the muscles and improve function. The patient will receive documentation of risks associated with the procedure, including bleeding, nerve damage, and sensation loss.
