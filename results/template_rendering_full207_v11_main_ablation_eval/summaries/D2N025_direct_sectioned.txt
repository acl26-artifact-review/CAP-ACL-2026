Chief Complaint
Follow-up for chronic congestive heart failure with diastolic dysfunction and planned right knee replacement.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports increasingly dyspneic over the past few months, prompting a change from furosemide to torsemide 20 mg daily. She reports an interest in having her right knee replaced this summer at East Metro. She has lost approximately 3.5 pounds since her last visit in April, which she attributes to weight loss. The patient reports leg cramps, particularly at night, which she relieved with pickle juice. She has been taking prilosec for two weeks to address heartburn, which has resolved. She is currently on an iron supplement and reports no issues with bowel movements. She is due for a colonoscopy, which she would like to schedule prior to her surgery.

Findings
Blood pressure is 132/78. Heart rate is 72 bpm. Lungs are clear to auscultation. Hemoglobin is 13.5 g/dL. Potassium is 3.9 mEq/L. Creatinine is 0.7 mg/dL. BUN is 23 mg/dL. Magnesium is 1.7 mg/dL. An A1c was 5.5% on the last check. An ekg was performed and is pending review.

Assessment
Chronic congestive heart failure with diastolic dysfunction. Preoperative evaluation for right knee replacement.

Plan
Order an electrocardiogram. Schedule a pre-operative examination within two weeks of surgery. Initiate magnesium supplementation twice daily. Arrange for a colonoscopy at Bartley Regional Hospital within the next month, pending insurance approval. Continue torsemide 20 mg daily. Counsel the patient on the importance of discontinuing clopidogrel one week prior to surgery. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist.
