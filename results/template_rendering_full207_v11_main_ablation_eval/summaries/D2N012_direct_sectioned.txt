Chief Complaint
Routine follow-up of chronic problems and medication adjustments.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports managing depression with Prozac 20mg daily, which he has recently weaned off of due to feeling slightly better. He also experiences intermittent asthma exacerbations, particularly during pollen season, using an inhaler approximately 85% of the time on warm days. He reports traveling with the family for sports tournaments, leading to occasional dietary adjustments and less adherence to his diet. He denies chest pain, shortness of breath, or belly pain. He reports some knee pain from running and picking up boxes.

Findings
Physical examination reveals no distress. Cervical lymph nodes are unremarkable. Thyroid exam is normal. Lung auscultation reveals bilateral expiratory wheezing, faint in quality. Heart sounds are regular. Abdomen is soft and non-tender. Lower extremities are not edematous. PFTs demonstrate mild expiratory wheezing. Hemoglobin A1c is elevated. Chest X-ray is normal.

Assessment
The patient's primary diagnoses include chronic depression, type 2 diabetes mellitus, and asthma. His diabetes is currently not optimally controlled, and his asthma is exacerbating.

Plan
Increase metformin to 1000 mg twice daily. Initiate Zoloft at 25 mg daily. Continue albuterol inhaler two puffs every four to six hours as needed. Add Symbicort two puffs twice daily during the summer months. Order a repeat hemoglobin A1c in three months. Schedule follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist. The nurse will administer medications and provide patient education.
