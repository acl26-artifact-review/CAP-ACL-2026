Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain that began after a fall with his partner two months ago. He reports taking ibuprofen and applying ice/bandage to the injury. He states he is not supposed to take ibuprofen for more than two weeks. Prior to the injury, the patient reports his knee was not bothering him. He denies weakness in the knee. The patient reports playing sports in high school and currently only walks his dog. He states he has not been swimming since his knee injury. The patient reports tenderness on the medial joint line of his knee and a slight feeling of instability in his left knee.

Findings
The patient denies blood in stool. Negative McMurray tests were performed on both knees.

Assessment
The clinician suspects a sprained or injured posterior cruciate ligament.

Plan
The clinician plans a cervical MRI. The clinician suggests a brace and physical therapy. The clinician suggests an MRI to evaluate for meniscus injury or other damage. The clinician recommends continuing ibuprofen as needed. The clinician suggests a prescription for a medication with less frequent dosing.
