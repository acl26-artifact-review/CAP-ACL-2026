Chief Complaint
Bilateral hand numbness and tingling.

History of Present Illness
Ms. Taylor reports experiencing bilateral hand numbness and tingling for approximately six weeks, initially following a car accident last December. She notes that the tingling and numbness worsened over the past month and is now more pronounced. She describes the right hand symptoms as primarily affecting the middle and ring fingers extending up the forearm, while the left hand symptoms are more prominent in the pinky and ring fingers. She reports occasional throbbing pain, but overall, the hands feel weaker, particularly with use. The symptoms are worse at night and fluctuate throughout the day. She has been taking nerve pain medication but has not been consistent with the regimen. She also reports mild neck stiffness since the accident. She denies any recent trauma or other neurological symptoms. Her medical history includes chronic kidney disease, gout, hypertension, anxiety, GERD, and pre-diabetes, which she managed through dietary changes.

Findings
Physical exam reveals diminished pinprick sensation in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand and the fourth and fifth fingers of the left hand. Reflexes are one plus and symmetric bilaterally. Tinel's signs are positive at the wrists and elbows bilaterally. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits except for reported shortness of breath and leg swelling.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome and possibly cubital tunnel syndrome. Further evaluation with EMG/NCV is warranted to confirm the diagnosis and rule out other causes.

Plan
I have scheduled an EMG/NCV to evaluate the patient's symptoms. I will discuss the results with the patient and consider referral to a hand clinic for possible carpal tunnel release surgery if symptoms do not resolve with conservative management. I have also advised the patient to continue wearing her splints and will follow up in four weeks to assess her progress. I will continue the patient on carvedilol 25 mg twice daily and lexapro 10 mg daily.
