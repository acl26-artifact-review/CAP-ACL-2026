Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left hand pain, which he describes as a dull ache, for the past month. He rates the pain as 6/10. The pain is exacerbated by gripping and lifting objects. The patient also reports shortness of breath, particularly with exertion. He describes it as feeling like he can't get enough air. He denies chest pain, palpitations, or recent trauma. He reports taking baby aspirin and brilinta as prescribed. He states he has been taking his medications as directed. He reports feeling generally well otherwise.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 138/82 mmHg. Heart rate is 78 bpm and regular. Respiratory rate is 18 breaths per minute. Oxygen saturation is 97% on room air. Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the left hand. Range of motion in the left hand is limited due to pain. The patient reports mild shortness of breath with minimal exertion.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms of left hand pain and shortness of breath are concerning for potential cardiac issues, specifically an inferior myocardial infarction and possible angina. The patient's history of a stent and current medications (brilinta, aspirin, crestor, toprol) are relevant.

Plan
Order an echocardiogram to assess cardiac function and rule out myocardial ischemia. Order liver function studies. Recommend the patient enroll in cardiac rehabilitation. Continue the patient's current medications: brilinta, crestor, and toprol. Follow up in one week to assess response to medications and echocardiogram results. ,
