Chief Complaint
Abdominal pain with right upper quadrant tenderness.

History of Present Illness
Karen reports onset of abdominal pain approximately ten days ago, initially mild and localized to the right side, now worsening over the past couple of days. The pain is exacerbated by meals and less so by rest. She reports nausea and some heartburn, but no vomiting. She has been using excedrin migraine for her migraines, and sumatriptan as needed. She denies fever.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild to moderate tenderness in the right upper quadrant, with an equivocal Murphy's sign. Cardiovascular exam is notable for a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur. Blood pressure is 128/78 mmHg. The patient reports checking her blood pressure approximately two to three times per week, with readings typically in the 120s-130s over 70s. No fever is present.

Assessment
Acute abdominal pain with right upper quadrant tenderness is concerning for possible gallbladder pathology or infection. Hypertension is currently well-controlled.

Plan
Order a CBC, comprehensive metabolic panel, urinalysis, pregnancy test, and right upper quadrant ultrasound. I will also order a refill for lisinopril 20mg and sumatriptan as needed. I will provide a prescription for a refill of sumatriptan. Continue the patient on excedrin migraine as needed. Advise the patient to avoid eating large meals and to follow up in four weeks or sooner if symptoms worsen. I will follow up on the test results today.
