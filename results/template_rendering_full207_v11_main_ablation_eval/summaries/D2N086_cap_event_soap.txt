S:
The patient reports having had the foot ulcer for approximately six weeks, initially noticing burning and stinging when wearing tight shoes. He describes a foul odor and his wife noticing his socks smelling. He reports experiencing throbbing pain in the foot and a hard, painful area in his calf muscle, with pain radiating up his leg to his knee. He also reports experiencing frequent coughing and difficulty catching his breath. He states his blood sugars have been trending in the 3-400 range over the past two weeks, with a peak of 5-600, and his A1c is 6.7%.

O:
Physical exam reveals a necrotic wound on the heel measuring 2x2 cm, with slough and erythema. Bilateral palpable pulses are present via Doppler. A slight fever is noted. The patient's vital signs are stable.

A:
The patient has a non-healing diabetic foot ulcer with signs of cellulitis and possible arterial insufficiency. He also presents with elevated blood sugars and potential signs of deep vein thrombosis.

P:
Order blood supply testing for the wound. Perform a debridement of the wound today. Obtain a culture and sensitivity of the wound. Consider alternative antibiotic therapy based on culture results. Continue current antibiotic regimen for 10-12 days. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 7 days. Refer to endocrinology for continued diabetes management.
