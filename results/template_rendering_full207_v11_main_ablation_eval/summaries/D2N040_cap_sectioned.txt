Chief Complaint
Headache

History of Present Illness
Carolyn reports a headache that started approximately one week ago, described as a dull pain in the back of her head. She has been experiencing flushing in her ears and occasional dizziness. She has taken acetaminophen and ibuprofen, but the headaches persist and return. The headache is worse in the evening. She attributes the onset of the headaches to increased stress levels. She has a history of headaches, but this is not the worst headache she has experienced. She denies any recent trauma. She has not been checking her blood sugars and has not been exercising regularly.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild posterior paraspinal muscular tenderness in the cervical spine and bilateral trapezius musculature. Extraocular movements are intact. The heart exam shows a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is unchanged from her previous exam. Vital signs are within normal limits. The patient denies fever.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a tension-type headache, likely exacerbated by stress and musculoskeletal tension. There is no evidence of acute neurological pathology.

Plan
I am prescribing flexeril 5 mg three times daily for muscle relaxation. I am also ordering an A1c and a CBC/Chem-12 to monitor her diabetes and overall health. Please continue to take acetaminophen as needed for the headache. I want you to call if the headaches become more severe or suddenly worsen, or if you develop a fever. We will schedule a follow-up in four weeks to assess your progress. Please try to get back into your exercise routine to help manage your blood sugars.
