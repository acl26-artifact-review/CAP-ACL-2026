Chief Complaint
Left hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand. The patient states the pain is constant and has been present for the past week. The patient denies any recent trauma. The patient reports that the pain is exacerbated by gripping objects. The patient reports taking ibuprofen for the pain, but it does not provide significant relief. The patient's father has arthritis.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the volar aspect of the left wrist. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Grip strength is decreased in the left hand. No swelling or erythema is noted. The patient's vital signs are stable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Order carpal tunnel ultrasound. Recommend wrist splinting. Advise patient on ergonomic adjustments at work. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
