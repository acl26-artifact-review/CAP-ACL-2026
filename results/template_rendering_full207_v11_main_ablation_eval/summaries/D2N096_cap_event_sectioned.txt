Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain that began approximately one week ago following a gymnastics class. He describes the pain as persistent and worsening with various movements, particularly overhead activities. He initially took two to three days of Tylenol, but found it ineffective. He reports pain with both passive and active range of motion, particularly with elevation. He denies any recent trauma or injury. He mentions a previous surgery on his left arm.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. No acute tenderness to palpation. Vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a rotator cuff tendinopathy, likely exacerbated by recent activity.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 600mg three times daily for one week. Recommend ice and heat therapy as needed. Schedule physical therapy evaluation in four weeks or sooner if symptoms persist. Advise patient to avoid heavy lifting and overhead activities. Consider shoulder radiographs if symptoms do not improve after three weeks.
