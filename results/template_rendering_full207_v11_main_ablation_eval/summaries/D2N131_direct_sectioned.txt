Chief Complaint
Susan reports persistent high blood pressure and concerns about its control, following an increase in her norvasc dosage.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a long-standing history of elevated blood pressure, which has recently become more difficult to control. She notes headaches possibly related to stress and the elevated blood pressure. She has had a kidney transplant approximately three years ago, which is currently stable and managed with immunosuppression medications. She takes all of her immunosuppression medications as prescribed.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a slight systolic ejection murmur at the cardiac auscultation. The neck is supple. The heart rate is irregular and rhythm is regular. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. The left flank scar from her kidney transplant is healed. There is no lower extremity edema. Creatinine levels are within normal limits. The electrocardiogram demonstrates normal sinus rhythm. Blood pressure readings at the office were elevated.

Assessment
The patient presents with uncontrolled hypertension, requiring further management.

Plan
Prescribe coreg 25 milligrams twice daily to assist with blood pressure control. Order a lipid panel and a complete metabolic panel. Order a carvedilol 25 milligrams twice a day. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess blood pressure response to the new medication. The nurse will schedule a follow-up appointment and provide medication instructions.
