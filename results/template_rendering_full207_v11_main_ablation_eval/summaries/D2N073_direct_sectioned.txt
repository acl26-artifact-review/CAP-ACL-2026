Chief Complaint
Patient reports difficulty donating blood due to a long-standing diagnosis of iron deficiency anemia, which she has been experiencing for many years.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of iron deficiency anemia dating back to age 13. She notes fatigue, occasional feverishness with chills, and shortness of breath with activity. She also experiences headaches. She had a hysterectomy in 1996, which addressed heavy menstrual bleeding, but does not believe this is the reason for her current anemia. Her last colonoscopy was approximately five to six years ago, and it was unremarkable. She currently takes ferrous sulfate 25 mg twice daily with vitamin C from orange juice, and reports having stopped taking the iron pills for about a year. She also takes over-the-counter vitamin B12. She reports feeling increasingly anxious and depressed lately.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no significant findings. Vital signs are within normal limits. The patient denies any history of pacemaker or defibrillator use or oxygen therapy.

Assessment
The patient presents with iron deficiency anemia, complicated by symptoms of fatigue, fever, and anxiety.

Plan
Schedule an upper endoscopy and colonoscopy. Send a referral to Dr. Flores, a hematologist, for further evaluation. Instruct the patient to resume taking ferrous sulfate with orange juice daily for 8-12 weeks, monitoring for improvement in iron levels. Provide instructions for bowel preparation prior to the endoscopy. Follow up in four weeks or sooner if symptoms worsen.
