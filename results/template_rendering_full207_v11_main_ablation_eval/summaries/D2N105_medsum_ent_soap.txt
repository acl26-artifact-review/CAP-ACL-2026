S:
The patient reports a new, worrisome heart murmur, with no prior awareness of its presence. She denies any current chest pain or shortness of breath. She reports leg pain, primarily in both legs, which has been present for approximately one month. She experiences occasional lightheadedness and dizziness, treated with meclizine as needed. She notes fatigue, particularly with increased activity. She reports feeling overly tired due to medication side effects. She states her ankles swell.

O:
Physical exam reveals a grade 4/6 systolic murmur at the right upper sternal border with radiation to the carotids. Heart rhythm is regular with sinus rhythm, but with frequent premature ventricular contractions (PVCs) noted on the EKG. The EKG shows an anterior septal infarct pattern. Blood pressure is within normal limits. Neurological exam is unremarkable.

A:
The primary concern is a possible aortic stenosis, likely contributing to the reported murmur and leg pain. The PVCs suggest a possible cardiac etiology for the reported fatigue. The clinician suspects aortic stenosis and plans to evaluate cardiac structure and function with an echocardiogram.

P:
Order an echocardiogram to assess cardiac structure and function, including aortic stenosis. Defer statin discussion to the PCP after the echocardiogram results. Continue current medications: simvastatin 20 mg and levothyroxine. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 3-4 weeks to discuss echocardiogram results and further management plan.
