S:
The patient reports intermittent back pain, primarily on the left, which is sometimes sharp and sometimes not, and occasionally moves. He also reports increased urine output, particularly at night, and unchanged appetite. He denies recent illness or fever. He reports that school is going well and mentions his physics teacher's socks. He notes that everyone seems to be sick these days. He has a brother who smokes and has been told not to smoke, but does not listen. He has a history of recurrent strep infections, having experienced four in the last five months.

O:
Physical exam reveals no tenderness to palpation of the back, no cervical lymphadenopathy, and clear lung sounds. Vital signs are within normal limits. Examination of the throat reveals no erythema or swelling, and the tonsils are midline.

A:
The patient's symptoms, particularly the recurrent strep infections and left back pain, raise concern for potential glomerular nephritis. A referral to an ENT is warranted to evaluate the possibility of tonsillectomy. Further investigations, including a CT scan and laboratory testing, are needed to assess the extent of the glomerular nephritis and rule out other causes of the symptoms.

P:
I am ordering a lipid panel, a UA, a CMP, a CBC, a vitamin D, and a TSH. I am referring the patient to an ENT for evaluation and consideration of tonsillectomy. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to review the results and discuss further management strategies. I will also counsel the patient's brother on the importance of avoiding smoking and encourage him to seek support if needed.
