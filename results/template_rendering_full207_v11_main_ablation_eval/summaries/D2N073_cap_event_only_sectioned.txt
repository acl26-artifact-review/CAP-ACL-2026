Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, which began approximately one week ago. He describes the pain as sharp and intermittent, and rates it as 6/10. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is worse with gripping and lifting objects. He denies any numbness or tingling. He reports no fever or other systemic symptoms. The patient reports no tenderness.

Findings
Examination reveals no swelling or erythema in the left hand. Range of motion is full and pain-free. Grip strength is normal. DTRs are 2+ bilaterally.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are most consistent with lateral carpal ligament instability.

Plan
Order wrist X-rays to evaluate for fractures. Provide wrist splinting and instruct the patient to avoid activities that exacerbate the pain. Follow up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
