Chief Complaint
Abdominal pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent abdominal pain, primarily in the lower right quadrant, for the past three weeks. The pain is described as sharp and intermittent, and is exacerbated by movement. He denies fever, nausea, or vomiting. He reports difficulty sleeping due to the pain. He states he has been drinking approximately 64 ounces of water daily. He reports drinking Gatorade and water throughout the day. He denies any recent trauma or changes in diet. He reports a family history of kidney disease.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the right lower quadrant. No rebound tenderness or guarding is present. Bowel sounds are normoactive bilaterally. The patient's temperature is 98.6°F, pulse 88 bpm, and blood pressure 130/80 mmHg. A genetic panel was performed and did not reveal clinically significant mutations. The patient's kidney is atrophied.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are concerning for possible kidney infection or kidney problems. Further investigation is warranted to rule out other potential causes of abdominal pain.

Plan
Order laboratory tests to evaluate for infection, including a complete blood count and urinalysis. Order abdominal radiographs to rule out obstruction. Recommend Tylenol instead of ibuprofen. Follow up in one week to assess response to treatment.
