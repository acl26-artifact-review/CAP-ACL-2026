Chief Complaint
Uncontrolled blood sugar and related symptoms.

History of Present Illness
Keith reports that his blood sugar levels have been creeping up slightly over the past few months, leading to feelings of being unwell. He's been maintaining a consistent diet, primarily consisting of fruit. He reports occasional nausea a couple of days ago, attributed to car travel, and occasional joint pain in his right knee, which he noticed when the doctor pointed it out. He's been taking protonix for reflux, which has improved his symptoms. He has a history of congestive heart failure and reports no recent weight gain or fluid retention. He also reports sleeping on his side to avoid laying flat. He has a history of right rotator cuff pain, which is currently well-managed. He's also been experiencing an elevated lyme titer.

Findings
Physical exam reveals an enlarged thyroid, clear lungs, and soft abdomen. The right knee shows erythema and fluctuant swelling. Lower extremity edema is noted on the right foot. Vital signs are stable. A rapid strep test was positive. Dragon reports a slightly elevated lyme titer and a positive rapid strep test.

Assessment
The patient presents with hyperglycemia, likely related to an underlying infection, evidenced by the elevated lyme titer and positive rapid strep test. His symptoms are consistent with a possible inflammatory response, potentially exacerbated by the infection. His history of congestive heart failure and right rotator cuff pain are relevant to his overall care.

Plan
Order a Western blot for lyme disease. Order amoxicillin 500mg three times daily for 10 days. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and monitor symptoms. Advise the patient to contact the office if any new symptoms develop.
