Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain that began approximately one week ago following a gymnastics class. He describes the pain as worsening with specific movements and reports pain with both active and passive range of motion. He previously took two to three days of acetaminophen without significant relief. He reports a history of left arm surgery. He denies recent trauma.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the left shoulder and pain with range of motion. Radiographs demonstrate no acute fracture or dislocation.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a musculoskeletal injury of the left shoulder, likely involving the rotator cuff.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 600 mg three times daily for one week. Advise rest and avoidance of activities that exacerbate the pain. Recommend ice or heat application as tolerated. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to assess response to treatment. Advise the patient to return sooner if symptoms persist or worsen. Consider repeat radiographs if symptoms do not improve after three weeks.
