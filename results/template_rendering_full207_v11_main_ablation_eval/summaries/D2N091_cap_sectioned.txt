Chief Complaint
Annual exam and concerns regarding insomnia and lingering knee issues.

History of Present Illness
Jerry is a 54-year-old male with a past medical history of osteoporosis and multiple sclerosis who presents for an annual exam. He reports traveling extensively over the summer, which has been stressful, and experiencing a lack of sleep. He has tried melatonin, meditation, and stretching, but finds nothing helpful. He continues to take fosamax for osteoporosis and reports no issues with his MS medication. He notes lingering issues with his right knee surgery, but otherwise reports no other symptoms, including denial of chest pain or shortness of breath. He reports insomnia and a stressful summer.

Findings
Physical exam reveals lung sounds are clear, heart sounds are normal. Lower extremity strength is 4/5 in the right leg and 3/5 in the left leg. Reflexes are intact. The right knee shows arthritic changes on examination. Vital signs are stable and within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's primary problems include osteoporosis, multiple sclerosis, lingering right knee issues, and insomnia. He has no acute findings.

Plan
Order a refill of fosamax 1 tablet per week, 11 refills. Continue the patient on their current MS medication and schedule a neurology follow-up. Follow up in four weeks to assess the lingering right knee issues and the effectiveness of the insomnia management strategies.
