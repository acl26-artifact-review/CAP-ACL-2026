Chief Complaint
Abdominal pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports abdominal pain, which he describes as sharp and intermittent. He states the pain is located in the right upper quadrant and is exacerbated by eating. He reports the pain has been present for approximately 3 days and has been getting worse. He denies fever, nausea, or vomiting. He reports occasional chills. The patient reports rare migraines. He uses excedrin migraine when needed. He takes sumatriptan occasionally. He is currently taking lisinopril 20 milligrams per day.

Findings
The patient reports right upper quadrant tenderness. A grade 3 systolic ejection murmur is heard. The patient reports nausea. The patient reports fatigue. The patient reports chills.

Assessment
Suspects an infection in the gallbladder may be contributing to the abdominal pain. Gallstones are considered a possible cause of abdominal pain. The patient has recent migraines.

Plan
The doctor ordered a right upper quadrant ultrasound. The doctor ordered a c-reactive protein test. The doctor ordered a complete blood count. Follow-up is scheduled. The doctor prescribed sumatriptan as needed for breakthrough migraine pain. The patient is advised to refrain from eating. A refill of lisinopril is requested.
