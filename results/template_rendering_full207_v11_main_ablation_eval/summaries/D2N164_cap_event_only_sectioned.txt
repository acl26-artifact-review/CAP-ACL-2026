Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a six-week history of progressive left shoulder pain, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies recent trauma and reports partial relief with acetaminophen. He states, 'It's really hard to lift things up.'

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. Vitals are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs to evaluate for rotator cuff pathology. Initiate physical therapy for targeted strengthening and range of motion. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
