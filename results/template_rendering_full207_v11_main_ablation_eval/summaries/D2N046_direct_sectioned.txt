Chief Complaint
Newly diagnosed ovarian cancer and associated symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing severe pain and bleeding, prompting her physician to order diagnostic tests. She reports mild depression related to the diagnosis. She denies vomiting but reports constipation and weight loss. She is 57 years old and reports her period started at age 13 and menopause occurred at age 28. She has not taken hormone replacement therapy and has no history of endometriosis. She was adopted and has no family history of gynecological cancers.

Findings
Abdominal CT imaging reveals a 3 centimeter left ovarian mass with associated localized lymph node involvement. No evidence of gross peritoneal or metastatic disease was identified. Physical exam reveals slight tenderness to palpation of the left lower quadrant. No external lesions were observed on the labia. The vaginal vault is within normal limits. The cervix is pink without lesions. Bimanual exam reveals a left adnexal mass.

Assessment
The patient is most likely diagnosed with stage three A ovarian cancer based on the CT findings and lymph node involvement.

Plan
Order blood tests including CA-125, HCG, AFP, and LDH to determine tumor type. Arrange for genetic counseling and testing to assess for genetic predisposition. Recommend surgical intervention including a hysterectomy, removal of both ovaries, and lymph node dissection. Recommend adjuvant chemotherapy with cisplatin and taxol, potentially including intraperitoneal chemotherapy. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to review pathology reports and assess treatment response.
