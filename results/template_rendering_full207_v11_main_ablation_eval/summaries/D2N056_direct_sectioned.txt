Chief Complaint
Recurrent lower respiratory infections and concern regarding frequent pneumonia episodes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of frequent lower respiratory infections, approximately monthly or every other month over the past six months, often requiring visits to urgent care and prescription of antibiotics. He attributes these infections to his work unloading hay, which began approximately six months ago. He reports symptoms including cough, mucus production, and low-grade fever, lasting as few as a couple of days or up to a week. He notes that his symptoms consistently begin when he is unloading hay. He denies any personal history of allergies and does not smoke. He also reports that ten siblings have similar symptoms and that his brother has been working around doing other things, leading to increased workload. He has not sought work for his family.

Findings
Vitals reveal a respiratory rate of 20 breaths per minute and oxygen saturation of 99% on room air. Physical exam demonstrates fine rales in the lung exam but no wheezes. The musculoskeletal exam is unremarkable, with no clubbing of the fingers. Chest x-ray reveals round glass opacities.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with hypersensitivity pneumonitis, also known as farmer's lung, likely triggered by exposure to mold and bacteria present in hay.

Plan
Prescribe a course of oral steroids to reduce lung inflammation. Order a CT scan of the lungs to confirm the diagnosis and a pulmonary function test to assess respiratory impairment. Recommend wearing a respirator when unloading hay to minimize exposure. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and further evaluate lung function.
