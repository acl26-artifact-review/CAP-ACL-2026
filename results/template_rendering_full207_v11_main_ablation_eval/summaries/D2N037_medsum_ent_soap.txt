S:
The patient reports new, bilateral elbow pain onset approximately one year and a half ago, primarily with use. He describes the pain as exacerbated by hand or arm use and relieved by rest. Ibuprofen 800mg three times daily has provided minimal relief. He denies any relief with icing. The patient reports pain with both flexion and extension, and with pronation and supination, but not with torsion. He states sensation is normal to the touch. The patient reports no current medications other than ibuprofen.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited range of motion on extension of the right elbow. Palpation of the medial elbow reveals tenderness. Pulses are equal bilaterally. X-rays demonstrate no fractures or bony misalignment. The patient's left elbow demonstrates limited range of motion on extension and some pain with flexion and extension. The patient's left elbow demonstrates some pain with torsion and twisting supination, but no pain with pronation. The patient's left elbow demonstrates no pain with supination.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with medial epicondylitis, commonly known as golfer's elbow. Further evaluation with an MRI is warranted to assess the extent of the injury and guide treatment decisions.

P:
I am ordering an MRI of both elbows to further evaluate the medial epicondylitis. I will also discuss the option of whole blood transfusion with the patient, which has shown promising results in similar cases. I will schedule the MRI and will follow up in two weeks to assess the results and discuss a treatment plan. I will counsel the patient on activity modification and conservative management strategies.
