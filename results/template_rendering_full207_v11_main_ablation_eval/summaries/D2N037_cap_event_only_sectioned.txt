Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, which he describes as having been present for a long time. He states the pain is exacerbated by golfing activities. He denies any recent injuries or trauma. He reports using over-the-counter pain relievers with limited relief.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral wrist and dorsal aspect of the hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No swelling or erythema is noted.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are most consistent with lateral epicondylitis.

Plan
A cervical MRI is planned. The patient will be referred to physical therapy for evaluation and treatment. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
