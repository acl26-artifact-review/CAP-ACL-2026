S:
The patient reports a primary care doctor referral for back pain that has been present for approximately two years. He states he has tried many things but nothing seems to work. The patient also reports experiencing sharp, stabbing pain in his back, which sometimes radiates down to his right leg and even his big toe, accompanied by numbness and tingling in his right leg and foot. He describes difficulty finding comfortable positions, particularly when sitting. The patient also reports gaining weight over the past years and used to enjoy playing golf, but this pain has significantly impacted his ability to do so.

O:
Physical exam reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with a positive lumbar paravertebral tenderness on palpation and a positive straight leg raise test. Neurological exam demonstrates decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at the L4 dermatome. Vitals are stable, with blood pressure within normal limits. No erythema or ecchymosis is noted.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with an L4-L5 herniated disc with radiculopathy, causing nerve impingement and resulting in radicular pain and sensory deficits.

P:
A spinal injection with a corticosteroid is ordered to reduce inflammation and alleviate pain. The patient will be monitored for response to the injection. A follow-up appointment will be scheduled to assess the effectiveness of the treatment and determine if further interventions are needed.
