Chief Complaint
Elevated and fluctuating blood pressures.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that his blood pressure has been elevated and fluctuating over the past three to four weeks, occasionally reaching over 180. He has experienced lightheadedness and slight headaches during these periods. He denies chest pain or difficulty breathing. He has been taking lisinopril 20mg twice daily and atorvastatin. He uses a pillbox and has been using the app to help manage his medications, though he found it a little confusing initially. His grandson helped him set it up. He reports feeling a little unsteady when he experiences dizziness and a slight headache when his blood pressure numbers are higher. He has not experienced any fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, trace bilateral lower extremity edema, and a blood pressure of 182/108. The patient reports feeling lightheaded.

Assessment
The patient's blood pressure is out of control. He has hyperlipidemia and osteoarthritis.

Plan
I am increasing the lisinopril dosage to 60 milligrams twice per day and adding 25 milligrams of hydrochlorothiazide daily. I'm also ordering a cholesterol panel, a comprehensive metabolic panel, and a urinalysis. I want you to check your blood pressure measurements twice a day for me for the next two weeks. Please continue to stay active as best as possible. I recommend you drink six to eight glasses of water per day. If you are feeling particularly dehydrated, increase that intake. I'm going to order shoulder radiographs. Please follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Please continue to take your medications as prescribed.
