Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing increasing shortness of breath, particularly with exertion, for approximately 10 days. He also notes swelling in his lower extremities, attributing it to dietary factors, including salty foods and recent house parties. He reports feeling lethargic. He is managing his type 1 diabetes with an insulin pump and has not observed recent blood sugar spikes. He has been trying natural solutions for depression, including outdoor stretching, but has not yet started any medications. He continues to take omeprazole for reflux and has been modifying his diet to reduce red bull and caffeine intake. He denies any chest pain, fever, or other concerning symptoms. He has not experienced acute shortness of breath at night, though he did have an episode about a week ago.

Findings
Physical exam reveals 1+ pitting edema in the lower extremities, a slight 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, crackles bilaterally at the bases of the lungs, and no jugular venous distension or carotid bruits. Oxygen saturation is adequate at rest. Laboratory results show no elevated white blood cell count.

Assessment
The patient is experiencing an acute heart failure exacerbation, likely secondary to fluid retention. He also continues to have type 1 diabetes, depression, and reflux.

Plan
Prescribe lasix 40 mg daily and instruct the patient to monitor and report weight changes. Order a follow-up hemoglobin A1c in one month. Refer the patient to psychiatry for ongoing support with depression. Continue omeprazole 20 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and adjust as needed. Advise the patient to call immediately if shortness of breath worsens or if he experiences any other concerning symptoms.
