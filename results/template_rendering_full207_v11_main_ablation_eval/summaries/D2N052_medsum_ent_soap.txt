S:
The patient reports some back pain on his right side lasting about a week, and he also noticed some blood in his urine. He describes the pain as constant and it has moved down to his right lower side. He denies real pain when urinating. He has experienced some nausea but no vomiting. He reports a family history of kidney stones.

O:
Vitals are stable. Physical exam reveals mild pain and tenderness to palpation of the abdomen, with no rebound or guarding. CVA is located near flank tenderness on the right. CT scan shows a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter, without evidence of hydronephrosis.

A:
The patient has a kidney stone with inflammation of the kidney. The CT scan confirms the presence of a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter.

P:
Rescribe ibuprofen 800 mg as needed for pain. Order a BMP and urinalysis with urine culture. Prescribe a strainer for urine. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one to two weeks to assess progress and discuss further treatment options, including lithotripsy if necessary. Provide instructions for increasing fluid intake and monitoring for worsening symptoms.
