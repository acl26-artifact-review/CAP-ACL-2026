O:
The right eye shows drusen, retinal thickening, and subretinal fluid with hemorrhage. The left eye shows retinal pigment epithelium changes and no hemorrhage. The cornea is clear in both eyes. Visual fields are relatively preserved.

A:
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with neovascular age-related macular degeneration, likely advanced to a stage with significant vision loss.

P:
I will initiate monthly injections of lucent into the vitreous to slow the progression of the wet macular degeneration. I will also schedule a follow-up to assess the effectiveness of the treatment and monitor for any further vision loss. I recommend the patient attempt to quit smoking and will discuss this further during the next visit.
