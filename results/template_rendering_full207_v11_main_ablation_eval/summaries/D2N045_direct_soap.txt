S:
The patient reports intermittent right knee pain exacerbated by running, particularly towards the end of the day. He describes ice and ibuprofen as previously effective. He denies any family history of arthritis. He states the pain is primarily worse on the outside of the right knee.

O:
Blood pressure was 120/60 mmHg. Heart rate was 58 bpm. Respirations were 14 breaths per minute. Pedal pulses were present and intact. No fever detected. The right knee revealed a small effusion and tenderness to palpation on the outside. X-rays showed no fracture or evidence of arthritis.

A:
The patient has a probable knee sprain due to overuse, likely related to running. There is no evidence of fracture or arthritis.

P:
Rescribe two Tylenol 500 mg tablets and two Ibuprofen 200 mg tablets to be taken three times daily. Order physical therapy for the right knee. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress. Advise a two-week period of reduced running and recommend weight training and squats at the gym under the guidance of a physical therapist.
