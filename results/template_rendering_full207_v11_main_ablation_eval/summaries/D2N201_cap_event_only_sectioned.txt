Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies recent trauma and states acetaminophen provides some relief. He also reports some stiffness and limited range of motion. The patient states, "It's really hard to reach overhead when I'm working."

Findings
Physical exam reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and positive impingement testing. Vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinitis.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs. Initiate physical therapy. Prescribe acetaminophen 500mg every 6 hours as needed for pain.
