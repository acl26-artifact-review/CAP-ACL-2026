Chief Complaint
Chronic lumbar back pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing back pain for approximately two years, initially following a basketball injury involving a fall. He has tried various treatments, including injections, but has not found sustained relief. The pain is described as sharp and throbbing at times. It radiates down his left leg, occasionally causing numbness in his toes. He reports experiencing weakness in his legs, particularly when standing for extended periods. He has also experienced episodes of incontinence, specifically once, related to prolonged standing. He has a history of smoking, though he declined to provide details. He currently sits at home watching television and has gained approximately eighty pounds in the past year. He reports that his fourth cousin had scoliosis and gained a lot of weight.

Findings
Physical examination reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness upon flexion and extension. Straight leg raise testing is positive at L4 dermatome. Neurological examination shows decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at L4 dermatome. Heart rate is regular and within normal limits. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
Based on the patient's reported symptoms, physical examination findings, and MRI results, the patient presents with a herniated disc at L4-L5 with radiculopathy, causing lumbar radiculopathy and resulting in his reported symptoms.

Plan
I recommend a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and pain. I will prescribe a medication to help with anxiety to facilitate the injection. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess the response to the injection and continue to manage his pain. I will also recommend physical therapy to improve strength and flexibility.
