Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left shoulder, which began approximately four days ago following a tackle during a football game. He describes landing awkwardly on his shoulder and experiencing immediate pain. The pain has been gradually worsening, particularly with activities like reaching overhead and lifting. He reports taking Tylenol for the pain, but it hasn't provided significant relief. He denies any numbness or tingling in his fingers and reports no neck pain or other joint problems. He had an appendectomy approximately three to four years ago and reports no other surgeries or significant medical problems. He does have a mild headache, but denies fever or chills.

Findings
Physical examination reveals pain to palpation of the acromioclavicular joint on the right shoulder. Range of motion is limited, with decreased abduction and adduction to 90 degrees. Radial pulse is present and palpable. Cervical spine exam is unremarkable. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 120/76 mmHg, heart rate 82 bpm, oxygen saturation 95% on room air. The nurse reports X-ray findings of an acute acromioclavicular joint fracture.

Assessment
The patient presents with an acute acromioclavicular joint fracture, likely secondary to a traumatic injury. Clinical findings are consistent with a shoulder sprain.

Plan
I'm going to order a cat scan of the right shoulder to get a better look of how the joint looks. I'm going to prescribe meloxicam 15 milligrams once a day for pain management. I'm also going to refer you to physical therapy to help strengthen your shoulder and avoid surgery. Please wear a sling for support. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
