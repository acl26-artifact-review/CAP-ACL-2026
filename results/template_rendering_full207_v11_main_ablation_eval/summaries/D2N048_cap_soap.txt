S:
The patient reports right foot pain following a tennis injury, describing a fall and twisting of his foot. He reports wrapping the foot and taking ibuprofen for the pain. He denies numbness or tingling. The patient reports that he is unable to continue playing tennis due to the injury.

O:
The patient's vitals are within normal limits: blood pressure 120/75, respiratory rate 18, temperature 97.1°F, and pulse 60 bpm. A physical exam reveals bruising of the plantar and dorsal aspects of the right foot, swelling, and warmth. The right foot exam reveals a positive piano key test of the first and second metatarsals. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds. The patient's left foot exam is normal. A stridor is noted. The patient reports stridor.

A:
The patient has a Lisfranc fracture, likely secondary to the tennis injury. The examination findings and X-ray confirm a dorsal displacement of the second metatarsal base with a 3mm separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and a bony fragment in the Lisfranc joint space. The patient's symptoms are consistent with a Lisfranc fracture.

P:
I am recommending surgery for the Lisfranc fracture. An MRI is ordered to further assess the fracture and any ligament injuries. The patient will follow up with me in approximately one week. The patient will wear a cast for six to eight weeks and use crutches for six to eight weeks. I am recommending physical therapy after surgery.
