Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a fall yesterday while attempting to change a light bulb on a ladder. He experienced a stumble and a twist of the knee. He denies any other injuries. He has been applying ice to the knee and taking ibuprofen, but reports minimal relief. He has a history of afib and is currently taking digoxin. He also had a previous nose job.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited by pain with forward flexion and rotation. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are present and equal bilaterally. Sensation is intact. Radiographs demonstrate no fractures but reveal mild joint effusion.

Assessment
Acute medial meniscus sprain or strain of the right knee.

Plan
Prescribe a knee brace and crutches for two days. Recommend ibuprofen 800 mg every six hours as needed. Order medications and procedures discussed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to reassess and consider an MRI if symptoms do not improve.
