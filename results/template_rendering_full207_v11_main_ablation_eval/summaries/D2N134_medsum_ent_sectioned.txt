Chief Complaint
Reports fatigue, joint pain, nausea, and loss of appetite related to a suspected Lyme disease diagnosis.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent fatigue and significant joint pain, particularly in her elbows, accompanied by nausea and a diminished appetite. She describes the joint pain as sharp and worsening with movement. She attributes the onset of these symptoms to a tick bite acquired while hiking approximately two weeks prior, and notes the presence of a bull's-eye rash on her abdomen. She has been monitoring her blood sugar, noting occasional fluctuations, and continues to take metformin as prescribed. She reports taking lisinopril for hypertension and monitoring her blood pressure at home, with readings generally within acceptable limits. She also reports experiencing body aches.

Findings
Physical examination reveals swelling in the bilateral elbow joints, with tenderness to palpation. A bull's-eye rash is observed on the left abdomen, appearing warm to the touch but without reported pain. Vital signs are stable, with no fever detected. Laboratory results confirm a positive Lyme titer.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms, combined with the positive Lyme titer and the presence of a bull's-eye rash, indicate a likely diagnosis of Lyme disease. Her existing type 2 diabetes and hypertension remain active medical concerns.

Plan
I have prescribed doxycycline 100 milligrams twice daily for three weeks. I have ordered a hemoglobin A1c to assess her diabetes control and an order for a lipid panel. I will also schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to monitor her progress and discuss further management strategies. The nurse will administer the medication and provide patient education regarding Lyme disease treatment and potential side effects. I have instructed the patient to continue taking her current medications, metformin and lisinopril, as directed.
