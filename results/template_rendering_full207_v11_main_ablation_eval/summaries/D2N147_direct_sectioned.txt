Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain following a football injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that approximately four days ago, he was tackled while playing football and experienced pain in his right shoulder. He describes a vague pain initially, which has progressed to a more significant ache, particularly with lifting and reaching. He has been taking acetaminophen for the pain, but it has not provided significant relief. He denies any fever or chills. He reports a small head injury during the tackle.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the acromioclavicular joint on the right shoulder. Abduction and adduction are limited to approximately 90 degrees. Pronation and supination elicit pain. Cervical spine exam is unremarkable. Vital signs are within normal limits: blood pressure 122/76 mmHg, heart rate 82 beats per minute, oxygen saturation 95% on room air. Right shoulder x-ray demonstrates an acute acromioclavicular joint fracture. The nurse performed a right shoulder x-ray prior to the examination.

Assessment
Acute acromioclavicular joint fracture of the right shoulder.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain management. Order a CT scan of the right shoulder to further evaluate the fracture. Refer the patient to physical therapy for rehabilitation and strengthening exercises. Instruct the patient to wear a sling for support and avoid activities that exacerbate the pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess healing and progress.
