Chief Complaint
Left ankle pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his left ankle following a fall yesterday while walking. He describes a feeling of 'extended and stretched' and 'killing me for a couple days.' He reports elevating and icing the ankle and taking ibuprofen. He denies prior significant ankle injuries. He and his wife have been walking on new trails at the new Rex Center.

Findings
Physical examination reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus and swelling of the left ankle. Palpation elicits tenderness on the lateral side. Range of motion is limited. The ankle demonstrates no laxity. Capillary refill is brisk bilaterally. Dorsalis pedis pulses are strong bilaterally. Neurovascular examination is intact. An X-ray of the left ankle reveals no fracture or bony abnormalities.

Assessment
Ankle sprain of the lateral ligament complex.

Plan
The patient will receive an air cast and crutches to stabilize the ankle and limit weight-bearing. Continue the RICE protocol. Follow up in two weeks to monitor progress. Continue NSAIDs as needed. Schedule physical therapy as appropriate.
