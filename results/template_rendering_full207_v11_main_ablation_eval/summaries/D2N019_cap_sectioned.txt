Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began yesterday after moving boxes. He describes a popping sensation and a strain while lifting. He has been taking ibuprofen, but the pain persists and throbs when the ice is removed. He reports tenderness to palpation and swelling in the area. He denies any prior injuries.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral epicondyle, swelling, and redness in the right elbow. Range of motion is limited due to pain. The patient reports pain with flexion and extension of the elbow and pain on the dorsal aspect of the forearm. X-rays show no fracture but some swelling.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with lateral epicondylitis.

Plan
Order motrin 800 mg every six hours. Provide a sling for comfort. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week if symptoms do not improve. Dragon, please order the medications and procedures.
