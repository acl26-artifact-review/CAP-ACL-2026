Chief Complaint
Tick bite around the knee causing pain and irritation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports acquiring a tick bite approximately one week ago while spending time in the woods and yard. He describes the area around his knee as burning and quite annoying. He denies fever or chills. He reports no other joint pain, but does experience typical arthritic pain. He reports hunting approximately two weeks ago and mentions a 'type thing' called 'windle season' where they train dogs. He has had previous tick bites but none recently. He has not been diagnosed with Lyme disease previously. He describes a general feeling of unwellness, accompanied by a headache. He denies cough or shortness of breath. He has been periodically checking his blood pressure at home and feels it is doing okay, and is taking lisinopril.

Findings
Physical examination reveals erythema and edema over the right patella, with some fluctuance. The right knee demonstrates mild tenderness to palpation. Range of motion is full. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 120/77 mmHg, heart rate 67 bpm, temperature 98.4°F, and respiratory rate 16 breaths per minute. The patient reports no fever. Examination of the lungs is clear bilaterally.

Assessment
Based on the clinical presentation, including the erythema, edema, and tenderness of the right knee, a possible Lyme disease diagnosis is suspected. Further investigation with blood tests is warranted.

Plan
I am starting you on doxycycline 100 mg twice daily for three weeks. I am also ordering a Lyme titer and Western blot to confirm or rule out Lyme disease. Continue your lisinopril 20 mg once daily. I am ordering a lipid panel to monitor your cholesterol levels. I will follow up with you in four weeks to assess your progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed. Please contact me if you experience any worsening symptoms or have any concerns.
