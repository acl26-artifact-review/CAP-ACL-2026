Chief Complaint
Right hip pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports ongoing right groin pain for over two years, initially triggered by walking around the infusion room, now present with any movement. The pain is rated 2-7 on a 10-point scale and is exacerbated by pivoting. The pain began in February 2020. The patient has experienced several bulging discs and has undergone prior surgeries including a partial clavicle repair and infertility surgery, as well as a gallbladder removal. She has intentionally lost 110 pounds through weight watchers, starting in 2018 and maintaining that weight for over a year. The patient reports limiting alcohol intake to less than five drinks per month and denies tobacco use.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a height of 5'6", weight of 169 pounds, blood pressure of 115/75, and a pulse rate of 67. The patient's ankles show no edema or tenderness. Range of motion is decreased in right hip with pain in the groin region during external and internal rotation, and resisted hip flexion. Deep tendon reflexes are normal throughout. No focal motor or sensory deficits are noted.

Assessment
Right hip degenerative joint disease.

Plan
Recommend a low-impact exercise program and NSAIDs as needed for pain management. Discuss the potential benefits of a corticosteroid injection into the right hip joint and obtain informed consent. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months to assess response to treatment and consider repeat injection if necessary. Provide written instructions regarding the injection procedure, potential risks, and post-injection care.
