Chief Complaint
Left flank pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent left flank pain for the past 3 days. He describes the pain as sharp and stabbing, rated 7/10 on a pain scale. It is exacerbated by movement and deep breathing. He denies fever, nausea, or vomiting. He reports a history of kidney stones 5 years ago and has been taking potassium citrate daily for that condition. He states he has been drinking less water than usual due to increased sodium intake from fast food.

Findings
Blood pressure 138/82 mmHg, heart rate 88 bpm. Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the left flank. No signs of fever or inflammation. Urinalysis shows microscopic hematuria.

Assessment
The patient presents with acute left flank pain, likely secondary to nephrolithiasis. Risk factors include prior history of kidney stones and recent dietary changes.

Plan
Order a CT scan of the abdomen and pelvis to evaluate for obstruction and stone location. Continue potassium citrate as prescribed. Advise the patient to increase fluid intake to 2 liters per day. Refer to urology for further management if indicated.
