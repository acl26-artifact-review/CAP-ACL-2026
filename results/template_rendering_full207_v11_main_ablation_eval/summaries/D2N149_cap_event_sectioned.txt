Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath

History of Present Illness
The patient reports shortness of breath for approximately two weeks, with onset gradually worsening. He states he wakes up with shortness of breath and feels short of breath when returning home. He reports coughing up blood for about three weeks. He denies recent trauma. He reports his blood sugar has been running high and down, and he wears a Dexcom. He reports his blood pressure has been running somewhat low and he takes norvasc on some days. He had a knee problem some time ago but denies swelling.

Findings
Oxygen saturation 94% on pulse oximetry. Lung exam reveals some rales and rhonchi on the right side, diminished breath sounds on the right side. Heart exam reveals a two-plus systolic ejection murmur. Examination reveals one plus nonpitting edema on both lower extremities. No calf tenderness.

Assessment
The patient presents with shortness of breath and cough with blood, suggestive of a possible pulmonary infection or other underlying cardiopulmonary issue. His diabetes and hypertension are contributing factors.

Plan
Order a chest x-ray and cat scan. Refer to the emergency department for further evaluation and management. Increase metformin dose to 750mg twice daily. Hold norvasc until further evaluation. Schedule follow-up in three weeks.
