Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain, exacerbated by weight-bearing. He states the pain began approximately 24 hours ago following a fall. He describes the pain as sharp and throbbing, with warmth noted. The patient denies any numbness or previous ankle injuries. He reports taking ibuprofen last night and this morning for the pain.

Findings
The patient's right ankle demonstrates mild warmth to palpation. Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the dorsomedial aspect of the right ankle. Range of motion is limited due to pain. The patient's right foot displays normal sensation to light touch.

Assessment
Right ankle sprain, consistent with clinical presentation and examination findings.

Plan
Order an air cast for the right ankle. Advise the patient to keep their leg elevated. Recommend icing the right ankle. Order crutches for weight-bearing. Schedule a follow-up in four weeks or sooner if symptoms persist. The patient should contact the office if pain persists.
