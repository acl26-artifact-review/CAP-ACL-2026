Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for the past three weeks, with no reported injury or trauma. The pain has been progressively worsening and is exacerbated by overhead activities. He denies any recent falls or direct trauma. He has been taking acetaminophen with limited relief. He reports difficulty reaching overhead and sleeping comfortably due to the pain. He has not tried icing the shoulder.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited range of motion in the left shoulder, particularly abduction and external rotation. Palpation of the greater tuberosity elicits tenderness. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the left hand. Neurological exam is intact. The x-ray of the shoulder was normal, showing no fractures or bony abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
An MRI of the left shoulder has been ordered to further evaluate the rotator cuff. Referral to physical therapy has been initiated for a six to eight week course to strengthen the shoulder musculature. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to physical therapy and MRI results.
