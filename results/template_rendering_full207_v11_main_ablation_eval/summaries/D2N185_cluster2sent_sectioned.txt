Chief Complaint
Ashley reports persistent abdominal pain and urinary symptoms.

History of Present Illness
Ashley presented with sudden onset abdominal pain and right-sided pain, initially attributed to a stomach virus. She experienced persistent vomiting and pain, prompting her parents to seek medical attention. Subsequent episodes included fever and similar symptoms, leading to concern for recurrent infection. She reports burning during urination and blood in the urine. She denies recent trauma. She takes pepcid 40mg daily and claritin for allergies. Her father had a renal transplant that failed, and he died of dialysis in his 40s. Her aunt also has kidney disease. She drinks only gatorade and water.

Findings
Ashley is a 14-year-old female with a history of atrophic right kidney and cysts in the kidney and ovaries. Lymph nodes are swollen. Physical exam reveals normoglycemia and blood pressure within normal limits. No abnormalities were noted on the physical exam other than the reported findings.

Assessment
The patient presents with end-stage renal disease, chronic kidney disease stage five, and hypertension. She also has obstructive neuropathy and a history of recurrent kidney infections.

Plan
Order an ultrasound to monitor kidney size and function, a urinalysis to assess protein and blood in the urine, and a blood pressure check. Avoid non-steroidal anti-inflammatory medicines like ibuprofen and opt to use tylenol instead. Monitor for dehydration. Follow up with the primary care physician in four weeks or sooner if symptoms worsen. Advise the patient and family to contact the clinic if any new symptoms develop.
