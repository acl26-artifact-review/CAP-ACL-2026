Chief Complaint
Low back pain following a golf injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports low back pain that began four days ago after a golf swing. He describes a sudden pop and immediate inability to continue his swing, requiring him to stop and loosen his back. He initially treated the pain with Tylenol and ibuprofen, but found little relief. He reports some improvement over the weekend, but the pain persists. He also experienced right foot drop and some difficulty with his right leg, which improved somewhat this morning. He has a history of surgery on his lower back ten years ago. He is currently managing his type 2 diabetes with metformin and regularly monitors his blood sugar. He admits to a sweet tooth and enjoys jelly beans.

Findings
Physical examination reveals lumbar spine pain to palpation at L5. Range of motion is limited with flexion and extension. Straight leg raise is positive for the right leg. Vital signs are stable. The x-ray demonstrates normal lumbar spine alignment. The diabetic lab results show an elevated hemoglobin A1c of 8%.

Assessment
Acute lumbar strain secondary to musculoskeletal injury.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily. Refer to physical therapy. Order an MRI to rule out disc herniation. Increase metformin to 1000 mg twice daily. Order a repeat hemoglobin A1c in two months. Refill lisinopril 20 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks.
