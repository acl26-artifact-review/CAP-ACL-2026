Chief Complaint
Fatigue and lightheadedness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling fatigued and lightheaded, particularly when standing. He also describes occasional bloating and reports feeling 'out of it.' He notes intermittent mild cough and occasional slight chest discomfort. He reports experiencing some weight gain and swelling in his lower extremities. He states the nurse previously discussed his diagnosis of congestive heart failure and hypertension. He reports managing his blood pressure with medication and has been monitoring it at home. He endorses fatigue from a review of systems completed upon check-in.

Findings
Physical exam reveals jugular venous distention and 1+ pitting edema in the lower extremities. A three out of six systolic ejection murmur is auscultated. Chest x-ray demonstrates mild pulmonary edema. The echocardiogram shows a reduced ejection fraction of 45% and mitral regurgitation. Vital signs are stable.

Assessment
The patient presents with congestive heart failure, hypertension, and related symptoms including fatigue, lightheadedness, and edema.

Plan
Increase lasix to 80 mg daily. Order an echocardiogram. Instruct the patient to monitor blood pressures at home and report any elevations. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks.
