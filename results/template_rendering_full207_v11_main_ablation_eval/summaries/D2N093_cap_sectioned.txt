Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath

History of Present Illness
Lawrence is a 62-year-old male with a past medical history of type I diabetes, congestive heart failure, depression, and reflux. He presents with complaints of shortness of breath. He reports swelling in his legs and stiffness upon waking, particularly after getting up in the morning. He notes that walking up a flight of stairs is difficult. He has experienced shortness of breath approximately 10 days ago and has not been concerned about it. He is on an insulin pump and reports that his blood sugars have been generally okay, though he admits to forgetting to monitor them on weekends. He has been cutting back on red bull and caffeine. He reports feeling lethargic and has been trying natural solutions for his depression, such as outdoor stretches, but is open to medication suggestions. He is currently taking omeprazole for his reflux. He denies any chest pain, fever, chills, or cough. He reports some swelling in his legs and stiffness upon waking.

Findings
Physical exam reveals vital signs within normal limits. Jugular venous distension is not appreciated. A grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur is heard. Crackles are present in the bases of the lungs. 1+ pitting edema is noted in the lower extremities.

Assessment
The patient is experiencing an acute heart failure exacerbation, likely secondary to volume overload and underlying congestive heart failure. He also has a history of type 1 diabetes and depression.

Plan
Start lasix at 40 milligrams daily. Monitor weight and report any worsening shortness of breath. Order a hemoglobin a1c test in one month. Refer to psychiatry for further evaluation and management of depression. Continue omeprazole at 20 milligrams daily. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment. Advise patient to call if experiencing worsening shortness of breath.
