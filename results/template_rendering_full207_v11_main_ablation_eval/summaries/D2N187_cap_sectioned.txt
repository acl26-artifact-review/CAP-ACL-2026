Chief Complaint
Sudden visual distortion and blurring of vision.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sudden visual distortion and blurring of vision. He states he has trouble adjusting to dim lights, particularly at restaurants, and reports that straight lines and door frames appear crooked. The patient also reports a history of macular degeneration and a previous difficulty adjusting to dim lights. He reports smoking a pack of cigarettes a day and occasionally attempts to quit, and he occasionally checks his blood pressure but doesn't always remember to do so. He reports experiencing visual distortion and blurring of vision.

Findings
Examination reveals drusen in the macula, retinal pigment epithelium subretinal fluid accumulation, a choroidal neovascular membrane, and hemorrhage in the right eye. The left eye shows no abnormalities. The patient's visual acuity is reduced when reading.

Assessment
The patient has neovascular age-related macular degeneration.

Plan
The patient will receive an injection of lucentes once a month to slow the rate of vision decline or stop further vision loss. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
