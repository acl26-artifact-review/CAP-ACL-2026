Chief Complaint
Reports ongoing shortness of breath with exertion and a history of chest pain following a recent heart attack.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing significant shortness of breath, particularly with exertion, following a recent heart attack and stent placement in the right coronary artery. He was hospitalized for 48 hours and reports feeling better at home, though still struggling with the experience. He's been advised to take it easy and is taking medication as prescribed. He reports some minor soreness at the cardiac catheterization site, which has resolved. He has not experienced any further chest pain since the stent placement. He continues to take brilinta twice daily and aspirin nightly.

Findings
Blood pressure is 127/70 mmHg. Jugular venous distension is absent. Carotid bruits are absent. Heart sounds reveal a slight 3/6 systolic ejection murmur at the left base. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. Radial pulse is palpable and intact. The cardiac catheterization site is clean, dry, and intact with no hematoma. Mild mitral regurgitation is noted on the echocardiogram.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is post-myocardial infarction with ongoing symptoms of shortness of breath and a history of chest pain. Mild mitral regurgitation is present.

Plan
Continue brilinta twice daily and aspirin nightly. Initiate cardiac rehabilitation. Order a six-month echocardiogram. Schedule a follow-up appointment in six weeks. Advise the patient to resume swimming gradually, starting with a few laps, and to monitor for any recurrence of chest pain or worsening shortness of breath. Order liver function studies and a lipid panel for follow-up.
