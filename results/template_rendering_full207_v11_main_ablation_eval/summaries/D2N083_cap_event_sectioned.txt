Chief Complaint
Evaluation of right elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began approximately four days ago following increased pottery activity. He describes the pain as primarily on the inside of the elbow and rates it as 6/10. The pain is exacerbated by wrist flexion and resisted wrist extension, and it keeps him from lifting and doing activities. He has tried ibuprofen with minimal relief. He denies any recent trauma or injury to the elbow.

Findings
Physical exam reveals moderate tenderness to palpation over the medial epicondyle of the right elbow. Range of motion is limited by pain. Wrist pronation and supination elicit pain. Radiographs of the right elbow demonstrate no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
Based on the clinical presentation and examination findings, the patient is diagnosed with medial epicondylitis.

Plan
I am ordering a sling to be worn at all times while awake. I am instructing the patient to apply ice to the elbow three times daily for 20 minutes. I am also prescribing ibuprofen 600 mg every 6 hours with food for one week. I would like to see you again in one week to assess your progress.
