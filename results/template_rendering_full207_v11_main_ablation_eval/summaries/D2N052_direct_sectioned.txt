Chief Complaint
Right flank pain and hematuria secondary to a suspected kidney stone.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right flank pain lasting approximately one week, with the pain having migrated to the right lower side. He also reports hematuria when urinating. Ibuprofen provided some relief, but the pain persists. The patient reports a family history of kidney stones and previously experienced similar symptoms, though he is unsure of the details. He reports having some pain over the weekend and participated in an adult Easter egg hunt.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild abdominal pain and tenderness to palpation. Vital signs are stable, with no fever or elevated blood pressure. A CT scan of the right flank demonstrates a 0.5-centimeter stone located in the proximal right ureter, with no evidence of hydronephrosis. Laboratory results are pending.

Assessment
The patient has a confirmed kidney stone in the proximal right ureter.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 800 mg as needed for pain. Instruct the patient to increase fluid intake to facilitate stone passage. Order a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one to two weeks to assess progress and consider further management options, including lithotripsy if necessary. The nurse will administer the prescribed medication and provide instructions regarding urine straining.
