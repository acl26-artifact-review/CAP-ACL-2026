S:
The patient reports difficulty swallowing over a period of time, with pain when swallowing, and reports stress related to moving. He has experienced approximately ten pounds of weight gain over the past month. He also reports epigastric pain.

O:
Blood pressure is 133/70 mmHg. Heart rate is 19 beats per minute. Oxygen saturation is 100%. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. The barium swallow revealed two areas of mild esophageal narrowing. Murphy's sign is negative. The patient's eyes are equal and reactive to light.

A:
The patient has acute esophagitis, likely secondary to esophageal narrowing. Weight gain is a significant finding.

P:
Rescribe protonix 40 mg once daily. Prescribe carafate 1 gram four times daily for one month. Schedule an upper endoscopy in one week. Advise the patient to decrease alcohol and caffeine intake and to eat slowly and chew food thoroughly. Advise the patient to avoid citrus foods, fruits, and spicy foods until symptoms improve.
