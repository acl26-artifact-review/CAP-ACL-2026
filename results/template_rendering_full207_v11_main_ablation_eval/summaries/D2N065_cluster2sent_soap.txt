S:
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall, describing it as a constant throbbing pain and warmth. He states the pain is exacerbated by touch and reports it feels warm. He has previously sprained this ankle and has taken ibuprofen and ice for the pain. He denies numbness or tingling in his foot. He reports ecchymosis on the lateral ankle.

O:
The patient's blood pressure was 120/80 mmHg, heart rate 80 bpm, and SpO2 98%. Capillary refill was less than 3 seconds in the right foot. The x-ray revealed no fractures or bony abnormalities. Palpation of the ankle revealed tenderness to the anterior, lateral, and soft tissue.

A:
The patient has a right ankle sprain with moderate tenderness and swelling. The lack of fracture findings is reassuring.

P:
The provider instructed the patient to keep his leg elevated, ice it five times a day for 20 minutes, and use an air cast to stabilize the ankle. The patient was advised to stay off the leg for one to two days and gradually increase weight-bearing as tolerated. The provider recommended contacting the office if pain persists after one week, potentially scheduling an MRI if needed.
