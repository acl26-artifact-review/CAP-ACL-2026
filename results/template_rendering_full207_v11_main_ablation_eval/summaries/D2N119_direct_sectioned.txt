Chief Complaint
Persistent asthma symptoms and difficulty managing them.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent asthma symptoms for the past six months, frequently triggered by colds and exacerbated by exposure to her cat. She reports using her albuterol inhaler almost daily and occasionally needing to use it multiple times per night. She has experienced multiple urgent care and emergency department visits over the past year, typically requiring prednisone treatment. She recalls being hospitalized as a child with asthma symptoms. She enjoys hiking and is looking forward to resuming outdoor activities, but is concerned about potential triggers.

Findings
Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 128/82 mmHg, respiratory rate 16 breaths per minute, oxygen saturation 99% on room air. Heart sounds are regular and without murmurs. Lung exam reveals diminished breath sounds bilaterally with occasional expiratory wheezing. No lymphadenopathy is palpated. Pulmonary function tests confirm an asthma diagnosis. No clubbing or edema is noted.

Assessment
Moderate to intermittent asthma, consistent with patient's reported symptoms and pulmonary function test results.

Plan
Start fluticasone propionate 500 mcg twice daily via inhaler, instructing the patient to use it consistently to prevent daily symptoms. Continue albuterol inhaler as needed, prn., with a maximum of two puffs every four to six hours. Provide an asthma action plan to guide symptom management. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 30 days to assess response to therapy and address any ongoing concerns. Advise the patient to avoid cat exposure and to discuss hiking plans with a pulmonologist prior to resuming outdoor activities.
