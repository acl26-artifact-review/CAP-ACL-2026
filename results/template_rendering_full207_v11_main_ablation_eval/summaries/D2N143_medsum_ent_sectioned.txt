Chief Complaint
Symptoms of discomfort, a change in discharge, and odor in her vaginal region, along with pain in the right side of her vagina.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing discomfort, a change in vaginal discharge, and an odor in her vaginal region. She notes that the right side of her vagina is starting to become painful, a symptom that has persisted. She recently learned that her partner has been in sexual encounters with other people, prompting her to seek evaluation for sexually transmitted infections (STIs). She denies fevers or chills. She has a history of an irregular pap smear approximately six months ago, which was normal. She has been dating her current partner for three years, and reports that he began cheating on her approximately three months ago.

Findings
A pelvic exam revealed no external lesions, normal labia majora and labia minora, and normal physiological vaginal discharge. Cervical motion tenderness and adnexal tenderness were absent. Swabs were obtained for testing for gonorrhea, chlamydia, trichomoniasis, syphilis, HIV, and hepatitis C.

Assessment
Screening for sexually transmitted infections.

Plan
I am going to send off std testing for gonorrhea, chlamydia, trichomoniasis, and I'll have you give some blood for syphilis, hiv and hepatitis c. These tests will take longer than others. We will call you if we see anything, but if you haven't heard anything, you can call us in three days for results. Please add that she is high risk due to an abnormal pap smear in the past, and the recent discovery of her sexual partner having multiple partners, for some period of time.
