Chief Complaint
Elevated blood sugars requiring further investigation and medication adjustment.

History of Present Illness
Natalie is a 39-year-old female presenting for a check-up following elevated lab results. She reports experiencing no symptoms of hyperglycemia, but her recent blood sugars were high. She's currently taking 1000mg of metformin daily and monitoring her blood sugars at home. She reports a diet focused on avoiding sugars and regular power walking (approximately 30 minutes daily). She has an ACL repair from five years ago and reports no issues with her knee. Her blood pressure is well-controlled on lisinopril, and she monitors it at home.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a systolic ejection murmur of approximately 3/6. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. Extremities are warm and well perfused. No swelling is noted. Her oxygen saturation is 98% on room air.

Assessment
The patient presents with hyperglycemia, as evidenced by an elevated A1c of 8.1%. This requires further investigation and adjustment of her current medication regimen.

Plan
I am prescribing Jardiance 10mg daily to help manage the patient's hyperglycemia. I'm also ordering a comprehensive metabolic panel (CMP) and lipid panel to assess her overall cardiovascular risk. I will follow up with the patient in four weeks to assess the effectiveness of the medication and further investigate the cause of the elevated blood sugars.
