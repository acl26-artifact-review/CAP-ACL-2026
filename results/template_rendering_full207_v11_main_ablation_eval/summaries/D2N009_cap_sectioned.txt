Chief Complaint
Back pain following a refrigerator move.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain that began five days ago after pushing a refrigerator through a room. He describes the pain as being on the lower right side of his back. He has a history of a previous discectomy. He has been taking ibuprofen and once tried tylenol and ibuprofen together, which provided some relief. He denies any numbness or tingling in his legs or any other systemic symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath, or abdominal pain. He reports difficulty bending over.

Findings
Physical examination reveals pain to palpation of the right lumbar spine, decreased flexion and extension of the back, and a positive straight leg raise on the right. Vital signs are stable. X-rays demonstrate normal bony alignment. Lab results are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient has a lumbar strain. There is no evidence of fracture or other acute pathology on the x-ray.

Plan
The doctor will order an MRI to rule out nerve compression. The patient will be prescribed meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily and ultram 50 milligrams every four hours as needed. Physical therapy will be scheduled following the MRI results. The patient can continue to take tylenol if desired and discontinue the ibuprofen. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
