Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a basketball injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a basketball injury, sustained when he fell to the ground while playing. He reports landing on his side and states the inside of his knee hurt. He received ice and an ace wrap at the scene and was unable to continue playing. The pain has persisted for seven days. He is currently using exercises and an ace wrap for management.

Findings
Examination reveals right knee swelling and effusion, with palpation tenderness to the medial aspect. Range of motion is decreased, with decreased range of motion in extension and flexion. The negative varus and valgus stress test was noted. X-ray demonstrates no fractures but shows right knee effusion.

Assessment
Right knee medial collateral ligament strain.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam fifteen milligrams daily for pain and swelling. Provide a right knee brace for a few days. Refer the patient to physical therapy for strengthening exercises. Schedule a follow-up appointment in seven days to reassess and consider further imaging if needed. Advise the patient to discontinue nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory medications. Encourage continued light walking and monitor blood sugars closely.
