Chief Complaint
Abnormal lab findings, low hemoglobin.

History of Present Illness
Vincent is a 56-year-old male presenting with abnormal lab findings. He reports feeling very lightheaded and has experienced decreased appetite. He mentions a slight nick from a knife while chopping onions a few weeks ago, but denies any bleeding from his stools or other injuries. He reports nausea when sitting in a car, and has been taking some caffeine. He had a possible lung cancer scare a few years ago, which was determined to be benign. He reports his knee replacement is doing okay and he can walk without problems, though he's not ready for running. He has been feeling lightheaded.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to the right lower quadrant upon palpation. The patient's heart rhythm is regular, and his lungs are clear. There is no edema noted in the lower extremities. Hemoglobin is 8.2.

Assessment
The patient's low hemoglobin level suggests anemia. He has a history of gastritis and a slight polyp which was biopsied.

Plan
I am ordering a complete blood count and protonix 40 milligrams once a day. I recommend reducing caffeine intake and avoiding ibuprofen and alcohol. Please follow up in two weeks. ,
