Chief Complaint
Back pain following a forceful movement.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain that began five days ago after pushing a refrigerator, localized to the lower right side of his back. He has a prior discectomy and reports concern about potential recurrence. He has been taking ibuprofen and once tried tylenol and ibuprofen together with some relief. He denies any neurological symptoms such as leg pain, numbness, or tingling. He also denies chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, or nausea. He reports difficulty bending over.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the right lumbar spine, decreased flexion and extension of the back, and a positive straight leg raise on the right. Vital signs are stable, with no fever and blood pressure within normal limits. The lumbar spine x-ray demonstrates normal bony alignment without fracture. Laboratory results are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient has a lumbar strain.

Plan
Order an MRI to rule out other pathology. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily and ultram 50 mg every four hours as needed. Refer to physical therapy upon receipt of MRI results. Advise patient to discontinue ibuprofen and continue with tylenol if desired. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist.
