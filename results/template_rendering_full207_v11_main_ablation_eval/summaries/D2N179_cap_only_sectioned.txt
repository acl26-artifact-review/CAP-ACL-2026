Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and numbness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports decreased sensation in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand and the fourth and fifth fingers of the left hand. He also reports pain in the left hand and reports tingling. The patient describes the numbness and tingling as being constant and worsening with activity. He reports stiffness in the hands. The patient reports a neck contusion sustained during a car accident last December and has been wearing a splint since. He also reports a broken wrist during the healing of the contusion. He denies any recent trauma. He reports that acetaminophen provides partial relief.

Findings
The patient's reflexes are 1+ and symmetric. The patient's toes are downgoing bilaterally. Examination reveals no other acute findings.

Assessment
The patient is being evaluated for possible carpal tunnel syndrome and cubital tunnel syndrome. Nerve irritation is suspected.

Plan
A nerve test, called an EMG test, is scheduled. The doctor plans to schedule a nerve test. A referral to an orthopedist may be considered. The patient should continue wearing splints. A follow-up visit will be scheduled.
