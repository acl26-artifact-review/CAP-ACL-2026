Chief Complaint
Sudden onset of right back pain with associated nausea and sweating.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a sudden onset of right back pain approximately one week ago while working in the barn. Initially, he attributed the pain to moving hay, but it was accompanied by nausea and sweating. The pain radiated down to his groin, though not all the way. He has not experienced further pain since, but reports tenderness and inflammation on examination. He denies seeing blood in his urine and reports urinary straining. He has never had a kidney stone before, although his father has had them. The patient reports moving hay.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the right abdominal quadrant. Vital signs are stable; blood pressure 120/80 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm. No fever detected. The patient reports no fever or elevated temperature.

Assessment
The patient presents with a likely kidney stone, based on the reported symptoms and physical exam findings.

Plan
Prescribe oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours for pain. Order a BMP and urinalysis with urine culture. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks to assess stone passage. If the stone has not passed in 1-2 weeks, consider lithotripsy.
