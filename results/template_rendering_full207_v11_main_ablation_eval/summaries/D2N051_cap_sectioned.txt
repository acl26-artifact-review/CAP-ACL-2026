Chief Complaint
An ulcer on his right foot that's been there for a couple of weeks.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports an ulcer on his right foot that has been present for approximately six to eight weeks. He recalls stepping on a nail in the backyard several weeks ago. He occasionally experiences numbness in his feet, particularly in colder weather. He also reports tension headaches and reports that his sugars have been running higher than normal. The patient reports a smoking history of 20+ years and quit approximately four years ago. He denies chest pain or shortness of breath. He reports taking aspirin daily and insulin.

Findings
The patient's vital signs are stable with a blood pressure of 127/80 and a heart rate of 60. A physical exam reveals a two by three centimeter ulcerated lesion on the right lateral foot near the fifth metatarsophalangeal joint, with associated granulation tissue and slight purulent discharge. There is no palpable dorsalis pedis or posterior tibial pulse. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. The heart exam reveals a two out of six systolic ejection murmur at the left base. Neck exam is unremarkable. The patient's skin is warm and dry.

Assessment
The patient has a non-healing ulcer of the right foot, likely secondary to diabetes and vascular compromise. He also has a history of coronary artery disease and neuropathy.

Plan
An arterial ultrasound of the lower extremities is ordered to assess blood supply. A podiatry consult will be arranged to manage the ulcer. The patient will continue aspirin daily and insulin as prescribed. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. The cardiologist will be contacted for further management of his coronary artery disease.
