Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain exacerbated by pottery making.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports moderate, persistent pain in the inside of their right elbow, which is worsened by activities such as pottery making. The patient also reports pain radiating down their arm with wrist flexion and moderate tenderness at the medial epicondyle with wrist flexion and resisted pronation of the right forearm. The patient reports fatigue for the past two months. He denies blood in stool. He reports difficulty taking medications twice daily and enjoys baking.

Findings
The clinician suspects cervical radiculopathy. Physical exam reveals tenderness at the medial epicondyle with wrist flexion.

Assessment
The patient presents with symptoms consistent with lateral epicondylitis.

Plan
Order a sling for the patient's right arm. Order six hundred milligrams of ibuprofen every six hours. Order an A1C test. Order a referral to diabetic counseling. Increase metformin dosage to 1000mg twice daily. Order a cervical MRI.
