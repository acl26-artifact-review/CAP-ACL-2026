Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a skiing injury five days ago, sustained during a jump and landing. He describes a 'stretch' and swelling. He was able to get out of the water by himself but it was very sore to climb up and any kind of squatting or bending is really pretty sore. He took Aleve twice a day and applied a cold pack, which helped somewhat. He has no prior knee injuries. He is a frequent water skier and loves spring and chicken time of year. He is not a baseball fan, but his husband makes him watch the meds all the time and they lose all the time so he is always upset. He is taking lisinopril 20 milligrams a day and reports no side effects. He checks his blood pressure at home and it's been about one twenty five over eighty most of the days.

Findings
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis and edema in the right knee. Range of motion is decreased, with a negative varus and valgus test and a negative Lachman's test. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable. Vital signs: blood pressure 120/77 mmHg. The patient's lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally and his heart is a regular rate and rhythm with no murmurs. The patient has decreased range of motion there is a palpable dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulse there is otherwise no lower extremity edema.

Assessment
The patient's clinical presentation is most consistent with a medial collateral ligament strain.

Plan
The doctor orders an x-ray of the patient's right knee. The doctor prescribes meloxicam 15 milligrams once a day. The doctor recommends physical therapy. The doctor orders a lipid panel. The doctor orders a tetanus shot. The doctor continues lisinopril 20 milligrams a day.
