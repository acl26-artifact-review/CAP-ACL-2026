Chief Complaint
Right middle finger pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports four days of right middle finger pain following a motor vehicle accident. He states he made an angry gesture after the accident, including flipping someone the bird, and that he is now experiencing pain in the distal phalanx of his right middle finger. He is taking digoxin for atrial fibrillation and is interested in whether the tramadol will be compatible with this medication.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness over the distal phalanx of the right middle finger. An x-ray demonstrates a distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger.

Assessment
The patient has a distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger.

Plan
Apply a finger splint and schedule a follow-up x-ray in two weeks. Prescribe tramadol 50 mg every six hours as needed for pain for two days.
