Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right shoulder pain for the past month, exacerbated by overhead activities like painting. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with minimal relief. He describes the pain as a dull ache, with sharp spikes when lifting objects. He rates the pain as 6/10 at rest and 8/10 with exertion.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the anterior aspect of the right shoulder. Range of motion is limited, particularly abduction and external rotation. No signs of inflammation are noted. Strength is reduced in abduction and external rotation.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with a rotator cuff strain.

Plan
Prescribe a course of NSAIDs for pain management. Recommend physical therapy for strengthening and range of motion exercises. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress.
