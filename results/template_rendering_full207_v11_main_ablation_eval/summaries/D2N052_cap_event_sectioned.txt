Chief Complaint
Back pain on the right side

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing back pain on their right side for approximately one week. The pain has been localized to the right lower side and has slightly moved down. He describes the pain as constant. He took ibuprofen, which provided some relief, but the pain persists. He also reports experiencing some nausea but no vomiting. The patient's father had kidney stones and passed a few of them. He has never had this before. The patient reports experiencing some pain during the easter break and was able to participate in an adult easter egg hunt.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the abdomen, particularly near the flank on the right. Vital signs are within normal limits. A CT scan revealed a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter.

Assessment
The patient presents with a confirmed kidney stone in the proximal right ureter, with no evidence of obstruction.

Plan
I am prescribing ibuprofen 800 mg as needed for pain. I am also ordering a BMP and urinalysis to assess for infection. I will order a urine culture if indicated. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately one to two weeks to assess progress and discuss potential lithotripsy if symptoms persist.
