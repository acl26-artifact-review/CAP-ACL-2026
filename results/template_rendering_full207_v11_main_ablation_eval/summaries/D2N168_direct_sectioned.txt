Chief Complaint
Elevated blood pressure and associated symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent, elevated blood pressure readings, most recently noted in the emergency room following a walk. He reports these episodes approximately once a month, often coinciding with periods of travel when he does not routinely monitor his blood pressure. He takes lisinopril as prescribed, but admits to infrequently checking his blood pressure while traveling. He reports experiencing headaches and lightheadedness associated with these episodes. He is currently managing his depression with weekly therapy and reports a strong support system including his boyfriend, mother, and brother. He denies chest pain or shortness of breath.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a systolic ejection murmur of moderate intensity, blood pressure of 192/110 mmHg in the office, trace pitting edema to the lower extremities bilaterally, and no carotid bruits. An electrocardiogram demonstrates changes consistent with chronic hypertension. An echocardiogram from three months ago shows a slightly leaky heart valve and stable heart murmur.

Assessment
The patient presents with uncontrolled hypertension and a slightly leaky heart valve.

Plan
Increase lisinopril to 40 milligrams once daily. Instruct the patient to monitor blood pressure regularly and report any significant changes through the patient portal. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess blood pressure control and adjust medication as needed. Continue psychotherapy as currently engaged.
