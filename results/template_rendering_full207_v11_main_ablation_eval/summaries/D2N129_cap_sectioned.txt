Chief Complaint
Yearly exam and concerns about diabetes and high blood pressure.

History of Present Illness
Beverly is a 24-year-old female patient with a history of type 2 diabetes and high blood pressure. She presents for her yearly exam. She reports that her blood sugars have been up and down because of her diet, specifically due to starting a job at a bakery where she's been snacking a lot and eating more desserts than usual. She's been eating a lot more desserts than she normally would. She reports no missed medications and has been sleeping well. She denies any other concerning symptoms or pain. She reports no difficulty resisting temptation.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal blood pressure, normal heart sounds, clear lung sounds, equal grips, intact neurovascular exam, and no edema. The doctor noted a grade 2 systolic ejection fraction murmur. Hemoglobin A1c is elevated.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is type 2 diabetes, with elevated blood sugars. Her blood pressure is currently well-controlled.

Plan
The doctor wants the patient to check her blood sugars in the morning and call them in about two weeks. The doctor wants to see the patient again in about two months to follow up. The patient should continue taking metformin twice a day and lisinopril 10 mg once a day. The doctor recommends a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist.
