Chief Complaint
Reflux, nausea, and vomiting.

History of Present Illness
Anna is a 44-year-old female with a past medical history of arthritis, gout, and reflux who presents for follow-up of her chronic problems. She reports that her arthritis has been improving with pilates and weightlifting, leading to increased mobility and reduced joint pain. She had an episode of gout in her right first toe approximately two months ago, which was controlled with medication. She continues to take allopurinol. Regarding her reflux, she has been making dietary modifications but has continued to experience episodes, culminating in vomiting in the mornings. She reports this is a significant problem. She has also been experiencing some abdominal pain and bloating. She denies fever, chills, weight loss, or blood in her stool. Her last bowel movement was approximately two days ago and was normal. She is currently taking omeprazole as prescribed and has no issues with it.

Findings
Physical examination reveals no acute distress. Vital signs are stable. A physical exam reveals some tenderness to palpation in the right upper quadrant, but no rebound tenderness, guarding, or peritoneal signs. The right knee shows some effusion and slight pain to palpation and decreased range of motion. An autoimmune panel was unremarkable. A right knee x-ray demonstrates residual arthritis. Dragon shows the vital signs are normal.

Assessment
The patient's primary concerns are related to persistent reflux and associated nausea and vomiting. The right upper quadrant tenderness raises concern for possible gallbladder pathology, warranting further investigation.

Plan
I am ordering a right upper quadrant ultrasound to evaluate for gallstones. I will continue the patient on omeprazole 40 mg once daily and allopurinol 100 mg once daily. Please schedule a follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and discuss further imaging if needed. I will also recommend continuing with physical therapy for the right knee.
