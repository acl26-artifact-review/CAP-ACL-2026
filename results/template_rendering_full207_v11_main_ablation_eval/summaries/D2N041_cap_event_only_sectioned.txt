Chief Complaint
Left hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, which began approximately one week ago. He describes the pain as sharp and intermittent, and rates it as 6/10. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is worse with gripping and lifting objects. He denies any numbness or tingling. He is currently taking ibuprofen as needed for the pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the distal wrist and dorsal aspect of the hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No signs of inflammation or swelling are noted. Strength is intact.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Order carpal tunnel ultrasound. Recommend wrist splinting and ice packs. Advise patient to avoid activities that exacerbate symptoms. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
