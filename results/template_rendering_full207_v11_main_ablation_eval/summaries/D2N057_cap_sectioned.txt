Chief Complaint
Pain in the big toe.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his big toe for approximately two weeks, with increased pain last week during a trade show convention. He describes the pain as throbbing, occasionally sharp, especially with movement. He reports walking on his heel to avoid bending the toe. He takes two ibuprofen daily, but finds it doesn't help. He denies any recent injury and doesn't recall when it started. He reports feeling a bone spur and experiences pain when the sheets come down on the toe at night. He also reports pain when pushing on the big toe and the adjacent toe, and when moving the toe up and down. He has a history of riding bikes and using a regular pedal, and he has no problems with his stomach.

Findings
Examination reveals a large spur on the top of the big toe joint and cartilage loss. The patient reports feeling pain when pushing on the big toe and the adjacent toe, and when moving the toe up and down. The patient reports pain when the sheets come down on the toe at night.

Assessment
The patient has foot pain and joint pain, likely secondary to hallux rigidus. He has a large spur on the top of the big toe joint and cartilage loss.

Plan
A plan is to start with an orthotic insert in the shoe and provide meloxicam once daily. The patient should follow up in two weeks to assess improvement. If improvement is not seen, a shot or surgery may be considered. The patient will receive an x-ray. The patient will continue to work and test the orthotics and medicine. The patient should see the doctor back in two weeks.
