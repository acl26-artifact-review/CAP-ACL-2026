Chief Complaint
Patient reports a tick bite and associated symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a tick bite approximately one week ago, located around the right knee, which has been causing burning and annoyance. He denies fever, chills, or other systemic symptoms, such as body aches or cough. He reports no other joint pain. The patient reports a headache and generally feeling unwell since the tick bite. He reports taking metformin twice daily and has been monitoring his blood pressure at home. He denies prior lyme disease diagnosis. He reports taking lisinopril for hypertension.

Findings
Blood pressure is stable at approximately 120/77 mmHg. Heart rate is 67 bpm. Temperature is 98.4°F. Blood sugars are running below 1.2%. Physical exam reveals erythema and edema with fluctuance over the right patella, and evidence of a bull's-eye rash. Range of motion of the right knee is full. The patient reports no pain with knee flexion and extension. Examination reveals no murmur, rub, or gallop.

Assessment
The patient likely has lyme disease based on the presentation of a bull's-eye rash and right knee symptoms. He also continues to manage hypertension with lisinopril and requires monitoring of his diabetes with metformin.

Plan
I am starting the patient on doxycycline 100 mg twice daily for three weeks. I am also ordering a Lyme titer and Western blot to confirm the diagnosis. I am continuing the patient on metformin 1000 mg twice daily. I am ordering a hemoglobin A1c to assess long-term blood sugar control. I am ordering a basic metabolic panel to monitor kidney function. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and monitor for any complications.
