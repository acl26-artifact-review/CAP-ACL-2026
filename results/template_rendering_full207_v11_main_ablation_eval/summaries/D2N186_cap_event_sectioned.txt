Chief Complaint
Left back pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left back pain, exacerbated by bending over and prolonged standing. He describes the pain as sharp and radiating, particularly around the L5 level. He underwent a microdiscectomy in November 2020 due to a disk herniation that wrapped around his spinal thecal sac. He was heavily dosed on gabapentin, which he found difficult to withdraw from. He has been taking ibuprofen 800mg daily for the pain. He denies any recent trauma. He reports random pain on the right side as well.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal range of motion in dorsiflexion. Pain is elicited with palpation on the left side at L5. No pain with extension or palpation on the right side. The patient demonstrates normal weightbearing ability. An X-ray shows no fractures or abnormalities in the spinal bone at the surgical site.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with a muscle strain related to spinal misalignment, likely secondary to the previous surgery.

Plan
Prescribe physical therapy referral to the VA's physical therapy department. Prescribe meloxicam instead of ibuprofen for pain management. Refer to pain management for further evaluation and potential non-narcotic pain strategies.
