Chief Complaint
Discuss a breast reduction.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports considering a breast reduction for approximately six to seven years. She states that the decision has been 'back of her mind' throughout that time. She describes experiencing pain in her neck and upper back, attributing it to her breasts and prolonged desk work. The pain has been present for approximately five years. She reports indentations in her shoulders from bra straps and primarily wears a 34E sports bra due to comfort and cost. She has not had children and is considering starting a family in the future. She reports occasional alcohol consumption and is currently studying psychology. She has a family history of breast cancer, with her mother diagnosed at age 40. She has been losing approximately 10 pounds over the past few months and desires to lose an additional 20 pounds. She reports experiencing numbness in her hands and nipple pain occasionally. She also reports excessive sweating.

Findings
The patient is currently wearing a 34E sports bra. Examination reveals asymmetrical breasts with significant ptosis bilaterally. There is no nipple retraction or discharge. No breast tenderness or palpable axillary lymphadenopathy is present. Shoulder range of motion is limited. The patient reports pain with impingement testing.

Assessment
The patient presents with symptomatic macromastia and significant ptosis, indicating a strong need for a breast reduction.

Plan
Schedule a consultation to discuss surgical options and address patient concerns. Obtain imaging studies, including shoulder radiographs, to assess the extent of the ptosis and guide surgical planning. Refer to physical therapy for rehabilitation following surgery. Advise the patient on activity modifications and scar care. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and address any concerns.
