Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain following a fall during mountain biking.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing pain in his right ankle following a fall while mountain biking. He states he was doing something he didn't really want to be doing and fell, resulting in ankle pain. He describes hearing a crack and a crackle during the fall. The patient has experienced a fever, peaking at 200 degrees Fahrenheit. He has taken Tylenol for the pain, but it provided no relief. He has been mountain biking for approximately three years, though he does not recall his favorite place to go.

Findings
Physical examination reveals edema and erythema on the lateral malleolus of the right ankle, accompanied by ecchymosis and excoriations. Palpation of the right lateral malleolus elicits pain. Range of motion is limited, with decreased flexion and extension and internal and external rotation of the ankle. Vital signs include a temperature of 98.6°F, blood pressure of 118/77 mmHg, and a heart rate of 77 beats per minute. Radiographic examination reveals no acute fracture or bony abnormality, but demonstrates a soft tissue injury.

Assessment
Acute right ankle sprain.

Plan
The patient will receive an air cast and crutches. Meloxicam 15 mg daily has been prescribed. Ice the affected area for 20 minutes five times daily, as tolerated. Weight-bearing will be as tolerated. Follow up in two weeks if symptoms persist, or sooner if there is no improvement.
