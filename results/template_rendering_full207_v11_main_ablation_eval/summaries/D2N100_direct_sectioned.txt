Chief Complaint
Acne on the face.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports acne located on the face, which started approximately two years ago and is present most days. She has been using Persa-gel and washing regularly, which provides some improvement. She reports no associated symptoms such as itching, bleeding, or pain. She lives with her parents and sister and has a dog, bird, and bunnies. She is in 7th grade and plays basketball, volleyball, and tap. She uses sunscreen SPF 30 in the summer. The patient reports a flare-up when she started school sports in the fall due to sweating. She denies any worsening with her menstrual cycle and reports regular periods.

Findings
A physical exam revealed multiple benign nevi on the trunk and extremities. The skin was scattered with skin-colored papules, open and closed comedones, and erythrema papules primarily on the forehead and central cheeks and chin. The chest and back were relatively spared. The remainder of the physical exam was normal.

Assessment
Mild to moderate acne, primarily comedonal with a small inflammatory component.

Plan
I recommend starting with a topical therapy. Every morning, the patient will wash their face with a mild cleanser and apply a noncomedogenic moisturizer with SPF 30 or higher. In the evening, wash the face with the same cleanser and allow it to dry. Apply adapalene 0.1% cream to the areas of acne, starting with a few nights per week and gradually increasing to nightly. If the medication is expensive or not covered by insurance, the patient can try over-the-counter gels such as Clean & Clear Persa-gel. Follow with a noncomedogenic moisturizer. The patient should be aware that retinoids may cause sensitivity and dryness. Schedule a follow-up in six months to assess progress.
