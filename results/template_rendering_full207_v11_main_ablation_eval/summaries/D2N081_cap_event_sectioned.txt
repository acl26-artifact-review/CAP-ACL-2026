Chief Complaint
Follow-up for stage three non-small cell lung cancer and management of chemotherapy side effects.

History of Present Illness
Beverly is a 53-year-old female with a recent diagnosis of stage three non-small cell lung cancer. She presents for follow-up during neoadjuvant chemotherapy. She was diagnosed with a 4.4 centimeter left upper lobe nodule. Molecular testing is pending at this time. She reports minimal nausea and fatigue, but does experience some mild cough and sore throat. She has been able to tolerate the chemotherapy regimen of cisplatine and etoposide and has been able to maintain her support system. She reports no difficulty with swallowing and no edema in her lower extremities. Her radiation therapy is currently at 45 Gy.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no cervical or supraclavicular lymphadenopathy. Lung exam demonstrates bilateral crackles, suggestive of mild radiation pneumonitis. Chest x-ray confirms radiation pneumonitis. Skin exam reveals erythema on the anterior chest wall on the left side. Vitals are stable, with oxygen saturation maintained at adequate levels.

Assessment
1. Stage three non-small cell lung cancer. 2. Radiation pneumonitis secondary to radiation therapy.

Plan
Continue current chemotherapy regimen of cisplatine and etoposide. Continue radiation therapy at 45 Gy. Prescribe low-dose prednisone (40 mg daily for 5 days) for radiation pneumonitis. Prescribe lidocaine swish and swallow (four times daily) for dysphagia. Schedule follow-up imaging in four weeks to assess tumor response and monitor for complications. Schedule physical therapy consultation.
