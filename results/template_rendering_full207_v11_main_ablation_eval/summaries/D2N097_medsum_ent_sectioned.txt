Chief Complaint
Annual exam and discussion of current health status.

History of Present Illness
Elizabeth is a 66-year-old female with a past medical history of significant for depression and hypertension who presents for her annual exam. She reports feeling well overall, citing the positive impact of vaccinations on her ability to see her grandchildren and attend social events. She states she's been attending therapy once a week, primarily virtually, and feels that in-person sessions have been particularly helpful. She is currently taking lisinopril as prescribed and reports using a blood pressure cuff daily with readings appearing normal. She denies any current chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting. She does report some nasal congestion. She reports that her blood pressures have been running okay since her last visit. She expresses a desire to schedule a mammogram as part of her annual exam maintenance.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a slight 2/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is stable. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Trace lower extremity edema is noted bilaterally. An EKG performed by the nurse shows no abnormalities. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 132/78 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, and oxygen saturation 98% on room air.

Assessment
The patient's depression is currently well-managed with her current strategies. Her hypertension is controlled with lisinopril. No acute medical concerns are identified.

Plan
I will schedule a mammogram for Elizabeth. I recommend continuing lisinopril 20 milligrams daily. I advise Elizabeth to monitor her blood pressures and contact me if they rise. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess her progress and address any ongoing concerns. I will also provide instructions on setting an alarm to remind her to take her medication.
