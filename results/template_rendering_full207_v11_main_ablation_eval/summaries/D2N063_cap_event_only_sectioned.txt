Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent ache in his left shoulder for the past six weeks. The pain has worsened with overhead activities and when lying on his affected side. He denies any recent injuries or trauma. He has tried acetaminophen for relief, but it only provides partial improvement. He describes the pain as a dull ache, with occasional sharp spikes. He rates the pain as a 6 out of 10 on a scale of 1 to 10, with 1 being no pain and 10 being the worst pain imaginable. He states he has difficulty reaching overhead and performing tasks like changing light bulbs. He denies any numbness or tingling in his arm or hand.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the anterior shoulder, with limited active abduction (60 degrees) and pain with resisted external rotation. Range of motion is reduced, particularly in external rotation. Neurological exam is intact, with full strength and sensation in the affected extremity. Vitals are stable. No acute findings on examination.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a rotator cuff tendinopathy, likely involving the supraspinatus tendon. The pain and limited range of motion are concerning for a partial tear or significant inflammation.

Plan
I am ordering shoulder radiographs to evaluate for any signs of rotator cuff tear or other structural abnormalities. I am also prescribing physical therapy to address the pain and improve range of motion. We will start with a low-impact program and gradually increase the intensity as tolerated. I am also prescribing naproxen 500mg twice daily for pain management. Follow up in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
