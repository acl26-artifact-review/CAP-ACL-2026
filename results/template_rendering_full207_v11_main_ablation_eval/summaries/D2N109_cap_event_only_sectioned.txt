Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent left shoulder pain for the past six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies any recent trauma. He reports using acetaminophen with partial relief. He states, 'It's a dull ache, mostly, but it gets sharper when I lift things.'

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and positive impingement signs. The patient's range of motion is reduced, and he reports pain with specific movements. Vitals are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are most consistent with rotator cuff tendinitis.

Plan
I'm ordering shoulder radiographs to evaluate for possible rotator cuff pathology. I'm also recommending physical therapy to address the weakness and improve range of motion. We'll continue the acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Let's schedule a follow-up in four weeks to assess progress.
