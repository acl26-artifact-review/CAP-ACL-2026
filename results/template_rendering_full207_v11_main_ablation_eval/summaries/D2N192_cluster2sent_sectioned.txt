Chief Complaint
Abdominal pain with right upper quadrant tenderness.

History of Present Illness
Karen reports abdominal pain started approximately ten days ago as mild discomfort in the right upper quadrant, which has progressively worsened over the past few days. She describes the pain as a 6/10 on a pain scale. She has experienced some nausea and mild heartburn, but no vomiting. She reports feeling fatigued and less hungry over the past week. She typically manages migraines with over-the-counter analgesics and uses sumatriptan as needed. She has a history of chronic migraines and hypertension. She denies recent fever or chills, and reports no recent travel or exposures. She has been taking excedrin for migraines and sumatriptan as needed.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is slightly elevated today. A grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur is heard. Physical exam reveals mild to moderate tenderness in the right upper quadrant, with an equivocal Murphy's sign. The remainder of the abdominal exam is unremarkable. Cardiovascular exam is normal with a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
Karen presents with acute abdominal pain, likely secondary to right upper quadrant tenderness, raising concern for possible gallstones or gallbladder infection.

Plan
I have ordered a CBC, comprehensive metabolic panel, urinalysis, and a right upper quadrant ultrasound. I will also order a repeat sumatriptan prescription. I will continue the patient on lisinopril 20 mg daily and will send a refill to the pharmacy. I will monitor blood pressure at home. I will keep the patient for observation and further evaluation based on the test results. If indicated, I will arrange for transfer to the emergency department.
