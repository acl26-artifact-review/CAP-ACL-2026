He reports pain with bending over. He has been taking ibuprofen and acetaminophen, with some relief. He denies nausea, vomiting, or other systemic symptoms. He reports no problems with bladder or bowel function.

O:
The patient's vital signs are stable. Heart sounds are regular and clear. Lung sounds are clear to auscultation. Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the right lumbar spine, decreased flexion and extension of the back, and a positive straight leg raise on the right. The lumbar x-ray shows no fracture. Lab results are unremarkable.

A:
The patient's presentation is consistent with a lumbar strain, likely exacerbated by his prior discectomy. There is no evidence of acute nerve compression or other serious pathology at this time.

P:
I am ordering an MRI of the lumbar spine to rule out nerve compression. I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain management and ultram 50 mg every four hours as needed for breakthrough pain. I am recommending physical therapy following the MRI results. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress.
