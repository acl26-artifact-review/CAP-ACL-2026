Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a basketball injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he sustained a knee injury seven days ago while playing basketball when he fell to the ground and was subsequently pushed by another player. He describes pain on the inside of his knee and reports swelling. He reports using ice and an ace wrap for the injury. He is currently taking meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. He checks his blood sugars every morning at home. He is currently diabetic and takes metformin 500mg twice daily.

Findings
Examination reveals right knee swelling and effusion. Palpation elicits tenderness on the medial aspect of the knee. Range of motion is decreased. The negative varus and valgus stress test was noted. X-ray shows no fractures, but demonstrates right knee effusion.

Assessment
The patient has a medial collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
The doctor will schedule a follow-up appointment in seven days to re-evaluate the patient's condition. The doctor will order a CT scan or MRI of the knee if needed. The doctor will discharge the patient. The doctor will prescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. The doctor will order a knee brace for a few days. The doctor will recommend physical therapy to strengthen the muscles around the right knee. The patient should continue exercising with the brace and monitor blood sugars daily.
