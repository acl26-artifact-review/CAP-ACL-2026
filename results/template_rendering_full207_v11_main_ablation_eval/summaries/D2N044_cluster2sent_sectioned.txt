Chief Complaint
Right knee pain for approximately six months.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a deep, achy pain in his right knee, exacerbated by activities such as standing and going up stairs. He describes the pain as worsening with prolonged sitting and reports difficulty with running, having reduced his mileage to approximately half a mile due to the pain. The patient reports that ibuprofen and acetaminophen provide some relief, but rest is often required. He denies any recent injury or bone fracture.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal gait and muscle strength is good with a three out of five for abduction bilaterally. Palpation elicits tenderness. The Lachman, anterior and posterior drawer, and McMurray tests are negative bilaterally. Neurological exam is intact. X-rays demonstrate no fractures or osteoarthritis. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and examination, the findings are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome.

Plan
Recommend physical therapy to address lower extremity strength, mobility, and running mechanics. Prescribe ibuprofen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
