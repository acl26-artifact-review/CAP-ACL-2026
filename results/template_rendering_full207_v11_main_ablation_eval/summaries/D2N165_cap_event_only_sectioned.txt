Chief Complaint
Left knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left knee pain for approximately six months, initially following a twisting injury during recreational hiking. He describes the pain as deep and aching, exacerbated by stairs and prolonged walking. He reports some improvement with rest and ice, but the pain persists. He denies any recent trauma. He reports that the pain is worse when getting from a seated position to standing. He reports pain when going up and down stairs. The patient reports knee pain. The patient reports pain after watching TV. He denies any swelling or instability.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the left knee. Range of motion is limited by pain. The patient has normal gait. The doctor heard a regular heart rate and rhythm with no murmurs.

Assessment
The patient has patellofemoral pain syndrome.

Plan
The patient will receive physical therapy. The patient will take ibuprofen as needed. Arrange follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
