Chief Complaint
Low back pain following a lifting incident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports low back pain that began approximately 48 hours ago after lifting heavy boxes while assisting his daughter. He describes a sudden 'pop' sensation and immediate pain. He initially attempted to rest, but the pain has persisted. He has used over-the-counter ibuprofen and ice packs with minimal relief. He denies any recent trauma. He reports some tingling in his toes on the right foot approximately Sunday afternoon, which resolved. He has been eating less regularly due to being on the go and has not had a home-cooked meal in weeks. He is currently taking metformin 1000mg twice daily and lisinopril 10mg daily.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation of the lumbar spine with pain reproduced with flexion and extension. Straight leg raise is negative. Blood pressure is slightly elevated in the office. A grade 2/6 systolic heart murmur is noted. The x-ray demonstrates normal bony alignment and no fracture. Hemoglobin A1c is elevated at 8%.

Assessment
Lumbar strain secondary to mechanical injury. Poorly controlled diabetes mellitus. Mild hypertension.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain. Continue ice application. Refer to physical therapy. Increase metformin to 1000 mg twice daily. Increase lisinopril to 20 mg daily. Order a hemoglobin A1c repeat in 6 months. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 4 weeks if symptoms persist. The nurse will assist with medication reconciliation and discharge instructions.
