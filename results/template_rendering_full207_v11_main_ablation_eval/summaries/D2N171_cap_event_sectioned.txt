Chief Complaint
Recurrent chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling a burning sensation in their chest, particularly when walking. This chest pain is not severe and typically subsides with rest. He has been experiencing this chest pain approximately three times a week over the past month. He denies any nausea, vomiting, or shortness of breath. He reports nasal congestion due to allergies.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slight 3/6 systolic ejection murmur. The patient's blood pressure is well-controlled and oxygenation is adequate. No carotid bruits or lower extremity edema are noted. The echocardiogram shows a slightly reduced ejection fraction of 45% and mild mitral regurgitation.

Assessment
The patient has known coronary artery disease and recurrent chest pain. The patient's history of coronary artery disease and the reported chest pain warrant further investigation.

Plan
Order a cardiac stress test. Order a lipid panel and initiate Crestor 20mg daily. Continue aspirin and toprol. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
