Chief Complaint
Neck pain and stiffness

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent, dull ache in his neck, exacerbated by prolonged computer use and stress. He describes the pain as a constant, low-level ache, with occasional sharp shooting pains. He also reports stiffness, particularly in the mornings, making it difficult to turn his head. He denies any recent trauma or injury. He reports occasional headaches, primarily tension-type, and reports fatigue. He mentions a car crash approximately six months ago, but reports no current symptoms related to it. He reports numbness in his left hand.

Findings
Physical exam reveals limited range of motion in the neck, particularly flexion and rotation. Palpation reveals mild tenderness over the cervical paravertebral muscles. Reflexes are intact. Neurological exam reveals intact sensation in the upper extremities, with no focal deficits. Blood pressure 130/80, pulse 72, temperature 98.6.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with cervical strain and possible myofascial pain syndrome. The reported numbness in the left hand is concerning and requires further investigation.

Plan
Prescribe muscle relaxants for short-term use. Recommend physical therapy for range of motion and strengthening exercises. Advise on ergonomic workstation setup. Order neurological exam to rule out peripheral neuropathy. Follow up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
