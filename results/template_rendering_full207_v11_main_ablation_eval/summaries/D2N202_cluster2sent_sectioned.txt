Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right shoulder pain that began approximately three weeks ago following remodeling activities. He initially iced the arm and took Advil. He has experienced intermittent shoulder pain, rated 3/10 without medication, which improves with acetaminophen, reaching a pain level of 2/10. He denies prior shoulder pain or numbness/tingling.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the right shoulder, tenderness to the greater tuberosity, brisk capillary refill, and equal and bilateral sensation. Right shoulder x-ray demonstrates no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order an MRI of the right shoulder to evaluate for additional pathology. Refer the patient to physical therapy for a six to eight week course. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief.
