S:
The patient reports a dull headache starting approximately one week ago, accompanied by flushing in the ears and occasional dizziness. He describes the headache as relieved by over-the-counter analgesics, but it recurs. He reports experiencing tightness in his neck and shoulders, attributing this to stress. He denies fever or visual disturbances, and states this is not the worst headache he has experienced. He reports a gradual onset of the headaches coinciding with increased stress levels. He has no history of head trauma.

O:
Physical exam reveals unremarkable extraocular movements, funduscopic exam shows no papilledema, and heart sounds are normal with a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur. Cervical and trapezius muscle examination reveals mild tenderness and tightness. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's headache is most likely a tension-type headache, likely related to musculoskeletal tension and stress.

P:
Rescribe flexeril 5 mg three times daily for muscle relaxation. Order a hemoglobin A1c and a comprehensive metabolic panel. Continue metformin 500 mg twice daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess headache response. Advise patient to call sooner if headaches worsen or become more severe.
