Chief Complaint
Chronic lumbar back pain with radiculopathy.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing chronic lumbar back pain for several weeks, exacerbated by flexion and extension. He describes occasional numbness in his toes and reports a past episode of urinary incontinence approximately two years ago. He recalls taking a white pill prior to this visit, but is unsure of its specific name. He reports weight gain and a family history of scoliosis in a fourth cousin.

Findings
Physical examination reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with right paravertebral tenderness upon palpation. Straight leg test is positive at the right lateral thigh dermatome. Neurological examination demonstrates decreased sensation to light touch at L4 dermatome. Vital signs are stable. MRI findings demonstrate disk desiccation, disk bulge with paracentral disk herniation resulting in moderate right for neural foraminal stenosis.

Assessment
Based on the patient's reported symptoms, physical examination findings, and MRI results, the patient's primary problem is a herniated disk at L4-L5 with radiculopathy.

Plan
I recommend a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and provide symptomatic relief. I will prescribe a strong anti-inflammatory medication to be taken three hours prior to the injection. Follow up in one week to assess response to the injection, and if pain persists, we will consider pain medication or surgical consultation.
