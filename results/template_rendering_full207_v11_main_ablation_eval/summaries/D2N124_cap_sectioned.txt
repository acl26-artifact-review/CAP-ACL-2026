Chief Complaint
Ankle injury following a basketball injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he injured his ankle while playing basketball. He describes landing awkwardly and twisting his ankle, resulting in immediate pain and swelling. He reports difficulty walking and instability in the ankle. He has been trying to maintain his regular workout routine, but the injury has disrupted his progress. He reports that the pain is exacerbated by weight-bearing and is currently rated as 6/10. He also mentions volunteering to coach a youth basketball team starting in April.

Findings
Physical examination reveals significant swelling and tenderness to palpation over the lateral aspect of the ankle. Dorsiflexion is limited due to pain. The ankle demonstrates decreased stability with the talar tilt test. Capillary refill is normal. An X-ray confirms a displaced lateral malleolar fracture at the Weber C level, with no evidence of medial or posterior malleolar fractures.

Assessment
The patient presents with a lateral malleolar fracture, resulting in pain, instability, and difficulty with ambulation.

Plan
The doctor recommends surgical intervention to stabilize the fracture with a plate and screws. He recommends crutches for mobility and advises the patient to ice and elevate the ankle. He prescribes meloxicam for pain management. Follow-up will be scheduled in approximately three months to assess healing and re-evaluate for return to activity, including coaching. The patient should continue coaching and we will schedule a follow-up appointment to discuss his return to exercise.
