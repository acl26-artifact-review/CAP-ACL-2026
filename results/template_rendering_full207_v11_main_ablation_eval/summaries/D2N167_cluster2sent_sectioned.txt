Chief Complaint
Left knee pain following a twisting injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a left knee injury approximately one week ago following a fall and twisting motion. He has experienced persistent left knee pain, rated 6/10, and reports limping. He has taken acetaminophen with limited relief and is familiar with kinesiology tape. He was advised against taking ibuprofen due to liver concerns. The patient suspects an MCL strain based on the mechanism of injury.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate edema and effusion in the left knee, with a range of motion of approximately 85%. Pain is elicited with flexion and extension. Lachman's and valgus/varus tests are negative. The left knee x-ray demonstrates no fractures or bony abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient likely has a medial collateral ligament strain of the left knee.

Plan
Prescribe tramadol 50 mg twice daily for breakthrough pain, along with as needed acetaminophen. Order a knee immobilizer for one week to restrict movement and reduce inflammation. Recommend icing the knee three to four times daily for 20-30 minutes. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to assess healing and adjust treatment as needed.
