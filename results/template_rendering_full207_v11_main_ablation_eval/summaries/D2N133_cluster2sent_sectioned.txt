Chief Complaint
The patient reports increasing knee pain, particularly with patellar inhibition.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of chronic back pain, which has been well-controlled with medication and therapy. He also reports a history of depression, currently managed with medication and increased outdoor activity. He describes sudden onset of significant knee pain, primarily associated with swimming and exacerbated by patellar inhibition. There were no prior injuries. He reports some mild pain with knee range of motion. He has had a previous carpal tunnel release performed a few years ago. He reports no leg pain or neurological symptoms.

Findings
Examination reveals normal knee range of motion without significant swelling. The patient's heart rhythm is regular, lungs are clear, and there is no palpable adenopathy. Grip strength is equal bilaterally. X-ray of the right knee is normal.

Assessment
The patient has chondromalacia of the patella.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 600 milligrams four times daily with food and instruct the patient to perform straight leg raises to strengthen the quadriceps. Continue current antidepressant medication and schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
