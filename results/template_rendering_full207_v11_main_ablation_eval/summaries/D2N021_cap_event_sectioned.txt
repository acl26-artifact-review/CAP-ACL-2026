Chief Complaint
Christine Hernandez is here today to discuss her concerns regarding weight gain, acne, and difficulty affording her medications.

History of Present Illness
Christine reports experiencing persistent weight gain over the past year, which she attributes to her previous birth control medication. She has been struggling with acne, particularly on her lower face and chest. She reports difficulty affording her current medications, specifically the unithroid and the clindamycin gel, due to the high cost. She has been taking her old thyroid medication until recently, and has been unable to afford the new one. She also mentions that her cousin suggested amino acids for her workout, and that she has been trying to get better. She has been trying to get better. She also reports experiencing rashes, and that she has n't been able to take the gel because of the cost. She also reports experiencing constipation.

Findings
Examination reveals no acute abnormalities. Vital signs are within normal limits. The patient reports swelling in her feet.

Assessment
The patient presents with weight gain, acne, and a history of thyroid issues. Further investigation is warranted to determine the underlying causes and appropriate management strategies.

Plan
Prescribe a low-dose unithroid to manage the thyroid condition. Prescribe benzoyl peroxide and clindamycin gel for acne. Order a comprehensive metabolic panel to evaluate liver function and rule out other potential causes of weight gain. Refer to physical therapy for musculoskeletal support. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed. Provide information on affordable medication options and resources.
