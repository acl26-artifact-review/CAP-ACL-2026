Chief Complaint
Pain in the top part of their right leg after bowling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the top part of their right leg following a bowling incident. He describes bowling, running up to the lane, and then hitting the ball into his leg instead of the lane. He fell as a result. He did not notice any swelling at the time, but reports some bruising on the back of his left hand. He reports difficulty walking and avoids stairs. He has a history of atopic eczema and takes fluocinonide when needed. He previously underwent a colectomy due to diverticulitis.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to the lateral aspect of the right upper leg. Lifting the leg does not elicit pain. X-rays demonstrate a normal femur with no fracture or swelling.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a contusion.

Plan
I am going to start the patient on mobic 15 milligrams once a day. I recommend the patient use ice for the pain. Please schedule follow-up in one week if symptoms are not improving.
