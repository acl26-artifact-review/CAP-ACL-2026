Chief Complaint
Anxiety regarding recent hepatitis C blood work results.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being anxious about a recent blood work result indicating hepatitis C. He states he has never been told he has hepatitis C in the past. He reports a history of occasional heavy alcohol and IV drug use in the past fifteen years, but is currently clean. He admits to drinking about a beer a day and occasionally more on weekends. He also smokes, down to one to two cigarettes a day. He reports feeling tired at the end of the day, particularly after work, and attributes this to work demands. He has hypertension, diabetes, and depression in his family history. He has been working a lot lately and feels like he's not getting enough time with his family.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no distress or jaundice. Heart exam shows a regular rate and rhythm with a two out of six systolic murmur. Lung exam is clear with no wheezes or rales. Abdomen is soft with normal bowel sounds. Musculoskeletal exam is unremarkable. Recent blood work shows a positive hepatitis C antibody test and an elevated AST of 39, with normal ALT, albumin, and bilirubin levels. Vitals are stable.

Assessment
The patient's recent blood work results are consistent with a diagnosis of hepatitis C. Further investigation is needed to determine the severity of liver disease and guide treatment.

Plan
I will order additional blood work, including HCV RNA and HCV genotype testing to confirm the diagnosis. I will also order a liver ultrasound to assess for fibrosis. I recommend scheduling a follow-up appointment in three weeks to discuss the results and develop a treatment plan. I will also advise the patient to discuss screening for his family members.
