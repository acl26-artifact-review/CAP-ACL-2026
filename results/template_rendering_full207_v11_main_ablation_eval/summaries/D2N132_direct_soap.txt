The patient reports a back injury following a fall during a surprise birthday party, where he was crunched behind others while hiding. He describes the injury as occurring when he jumped up and possibly twisted during the event. He reports pain with palpation when lying flat and with twisting motions, but denies pain or numbness radiating down his legs. He reports using ibuprofen for the pain. He also takes medication for anxiety.

O:
The patient's examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the lumbar region with movement. Range of motion is limited by pain. Neurological examination is intact, with no reported sensory deficits or reflexes. X-ray reveals a healed lumbar fusion at L4, with no acute fractures or compression fractures. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's presentation is consistent with a lumbar strain secondary to trauma. There is no evidence of nerve compression or radiculopathy.

P:
Continue ibuprofen 800 mg three times daily with food. Apply ice and heat alternating to the lumbar region. Encourage ambulation. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week if symptoms do not improve or worsen. Order the prescribed medications and procedures.
