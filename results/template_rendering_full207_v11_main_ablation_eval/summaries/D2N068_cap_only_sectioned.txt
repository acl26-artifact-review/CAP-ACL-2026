Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand that started approximately two weeks ago and has been gradually worsening. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, and it is exacerbated by movement. The patient also reports feeling short of breath, particularly with exertion, and reports feeling 'out of sorts.' He denies chest pain or palpitations. He reports fatigue and lightheadedness. He has a history of congestive heart failure and reports feeling 'out of it.' He has struggled with healthy food intake over the past year. He reports seasonal changes. He has had heart failure for several years. The patient denies nausea or diarrhea.

Findings
Physical exam reveals jugular venous distention to approximately 8 centimeters and 1+ pitting edema in the lower extremities. A grade 3 out of 6 systolic ejection murmur is present. Fine crackles are present in the lungs. The doctor will perform a quick physical exam.

Assessment
The patient has congestive heart failure, fluid retention, and presents with left hand pain and shortness of breath. The echocardiogram shows reduced heart pumping function and a leaky mitral valve.

Plan
The doctor orders an echocardiogram. The patient will increase the lasix dose to 80 mg once a day. The doctor instructs the patient to monitor blood pressure at home and report elevated readings. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
