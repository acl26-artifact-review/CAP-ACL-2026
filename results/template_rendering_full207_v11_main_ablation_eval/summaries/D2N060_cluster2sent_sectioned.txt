Chief Complaint
Right foot pain following a soccer injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right foot pain following a fall during a soccer game, sustained yesterday while attempting to steal the ball. He has a history of soccer-related injuries but none previously affecting this foot. Since the injury, he has elevated his foot, applied ice, wrapped it with a trainer, and taken ibuprofen. Without ibuprofen, he rates his pain as a 3. He reports no numbness or tingling in the left foot.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bruising and swelling on the left foot, tenderness to palpation, and a positive ankle-plantarflexion test. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds dorsalis pedis. Motor and sensory function are intact. Radiographic imaging reveals a subtle dorsal displacement of the base of the second metatarsal with a 3 millimeter separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and the presence of a bony fragment in the Lisfranc joint space. Vitals are stable, with blood pressure 118/72, heart rate 58, respiratory rate 14, and oxygen saturation 99% on room air.

Assessment
The patient has a Lisfranc fracture, involving the second metatarsal bone at the top of the foot, with ligamentous injury. This is likely to require surgical intervention to restore bone alignment and stabilize the joint.

Plan
An MRI will be ordered to assess ligamentous injury. The patient will likely require surgical fixation of the Lisfranc fracture with plates and screws. A surgical consult will be arranged. The patient will be placed in a cast and instructed to use crutches for six to eight weeks. Weight-bearing will be progressed gradually based on tolerance. Follow-up will be scheduled in one week to assess healing and adjust the plan. The patient will be advised that this is a day-surgery procedure.
