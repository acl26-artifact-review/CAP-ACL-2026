Chief Complaint
Follow-up after anterior stemi and LAD stent placement.

History of Present Illness
Russell Ramirez is a 45-year-old male with a history of coronary artery disease, status post prior RCA stent placement in 2018, hypertension, and diabetes mellitus. He presents for follow-up after an anterior stemi and LAD stent placement. He reports feeling tired occasionally but overall feels okay. He has been able to enjoy some spring weather and is hoping to get back to walking the trails at the Rex Center and taking nature pictures. He reports no chest pain or shortness of breath and denies leg swelling. He has been taking his medications as prescribed and reports they have been helping. He typically sleeps with two pillows. He enjoys gardening and photography. He is currently taking aspirin 81 milligrams daily, brilinta 90 milligrams twice daily, atorvastatin 80 milligrams daily, toprol 50 milligrams daily, and aldactone 12.5 milligrams daily.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a 3/6 systolic ejection murmur heard at the left base, consistent with his previous stent. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Extremities are warm and dry, with no edema. Radial pulse is palpable and strong. EKG shows normal sinus rhythm with expected changes post-MI.

Assessment
1. Coronary artery disease. 2. Newly reduced left ventricular dysfunction. 3. Hypertension. 4. Diabetes mellitus.

Plan
1. Continue aspirin 81 milligrams daily and brilinta 90 milligrams twice daily. 2. Continue atorvastatin 80 milligrams daily. 3. Initiate toprol 50 milligrams daily. 4. Order aldactone 12.5 milligrams daily. 5. Refer to cardiac rehabilitation for education and support. 6. Schedule a follow-up echocardiogram in approximately two months.
