Chief Complaint
Patient reports positive lab work for hepatitis C and seeks evaluation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being surprised by a positive hepatitis C diagnosis, as he has not experienced any symptoms. He asks if any previous doctors have ever informed him of the diagnosis. He mentions a history of occasional intravenous drug use in the past, but has been clean for over fifteen years. The patient also inquired about a family history of alcohol use and smoking. He reports current daily alcohol consumption and a history of smoking one to two cigarettes per day. He is also concerned about potential risks to his family.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no signs of distress or jaundice. Vital signs are within normal limits. Lung exam is clear. Abdominal exam is unremarkable. The patient's muscle exam is normal. Lab results show an elevated AST at 39, with other liver panel values within normal limits. Hepatitis C antibody test was positive.

Assessment
The patient's recent lab results confirm a diagnosis of hepatitis C infection. Further investigation is needed to determine the viral genotype and assess liver fibrosis.

Plan
I will order additional blood work, including hepatitis C RNA and HCV genotype testing, to determine the severity of the infection. I will also order an ultrasound elastography to assess liver fibrosis. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to discuss the results and determine the appropriate treatment plan. I will also assist the patient in setting up appointments with his family physician.
