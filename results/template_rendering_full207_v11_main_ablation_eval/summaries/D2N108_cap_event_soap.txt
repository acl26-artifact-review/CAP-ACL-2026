O:
The physical exam reveals a one by two inch circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot, proximal to the fifth metatarsal head. The wound is red and has drainage. There is some edema around the wound. No necrosis or odor is present. There is no bony exposure. Vital signs are normal.

A:
The patient has a diabetic foot ulcer with signs of infection.

P:
The doctor ordered an ABI to assess blood supply, a debridement, and clindamycin 400mg twice daily for seven days. The doctor ordered a surgical shoe to protect the foot during the healing process. The doctor recommended follow-up in two weeks to assess healing.
