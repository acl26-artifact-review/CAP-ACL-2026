Chief Complaint
Karen Nelson reports a new right eye twitch.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a new right eye twitch observed approximately two weeks prior, as noted by her pediatrician. The father reports the twitch occurs intermittently and denies associated pain, seizures, or other neurological symptoms. There is no family history of seizures or Tourette's syndrome. The patient denies fever, chills, cough, headache, or sleep disturbances. The father states he has not observed any other symptoms.

Findings
Karen Nelson is a 3-year-old female with no significant past medical history. Physical exam reveals normal reflexes (bilateral upper and lower extremities), intact sensation, and no pronator drift. Gait is steady and heel-toe walking is normal.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with transient tic, likely benign and not impacting her daily function.

Plan
Observe the tic for worsening and impact on school and activities. Advise parents to avoid drawing attention to the tic. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if the tic worsens or if there are any changes in her neurological status.
