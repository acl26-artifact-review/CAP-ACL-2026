Chief Complaint
Patient presents with chest pain and shortness of breath following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports falling while walking outside, resulting in pain in his chest and shortness of breath. He initially dismissed the chest pain, attributing it to heartburn, but then experienced significant shortness of breath. He denies any recent trauma or loss of consciousness. He reports eating a lot of chips and salty foods recently. He has been taking his medications as prescribed, though he admits to missing a few doses due to forgetting. He has been sleeping well and reports no recent chest pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals blood pressure of 128/72 mmHg, heart rate 78 bpm, and respiratory rate 18 breaths per minute. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Jugular venous distension is not present. A two out of six systolic ejection murmur is heard. Lower extremities show trace edema. Echocardiogram reveals preserved ejection fraction of 55% and mild to moderate mitral regurgitation. Weight is not currently recorded.

Assessment
The patient presents with chest pain and shortness of breath, likely secondary to musculoskeletal injury and exacerbated by underlying hypertension and mild mitral regurgitation. His recent dietary habits are contributing to his hypertension.

Plan
Continue bumex 2 mg daily, cozaar 100 mg daily, and norvasc 5 mg daily. Order a renal artery ultrasound to rule out other causes of chest pain. Schedule a consultation with a nutritionist to address dietary concerns. Instruct the patient to weigh himself daily and call if he gains three pounds in two days. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
