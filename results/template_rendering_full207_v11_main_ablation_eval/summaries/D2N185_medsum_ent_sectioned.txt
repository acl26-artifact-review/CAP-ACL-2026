Chief Complaint
Ashley James presents for evaluation of abnormal renal ultrasound with an atrophic right kidney and reports persistent abdominal pain and burning during urination.

History of Present Illness
Ashley James is a 14-year-old female who was previously healthy and presents for evaluation of an abnormal renal ultrasound revealing an atrophic right kidney. The patient reported feeling sick out of nowhere one day, experiencing stomach pain and bending over in pain. She initially thought it was an appendix issue. She was treated for a stomach virus and sent home, but the next day she was twice as bad as the first day. She was taken to her doctor, who performed an ultrasound and found that one kidney was bigger. Subsequently, she experienced cysts on her kidney and ovaries, along with swollen lymph nodes and infection. She followed up with her doctor, but he was out of town, so she went to Atlanta where she was treated. After treatment, she reported feeling better, but still complains of pain and burning, especially when eating. She takes pepcid and claritin daily. She has a family history of kidney disease, with her father having a kidney transplant and her great aunt also having kidney disease. Her grandfather had obstructive neuropathy and died of a massive heart attack at 47. She also had breast cancer.

Findings
Physical exam reveals the patient to be well-appearing and alert. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are regular. Blood pressure is within normal limits. No edema is noted. Laboratory findings are pending.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is concerning for a complex renal issue, including an atrophic right kidney, potential infection, and possible cysts. Further investigation is warranted to determine the etiology and guide management.

Plan
Order renal ultrasound follow-up in one month. Recommend a urinalysis to assess for protein and blood. Continue current medications (pepcid and claritin). Advise the patient to stay hydrated and avoid carbonated beverages. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and further investigate the renal abnormalities. Refer to a nephrologist for ongoing management.
