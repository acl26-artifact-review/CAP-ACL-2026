Chief Complaint
Back pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain, which he attributes to a prior lumbar discectomy. He has been taking ibuprofen for pain relief and previously tried ibuprofen and acetaminophen together, finding some benefit. He denies chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting. He reports a positive straight leg raise on the right. He states he does not believe there is anything wrong with the area where he had surgery previously.

Findings
The patient is a 55-year-old male with a history of prior discectomy. Vital signs are stable, with normal blood pressure and heart sounds. Physical examination reveals pain to palpation of the right lumbar spine, decreased flexion and extension of the back, and a positive straight leg raise on the right. X-rays of the lumbar spine do not show fracture. Laboratory results are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a lumbar strain.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily and ultram 50 milligrams every four hours as needed. I am ordering an MRI to evaluate the lumbar spine. We will refer the patient to physical therapy once the MRI results are available. Continue ibuprofen and acetaminophen as needed.
