S:
The patient reports right shoulder pain that began three weeks ago during remodeling, following a fall while painting. He describes the pain as intermittent, rated 6/10 without medication, and decreases to 4/10 with ibuprofen. He initially experienced numbness in the arm but no longer does. He denies chest pain, dyspnea, or syncope.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the right shoulder with tenderness to the greater tuberosity. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds. Neurological exam shows brisk response to sensation and touch. Right shoulder x-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

P:
Order an MRI of the right shoulder. Refer to physical therapy (6-8 weeks). Recommend continued ibuprofen as tolerated. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to evaluate progress.
