Chief Complaint
Tick bite and associated symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports having a tick bite around their knee that has been present for over a week. He describes the area as burning and annoying. He denies fever or chills. He reports typical arthritic pain in other joints. He states he was hunting a couple of weeks ago and that hunting season is open. He reports taking metformin twice a day and checking his blood sugars periodically. His blood sugars are running below 150. He is taking lisinopril 20mg once daily and reports no side effects. He reports generally feeling unwell. He denies cough or shortness of breath.

Findings
Physical exam reveals erythema and edema over the right patella, with fluctuance. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 120/70 mmHg, heart rate 67 bpm, temperature 98.4°F. The patient's knee is not significantly limited in range of motion. Skin examination reveals a bull's-eye rash over the right knee. The patient's lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. The patient's heart rhythm is regular and without murmurs.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is concerning for possible Lyme disease, given the tick bite and the presence of a bull's-eye rash. Further investigation is warranted to confirm the diagnosis.

Plan
Start doxycycline 100 mg twice daily for three weeks. Order a Lyme titer and Western blot test. Order a lipid panel. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment. Continue lisinopril 20 mg once daily. Continue metformin 1000 mg twice daily. Monitor blood sugars regularly.
