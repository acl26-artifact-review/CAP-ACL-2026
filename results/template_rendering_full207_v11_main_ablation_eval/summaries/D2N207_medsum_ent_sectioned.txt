Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath for approximately one week. He states that the symptoms were present about six months ago and improved with lasix dose adjustments. He has a history of coronary artery disease, including a stent placement in the right coronary artery and a prior ejection fraction of approximately 35%. He reports having had some kidney dysfunction, stage three. He admits to a history of foot pain, recently experiencing pain in both feet and taking motrin for relief, which he discontinued quickly. He has been consuming a diet high in salt due to holiday gatherings. He denies any recent trauma. He reports urinating about the same amount as usual, but has experienced nocturia, sometimes waking twice a night to urinate. He has been sleeping with two pillows to elevate his head.

Findings
Physical exam reveals jugular venous distention to 9 cm, bibasilar crackles with occasional rhonchi, one plus pitting edema bilaterally, and a three out of six systolic ejection murmur at the left base. Blood pressure is 130/80 mmHg. The patient reports feeling short of breath at rest, particularly when walking stairs or quickly, and when lying flat at night.

Assessment
The patient is experiencing an acute heart failure exacerbation secondary to underlying coronary artery disease and chronic kidney disease.

Plan
I am increasing the lasix dose from 40 mg daily to 80 mg daily, divided into two doses. I am ordering a chest x-ray to rule out other causes of the shortness of breath. I am also ordering an echocardiogram to assess cardiac function. I will call Dr. White to schedule a consultation. I am recommending a referral to a dietitian for dietary counseling. I will order a complete metabolic panel to monitor kidney function. I will also order a uric acid level to evaluate for possible gout. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in two days to assess response to treatment.
