Chief Complaint
The patient reports experiencing difficulty catching her breath and wheezing when attempting to be active.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a long-standing history of anemia dating back to age 13, initially discovered due to difficulty donating blood around December of this year. She has experienced fatigue, occasional fever and chills, and headaches. She notes worsening anxiety and depression recently. She has been taking ferrous sulfate 25 mg daily and vitamin B12 over the counter. She denies a history of polyps or sedation issues. She has not had a colonoscopy since 1996.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal findings without significant findings. Hemoglobin was low at 01/20/2020, stable hematocrit, and normal iron labs, with low ferritin. The patient reports feeling cold easily and experiencing chilling sensations.

Assessment
The patient presents with iron deficiency anemia, likely secondary to chronic blood loss.

Plan
I have scheduled an upper endoscopy and colonoscopy to investigate the source of the anemia. I will also order a bowel preparation for the colonoscopy. I recommend starting ferrous sulfate with orange juice daily and monitoring for improvement. Please call if you experience worsening symptoms.
