Chief Complaint
Abnormal laboratory findings, including low hemoglobin.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports dizziness and lightheadedness, with no reported bleeding from any site. He reports a slight nick from a knife while chopping onions approximately one week ago, but no other injuries. He reports a slightly decreased appetite and wishes for weight loss. He denies nausea, vomiting, or fever. He reports that sitting in a car can cause nausea. He reports that his knee replacement is doing okay and he can walk without problems, though he cannot run yet. He recalls a previous possible lung cancer diagnosis several years ago, which was determined to be benign after biopsy.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to the right lower quadrant upon palpation of the abdomen. The patient's lower extremities are without edema. The heart rhythm is regular. Lung sounds are clear.

Assessment
The patient's laboratory findings reveal a hemoglobin of 8.2, which is below normal. Gastritis is noted on endoscopy, with a mild polyp discovered and biopsied.

Plan
Prescribe proton pump inhibitor (PPI), protonix 40 mg daily. Advise the patient to reduce caffeine intake and avoid ibuprofen and alcohol. Order a complete blood count (CBC). Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to reassess symptoms and monitor hemoglobin levels.
