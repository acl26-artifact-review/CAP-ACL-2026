Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent pain in the left hand for the past month, worsening with gripping and lifting. He denies any recent injury. He mentions using cream for the pain. The patient reports being cold.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the left hand. No swelling or range of motion limitations are observed.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Prescribe a topical cream for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess response to treatment. Consider referral to orthopedics if symptoms do not improve.
