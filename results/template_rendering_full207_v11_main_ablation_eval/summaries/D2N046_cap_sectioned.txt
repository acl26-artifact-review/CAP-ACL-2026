Chief Complaint
Evaluation of a newly diagnosed ovarian cancer.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing severe pain and bleeding, prompting her doctor to order tests. She also reports constipation and weight loss. She denies vomiting. She is 54 years old and had her period at 13 years old, with menopause at 28. She has been married and has no children. She reports a family history of gynecological cancers, having been adopted. She denies any history of endometriosis. She has not taken any hormone replacement therapy.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a palpable left adnexal mass. Abdominal exam reveals slight tenderness to palpation in the left lower quadrant. CT imaging demonstrates a 3 cm left ovarian mass with associated involvement of nearby lymph nodes. There is no evidence of widespread peritoneal or metastatic disease.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with ovarian cancer, stage three A, based on the CT findings and lymph node involvement.

Plan
The doctor recommends initiating ca-125, hcg, afp, and ldh testing. Genetic counseling and testing are recommended. A hysterectomy and lymph node dissection will be performed. Chemotherapy with cisplatin and taxol will be administered post-surgery. Consideration is being given to intraperitoneal chemotherapy via a catheter. Follow-up will be scheduled in four weeks to assess progress and address any further concerns. The patient has a favorable prognosis given her age and the stage of the disease.
