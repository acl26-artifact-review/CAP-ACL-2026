Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a suspected injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a right knee injury three days ago sustained while playing soccer, characterized by a pop sensation and subsequent swelling. The patient reports some swelling has resolved, and the pain is currently rated 4-5 on a scale of 1 to 10. He has been taking ibuprofen regularly for pain relief. He describes pain primarily along the medial joint line upon palpation. He reports attempting to participate in pickup soccer games over the weekend, resulting in a pop in his right knee.

Findings
The patient exhibits a marked limp on the right leg upon ambulation. A significant effusion is present in the right knee, accompanied by mild ecchymosis. Passive range of motion is limited, with approximately 115 degrees of terminal extension and 20-30 degrees of terminal flexion secondary to pain. A positive Lachman exam was noted. Neurovascular examination of the bilateral lower extremities is intact. Muscle strength is a 5 out of 5 in the right lower extremity.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is highly suggestive of an anterior cruciate ligament tear.

Plan
An MRI is ordered to evaluate the integrity of the ACL. Meloxicam 50 mg daily is prescribed for pain and inflammation. The patient is instructed to ice the knee as needed. A knee immobilizer and crutches are recommended for ambulation. Activity modification, specifically avoiding jogging, is advised until further evaluation. The patient is scheduled for an MRI and will be contacted with the results promptly.
