Chief Complaint
Dysuria and urgency

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing dysuria and urinary urgency for the past 3 days. He describes the pain as sharp and constant. He denies fever, chills, or flank pain. He reports increased frequency. He states he has tried increasing fluid intake, but this has not provided relief.

Findings
Physical exam is unremarkable. Vital signs are normal. Urinalysis shows microscopic hematuria.

Assessment
Differential diagnosis includes urinary tract infection, nephrolithiasis, and interstitial cystitis.

Plan
Order urine culture and sensitivity. Initiate empiric antibiotic treatment with trimethoprim/sulfamethoxazole. Follow up in 48-72 hours.
