Chief Complaint
The patient reports shortness of breath and difficulty swallowing related to their lung cancer treatment.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a dry cough, with no phlegm, and notes pain with swallowing, particularly when taking liquids. He describes experiencing nausea and fatigue with recent chemotherapy, but reports symptom relief with zofran and rest. He mentions a history of jitteriness and weight gain associated with previous medications. He reports difficulty tolerating liquids due to pain and has been relying on shakes. The patient's husband provides emotional support.

Findings
The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. Examination reveals bilateral crackles in the lungs, likely related to fluid accumulation secondary to the lung cancer diagnosis. A chest x-ray from prior to the visit showed mild radiation pneumonitis with inflammation of the lungs. Skin examination reveals erythema on the anterior chest wall on the left side, potentially related to radiation therapy. No lower extremity edema is present.

Assessment
The patient has stage three non-small cell lung cancer, complicated by radiation pneumonitis and esophageal inflammation secondary to radiation exposure.

Plan
Prescribe a low-dose prednisone of 40 mg daily for five days to manage radiation pneumonitis. Prescribe lidocaine swabs for swallowing, to be used four times daily after each swallow. Continue chemotherapy and radiation therapy as scheduled. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess treatment response and manage side effects. Advise the patient to maintain adequate hydration and continue taking ensure shakes as needed.
