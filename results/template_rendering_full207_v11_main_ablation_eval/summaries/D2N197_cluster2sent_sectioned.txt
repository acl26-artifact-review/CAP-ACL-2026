Chief Complaint
Headache

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a worsening headache over the past three weeks, accompanied by elevated blood pressure readings. She has been taking Tylenol with limited relief, and reports no congestion or cough. She denies prior migraines or similar headaches, and reports no recent trauma. She has been monitoring her blood pressure weekly, noting readings consistently above 120/80. She reports no changes in urination.

Findings
Blood pressure readings have been consistently elevated, ranging from 120/80 to 160/100 mmHg. No frontal or maxillary sinus tenderness was noted. No lymphadenopathy was palpated.

Assessment
The patient presents with a headache and hypertension.

Plan
Increase lisinopril to 40 mg once daily and order blood work including a hemoglobin, A1C, and laboratory tests. Provide diabetic education and schedule a follow-up visit to assess blood pressure control and adjust medication as needed.
