Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath with CHF and COPD.

History of Present Illness
Gloria is a 46-year-old female presenting with shortness of breath, compounded by a history of diabetes and back pain. She reports the shortness of breath has been ongoing for about two weeks, primarily at night, making it difficult to fall asleep and breathe. She describes choking sensations during sleep. Her pulse oximetry at home is around 92%, which is slightly low. She reports weight gain and swelling in her ankles. She admits to not taking her lasix regularly and has been enjoying a large party last weekend with lots of food. She is taking metformin and has had an A1c of 7.5. She reports feeling like she's bringing a whole bunch of yuck up in the morning. She denies chest pain or numbness in her legs. She has been sitting on the couch watching TV a lot due to her back pain.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness in the paraspinal areas of the patient's back. Lung exam reveals crackles in both bases and intermittent wheezing. There is one plus pitting edema in both ankles. Neurological exam is normal. The patient's heart rate is regular and at a normal rate. The patient's blood pressure is stable.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with an exacerbation of congestive heart failure, complicated by diabetes and musculoskeletal pain.

Plan
Increase lasix dose to 60 milligrams for the next four days. Monitor weight daily. Order albuterol and atrovent as needed. Order physical therapy. Order naproxen and flexeril. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two days to reassess symptoms and adjust treatment as needed.
