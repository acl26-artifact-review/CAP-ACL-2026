S:
The patient reports a history of falling during the pandemic due to sitting at home and increased eating. He describes the elbow pain as sharp and radiating down his forearm. He reports trying ibuprofen a few days ago but often forgets to take it. His blood sugar levels typically range between 80 and 100 when checked, and he does not take insulin daily. He denies any abdominal pain.

O:
The patient's blood pressure was 150/90. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds reveal a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur. An X-ray showed no fracture or bony abnormality. The right elbow exam revealed pain to palpation of the olecranon area with mild pain on flexion and extension, but normal range of motion.

A:
The patient has a contusion to the right elbow, likely from a lacrosse stick impact. He presents with elevated blood pressure and mild hyperglycemia. The elbow pain is likely due to inflammation and swelling.

P:
Rescribe 800 mg ibuprofen twice daily for pain and inflammation. Recommend icing the elbow three times daily for 20 minutes. Continue with 20 mg lisinopril daily. Order an A1C test to assess diabetes control. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to monitor healing and adjust the plan as needed.
