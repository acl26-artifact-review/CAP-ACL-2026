Chief Complaint
Ankle pain following a basketball injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports an ankle injury sustained while playing basketball, specifically landing awkwardly and twisting his ankle. He describes increased pain and instability, particularly when walking, and notes significant swelling and bruising. He recently resumed a regular exercise routine.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness over the medial deltoid region and significant swelling on the lateral aspect of the ankle. Capillary refill is brisk. The patient reports difficulty standing on the affected ankle. An x-ray demonstrates a displaced lateral malleolus fracture at the Weber C level, with no evidence of medial or posterior malleolar fractures. The fracture is considered unstable.

Assessment
The patient presents with a lateral malleolar fracture requiring surgical intervention.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam for pain and inflammation, instructing the patient to ice and elevate the ankle regularly. Order crutches for weight-bearing support. Estimate a recovery period of approximately three months, with a planned surgical fixation involving a plate and screws. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and continue rehabilitation.
