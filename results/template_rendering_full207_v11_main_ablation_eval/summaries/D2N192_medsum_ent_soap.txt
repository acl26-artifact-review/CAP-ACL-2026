S:
The patient presents with a 10-day history of abdominal pain, which started as mild discomfort and has been worsening over the past few days. The pain is exacerbated by meals and somewhat relieved by rest. He reports nausea and some heartburn, but denies vomiting. He has not experienced any urinary symptoms or diarrhea. His appetite has decreased slightly, and he feels more fatigued. He reports that his migraines have been rare recently, and he primarily uses excedrin for them, with sumatriptan as needed. He is not checking his blood pressures daily.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness in the right upper quadrant with mild to moderate tenderness. A grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur is heard. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable. Blood pressure is slightly low, reading approximately 120/70.

A:
The abdominal pain and right upper quadrant tenderness raise concern for possible gallbladder pathology, such as cholecystitis or gallstones. The murmur is unchanged from the previous examination. The patient's reported fatigue and decreased appetite warrant further investigation.

P:
I have ordered a CBC, chemistry panel, urinalysis, and pregnancy test. I am also ordering an ultrasound of the right upper quadrant. I will prescribe a refill of lisinopril 20mg daily. I will also prescribe a refill of sumatriptan as needed. I will follow up with the patient in four weeks to assess his progress.
