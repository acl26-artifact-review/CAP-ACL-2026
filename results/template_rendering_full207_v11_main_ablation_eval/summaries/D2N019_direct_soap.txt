S:
The patient reports right elbow pain following a box-lifting incident yesterday. He describes a popping sensation and strain while lifting. He reports ice provides some relief, but ibuprofen provides only temporary relief, with throbbing returning upon ice removal. He denies any other symptoms. He reports a history of anaphylaxis and a previous carotid surgery.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness over the lateral epicondyle, swelling, and redness of the right elbow. Range of motion is limited with flexion and extension. Dorsal forearm pain is noted. X-ray shows normal bony alignment with mild swelling at the lateral epicondyle. No fracture is identified.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with lateral epicondylitis. The history of anaphylaxis is noted for future management.

P:
Rescribe ibuprofen 800 mg every six hours as needed. Provide a sling for comfort. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week if symptoms do not improve. Order medications and procedures.
