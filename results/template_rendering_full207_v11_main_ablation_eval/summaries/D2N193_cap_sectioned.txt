Chief Complaint
A hacking cough for about three days now.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports having this hacking cough for about three days now. It started off with a gradual cough and has now progressed to coughing up some green phlegm. He mentions a sore throat and nasal congestion. He's been drinking lots of water and taking over-the-counter cough medication, but it hasn't been helping. He reports being around his sister a few days ago, who has had similar symptoms, and noticed he started to develop a cough shortly after. He denies fever, shortness of breath, body aches, nausea, or vomiting. He also mentions a history of seasonal allergies around spring time, with sneezing and runny nose. He's been trying to avoid salty foods and has had some difficulty with sugar intake. He was on vacation last week and didn't get sick, but he did notice that many people had vacationed last week. He reports that his mother died of pancreatic cancer.

Findings
Physical exam reveals bilateral maxillary sinus tenderness. The patient's lungs are clear to auscultation. A systolic ejection murmur is present at the left base. Blood pressure is elevated at 140/72 mmHg. The patient's blood pressure has been creeping up slightly. The patient reports a cough and nasal congestion.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with an upper respiratory infection, likely viral in origin, given his exposure to a sick family member. He has a cough and sinus tenderness.

Plan
I'm going to start your physical exam. We will check you for COVID. Continue with the cough medicine you've been taking. I recommend coricidin hbp. We will continue you on your metformin five hundred milligrams daily or twice a day. We will schedule your hemoglobin a1c at your next visit. I will increase your hydrochlorothiazide dose to twenty five milligrams once a day. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Order shoulder radiographs.
