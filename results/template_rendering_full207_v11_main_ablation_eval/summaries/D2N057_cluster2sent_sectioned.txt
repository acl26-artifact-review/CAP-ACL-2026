Chief Complaint
Right big toe pain and inflammation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a two-week history of intermittent right big toe pain, initially mild but worsening last week, particularly after prolonged walking. He describes the pain as throbbing with occasional sharp, stabbing sensations, especially with movement. He avoids bending the toe and has been walking on his heel to minimize the pain. He denies any recent injury and reports taking two ibuprofen daily with minimal relief. He also notes feeling a bone spur in the area. The patient reports no prior gastrointestinal issues.

Findings
Examination reveals a large spur on the top of the right big toe joint. X-ray confirms a large spur on the top of the right big toe joint.

Assessment
The patient has hallux rigidus with a prominent bone spur, causing significant pain and functional impairment.

Plan
Continue orthotics and meloxicam once daily, monitoring for gastrointestinal side effects. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess response to treatment and discuss potential surgical options if conservative measures fail.
