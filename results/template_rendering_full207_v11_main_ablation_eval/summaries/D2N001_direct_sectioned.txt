Chief Complaint
Annual physical examination and review of chronic medical conditions.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports doing well with congestive heart failure management, including a low-sodium diet and adherence to her medications. She has been traveling frequently and reports hiking regularly, including a recent trip to the Northern Cascades. She reports that her depression is well-managed through ongoing therapy, which she attends weekly. She also reports that her blood pressure has been difficult to control, often fluctuating with stress levels. She admits to occasionally forgetting to take her blood pressure medications, particularly during periods of high work stress. She denies any current symptoms of chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a systolic ejection murmur of III/VI, best heard at the left sternal border. Lower extremity pitting edema is present bilaterally, grade 1. The patient's blood pressure reading in the office is elevated at 142/88 mmHg. An echocardiogram from one year ago showed a left ventricular ejection fraction of 45% and mitral regurgitation. A lipid panel from one year ago showed slightly elevated cholesterol levels. The patient reports nasal congestion related to fall allergies.

Assessment
1. Congestive heart failure, stable. 2. Depression, well-managed. 3. Hypertension, uncontrolled.

Plan
Continue current medications for congestive heart failure, increasing lisinopril to 40 mg daily. Initiate lasix 20 mg daily as needed for symptom control. Schedule a repeat echocardiogram in six months. Continue psychotherapy weekly. Advise the patient to monitor blood pressure daily and record readings. Order a repeat lipid panel in one year. Schedule a mammogram. Finalize the clinical note.
