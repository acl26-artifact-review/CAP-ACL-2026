Chief Complaint
Patient reports elevated blood sugar levels and seeks follow-up.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of elevated blood sugar levels, primarily linked to increased sugar consumption, particularly during the holidays due to family gatherings and treats. She reports feeling lightheaded and experiencing visual disturbances approximately one week prior to presentation. She has been consistently following a diet but reports difficulty maintaining it due to family events. She reports feeling anxious, which triggers episodes of rapid heart rate. She is currently taking protonix 40mg daily for reflux and metoprolol 25mg twice daily for atrial fibrillation. She denies fever, chills, chest pain, abdominal pain, or urinary symptoms.

Findings
Physical examination reveals an enlarged thyroid gland, non-tender. The patient's heart rate is irregular and rhythm is abnormal. Lungs are clear to auscultation. No edema is noted in the lower extremities. Blood glucose levels are elevated, as are hemoglobin A1c levels.

Assessment
The patient's elevated blood glucose levels and hemoglobin A1c levels indicate diabetes mellitus. She has a history of atrial fibrillation and osteoarthritis.

Plan
Order a complete metabolic panel and diabetes labs. Start metformin 500 mg twice daily. Order a refill of metoprolol 25 mg twice daily. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to review lab results and adjust management as needed. Provide the patient with instructions on glucometer use and blood sugar monitoring. Refer to a registered dietitian for dietary counseling.
