Chief Complaint
Persistent facial rash and discomfort.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent facial rash that began approximately two months ago. He initially used doxycycline twice daily, noticing improvement, and then reduced the dose to once daily, at which point the improvement plateaued. He has been using metrocream after each face wash and reports no dryness. He discontinued elidel due to concerns about reading misinformation online. He has been using vanicream cleanser and no other lotions. He reports feeling dry when using the regular face wash and has been using petroleum jelly to moisturize.

Findings
Examination reveals mild erythema and papules around the perioral area and medial cheeks. Vital signs are stable. No signs of systemic distress.

Assessment
Perioral dermatitis and mild facial erythema.

Plan
Continue doxycycline once daily. Prescribe tacrolimus ointment for application to the affected areas twice daily. Recommend gentle cleansing with vanicream cleanser and petroleum jelly for moisturization. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response and adjust treatment as needed. Advise patient to avoid using any other skincare products until further evaluation.
