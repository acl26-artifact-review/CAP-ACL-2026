Chief Complaint
Sudden visual distortion and blurring of vision.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sudden visual distortion and blurring of vision, accompanied by difficulty reading and straight lines appearing crooked. He previously experienced a decrease in vision when reading and noted distortions in door frames. He reports a history of macular degeneration.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure was difficult to assess due to reported difficulty remembering to do so. Examination revealed clear corneas, no movement of vessels, no peripheral contact, no intraocular lenses, and no visual field changes. Diagnostic tests, including optical coherence tomography (OCT) and fluorescein angiography, demonstrate drusen deposits, retinal pigment epithelium subretinal fluid accumulation, choroidal neovascular membrane visualization, and focal areas of leakage in the right eye consistent with neovascular age-related macular degeneration.

Assessment
The patient presents with neovascular age-related macular degeneration, advanced to a stage indicative of potential severe vision loss.

Plan
Initiate monthly intravitreal injections of lucenté into the vitreous portion of the eye after numbing, and schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess treatment response and monitor for disease progression.
