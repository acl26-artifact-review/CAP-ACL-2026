Chief Complaint
Non-healing foot ulcer on the right foot.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a two-month history of a red, draining foot ulcer that recently appeared. He believes the ulcer may have resulted from walking barefoot on the beach. He previously received antibiotics from his primary care physician but did not pick them up. The patient reports occasional throbbing pain, but not specific pain, and notes the ulcer started to drain and appears red. He has not experienced this before. He takes medication for his diabetes but does not regularly check his blood sugar levels.

Findings
Examination reveals a one by two inch circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot, proximal to the fifth metatarsal head. The wound is accompanied by redness, drainage, and edema. There is no evidence of necrosis, odor, or bony exposure. An x-ray previously revealed no osteomyelitis. Vital signs are normal, with no fever. The patient's blood pressure is within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient has a diabetic foot ulcer requiring further evaluation and management.

Plan
An ankle brachial index will be ordered to assess blood supply. A debridement will be performed to remove dead tissue. The patient will be prescribed clindamycin 400 mg twice daily for seven days to treat the infection. A surgical shoe will be provided for the patient to wear for one month to protect the ulcer. Follow-up will be scheduled in two weeks to assess healing and consider further debridement. The patient is advised to call the office with any questions or concerns, and to seek urgent care or the emergency room if he develops a fever or any signs of worsening condition.
