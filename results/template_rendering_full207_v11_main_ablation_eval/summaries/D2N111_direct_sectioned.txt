Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a trampoline injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports injury to his right knee while playing at a trampoline park, describing a snapping sensation and immediate pain upon landing. He states this is the first time he has ever attempted this activity. He reports using Tylenol for the pain, which provided minimal relief. He has not tried ice or heat. He reports the pain has persisted for approximately one week. The patient reports difficulty walking with a slight limp.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild ecchymosis and edema along the lateral aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is adequate, though with some pain with movement. The patient demonstrates a slight gait abnormality. Blood pressure is 180/80 mmHg. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart rhythm is regular with no murmurs appreciated.

Assessment
Right knee lateral ligament strain.

Plan
Order a right knee x-ray to rule out fracture. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain and inflammation. Refer to physical therapy for rehabilitation. Order an echocardiogram to assess hypertension management. Order an A1C to assess diabetes control. Order a lipid panel. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
