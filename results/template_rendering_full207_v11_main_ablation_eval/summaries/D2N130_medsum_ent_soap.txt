S:
The patient reports left elbow pain, describing it as stiff and painful with flexion and extension, exacerbated by prolonged typing. She notes palpable swelling at the elbow and reports pain with palpation of the olecranon. She denies numbness, tingling, or weakness in the arm. The patient reports no fever or chills and states she hasn't experienced any recent seizures. She reports being doing well with therapy for depression and denies any suicidal ideation. She reports being doing better with her peptic ulcer disease since the endoscopy procedure. She endorses nasal congestion from allergies.

O:
Physical examination reveals erythema and edema of the left elbow. Palpation of the left olecranon elicits tenderness. A palpable left radial pulse is present. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. The left arm x-ray demonstrates no evidence of fracture. The endoscopy results show gastritis consistent with a history of peptic ulcer disease.

A:
The clinician diagnoses olecranon bursitis. The patient's epilepsy is well-controlled with current medication regimen. The patient is managing depression effectively with ongoing therapy.

P:
The clinician prescribes meloxicam 15 mg once daily for the left elbow pain. A refill of Keppra 500 mg twice daily is ordered. Lexapro 10 mg once daily is prescribed for depression. Protonix 40 mg once daily before meals is prescribed for the peptic ulcer disease. The nurse will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks.
