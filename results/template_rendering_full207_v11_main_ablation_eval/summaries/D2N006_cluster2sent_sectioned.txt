Chief Complaint
Persistent nausea, vomiting, and reflux symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports ongoing episodes of nausea, vomiting, primarily in the mornings, and persistent reflux despite dietary modifications and omeprazole. He also describes abdominal pain and bloating. He has a history of gout, managed with allopurinol, and arthritis, addressed with physical therapy and weight-bearing exercises. He experienced a gout flare approximately two months ago and was successfully treated with medication. He reports no further gout flares.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild right knee effusion and persistent arthritis. The autoimmune panel was unremarkable. Right knee x-ray demonstrates residual arthritis without fracture. Vital signs are stable.

Assessment
The patient presents with persistent gastroesophageal reflux disease and associated symptoms of nausea and vomiting.

Plan
Order a right upper quadrant ultrasound to evaluate for gallstones. Continue omeprazole 40 mg once daily. Prescribe allopurinol 100 mg daily. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and imaging results. Encourage continued participation in physical therapy and adherence to pilates regimen.
