Chief Complaint
Severe acid reflux symptoms, including heartburn and throat irritation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a several-year history of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), with symptoms worsening over the past few years. He currently takes 40 milligrams of omeprazole twice daily, but reports continued heartburn despite this medication. He identifies spicy foods, carbonated beverages, citrus products, and tomato-based foods as potential triggers. He also reports significant stress and anxiety. He has had a baby with intestinal surgery at one month old and his grandmother had esophageal cancer. He has previously tried omeprazole and ranitidine, which provided some relief. He has experienced a two-month period of decreased appetite.

Findings
Physical examination was unremarkable except for well-healed abdominal surgical scars. Vital signs were within normal limits. No findings of dysphagia were noted.

Assessment
Clinical findings are consistent with poorly controlled GERD.

Plan
Prescribe nexium 40 milligrams twice daily and send to pharmacy on file. Schedule an upper endoscopy (EGD) for further evaluation.
