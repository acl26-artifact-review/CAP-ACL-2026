Chief Complaint
Breast concerns and anxiety.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing anxiety, particularly around November and December when on progesterone, and recently at random times. She also notes increased breast lumps, which were smaller after previous cycles but remain present. She discontinued camila birth control and reports feeling some bumps. The patient's husband is not yet vaccinated, and she actively participates in the vaccine fight at home. She previously took buspar and singulair, with concerns about singulair potentially causing anxiety. She describes increased irritability before starting progesterone. She has had a normal Pap in 2019 and notes an increase in abnormal paps in others with immunocompromising factors and stress. The patient feels a need for continued surveillance given her age and family history of breast cancer.

Findings
The patient's most recent Pap was performed in 2019. They report experiencing some bumps and soreness related to breastfeeding.

Assessment
The patient's persistent breast lumps and history of anxiety warrant further investigation and management.

Plan
Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess the stability of the breast lumps and continue monitoring for anxiety symptoms. Consider further imaging if clinically indicated.
