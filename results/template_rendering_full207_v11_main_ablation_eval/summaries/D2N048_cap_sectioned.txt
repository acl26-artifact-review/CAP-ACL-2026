Chief Complaint
Right foot pain following a tennis injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right foot pain that began approximately two days ago following a fall while playing tennis. He describes attempting to volley the ball and subsequently falling on his foot. He reports immediate pain and difficulty weight-bearing. He took ace wraps and iced the foot last night. He reports some relief with ibuprofen, but it wears off. He denies numbness or tingling. He previously injured his left leg. He has been playing tennis as a hobby and is trying to learn, but cannot afford lessons. He reports that he is concerned about being unable to drive.

Findings
Physical exam reveals bruising of the plantar and dorsal aspects of the right foot. There is associated swelling in the midfoot. Palpation elicits tenderness. The Lisfranc test is positive at the first and second metatarsal levels. The right foot is warm to the touch. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the right foot. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong. The patient reports stridor. Vitals are stable: blood pressure 120/75 mmHg, respiratory rate 18, temperature 97.1°F, and oxygen saturation 100%. Lung exam reveals mild stridor. Abdominal exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient has a Lisfranc fracture of the right foot, consistent with the mechanism of injury and physical exam findings. There is significant foot instability.

Plan
I am recommending surgical intervention to stabilize the Lisfranc fracture with plates and screws. An MRI will be ordered to assess ligamentous injury. The patient will wear a cast for six to eight weeks and use crutches during that time. Follow-up will be in one week. Physical therapy will be recommended after surgery. I will order an MRI to further assess the fracture and any injury to the ligaments.
