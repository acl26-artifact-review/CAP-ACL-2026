Chief Complaint
Left leg pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports severe pains in their legs. He also reports feeling overly tired and states that his leg pain has worsened over the past six months. He describes the pain as bilateral and constant, with increased intensity with activity. The patient denies any recent trauma. He reports taking acetaminophen for the pain, which provides minimal relief. He also mentions feeling fatigued.

Findings
Examination reveals decreased range of motion in the left leg and tenderness to palpation. Neurological exam is intact. No signs of acute distress.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are concerning for peripheral neuropathy.

Plan
Order nerve conduction studies to evaluate for peripheral neuropathy. Recommend physical therapy for gait training and pain management. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
