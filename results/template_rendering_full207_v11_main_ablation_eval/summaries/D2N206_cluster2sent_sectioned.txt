Chief Complaint
Patient reports experiencing pain for the last two weeks.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent pain for the last two weeks, occasionally radiating to one testicle, and describes episodes of chills. He reports passing stones previously and has undergone prior surgeries. The patient denies nausea or vomiting and reports using ibuprofen for pain relief. He reports that many patients experience minor pain.

Findings
Blood pressure was slightly elevated, likely due to pain. Physical examination revealed right-sided abdominal tenderness consistent with a kidney stone. A CT scan confirmed a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter with no evidence of high-flow nephrosis. No tenderness to palpation of the abdomen was noted.

Assessment
The patient has a confirmed kidney stone with no evidence of obstruction or significant renal compromise.

Plan
Prescribe tramadol 50 mg as needed for breakthrough pain. Continue ibuprofen for pain management. Order a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one to two weeks if symptoms persist or if the stone is not passed. Arrange for a strainer to be used when urinating to facilitate stone passage.
