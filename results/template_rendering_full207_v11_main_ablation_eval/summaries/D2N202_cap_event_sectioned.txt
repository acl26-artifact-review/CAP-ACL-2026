Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain following a fall while painting.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right shoulder pain that began approximately three weeks ago following a fall while painting a room. He reports bracing himself during the fall. He initially iced the arm and took Advil. He denies prior shoulder injuries. He reports intermittent pain, with a current pain level of approximately 6/10, which improves with acetaminophen. He mentions remodeling his house and recently visited Lowe's, where he learned about sales and flooring.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the right shoulder and tenderness to the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Neurovascular exam shows brisk capillary refill and equal sensation. Right shoulder x-ray demonstrates no fracture or bony abnormality. The patient reports numbness was initially present but has resolved.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order an MRI of the right shoulder to rule out other pathology. Refer to physical therapy for a 6-8 week course. Continue acetaminophen as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to evaluate progress.
