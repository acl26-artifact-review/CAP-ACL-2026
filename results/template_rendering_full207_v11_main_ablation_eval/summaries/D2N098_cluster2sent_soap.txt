S:
The patient reports pain in his left shoulder and elbow for approximately five months, following a fall five months ago. He describes constant pain in the left shoulder and left elbow, worsened by lifting, and reports difficulty lifting more than 15-20 pounds. He has stopped going to the gym due to the pain. He denies numbness or tingling. He has tried icing the area but has not taken any pain medication. He reports that the pain is worse when lifting.

O:
Physical examination reveals positive Hawkins-Kennedy and Neer's tests. Palpation of the left shoulder and elbow reveals tenderness to palpation, but no warmth, erythema, or deformity.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with impingement syndrome of the left shoulder.

P:
We will initiate physical therapy as the first treatment option. A referral is requested for Dr. Martha Rivera to provide physical therapy two days per week. We will follow up if symptoms worsen or do not improve with physical therapy.
