Chief Complaint
The patient reports experiencing high blood sugar and related symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing high blood sugar, initially noticing symptoms approximately two weeks ago, including lightheadedness and visual disturbances. He also reports a history of osteoarthritis, atrial fibrillation triggered by anxiety, and reflux managed with protonix 40mg daily. He was recently in the emergency room due to elevated blood sugar levels. He denies fever, chills, chest pain, shortness of breath, or abdominal pain. He reports that his blood sugars have been running high for several months. He is currently taking protonix 40mg daily and reports that the medication is helping.

Findings
The patient is a 52-year-old female. Laboratory results show elevated blood sugar and hemoglobin A1c. A physical exam reveals an enlarged thyroid, but it is non-tender, and there are no carotid bruits or edema in the lower extremities. The patient's heart rate and rhythm are irregular, but the rate is good. Lung sounds are clear. The abdomen is soft and non-tender.

Assessment
The patient has diabetes mellitus.

Plan
Order a complete metabolic panel and autoimmune labs. Start metformin 500 milligrams twice a day. Order a refill on metoprolol 25 milligrams twice a day. Continue the patient on protonix 40 milligrams once daily. The nurse will provide education on glucometer use and blood sugar testing.
