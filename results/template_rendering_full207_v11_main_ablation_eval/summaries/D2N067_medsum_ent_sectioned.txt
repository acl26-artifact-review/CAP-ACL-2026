Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right knee pain two weeks ago following a skiing injury. He describes skiing down a black diamond slope and colliding with another skier, resulting in a twist of his knee. He reports hearing a pop at the time of the injury. He has been wearing a brace and using THC cream for pain relief. He reports that his left knee does not hurt and feels unstable. He has had previous surgery on his right knee at age 14 due to a gymnastics injury. He describes a history of problems with his knee since that time. He states his pain tolerance is high and he continues to ski and ski very well. He reports that his pain is exacerbated by walking for longer periods of time, particularly when walking his dog. He has not taken any medications other than THC gummies from his grandmother.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild swelling and bruising on the right knee. The patient demonstrates a slight limp. Range of motion is limited, with decreased active abduction. Pain is elicited with impingement testing. Dorsiflexion causes pain, while plantar flexion does not. The patient's gait is affected, exhibiting a noticeable limp.

Assessment
The patient presents with a strained posterior cruciate ligament in the right knee, likely secondary to the previous injury and skiing incident. Clinical findings suggest moderate pain and functional limitations, consistent with a postero-lateral corner injury.

Plan
I am recommending physical therapy three times a week to help restore strength and range of motion to the right knee. I am prescribing meloxicam 15mg once daily for pain and inflammation, and ibuprofen 800mg twice daily as needed for the left knee. I recommend avoiding skiing and gymnastics activities until physical therapy is complete. Please schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
