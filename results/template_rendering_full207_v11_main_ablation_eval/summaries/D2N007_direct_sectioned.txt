Chief Complaint
Annual exam and ongoing depression and chronic back pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports struggling with depression over the past year due to remote work and social isolation. He reports that his depression has been on and off, and he believes increasing his dose of prozac might help. He also reports chronic back pain, which is exacerbated by prolonged sitting, particularly at his desk. The pain is often worse when sitting and improves with movement. He denies numbness or tingling in his legs, but reports a 'falling asleep' sensation. He has a history of congenital artery bypass grafting at age 27, which has been stable. He is currently taking prozac 20 milligrams daily.

Findings
Physical examination reveals no distress. The neck is unremarkable. Heart rate is 68 beats per minute with a regular rhythm and a three out of six systolic ejection murmur. Lungs are clear to auscultation. The abdomen is soft. Lumbar spine examination reveals decreased flexion and pain to palpation. Lower extremity strength is normal, and there is no edema. An ECG shows stable findings from last year. Lumbar spine x-rays are stable from last year. An echocardiogram reveals no acute abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient presents with chronic depression and chronic back pain.

Plan
Order a physical therapy referral for the chronic back pain. Increase the prozac dosage from 20 milligrams to 40 milligrams, one tablet daily. Order an echocardiogram. Order aspirin 81 milligrams daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed. The nurse will assist with scheduling these orders.
