He denies sensory loss or weakness in the affected face. He reports occasional headaches, but no history of migraines. He has tried ibuprofen and topical analgesics without relief. He denies any history of multiple sclerosis or brain tumors.

O:
An MRI reveals a small blood vessel abutting and potentially compressing the trigeminal nerve. The patient's blood pressure is mildly low.

A:
The patient likely has trigeminal neuralgia due to compression of the trigeminal nerve by the blood vessel.

Rescribe tegretol to start, and monitor for side effects. If pain persists, discuss surgical options for nerve decompression.
