Chief Complaint
Right leg pain following a bowling injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right leg pain following a bowling injury. He describes hitting the bowling ball into his leg while running to the lane, resulting in a fall. He denies significant swelling but reports mild bruising to the back of his right leg. He was able to walk with some difficulty but avoided stairs. He has a history of atopic eczema treated with fluocinonide when needed and a previous surgical history of a colectomy due to diverticulitis.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to the lateral aspect of the right upper leg. Range of motion is intact in the lower leg. X-ray demonstrates no fracture or significant swelling.

Assessment
Contusion of the right upper leg.

Plan
Start mobic 15 milligrams once daily. Recommend ice packs for pain relief. Follow up in one week if symptoms do not improve. Dragon, please order the prescribed medications and procedures.
