Chief Complaint
Right knee pain for approximately six months.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain that began approximately six months ago, with no identifiable traumatic event. He describes the pain as a deep, achy sensation, particularly exacerbated by sitting and standing for extended periods, as well as going up and down stairs. He has tried ibuprofen and Aleve, which provide some relief but not complete pain resolution. He denies any prior injuries or fractures. He reports working from home with a desk setting, which is primarily located downstairs, but he does have to go upstairs in the morning. He enjoys running and cycling, but the knee pain has significantly limited his ability to run, reducing his activity to approximately half a mile. He describes the pain as being 'behind his kneecaps'.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal gait. Muscle strength is good, with abduction of the legs bilaterally at 3/5. Palpation reveals tenderness in the knee. The patellar grind test is positive upon standing. No redness, swelling, or effusion is noted. Neurological exam is intact, with symmetrical reflexes. X-rays demonstrate no fractures or osteoarthritis. Vital signs are within normal limits: blood pressure 120/70, heart rate 60, respiratory rate 14.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and examination findings, patellofemoral pain syndrome is the most likely diagnosis.

Plan
Recommend physical therapy to address lower extremity strength and running mechanics. Continue ibuprofen or Aleve as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as necessary.
