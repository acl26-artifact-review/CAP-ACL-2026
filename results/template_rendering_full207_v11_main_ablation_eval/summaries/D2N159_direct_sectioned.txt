Chief Complaint
Right hand numbness and tingling with associated pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing numbness and tingling in his right hand for approximately six months, with increasing persistence over the past month. The symptoms are most prominent when typing on the keyboard, and he reports pain primarily during keyboard use. Ibuprofen and avoiding keyboard use provide some relief. The patient also notes pain when moving his mouse.

Findings
Examination reveals mild weakness in right hand grip strength. A positive Tinel's sign was elicited over the right wrist. Examination of the right hand reveals tenderness to palpation over the wrist and fingers, particularly the thumb, index, and middle fingers.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Prescribe a wrist splint to wear at night. Increase ibuprofen dosage to 600 milligrams four times daily. Order an EMG to evaluate nerve conduction studies in the right wrist. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to conservative management or discuss surgical options if symptoms persist.
