Chief Complaint
Annual exam and management of hypertension and depression.

History of Present Illness
Ralph is a 62-year-old male with a past medical history of depression and a prior lobectomy, who presents for his annual exam. He reports that his depression has been well-managed with prozac, and he has not experienced any incidents in a while. He describes a struggle with hypertension, particularly on weekends, and reports occasional shortness of breath with exertion, often accompanied by chest pain during runs, typically covering 4 to 5 miles daily. He also reports nasal congestion from pollen. He denies any recent trauma or blood in stool. He has been taking his medications regularly during the business week but occasionally forgets to bring them on weekends.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a blood pressure of 120/80 mmHg, a systolic ejection murmur of 2/6, and clear lung sounds. An EKG shows no abnormalities, and a chest x-ray is normal. The sinus palpation reveals some congestion.

Assessment
The patient's primary diagnoses include hypertension and depression. He is currently managing these conditions effectively with medication and lifestyle modifications.

Plan
I will order a refill of prozac 20 milligrams daily. I will also order an echocardiogram to evaluate the systolic ejection murmur. Furthermore, I will order routine blood work. I will follow up with the results and schedule a patient portal communication.
