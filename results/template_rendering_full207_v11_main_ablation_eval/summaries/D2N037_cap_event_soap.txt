S:
The patient reports pain in the left elbow, particularly with flexion and extension, and some limited range of motion on extension. He also reports pain with pronation and supination of the right elbow, but not with torsion. The patient states the pain has been present for approximately a year and a half and began after lifting weights.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the left elbow. Range of motion is limited on extension of the left elbow. Pulses are equal bilaterally. Sensation is intact to light touch in all dermatomes.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with medial epicondylitis (golfer's elbow).

P:
A cervical MRI is ordered to further evaluate the patient's elbow pain. We will also discuss a whole blood transfusion as a potential treatment option, pending the MRI results.
