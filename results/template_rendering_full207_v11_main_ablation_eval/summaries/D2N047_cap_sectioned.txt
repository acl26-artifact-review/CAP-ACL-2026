Chief Complaint
Difficulty urinating

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty urinating for approximately six months, with a perceived weak urinary stream and occasional incomplete bladder emptying. He reports multiple nighttime awakenings to urinate, approximately three to four times per night. He denies any burning sensation during urination. He reports occasional diarrhea last week and has experienced urinary hesitancy. The symptoms have been progressively worsening over the past six months. The patient reports hand pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals an enlarged prostate and a slight systolic ejection murmur at the left base. The patient's heart rhythm is regular, and there is no shortness of breath. The abdomen is non-tender and non-distended. The lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with urinary retention and prostate enlargement.

Plan
I am ordering routine laboratory tests, including a PSA, to evaluate for prostate cancer. I am also prescribing flomax 0.4 mg once daily at night to help with urinary retention. I will refer the patient to a urologist for further evaluation and management. I will order an echocardiogram to follow up on the heart murmur. I will continue the patient on lipitor 40 mg daily and metoprolol as prescribed. I will order a hemoglobin A1c to monitor diabetes control. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in three to four weeks, and I encourage the patient to contact me or message me through the patient portal if he has any concerns.
