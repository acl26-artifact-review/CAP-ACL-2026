Chief Complaint
Chronic lumbar back pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a two-year history of chronic lumbar back pain, which he attributes to movement furniture one weekend. He has tried various treatments, including physical therapy and strong pain medications, but has experienced only temporary relief and is averse to surgery. He describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation that radiates down his right leg to his big toe. He reports difficulty getting comfortable, particularly when sitting. He has gained some weight over the past years due to difficulty with activity related to his back pain and has been trying to stay active. He previously enjoyed playing golf, but this has been significantly impacted by his back pain. He denies any family history of spine conditions. He quit smoking twenty years ago.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no overlying erythema. Midline tenderness is present at L4-L5 with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness. Lumbar flexion elicits pain. The straight leg raise test is positive on the right. Neurological exam demonstrates decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at L4 dermatome. Patellar and Achilles reflexes are symmetrical. Vitals are stable and unremarkable. MRI reveals a disk herniation with nerve impingement at L4-L5.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with an L4-L5 herniated disc with radiculopathy.

Plan
I am recommending a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and provide symptomatic relief. This injection will target the lumbar spine under fluoroscopy to ensure accurate placement. We will monitor the patient's response to the injection and adjust the treatment plan as needed. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress. I will also recommend continued physical therapy to strengthen the muscles supporting the spine and improve posture.
