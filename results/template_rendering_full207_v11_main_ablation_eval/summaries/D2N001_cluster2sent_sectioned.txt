Chief Complaint
The patient reports ongoing hypertension concerns and difficulty adhering to her blood pressure medication regimen.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that her blood pressure is often elevated, particularly when experiencing stress. She acknowledges difficulty consistently taking her blood pressure medications, attributing this to the demands of her work. She also reports some nasal congestion related to fall allergies and experiences occasional chest pain. The patient is currently enrolled in therapy and reports feeling well. She is taking her medications for congestive heart failure and depression.

Findings
Physical examination reveals elevated blood pressure readings at the office, with a systolic blood pressure of 152/92 mmHg. A three out of six systolic ejection murmur is auscultated. The patient demonstrates one plus pitting edema in her lower extremities. An echocardiogram from last year showed a low-ish pumping function of her heart at 45% and mitral regurgitation. A lipid panel from last year showed slightly elevated cholesterol.

Assessment
The patient presents with hypertension, congestive heart failure, and mild hyperlipidemia.

Plan
I will increase the patient's lisinopril to 40 milligrams daily and initiate lasix at 20 milligrams daily. I will order an echocardiogram to monitor her heart function. I will schedule a mammogram for her annual exam. I recommend the patient continue with her current therapy and monitor her blood pressure daily, recording the readings to share at follow-up.
