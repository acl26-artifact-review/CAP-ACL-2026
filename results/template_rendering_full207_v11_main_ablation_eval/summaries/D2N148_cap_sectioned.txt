Chief Complaint
Back injury

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining a back injury four months ago while moving boxes. He initially tried physical therapy at his gym but found it wasn't helpful. He was concerned about the delay in seeing a doctor and the symptoms persisted, leading him to seek medical attention now. He describes experiencing a sharp pain when twisting or rolling over in bed, which subsides when repositioned. He has been taking tylenol and ibuprofen, but finds they only provide temporary relief. He reports periodic numbness and tingling in his feet, which occurred initially with the injury but has since resolved. He denies any bladder or bowel issues. He had a total knee replacement two years ago on his right knee, which went well and he has regained approximately 95% function.

Findings
The patient's vital signs are stable, with a blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg, heart rate of 68 bpm, and oxygen saturation of 98% on room air. A bony protuberance was noted on the C5-C6 vertebrae. Physical exam reveals decreased flexion and extension of the lumbar spine. Straight leg raise testing is positive. Lower extremity strength is equal bilaterally. The patient reports spasm in the paraspinal muscles of the lumbar spine. Examination reveals no acute tenderness to palpation of the lumbar spine.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with an acute lumbar disc herniation.

Plan
I am ordering a lumbar spine MRI to further evaluate the patient's condition. I will also refer the patient to a physical therapist for continued rehabilitation. Furthermore, I am prescribing meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily to manage pain.
