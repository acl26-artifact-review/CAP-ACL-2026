He has been using saline mist and Tylenol.

Findings
Examination reveals clear mucous. The patient reports pulling on his ears and experiencing ear pain. The patient is rubbing his nose frequently. The patient's rectal temperature was 100.3°F. The patient is sweating.

Assessment
The patient likely has a viral infection, possibly a sinus infection. Given the symptoms, a bacterial infection cannot be ruled out at this time.

Plan
Continue saline mist and Tylenol as needed. Follow up in one week if symptoms do not improve or if a barky cough develops. If a barky cough develops, administer half a teaspoon of sister's medicine. Order shoulder radiographs and initiate physical therapy. Arrange follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Give the patient half a teaspoon of ibuprofen. Give the patient agave as an alternative to honey. Give the patient a soft brush and baby oil. Check out in six weeks.
