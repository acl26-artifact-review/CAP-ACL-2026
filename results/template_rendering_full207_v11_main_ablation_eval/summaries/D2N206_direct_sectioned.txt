Chief Complaint
Patient reports right flank pain radiating to the groin, likely related to a kidney stone.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a two-week history of right flank pain, which is exacerbated by drinking and physical activity. The pain radiates down to the groin and is often under control, but can become more intense. He reports an episode of testicular pain, which resolved. He has passed stones previously and had prior surgery. He has used ibuprofen for pain relief. He denies nausea, vomiting, or fever. Family history includes multiple cases of kidney stones.

Findings
Blood pressure is slightly elevated, likely due to pain. Palpation of the abdomen reveals tenderness in the right flank. No other significant physical exam findings were noted. CT scan confirms a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter with no evidence of obstruction or high-flow nephrosis.

Assessment
Patient has a confirmed kidney stone, likely causing the reported flank and groin pain.

Plan
Continue ibuprofen as needed for pain relief. Prescribe tramadol 50 mg for breakthrough pain. Order a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture. Follow up in one to two weeks if symptoms persist or if the stone is not passed. Advise the patient to continue using a strainer when urinating to attempt to catch the stone. Consider shockwave lithotripsy if the stone is not passed within two weeks.
