Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath primarily during mild exercise and walking up her driveway, occasionally accompanied by palpitations. She attributes this to potential triggers related to her back pain. She has a history of depression treated with effexor and chronic back pain managed with gabapentin. She reports feeling extremely low on some days and has been taking effexor for her depression. She has a history of smoking and reports mild wheezes on exam. She denies other acute issues.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild wheezing in all lung fields. The patient's diaphragm appears slightly flattened, consistent with possible COPD in a smoker.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

Plan
I am prescribing combivent, two puffs twice a day, albuterol, two puffs as needed, and a prednisone taper pack. I am also prescribing albuterol for shortness of breath and prednisone to help with inflammation. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
