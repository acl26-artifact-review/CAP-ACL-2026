Chief Complaint
Left back pain and recurrent sore throats.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent left back pain, primarily on the left side, for several weeks. He describes the pain as often occurring with activities like lifting or bending. The patient also reports recurrent episodes of sore throat and pharyngitis, with four documented strep infections in the past five months. He reports that he is a smoker and is concerned about the impact on his family. The patient has noted that several individuals at school and in his community have been experiencing illness, including COVID-19. He denies any recent trauma to the back. He also reports a history of questioning the choices of individuals who engage in physical activities.

Findings
Physical examination reveals no rebound tenderness or guarding upon palpation of the back. Lung sounds are clear, with no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi. Heart sounds are normal, with no murmurs, rubs, or gallops. Neurological exam is unremarkable. Laboratory results are pending, including a lipid panel, UA, CMP, CBC, vitamin D, and TSH.

Assessment
The patient presents with persistent left back pain, likely related to musculoskeletal etiology, and a history of recurrent strep infections, potentially contributing to glomerular nephritis.

Plan
I am referring the patient to an ENT specialist for evaluation and consideration of tonsillectomy to address the recurrent strep infections. I am ordering a comprehensive laboratory panel, including a lipid panel, UA, CMP, CBC, vitamin D, and TSH, to further evaluate the patient's condition and assess for kidney function. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to monitor progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed. I will also discuss the importance of smoking cessation with the patient and offer resources for support.
