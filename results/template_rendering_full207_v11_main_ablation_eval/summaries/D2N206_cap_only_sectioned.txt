Chief Complaint
Right flank pain radiating to the groin.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right flank pain radiating to the groin, which began approximately 24 hours ago. He describes the pain as sharp and constant. He rates the pain as 7 out of 10. He reports a history of kidney stones and previous episodes of hematuria approximately 3 years ago. He denies nausea, vomiting, or fever. He reports taking ibuprofen 400mg every 6 hours as needed for the pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the right flank. The patient's abdomen is soft and non-distended. Vital signs are within normal limits. Urinalysis reveals microscopic hematuria.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a possible kidney stone. He has a history of kidney stones, increasing the likelihood of a recurrence.

Plan
Order a CT scan of the abdomen and pelvis to evaluate for a kidney stone. Continue ibuprofen 400mg every 6 hours as needed for pain. Instruct the patient to increase fluid intake to promote urinary output. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 24-48 hours to assess response to treatment.
