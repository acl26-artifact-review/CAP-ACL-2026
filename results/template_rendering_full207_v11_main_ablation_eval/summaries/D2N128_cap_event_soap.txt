S:
Carolyn reports osteoarthritis flares, primarily in her elbows, exacerbated by prolonged computer use. She experiences occasional numbness with prolonged typing and describes seeking relief by shaking her arms. She also reports a history of gout, with a recent episode three months ago, and stable psoriasis.

O:
Physical exam reveals edema and inflammation in the right olecranon, tenderness to palpation, and pain with range of motion of the right elbow. The abdomen is soft, and lower extremities are not edematous. Vital signs are within normal limits. Autoimmune panel and uric acid levels are within normal ranges. Cervical lymphadenopathy is absent.

A:
The primary problem is osteoarthritis of the right elbow and stable gout and psoriasis.

P:
Continue colchicine 0.6 mg twice daily as needed for gout flares. Order a right elbow x-ray. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain and inflammation. Continue topical steroid cream for scalp psoriasis. Schedule follow-up to review x-ray results and assess response to medication.
