Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 68-year-old male with a history of atrial fibrillation who presents with 3 days of right shoulder pain. The pain is constant, rated 7/10, and is exacerbated by movement. He reports that the pain started gradually and has worsened over the past few days. He denies any trauma. He reports that he has been unable to lift his right arm due to pain. He reports that he has been unable to sleep due to the pain. He reports that he has been unable to eat due to the pain. He reports that he has been unable to work due to the pain. He reports that he has been unable to drive due to the pain. He reports that he has been unable to care for himself due to the pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the right shoulder. Range of motion is limited by pain. No signs of inflammation. Neurological exam is intact.

Assessment
Subacromial bursitis

Plan
Continue NSAIDs. Physical therapy. Follow up in 2 weeks.
