Chief Complaint
The patient reports right knee pain following a basketball injury, characterized as achy and exacerbated by weight-bearing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports the knee pain began yesterday during a basketball game after a rebound attempt, describing a 'pop' sensation and subsequent swelling. He reports the swelling has decreased somewhat overnight, but he continues to favor the leg. He denies blood in stool. He reports taking tylenol yesterday for the pain, which provided some relief. He has been taking metformin 500mg daily, but has been skipping doses due to stomach upset. His blood sugars when taking the medication are approximately 150. He has been checking his blood pressure at home, reporting readings around 140/80, but has not been consistently monitoring it. He reports his blood pressures have been in the one forties to one fifties range on the top number and eighty on the bottom number. He has been eating anything he wants and has been avoiding strawberries due to the sugar content.

Findings
Physical examination reveals edema and mild erythema in the medial aspect of the right knee. Palpation elicits tenderness. Range of motion is limited due to pain. The patient's lungs sound clear to auscultation. The patient's heart rate is regular and auscultation reveals a grade 2 systolic ejection murmur. An X-ray of the right knee is normal, showing no fractures or bony abnormalities. The MRI is pending.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with an MCL strain, likely sustained secondary to the basketball injury. He also continues to have hypertension and suboptimal glycemic control.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. I am placing a knee immobilizer on the patient and instructing him to keep it on for the next two weeks. I am ordering an MRI of the right knee to assess the extent of the MCL injury. I am increasing the norvasc dosage. I am ordering a consultation with a nutritionist to address his dietary habits and improve his diabetes management. I am also scheduling a follow-up appointment in two weeks to monitor his progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed. I will provide a logbook for the patient to track his blood sugars daily.
