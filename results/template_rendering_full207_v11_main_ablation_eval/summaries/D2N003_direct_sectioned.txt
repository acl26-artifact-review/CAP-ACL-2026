Chief Complaint
Back pain, with associated urinary symptoms and dizziness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain that began approximately four days ago and is similar to pain experienced during previous kidney stone episodes two years ago. The pain is shifting, initially on the right side and now more prominent on the left. He describes intermittent episodes of dizziness, particularly with exertion, accompanied by mild abdominal discomfort. He reports blood in his urine, which he notes is difficult to detect but appears off-color. He has been diligent with his migraine medication (Imitrex) and acid reflux medication (Protonix). He has been ordering take-out meals due to difficulty with stairs and pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the right-hand and lower right quadrant. Blood pressure is elevated. Creatinine is slightly elevated. Abdominal x-rays show a possible kidney stone. The patient reports favoring his back when walking due to pain, and muscle aches.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a recurrence of kidney stones, complicated by possible obstruction, and exacerbated by musculoskeletal pain. He continues to experience migraine and acid reflux symptoms.

Plan
Order a CT scan of the abdomen and pelvis without contrast to evaluate for kidney stones. Prescribe ultram 50 mg every six hours as needed for pain. Instruct the patient to increase fluid intake and strain urine. Order a refill of protonix 40 mg daily. The nurse will schedule the CT scan and follow up in one to two days.
