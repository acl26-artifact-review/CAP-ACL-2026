Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent, dull chest pain for the past three weeks. The pain is described as a tightness and is not sharp. It is worse with exertion and relieved by rest. He denies shortness of breath, palpitations, or syncope. He reports a family history of coronary artery disease. He currently takes aspirin daily.

Findings
Blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, regular rhythm. Auscultation reveals normal heart sounds. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally.

Assessment
Possible angina pectoris. Rule out acute coronary syndrome.

Plan
Order serial cardiac enzymes, obtain a 12-lead ECG, and initiate aspirin 81 mg daily. Recommend cardiac stress test if initial evaluation is inconclusive. Schedule follow-up in 24-48 hours.
