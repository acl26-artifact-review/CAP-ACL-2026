Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent left shoulder pain for the past three weeks. He describes the pain as dull and aching, with some sharp episodes, particularly with overhead activities like painting. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with minimal relief. He states, 'It's really limiting my ability to do things around the house.'

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the anterior shoulder. Range of motion is limited by approximately 15 degrees in abduction and external rotation. There is no swelling or erythema. Neurological exam is intact, with full strength and sensation in the affected extremity.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a subacute rotator cuff strain. Differential diagnoses include adhesive capsulitis and bursitis.

Plan
I will order an MRI of the shoulder to evaluate the rotator cuff tendons. We will initiate physical therapy focusing on range of motion and strengthening exercises. I will prescribe ibuprofen 600mg every 6 hours as needed for pain.
