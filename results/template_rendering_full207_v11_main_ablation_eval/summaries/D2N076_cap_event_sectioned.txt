Chief Complaint
Concerned about a rare syndrome.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being diagnosed with a rare syndrome approximately two years ago. She notes intermittent episodes of reflex, particularly when feeding. She was previously taking cow's milk formula but switched to a ripple protein formula due to suspected sensitivities. She has been receiving speech therapy, occupational therapy, and physical therapy. The patient's twin has a similar presentation, but is doing well. The family lives in Dallas, Georgia. She reports a lot of wet diapers, around 8 to 10 per day, and drinks a lot. She has seen a geneticist, an orthopedic surgeon, an ophthalmologist, and an audiologist. She is currently scheduled to start speech therapy within the next couple of weeks. She describes her symptoms as a whirlwind of stuff, and has seen many specialists.

Findings
Physical exam reveals the patient to be well-nourished, slightly fussy, with small appearing eyes and mild hypotonia of the lower extremities. External genitalia are normal.

Assessment
The patient has been diagnosed with a rare syndrome. The patient has congenital anomalies of the kidney and urinary tract.

Plan
We will obtain a kidney ultrasound today. If there are abnormalities, we will determine next steps and future follow-up. The family lives in Dallas, Georgia, so her follow-up should be at the town center location. The doctor will call with the results.
