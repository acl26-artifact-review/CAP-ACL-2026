Chief Complaint
Right knee pain, exacerbated by swimming.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right knee pain that began approximately one month ago following an increase in swimming frequency. He describes using the breaststroke technique, which he believes contributes to the pain. The pain is most pronounced after a couple of laps and has worsened over time. He denies any specific injury or trauma. He reports that his chronic back pain has been well-controlled with current medication and therapy, and he is currently stable with his depression treatment. He reports sleeping well and eating well. He denies any feelings of harming himself or others.

Findings
Examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation of the right knee. Range of motion is limited by pain. The patient reports pain with patellar inhibition. X-rays demonstrate normal findings. The patient's heart rhythm is regular, lungs are clear to auscultation, and there is no palpable adenopathy. Thyroid examination is unremarkable. Grip strength is equal bilaterally. No swelling is noted in the knee joint.

Assessment
The patient presents with chondromalacia of the patella, likely secondary to overuse and the breaststroke swimming technique.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 600 milligrams four times daily with food for symptomatic relief. Recommend straight leg raising exercises to strengthen the quadriceps. Advise the patient to avoid lunges and the breaststroke swimming technique until the pain subsides. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess response to treatment. Continue current depression medication and therapy.
