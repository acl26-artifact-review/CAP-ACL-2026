Chief Complaint
To discuss a breast reduction consultation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of macromastia, dating back approximately six to seven years, and expresses a desire for breast reduction. She has attempted nonsurgical treatments with minimal relief. She experiences back and neck pain, often attributed to her breasts, particularly when working at her desk. She reports bra strap indentations and typically wears a 34E sports bra due to comfort and cost considerations. The patient has no children currently but is considering starting a family in the future. She takes propranolol for migraines and has lost 10 pounds recently, aiming to lose an additional 20. Her mother was diagnosed with breast cancer at age 40, and she began mammograms at age 30. She occasionally consumes wine and is currently studying psychology.

Findings
Physical examination reveals asymmetrical breasts with significant ptosis bilaterally. There is no nipple retraction or discharge palpably. No breast tenderness, masses, or axillary lymphadenopathy is present. Measurements indicate a significant discrepancy between the breasts, with one breast being lower than the other. The patient is currently wearing a C cup bra.

Assessment
The patient presents with symptomatic macromastia, a clear indication for breast reduction.

Plan
We will submit documentation to the insurance company for approval. I will take photographs of the patient today. Following approval, we will schedule a surgical consultation and discuss a suitable date. Post-operatively, the patient will experience a period of activity restrictions, including no heavy lifting for six weeks and limited driving for two to three weeks. Nipple sensation is typically preserved after breast reduction, although minor alterations are possible. The patient will receive detailed post-operative instructions regarding wound care and scar management.
