Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and concern about injections.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports they do not want to be as active as they were previously, describing the pain as aching and sharp. They are concerned about risks associated with an injection. The patient reports pain in the left hand, which began approximately three weeks ago after lifting a box of books. The patient has a history of back pain and his father has spine conditions. He tried to take some ibuprofen and Tylenol for the pain. The patient denies numbness, tingling, or weakness. He does not smoke.

Findings
Examination reveals lumbar paravertebral tenderness. The patient reports midline tenderness to palpation. There is no reported numbness, tingling, or weakness.

Assessment
The patient has radiculopathy and nerve impingement.

Plan
The doctor will provide the patient with paperwork. A spinal injection is recommended. A strong NSAID is prescribed. A corticosteroid is prescribed. Fluoroscopy is ordered.
