Chief Complaint
Reports back pain on the right side and hematuria.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing back pain on their right side for approximately one week, with the pain having migrated down to their right lower side. They also report seeing blood in their urine. The pain is described as constant. They took ibuprofen, which provided some relief, but the pain persists. They report feeling slightly nauseated. The patient's father had kidney stones. They did not participate in the Easter egg hunt due to the pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the abdomen, with localized pain on the right flank. Vital signs are within normal limits. A CT scan revealed a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter, with no evidence of hydronephrosis.

Assessment
The patient has a kidney stone in the proximal right ureter.

Plan
I am recommending that you increase your fluid intake to help facilitate the passage of the stone. I am prescribing ibuprofen 800 mg as needed for pain. I am also ordering a urine culture and a BMP to assess for infection. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately one to two weeks to assess your progress and discuss further treatment options, including lithotripsy, if necessary.
