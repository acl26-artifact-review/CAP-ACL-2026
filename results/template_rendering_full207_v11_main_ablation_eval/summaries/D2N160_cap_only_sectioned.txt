Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the right elbow, exacerbated by flexion and extension, and accompanied by radiating pain down the forearm. He states the pain began approximately one week ago following a collision while playing lacrosse with his children. He reports trying Advil, which provided some relief. The patient denies chest pain and reports a history of hypertension and takes lisinopril and metformin daily, though he occasionally forgets to take his medications. He was playing with his children when he was hit.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the right elbow. Range of motion is limited due to pain. A grade 2 murmur is auscultated. The patient's blood sugar is between eighty and one hundred.

Assessment
The patient has a grade 2 murmur and right elbow pain, likely secondary to a musculoskeletal injury.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs. Initiate physical therapy. Continue lisinopril and metformin as prescribed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
