Chief Complaint
Follow-up of chronic problems, primarily osteoarthritis flare-ups in the elbows.

History of Present Illness
Carolyn is a 49-year-old female presenting for follow-up of her chronic problems. She reports osteoarthritis flare-ups, particularly in her elbows, exacerbated by prolonged computer use and sitting at her desk. She describes increased stiffness and pain in both elbows, with the right elbow being more affected. She also reports occasional numbness in her hands when typing for extended periods, which she manages with frequent arm shaking. She has a history of gout, with a recent episode three months ago, controlled with medication. Her psoriasis is currently under control with topical steroid cream. She reports no other joint pain or recent flare-ups of gout.

Findings
Physical exam reveals edema and inflammation in the right olecranon, with tenderness and effusion. Palpation of the right elbow elicits pain. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. Cervical lymph nodes are unremarkable. Vital signs are stable. The autoimmune panel and uric acid levels are within normal limits. A right elbow x-ray has been ordered.

Assessment
The patient's primary concern is osteoarthritis flare-ups in her elbows, likely exacerbated by prolonged static postures. Her gout is currently well-controlled. Her psoriasis is stable.

Plan
Continue colchicine 0.6 mg twice daily as needed for gout flare-ups. Order a right elbow x-ray to evaluate the osteoarthritis. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain and inflammation. Follow up on the x-ray results and adjust management as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust the plan.
