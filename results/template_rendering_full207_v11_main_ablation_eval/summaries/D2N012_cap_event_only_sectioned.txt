Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing progressive left shoulder pain over the past six weeks. The pain is exacerbated by overhead activities and when lying on the affected side. He denies any recent trauma or injury. He reports using acetaminophen for partial relief. He states, 'It's been getting worse and worse.'

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. Vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
I'm ordering shoulder radiographs to evaluate for rotator cuff pathology. I'm also recommending physical therapy to address the shoulder pain and improve range of motion. We will continue the patient's current acetaminophen regimen as needed. I'll schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
