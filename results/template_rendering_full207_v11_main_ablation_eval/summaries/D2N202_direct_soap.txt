S:
The patient reports right shoulder pain that began three weeks ago following a fall while painting. He describes the pain as intermittent and rates it as 6/10 without medication, decreasing to 4/10 with ibuprofen. He initially experienced numbness and tingling, which has resolved. He denies chest pain, dyspnea, or syncope. He reports icing the arm and taking Advil after the fall.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the right shoulder with tenderness to the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds. Sensation and touch are equal and bilateral. Right shoulder x-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy, likely secondary to trauma. No acute fracture is present.

P:
Order an MRI of the right shoulder. Refer to physical therapy for a 6-8 week course. Recommend continued use of ibuprofen as tolerated. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to evaluate progress and consider further treatment options, such as a steroid injection if needed.
