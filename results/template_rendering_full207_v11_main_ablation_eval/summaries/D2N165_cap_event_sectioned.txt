Chief Complaint
The patient reports knee pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports knee pain for approximately six months, initially with no identified trauma. He describes the pain as deep and achy, often felt behind the kneecaps, and occasionally accompanied by a creaking sensation. The pain worsens with activities such as getting from a seated position to standing and with stair climbing. He reports that rest provides some relief. He has previously tried ice and ace bandages and taken ibuprofen and aleve, but prefers to avoid medications. He enjoys running and has been running five to six miles a day, but now can only manage half a mile before experiencing pain. He typically runs outdoors, but also uses a treadmill during the winter. He has never broken a bone, although he did break his arm in sixth grade. He denies any prior knee injuries. The patient reports no swelling or redness in his knees.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal gait. Strength is 3/5 for abduction of the bilateral legs. Palpation of the retinacular retinaculum is tender bilaterally. The patellar and achilles reflexes are symmetrical. A positive patellar grind test is noted. X-rays show no fractures or osteoarthritis.

Assessment
The patient has patellofemoral pain syndrome.

Plan
The patient will receive physical therapy to address lower extremity strength and mobility and correct any incorrect running mechanics. The patient will take ibuprofen as needed for pain relief. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
