Chief Complaint
Reports back pain, particularly on the left.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing back pain, primarily on the left side, for several weeks. He describes the pain as intermittent, sometimes sharp, sometimes dull, and occasionally moving. He notes that the pain is not always consistent, sometimes present, sometimes absent. He also reports increased fatigue and an uncharacteristic increase in urination at night, necessitating more frequent trips to the restroom. He denies any recent trauma or injury. He reports having experienced similar episodes in the past, including frequent sore throats and 'BFS' (which he doesn't fully understand). He reports riding a bicycle occasionally. He mentions his brother smokes, and he has been told not to smoke, but he doesn't listen. He reports that school is going well, though he struggles with his physics class.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no tenderness to palpation of the back, normal range of motion, and no palpable lymphadenopathy. Lung sounds are clear to auscultation. Heart sounds are regular with no murmurs. The patient reports no pain with abdominal palpation. Examination of the throat reveals no erythema or swelling. The patient's tonsils are midline.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is concerning for possible musculoskeletal back pain, potentially exacerbated by recurrent strep throat episodes, which may be contributing to glomerular nephritis. Further investigation is warranted to rule out these possibilities.

Plan
I am ordering a CT scan to evaluate the back pain and to investigate the recurrent strep throat episodes. I am also referring the patient to an ENT specialist to consider a tonsillectomy if indicated. I am ordering a comprehensive laboratory panel including a lipid panel, UA, CMP, CBC, and Vitamin D to assess his overall health and identify any abnormalities. I have scheduled a follow-up appointment in one week to review the results and discuss further management strategies. The patient should return to the clinic if symptoms persist or worsen.
