Chief Complaint
Right finger pain

History of Present Illness
Dragon reports being a 48-year-old female presenting with right hand index finger pain. He has been taking ibuprofen but continues to experience significant pain. He reports recent constipation and uses miralax for management. He had his appendix removed at age 18. He describes tenderness over the distal phalanx of his index finger and reports difficulty with flexion. He believes he sustained a contusion.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness over the distal phalanx of the index finger. X-rays demonstrate no fractures.

Assessment
The diagnosis is a right hand index finger contusion.

Plan
Order an aluminum foam splint and motrin. Arrange follow-up as needed.
