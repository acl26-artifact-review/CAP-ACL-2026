Chief Complaint
Right foot pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his right foot following a fall while yard working yesterday, where he dropped a landscape brick on his foot. He previously injured his ankle twenty years ago, which healed with a cast, but occasionally experiences soreness and swelling. He also reports soreness in his other ankle and has had surgery on that ankle, occasionally experiencing instability. He reported the incident to a few names and sought medical attention. He has not taken any medications for the pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bruising to the right foot, associated swelling, and tenderness to palpation at the midfoot. Capillary refill is brisk in less than three seconds. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong and bounding. Neurological exam reveals intact motor and sensation. X-ray demonstrates dorsal displacement of the base of the second metatarsal with a three millimeter separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and presence of bony fragments.

Assessment
The patient's right foot pain is due to a Lisfranc fracture, involving the second metatarsal bone and the top of the foot. This requires surgical intervention to restore bone alignment and ligament stability.

Plan
An MRI will be ordered to assess ligament injuries. An outpatient surgery is planned for tomorrow, followed by discharge that evening. A follow-up appointment will be scheduled in 24 hours post-procedure, and again in two weeks. The patient will receive a cast and crutches, with weight-bearing restrictions for six to eight weeks, advancing ambulation gradually based on healing and tolerance. The surgeon will use plates and screws to align the bones and ligaments.
