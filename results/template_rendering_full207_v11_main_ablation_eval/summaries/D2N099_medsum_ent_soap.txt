O:

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a viral upper respiratory infection, likely a sinus infection. There is no evidence of acute distress or significant findings. The clinician suspects a viral etiology, considering the shared exposure and the patient's symptoms.

P:
The clinician recommends keeping the patient well-hydrated and resting. The clinician suggests monitoring for a barking cough, which would indicate a more severe infection. If a barking cough develops, the clinician recommends administering half a teaspoon of the patient's sister's medication. The clinician recommends Zarbee's for the stuffy nose. The clinician recommends using head & shoulders, selsun blue, or nizoral ad on the cradle cap. The clinician recommends using a soft brush and baby oil to remove the cradle cap scales. The clinician recommends using honey as a substitute for agave. The clinician recommends following up in one week or sooner if symptoms worsen.
