S:
Randy has been feeling sick for several days, experiencing a runny nose and stuffiness. He also reports irritability and a low-grade fever. The patient's mother has been using saline mist and a humidifier. He has been given Tylenol for fever and has been experiencing worsening cradle cap.

O:
Physical exam reveals a temperature of 100.3°F. The patient's skin shows worsening cradle cap.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a viral upper respiratory infection. The worsening cradle cap is noted.

P:
Continue saline mist and humidifier use. Administer Tylenol as needed for fever. Recommend using a gentle shampoo like Head & Shoulders or Selsun Blue for the cradle cap. Monitor for a barking cough, which would indicate a more severe infection; if present, administer half a teaspoon of the sister's medication for five days.
