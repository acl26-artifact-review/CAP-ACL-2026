Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a knee injury sustained while skiing on Saturday during a snowfall. He describes a 'pop' sensation and immediate pain following a cartwheel-like fall. He has been taking Advil and icing the knee. He denies prior knee injuries. The patient reports some pain with flexion and extension, and limited range of motion. He also reports a bruised shoulder following the fall.

Findings
Vitals: Temperature 97.9°F, Heart Rate 64 bpm, Respiratory Rate 16 breaths/min, Blood Pressure 110/74 mmHg, Oxygen Saturation 98%. Examination reveals edema and ecchymosis around the right knee. Pain to palpation. Positive pain with flexion and extension. Range of motion is limited but within normal limits. The patient's right shoulder demonstrates bruising.

Assessment
Right knee contusion.

Plan
Order a right shoulder x-ray. Advise rest, ice, and elevation. Prescribe ibuprofen 650mg every 6-8 hours as needed for pain. Schedule follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist or do not improve. Refer to physical therapy as appropriate.
