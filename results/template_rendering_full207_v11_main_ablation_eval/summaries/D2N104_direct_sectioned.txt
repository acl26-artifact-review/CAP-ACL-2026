Chief Complaint
Ongoing management of psoriatic arthritis.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports ongoing management of psoriatic arthritis. She reports decreasing her prednisone dose and is currently on one dose, which appears to be sufficient. She experienced occasional extra doses due to increased pain in her feet, particularly on days with increased activity. She also reports experiencing adverse effects from a recent COVID vaccine, including significant pain that resolved with a second dose. She notes bruising, which she attributes to prednisone, and reports that these appear without her noticing.

Findings
Physical exam reveals good range of motion in the toes and nails. Examination reveals psoriatic nail changes. No acute findings were noted.

Assessment
Psoriatic arthritis.

Plan
Continue methotrexate therapy. Discontinue prednisone as previously discussed, monitoring for any flares. Schedule blood work for the next visit. Follow up in three months.
