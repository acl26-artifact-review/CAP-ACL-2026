Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a twisting injury sustained while skiing last weekend. He describes the injury as a 'stretch' followed by swelling. He was able to get out of the water by himself but was very sore to climb up and any kind of squatting or bending is really pretty sore. He denies any popping sensation at the time of injury. He has taken Aleve twice a day and applied a cold pack, which has provided some relief. He has no prior knee injuries. He is a frequent water skier and enjoys the lake.

Findings
Physical exam reveals right knee ecchymosis and edema with an effusion. Range of motion is decreased. Lachman's test and varus/valgus stress tests are negative. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are present. Vital signs: Blood pressure 120/77 mmHg. Heart and lungs are unremarkable. Neck exam is negative for lymphadenopathy.

Assessment
Based on the clinical presentation and physical exam findings, the patient likely has a medial collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
I am ordering an x-ray of the right knee to rule out fracture. I am also prescribing meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain and inflammation. I will refer you to physical therapy to strengthen the muscles around your knee. I recommend resting your knee and avoiding activities that exacerbate the pain. Please follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
