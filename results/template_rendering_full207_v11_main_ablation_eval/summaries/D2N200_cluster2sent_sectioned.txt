Chief Complaint
Reports headaches and elevated blood pressure.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of high blood pressure for an extended period, previously prescribed chlorthalidone which he discontinued due to stomach discomfort. He experiences headaches, often described as throbbing, located on the top of his head, and reports occasional dizziness and blurred vision associated with these headaches. He occasionally takes Tylenol for headache relief. He has been experiencing these symptoms for an extended period.

Findings
Physical examination revealed blood pressure readings consistently above 170/98, with bilateral pitting edema noted on the upper extremities and lower extremities (1+). Heart rate was within normal limits, and oxygen saturation was adequate. Lung auscultation revealed a grade 2 systolic ejection murmur, which was present last year. No jugular venous distention or carotid bruits were detected.

Assessment
The patient presents with uncontrolled hypertension and associated edema.

Plan
The patient will continue his current metformin regimen, increasing the dosage to 1500 mg twice daily. Blood pressure will be monitored three times per week at home using a recommended monitor. A nutritionist consultation will be arranged to discuss dietary modifications, focusing on reducing sodium intake. Follow-up is scheduled in one month to assess blood pressure control and medication effectiveness.
