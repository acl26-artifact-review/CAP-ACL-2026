S:
The patient reports back pain that began six days ago after walking approximately 30-60 minutes daily. He describes the pain as a spasm and notes it is present equally on both sides. He has experienced some nasal congestion related to seasonal allergies. He reports taking ibuprofen and applying heat to the area, but the pain persists. He has been dieting with his boyfriend and reports good blood pressure readings at home.

O:
The patient's blood pressure is well-controlled at the office visit. Heart rate is within normal limits. Physical exam reveals mild systolic ejection murmur, grade II/6. Lumbar spine x-ray shows normal bony alignment and no fracture. Labs are unremarkable with no elevated white blood cell count or signs of infection. Palpation of the right lateral lumbar spine reveals tenderness.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a lumbar strain, possibly related to recent exercise or lifting. There is no evidence of acute pathology.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15 mg PO once daily for pain. Refer to physical therapy for strengthening exercises. Continue lisinopril 10 mg PO daily. Order lisinopril refill. Schedule follow-up in 2 weeks to assess response to treatment.
