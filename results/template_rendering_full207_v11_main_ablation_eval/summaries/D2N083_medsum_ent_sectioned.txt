Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began approximately four days ago, following increased pottery activity. He describes the pain as primarily on the medial aspect of the elbow and rates it as 6/10. The pain does not radiate, but can extend into the forearm. Ibuprofen provides mild relief. He denies any prior elbow injuries or trauma. He reports difficulty with lifting and performing activities due to the pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate tenderness to palpation over the medial epicondyle of the right elbow. Range of motion is limited by pain. Wrist pronation and supination elicit pain. X-ray of the right elbow demonstrates no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
Based on the clinical presentation and examination findings, the patient is diagnosed with medial epicondylitis.

Plan
I am ordering a sling to be worn during the day. I am also recommending ice packs applied to the elbow for 20 minutes, three times daily. The patient will take ibuprofen 600 mg every 6 hours as needed for a period of one week. I would like to schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess progress.
