S:
The patient reports a low back pain that started approximately two weeks ago following a lifting incident involving a box of books. He describes the pain as achy and sharp in his low back, and reports that it now radiates down his left leg. He states that sitting is usually okay, but bending forward causes pain. He denies numbness or tingling, or any weakness. He reports no bowel or bladder control issues. He has tried ibuprofen and Tylenol, which provide some relief.

O:
Blood pressure is 130/70 mmHg. Respiratory rate is 18 breaths per minute. Heart rate is regular and 72 beats per minute. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Musculoskeletal exam reveals tenderness at L4-L5 with right paravertebral tenderness. Straight leg raise test is positive. Range of motion is full in all directions. No overlying redness or bruising. Neurological exam reveals intact sensation and motor strength in the lower extremities. MRI reveals a disk desiccation, disk bulge with paracentral disk herniation resulting in moderate right neuroforaminal stenosis.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with lumbar radiculopathy secondary to a herniated L4-L5 disc. The radiculopathy is causing nerve impingement, resulting in pain radiating down the left leg.

P:
I recommend a spinal injection with a strong NSAID and corticosteroid under fluoroscopy to reduce inflammation and provide pain relief. I will schedule this procedure today. I will provide the patient with written instructions and a discussion of potential risks and benefits.
