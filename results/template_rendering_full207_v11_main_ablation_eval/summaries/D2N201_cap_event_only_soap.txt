S:
The patient reports tingling in their left leg and occasional sharp pain in their lower back. He states the pain started approximately two weeks ago after lifting a heavy box at work. He denies any fever or recent trauma.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the lumbar paraspinal muscles. Straight leg raise test is negative bilaterally. Reflexes are 2+ bilaterally.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a lumbar disc herniation.

P:
A prescription for a muscle relaxant is ordered. A corticosteroid injection is ordered to the lumbar region. Follow up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
