Chief Complaint
Increased thirst and frequent urination

History of Present Illness
The patient reports increasing thirst and frequent urination over the past month, along with increased nighttime urination. He also reports increased fatigue. He denies polyuria, polydipsia, or polyphagia. He reports a family history of type 2 diabetes. He states he has been eating more sugary snacks lately. He reports feeling willpower is low.

Findings
Blood glucose level 288 mg/dL. Hemoglobin A1c 8.2%.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and laboratory findings are consistent with type 2 diabetes mellitus.

Plan
Order a fasting blood glucose test. Initiate metformin 500mg twice daily. Schedule follow-up in 4 weeks to assess response to treatment and adjust medication as needed. Counsel the patient on lifestyle modifications including diet and exercise.
