Chief Complaint
Right hand numbness and tingling, primarily affecting the thumb, index, and middle fingers.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing numbness and tingling in his right hand for approximately six months, with the symptoms becoming more persistent over the past month. The numbness and tingling are most prominent when typing on the keyboard, particularly with the pain being exacerbated when gripping the keyboard. Ibuprofen and avoiding keyboard use provide some relief. Pain is also experienced with wrist extension and flexion, and when moving the wrist. The patient denies any recent trauma or other neurological symptoms. He reports no nighttime awakenings due to the symptoms.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the right wrist. Grip strength is reduced on the right hand. Tinel's sign is positive over the right wrist. The patient grimaces when the wrist is tapped, indicating pain. Examination reveals pain on wrist extension and flexion.

Assessment
Based on the patient's symptoms, physical examination findings, and the positive Tinel's sign, carpal tunnel syndrome is the most likely diagnosis.

Plan
I am prescribing a wrist splint to wear at night and continue with ibuprofen up to 600 milligrams four times daily. I am ordering an EMG to further evaluate the nerve conduction studies in the right wrist.
