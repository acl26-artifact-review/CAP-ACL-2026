Chief Complaint
Chronic abdominal pain and alternating constipation and diarrhea.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports chronic abdominal pain, primarily in the mid-abdomen, for approximately two years. He initially sought care at an urgent care facility three months ago, where a CT scan revealed no abnormalities. The patient experiences alternating episodes of constipation and diarrhea, with the diarrhea often triggered by specific foods, such as creamy pasta sauces. He reports a stable weight and no family history of gastrointestinal disorders. He currently takes Crestor and olmesartan, and occasionally uses Tylenol for pain. He consumes a couple of beers after working in the yard on Saturdays and denies any history of heavy alcohol or drug use. His primary care physician noted slightly elevated liver enzymes four months ago, which are scheduled to be rechecked in six months.

Findings
Physical examination revealed a slight hepatomegaly and increased stool burden in the colon. Lung sounds were clear bilaterally, and heart sounds were normal with no murmurs. The patient's eyes and skin did not show any signs of jaundice. Liver enzymes were slightly elevated.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with a constellation of gastrointestinal symptoms, including constipation, diarrhea, and mild hepatomegaly. Further investigation is warranted to determine the underlying etiology.

Plan
Order a liver ultrasound and blood work to evaluate liver function. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to review test results and assess symptom management. Recommend a fiber supplement to address constipation. Counsel the patient to increase water intake and avoid trigger foods for diarrhea. Contact the patient's primary care physician to discuss potential adjustments to his Crestor medication and schedule a follow-up appointment.
