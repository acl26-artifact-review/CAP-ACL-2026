Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain following a lacrosse injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began approximately one week ago after being hit with a lacrosse stick while playing with his children. He describes the pain as sharp and present with any movement. He denies any recent trauma aside from the impact. He tried taking Advil, which provided some relief. He mentions his son was 'rough' with him during the incident. He has been avoiding playing lacrosse with his children.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the olecranon area of the posterior elbow, mild pain with flexion and extension, and normal range of motion. An x-ray revealed no fracture or bony abnormality. Blood pressure was 150/90 mmHg. The heart exam revealed a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with an elbow contusion and inflammation.

Plan
Prescribe 800 mg ibuprofen twice daily for pain and inflammation. Recommend icing the elbow three times daily for 20 minutes. Advise the patient to avoid playing lacrosse with his children for two weeks to allow for healing. Continue current lisinopril 20 mg daily for hypertension. Order an A1C test to assess diabetes control. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist.
