S:
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately three weeks, with difficulty reaching and lifting objects. He reports taking two extra strength Tylenol every six to eight hours, which provides some relief. He denies numbness or tingling in his arms or hands. He describes renovating his basement and feeling it took a toll on him.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder with tenderness to palpation of the greater tuberosity. Capillary refill is less than three seconds. Sensation is intact to light touch. Shoulder x-ray shows no fractures.

A:
The patient's left shoulder pain is most likely due to a rotator cuff tendinopathy. The MRI will help rule out other causes.

P:
Order an MRI of the left shoulder. Refer to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. Continue Tylenol as needed. Consider a steroid injection if symptoms do not improve. Schedule a follow-up visit in four weeks.
