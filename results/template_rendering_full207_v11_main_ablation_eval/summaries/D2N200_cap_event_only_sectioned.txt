Chief Complaint
Reports dizziness and elevated blood pressure.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent dizziness, particularly when standing up quickly. He also states his blood pressure has been elevated, as measured at home. He takes chlorthalidone and tylenol. He denies chest pain, shortness of breath, or recent trauma.

Findings
Blood pressure is 152/92 mmHg. Patient reports dizziness upon standing. Physical exam is unremarkable except for reported symptoms.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms of dizziness and elevated blood pressure are concerning for hypertension. Further evaluation is warranted.

Plan
The doctor will monitor the patient's blood pressure three times a week. The doctor will continue the patient's current medication regimen.
