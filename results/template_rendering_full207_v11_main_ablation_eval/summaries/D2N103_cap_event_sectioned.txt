Chief Complaint
Status post mitral valve repair, ongoing management of atrial fibrillation and related symptoms.

History of Present Illness
Ms. Sanchez presents for follow-up care post mitral valve repair performed on 8/3/2020. She reports feeling generally better since surgery, though she continues to experience occasional chest discomfort and shortness of breath, particularly with exertion. She describes these episodes as lasting a few minutes and resolving on their own, prompting her to slow down and rest. She is currently taking coumadin 4mg daily, lasix 4mg daily, and atenolol 50mg daily. She reports occasional mild irritability with the atenolol, but has adapted to it. She is diligent about taking her medications, utilizing a reminder app. She notes intermittent episodes of atrial fibrillation with irregular rhythm, which she has been managing with medication and lifestyle modifications. She reports a history of mitral regurgitation and atrial fibrillation prior to surgery.

Findings
Physical exam reveals regular rhythm with irregularly irregular heart sounds. Trace peripheral edema is noted bilaterally. Lung sounds are clear with reduced breath sounds. The patient's head and neck are unremarkable. No JVD detected. The patient's abdomen is soft and non-tender.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is atrial fibrillation with a controlled ventricular response post-mitral valve repair. She also presents with intermittent chest discomfort and shortness of breath, likely related to the underlying atrial fibrillation and potential residual cardiac issues. She is managing her medications effectively.

Plan
Continue current medications: coumadin 4mg daily, lasix 4mg daily, and atenolol 50mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 6-9 months to reassess atrial fibrillation control and overall cardiac status. Advise the patient to continue monitoring for chest pain and shortness of breath and to report any significant changes immediately. Encourage continued adherence to medication regimen and lifestyle modifications, including regular exercise as tolerated.
