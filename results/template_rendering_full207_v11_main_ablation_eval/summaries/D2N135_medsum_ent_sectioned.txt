Chief Complaint
Evaluation of a high blood sugar.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing high blood sugar, initially with increased sweets consumption over the holiday season. She began to feel light-headed and dizzy approximately one week ago, accompanied by visual disturbances. She reports that she has been taking protonix as prescribed and that it has been helping with her reflux. She has a history of atrial fibrillation, which is only exacerbated by anxiety. The patient denies fever, chills, chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, or burning during urination. She does have osteoarthritis and reports occasional muscle aches. She notes that her heart rate increases when she feels anxious.

Findings
Physical exam reveals the patient appears in no distress. Thyroid is slightly enlarged but non-tender. No carotid bruits are auscultated. Heart rate is irregular and rhythm is abnormal. Lungs are clear to auscultation. Abdomen is soft and non-tender. No edema in the lower extremities is noted. Blood sugar and hemoglobin A1c are elevated.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and laboratory findings are consistent with diabetes mellitus. Further investigation is warranted to determine the type of diabetes and guide appropriate management.

Plan
Order a complete metabolic panel and initiate metformin 500 mg twice daily. Order a refill on metoprolol 25 mg twice daily. The nurse will provide education on blood sugar monitoring and glucometer use. Follow up in four weeks or sooner if symptoms persist. Schedule a follow-up appointment to discuss autoimmune lab results and potential need for insulin therapy.
