Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and numbness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left hand pain and numbness, which began approximately one week ago following a fall while using a rake. He describes the pain as sharp and aching, with numbness primarily in his thumb and index finger. He also reports some numbness in his hand. The patient denies any recent trauma to the arm. He reports a previous tennis injury and has been taking flonase for allergies.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the dorsum of the left hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. There is no swelling or erythema. Strength is intact. The patient reports numbness and tingling in the affected area.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are most consistent with a contusion and possible nerve entrapment of the median and ulnar nerves in the left hand.

Plan
Order wrist and hand radiographs to rule out fracture. Prescribe motrin for pain relief. Recommend ice packs and elevation. Follow up in one week to assess response to treatment.
