Chief Complaint
Headache

History of Present Illness
Carolyn reports a headache that started approximately one week ago, described as a dull pain in the back of her head. She experiences flushing in her ears and occasional dizziness with the headaches. She has taken Tylenol and Advil, but the headaches persist and return. She attributes the headaches to increased stress levels. She has no prior history of severe headaches and denies any recent trauma to the head. She has not been checking her blood sugars and has not been exercising regularly in the past week due to the stress. She is still working on the computer for work.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild posterior paraspinal muscular tenderness in the cervical spine and bilateral trapezius musculature. Extraocular movements are intact. The heart exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is unchanged from her previous exam. The patient's vital signs are unremarkable. The funduscopic exam shows no papilledema.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a tension-type headache, likely exacerbated by stress, muscle tension, and possibly related to prolonged computer use. There is no evidence of acute neurological pathology.

Plan
Prescribe flexeril 5 mg three times daily for muscle relaxation. Continue Tylenol as needed for pain. Order an A1c and a CBC and a chem-12 to monitor diabetes and overall health. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment. Encourage regular exercise and stress management techniques. Advise the patient to call sooner if the headache worsens or becomes more severe, or if she develops a fever.
