Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right knee pain for the past three months, worsening with activity and stairs. He denies a specific injury. He reports using ibuprofen with limited relief. He states the pain is most noticeable in the morning and improves with movement. He denies any swelling or warmth.

Findings
The patient's right knee demonstrates mild tenderness to palpation over the medial joint line. Range of motion is limited by pain, with decreased flexion and extension compared to the left knee. No effusion is palpable. Vitals are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with medial compartment osteoarthritis of the right knee.

Plan
Prescribe naproxen 500mg twice daily for pain management. Recommend physical therapy for range of motion and strengthening exercises. Schedule follow-up in 6 weeks to assess response to treatment.
