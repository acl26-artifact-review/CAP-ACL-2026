Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain and swelling following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports falling on icy pavement yesterday, resulting in right ankle pain and swelling. He initially could not walk and required assistance. He has been elevating and icing the ankle. Ibuprofen has provided some relief. He denies prior ankle injuries but recalls playing soccer for many years, including community league play. His wife accompanies him to appointments. He has no numbness in the foot. He has no other questions.

Findings
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis and swelling over the lateral malleolus. Palpation elicits tenderness. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong. Neurovascular exam is intact. X-ray shows no fracture.

Assessment
Right ankle sprain.

Plan
Prescribe an aircast and crutches for weight-bearing. Advise elevation and ice. Continue ibuprofen as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress and consider return to soccer activity.
