Chief Complaint
Reports feeling out of sorts lately, lack of energy, and lightheadedness.

History of Present Illness
Brian is a 58-year-old male with a past medical history of congestive heart failure and hypertension. He presents today for follow-up of his chronic problems. He reports feeling 'out of sorts' for about five weeks, primarily since labor day, and describes a lack of energy and occasional lightheadedness. He notes that this is exacerbated by exertion. He admits to struggling with healthy food intake due to construction on his kitchen over labor day weekend, leading to eating out more often. He reports slight cramps that resolve within an hour. He denies fever, chills, nausea, or diarrhea. He has been taking his medications consistently and monitors his blood pressure regularly with his wife's assistance. He reports seasonal changes as a contributing factor. He has had heart failure for a year and has been managing it well with medication. He suspects a cold.

Findings
Physical exam reveals jugular venous distention to approximately 8 centimeters, pitting edema 1+ in the lower extremities, and a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur. Lung exam reveals fine crackles at the bases bilaterally. Chest x-ray shows some fluid in the lungs. An echocardiogram performed four months ago showed reduced heart pumping function and a leaky mitral valve. Blood pressure is stable.

Assessment
The patient has congestive heart failure, likely exacerbated by fluid retention, and hypertension. He also presents with symptoms suggestive of a possible viral upper respiratory infection.

Plan
Increase lasix dose to 80 mg once daily. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Order an echocardiogram. Instruct the patient to monitor blood pressure at home and report elevated readings. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist. The doctor will perform a quick physical exam and review test results.
