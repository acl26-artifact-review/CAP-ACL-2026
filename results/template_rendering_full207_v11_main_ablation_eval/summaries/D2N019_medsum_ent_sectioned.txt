Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain following a recent move.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began yesterday while moving boxes from a truck into the house. He describes a popping sensation and strain while lifting a box and placing it on a shelf. He has been applying ice to the area and taking ibuprofen, but the pain persists and throbs upon removal of the ice. The patient reports no other relevant medical history. He reports a history of anaphylaxis managed with an epinephrine auto-injector.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral epicondyle of the right elbow, mild swelling, and some redness. Range of motion is limited due to pain with flexion and extension. Dorsal forearm pain is present. X-ray of the right elbow demonstrates normal bony alignment and no fracture. There is some swelling noted on the lateral elbow.

Assessment
Lateral epicondylitis, likely secondary to the recent forceful movement.

Plan
I am prescribing ibuprofen 800 milligrams every six hours as needed for pain relief. I am also providing a sling for comfort and support. Follow up in approximately two weeks if symptoms do not improve, or if you experience any worsening. Please call the office if you have any questions or concerns.
