Chief Complaint
Difficulty swallowing and chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty swallowing certain foods for several weeks, accompanied by pain when swallowing, particularly larger pieces. He attributes this to increased stress related to a recent move and a diet consisting of pizza and burgers. He has gained approximately 10 pounds over the past month. He reports occasional epigastric pain. He denies blood in his stool or dark, tarry stools. He also reports increased alcohol consumption.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal vital signs, including blood pressure of 130/70 mmHg and oxygen saturation of 100%. The patient's throat appears normal, with no obvious ulcers or thrush. The abdomen is nondistended, with active bowel sounds. Murphy's sign is negative. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. The patient's reflexes are normal. A barium swallow revealed mild narrowing in the mid and lower portions of the esophagus, suggestive of esophagitis.

Assessment
Acute esophagitis.

Plan
Prescribe proton pump inhibitor (PPI) - 40 mg once daily, and carafate - 1 gram four times daily for one month. Schedule an upper endoscopy for follow-up. Advise the patient to avoid citrus fruits, spicy foods, caffeine, and alcohol. Recommend eating slowly and chewing food thoroughly. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week.
