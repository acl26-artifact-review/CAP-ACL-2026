Chief Complaint
Larry reports chronic lumbar back pain for approximately two years, with associated radicular symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient describes a gradual onset of back pain following a weekend of furniture moving. He has tried physical therapy and strong pain medications with limited long-term relief. He has also been advised of surgical options. He reports numbness and tingling in his right leg and foot, and difficulty with comfort, particularly when sitting. He quit smoking twenty years ago. He has gained weight over the past years and enjoys playing golf, but this has been impacted by his back pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness. Lumbar flexion elicits pain. Straight leg raise test is positive on the right. Neurological examination demonstrates decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at the L4 dermatome. Patellar and Achilles reflexes are symmetrical. Vitals are within normal limits. MRI reveals a disk herniation with nerve impingement at L4-L5.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with an L4-L5 herniated disc with radiculopathy.

Plan
I recommend a spinal injection with a corticosteroid under fluoroscopy to reduce inflammation at the L4-L5 level. I will initiate this procedure today. Follow-up will be scheduled based on the patient's response to the injection, with intervals not exceeding four times per year.
