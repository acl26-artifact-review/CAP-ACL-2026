Chief Complaint
Back pain for two years.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain for approximately two years, initially following a weekend of furniture moving. He has tried various physical therapies and strong pain medications, but none have provided sustained relief. The pain is described as sharp and stabbing, radiating down his right leg to his big toe. He experiences numbness and tingling in his right leg and foot, and difficulty finding comfortable positions to sit. He has gained some weight over the past years and used to enjoy playing golf, but this pain has limited his activity.

Findings
Physical exam reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with a positive lumbar flexion test and decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at L4 dermatome. Vitals are stable. MRI confirms a L4-L5 disc herniation with nerve impingement.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with a L4-L5 disc herniation with radiculopathy.

Plan
I am recommending a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and provide symptomatic relief.
