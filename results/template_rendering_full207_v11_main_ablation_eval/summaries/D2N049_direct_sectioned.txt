Chief Complaint
Left back pain and suspected kidney stone

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing significant left back pain for approximately three days, initially localized to the left back and now radiating lower, with no stone passage. He has a history of recurrent kidney stones, with this being his third episode in approximately five years. He reports some chills and nausea, and significant pain, making it difficult to find comfortable positions. He has been taking Tylenol and ibuprofen to manage the pain, but finds it is not fully effective. He has been trying to curb his french fry habit and would like to be connected with a dietitian to help with this.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the right flank and abdominal examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the abdomen. Blood pressure readings have ranged from one thirty to one forty-nine with a bottom number of seventy to eighty. The patient reports no chest pain or abdominal pain. A grade two systolic murmur is heard on heart auscultation. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally.

Assessment
The patient presents with a likely kidney stone, evidenced by his history, current symptoms, and physical exam findings. He also has hypertension and diabetes, which require ongoing management.

Plan
Prescribe oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours as needed for pain. Continue Tylenol as needed. Instruct the patient to strain urine to monitor for stone passage. Refer to urology for further evaluation and management of recurrent kidney stones. Refer to a registered dietitian for dietary counseling to address his french fry habit and support diabetes management. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust management as needed.
