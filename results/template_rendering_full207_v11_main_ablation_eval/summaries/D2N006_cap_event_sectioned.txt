Chief Complaint
Follow-up of chronic problems, including reflux, gout, and arthritis.

History of Present Illness
Anna is a 44-year-old female with a past medical history of arthritis, gout, and reflux. She reports that her arthritis has been improving with pilates and weightlifting, leading to increased mobility. Her right knee has improved with physical therapy, allowing her to walk approximately a mile and a half daily. She continues to experience occasional episodes of reflux, resulting in morning nausea and vomiting. She has been making dietary modifications but has not completely eliminated stone intake. She is currently taking allopurinol for gout and omeprazole for reflux. She reports no recent flare-ups of gout. She has been experiencing abdominal pain and bloating.

Findings
Physical exam reveals abdominal tenderness in the right upper quadrant, no rebound tenderness or guarding, and a slight effusion and pain with palpation of the right knee. Vital signs are stable. Autoimmune panel results were unremarkable. Right knee X-ray shows residual arthritis.

Assessment
The patient presents with ongoing symptoms of reflux, gout, and arthritis. Right upper quadrant tenderness raises concern for possible gallbladder pathology. The patient's right knee shows persistent arthritis.

Plan
Order a right upper quadrant ultrasound to evaluate for gallbladder pathology. Continue omeprazole 40 mg daily and allopurinol 100 mg daily. Order allopurinol refill. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and monitor symptoms. Recommend continued physical therapy for the right knee.
