Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a trampoline fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he injured his right knee four days ago while jumping on a trampoline. He describes a popping sensation at the time of injury, followed by significant swelling. He took ibuprofen and applied ice, which provided some relief, but the knee remains difficult to move. He has been able to stand with some weight, but it hurts. He reports managing his diabetes with diet and occasionally forgetting to check his sugars.

Findings
Physical exam reveals significant swelling in the patient's right knee. Palpation elicits tenderness on the medial aspect of the patella. Range of motion is limited due to pain, with decreased flexion and extension. The McMurray's test is negative. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are normal with no murmurs. The patient reports difficulty with weight-bearing.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with an injured medial collateral ligament (MCL) with associated swelling and pain. His diabetes management is also a concern.

Plan
I am placing the patient in a straight leg brace to immobilize the knee and reduce pain. I will prescribe a course of mobic for pain relief and to reduce inflammation. I will order an MRI to evaluate the extent of the MCL injury and rule out other potential damage. I will also order a hemoglobin A1c to assess his diabetes control. I will refill his metformin prescription to continue his current regimen.
