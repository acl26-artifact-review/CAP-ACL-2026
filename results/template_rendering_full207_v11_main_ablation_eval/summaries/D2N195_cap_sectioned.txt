Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a basketball injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain that began after a basketball injury yesterday, specifically during a rebound attempt. He describes a 'pop' sensation and immediate swelling. He was able to bear weight briefly but now favors the leg. The swelling has decreased somewhat overnight, but he continues to experience pain and difficulty walking. He took Tylenol yesterday with minimal relief. He has a history of similar knee pain, but this is more acute and painful. He reports being unhappy about the game last night.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the medial aspect of the right knee. There is some edema and ecchymosis. Range of motion is limited due to pain. The patient reports a pain level of four on a scale of one to ten. The patient's blood pressure is 140 over 80. His heart rate is normal. The lungs sound clear. The doctor notes a grade two systolic ejection murmur.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with a medial knee sprain, likely an MCL strain, secondary to a basketball injury. He has a history of similar knee pain.

Plan
Prescribe a knee immobilizer for two weeks. Prescribe meloxicam fifteen milligrams as needed for pain and swelling. Order an MRI to evaluate the MCL. Refer the patient to nutrition counseling to improve his diet. Increase the norvasc dose to five milligrams daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
