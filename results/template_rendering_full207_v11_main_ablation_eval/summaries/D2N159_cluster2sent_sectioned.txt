Chief Complaint
Right hand pain and numbness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing pain and numbness in his right hand, primarily associated with prolonged keyboard use. The numbness and tingling have been present continuously and is exacerbated by typing. He has been using ibuprofen to manage the pain, finding it helpful. He reports decreased grip strength in the right hand. Examination reveals pain with wrist flexion and extension, and positive Tinel's sign. The patient's thumb, index, and middle fingers experience the most pronounced symptoms.

Findings
Physical examination reveals decreased right hand grip strength and grimacing with palpation of the wrist. A positive Tinel's sign was elicited.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
I am ordering an EMG to evaluate nerve conduction studies in the wrist. We will initiate a wrist splint for nighttime use and increase ibuprofen to 600 mg four times daily. Follow-up will be scheduled after the EMG results are available to determine the need for physical therapy or consideration of surgical intervention.
