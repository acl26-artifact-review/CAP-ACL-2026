S:
The patient reports a deep, achy pain in his knees, described as feeling 'behind' the kneecaps, and occasionally hearing creaking. He also describes pain worsening with activities like getting from a seated to standing position, particularly after watching TV or going up and down stairs. He reports difficulty running, now limited to half a mile, and mentions taking ibuprofen and ace bandage in the past. He denies any specific injury triggering the pain, but does recall a broken arm in sixth grade. He reports no swelling or redness in his knees. The patient states he prefers running outdoors on the treadmill during the winter but enjoys running outdoors when weather permits.

O:
Vital signs are within normal limits. Physical exam reveals normal gait. Strength is 3/5 for abduction of legs bilaterally. Knee exam reveals no erythema or effusion. Lachman's test and McMurray's test are negative bilaterally. Neuro exam shows symmetrical patellar and Achilles reflexes. X-ray of both knees shows no fractures or osteoarthritis.

A:
Based on the patient's reported symptoms, physical exam findings, and review of the MRI, the patient's symptoms are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome. This condition is common in active young people and involves the way the kneecap moves along the groove of the thigh bone.

P:
I recommend physical therapy to address lower extremity strength, mobility, and correct any incorrect running mechanics. Continue taking ibuprofen or anti-inflammatory medication as needed for pain relief. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed. Please take it easy from running for now and gradually increase your mileage as tolerated.
