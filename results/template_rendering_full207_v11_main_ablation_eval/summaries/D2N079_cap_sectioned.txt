Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain that has been present for approximately three weeks. He recalls no specific injury but mentions strenuous basement work and reaching overhead. He describes the pain as worsening with pressure and overhead reaching, and reports difficulty reaching for objects. He has been taking two extra strength Tylenol every six to eight hours, which provides some relief. He denies numbness or tingling. He has experienced aches and pains in the past but this is different. He reports unbearable pain when laying on the shoulder.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder, with tenderness to palpation over the greater tuberosity. Capillary refill is less than three seconds. Neurological exam is intact. Cervical x-ray shows no fractures.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
I am ordering a cervical MRI to rule out other pathology. I am also referring the patient to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. The patient should continue taking Tylenol as needed. If symptoms do not improve, we will consider a steroid injection. I have scheduled a follow-up visit in four weeks after the patient completes physical therapy.
