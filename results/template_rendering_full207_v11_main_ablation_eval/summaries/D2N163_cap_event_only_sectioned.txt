Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent, dull chest pain for the past month, described as a tightness rather than sharp. The pain is exacerbated by exertion and relieved by rest. He denies shortness of breath, palpitations, or syncope. He reports a family history of coronary artery disease.

Findings
Blood pressure 138/82 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, regular rhythm. Auscultation reveals normal breath sounds bilaterally. No murmurs appreciated.

Assessment
Chest pain, etiology uncertain. Differential includes musculoskeletal pain, angina, and anxiety.

Plan
Order ECG, troponin, and chest X-ray. Counsel patient on risk factors for coronary artery disease and lifestyle modifications. Schedule follow-up in 24-48 hours to assess results and further evaluate.
