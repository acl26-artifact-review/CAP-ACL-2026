Chief Complaint
Low back pain with right foot tingling and pins and needles in the right foot.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports low back pain that began approximately two hours after raking leaves yesterday. He describes the pain as tightening and reports pins and needles in his right foot. He previously experienced similar pain three years ago, which resolved after a day. He tried ice and two Advils, but they provided no relief. The pain is exacerbated when bending over, particularly when observed by his husband, and relieved by a hot shower. He reports a long-term history of back pain and denies any recent trauma. He states his partner also experiences pain when standing. He is concerned about a potential long-term injury and worries about his partner's pain tolerance.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal dorsiflexion and equal pulses bilaterally. Examination reveals some palpation tenderness around the L5 vertebra. The patient reports tingling in his right leg. An x-ray of the lumbar spine revealed no acute abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a lumbar sprain, likely exacerbated by recent physical activity.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam for pain relief. I am also prescribing physical therapy to address the underlying musculoskeletal issues and improve flexibility and strength. I recommend a follow-up in two weeks to assess improvement, and we will consider an MRI if symptoms persist or worsen. Let's schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to reassess.
