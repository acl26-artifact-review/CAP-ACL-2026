S:
The patient reports a dull headache in the back of the head starting approximately one week ago. He describes accompanying flushing in the ears and occasional dizziness. He has taken Tylenol and Advil, but the headaches persist and return. The headache is worse in the evening. He reports increased stress levels coinciding with the onset of the headaches. He denies fever or visual changes. He has a history of headaches but this is not the worst he has experienced. He has not been checking his blood sugars.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild posterior paraspinal muscular tenderness in the cervical spine and bilateral trapezius musculature. Extraocular movements are intact. Funduscopic exam shows no papilledema. Heart exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, unchanged from prior exam. Blood pressure is within normal limits. Heart rate is 78 bpm. Temperature is 98.6°F.

A:
I suspect a tension-type headache, likely related to musculoskeletal tension, stress, and possibly exacerbated by computer use. The patient's reported muscle tenderness supports this assessment. The headache is not indicative of a serious neurological event.

P:
Continue Tylenol as needed for headache. Prescribe flexeril 5 mg three times daily for muscle relaxation. Order a hemoglobin A1c and CBC/chem-12. Continue metformin 500 mg twice daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks. Advise patient to monitor blood sugars daily and report any significant changes. Encourage return to exercise routine to improve blood sugar control. Advise patient to call sooner if headaches worsen or become more severe.
