Chief Complaint
Anxiety regarding recent hepatitis C blood work results.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being surprised by the positive hepatitis C antibody test and feeling fine. She previously denied ever being told she had hepatitis C. She reports occasional heavy drinking in the past (years ago) and occasional IV drug use, but has been clean for fifteen years. She also reports smoking one to two cigarettes a day, having previously smoked a pack and a half a day. She reports feeling tired at the end of the day, particularly after work, and notes that her family has a history of hypertension, diabetes, and depression.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a two out of six systolic murmur. The patient appears in no distress and has no jaundice. Lung sounds are clear. Abdomen is soft with normal bowel sounds. The patient's AST is elevated at 39. Examination revealed no gait disturbance or edema.

Assessment
The patient's recent blood work confirms a diagnosis of hepatitis C. Further investigation is needed to determine the severity of liver disease and guide treatment.

Plan
I am ordering a cervical MRI to assess the liver. I will also order additional blood work to determine the severity of the liver disease. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to discuss the results and discuss treatment options. I will also recommend that her family members be screened for hepatitis C.
