Chief Complaint
Low back pain with left leg radiculopathy.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports low back pain that began approximately two weeks ago following lifting a box of books. He describes the pain as both aching and sharp, and it is now radiating down his left leg. He denies any recent trauma. He has tried ibuprofen and acetaminophen with some relief. He reports no numbness or weakness. He has a history of playing soccer as a midfielder. He is a fan of the Tottenham Hotspur football team.

Findings
Vital signs are stable, with blood pressure at 130/70 mmHg and respiratory rate at 18. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Musculoskeletal exam reveals tenderness at L4-L5 with right paravertebral tenderness. Straight leg raise test is positive for left leg radiculopathy. Reflexes are symmetrical. No sensory deficits are noted. MRI reveals a disk desiccation, disk bulge with paracentral disk herniation resulting in moderate right neuroforaminal stenosis.

Assessment
Lumbar back pain at L4-L5 secondary to a herniated disk with radiculopathy.

Plan
Recommend a spinal injection with a strong NSAID and corticosteroid under fluoroscopy to reduce inflammation and provide pain relief. Schedule the injection today. Provide written instructions and address any questions the patient may have.
