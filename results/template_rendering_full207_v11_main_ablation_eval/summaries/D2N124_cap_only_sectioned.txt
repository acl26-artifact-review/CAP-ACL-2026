Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and difficulty walking.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, rated as 6/10, and difficulty walking, reporting that it is difficult to put weight on the left foot. He states the pain has been present for approximately one week and is exacerbated by weight-bearing. He denies any recent trauma. He reports taking acetaminophen for the pain, with minimal relief. He also reports feeling unsteady and has experienced some dizziness.

Findings
The patient's gait is antalgic. The left foot demonstrates decreased range of motion and tenderness to palpation. Neurological exam reveals intact sensation to light touch in the left foot. Vitals are stable.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a lateral malleolar fracture and secondary pain and instability.

Plan
Order X-rays of the left foot and ankle. Recommend crutches for weight-bearing. Refer to orthopedics for evaluation and consideration of surgical intervention. Schedule follow-up in one week to assess healing and adjust weight-bearing as appropriate.
