Chief Complaint
Difficulty swallowing and a past medical history of diabetes and high blood pressure.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty swallowing and has been experiencing it for the last four to five weeks. He describes it as something that seems stuck in his throat and sometimes painful, particularly when eating solid foods. He often needs to wash down solid foods with liquids to help them go down. He reports a feeling of needing help pushing solids down. He denies any coughing, vomiting, or blood in his stool or sputum. He reports no chest pain or shortness of breath. He has no weight loss. He is currently taking metformin 500 milligrams twice a day and norvasc. He checks his blood sugars approximately 80% of the time. He reports that his numbers are generally within the target range. He is doing well with his diabetes and blood pressure management at home.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no neck swelling or masses. The thyroid is normal. No palpable lymph nodes. Throat exam shows no redness or masses. Lung exam is clear. Heart exam is normal with no murmurs. Abdomen exam reveals some epigastric tenderness. Neurological exam is normal. Hemoglobin A1c was 7.4% at last visit.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with a narrowing in the throat, potentially a stricture, requiring further investigation. Consider referral to gastroenterology for a scope.

Plan
I am ordering a referral to gastroenterology for a scope. I am recommending the patient start on prilosec 20 milligrams once a day. I am also recommending that the patient chew their foods frequently, especially solid foods, to help them go down. I am ordering a hemoglobin A1c test and blood work to assess kidney function. I am also ordering an eye referral to ophthalmology. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to review the results and adjust the plan as needed. I will also send a refill for norvasc to the pharmacy.
