Chief Complaint
Patient reports positive hepatitis C laboratory results and expresses concern.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being surprised by a positive hepatitis C laboratory result, despite feeling well. He reports a history of past intravenous drug use, which he has been clean from for over fifteen years. He admits to occasional alcohol consumption and smoking one to two cigarettes per day. The patient reports no prior diagnoses of hepatitis C or related conditions. He denies any family history of significant liver disease.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal vital signs and no apparent distress. The patient appears in no acute distress. Examination findings include a grade two out of six systolic ejection murmur, clear lung sounds without wheezes or rales, and a soft abdomen without hepatosplenomegaly. Gait is normal and there is no edema. Lab results show an elevated AST of 39 U/L, with normal ALT, albumin, and bilirubin levels. The HCV antibody test was positive.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with hepatitis C infection.

Plan
Order hepatitis C RNA and HCV genotype testing to confirm the diagnosis and determine the viral genotype. Order an ultrasound elastography to assess liver fibrosis. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to review test results and discuss treatment options. Refer the patient to his primary care physician to coordinate care and schedule follow-up appointments.
