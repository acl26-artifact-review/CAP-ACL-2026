Chief Complaint
Follow-up on poorly controlled blood sugars and medication review.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that her sugars have not been well controlled despite current medications. She is currently taking two milligrams of glimepiride twice daily. She reports that she has not been increasing her carbohydrate intake and has not been consistently following a healthier diet. She reports that she drinks club soda and rarely drinks sodas. She reports that her feet feel like hell and has been using a foot scrub to moisturize them. She reports that she is still smoking and not ready to quit. She reports that she has been coughing recently due to congestion.

Findings
Blood pressure was 140/60. Pulse is regular and at 78 beats per minute. Lungs are clear to auscultation. Examination reveals thickened nails and mildly dry skin with no skin breakdown. Sensation is decreased in the tips of the first and second toes. Carotids are normal. The patient reports no headaches or dizziness. The patient reports no chest pain or pressure.

Assessment
Poorly controlled diabetes mellitus. Peripheral neuropathy. Hypertension.

Plan
Increase glimepiride to four milligrams twice daily. Continue metformin, lisinopril/hydrochlorothiazide, and amlodipine. Order blood work to assess kidney function. Recommend foot moisturizing with a foot scrub. Schedule follow-up in four months.
