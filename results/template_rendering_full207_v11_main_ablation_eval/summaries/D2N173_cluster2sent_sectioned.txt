Chief Complaint
Upper respiratory infection and positive COVID test.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing a cold approximately one week ago, accompanied by post-nasal drip and congestion. He denies fever or shortness of breath, though he thought he had a slight fever earlier. He has received both COVID vaccinations and reports no illness among family members. He is currently taking Crestor 20 mg daily with no reported muscle aches. He reports bilateral rhonchi and some wheezing in his lungs. The patient is positive for COVID.

Findings
The patient's oxygenation is 96%. Cervical lymphadenopathy was noted on examination. Lipid panel shows generally good cholesterol levels. The patient's vital signs are stable.

Assessment
The patient presents with a confirmed COVID-19 infection and concurrent upper respiratory symptoms.

Plan
Continue Crestor 20 mg daily and order a repeat lipid panel in six months. Schedule a stress test in six months. Advise the patient to call if symptoms worsen.
