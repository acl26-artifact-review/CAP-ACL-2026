S:
The patient reports a painful right foot injury sustained from dropping a landscape brick, with a current pain level of eleven out of ten. He describes numbness in the entire foot and reports previous ankle injuries with occasional soreness. He has not taken any medications for the pain.

O:
The physical exam reveals bruising on the right foot, swelling, and tenderness to palpation. Capillary refill is brisk bilaterally. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong and bounding. The x-ray shows dorsal displacement of the base of the second metatarsal with a 3mm separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and presence of bony fragments. The patient reports a Lisfranc fracture.

A:
The patient presents with a Lisfranc fracture of the right foot, requiring surgical intervention to restore bone alignment and ligament stability.

P:
Order an MRI to assess ligament injuries. Schedule outpatient surgery for tomorrow with same-day discharge. Provide a cast and crutches for weight-bearing restrictions for 6-8 weeks. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 24 hours post-procedure and again in two weeks. Advise the patient to avoid weight-bearing on the affected foot.
