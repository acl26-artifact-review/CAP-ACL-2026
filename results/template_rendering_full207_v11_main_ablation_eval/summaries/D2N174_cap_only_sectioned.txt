Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain, which he describes as aching and sharp, particularly with repetitive motion. He states the pain began approximately two weeks ago following increased cycling. He reports morning stiffness and reports inflammation in the knees in the morning and afternoon. The patient reports knee pain related to repetitive motion. He denies chest pain, abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting. He reports bilateral edema. The patient is currently taking aspirin every day and reports no problems with crestor.

Findings
There is a mild effusion present on the right knee. There is mild erythema present on the right knee. The patient reports pain in the right knee. The patient reports left hand pain.

Assessment
The patient is experiencing knee pain, likely related to repetitive motion and exacerbated by increased cycling. Concern for a possible flare-up of lupus given the history and symptoms.

Plan
The doctor will order an autoimmune panel and a lipid panel. The doctor will order an echocardiogram. Start physical therapy focusing on strengthening and range of motion. Continue aspirin. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
