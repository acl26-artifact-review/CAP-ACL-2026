Chief Complaint
Left ankle pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a left ankle sprain two days ago, resulting from a slip and fall while walking to her car. She describes twisting the ankle inward and experiencing immediate pain and swelling. Ibuprofen and ice with elevation provided some relief. She denies any bleeding or fractures. She reports stable blood sugar levels with her current diet and regular blood glucose monitoring. She is due for a diabetic eye exam in the next couple of weeks.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral malleolus and fifth metatarsal. There is moderate swelling and erythema of the left ankle. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are intact and equal. Sensation is intact. Neck exam is unremarkable. Lungs are clear to auscultation. Heart exam is normal. Systolic blood pressure is 120/70 mmHg.

Assessment
Ankle sprain, likely grade 2. Stable diabetes mellitus with good glycemic control.

Plan
Order an ankle x-ray to rule out fracture. Apply an air splint and provide crutches for weight-bearing restriction. Prescribe naproxen 500 mg twice daily for pain. Instruct the patient to keep the ankle elevated and apply ice. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess healing and weight-bearing tolerance. Order a hemoglobin A1c laboratory test. Refill norvasc medication. Schedule a diabetic eye exam as previously arranged.
