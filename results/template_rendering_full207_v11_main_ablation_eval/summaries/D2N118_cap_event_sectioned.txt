Chief Complaint
Kidney stone pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent pain related to kidney stones, with a history of seven or eight episodes. He presented to the emergency room last week and felt better, but the pain has returned. He states that this stone has passed, but he is still experiencing some discomfort. He reports taking percocet for pain relief. He has a history of hypertension and diabetes. He is currently taking 2.5 mg of norvasc daily and 500 mg of metformin daily. He reports that his blood pressure is typically good at home, but it was elevated during the office visit. He has been struggling to maintain a healthy diet, particularly with fast food and sweets. He is not regularly checking his blood sugar levels. He denies chest pain or belly pain. He has been experiencing insomnia and has been using meditation apps to help him sleep. He has been using melatonin as needed to help him sleep. He is not taking any sleeping pills regularly.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Blood pressure is 150/90. The patient's A1C was 6.7%.

Assessment
The patient presents with a history of recurrent kidney stones and type 2 diabetes. He is currently managing his hypertension with norvasc.

Plan
The doctor orders a urine culture and a urinalysis. The doctor orders a referral to urology for further evaluation. The doctor recommends a nutrition consult to help with dietary changes. The doctor prescribes 10 mg of melatonin as needed for insomnia. Follow up in three months.
