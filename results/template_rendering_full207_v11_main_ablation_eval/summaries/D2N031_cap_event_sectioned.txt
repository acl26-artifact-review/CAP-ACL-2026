Chief Complaint
Establish care for a history of DCIS.

History of Present Illness
The patient presents to establish care for a history of DCIS. She reports having undergone a right breast lumpectomy in January 2020 and subsequent pathology confirmed DCIS with negative margins and five lymph nodes were removed, which were benign. She has had a negative mammogram in January 2021. She reports skin thickening at the medial inferior aspect of the right breast, which she attributes to radiation therapy. She denies any recent trauma or other concerning symptoms. She reports no other medical problems and is currently taking coq10, vitamin D, vitamin C, fish oil, and elderberry fruit. She has been closely monitored by her primary care physician for high cholesterol and reports working on lifestyle changes to manage it. She has had no recent weight loss or gain, headaches, bone pain, urinary symptoms, or blood in the stools. She reports some back and knee pain, which she attributes to age, and has no family history of cancer.

Findings
Examination reveals skin thickening at the medial inferior aspect of the right breast. No palpable masses are appreciated. The patient's ecog performance status is zero.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with stage zero DCIS with no evidence of recurrence.

Plan
Continue to observe the patient for DCIS. Schedule a mammogram in April 2022 for follow-up. Continue the patient's current medications: coq10, vitamin D, vitamin C, fish oil, and elderberry fruit. Refer to physical therapy for management of shoulder pain.
