Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right knee pain for the past three months, initially following a twisting injury during recreational soccer. He denies any acute worsening of the pain recently. He describes the pain as a dull ache, exacerbated by weight-bearing and prolonged activity. He reports some stiffness in the morning. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with minimal relief. He denies any swelling or instability.

Findings
Examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the medial aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited by pain. Lachman's test is negative. McMurray's test is negative. No effusion is palpable.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with medial collateral ligament sprain.

Plan
Prescribe ice packs for 20 minutes every 2-3 hours. Recommend activity modification to avoid weight-bearing activities. Instruct patient to continue with over-the-counter ibuprofen as needed. Follow up in two weeks to assess progress.
