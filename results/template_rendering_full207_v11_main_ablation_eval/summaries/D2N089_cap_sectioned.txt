Chief Complaint
Annual exam and management of chronic conditions.

History of Present Illness
Andrea is a 52-year-old female with a past medical history significant for rheumatoid arthritis, atrial fibrillation, and reflux. She presents today for her annual exam. Over the past year, she reports walking and yoga have helped with her arthritis, experiencing no recent joint pain or flare-ups. However, she continues to experience palpitations, particularly when stressed, and has been trying meditation and running with her dog to manage stress. She reports taking methotrexate once weekly and continues to take protonix 40mg daily, avoiding soda and spicy foods. She notes a recent episode of atrial fibrillation approximately one week ago. She reports nasal congestion related to allergies.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a systolic ejection murmur of 2/6 at the right ventricle. The right elbow demonstrates edema and erythema, with tenderness to palpation. Vital signs are stable. The event monitor shows intermittent atrial fibrillation with conversion pauses. Autoimmune panel results are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient has rheumatoid arthritis, atrial fibrillation, and reflux. She is currently managing these conditions with medication and lifestyle modifications.

Plan
Continue methotrexate 2.5 mg weekly. Order a cardiology referral for a cardiac ablation to address the atrial fibrillation. Prescribe protonix 40 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and address any ongoing concerns. Refer to cardiology for further evaluation and management of the atrial fibrillation.
