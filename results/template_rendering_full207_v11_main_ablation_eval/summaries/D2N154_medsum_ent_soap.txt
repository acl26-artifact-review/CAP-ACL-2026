S:
The patient reports pain in the heel and calf after workouts and upon waking, exacerbated by sitting and prolonged inactivity. He describes the pain as tight and almost tearing. He reports no stretching habits and denies any significant lower extremity stretching. He reports pain primarily on the right side. The patient reports a history of training in triathlon, running approximately 10 kilometers, cycling for 45-60 minutes, and swimming for 30-60 minutes. He trains five days a week, alternating between running and cycling, with no swimming on those days. He reports taking ibuprofen as needed for the pain.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the right heel. Thompson's test is negative. Range of motion is full and without pain. Vital signs are within normal limits. Neurological exam is unremarkable.

A:
Diagnosis is Achilles tendinitis, likely secondary to overuse and training volume. The patient's training regimen is a significant contributing factor.

P:
Order ibuprofen 3 tablets every 6 hours. Schedule a physical therapy evaluation in 7-10 days to assess and address muscle weakness and flexibility. Advise patient to reduce training volume for one to two weeks. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess progress.
