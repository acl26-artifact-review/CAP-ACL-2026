Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain following a football injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining a shoulder injury during tackle football. He denies fever or chills. He reports taking Tylenol for pain and describes a slight headache. He reports no numbness or tingling in his fingers. He has no prior shoulder injuries. He states he is able to take a little bit of something for the pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals pain to palpation of the acromioclavicular joint in the right shoulder with decreased abduction and adduction. The right shoulder x-ray demonstrates an acute acromioclavicular joint fracture. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 122/76, heart rate 82, oxygen saturation 95% on room air. The cervical spine exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient presents with an acute acromioclavicular joint fracture of the right shoulder.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily, order a CT scan of the right shoulder, and refer to physical therapy for shoulder strengthening and rehabilitation. A sling will be provided for support. Follow up in four weeks to assess healing and function.
