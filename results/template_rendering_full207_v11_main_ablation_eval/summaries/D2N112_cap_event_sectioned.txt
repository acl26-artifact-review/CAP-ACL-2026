Chief Complaint
Lower back pain and leg pain radiating down the left leg.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a long-standing history of back pain, exacerbated by prolonged sitting and certain movements. Approximately one month ago, the pain intensified and began radiating down his left leg, accompanied by an electrical-like sensation. He describes the pain as ranging from 6/10 to 13/10. He has attempted to manage the pain with NSAIDs without significant relief. He also mentions a past fall off a roof approximately 30 years ago, which he believes may have contributed to the current issue. He reports difficulty with standing and walking, and uses a cane for support. He frequently encounters challenges communicating his symptoms due to other patients' distracting narratives and difficulty eliciting a clear chief complaint.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation at the L4 vertebral level. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Strength is 4/5 in the left lower extremity and 5/5 in the right. Reflexes are brisk bilaterally. Neurological exam shows no sensory deficits. MRI findings demonstrate a disc herniation at L4-L5 with nerve root impingement.

Assessment
Acute lumbar disc herniation at L4-L5 with nerve root impingement, causing radiculopathy and lower extremity pain.

Plan
I am recommending an epidural steroid injection to address the inflammation at the site of the herniation. I will also refer you back to physical therapy to strengthen the area and improve your mobility. I would like to schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately one month to assess your progress.
