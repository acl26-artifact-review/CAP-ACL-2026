Chief Complaint
Upper respiratory infection with shortness of breath and elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling tired and short of breath, particularly when lifting or carrying heavy bags of soil over the past week. He states this started after some landscaping work. He denies recent trauma. He reports feeling a little warm but attributes it to exertion. He denies any history of seasonal allergies. He reports a history of depression and hypertension, and is currently taking medication for depression. He has been monitoring his blood sugar levels while working, but not so much on weekends. He denies nausea or vomiting and diarrhea. He reports elbow pain and knee fatigue.

Findings
Physical exam reveals scattered rhonchi bilaterally in the lung exam, clearing with cough. The patient has edema of the lower extremities and reports pain to palpation of the elbows bilaterally. Chest x-ray shows no evidence of airspace disease or pneumonia. Hemoglobin A1c is elevated at 8%.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a viral upper respiratory infection. He has a history of type 2 diabetes and hypertension, which are being managed.

Plan
I'm going to order a COVID test to rule out COVID. I'm prescribing robitussin for his cough and ibuprofen for pain. I'm increasing his metformin to 1000 milligrams twice daily. I'm also ordering a hemoglobin A1c in four months. I'll schedule a follow-up appointment in four months to assess his progress. The nurse will help him with refills of his lisinopril.
