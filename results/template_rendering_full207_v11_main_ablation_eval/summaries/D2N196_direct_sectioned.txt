Chief Complaint
Elevated and fluctuating blood pressures.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that his blood pressure has been elevated and fluctuating over the past three to four weeks, occasionally reaching over 180 mm Hg. He has experienced lightheadedness and occasional headaches, particularly when blood pressure readings are higher. He denies recent trauma or chest pain. He has been taking lisinopril 20 mg twice daily and atorvastatin. He reports rarely missing doses and uses a pillbox and has used the app with his grandson's assistance. He has not experienced any recent fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, bibasilar rales, and trace lower extremity edema bilaterally. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable. Vitals include a blood pressure of 178/102 mmHg. The patient reports feeling lightheaded when blood pressure is elevated.

Assessment
The patient's blood pressure is out of control, requiring adjustment of medication therapy. No other acute findings are noted.

Plan
Increase lisinopril to 60 mg twice daily and add hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily. Order a cholesterol panel, CBC with differential, comprehensive metabolic panel, and urinalysis. Continue atorvastatin. Follow up in two weeks to assess blood pressure control. Advise the patient to monitor blood pressure twice daily and record readings. Encourage adequate hydration, approximately six to eight glasses of water daily, especially with increased activity. Limit alcohol consumption.
