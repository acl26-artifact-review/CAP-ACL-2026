Chief Complaint
Anna reports left elbow pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left elbow pain for approximately one month, exacerbated by repetitive motions such as typing, particularly during her work shift. She describes a noticeable swelling at the tip of her left elbow, which worsens with movement. She reports some stiffness, particularly upon attempting to straighten her elbow. She denies any recent trauma or numbness/tingling. She reports no recent seizures and is currently taking Keppra 500 mg twice daily. She reports being doing well with therapy for depression and denies any suicidal ideation. She reports being doing better with her peptic ulcer disease since the endoscopy. She endorses nasal congestion from allergies. The patient reports no fever or chills.

Findings
Physical examination reveals erythema and edema of the left elbow. Palpation elicits tenderness at the olecranon. A palpable left radial pulse is present. The patient's vital signs are stable. Left arm x-ray demonstrates no fracture. Endoscopy results show gastritis consistent with a prior peptic ulcer episode.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with olecranon bursitis.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg orally once daily for pain relief. I have ordered a refill of Keppra 500 mg twice daily. I am also prescribing Lexapro 10 mg orally once daily to address the patient's depression. I will continue the patient on protonix 40 mg orally once daily before meals. I recommend ice packs to the affected area as needed. The nurse will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress.
