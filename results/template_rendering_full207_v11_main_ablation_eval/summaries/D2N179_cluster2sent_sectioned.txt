Chief Complaint
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his hands and fingers.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his hands and fingers for several weeks, beginning after a car accident in December when he broke his wrist. He continues to wear a splint on his right wrist. He also experiences mild neck stiffness following the accident and reports taking nerve pain medication inconsistently. He describes the sensation as a 'zing' up his wrist and into his elbow, and the tingling is often exacerbated by movement. The symptoms are primarily bilateral, extending from the pinky and ring finger on the left.

Findings
The patient is a 60-year-old right-handed male with fatigue, weight gain, excessive appetite, bloating, floaters, shortness of breath, leg swelling, nail bed changes, dryness, itching, gout, and muscle pain. Examination reveals diminished pin prick sensation in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric, with knees 2+ and toes downgoing bilaterally. Tinel signs are positive at the wrists and elbows. The neurologic examination is normal except for the right hand.

Assessment
The patient's presentation suggests bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome, potentially complicated by cubital tunnel syndrome, secondary to past trauma.

Plan
Schedule an EMG test to determine the source of nerve irritation. We will consider referral to an orthopedist for further evaluation and potential treatment, including injections or carpal tunnel release surgery if indicated. Continue wearing the splint and follow up after the EMG results are available.
