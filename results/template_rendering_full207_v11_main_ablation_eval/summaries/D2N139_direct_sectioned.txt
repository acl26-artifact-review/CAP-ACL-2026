Chief Complaint
Hypertension and palpitations.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of hypertension and palpitations, with recent presentation to the emergency room in March due to high blood pressure and palpitations. He attributes the palpitations to recent coffee consumption and reports no breakfast that day. He has been taking benicar hct 40-25 mg daily and amlodipine 10 mg daily for approximately one month. His father has a history of hypertension. He reports his blood pressure has been running between 146 and 155 mmHg recently. He is attempting to reduce his salt intake. He denies any recent trauma.

Findings
Blood pressure readings at home range from 146 to 155 mmHg. Potassium level was 4.0 one week ago. Physical exam reveals normal heart and lung sounds. Ankle and foot exams are unremarkable.

Assessment
Resistant hypertension.

Plan
Start spironolactone 25mg daily. Monitor potassium and electrolytes at follow-up. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess blood pressure and electrolyte levels. Provide the patient with information on the DASH diet and the American Heart Association website for blood pressure management resources. Discuss potential side effects of spironolactone, including dizziness and gynecomastia. If symptoms persist, consider referral to the resistant hypertension clinic for further evaluation and potential renal denervation.
