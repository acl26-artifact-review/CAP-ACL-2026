Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began approximately one and a half years ago and has been progressively worsening. He reports pain with both flexion and extension, particularly with palpation. Ibuprofen 800 mg three times daily provides minimal relief. The patient denies any recent trauma. He reports no prior sports injuries, but notes that he has not been pitching in the past year and a half. He has not tried dry needling.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited range of motion on extension of the right elbow and mild tenderness to palpation on the medial aspect of the elbow. Pulses are equal bilaterally. Sensation is intact to the touch. X-rays demonstrate no bony abnormalities.

Assessment
Medial epicondylitis, right elbow.

Plan
Order an MRI of the right elbow to further evaluate the findings. Consider whole blood transfusion as a potential therapeutic intervention to promote healing, pending MRI results. Schedule the MRI and will discuss the results in approximately two weeks. Follow up in two weeks if symptoms persist, considering the patient's upcoming delivery.
