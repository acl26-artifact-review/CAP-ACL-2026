Chief Complaint
Severe right elbow pain, particularly with flexion and extension.

History of Present Illness
Dylan reports experiencing significant right elbow pain for approximately one and a half years, initially during weightlifting. The pain is exacerbated by use and relieved somewhat by ice. He reports playing basketball, baseball, and football in the past, and denies any prior similar injuries. He has been taking ibuprofen 800mg three times daily for pain relief. He has not been pitching recently. He also mentions a desire to be ready for an upcoming newborn arrival.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited range of motion in extension of the right elbow with pain on palpation. Range of motion is normal in flexion. Distal pulses are equal bilaterally. Sensation is intact to light touch in all fingers. No signs of swelling or erythema are noted. Cervical and Thoracic X-rays are unremarkable.

Assessment
Based on the clinical presentation and examination findings, the most likely diagnosis is medial epicondylitis (golfer's elbow).

Plan
A cervical MRI is ordered to rule out other pathology. We will also discuss a whole blood transfusion as a potential treatment option to promote healing. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately two weeks to assess progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.
