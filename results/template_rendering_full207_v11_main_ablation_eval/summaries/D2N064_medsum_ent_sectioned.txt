Chief Complaint
Left ankle pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a left ankle sprain following a fall while walking. He describes feeling a 'stretch and extended' sensation and significant pain for the past few days. He was able to walk back to the house but reported pain. He has taken ibuprofen and applied ice to the ankle. He has not done anything substantial to help with the pain. The patient mentions going on walks with his wife behind the new Rex Center trails and asks about them.

Findings
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus and swelling of the left ankle. There is tenderness to palpation on the lateral side. Neurovascular exam is intact with brisk capillary refill and strong dorsalis pedis pulses. X-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormalities. The left ankle exam is bilaterally normal.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with an ankle sprain of the lateral ligament complex.

Plan
I am going to provide an air cast to stabilize the ankle and prescribe crutches to limit weight-bearing. I recommend continuing the RICE protocol. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to monitor progress and will have you continue taking NSAIDs as needed. Please contact the office if your pain worsens or if you have any concerns.
