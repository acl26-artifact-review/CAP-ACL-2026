Chief Complaint
Six weeks post-right medial unicompartmental arthroplasty, with numbness and follow-up.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports six weeks post-right medial unicompartmental arthroplasty. She reports persistent numbness on the affected side, which she attributes to nerve healing. She states the knee is good, but the numbness remains. She works as an ups driver and is currently unable to perform the job due to lifting restrictions. She is awaiting a return-to-work note.

Findings

Radiographs show stable implant placement with no loosening or early complications. Gait is antalgic with a slight limp and decreased velocity. Reflex time is normal.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is persistent post-operative numbness following right total knee replacement, complicated by limitations related to her job as an ups driver.

Plan
Schedule a telemedicine follow-up in six weeks to assess progress and discuss return-to-work considerations. Provide a note to UPS excusing her from lifting greater than 10 pounds or prolonged standing/sitting for more than 30 minutes. Arrange follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Continue physical therapy as prescribed.
