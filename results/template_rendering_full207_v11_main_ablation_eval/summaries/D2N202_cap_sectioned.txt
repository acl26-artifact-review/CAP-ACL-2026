Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right shoulder pain that began approximately three weeks ago while remodeling his house, specifically while painting on a ladder. He fell while bracing himself. He initially iced the arm and took Advil. He rates his pain as a 6 without medication and a 4 with medication. He denies prior shoulder pain or injury. He reports intermittent pain. He reports numbness and tingling initially, but this has resolved. He has been to Lowe's and is considering flooring options.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the right shoulder and tenderness to the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds. Neurovascular exam is intact. The right shoulder x-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
The doctor orders an MRI of the right shoulder and refers the patient to physical therapy. The doctor recommends the patient continue taking an NSAID if tolerated and recommends taking Tylenol. The doctor recommends follow-up in two weeks to evaluate progress.
