Chief Complaint
Left knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he fell while helping his niece learn to ride her bike, twisting his knee in an 'unfunny way.' He states he's been limping since the incident and the pain has not improved. He has taken Tylenol and kinesiology tape, but the pain is still throbbing, particularly on the medial aspect of the knee. He reports pain on flexion and extension. He was previously taking ibuprofen, but it's not helping. He's concerned about potential liver damage from excessive Tylenol intake. He reports pain in the left hand.

Findings
Examination reveals moderate edema and effusion in the left knee. Range of motion is limited, but not severely restricted. Pain is elicited with palpation on the medial aspect of the knee. Lachman's and valgus/varus tests are negative. A x-ray revealed no fractures or bony abnormalities. The patient's vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
Based on the clinical presentation and physical examination, the patient likely has an MCL strain. There are no signs of significant ligamentous injury or fracture.

Plan
Prescribe a knee immobilizer to restrict movement and reduce inflammation. Recommend ice application three to four times daily for 20-30 minutes. Prescribe tramadol 50mg twice daily for breakthrough pain, as needed, along with Tylenol for breakthrough pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and consider further imaging if symptoms persist.
