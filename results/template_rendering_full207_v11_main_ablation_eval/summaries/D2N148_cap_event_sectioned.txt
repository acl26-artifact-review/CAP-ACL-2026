Chief Complaint
Reports back pain and tingling in the toes following a back injury while moving boxes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a back injury approximately four months ago while moving boxes, resulting in lower back pain. He initially tried physical therapy at his gym but found it ineffective. He experienced numbness and tingling in his toes, which resolved after about an hour. He has been taking ibuprofen and acetaminophen, but finds the relief short-lived. He was concerned about maxing out his insurance and delayed imaging. He had an urgent care visit where an MRI was suggested but declined due to cost concerns. He has no bladder or bowel issues.

Findings
Physical exam reveals decreased flexion and extension of the lower back, a positive straight leg raise, and mild spasm of the paraspinal muscles. Vital signs are normal: temperature 98.6°F, blood pressure 137/85 mmHg, heart rate 68 bpm, oxygen saturation 98% on room air. The neck exam reveals bony protuberance of the C5-C6 with no pain on palpation. Lumbar spine X-ray shows no acute bony abnormality. The patient's right knee has been replaced two years ago.

Assessment
Acute disc herniation is suspected based on the patient's symptoms and physical exam findings.

Plan
Order a lumbar spine MRI to confirm the diagnosis. Refer the patient to physical therapy. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain management. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist or worsen.
