Chief Complaint
Right knee pain and clicking sensation with walking.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a right knee injury approximately two and a half weeks ago, resulting from stepping off a curb. Initially, ankle pain was more prominent, but now the knee is the primary source of discomfort. He describes a clicking sensation when walking, particularly when standing on one foot for extended periods. He reports that the knee feels 'locked' at times, especially when squatting, and describes a fear of a serious injury. He has been taking ibuprofen but finds it only provides temporary relief during the day. He reports numbness in his right foot, related to a previous foot injury sustained ten years ago. He denies any recent trauma. He has been riding a Peloton bike indoors for the winter and enjoys rock music rides.

Findings
Physical exam reveals slight edema and a slight effusion in the right knee. Palpation of the lateral knee is unremarkable. Range of motion is full, with a negative Lachman's test. A positive varus test is noted. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable. The patient reports that the knee hurts when resting on the heel but not when it's flat on the floor. He reports clicking sensation when squatting. The patient reports no pain with palpation of the knee.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and physical examination, he likely has a lateral collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily. I am referring you to physical therapy to strengthen the muscles around the knee and prevent future injuries. Continue icing the knee as needed, especially during the day. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist or worsen.
