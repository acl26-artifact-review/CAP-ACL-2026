Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath with CHF and COPD.

History of Present Illness
Gloria is a 46-year-old female presenting with a two-week history of increasing shortness of breath. She reports difficulty falling asleep and choking a few minutes after lying down. She notes swelling in her ankles and reports weight gain. She admits to enjoying a large party last weekend and consuming a lot of food, and she's not sure how much salt is in the food she eats. She was previously taking lasix and on diet control to limit salt intake, but she hasn't been taking it regularly. She reports no chest pain but feels pain when she can't breathe. Her blood sugars have been running a little high, and she's been checking them with an accu-chek a couple of weeks ago, with a reading of around 2.3. She's been experiencing some nausea and has been peeing a lot. She denies fever.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild jugular venous distension, crackles in both lung bases, intermittent wheezing, and one plus pitting edema in both ankles. The patient's heart rate is regular and 78 bpm. The abdomen is slightly distended without tenderness. Neurological exam is normal. Neck exam reveals mild swelling.

Assessment
The patient has an exacerbation of congestive heart failure and uncontrolled diabetes mellitus.

Plan
Increase lasix dose to 60 milligrams for the next four days, monitoring weight daily. Order albuterol and atrovent as needed for shortness of breath. Order a hemoglobin a1c test within two days. Order naproxen and flexeril for back pain. Schedule physical therapy. Arrange follow-up in two days to reassess and adjust treatment as needed.
