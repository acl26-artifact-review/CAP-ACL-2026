Chief Complaint
Left hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand. He states the pain has been present for approximately one month and has been gradually worsening. The patient describes the pain as sharp and intermittent, and it is exacerbated by gripping and lifting objects. He denies any recent injuries or trauma. He reports that the pain is not interfering with his daily activities, but it does make it difficult to perform certain tasks. The patient is currently taking ibuprofen as needed for the pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal interphalangeal joint of the left hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. There is no swelling or erythema. Grip strength is normal. Tinel's sign is negative.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a diagnosis of carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Order nerve conduction studies to evaluate for carpal tunnel syndrome. Recommend wrist splinting for comfort and support. Advise the patient to avoid activities that aggravate the symptoms. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
