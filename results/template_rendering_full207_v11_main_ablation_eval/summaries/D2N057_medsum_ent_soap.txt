S:
The patient reports intermittent toe pain, redness, and stiffness for approximately two weeks, with a significant exacerbation last week during a trade show convention where he was unable to walk. He describes a throbbing pain occasionally accompanied by sharp, stabbing pain, particularly with movement. He walks on his heel to avoid bending the toe. The patient reports difficulty with the sheets at night when they come down on the affected toe. He has tried ibuprofen with limited relief. The patient denies any injury and reports no prior stomach problems.

O:
The patient's big toe exhibits a large spur and significant cartilage loss as visualized on the x-ray. There is palpable bone spurs on the dorsal aspect of the great toe joint. The patient reports tenderness to palpation on the dorsal and lateral aspects of the great toe joint, with crepitus noted upon range of motion testing. The patient's foot demonstrates limited plantar flexion (approximately 20 degrees).

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with hallux rigidus with significant cartilage loss and a large spur, likely causing the reported pain and stiffness. The patient's reported difficulty with the sheets at night suggests significant inflammation and discomfort.

P:
Rescribe an orthotic insert for the patient's shoe. Start meloxicam 15mg daily for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess treatment efficacy and discuss potential interventions, including a shot or joint fusion if symptoms persist. Advise the patient to continue with ibuprofen as needed, monitoring for gastrointestinal side effects. Counsel the patient on the importance of maintaining activity levels while managing his condition and to avoid excessive walking or prolonged standing. Schedule a return precautions for worsening symptoms or lack of improvement.
