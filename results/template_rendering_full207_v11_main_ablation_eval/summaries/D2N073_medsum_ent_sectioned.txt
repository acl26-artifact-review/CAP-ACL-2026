Chief Complaint
Patient reports an inability to donate blood due to anemia and fatigue.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a long-standing history of iron deficiency anemia, dating back to age 13. She reports feeling fatigued frequently throughout the day and experiencing occasional feverish chills. She also notes episodes of wheezing and headaches, which are not typical for her. She reports heavy menstrual periods prior to her hysterectomy in 1996. She has not received a recent colonoscopy and reports it was performed approximately five to six years ago with no polyps noted. She currently takes ferrous sulfate 25 mg twice daily and vitamin B12 over-the-counter. She denies any history of sedation issues.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal findings without significant findings. Vital signs are within normal limits. The patient denies any pacemaker or defibrillator use, sleep apnea, or oxygen use at night.

Assessment
The patient presents with iron deficiency anemia and associated symptoms of fatigue, chills, and headaches. Further investigation is warranted to determine the etiology of the anemia.

Plan
Order an upper endoscopy and colonoscopy to evaluate for sources of bleeding. Schedule a referral to a hematologist for further evaluation and potential intravenous iron therapy. Arrange for a capsule endoscopy if initial investigations are inconclusive. Prescribe ferrous sulfate 25 mg twice daily with orange juice to enhance absorption. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to reassess blood work and symptoms.
