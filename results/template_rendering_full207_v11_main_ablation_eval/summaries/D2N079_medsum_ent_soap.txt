S:
The patient reports left shoulder pain that has been present for approximately three weeks. He recalls no specific injury but has been actively renovating his basement. He reports difficulty reaching for objects and lifting, particularly overhead. He describes the pain as constant and worsening with pressure, especially when lying on the affected shoulder. He has been taking two extra-strength Tylenol every six to eight hours, which provides some relief but not complete resolution. He denies any numbness or tingling. He reports no prior history of shoulder pain beyond occasional aches and pains that Tylenol can manage.

O:
Capillary refill is less than three seconds. Neurological exam is intact. Shoulder range of motion is limited. Examination reveals tenderness of the greater tuberosity of the humerus. No tenderness at the sternoclavicular or acromioclavicular joints. Hand grips are good.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to a rotator cuff tendinopathy. An MRI is ordered to rule out other pathology.

P:
I am ordering an MRI for your left shoulder. I am also referring you to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. Continue taking Tylenol as needed. If your symptoms do not improve, we will consider a steroid injection. Please schedule a follow-up visit with me after completing your physical therapy. The nurse will provide you with educational materials on physical therapy and schedule a follow-up visit.
