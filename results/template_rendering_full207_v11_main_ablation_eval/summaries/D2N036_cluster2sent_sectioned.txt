Chief Complaint
Numbness and tingling in the fingers and pain in the wrist.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his fingers and pain in his right wrist and hand for several months. The pain and numbness have been gradually increasing over time. He denies any recent trauma. He reports weakness in his left hand and occasional tingling in his finger tips. He denies any prior diagnoses of rheumatoid arthritis.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bilateral Tinel's signs. The patient reports pain with wrist and hand movement. Examination of the right hand reveals no abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient is being evaluated for possible carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Order an EMG to evaluate nerve conduction studies. Recommend activity modification, including reducing repetitive motions, and provide a wrist splint. Prescribe ibuprofen 600 mg every six hours as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess treatment response and consider surgical intervention if necessary.
