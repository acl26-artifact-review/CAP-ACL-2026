The patient reports a history of headaches for years, initially menstrual-related but now occurring with stress and more frequently and severely recently. He also reports experiencing tinnitus for several months and intermittent blurred vision. He used to have tumor growth but now exercises regularly to lose weight, but experiences exacerbated headaches with exercise. He is currently feeling nervous about his headaches.

O:
The funduscopic exam reveals papilledema. Neurological exam is intact. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms are concerning for idiopathic intracranial hypertension, and the papilledema supports this diagnosis. Further investigation is warranted to confirm and rule out other causes.

P:
Order an urgent MRI of the brain for further evaluation. Schedule a consultation with a neuro-ophthalmologist for a complete eye exam. Obtain pre-authorization for the MRI from the insurance company. Follow up with the patient regarding the results of the MRI and the neuro-ophthalmologist consultation.
