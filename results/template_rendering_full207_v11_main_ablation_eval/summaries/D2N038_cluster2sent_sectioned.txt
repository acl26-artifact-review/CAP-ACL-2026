Chief Complaint
The patient reports a positive hepatitis C antibody test and expresses anxiety regarding the diagnosis.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being surprised by the positive hepatitis C test result, as he has been feeling well and it was discovered incidentally during a routine physical exam. He denies a history of intravenous drug use or known hep C partners. He also reports a history of hepatosplenomegaly.

Findings
Physical exam revealed a regular rate and rhythm with a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur, clear lung sounds, and normal bowel sounds. Liver panel showed an elevated AST of 39 U/L, with normal albumin and bilirubin levels. The patient's recent lab work confirmed a positive HCV antibody test.

Assessment
The patient has high blood pressure, diabetes, and depression, with a confirmed hepatitis C infection.

Plan
The plan includes ordering additional blood work, including HCV RNA and HCV genotype testing, and a liver ultrasound with elastography to assess liver fibrosis. The physician will facilitate scheduling appointments with the patient's primary care physician and will consider prescribing medication based on the results of the further testing and liver fibrosis assessment.
