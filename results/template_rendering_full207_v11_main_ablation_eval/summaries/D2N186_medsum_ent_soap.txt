S:
The patient reports left side low back pain, exacerbated by bending over and random occurrences, and reports that stretching helps. He denies any pain with flexion or extension, and reports no pain with palpation to the right side. He describes the pain as 'horrible awful things' and reports nerve pain. He reports a muscle pull from a misalignment in his spine. He previously tried ibuprofen 800mg daily and reports difficulty withdrawing from it. He has been to physical therapy at the VA.

O:
An X-ray revealed no fractures or abnormalities in the spinal bone, and the dorsiflexion test was normal. Range of motion is normal. Palpation to the left side revealed tenderness at L5. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a muscle strain secondary to spinal misalignment. He is experiencing left-sided low back pain and reports nerve symptoms. A referral to pain management is indicated to explore alternative pain management strategies.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam instead of ibuprofen. Refer to pain management for further evaluation and potential non-narcotic pain management strategies. Schedule physical therapy at the VA for continued musculoskeletal support.
