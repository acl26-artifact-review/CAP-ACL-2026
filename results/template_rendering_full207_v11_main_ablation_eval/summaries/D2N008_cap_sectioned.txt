Chief Complaint
Low hemoglobin level.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue and dizziness over the past couple of months, particularly with increased exertion. She notes swelling in her ankles and reports difficulty with salt intake, particularly while traveling. She had a colonoscopy three years ago that revealed mild diverticulosis with no issues since. She also has a history of kidney stones two years ago with no recent flares. She reports seasonal allergies and nasal congestion. She denies any weight loss or loss of appetite, or episodes of passing blood in the stool or urine.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is within normal limits. Heart rate is appropriate. Physical exam reveals trace to one plus pitting edema in the ankles. A systolic ejection murmur is heard. The doctor notes nasal congestion.

Assessment
The patient's low hemoglobin level is concerning and requires further investigation. Her congestive heart failure, diverticulosis, and seasonal allergies are contributing factors.

Plan
The doctor plans to order an anemia profile and a referral to the gastroenterologist for an endoscopy and colonoscopy. The doctor also orders lasix 40 milligrams once daily, toprinol 50 milligrams daily, and lisinopril 10 milligrams a day. The doctor recommends monitoring weight daily and calling if it increases. The gastroenterologist will perform an endoscopy and colonoscopy.
