Chief Complaint
Concerned about all the medications he's taking and dizziness when standing up quickly.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports concern about all the medications he's taking, but feels he's doing okay. He's been taking aspirin and brilinta, and fish oil. He reports feeling dizzy when standing up quickly. He's taking crestor for cholesterol and metoprolol for heart rate. He had a heart attack six months ago and received a stent in the left anterior descending artery. He also has diabetes and reports feeling down. He's been following his glucose levels, which have been around 130, and his A1c was around 60. He's been avoiding motrin and advil. He's a fan of San Francisco 49ers and Jimmy G.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slight 3/6 systolic ejection murmur at the left base, and one plus nonpitting edema in the lower extremities. His blood pressure is 127/80. His heart rate is stable. Kidney function is stable.

Assessment
The patient has coronary artery disease, stage three chronic kidney disease, and diabetes mellitus. He is also experiencing depression and dizziness.

Plan
Refer the patient for cardiac rehabilitation. Order a hemoglobin A1c. Schedule a follow-up with the nephrologist. Refer the patient to a dietitian for diabetes education. Prescribe medication refills for aspirin, brilinta, crestor, and metoprolol. Consider a referral to psychiatry for depression management. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks.
