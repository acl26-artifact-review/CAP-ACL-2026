Chief Complaint
Tick bite and associated symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a tick bite approximately one week ago, located on his knee. He describes the area as burning and mildly annoying. He denies fever or chills. He reports no other joint pain, but mentions typical arthritic pain. He has been hunting recently and reports a recent hunting trip. He is taking lisinopril for hypertension and metformin for diabetes. He checks his blood pressure periodically and reports it is generally well-controlled. He has not been previously diagnosed with Lyme disease. He reports occasional headaches and dizziness. He is a big pie fan, particularly chocolate pie.

Findings
Physical exam reveals erythema and edema over the right patella, consistent with a bull's-eye rash. Vital signs are normal: blood pressure 120/70 mmHg, heart rate 67 bpm, temperature 98.4°F. The knee is mildly tender to palpation but full range of motion is present. Lung exam is clear bilaterally.

Assessment
Based on the presence of a bull's-eye rash and the history of a tick bite, Lyme disease is suspected. Further investigation with Lyme titer and Western blot is warranted.

Plan
Start doxycycline 100 mg twice daily for three weeks. Order a Lyme titer and Western blot. Order a lipid panel and a basic metabolic panel. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily and metformin 1000 mg twice daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
