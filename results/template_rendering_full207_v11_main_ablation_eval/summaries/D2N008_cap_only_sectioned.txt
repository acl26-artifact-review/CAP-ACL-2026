Chief Complaint
Swelling in legs and nasal congestion.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports swelling in their legs, particularly in the ankles, which has been present for approximately two weeks. They report experiencing nasal congestion and difficulty breathing through their nose. The patient also reports fatigue and dizziness, particularly in the past week. They have a history of congestive heart failure and kidney stones, and a prior colonoscopy. The patient has difficulty with salt intake. They also have a history of diverticulosis.

Findings
The doctor notes trace to one plus pitting edema in the ankles. The doctor hears a systolic ejection murmur. The patient's blood pressure is 138/82 mmHg, heart rate 88 bpm, and respiratory rate 18 breaths per minute.

Assessment
The patient has congestive heart failure, seasonal allergies, and fatigue. The patient's symptoms are consistent with edema and nasal congestion.

Plan
The doctor orders a referral to the gastroenterologist for an endoscopy and a colonoscopy. The gastroenterologist will perform an endoscopy and a colonoscopy. The doctor orders salt intake reduction.
