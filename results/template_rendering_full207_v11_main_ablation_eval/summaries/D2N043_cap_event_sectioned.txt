Chief Complaint
Patient reports wanting to quit smoking and is experiencing difficulty.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a 20+ year history of smoking, starting in high school and currently smoking a pack and a half per day. He attempted to quit cold turkey a year ago and was successful for approximately one year before stress at work led to a return to smoking. He is motivated to quit due to the impending arrival of his daughter. He has tried meditation and recently joined a gym to manage stress. He reports using a nicotine patch to help with the process.

Findings
The patient's oxygen saturation is 98% on room air. His blood pressure is 128/88. Heart rate is 68 bpm. Lung exam reveals clear and equal bilateral lung sounds with no wheezes or rhonchi. Neck exam is unremarkable, with no palpable lymphadenopathy and no carotid bruits. A regular and rhythmic heart rate was noted during the exam. The patient's oxygen saturation was 98% on room air.

Assessment
The patient presents with nicotine dependence and a history of type 2 diabetes. He also has a history of gout.

Plan
The doctor wants the patient to stop smoking. The provider recommends setting a quit date. The nicotine patch dosage will be decreased over time. The patient will start with a 21 milligram nicotine patch. The patient will have a follow-up appointment in two weeks. The doctor wants the patient to consider meditation or yoga to help manage stress. Order hemoglobin a1c for his next blood draw. Continue allopurinol for his gout. Schedule a gym membership for the patient.
