Chief Complaint
Follow-up of chronic problems and managing depression and hypertension.

History of Present Illness
Edward is a 59-year-old male with a past medical history of depression, hypertension, and a prior rotator cuff repair. He presents for follow-up, reporting that he's been doing well managing his depression over the past six months through counseling and swimming, and that his blood pressure has been generally well-controlled. However, he occasionally experiences fluctuations due to dietary choices and reports some ankle swelling, particularly in the evenings. He's currently taking norvasc and checks his blood pressure at home weekly. He continues with home exercises for his rotator cuff repair and is doing well with that. He denies any current nasal congestion or chest pain/shortness of breath. He does note some occasional ankle swelling.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a blood pressure of 156/94 mmHg, a slight systolic ejection murmur of 3/6, clear lung sounds, and stable lower extremity exam with 1+ pitting edema in the ankles. An EKG shows no evidence of coronary artery disease. Lab results are stable, including a normal creatinine level. Dragon shows an ekg with no abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is depression, which is currently well-managed. His hypertension is not optimally controlled, and he continues to experience some ankle edema. The rotator cuff repair is progressing well.

Plan
Increase norvasc to 10 mg daily. Order an echocardiogram and lipid panel. Continue home exercises for the rotator cuff repair. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to reassess blood pressure control and ankle edema. Advise the patient to monitor his salt intake and discuss strategies for managing the edema.
