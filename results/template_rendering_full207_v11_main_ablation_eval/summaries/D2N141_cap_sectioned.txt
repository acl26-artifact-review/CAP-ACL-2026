Chief Complaint
Follow-up for adult hydrocephalus.

History of Present Illness
Mr. Watson is an 86-year-old male returning for follow-up on adult hydrocephalus. He initially presented with eight to ten years of progressive gait impairment, cognitive impairment, and decreased bladder control in May 2020. A diagnosis of adult hydrocephalus with a spino catheter protocol was established in June 2020, and he underwent shunt surgery on August 1st, 2020. A Medtronic Strata programmable shunt was placed in the ventricular peritoneal configuration at a level of 2.0. Since surgery, he has reported improved walking and cognitive function, though bladder control remains an issue. He reports wearing diapers. He denies recent hospitalizations or illness since the shunt surgery three months ago. He reports no headaches or pain at the shunt site. He reports no belly pain. He feels like he can have a normal conversation with others again. His daughter helps him keep things straight.

Findings
Examination reveals a blood pressure of 124/80, a heart rate of 64, respirations of 18, and a weight of 174 pounds. The shunt site is clean, dry, and intact with a confirmed setting of 2.0. Cranial nerves are intact. Pupils are equal and reactive. Extraocular movements are full. The face is symmetrical. There is no disarthria. Motor strength and bulk are normal. Coordination is slow from finger to nose. A CT scan from 10/15/2020 shows a frontal horn span at the level of foramen of monro of 4.6 centimeters with a third ventricular contour that is flat with a span of 10 millimeters. There is a tiny amount of blood in the right front region with a tiny subdermal collection. There is also substantial small vessel ischemic change. The patient's gait score on the Tinetti assessment is 6 to 8 out of 12 and the balance score is 12 out of 16, for a total score of 18 to 20 out of 28. The patient scored 26 out of 30 on the mmse when tested with spelling and 25 out of 30 when tested with calculations.

Assessment
The patient has adult hydrocephalus, with associated gait impairment and cognitive impairment. He is experiencing urinary incontinence and urgency.

Plan
The shunt setting will be left unchanged for another two to three months. Mr. Watson will follow up in three months to reassess his condition. The doctor recommends Mr. Watson enroll in a driver safety program. The doctor recommends a follow-up in three months.
