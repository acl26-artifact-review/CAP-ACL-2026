Chief Complaint
Ankle swelling

History of Present Illness
The patient reports swelling in their ankles for approximately one to two months after starting norvasc. The swelling is bilateral and is not relieved by elevation. The patient denies any recent trauma. He reports some discomfort with walking. He is currently taking norvasc 5 milligrams once a day.

Findings
The patient exhibits edema in their legs and ankles. Examination reveals no signs of redness or warmth. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's edema is likely related to his norvasc medication and may be exacerbated by reduced mobility. Further evaluation is warranted to rule out other causes of edema.

Plan
Continue norvasc 5 mg once daily. Recommend physical therapy to improve mobility and reduce swelling. Order a complete blood count and basic metabolic panel to evaluate for underlying causes of edema. Follow up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
