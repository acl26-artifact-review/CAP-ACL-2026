Chief Complaint
Neck pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sharp, incapacitating pain in the neck. He has a history of multiple car crashes, including a crash in February of this year. He previously received therapy for a few months after the second crash and underwent surgery after the third crash, but he has been experiencing neck pain since this latest crash. He reports fatigue since the accident. His therapist said he should see a lawyer, and his mother said he should see a therapist. He was previously prescribed fentanyl, but he has not received a prescription for several weeks. He has been experiencing bruising and swelling in his neck.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited neck range of motion, with pain on palpation of the neck muscles and bony process. The patient reports pain with forward flexion and backward extension of the neck. He reports occasional dizziness and numbness in his left arm and right leg. He reports body pain. The examination does not reveal any bruising or swelling.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are most consistent with a neck sprain.

Plan
An MRI is ordered to further evaluate the neck. The clinician recommends applying ice to the affected area. The doctor recommends performing range-of-motion exercises. The doctor prescribed robaxin, to be taken every six to eight hours. A referral to physical therapy is considered if symptoms persist. Follow-up is scheduled in four weeks if symptoms do not improve.
