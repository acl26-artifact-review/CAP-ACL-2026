The patient reports a tick bite around his knee that has been present for over a week, causing burning and annoyance. He denies fever or chills. He reports no other joint pain beyond his typical arthritic pain. He states he went hunting two weeks ago and that hunting season is open. He mentions a type of dog training facility where he hunts. He reports no side effects from lisinopril. He generally monitors his blood sugar intake and reports feeling generally unwell. He has had a headache since the tick bite.

O:
The patient's vital signs are within normal limits: blood pressure is 120/70 mmHg, heart rate is 67 bpm, and temperature is 98.4°F. A bull's-eye rash is noted over the right knee. Physical exam reveals erythema and edema over the right patella with fluctuance. Knee range of motion is full. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. The heart exam is normal.

A:
The patient's presentation is concerning for possible Lyme disease based on the right knee findings and the tick bite history. He has not been previously diagnosed with Lyme disease. The bull's-eye rash is consistent with Lyme disease.

P:
I am ordering doxycycline 100 mg twice daily for three weeks. I am ordering a Lyme titer and Western blot. I am ordering a lipid panel. I am continuing lisinopril 20 mg daily. I am ordering a hemoglobin A1c. I am ordering a basic metabolic panel. I will follow up on the patient's blood sugar control and blood pressure management.
