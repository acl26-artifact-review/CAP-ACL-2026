Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain and stiffness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain and stiffness for approximately two weeks, initially mild but progressively worsening. He describes the pain as aching and sharp, particularly with movement. The patient reports that the pain is worse in the morning and improves with activity. He denies any recent injury. He reports a history of osteoarthritis and currently takes clobetasol. He states the medication controlled a previous gout flare-up three months ago. He also reports numbness during typing.

Findings
Physical examination reveals right elbow tenderness to palpation and limited range of motion. No swelling is noted. Right elbow x-ray is ordered.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with osteoarthritis and possible nerve entrapment contributing to the numbness. Gout is ruled out given the patient's current medication and stable condition.

Plan
Order a right elbow x-ray. Prescribe meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily for pain and swelling. Prescribe colchicine 0.6 milligrams twice a day as needed for gout flare-ups. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
