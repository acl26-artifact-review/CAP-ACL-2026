Chief Complaint
Cough and right knee pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a cough that started approximately one week ago, accompanied by yellow sputum production. He describes feeling progressively worse over the last two days. He denies fever but feels warm. He reports some achiness and no shortness of breath. He also reports a right knee injury sustained this morning from a fall down the stairs while coughing. The patient does not have any known sick contacts. He is currently taking lisinopril 20mg daily and reports taking it regularly. He does not check his blood pressure or blood sugar levels. He takes a white pill twice daily, which he attributes to his wife putting it in his medication box. He is a teacher and is a Brown's fan. He reports twisting his right knee this morning while coughing and falling down the stairs.

Findings
Vital signs: Temperature 98.4°F, blood pressure 125/62 mmHg, heart rate 80 bpm, oxygen saturation 97% on room air. Facial exam reveals pain to palpation of the bilateral maxillary sinuses and erythema and edema of the nasal turbinates bilaterally with associated yellow discharge on nasal exam. Cervical lymphadenopathy is present bilaterally. Lung exam reveals coarse rhonchi bilaterally that clear with cough. Right knee exam reveals pain to palpation and decreased flexion and extension with ecchymosis to the right medial knee. The patient is able to bend and straighten his knee, but it hurts. There is no evidence of effusion. The patient is limping slightly. The patient is taking over-the-counter cough and cold medication, which provides minimal relief. Hemoglobin A1c is pending. Lipid panel is pending.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with a viral syndrome with mild bronchitis and a right medial collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
Prescribe guaifenesin 600 mg twice daily for cough. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for right knee pain. Order right knee x-ray. Refer to physical therapy for right knee. Order a lipid panel. Schedule follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Instruct patient to monitor symptoms and report any worsening via the patient portal. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily.
