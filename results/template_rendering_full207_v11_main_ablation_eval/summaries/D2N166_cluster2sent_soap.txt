S:
The patient reports low back pain that began two weeks ago after lifting a heavy object, characterized as both achy and sharp. He describes radiating pain down his left leg. He denies any numbness, tingling, or weakness. He has tried ibuprofen and acetaminophen with minimal relief. He reports a family history of back pain.

O:
Blood pressure is 130/70 mmHg. Respiratory rate is 18 breaths per minute. Heart sounds are regular and without murmurs. Lung auscultation is clear bilaterally. Back examination reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with right paravertebral tenderness. Straight leg test is positive. Reflexes are symmetrical.

A:
The patient presents with lumbar radiculopathy secondary to a herniated L4-L5 disc, likely contributing to his reported pain and radiating symptoms.

P:
I recommend a spinal injection with a strong NSAID and corticosteroid under fluoroscopy to reduce inflammation and provide symptomatic relief. I will schedule an appointment for you today to discuss this further and obtain informed consent. I will also provide you with written instructions and a pain management plan.
