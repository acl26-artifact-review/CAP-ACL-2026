Chief Complaint
Elevated blood glucose levels and recent hospital admission for management of hyperglycemia.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing elevated blood glucose levels, leading to his recent admission to the emergency room for treatment. He attributes this to increased stress and strain over the past couple of weeks, coupled with inconsistent sugar intake. He was treated in the emergency room for hyperglycemia and placed on metformin. He reports experiencing anxiety, which has been somewhat improved with the weekend approaching. He has not experienced any recent seizures.

Findings
Vital signs are stable. Physical examination reveals trace pitting edema to the lower extremities. Laboratory results demonstrate a current blood glucose level of 162 mg/dL and a hemoglobin A1c of 8%.

Assessment
Newly diagnosed diabetes mellitus.

Plan
Continue metformin 500 mg twice daily. Order a follow-up hemoglobin A1c test. Schedule a neurology consultation to review epilepsy management. Arrange follow-up in four weeks to assess glycemic control and adjust medication as needed.
