Chief Complaint
Patient reports falling and experiencing chest pain and shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports falling outside, resulting in ankle swelling and chest pain. He initially dismissed the chest pain but developed shortness of breath. He reports inconsistent medication adherence, taking bumex sporadically and occasionally missing doses. He admits to struggling with dietary adherence, frequently consuming salty foods and snacks, including chips and pizza. He has been attempting to quit smoking, having stopped approximately two years ago, with only one cigarette smoked per week recently. He denies sleep disturbances and chest pain beyond the recent episode.

Findings
Physical examination reveals blood pressure of 128/72 mmHg. Vital signs are stable. Neck examination is unremarkable, with no jugular venous distension. Lung auscultation is clear bilaterally. A grade 2 systolic ejection murmur is heard. Lower extremities demonstrate trace edema. Echocardiogram demonstrates a preserved ejection fraction of 55% and mild to moderate mitral regurgitation. Weight is not currently recorded.

Assessment
The patient presents with symptoms consistent with congestive heart failure, likely exacerbated by uncontrolled hypertension and dietary indiscretion. Mild mitral regurgitation is present.

Plan
Continue bumex 2 mg daily. Prescribe cozaar 100 mg daily. Continue norvasc 5 mg daily. Order a renal artery ultrasound to rule out obstructive causes of hypertension. Refer to nutrition for dietary counseling, focusing on reducing sodium intake. Instruct the patient to weigh themselves daily and report any weight gain exceeding 3 pounds in two days. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and adjust medications as needed.
