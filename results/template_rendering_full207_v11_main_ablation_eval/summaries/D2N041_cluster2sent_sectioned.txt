Chief Complaint
Patient reports fatigue and fever.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue for the past four days and felt feverish last night, noting sweating. The patient reports difficulty swallowing, which occurs every time they swallow. The patient denies having chills. The patient reports a stuffy nose but no significant discomfort. The patient reports swollen tonsils. The patient reports no stomach pain. The patient drinks water primarily, with occasional wine. The patient reports blood pressure fluctuations, ranging from 120/80 to 140/80. The patient reports blood sugar fluctuations, ranging from 80-90 in the morning to 140 at night. The patient takes metformin 1000mg twice daily with breakfast and dinner.

Findings
The doctor observes a low-grade fever (104°F), blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg, and heart rate of 84 bpm. The doctor notes facial examination reveals no pain upon palpation of the sinuses, nasal edema and erythema, and bilateral exudates in the throat. The doctor notes cervical lymphadenopathy on the right side. The doctor observes coarse rhonchi at the lung bases that clear with coughing. The doctor notes a regular heart rhythm without murmurs or rubs.

Assessment
The patient presents with symptoms consistent with a likely upper respiratory infection and possible strep throat.

Plan
The doctor plans a rapid strep test, amoxicillin 500mg three times daily for 10 days, lidocaine swish and swallow, and increased lisinopril to 40mg daily. The doctor plans a lipid panel and hemoglobin A1c testing.
