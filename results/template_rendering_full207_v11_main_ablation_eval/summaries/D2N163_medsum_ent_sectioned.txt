Chief Complaint
Newly diagnosed hypercholesterolemia.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being newly diagnosed with elevated cholesterol after an annual checkup and seeks evaluation. He enjoys regular cycling and a diet including chocolate chip cookies. He reports a family history of a cousin who died of a heart attack at age 44 and mentions his grandparents also had similar issues. He has been living as a non-binary individual for two years and is focused on maintaining good health. He reports no current smoking exposure and no exposure to secondhand smoke.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal vital signs, including blood pressure. The heart and lungs are clear to auscultation. A thorough eye exam reveals no abnormalities. The neck exam is unremarkable, with no thyroidomegaly. Abdominal exam is also unremarkable.

Assessment
Elevated cholesterol levels.

Plan
I will order a full cholesterol panel, including glucose, a complete metabolic panel, thyroid studies, and liver panels, as well as genetic testing to assess for familial risk. I will also schedule a follow-up appointment in three to six months after the initial results and a consultation with a registered dietitian to discuss dietary modifications. I recommend continuing the patient's current activity level, including cycling, and will schedule a follow-up appointment to monitor progress.
