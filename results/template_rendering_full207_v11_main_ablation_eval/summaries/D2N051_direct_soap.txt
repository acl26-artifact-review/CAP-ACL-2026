A:
The patient has a non-healing ulcer on his right foot. The ulcer is likely related to his diabetes and vascular status. The patient's diabetes is not well controlled, contributing to impaired wound healing. The patient's vascular status is a concern given his history of heart attack and current medications. The patient's neuropathy may contribute to the ulceration.

P:
Order an arterial ultrasound of the lower extremities. Arrange a podiatry consultation for wound care management. Order a hemoglobin A1c test. Schedule a follow-up appointment with the primary care physician to optimize diabetes management. Continue aspirin as prescribed. Refer to a vascular surgeon for consideration of potential interventions to improve blood flow to the foot if wound healing does not progress.
