Chief Complaint
The patient reports to the clinic for a surgical consult regarding a biopsy of a lesion on her back.

History of Present Illness
The patient first noticed a lesion on her right inferior back approximately one month ago. Her dermatologist performed a biopsy, which revealed melanoma. She reports that her mother and sister both had melanoma, and her grandfather had pancreatic cancer. She denies any prior skin cancers and has no other known health problems. She is allergic to augmentin. The patient owns an auto repair shop and occasionally smokes. She had previous surgeries for her gall bladder and appendix.

Findings
Examination revealed a one centimeter shave biopsy site with no residual pigmentation. There is a soft lymph node in the right groin, which is nontender. No other suspicious moles were identified on examination. The right inguinal lymph node felt slightly abnormal.

Assessment
The patient presents with a newly diagnosed melanoma. Biopsy revealed an intermediate thickness melanoma, classified as Clark level 4 with a Breslow thickness of 0.7 millimeters. The lesion is classified as superficial spreading.

Plan
I recommend an ultrasound of the right groin to rule out metastatic disease. If the ultrasound is normal, I recommend a wide local excision with a one to two centimeter margin. Skin graft closure may be needed. I will schedule the ultrasound and discuss the procedure with the patient. The patient should continue to follow up with her dermatologist regularly and should apply sunscreen diligently. I will follow up with the patient in four weeks if the ultrasound is normal and the excision is performed.
