Chief Complaint
Neck pain following a car accident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports neck pain following a car accident approximately one week ago. He describes a sudden forward movement of his head and neck during the collision, leading to persistent pain. The pain is rated as 7/10 and is exacerbated by neck movement. He has tried ibuprofen, which provided mild relief. He denies any prior neck injuries or surgeries. He reports headaches early on, which have persisted. He denies any hearing or visual disturbances. He has a history of playing football years ago but denies any neck issues related to that activity. He did not seek emergency room care after the accident, citing the cost of a $500 visit. He describes the pain as localized to the back of his neck and not radiating to his upper back or shoulders.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the cervical soft tissues. Neck flexion elicits pain. Lateral bending elicits pain. Rotation of the neck is limited and painful. No signs of swelling or bruising are observed. Neurological exam is unremarkable, with no reported sensory or motor deficits.

Assessment
Based on the history and physical examination, the patient is likely experiencing a whiplash-associated neck injury, consistent with a cervical strain. The MRI is ordered to rule out concussion or other structural abnormalities.

Plan
I am prescribing motrin 600 mg every 6-8 hours and flexeril 10 mg every 12 hours as needed. I have ordered an MRI to assess for concussion or other structural abnormalities. I am referring the patient to physical rehabilitation or a chiropractor for further evaluation and treatment once the MRI results are available. I have scheduled a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.
