Chief Complaint
Reports shortness of breath and inability to lie flat.

History of Present Illness
Christina reports that she started experiencing ankle swelling several weeks ago, which she was unable to resolve with rest and elevation. She describes significant difficulty breathing, particularly when lying flat, requiring propping herself up in bed for comfort. She contacted emergency services due to her inability to talk and the difficulty breathing. She reports a trip to the ER and subsequent hospital stay. She admits to enjoying salt and vinegar potato chips and eating them frequently, and she's been eating out a lot. She's been trying to watch her diet, but has had some slips. She reports feeling like she's been sleeping well lately. She's been taking her medications as prescribed, but has been struggling to remember to take them consistently. She has a blood pressure cuff at home but hasn't been using it regularly. She's been having difficulty with incontinence.

Findings
Vital signs: Blood pressure 128/72 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, respirations 18 breaths per minute, oxygen saturation 96%. Lung exam is clear bilaterally. Jugular venous distension is absent. Lower extremities show trace edema. A two over six systolic ejection murmur is heard. Echocardiogram shows a preserved ejection fraction of 55% and mild-to-moderate mitral regurgitation.

Assessment
The patient presents with heart failure, likely exacerbated by dietary indiscretion and uncontrolled hypertension. She also has a history of insomnia and incontinence.

Plan
Continue on bumex 2 mg daily. Recommend dietary modification, avoiding salty foods and limiting potato chip consumption. Encourage daily weight monitoring and call if weight increases by three pounds in two days. Order a renal artery ultrasound. Schedule a consultation with a registered dietitian. Order a pill box and encourage setting an alarm on her phone to aid medication adherence. Follow up in three months.
