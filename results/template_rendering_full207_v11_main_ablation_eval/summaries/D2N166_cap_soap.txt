S:
The patient reports they have been having low back pain for the past two weeks, which started after lifting a box of books. He describes the pain as aching and sharp, radiating down his left leg. He denies numbness, tingling, or weakness. He reports that sitting is usually okay, but bending forward causes pain. He denies any loss of bowel or bladder control. The patient tried ibuprofen and Tylenol, which provided some relief.

O:
The patient's blood pressure is 130/70 mmHg, respiratory rate is 18, and heart rate is regular and at 68 bpm. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Palpation reveals tenderness at the L4-L5 disc space with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness. Straight leg raise test is positive. Neurological exam reveals intact sensation and reflexes.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a lumbar herniated disc with radiculopathy. A spinal injection with a strong NSAID and corticosteroid is recommended.

P:
Order a spinal injection with fluoroscopy under guidance. Prescribe a strong NSAID and corticosteroid. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess response to treatment. Provide the patient with paperwork outlining the procedure and potential risks.
