Chief Complaint
Upper respiratory infection and shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling tired and shortness of breath, particularly when lifting or carrying heavy bags of soil over the past week. He states he likely caught a cold and feels it's gotten worse. He denies any history of seasonal allergies. He reports taking medication for depression and monitoring his sugar levels while working, but not consistently on weekends. He denies nausea or vomiting. He reports feeling a little warm but attributes it to exertion.

Findings
Physical exam reveals scattered rhonchi bilaterally in the lungs, which clear with coughing. There is some edema noted in the lower extremities and pain to palpation of the elbows bilaterally. Chest x-ray is normal. Diabetic lab values show a slightly elevated hemoglobin A1c of 8%.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with a viral upper respiratory infection. The patient's history of type 2 diabetes and hypertension are being managed appropriately.

Plan
Order a COVID test to rule out COVID-19. Prescribe robitussin for cough and ibuprofen/acetaminophen for fever. Continue lisinopril 20mg daily and metformin 1000mg twice daily. Order a lipid panel. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
