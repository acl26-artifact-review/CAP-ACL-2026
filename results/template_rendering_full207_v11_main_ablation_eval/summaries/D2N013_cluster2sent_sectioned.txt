Chief Complaint
John presents with a high blood sugar and recent emergency room visit for management of hyperglycemia.

History of Present Illness
John reports being admitted to the emergency room due to significantly elevated blood sugar levels. He was treated in the emergency room and is currently taking metformin. He also reports experiencing anxiety, with a possible exacerbation during the winter months. He has a history of epilepsy, last seen by his neurologist approximately three months ago. He denies recent seizures. He reports trace pitting edema to his lower extremities. He has not experienced chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, vomiting, or dizziness. He reports that he did not receive medication in the emergency room.

Findings
John is a 55-year-old male with a hemoglobin A1c of 8% and a current blood glucose reading of 162 mg/dL. Physical examination reveals normal lung sounds and a regular heart rhythm. Vitals are stable. No acute findings were noted on physical exam.

Assessment
The patient presents with newly diagnosed type 2 diabetes mellitus and persistent hyperglycemia.

Plan
Order hemoglobin A1c for future monitoring. Continue metformin 500 mg twice daily. Continue keppra. Schedule a follow-up appointment with the patient in four weeks to assess response to treatment and adjust medication as needed.
