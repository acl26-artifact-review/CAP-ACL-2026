Chief Complaint
Left elbow pain and stiffness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left elbow pain that has been present for several weeks, exacerbated by prolonged typing and overhead positioning at her desk. She describes difficulty opening and closing her left elbow, with significant stiffness, particularly after work. The patient reports no recent trauma. She has been consistently taking Keppra for epilepsy and reports feeling somewhat down and stressed, but denies any thoughts of self-harm. She has been attending therapy and has not pursued medication for her depression. She reports that her peptic ulcer disease has been well-controlled with protonix.

Findings
Physical examination reveals erythema and edema of the left elbow. Palpation elicits tenderness at the olecranon. Range of motion is limited due to stiffness. Radial pulse is present and stable. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. Left arm x-ray demonstrates no fracture. Endoscopy revealed gastritis consistent with her peptic ulcer disease. Nasal congestion is present.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with olecranon bursitis. She continues to have stable seizure control with Keppra and is managing her depression with therapy. Her peptic ulcer disease is currently well-controlled.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for left elbow pain. Order a refill of Keppra 500 mg twice daily. Initiate lexapro 10 mg once daily for depression. Continue protonix 40 mg once daily before meals. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess response to treatment. Advise the patient to avoid repetitive overhead motions and to apply ice to the affected area.
