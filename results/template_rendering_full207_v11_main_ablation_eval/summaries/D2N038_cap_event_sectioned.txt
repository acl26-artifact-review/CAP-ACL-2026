Chief Complaint
Reports having diabetes and a positive hepatitis C diagnosis.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports having diabetes and a positive hepatitis C diagnosis. He states he was surprised by the positive lab results, as he has not experienced symptoms. He previously used intravenous drugs but has been clean for fifteen years. He also admits to smoking one to two cigarettes per day and occasionally drinking alcohol. He reports no family history of diabetes or other significant medical conditions. A physical exam reveals a grade two out of six systolic ejection murmur, clear lungs without wheezes, and an abdomen that is soft with no hepatosplenomegaly. The patient's vitals are within normal limits.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade two out of six systolic ejection murmur, clear lungs without wheezes, and an abdomen that is soft with no hepatosplenomegaly. The patient's vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient has hepatitis C and diabetes. He is currently experiencing no symptoms of either condition.

Plan
The plan includes ordering additional blood work to confirm the hepatitis C diagnosis and assess liver fibrosis. An ultrasound elastography will be performed to determine the severity of liver disease. The patient will be scheduled for a follow-up appointment in three weeks to discuss the results and determine the appropriate course of treatment. The patient's family physician will be contacted to coordinate care.
