Chief Complaint
Right flank pain and urinary symptoms related to a suspected kidney stone.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right flank pain approximately one week ago, initially attributed to heavy lifting but later confirmed as related to a kidney stone. He describes sudden onset of pain, sweating, nausea, and reports straining to urinate. He notes blood in the urine and has been using a strainer to collect stones. The patient reports a prior stroke.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the right flank. Vital signs are stable. CT scan confirms a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter with no evidence of hydronephrosis. Laboratory results are pending BMP and urinalysis.

Assessment
The patient has a confirmed kidney stone in the proximal right ureter.

Plan
Recommend increased fluid intake to facilitate stone passage. Prescribe oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours for pain, as needed. Continue acetaminophen for breakthrough pain. Order BMP and urinalysis to evaluate for infection and rule out other causes. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks to assess stone passage and discuss potential lithotripsy if necessary.
