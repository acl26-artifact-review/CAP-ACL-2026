S:
The patient reports a finger injury sustained while skiing this past weekend, specifically a jammed index finger. He describes the injury as occurring during a fall down double diamonds and double black diamond slopes. He reported pain approximately five days ago. He took ibuprofen, but it did not provide relief. He rates his pain as a 7 out of 10. He has chronic constipation and takes miralax, which he reports helps. He had his appendix removed at age seven. He denies any other injuries sustained during the skiing incident.

O:
The right index finger x-ray demonstrates no fractures or bony abnormalities. Palpation of the DIP joint reveals tenderness with palpation, percussion, and with joint movement. The patient reports pain with all three maneuvers.

A:
The patient has a sprain of the distal interphalangeal joint (DIP joint) of the right index finger.

P:
Rescribe a splint for the right index finger. Prescribe 14 milligrams of mobic daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess healing. Order the medications and procedures mentioned.
