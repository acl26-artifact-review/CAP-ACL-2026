Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, which began approximately one week ago. He describes the pain as sharp and intermittent, and it worsens with use. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is present at rest and with activity. He states he has tried over-the-counter pain relievers with minimal relief.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the distal volar aspect of the left hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No swelling or erythema is noted.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a possible lateral carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Order nerve conduction studies to evaluate for carpal tunnel syndrome. Recommend wrist splinting and activity modification. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
