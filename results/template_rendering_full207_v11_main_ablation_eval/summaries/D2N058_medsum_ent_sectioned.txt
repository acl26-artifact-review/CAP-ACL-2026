Chief Complaint
Follow-up after an anterior ST-emi and subsequent drug-eluting stent placement, with ongoing management of reduced ejection fraction and moderate mitral regurgitation.

He has been able to enjoy some spring weather and is hoping to get back to walking the trails behind the Rex Center and taking nature photographs. He reports that his heart pumps a little low and he might retain fluid on his legs if he's not careful.

Findings

Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Extremities are not swollen. The cath site is clean and dry with no hematoma. The radial pulse is palpable. An EKG shows normal sinus rhythm with expected changes post-MI.

Assessment
The patient's primary diagnosis remains coronary artery disease with reduced ejection fraction. Secondary diagnoses include moderate mitral regurgitation and hypertension.

The patient will follow up with the clinic in two months for repeat labs and echocardiogram results.
