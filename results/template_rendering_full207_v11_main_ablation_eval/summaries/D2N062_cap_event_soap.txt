S:
The patient reports difficulty swallowing certain foods and pain when swallowing, particularly larger pieces, over the past several weeks. He also reports epigastric pain and occasional abdominal discomfort. He has gained approximately ten pounds over the last month. He admits to increased stress related to a recent move and a diet consisting of pizza and burgers.

O:
Blood pressure is 130/70 mmHg. Heart rate is 72 bpm. Oxygen saturation is 100%. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. No rash, lesions, or bruising noted. Esophageal examination reveals mild narrowing in the mid and lower portions of the esophagus as seen on barium swallow.

A:
Cute esophagitis is the primary diagnosis. Weight gain is a contributing factor.

P:
Rescribe proton pump inhibitor (PPI) - 40 mg daily. Prescribe carafate - 1 gram four times daily for one month. Schedule an upper endoscopy for follow-up. Advise the patient to avoid citrus fruits, spicy foods, alcohol, and caffeine. Recommend eating slowly and chewing food thoroughly. Schedule a follow-up appointment next week.
