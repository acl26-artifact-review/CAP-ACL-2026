Chief Complaint
Right knee injury following a soccer game.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he sustained a right knee injury while playing pickup soccer over the weekend. He describes a 'pop' sensation and immediate pain following a plank kick. The pain has persisted for approximately three days, with some swelling initially, but the swelling has decreased. He reports difficulty with knee flexion and extension, describing a feeling of looseness and a need to assist in straightening the leg. He rates the pain as 6-7 out of 10, and reports taking ibuprofen with some relief. He has been able to bear weight on the leg, but feels it is unstable. He reports a limp upon ambulation.

Findings
Physical examination reveals significant effusion in the right knee, mild ecchymosis. Palpation of the medial joint line elicits tenderness. Range of motion is limited; passive range of motion is reduced to 115 degrees of flexion and 20-30 degrees of extension secondary to pain. Lachman's test is positive. Neurovascular examination of the bilateral lower extremities is intact. Muscle strength is rated as 5/5. The patient's gait demonstrates a marked right-sided limp.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is highly suggestive of an anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) tear.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI to evaluate the integrity of the ACL. I will also order meloxicam 50 mg daily to reduce swelling and pain. Please wear a knee immobilizer and use crutches when walking to minimize stress on the knee. Please follow up in four weeks, or sooner if symptoms persist. Please slow your jogging significantly until we have the MRI results.
