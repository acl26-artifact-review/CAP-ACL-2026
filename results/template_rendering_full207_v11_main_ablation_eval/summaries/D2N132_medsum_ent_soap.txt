S:
The patient reports back pain following a fall during a surprise birthday party, describing the injury as a twisting motion and subsequent impact with a staircase railing. He denies pain radiating down his legs. He reports some stiffness with rotation and reports no numbness or pain. He has a history of lumbar fusion. He is currently taking ibuprofen for anxiety and continues to take medication for anxiety. He reports experiencing more pain when lying flat and with twisting motions. He denies any pain shooting down his legs when lifted.

O:
The patient underwent an X-ray which revealed no significant abnormalities or compression fractures, except for the previously placed lumbar fusion. Examination reveals no focal neurological deficits. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a lower back strain, likely exacerbated by the original fusion and the recent injury. There is no evidence of nerve compression or radiculopathy.

P:
Rescribe ibuprofen 800mg three times daily with food. Advise ice and heat alternating on the back. Encourage ambulation. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week if symptoms do not improve or worsen. Order medications and procedures.
