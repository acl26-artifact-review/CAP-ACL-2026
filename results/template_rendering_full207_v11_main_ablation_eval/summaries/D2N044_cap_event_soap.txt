S:
The patient reports a six-month history of knee pain, described as a deep achy pain behind the kneecaps, exacerbated by sitting and going up and down stairs. He reports taking ibuprofen and aleve to manage the pain, but doesn't find it completely effective. He mentions tenderness upon palpation of the knees and a positive patellar grind test. He states he's a runner who has had to significantly reduce his mileage due to the pain, and he enjoys cycling. He works from home and his office is downstairs. He reports no prior knee injuries.

O:
Blood pressure is 120/70, heart rate is 60, respiratory rate is 14. Gait is normal. Lower extremity strength is good, but abduction strength is reduced (3/5 bilaterally). Lachman, anterior and posterior drawer, and McMurray tests are negative bilaterally. Neurological exam is normal. X-rays show no fractures or osteoarthritis. No redness, swelling, or effusion is noted.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome.

P:
Recommend physical therapy to address lower extremity strength and running mechanics. Continue ibuprofen or aleve as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 4 weeks to assess progress.
