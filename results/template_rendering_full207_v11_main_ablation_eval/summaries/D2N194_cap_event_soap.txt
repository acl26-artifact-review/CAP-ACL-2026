S:
The patient reports a left shoulder pain for approximately three weeks, exacerbated by activity and overhead reaching. He describes the pain as waxing and waning, and reports it is constant, particularly when lying on his left side. He has been taking Tylenol every six to eight hours for the pain, which provides some relief. He denies any numbness or tingling in his arm or hand.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder, tenderness to palpation of the greater tuberosity, and brisk capillary refill bilaterally. Neurological exam is intact. The x-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

P:
Order an MRI of the left shoulder to rule out other pathology. Refer the patient for physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. Continue Tylenol PRN for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately two weeks.
