Chief Complaint
Sore throat and difficulty swallowing for four days.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a sore throat for four days, accompanied by difficulty swallowing and chills. He also notes some nasal congestion and reports feeling sweaty, though he didn't take his temperature. He mentions that his kids have been having runny noses and coughs. He reports that he drinks mostly water and no wine. He was vaccinated for COVID but felt sick for two days afterward. He has a history of strep throat as a child but no subsequent cases. He works as a cashier at a supermarket. He is currently taking lisinopril 20mg daily with his multivitamin and vitamin D, metformin 1000mg twice daily, and reports that his blood pressure is often elevated. He is also a fan of the New York Yankees.

Findings
The patient's vital signs reveal a low-grade fever of 100.4°F, blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg, and a heart rate of 84 bpm. A physical exam reveals bilateral erythema and edema of the peritonsillar space with exudates present bilaterally. The patient's throat is erythematous. Cervical lymphadenopathy is present on the right side. Lung auscultation reveals coarse rhonchi at the bases, which clear with coughing.

Assessment
The patient presents with signs of an upper respiratory infection, with concern for possible strep throat based on the physical exam findings. He also has hypertension and diabetes.

Plan
I'm going to swab you for a rapid strep test and if that's positive, I'm going to prescribe amoxicillin 500mg three times a day for ten days. I'm also going to give you some lidocaine swish and swallow to help with some of the pain. I want to increase the lisinopril to 40mg once a day. I'm going to order a hemoglobin A1c to monitor his diabetes. Please follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
