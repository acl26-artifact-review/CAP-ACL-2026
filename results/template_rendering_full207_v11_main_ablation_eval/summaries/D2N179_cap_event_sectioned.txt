Chief Complaint
Numbness and tingling in both hands.

History of Present Illness
Mr. Cook reports numbness and tingling in both hands for approximately four weeks, following a car accident last December. He describes the symptoms as worsening over the past month, particularly at night. He notes mild stiffness in his neck, related to a contusion sustained during the accident. He has been wearing splints on both wrists, but the symptoms persist. He has been taking coreg for hypertension and lexapro for anxiety. He reports some nail bed changes and dryness, and has been experiencing some muscle pain. He denies any history of thyroid issues. He reports mild bloating and excessive appetite.

Findings
Examination reveals decreased sensation to pinprick in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand and the fourth and fifth fingers of the left hand. Tinel's signs are positive at both wrists and both elbows. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
Based on the patient's symptoms, exam findings, and history, bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome is the most likely diagnosis. Further evaluation with nerve conduction studies is warranted to rule out other causes and confirm the diagnosis.

Plan
I am ordering an EMG to evaluate the nerve conduction studies. I will also refer the patient to an orthopedist for consideration of carpal tunnel release surgery if conservative management fails. The patient should continue wearing his splints. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
