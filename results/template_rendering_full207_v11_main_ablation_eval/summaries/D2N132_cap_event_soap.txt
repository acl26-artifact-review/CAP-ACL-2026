S:
The patient reports a back injury sustained at a surprise birthday party involving a fall down a staircase. He describes pain exacerbated by lying flat and with twisting motions, and reports no radicular symptoms. He takes ibuprofen for the pain and also takes medication for anxiety.

O:
The examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the lumbar region with increased pain upon rotation. Neurological examination is unremarkable, with no leg pain or numbness on lifting or dorsiflexion. X-ray shows no compression fractures or signs of acute instability beyond the previous fusion site.

A:
The patient presents with a lumbar strain secondary to a recent injury.

P:
Rescribe ibuprofen 800mg three times daily with food. Recommend ice and heat alternating on the lower back. Schedule follow-up in one week if symptoms persist or worsen.
