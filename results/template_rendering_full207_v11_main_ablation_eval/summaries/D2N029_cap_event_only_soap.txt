O:
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the distal left wrist. Range of motion is full and without limitation. There is no swelling or erythema. Neurological exam is intact, with full strength and sensation in the left hand.

A:
The patient's left hand pain is concerning for possible carpal tunnel syndrome or other musculoskeletal issues. Given the family history of melanoma, a thorough evaluation is warranted.

P:
Order wrist X-ray to evaluate for carpal tunnel syndrome. Refer to orthopedics for further evaluation if X-ray is abnormal. Advise patient to avoid repetitive movements and use ergonomic equipment.
