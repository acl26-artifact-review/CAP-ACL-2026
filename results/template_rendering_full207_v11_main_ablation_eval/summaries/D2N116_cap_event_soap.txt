S:
The patient reports left shoulder pain for several weeks, exacerbated by yard work projects. He describes the pain as tenderness on the outside of the shoulder with arm elevation, and reports no numbness. He has been taking Advil and icing the area. He also reports soreness in his feet and ankles, attributing it to yard work, and mentions trying epsom salt soaks. He denies any prior significant shoulder injuries but recalls a baseball injury as a teenager. The patient reports no fever and his vital signs are within normal limits.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the right shoulder, with tenderness to palpation at the greater tuberosity. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds. Neurological exam is intact. Blood pressure is 160/104. Oxygen saturation is 99%.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy, likely exacerbated by the recent yard work activities. Further investigation with an MRI is warranted to rule out other pathology.

P:
Order an MRI of the right shoulder. Refer the patient to physical therapy for 6-8 weeks. Continue Advil as needed for pain relief. Consider a corticosteroid injection if symptoms do not improve after physical therapy.
