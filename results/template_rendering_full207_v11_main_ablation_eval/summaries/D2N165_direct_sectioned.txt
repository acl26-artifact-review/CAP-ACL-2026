Chief Complaint
Right knee pain for approximately six months.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a deep, aching pain in his right knee that has persisted for approximately six months. He recalls no specific inciting event. The pain is exacerbated by activities such as rising from a seated position, watching television, and climbing stairs. He reports some creaking sensations in his knees. He has previously tried ice and ace bandages and occasionally takes ibuprofen or aleve. He enjoys running, typically five to six miles daily, but now can only manage half a mile before experiencing significant pain. He primarily runs on a treadmill during the winter and enjoys outdoor runs when weather permits. He denies prior knee injuries, except for a broken arm in sixth grade.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal gait. Strength is 3/5 for abduction bilaterally. Knee exam reveals no erythema, warmth, or effusion. Palpation of the retinacular retinaculum is tender. The patellar grind test is positive. Lachman's anterior and posterior drawer tests and McMurray tests are negative bilaterally. X-rays of both knees show no fractures or osteoarthritis. Vital signs are within normal limits. Neurological exam reveals symmetrical patella and Achilles reflexes.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history, physical examination, and review of the MRI, the patient's symptoms are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome.

Plan
Recommend physical therapy to address lower extremity strength, mobility, and running mechanics. Continue ibuprofen or aleve as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as necessary. Encourage the patient to take it easy from running for now and continue with gentle range-of-motion exercises.
