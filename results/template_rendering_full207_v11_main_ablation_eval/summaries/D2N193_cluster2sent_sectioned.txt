Chief Complaint
Patient reports fatigue and a hacking cough for the past three days.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue for the past two months, with a hacking cough and green phlegm for approximately three days. He denies blood in stool, fever, shortness of breath, body aches, nausea, or vomiting. The patient reports seasonal allergies around spring and drinking lots of water. He reports feeling okay on vacation, but started feeling poorly after returning from vacation. He was around a family member who was sick. The patient continues metformin 500mg daily and reports difficulty with sugar intake.

Findings
The clinician examines the patient's sinuses and finds tenderness. The clinician examines the patient's mouth and finds erythema. The clinician finds anterior cervical adenopathy. The clinician hears a regular heart rate and rhythm with a murmur. The clinician finds clear lungs and no edema in the lower extremities. Blood pressure is around 140/72.

Assessment
The clinician assesses an upper respiratory infection.

Plan
The clinician recommends rest and symptom management. The clinician recommends a COVID test. The clinician increases hydrochlorothiazide to 25mg daily. The clinician recommends a hemoglobin A1c checked every three months.
