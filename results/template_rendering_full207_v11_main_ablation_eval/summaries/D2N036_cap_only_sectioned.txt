Chief Complaint
Left hand pain and numbness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand, primarily at night, for several months. He also experiences numbness in his fingers, particularly the little finger, and reports weakness in the left hand. The patient reports dropping things occasionally. He denies a history of rheumatoid arthritis. He states he drinks a beer or two on Fridays and Saturdays. He describes repetitive type movements. The patient reports tingling in his fingers.

Findings
Examination reveals decreased sensation in the left hand. The patient demonstrates weakness with grip strength testing.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are most consistent with peripheral neuropathy, likely secondary to repetitive strain injury.

Plan
The doctor will have a nurse in to assess the patient's current medications. The doctor will discharge the patient. The doctor will see the patient in two weeks. Order nerve conduction studies. Initiate physical therapy for repetitive strain injury. Advise patient on ergonomic adjustments to reduce repetitive movements.
