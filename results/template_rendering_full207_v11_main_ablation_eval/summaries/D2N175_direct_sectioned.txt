Chief Complaint
Abnormal laboratory results, including elevated blood sugars.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports elevated blood sugars on routine laboratory testing, prompting this visit. She has been taking 1000 milligrams of metformin daily and reports maintaining a diet focused on avoiding sugars and engaging in daily power walking. She denies any symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. She confirms checking her blood sugars in the morning. Her blood pressure has been stable on current medication.

Findings
Physical examination reveals clear lung sounds, normal heart sounds with a systolic ejection murmur of 3/6 at point of maximal impulse. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. Lower extremities are without edema. Oxygen saturation is 98% on room air. Blood pressure is 128/78 mmHg. Temperature is 98.6°F.

Assessment
Type 2 diabetes mellitus, poorly controlled.

Plan
Initiate Jardiance 10 milligrams daily. Order a lipid panel and CBC. Continue current lisinopril therapy. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess hemoglobin A1c and adjust medication as needed. Advise the patient to continue with her current diet and exercise regimen.
