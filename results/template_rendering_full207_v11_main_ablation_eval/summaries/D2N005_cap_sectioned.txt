Chief Complaint
Pain in the right middle finger following a motor vehicle accident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the right middle finger following a motor vehicle accident approximately five days ago. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, exacerbated by pressure. He states he became angry during the accident and, in a moment of frustration, flipped the driver of the other vehicle the bird. He reports no other injuries. The patient denies any prior history of similar injuries or conditions. He is currently taking digoxin for atrial fibrillation.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal phalanx of the right middle finger. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable. An X-ray confirms a distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger.

Assessment
The patient has a distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger.

Plan
Prescribe tramadol 50 mg every six hours as needed for pain for two days. Provide a finger splint and schedule a follow-up X-ray in two weeks. }
