Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies recent trauma and reports partial relief with acetaminophen. He also reports a history of anxiety and GERD.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. Blood pressure is 138/82 mmHg. Heart rate is 78 bpm.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy, complicated by a history of anxiety and GERD.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs. Initiate physical therapy. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
