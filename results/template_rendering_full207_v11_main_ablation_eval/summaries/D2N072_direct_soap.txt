S:
The patient reports right finger pain following a skiing injury on Sunday, involving a moguls jump where he got his strap caught. He describes the pain as worsening with movement. He has been taking ibuprofen with limited relief. The patient reports constipation and takes miralax. He had his appendix removed at age 18.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness over the distal phalanx of the right index finger and the distal interphalangeal joint. Range of motion is limited due to pain. X-rays demonstrate no fractures and normal alignment of the finger joints.

A:
The patient has a right hand index finger contusion.

P:
Rescribe an aluminum foam splint and ibuprofen 600 mg every six hours for one week. Schedule follow-up if symptoms do not improve within one week.
