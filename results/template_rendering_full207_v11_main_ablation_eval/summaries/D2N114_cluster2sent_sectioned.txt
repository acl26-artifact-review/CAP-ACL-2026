Chief Complaint
Increasingly severe headaches, particularly with exercise.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of headaches since adolescence, initially associated with menstruation, but now occurring more frequently and with greater severity. He also reports experiencing tinnitus for several months and recently, blurred vision intermittently. He previously experienced tumor growth but has since transitioned to Nike. He is currently attempting to increase exercise and weight loss but finds that exercise exacerbates his headaches.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal cranial nerves, gait, and heart sounds. Funduscopic examination demonstrates papilledema. Blood pressure is within normal limits. The patient reports no numbness or tingling. A recent MRI of the brain is pending approval from insurance. The physician notes that the front office team will work on obtaining pre-authorization for the MRI.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with idiopathic intracranial hypertension, complicated by papilledema. Further investigation is warranted to confirm the diagnosis and rule out other etiologies.

Plan
Order a recent MRI of the brain for confirmation of idiopathic intracranial hypertension. Schedule a follow-up spinal tap to evaluate intracranial pressure. Refer the patient to a neuro-ophthalmologist for a comprehensive eye examination. Continue monitoring for worsening visual symptoms and headache severity.
