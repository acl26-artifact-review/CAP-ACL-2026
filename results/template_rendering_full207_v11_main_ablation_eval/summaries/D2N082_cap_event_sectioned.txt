Chief Complaint
Patient reports anxiety regarding abnormal lab work and referral to you.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being anxious about abnormal lab work that prompted a referral to you. He states he doesn't really understand what the immunoglobulin A level means, but that she told him it was low. He has experienced frequent colds and always says his colds linger for a long time, but he's unsure if it's more than usual this winter. He denies any abdominal infections or frequent diarrhea. His family is generally healthy, and he owns a donut shop. He was started on metformin by his PCP for type two diabetes. He has tried to donate blood but was denied due to recent international travel. The patient reports that he has had a history of an immunoglobulin deficiency.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a regular rate and rhythm heart, clear lung sounds, and no lymphadenopathy. The patient's abdomen is soft and non-tender to palpation. Vital signs are within normal limits. Lab results show a low immunoglobulin A level.

Assessment
The patient's low immunoglobulin A level is consistent with an immunoglobulin deficiency. He may be experiencing frequent respiratory tract infections and/or sinus infections. He has been diagnosed with type two diabetes.

Plan
I will order additional blood work to evaluate for other antibody deficiencies. I will follow up with the patient in four weeks if symptoms persist or if there are any changes to his abdominal tract. I will also recommend that he inform any healthcare providers of his history of an immunoglobulin deficiency when receiving blood products.
