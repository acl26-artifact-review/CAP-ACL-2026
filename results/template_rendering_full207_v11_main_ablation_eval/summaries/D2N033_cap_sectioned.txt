Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a trampoline fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he injured his right knee four days ago while jumping on his children's trampoline. He describes a sudden 'pop' sensation at the time of the injury, followed by significant swelling. He took ibuprofen and applied ice, which provided some relief, but he continues to experience significant pain and difficulty with mobility. He reports pain with weight-bearing and range of motion, particularly with flexion and extension. He describes pain on the inside of the knee and some pain on the outside with palpation. He states that the pain is worse when pushing on the inside of the knee and when bending the knee back.

Findings
Physical examination reveals significant swelling in the patient's right knee. Palpation elicits tenderness on the medial aspect of the knee. Range of motion is limited due to pain with flexion and extension. The McMurray's test is negative. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are regular with no murmurs. Vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with an injury to the medial collateral ligament (MCL) and possible meniscus injury, given the reported pop and swelling. Further evaluation with an MRI is warranted to assess the extent of the injury.

Plan
The doctor orders a straight leg brace to immobilize the knee and reduce pain. The doctor prescribes mobic for pain relief and to reduce inflammation. The doctor recommends applying ice to the knee for 15 minutes every hour. The doctor orders an MRI to evaluate the extent of the injury. The doctor orders a repeat hemoglobin A1c to monitor diabetes control. The doctor refills the metformin prescription.
