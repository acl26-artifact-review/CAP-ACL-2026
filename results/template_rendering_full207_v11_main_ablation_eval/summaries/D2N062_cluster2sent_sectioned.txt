Chief Complaint
Difficulty swallowing and chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty swallowing over a period of time, particularly certain foods, accompanied by pain when swallowing down the chest. He reports increased stress related to a recent move and has experienced weight gain of approximately ten pounds over the past month. He denies recent trauma. The patient reports epigastric pain and has not observed any blood in his stool.

Findings
Blood pressure is 130/77 mmHg. Heart rate is 19 beats per minute, within normal limits. Oxygen saturation is 100%. The patient's abdomen is nondistended with active bowel sounds. Murphy's sign is negative, and there are no peritoneal signs. Lung auscultation is clear bilaterally. The patient's eyes are equal and reactive to light. A barium swallow revealed two areas of mild esophageal narrowing in the mid and lower portions of the esophagus, consistent with esophagitis.

Assessment
Acute esophagitis with esophageal narrowing.

Plan
Prescribe protonix 40 mg daily and carafate 1 gram four times daily for one month. Schedule an upper endoscopy for follow-up in one week. Advise the patient to decrease alcohol and caffeine intake and to modify his diet to include smaller, more frequent meals and avoid citrus fruits, spicy foods, and large pieces of food. Encourage slow chewing and swallowing. Follow up in one week for endoscopy results and further management.
