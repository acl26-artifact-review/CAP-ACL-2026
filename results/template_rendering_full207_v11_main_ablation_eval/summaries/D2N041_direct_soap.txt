S:
The patient reports a sore throat for four days, accompanied by chills and sweating. He reports difficulty swallowing and some nasal congestion. He states he drinks mostly water, with some wine at night. He denies stomach upset. He reports his tonsils are swollen. He notes that his household members have only mild runny noses.

O:
The patient's temperature is 104.4°F. Blood pressure is 130/80 mmHg. Heart rate is 84 bpm. Examination reveals edema and erythema of the nasal turbinates bilaterally with clear discharge. Cervical lymphadenopathy is present on the right. Lungs reveal coarse rhonchi at the bases that clear with cough. The heart is regular rate and rhythm with no murmurs.

A:
The patient presents with symptoms consistent with an upper respiratory infection, possibly including streptococcal pharyngitis.

P:
Order a rapid strep test. Prescribe amoxicillin 500 mg three times daily for 10 days. Recommend lidocaine throat spray for symptomatic relief. Advise ibuprofen as needed for pain and fever. Order a lipid panel. Increase lisinopril to 40 mg daily. Order a hemoglobin A1c. Follow up to assess response to treatment and adjust medications as needed.
