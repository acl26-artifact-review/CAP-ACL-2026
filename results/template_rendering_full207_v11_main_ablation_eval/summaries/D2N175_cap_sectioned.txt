Chief Complaint
Abnormal lab results, elevated blood sugars.

History of Present Illness
Natalie reports presenting today for a check-up following abnormal lab results indicating elevated blood sugars. She states she was 'shocked' when the results came back and hopes to figure out what's going on. She's currently taking 1000mg of metformin daily and reports a diet that's been 'really great lately' with avoiding sugars and power walking every day. She denies any symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. She confirms checking her blood sugars in the morning and reports no difficulty urinating or burning when she urinates. She reports stable high blood pressure and is monitoring it at home. She has a history of diabetes and an ACL repair on her right knee, which she reports has been going well with no issues.

Findings
Physical exam reveals blood pressure 132/78, oxygen saturation 98%, temperature 98.6°F. The patient's lungs are clear to auscultation. A systolic ejection murmur of approximately 3 over 6 is heard. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. Lower extremities are normal. No edema is noted. The patient denies headaches or belly pain.

Assessment
The patient presents with a primary diagnosis of diabetes mellitus with elevated hemoglobin A1c and elevated blood sugars. She also has a history of high blood pressure, which is currently well-controlled.

Plan
I am going to add Jardiance 10mg daily to the patient's regimen. I'm also ordering a lipid panel and a CMP. I'll continue the patient on lisinopril for their hypertension. Please follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist or if the hemoglobin A1c is not improved. Schedule physical therapy to address the knee and encourage continued power walking.
