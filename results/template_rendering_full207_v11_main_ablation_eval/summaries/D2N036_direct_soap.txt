S:
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his fingers and pain in his wrist, which has been ongoing for several months. He states that it started on its own and is more noticeable at night, sometimes waking him up. He reports that his hands feel asleep and that he has to shake them out to relieve the sensation. He also notes that he drops things occasionally due to decreased sensation. The patient drinks approximately two beers on Friday and Saturday evenings. He denies any history of rheumatoid arthritis.

O:
The patient's right hand demonstrates weakness on palpation compared to the left hand. Tinel's signs are positive bilaterally. Grip strength is decreased on the left hand. The doctor performed a physical exam including listening to the patient's heart and lungs, and palpating the patient's fingers.

A:
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome. Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome is suspected.

P:
Order an EMG study. Recommend activity modification, eliminating repetitive motions for two weeks. Prescribe a wrist splint to wear. Prescribe ibuprofen 600 mg every six hours as needed.
