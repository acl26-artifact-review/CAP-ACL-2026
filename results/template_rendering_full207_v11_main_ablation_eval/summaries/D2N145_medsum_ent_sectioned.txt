Chief Complaint
Patient reports a small, leftover rash on her face.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent facial rash that has been present for several weeks. She initially started using doxycycline once a day, noticing improvement, but the rash plateaued. She discontinued the twice-daily doxycycline and began using metro cream after each face wash. She reports no other lotions or cleansers. She stopped breastfeeding approximately one year ago. She read some information about elidel and became concerned, so she avoided using it. She is using sulfacetamide wash twice a day. She reports feeling dry when using the face wash twice a day. She is sun sensitive and takes precautions when outdoors. She is currently using the doxycycline twice a day and the metro cream after each face wash.

Findings
Examination reveals faint erythematous papules on the bilateral medial cheeks. The patient's skin type is IV, indicating a tendency to tan rather than burn. Vital signs are within normal limits. No physical exam findings were noted beyond the facial rash.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with perioral dermatitis and rosacea, likely exacerbated by topical medication use.

Plan
Prescribe metro cream for application to the affected facial rash twice daily. Recommend a gentle cleanser such as vanicream for cleansing. Advise the patient to continue using the doxycycline twice daily until the rash resolves, then reduce to once daily for an additional week before discontinuing. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess treatment response. Provide patient with a sample of vanicream and bruder mask for topical application. Advise patient to avoid using any other skincare products until further evaluation.
