Chief Complaint
Patient reports fatigue and foot pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue for the past two months, with no specific triggers. He denies fever or chills. He also reports pain in his foot, which he describes as aching and intermittent. He notes occasional numbness and tingling in his feet. The patient has a history of smoking, reporting at least two packs per day for over twenty years. He reports a history of a heart attack in 2018 and takes aspirin daily. He denies current chest pain or shortness of breath. He reports a two out of six systolic ejection murmur.

Findings
The clinician observes a two by three centimeter ulcerated lesion on the right lateral foot with granulation tissue and purulent discharge. Jugular venous distension is not present. Carotid bruits are not present. Lung sounds are clear. No pain upon palpation of the right foot.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are concerning for peripheral arterial disease and a complicated foot ulcer.

Plan
Order an arterial ultrasound of the lower extremities. Order a podiatry consult for the right foot ulcer. Recommend continued aspirin therapy. Recommend discussing diabetes management with the primary care physician. Recommend continued statin therapy.
