Chief Complaint
Right hand pain and numbness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right hand pain and numbness for the past month, primarily with wrist extension and gripping. He denies any recent trauma. He reports difficulty with fine motor tasks such as buttoning shirts. He reports taking ibuprofen as needed for the pain. He states the pain is worse when pulling his hand up towards you. He also reports tingling in his right hand. The patient reports pain when wrist is pulled up towards you. The patient reports pain on extension. The patient reports numbness. The patient reports tingling.

Findings
The doctor identifies a positive Tinel's sign. The patient reports decreased grip strength. The patient reports pain.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
A nerve conduction study will be ordered. The doctor orders a wrist splint. The doctor prescribes ibuprofen.
