Chief Complaint
Follow-up for stage three non-small cell lung cancer and to address persistent cough and fatigue.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent, dry cough and mild fatigue, which she attributes to her ongoing chemotherapy regimen for stage three non-small cell lung cancer. She describes the cough as lingering and intermittent, sometimes worse and sometimes better, and attempts to drink fluids to alleviate it. She also reports slight fatigue, though it is not overwhelming. She denies nausea or vomiting. She has been eating but not as much as she normally would due to the cough. The patient reports that she has been feeling supported by the nursing staff and office staff.

Findings
Physical examination revealed no cervical or supraclavicular lymphadenopathy. Lung auscultation revealed bilateral crackles, suggestive of mild pulmonary inflammation. Examination of the chest revealed erythema on the anterior chest wall on the left side, likely related to radiation therapy. Vital signs were stable, with no significant abnormalities noted.

Assessment
The patient's primary diagnosis remains stage three non-small cell lung cancer. Secondary findings include radiation pneumonitis, evidenced by cough and crackles in the lungs, and mild fatigue.

Plan
The patient will continue with the current chemotherapy regimen of cisplatin and etoposide, and radiation therapy at 45 grade. A low dose of prednisone, 40 milligrams daily for five days, has been prescribed to manage the inflammation associated with radiation pneumonitis. Lidocaine will be administered for swishing and swallowing four times a day to help with swallowing and prevent dehydration. Repeat imaging will be performed following radiation completion to assess tumor response. Follow-up will be scheduled in four weeks, or sooner if symptoms worsen.
