S:
The patient reports experiencing right-sided abdominal pain for several weeks, described as a severe, squeezing pain that comes and goes, accompanied by nausea and vomiting. The pain is located under his ribs on the right side and worsens after eating, sometimes occurring two to three hours after meals. He reports a diet of type 2 diabetes managed with diet and exercise, and has lost 25 pounds over the past three months by reducing sugar and adopting a keto diet. He enjoys photography and nature, particularly landscape photography, and has recently been taking photographs in the new park behind the Rex Center. He has not experienced fever or vomiting. He has a family history of gallbladder disease, with both his parents having had their gallbladders removed. He reports that he likes pork rinds and has had to cut them out of his diet.

O:
Blood pressure is 128/88 mmHg, respiratory rate is 16, pulse rate is 68, and oxygen saturation is 99% on room air. The patient's heart sounds are regular and rhythmic with no murmurs. Lung auscultation is clear and equal bilaterally. Abdominal exam reveals positive bowel sounds, soft and non-distended abdomen, with slight guarding in the right upper quadrant, and Murphy's sign is present. The ultrasound shows multiple gallstones in the gallbladder and mild thickening of the common bile duct, measuring 4.1 mm in diameter. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient has mild cholecystitis with gallstones. The pain is likely related to gallbladder inflammation and dietary factors, particularly high-fat intake. The patient's diabetes is being managed, but the pain suggests a need for further investigation and potential surgical intervention.

P:
I recommend a laparoscopic cholecystectomy. The surgery will take approximately one to one and a half hours and the patient will likely spend the night in the hospital for monitoring. He should avoid high-fat foods after surgery and maintain a high-fiber diet. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in one to two weeks to assess his recovery. The nurse will obtain paperwork for diabetes management. I will arrange for a consultation with the patient's endocrinologist to discuss his diabetes management plan.
