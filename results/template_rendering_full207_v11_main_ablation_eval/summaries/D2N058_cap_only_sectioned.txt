Chief Complaint
Left hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand. The pain is constant and has been present for the past month. The patient denies any recent trauma. He reports that the pain is worse with gripping and lifting objects. He denies any numbness or tingling. The patient is currently taking brilinta 90 milligrams twice daily, aspirin 81 milligrams daily, atorvastatin 80 milligrams daily, toprol 50 milligrams daily, and aldactone. He reports sleeping with two pillows.

Findings
The patient denies leg swelling. Examination reveals no acute distress. Vitals are stable.

Assessment
The patient has coronary artery disease, reduced ejection fraction, mitral regurgitation, and diabetes mellitus.

Plan
The patient should be referred to cardiac rehab. The patient should continue taking brilinta, aspirin, atorvastatin, toprol, and aldactone. Follow up in four weeks.
