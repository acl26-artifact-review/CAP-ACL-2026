Chief Complaint
Right wrist pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he injured his right wrist yesterday morning while doing laundry. He slipped and fell, attempting to catch himself by extending his arms. He describes the pain as severe, particularly with movement. He has applied ice and used an ACE wrap, and taken ibuprofen, which provides some relief. He denies prior wrist injuries. He reports some tingling in his fingers, but is unsure if this is related to the wrist pain. The pain has been ongoing since the fall.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the dorsum of the right wrist. Range of motion is limited due to pain. There is mild swelling and ecchymosis. Radial deviation elicits pain. Ulnar deviation elicits pain. Tinel's sign is negative. There is no palpable edema in the hand or wrist. The patient's vital signs are stable, with a blood pressure of 140/90 mmHg and a heart rate of 80 bpm. The patient's respiratory rate is 20 breaths per minute. The patient's temperature is 99.7°F. A colles' fracture is noted.

Assessment
Colles' fracture of the right wrist. The patient's pain and limited range of motion suggest a significant injury.

Plan
The patient will be placed in a thumb spica brace for six weeks. The doctor will prescribe 50 mg of ultram every six hours for pain management. Physical therapy will be scheduled in three weeks post-surgery. Follow-up will be scheduled in one month to assess healing and range of motion. The patient should contact the office if he experiences any worsening of symptoms or develops new symptoms.
