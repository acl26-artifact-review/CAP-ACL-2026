Chief Complaint
The patient reports experiencing persistent flank pain and hematuria related to a recent kidney stone.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right flank pain for approximately one week, accompanied by hematuria. The pain is described as constant and is exacerbated by movement. He reports taking ibuprofen for pain relief, but finds it only provides partial relief. The patient also reports nausea and has not experienced vomiting or fever. He denies any recent trauma. He states he was unable to participate in the Easter egg hunt due to pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the right flank, with no rebound tenderness or guarding. Vital signs are stable, including blood pressure 128/78 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, and temperature 98.6°F. A CT scan of the abdomen and pelvis demonstrates a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter, with no evidence of hydronephrosis. Laboratory results show a BMP within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient has a confirmed kidney stone in the proximal right ureter.

Plan
The patient should increase fluid intake to facilitate stone passage. I am prescribing ibuprofen 800 mg as needed for pain relief. I am ordering a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture. If indicated by the urinalysis results, I will consider initiating antibiotics. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in one to two weeks to assess progress and discuss further treatment options, including lithotripsy if necessary.
