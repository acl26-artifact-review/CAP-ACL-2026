S:
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his hands and fingers, primarily on the left, for several weeks following a car accident last December. He also describes mild neck stiffness and reports not consistently taking prescribed nerve pain medication. He reports experiencing a 'zing' sensation in his wrist and extending into his elbow. He has a history of carpal tunnel injury.

O:
The patient is a 60-year-old right-handed male with fatigue, weight gain, excessive appetite, bloating, and nail bed changes. He reports floaters. Cardiovascular exam reveals shortness of breath and leg swelling. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric. Tinel signs are positive at the wrists and elbows. The remainder of the physical exam is normal.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with possible bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome or cubital tunnel syndrome, likely secondary to his past trauma. Further evaluation is needed to determine the specific etiology and severity.

P:
Schedule an EMG test to evaluate for carpal tunnel syndrome or cubital tunnel syndrome. Refer to an orthopedist for consideration of splinting, injections, or carpal tunnel release surgery if indicated. Continue wearing the splint on the right wrist.
