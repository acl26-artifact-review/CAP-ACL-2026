Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain following a lacrosse injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began approximately one week ago after being hit with a lacrosse stick while playing with his children. He describes the pain as sharp and present with movement. He denies a specific injury mechanism beyond the impact. He tried taking Advil, which provided some relief. His son checked him several times with a sick.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the posterior elbow, mild pain with flexion and extension, and normal range of motion. An x-ray revealed no fracture or bony abnormality. The patient's blood pressure was 150/90 mmHg. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. The heart exam reveals a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur.

Assessment
Right elbow contusion with swelling and inflammation.

Plan
Prescribe 800 mg ibuprofen twice daily for pain and inflammation. Advise icing the elbow three times daily for 20 minutes. Recommend avoiding lacrosse activity for two weeks. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Order an A1C test to assess diabetic control. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist.
