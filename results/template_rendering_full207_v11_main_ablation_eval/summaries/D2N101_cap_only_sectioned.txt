Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain and back pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies any recent trauma. He describes the shoulder pain as a dull ache, and reports partial relief with acetaminophen. The patient also reports back pain, which he attributes to working at a desk all day, and describes as a build-up of pain. He reports a loss of approximately 10 pounds over the past few months and desires to lose an additional 20 pounds. The patient is considering a breast reduction after six or seven years. He reports nipple pain. He also reports excessive sweating. The patient was recommended to start mammograms at age 30 and has a family history of breast cancer.

Findings
The patient reports pain in the left hand and reports numbness. Physical exam reveals reduced active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. The doctor took photos of the patient today.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy and back pain. The patient is a good candidate for a breast reduction.

Plan
The doctor will review instructions with the patient before surgery. The patient will have their insurance switched over. The doctor will work with the patient on a surgery date. The patient will notify the office as soon as they have the insurance information. Order shoulder radiographs. Initiate physical therapy. Continue acetaminophen as needed. Arrange follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
