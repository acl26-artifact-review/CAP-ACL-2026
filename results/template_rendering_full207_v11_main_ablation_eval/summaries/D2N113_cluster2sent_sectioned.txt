Chief Complaint
The patient reports anxiety regarding recent hepatitis C diagnosis.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of fatigue and recent blood work revealing a positive hepatitis C antibody test and elevated AST levels. He denies a history of intravenous drug use or known hepatitis C-positive partners. He previously reported feeling well but has recently experienced increased fatigue. The patient also reports a history of depression.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal lung sounds, clear to auscultation bilaterally. The patient's abdomen is soft with normal bowel sounds and no hepatosplenomegaly. Examination of the musculoskeletal system reveals no gait disturbance or edema. Heart sounds are regular rate and rhythm with a two out of six systolic murmur, unchanged from last year. Skin exam reveals no jaundice.

Assessment
The patient has confirmed hepatitis C infection with elevated liver enzymes and requires further evaluation for fibrosis and genotype determination.

Plan
Order hepatitis C RNA testing and HCV genotype testing to confirm the diagnosis. Order a liver ultrasound to assess for liver fibrosis. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to review test results and discuss treatment options.
