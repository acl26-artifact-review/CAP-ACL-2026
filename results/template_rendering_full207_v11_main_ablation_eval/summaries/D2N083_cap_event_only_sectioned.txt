Chief Complaint
Left elbow pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent medial elbow pain for the past month, exacerbated by activities like lifting and gripping. He denies any specific injury. He reports using over-the-counter ibuprofen with minimal relief. He states the pain is most noticeable when he extends his arm and grasps objects.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the medial epicondylitis area. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No swelling is present.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with medial epicondylitis.

Plan
The doctor instructs the patient to apply ice to the elbow three times daily. The doctor orders radiographs to rule out other causes of the pain. The doctor recommends physical therapy to address strength and flexibility.
