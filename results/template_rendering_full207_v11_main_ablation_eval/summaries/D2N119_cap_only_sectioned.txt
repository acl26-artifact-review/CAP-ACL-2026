Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand. He states the pain has been present for approximately one month and has been gradually worsening. The patient denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is exacerbated by gripping and lifting objects. He denies numbness or tingling. The patient reports using ibuprofen for the pain, with some relief.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal interphalangeal joint of the left hand. Range of motion is limited. No swelling or erythema is noted. Grip strength is decreased in the left hand compared to the right.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Order carpal tunnel ultrasound. Recommend wrist splinting. Advise patient on ergonomic adjustments at work. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
