Chief Complaint
Yearly physical exam and discussion of diabetes and hypertension management.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 24-year-old female with a history of type 2 diabetes and hypertension. She reports increased snacking due to a new bakery job, leading to elevated blood sugar levels. Her blood pressure has been well-controlled. She has been sleeping well and reports no concerns about self-harm. She is currently taking metformin twice daily as prescribed.

Findings
Physical exam revealed normal vital signs, including a blood pressure of 128/78 mmHg. Neurological exam was intact. The patient's heart rhythm was regular, and a grade 2 systolic ejection fraction back ejection murmur was noted. Lung sounds were clear. Grip strength was equal bilaterally. Hemoglobin A1c is elevated, indicating out-of-control blood sugars.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is poorly controlled type 2 diabetes, likely related to dietary changes. Hypertension is currently well-managed.

Plan
The patient will continue taking metformin twice daily. I recommend a dietary modification to reduce snacking and sweets, and I want the patient to check her fasting blood glucose in the morning. Follow up in two weeks to review morning blood glucose results and then again in two months to assess ongoing diabetes control. Continue the patient on lisinopril 10 mg daily.
