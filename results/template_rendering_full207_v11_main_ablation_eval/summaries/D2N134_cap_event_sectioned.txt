Chief Complaint
Abnormal lab findings, including a positive Lyme titer.

History of Present Illness
Michelle is a 31-year-old female with a past medical history of type 2 diabetes and hypertension who presents with abnormal lab findings. She reports having been bitten by an insect approximately two weeks ago, which resulted in a bull's-eye rash. She has been experiencing fatigue and joint pain, particularly in her elbows. She also reports nausea and a decreased appetite, making it difficult to eat. She is currently taking metformin for her diabetes and lisinopril for her hypertension. She has been monitoring her blood pressure at home and reports it has been well-controlled. She has been experiencing some swelling in her elbow joints.

Findings
Physical examination reveals swelling of the bilateral elbow joints, with tenderness to palpation. A bull's-eye rash is noted on the left abdomen, which is warm to the touch. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 132/82 mmHg, heart rate 78 bpm, temperature 98.6°F.

Assessment
The patient's presentation, including the positive Lyme titer, bull's-eye rash, and reported symptoms of fatigue, joint pain, and nausea, is most consistent with Lyme disease. Her type 2 diabetes and hypertension remain stable.

Plan
I am ordering doxycycline 100 mg twice daily for three weeks to treat the Lyme disease. I will consult with an infectious disease specialist to discuss the treatment plan and monitor for any potential side effects. I will also order a hemoglobin A1c to monitor her diabetes control and continue her current metformin dosage. I will order a lipid panel to assess her cardiovascular risk. I have scheduled a follow-up appointment in one week to assess her response to treatment and address any further questions.
