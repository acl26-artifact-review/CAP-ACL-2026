Chief Complaint
Follow-up for chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports an episode of chest pain while performing yard work over the weekend, accompanied by shortness of breath and a feeling of anxiety. He has never experienced this before. He denies any other symptoms since the event, though he felt a little nervous about it. He reports no family history of heart disease. He had knee surgery approximately two months ago and has been feeling well since then. He denies chest pain during physical therapy exercises. His blood pressure was elevated at 180/95 in the emergency room, but seemed to regulate after the event. He currently takes lisinopril 10mg daily.

Findings
Physical exam reveals an enlarged thyroid gland, but it is not tender. A carotid bruit is present on the right side. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds reveal a systolic ejection murmur of 3/6. The abdomen is soft. The healed scar from his prior knee surgery is noted. Extremities are not swollen. An echocardiogram shows a low ejection fraction.

Assessment
The patient presents with a concerning episode of chest pain, potentially related to hypertension or underlying cardiac disease. His elevated blood pressure and low ejection fraction warrant further investigation.

Plan
I will initiate a regimen of carvedilol 25mg twice daily to improve cardiac function and address the elevated blood pressure. I will order a cardiac catheterization to evaluate for coronary artery disease. We will continue the patient's lisinopril 10mg daily and monitor his blood pressure closely. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess his response to treatment.
