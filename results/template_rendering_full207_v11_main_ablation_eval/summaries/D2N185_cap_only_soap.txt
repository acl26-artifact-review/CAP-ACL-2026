S:
The patient reports experiencing abdominal pain, primarily in the lower right quadrant, which he describes as sharp and intermittent. He states the pain started approximately three days ago and has not improved with over-the-counter pain relievers. He also reports experiencing burning during urination and reports blood in his urine. The patient states he is vomiting.

O:
Blood pressure is 132/84. Heart rate is 88 beats per minute and regular. Temperature is 98.6°F. The patient's abdomen is soft and non-tender to palpation, with mild guarding. Neurological exam is unremarkable. There is no edema noted.

A:
The patient's symptoms are concerning for possible urinary tract infection or kidney stone. Given the reported blood in urine and pain, end-stage renal disease is also a consideration. The family history of kidney disease and obstructive neuropathy further supports the possibility of chronic kidney disease.

P:
Order a urinalysis and urine culture to evaluate for urinary tract infection. Start Tylenol 650mg every 4-6 hours as needed for pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess response to treatment and monitor symptoms. Advise the patient to increase fluid intake to at least 2 liters per day.
