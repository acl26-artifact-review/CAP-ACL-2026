Chief Complaint
Follow-up of chronic problems, primarily elbow pain and osteoarthritis symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the right elbow, primarily exacerbated by prolonged computer use. He describes this as stiffness and tenderness. He also reports osteoarthritis flares in both elbows, particularly with repetitive movements. He notes numbness during typing, relieved by shaking his arms. He has a history of gout, with a recent flare-up three months ago, which was controlled by medication. He reports stable psoriasis, currently managed with a topical steroid cream. He states that his osteoarthritis has been flaring up lately.

Findings
Physical exam reveals edema and inflammation in the right olecranon, with tenderness and effusion. Palpation of the right elbow elicits pain. The patient reports pain with flexion and extension of the right arm and stiffness. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. Cervical lymph nodes are not palpable. Vital signs are within normal limits. The autoimmune panel results show no systemic inflammatory process.

Assessment
The patient's primary problems are osteoarthritis of the elbows and a history of gout. There is no evidence of systemic inflammation.

Plan
Order a right elbow x-ray to evaluate the osteoarthritis. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain and swelling. Continue colchicine 0.6 mg twice daily as needed for gout flare-ups. Schedule a follow-up in four weeks to review the x-ray results and assess treatment response.
