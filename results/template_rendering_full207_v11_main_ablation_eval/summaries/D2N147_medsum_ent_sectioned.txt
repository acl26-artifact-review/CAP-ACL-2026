Chief Complaint
Right shoulder pain following a football injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of playing football in the past and sustaining a tackle that resulted in shoulder pain. He describes the current pain as worsening over the past four days, particularly with reaching and lifting. He reports some difficulty lifting a box, but denies any numbness or tingling in his fingers. He has taken Tylenol, but it hasn't provided significant relief. He denies any fever or chills. He reports a minor head injury during the tackle.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation at the acromioclavicular joint. Range of motion is decreased, with pain on abduction and pronation. Vital signs are normal: blood pressure 122/76 mmHg, heart rate 82 bpm, oxygen saturation 95% on room air. The cervical spine exam is unremarkable. The nurse reports the right shoulder x-ray showed an acute acromioclavicular joint fracture.

Assessment
Acute acromioclavicular joint fracture of the right shoulder.

Plan
I am placing the patient in a sling for comfort. I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg daily. I am ordering a cat scan of the right shoulder to assess the fracture further. I am referring the patient to physical therapy to strengthen the shoulder and avoid surgery. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
