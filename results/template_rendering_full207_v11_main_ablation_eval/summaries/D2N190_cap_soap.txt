The patient reports he fell off his tandem bike two weeks ago, resulting in a right knee injury. He took ibuprofen and a bandage, but the pain persists. He describes the pain as lingering and reports it worsens when walking up inclines. He also reports mild hand pain. The patient denies chest pain, dyspnea, or syncope. He reports feeling weak.

O:
The patient's right knee exhibits tenderness to palpation on the medial joint line. There is a one plus posterior drawer and negative Lachman tests. The patient reports a dime-size abrasion over the medial patella. The skin on the knee is intact. The patient's left knee has full range of motion and is symmetric. There is no crepitus. The patient reports no tenderness on the lateral side. The popliteal space is nontender. The patient's blood pressure is mildly low. Heart rate is 78 bpm. Neurologic examination is nonfocal. The patient has abrasions on his hands.

A:
The primary problem is a right knee injury, likely involving a sprained posterior cruciate ligament. A possible medial joint line tenderness is present. The patient's symptoms suggest instability and potential weakness.

P:
Order an MRI of the right knee to evaluate for ligamentous injuries and meniscal damage. Prescribe ibuprofen up to three times daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 4-6 weeks for physical therapy evaluation. Refer the patient to physical therapy. Consider a brace for the knee to provide support and stability.
