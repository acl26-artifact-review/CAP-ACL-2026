Chief Complaint
Acne vulgaris

History of Present Illness
The patient reports acne that is improving on his face, but with a mild flare on his back. He has been using benzoyl peroxide wash (5-10% for the chest and back) and PanOxyl 10% foaming wash daily, along with tretinoin cream at night and sunscreen. He reports some redness, peeling, and dryness with the medications, and has been washing his chest and back with the higher strength benzoyl peroxide wash. He states that the acne on his face is improving, but the acne on his back is a concern.

Findings
Physical exam reveals scattered inflammatory papules and pustules across the upper back and posterior shoulders, with less than 5 inflammatory papules on the forehead, cheeks, and chin.

Assessment
Acne vulgaris, mild flare on the back.

Plan
The patient will continue current treatment plan for the face and initiation treatment with PanOxyl 10% foaming wash to the back in the shower daily. A new refill for clindamycin lotion (11 refills) and tretinoin cream (3 refills) will be sent. Follow up in one year.
