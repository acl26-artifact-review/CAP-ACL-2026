Chief Complaint
Back pain for a while

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain for a couple of years and tried a couple of things but nothing seems to work. It started after a basketball fall. The pain is sharp and throbbing sometimes, and it radiates down his left leg, causing numbness. He has experienced some weakness when standing for long periods and has had one instance of incontinence. He tried some injections previously and felt they weren't effective. He's gained about eighty pounds in the past year and watches TV all day. His fourth cousin had scoliosis and needed oxygen. He smoked for a while. He's currently sitting at home.

Findings
Examination reveals midline tenderness on the L4-L5 disc space with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness when bending forward. There is decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at the L4 dermatome. Reflexes are symmetrical. The patient reports pain with lumbar flexion and extension.

Assessment
The patient has a herniated disk, likely causing radiculopathy and resulting in his symptoms.

Plan
A spinal injection with a corticosteroid is ordered. A prescription for pain medication is ordered. A corticosteroid will be injected. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
