Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath with CHF and COPD.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing increasing shortness of breath over the past two weeks, particularly at night. She describes a feeling of choking and difficulty catching her breath when lying down. She reports difficulty sleeping due to shortness of breath and inability to find a comfortable position. She has been enjoying large amounts of food recently, including a large party with lots of sweets, and is concerned about weight gain. Her blood sugar checks have been around 2-3% above her target, and her last A1c was 7.5. She is currently taking metformin and reports inconsistent adherence to her lasix regimen.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild jugular venous distension, crackles in both lung bases, intermittent wheezing, and one plus pitting edema in both ankles. The heart rate is regular at 72 beats per minute. Oxygen saturation is 92% on room air. The abdomen is slightly distended without tenderness. Neurological exam is normal. Neck exam reveals mild swelling.

Assessment
Exacerbation of congestive heart failure secondary to underlying CHF and COPD. Poorly controlled diabetes mellitus.

Plan
Increase lasix dose to 60 mg daily, titrating as needed based on weight monitoring. Prescribe albuterol and atrovent for rescue breathing. Order a repeat hemoglobin A1c to assess glycemic control. Order a fasting blood glucose to assess current blood sugar levels. Continue metformin as currently prescribed. Recommend weight monitoring daily. Initiate naproxen and flexeril for back pain. Provide exercises for sitting and encourage limiting time on the couch. Schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately two days to reassess symptoms and adjust treatment as needed.
