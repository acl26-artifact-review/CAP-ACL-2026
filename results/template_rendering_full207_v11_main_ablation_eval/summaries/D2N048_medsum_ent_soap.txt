S:
The patient reports experiencing right foot pain following a tennis injury, specifically a fall while volleying. He describes twisting his foot and immediately feeling pain. He has previously injured his left foot. He denies numbness or tingling. The patient states he wrapped the foot with ace wraps and iced it last night. He took ibuprofen, which provided some relief but wore off. He experienced this injury approximately two days ago. He does not have any numbness or tingling. He denies any loss of sensation.

O:
Vitals are stable: blood pressure 120/75, respiratory rate 18, temperature 97.1°F, pulse 60 bpm. Physical exam reveals bruising of the plantar and dorsal aspects of the right foot. Associated swelling is present at the midfoot. Palpation of the midfoot elicits tenderness. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds in the right foot. Neurologic exam is intact; sensation is preserved. Pedal pulses are strong and equal bilaterally. The left foot exam is normal.

A:
The patient presents with right foot pain consistent with a Lisfranc fracture, likely due to the mechanism of injury. The examination findings support this diagnosis. Further investigation with an MRI is warranted to assess ligamentous injuries.

P:
I am recommending surgery for the right foot to stabilize the fracture and prevent further complications. An MRI will be ordered to assess ligamentous injuries. The patient will be discharged the same day with crutches and a cast. Follow-up will be scheduled in one week. Physical therapy will be recommended post-operatively to strengthen the muscles and improve range of motion.
