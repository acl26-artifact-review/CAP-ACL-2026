Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent chest pain for the past week, described as a dull ache, occasionally sharp, primarily brought on by exertion. He denies shortness of breath, palpitations, or nausea. He reports the pain is worse with walking uphill. He denies any recent trauma or fever. He takes lisinopril 20mg daily for hypertension.

Findings
Blood pressure 138/82 mmHg, heart rate 88 bpm, regular rhythm. Lung sounds clear bilaterally. No murmurs auscultated.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are concerning for possible angina pectoris. Differential diagnosis includes musculoskeletal chest pain and gastroesophageal reflux.

Plan
Order EKG and cardiac enzymes (Troponin). Start aspirin 81mg daily. Recommend cardiac rehabilitation program. Schedule follow-up in 24-48 hours to assess response to treatment.
