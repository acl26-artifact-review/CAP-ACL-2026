S:
The patient reports persistent itchy scalp pain for over six months, accompanied by significant dandruff and an embarrassing appearance.

O:
Physical exam reveals demarcated, scaly, erythematous plaques across the back of the scalp, consistent with a patchy distribution and significant dander present on clothing.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with scalp psoriasis.

P:
Rescribe clobetasol 0.05% solution to be applied twice daily to the affected areas of the scalp. Recommend continued use of T-Gel shampoo. Advise the patient that a cure is not possible and flare-ups can be unpredictable, but steroid medications will help manage symptoms. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months or sooner if symptoms worsen.
