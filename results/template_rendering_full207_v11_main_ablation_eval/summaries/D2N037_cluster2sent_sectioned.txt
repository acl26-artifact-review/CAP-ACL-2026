Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his right elbow, which began recently. He has taken ibuprofen 800 mg three times daily for the pain. He describes pain with both flexion and extension, and limited range of motion on extension. Palpation reveals pain on the medial side of the elbow. He describes pain with pronation but not supination. He asks if this is golfer's elbow. He is considering dry needling.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal sensation in the fingers. X-rays show no fractures or bony misalignment. The patient demonstrates limited range of motion on extension of the arm.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with medial epicondylitis.

Plan
A musculoskeletal MRI is ordered to further evaluate the right elbow. The patient will be scheduled for the MRI and a whole blood transfusion, anticipated within the next two weeks, to support recovery and accommodate the upcoming delivery. Follow-up will be arranged in four weeks if symptoms persist.
