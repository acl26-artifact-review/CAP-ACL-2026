S:
The patient reports a right ankle sprain following a fall while mountain biking. He describes tingling and numbness in his toes, and reports some numbing in his heart. He reports a fever of 200, and has been experiencing pain. He states he took Tylenol for the pain, but it did not help. He reports hearing a crackle when he fell. He has been mountain biking for three years, and his favorite place to go is not North Point or Colorado. He is currently limping.

O:
Temperature is 98.6°F. Blood pressure is 118/77 mmHg. Heart rate is 77 beats per minute. Physical exam reveals edema and erythema on the lateral malleolus of the right ankle, with associated ecchymosis and excoriations. There is pain to palpation of the right lateral malleolus and decreased flexion and extension of the right ankle with internal and external rotation. The knee and foot are uninjured.

A:
The patient has an acute right ankle sprain with associated edema and pain. The reported fever and tingling/numbness warrant further investigation.

P:
I am placing the patient in an air cast and ordering crutches. I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg once daily. Advise icing the area for 20 minutes five times a day. Instruct the patient to weight-bear as tolerated. I anticipate the patient will start feeling better in the next two weeks. I will schedule a follow-up appointment if symptoms do not improve or worsen.
