Chief Complaint
Numbness and tingling in the fingers and pain in the wrist.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his fingers and wrist for approximately three months, with pain primarily in the right wrist and hand. The pain and numbness began gradually and have been ongoing. He notes that the symptoms are worse at night and often wakes with the hands feeling numb, requiring shaking to alleviate the sensation. He describes performing landscaping and heavy labor daily, involving repetitive motions and lifting heavy objects. He reports occasional alcohol consumption (a beer or two on Fridays and Saturdays). He denies any previous diagnoses of rheumatoid arthritis. The patient has noticed weakness in his left hand and occasionally drops objects due to reduced sensation.

Findings
Physical exam reveals decreased grip strength on the left hand and a positive Tinel's sign on the left wrist. Palpation of the right wrist reveals no abnormalities. The patient reports feeling a 'zing' sensation in the fingertips when tapping the wrist on the left side.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome, likely exacerbated by repetitive occupational activities.

Plan
I will order an EMG to further evaluate the nerve conduction studies. We will also provide a wrist splint for comfort and support. I am prescribing ibuprofen 600 mg every six hours as needed for pain relief. I would like to schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress and discuss potential surgical intervention if conservative measures are insufficient. I will also explore options for modifying his landscaping work to reduce repetitive motions and consider referring him to a landscape designer for project planning.
