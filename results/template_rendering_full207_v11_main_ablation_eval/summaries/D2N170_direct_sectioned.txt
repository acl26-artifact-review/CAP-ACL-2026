Chief Complaint
Follow-up to an abnormal lab finding, specifically low hemoglobin.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue over the past two months, with a gradual decline in energy levels and reduced ability to exercise. He reports no recent trauma. He denies any blood in the stool or significant dark stools. He denies heavy menstrual bleeding. He reports no chest pain, shortness of breath, fever, or body aches. He has quit smoking several years ago and reports no recent tobacco use. He reports managing his congestive heart failure with a low-sodium diet and continues his current diuretic regimen. He reports no recent weight loss, but has noticed a slight reduction in recent months due to increased exercise.

Findings
Vital signs are stable. Physical exam reveals a systolic ejection murmur of moderate intensity. The abdomen is tender to palpation in the right lower quadrant. No lower extremity edema is noted. Hemoglobin is 8.2.

Assessment
The patient presents with anemia, likely secondary to an as yet undetermined etiology. His chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and congestive heart failure are stable.

Plan
I will order hemoglobin and iron studies to evaluate for anemia. I have ordered an endoscopy and colonoscopy to evaluate for sources of bleeding. I will refer the patient to gastroenterology for these procedures. I will continue the patient on toprol, lisinopril, and lasix 20 mg daily. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to review the lab results and discuss further management.
