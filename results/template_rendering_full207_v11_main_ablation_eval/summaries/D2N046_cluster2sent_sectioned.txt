Chief Complaint
Evaluation of a newly diagnosed ovarian cancer and related symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing severe pain and bleeding related to her newly diagnosed ovarian cancer. She also reports constipation and weight loss. She denies vomiting. She takes no oral hormone replacement therapy and has no history of endometriosis. She reports a family history of gynecological cancers.

Findings
A physical exam revealed a left adnexal mass, with slight tenderness to palpation of the left lower quadrant. The abdominal CT scan showed a three centimeter left ovarian mass with associated localized lymph node involvement, and no evidence of gross peritoneal or metastatic disease.

Assessment
Based on the CT findings, the patient is most likely diagnosed with stage three ovarian cancer.

Plan
I will order blood tests including CA-125, HCG, AFP, and LDH to further characterize the tumor. I will also arrange for genetic counseling and testing to assess for a genetic predisposition to ovarian cancer. I recommend a hysterectomy with lymph node dissection and subsequent chemotherapy with cisplatin and taxol, potentially followed by intraperitoneal chemotherapy. I will perform surgery after recovery and send tissue samples for pathology to determine the stage of the cancer.
