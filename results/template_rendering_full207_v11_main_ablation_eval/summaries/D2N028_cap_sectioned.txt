Chief Complaint
Chronic abdominal pain and diarrhea.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing chronic abdominal pain, primarily in the mid-abdomen, for approximately two years. He describes intermittent episodes of pain, often accompanied by constipation or explosive diarrhea. The diarrhea is often triggered by specific foods, such as creamy pasta dishes. He reports a history of constipation, with infrequent bowel movements, typically occurring three to four times per week, and describes the stool as hard. He notes that the pain improves with bowel movements. He has had a CT scan of the abdomen in January 2020, which showed no abnormalities. He previously sought treatment for similar symptoms at an urgent care facility a few months ago, but the CT scan did not reveal any specific cause. He has been taking Crestor and olmesartan daily for several months. He denies any recent trauma or significant medical events. He reports a family history of irritable bowel syndrome in his sister.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a slight hepatomegaly and increased stool burden in the colon. The patient reports tenderness to palpation in the left lower quadrant. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are normal. Liver enzymes are slightly elevated. The patient denies any signs of jaundice or swelling.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with chronic abdominal pain, constipation, and possible liver dysfunction. Further investigation is warranted to determine the underlying cause.

Plan
Order liver enzyme testing and a liver ultrasound. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to review test results and assess treatment progress. Recommend a fiber supplement to manage constipation. Advise the patient to discuss Crestor with his primary care physician. Schedule a liver ultrasound within the next two weeks.
