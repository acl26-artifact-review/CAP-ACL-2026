Chief Complaint
Bilateral hand numbness and tingling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports bilateral hand numbness and tingling for approximately four weeks, initially following a car accident last December where she sustained wrist fractures. The fractures have since healed, but the symptoms have worsened over the past month. She describes the tingling as most prominent in her fingers, particularly the right middle, ring, and index fingers extending to the forearm, and the left pinky and ring fingers. She has been experiencing mild neck stiffness since the accident and reports a contusion. She has been taking nerve pain medication intermittently and notes that the tingling improves with splint use. She denies any loss of sensation in general, but feels less sensitive than expected. She has a history of pre-diabetes, which she controlled through dietary changes. She is currently taking carvedilol 25 mg twice daily for hypertension and lexapro 10 mg daily for anxiety. She is allergic to sulfa drugs, which cause a rash.

Findings
Physical examination reveals diminished pinprick sensation in the third and fourth fingers of the right hand and the fourth and fifth fingers of the left hand. Reflexes are one plus and symmetric. Tinel's signs are positive at both wrists and both elbows. The patient reports fatigue and weight gain during the review of systems.

Assessment
Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome is concerning. Bilateral cubital tunnel syndrome cannot be ruled out at this time.

Plan
Schedule electrodiagnostic studies (EMG/NCV) to evaluate the extent of nerve involvement. If symptoms do not resolve, consider referral to hand clinic for possible carpal tunnel release surgery or symptomatic injection. Advise the patient to continue wearing her splints. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
