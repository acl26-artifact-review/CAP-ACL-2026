Chief Complaint
Annual exam and blood pressure check.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling good and has been able to attend more family events since vaccinations began. He discusses his depression, noting he has been attending weekly therapy sessions for the past year and is currently managing his symptoms with strategies. He also reports some nasal congestion but denies chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, or nausea/vomiting. He is taking lisinopril as prescribed.

Findings
Blood pressure readings are stable. The patient's heart rate is within normal limits. Physical examination reveals a slight 2/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is stable. The lungs are clear, and there is trace lower extremity edema bilaterally. An EKG performed by the nursing staff shows no abnormalities. Carotid bruits are absent.

Assessment
Elizabeth is a 66-year-old female with a past medical history of significant depression and hypertension who presents for her annual exam.

Plan
Continue lisinopril at 20 milligrams daily. Schedule a mammogram as part of the annual exam maintenance. Advise the patient to monitor blood pressure and contact the clinic if readings rise. Follow up in four weeks to assess ongoing management of depression and hypertension.
