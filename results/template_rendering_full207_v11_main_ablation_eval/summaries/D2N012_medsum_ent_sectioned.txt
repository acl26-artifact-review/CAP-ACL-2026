Chief Complaint
Chronic problems and management of depression, diabetes, and asthma.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports managing his depression and maintaining control of his diabetes, but notes persistent issues including asthma exacerbated by pollen seasons and ongoing knee pain from running. He has been traveling with the kids to sports tournaments, leading to dietary adjustments and occasional unhealthy food choices. He reports feeling lightheaded occasionally, particularly when walking up stairs, attributing it to fatigue. He has been using his inhaler approximately 85% of the time when outdoor temperatures exceed 85 degrees. He has weaned himself off of Prozac, feeling slightly better, but experiences occasional depressive episodes. He is interested in adjusting his medication dosage or trying a different medication for depression.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no apparent distress. Cervical lymphadenopathy is absent. Thyroid exam is unremarkable. Lung auscultation reveals bilateral expiratory wheezing, faint in intensity. Heart sounds are regular and rhythmic. Abdomen is soft and non-tender. Lower extremities demonstrate no edema. PFTs are within normal limits. Hemoglobin A1c is elevated. Chest X-ray is normal.

Assessment
The patient presents with chronic depression, type 2 diabetes, and asthma. He is currently managing these conditions with medication and lifestyle modifications.

Plan
Increase metformin dosage to 1000 mg twice daily. Initiate Zoloft at 25 mg daily. Continue albuterol inhaler (2 puffs every 4-6 hours as needed) and add symbicort (2 puffs twice daily) during the summer months. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months to review hemoglobin A1c and assess response to medications. Arrange for a nurse visit to assist with medication administration and provide diabetes education.
