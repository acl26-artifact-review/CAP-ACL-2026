Chief Complaint
Right leg pain following a bowling injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he injured his right leg while bowling. He fell after hitting the ball into the lane and then into the gutter. He describes some bruising but not significant swelling. He has been able to walk with some difficulty, but not without significant discomfort. He has been taking ibuprofen and Tylenol for the pain.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to the lateral aspect of the right upper leg. No significant swelling or bruising is observed. Range of motion is limited due to pain. X-ray findings are normal, showing no fracture or significant abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with a contusion of the right upper leg.

Plan
I am starting the patient on an anti-inflammatory medication, mobic 15 milligrams once daily. I recommend ice packs for pain relief. Follow up in one week if symptoms persist, or sooner if they worsen.
