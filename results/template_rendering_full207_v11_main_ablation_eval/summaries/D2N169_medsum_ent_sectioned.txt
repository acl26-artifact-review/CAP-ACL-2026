Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath following a recent, prolonged bicycle ride.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath and lightheadedness approximately three hours into a three-hour bike ride over the weekend. He describes this as a surprise, as he typically experiences no issues with his usual morning bike rides. He denies chest pain, but does recall feeling some discomfort that resolved within an hour. He reports a mild cough approximately a year and a half ago, which resolved spontaneously. He is currently taking imitrex for migraines, protonix for reflux, and continues to take imitrex as needed. He had cataract surgery approximately four and a half months ago with good visual acuity since then. He denies fever, nausea, or abdominal pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal oxygenation. A slight expiratory wheeze is heard bilaterally. Trace lower extremity edema is observed. The chest x-ray is normal, demonstrating no pulmonary edema or pneumonia. Pulmonary function tests are within normal limits. No murmur was detected on auscultation.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with an asthma exacerbation triggered by exercise and potentially exacerbated by underlying allergies.

Plan
I am prescribing an albuterol inhaler, two puffs every four hours as needed for wheezing or shortness of breath. I am ordering a pulmonary referral for further evaluation. I will continue the patient's current imitrex regimen as needed for migraine management. I recommend a follow-up appointment in approximately two weeks to reassess symptoms and adjust the treatment plan as necessary. Please contact the office immediately if shortness of breath recurs.
