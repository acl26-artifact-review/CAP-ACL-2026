S:
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately three weeks, with no specific injury recalled. He reports activity with renovating his basement and mentions taking Tylenol as needed. He denies prior shoulder pain and reports using Tylenol previously for similar aches and pains. He states he has been taking Tylenol every six to eight hours.

O:
The patient's cap refill is brisk, less than three seconds. Motor and sensation are intact to light touch. Left shoulder range of motion is limited. Tenderness is noted to the greater tuberosity of the humerus. No tenderness is present at the sternoclavicular or clavicular joints. X-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy. The examination findings support this diagnosis.

P:
Order an MRI of the left shoulder. Refer the patient for physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. Continue to take Tylenol PRN as needed. Follow up in approximately two weeks.
