Chief Complaint
Follow-up of chronic problems, primarily related to osteoarthritis and gout.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports chronic osteoarthritis, primarily in her elbows, which has been exacerbated by prolonged computer use. She describes increased flare-ups related to this activity. She also reports a history of gout, with a recent episode approximately three months ago involving toe inflammation, which was controlled by medication. She has been using clobetasol for her scalp with good results. She experiences occasional numbness in her hands when typing for extended periods, which she manages with frequent arm shaking. She denies any other joint pain. The patient reports that her psoriasis is currently under control.

Findings
Physical examination reveals edema and inflammation of the right olecranon, with tenderness and effusion upon palpation. Pronation and supination of the right arm elicit pain. Flexion and extension of the right arm cause pain and stiffness. The patient's abdomen is soft, and there is no lower extremity edema. Vital signs are stable. The autoimmune panel results are unremarkable, showing no systemic inflammatory process. ESR is within normal limits. Uric acid levels are within the therapeutic range.

Assessment
The patient's primary diagnoses include chronic osteoarthritis and well-controlled gout. There is no evidence of systemic inflammatory disease.

Plan
Continue colchicine 0.6 milligrams twice a day as needed for gout flare-ups. Order a right elbow X-ray to evaluate the osteoarthritis. Prescribe meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily for pain and inflammation. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to review the X-ray results and assess treatment response. Continue the current clobetasol regimen for the scalp.
