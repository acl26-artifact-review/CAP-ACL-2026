Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a pop during a soccer game.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing a pop in his right knee three days ago while playing pickup soccer. He describes subsequent swelling, but the swelling has decreased somewhat. He reports limited functionality, stating he can't move his knee as well as before and it feels 'loose.' He rates the pain as 6-7 out of 10, with ibuprofen providing some relief. He has been taking ibuprofen regularly. He can bend his leg but reports pain at the end of the range of motion, and straightening is difficult, requiring assistance. He can bear weight on the knee but feels it is unstable. The patient reports moderate limp upon ambulation.

Findings
Physical exam reveals significant effusion in the right knee and mild ecchymosis. Palpation elicits tenderness along the medial joint line. Range of motion is limited; passive range of motion is restricted to 115 degrees extension and 20-30 degrees flexion secondary to pain. Lachman's test is positive. Neurovascular exam is intact bilaterally, with a muscle strength rating of 5/5.

Assessment
Based on the examination findings, a right anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) tear is highly suspected.

Plan
I have ordered an MRI to evaluate the integrity of the ACL. In the meantime, I am prescribing meloxicam 50 mg daily for pain and inflammation, and recommending ice therapy as needed. A knee immobilizer and crutches are ordered to limit weight-bearing and prevent further injury. I have scheduled the patient with the scheduler to obtain an MRI and will follow up with the patient as soon as the results are available.
