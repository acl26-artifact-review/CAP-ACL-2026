S:
The patient reports back pain following a refrigerator move, approximately five days ago. He states the pain is on the lower right side of his back and worsens with bending over. He denies any neurological symptoms such as pain in his legs, numbness, or tingling. He reports no chest pain, shortness of breath, or abdominal pain. He has been taking ibuprofen and occasionally combined acetaminophen and ibuprofen with some relief. He denies any bowel or bladder symptoms.

O:
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the right lumbar spine, decreased flexion and extension of the back, and a positive straight leg raise on the right. Heart sounds are regular and rhythmic. Lungs are clear to auscultation. The lumbar x-ray demonstrates normal bony alignment without fracture. Laboratory results are unremarkable.

A:
The patient is diagnosed with a lumbar strain. The reported injury is consistent with this diagnosis, and the physical exam findings support it. There is no evidence of fracture or other serious pathology.

P:
Order an MRI of the lumbar spine to rule out other causes of the back pain. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily. Prescribe ultram 50 mg every four hours as needed for pain. Refer to physical therapy upon receipt of the MRI results. Advise patient to discontinue ibuprofen. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
