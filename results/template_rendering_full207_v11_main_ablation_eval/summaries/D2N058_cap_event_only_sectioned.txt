Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent chest pain for the past month, described as a dull ache, occasionally sharp, and radiating to his left arm. He rates the pain as 6/10 on a scale of 1 to 10. He denies shortness of breath, palpitations, or nausea. He reports a family history of heart disease. He currently takes aspirin daily and reports taking brilinta 90 milligrams twice daily and atorvastatin 80 milligrams daily. He also takes toprol 50 milligrams daily and aldactone.

Findings
Blood pressure 142/88 mmHg, heart rate 78 bpm, regular rhythm. Physical exam reveals no edema or rales in lung auscultation. The patient's left arm is not visibly swollen or erythematous. EKG shows no acute ST-segment elevations or reciprocal changes.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and EKG findings are concerning for possible angina pectoris. Further evaluation is warranted to rule out acute coronary syndrome.

Plan
Order cardiac enzyme levels, stress test, and cardiology consult. Continue current medications. Advise patient to report any worsening chest pain immediately.
