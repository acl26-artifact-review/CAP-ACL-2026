S:
The patient reports a left ankle sprain following a slip and fall two days prior, characterized by pain, swelling, and tenderness. She reports the pain is primarily on the outside of her ankle and denies any bleeding or significant instability. Ibuprofen and ice with elevation provided some relief. She reports no pain above her ankle, in her calf, or lower leg.

O:
The patient's left ankle demonstrates tenderness to palpation over the lateral malleolus and the fifth metatarsal. There is some swelling and redness observed. Neurological exam reveals intact sensation and pulses. Neck exam is unremarkable. Lungs are clear to auscultation. Heart exam reveals a two over six systolic ejection murmur, unchanged from previous examinations. Abdomen exam is normal. The patient's blood pressure is 120/70 mmHg.

A:
The patient's primary problem is an ankle sprain. There is no evidence of fracture, but an X-ray is warranted to rule out a fracture.

P:
Order an ankle X-ray. Apply an air splint and provide crutches for non-weightbearing. Prescribe naproxen 500 mg twice daily for pain control. Elevate the ankle. Apply ice. Schedule a follow-up appointment to review the X-ray results and assess weight-bearing tolerance. Order a hemoglobin A1c test. Refill the norvasc prescription. Schedule the patient's ophthalmology appointment for the next couple of weeks.
