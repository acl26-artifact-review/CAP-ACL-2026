Chief Complaint
Status post mitral valve repair, ongoing management of atrial fibrillation and related symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling generally better since her mitral valve repair on 8/3/2020, but continues to experience occasional chest pain and shallow breathing, particularly with exertion. She describes episodes of chest pain lasting a few minutes, followed by relief. She attributes this to her atrial fibrillation and reports learning to slow down during episodes. She is currently taking coumadin 4 mg daily, lasix 4 mg daily, and atenolol 50 mg daily. She reports occasional mild irritability with the atenolol, but this has lessened over time. She is taking her medications regularly with the assistance of a reminder app.

Findings
Physical examination reveals trace peripheral edema in the lower extremities. Heart rhythm is irregularly irregular. S1 is slightly accentuated, and there is no S3. The lungs demonstrate reduced breath sounds bilaterally, but auscultation and percussion are clear. No JVD is detected.

Assessment
The patient presents with atrial fibrillation and related symptoms, managed with current medications. She is recovering well from her mitral valve repair.

Plan
Continue current medications: coumadin 4 mg daily, lasix 4 mg daily, and atenolol 50 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in six to nine months to reassess her atrial fibrillation control and overall recovery.
