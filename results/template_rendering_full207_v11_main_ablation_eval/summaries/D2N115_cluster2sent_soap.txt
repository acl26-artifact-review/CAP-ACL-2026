S:
The patient reports experiencing neck pain following a car accident, describing a rapid forward movement of his head and neck. He reports the pain is not constant but worsens with head movement. He has tried ibuprofen with limited relief. He denies headaches, visual disturbances, or hearing problems. The patient reports the pain is present with neck flexion, extension, and rotation. He reports positive tenderness to palpation of the neck soft tissues, back, and shoulders. He reports the pain is exacerbated by neck flexion and extension. He reports the pain is present with head rotation. The patient states he did not go to the emergency room after the accident and did not experience any swelling or bruising on his neck.

O:
Physical examination reveals positive pain upon lateral neck bending. There are no signs of fracture or bony abnormalities on the x-ray.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with whiplash-associated neck pain. A likely diagnosis is neck strain.

P:
I am prescribing motrin 600 mg every 6-8 hours and flexeril 10 mg as needed. I have ordered an MRI to rule out concussion or nerve impingement. I am referring the patient to physical rehabilitation or a chiropractor for evaluation and management. I have scheduled a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress.
