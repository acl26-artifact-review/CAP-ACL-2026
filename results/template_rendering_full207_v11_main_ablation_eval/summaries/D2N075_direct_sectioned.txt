Chief Complaint
The patient reports three months prior to this visit, she experienced chest pain, which resolved after four days, and has not returned. She now seeks evaluation for persistent discomfort.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports the initial episode of chest pain occurred in early October 2020 and was not associated with any known triggers. The pain was sharp and felt like a heart attack. She denies heartburn or indigestion prior to this event. The pain resolved within four days with rest and did not recur. She reports experiencing difficulty swallowing approximately three months ago, which has since resolved. The patient attributes this stress to a recent job transition. She denies any family history of gastrointestinal problems. She had her gall bladder removed several years ago.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to light palpation in the upper abdominal quadrants, but no tenderness to deep palpation. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. No acute findings were noted on physical examination.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is concerning for potential esophageal or gastrointestinal etiology, given her history of chest pain and dysphagia. Musculoskeletal etiologies and anxiety related to the recent job transition should also be considered. Rule out cardiac etiology given prior EKG and blood work.

Plan
The patient has elected to monitor for any additional symptoms. She will call the office if she experiences any recurrence of chest pain or dysphagia. We will schedule an upper endoscopy if symptoms persist or worsen. Follow up in four weeks to assess for symptom resolution.
