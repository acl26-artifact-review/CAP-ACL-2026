Chief Complaint
Anxiety regarding abnormal lab work and a referral to this specialist.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports anxiety about recent abnormal lab results received from his primary care physician, prompting a referral to this specialist. He states he doesn't fully understand the significance of the abnormal results, specifically mentioning a low immunoglobulin A level. He admits to frequently experiencing infections, though he doesn't feel it's 'more than usual' in the wintertime, and his colds often linger. He also reports a history of type 2 diabetes, diagnosed recently, and attributes this to his donut shop ownership. He has no family history of immune deficiencies. He recently traveled to Zambia and was unable to donate blood due to his recent international travel. He has no reported abdominal infections or tenderness to palpation.

Findings
Physical exam reveals regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs. Lung exam is clear with no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi. Neck exam is unremarkable, with no lymphadenopathy. The patient's weight and vital signs are within normal limits. Lab results confirm an IgA deficiency, consistent with the PCP's findings.

Assessment
The primary problem is an IgA deficiency, contributing to recurrent infections. The patient's diabetes and recent travel history are relevant comorbidities.

Plan
I have ordered additional blood work to evaluate for other antibody deficiencies. We will monitor for recurrent infections or significant changes in abdominal symptoms. I recommend a follow-up in four weeks to assess the results of the additional testing and discuss further management strategies. Please ensure you inform any future blood product providers of your IgA deficiency history.
