Chief Complaint
Lower back pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of acute disk herniation four months ago, and now experiences lower back pain. He describes the pain as sharp and intermittent, exacerbated by certain movements and prolonged sitting. He denies any recent trauma. The patient reports tingling in his feet and numbness. He also reports spasm. He has tried heat and ice. The patient reports pain. He took some ibuprofen and acetaminophen.

Findings
The patient reports a positive straight leg raise. Examination reveals neck pain. The patient reports no pain.

Assessment
The patient has an acute disk herniation and reports lower back pain.

Plan
The doctor orders a lumbar spine MRI. The doctor prescribes meloxicam fifteen milligrams once a day. The doctor recommends physical therapy. Arrange follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
