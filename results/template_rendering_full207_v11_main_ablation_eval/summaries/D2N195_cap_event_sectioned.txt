Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain that began after playing basketball, characterized as a 'pop' with subsequent swelling and difficulty walking. The pain has decreased somewhat overnight, but he still favors the leg. He has tried tylenol with minimal relief. He reports playing basketball only two years ago. He was unhappy about the last game. He denies any recent trauma. He has not checked the blood pressure at home.

Findings
The patient reports pain to palpation of the medial aspect of the right knee with some edema and ecchymosis. The right knee exam shows a negative varus and valgus test of my maneuvers. The patient's blood pressure is 140/88. The patient's a1c is 7.2. The patient's lungs sound clear. The patient's heart rate is 72 bpm with a grade 2 systolic ejection murmur. The patient's right knee x-ray is normal.

Assessment
The patient has a medial knee strain, likely secondary to a basketball injury.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. Order a knee MRI to evaluate for a possible MCL tear. Prescribe a knee immobilizer and instruct the patient to stay off the leg for two weeks. Schedule a nutrition consult to address dietary concerns. Continue metformin 500mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks.
