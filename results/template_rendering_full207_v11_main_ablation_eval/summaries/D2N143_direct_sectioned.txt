Chief Complaint
Symptoms of discomfort, a change in vaginal discharge, and odor in her vaginal region, accompanied by right-sided vaginal pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing discomfort, a change in vaginal discharge, and odor in her vaginal region, with right-sided vaginal pain. She states that the right side of her vagina is starting to become painful. She denies fevers or chills. She recently learned that her partner has been in sexual encounters with other people and wants to be evaluated for all STDs.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no external lesions, normal labia majora and labia minora, normal physiological discharge with normal color and smell. Pelvic exam demonstrates no cervical motion tenderness or adnexal tenderness or masses. Swabs were obtained for STD testing. Cervix is normal. Vaginal wall is normal. No cervical motion tenderness and no adnexal tenderness or masses noted.

Assessment
Screening for sexually transmitted infections.

Plan
The patient was screened for gonorrhea, chlamydia, trichomoniasis, syphilis, HIV, and hepatitis C. Swabs were obtained for testing. Results will be communicated to the patient in approximately three days. The patient was counselled regarding risk factors related to recent sexual activity and provided instructions for follow-up if symptoms persist.
