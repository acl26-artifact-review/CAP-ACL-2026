Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following swimming.

History of Present Illness
Rebecca is a 27-year-old female patient with a history of carpal tunnel release and chronic back pain. She reports increased right knee pain over the past month, primarily experienced while swimming using the breaststroke. She has been swimming more regularly for the past month to become more active. The pain started approximately two laps ago and has been increasing with each subsequent lap. She denies any injury or trauma. Her chronic back pain has been well-controlled with medication and therapy. She is currently taking medication and attending therapy sessions and reports feeling well. She is sleeping well and eating well. Her carpal tunnel release is stable and has been well-managed.

Findings
Physical exam reveals pain with palpation of the patella and with patellar inhibition. Range of motion is limited due to pain. X-ray of the right knee demonstrates normal findings. The patient's heart rhythm is regular, lungs are clear, and there is no palpable adenopathy. Grip strength is equal bilaterally. No other significant findings on physical exam.

Assessment
The patient presents with chondromalacia of the patella, likely secondary to overuse and the breaststroke swimming technique.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 600 mg four times daily for pain relief. Recommend straight leg raising exercises to strengthen the quadriceps. Advise the patient to avoid lunges and the breaststroke swimming technique. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess response to treatment. Continue current antidepressant medication and therapy as planned.
