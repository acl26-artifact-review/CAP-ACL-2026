Chief Complaint
Back pain, reported following a refrigerator move.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain that began five days ago after pushing a refrigerator through a doorway. He describes the pain as being on the lower right side of his back. He has a prior history of lumbar discectomy. He has been taking ibuprofen and once tried tylenol and ibuprofen together, which provided some relief. He denies any neurological symptoms such as leg pain, numbness, or tingling. He also denies chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting. He reports difficulty bending over. He has no problems with bladder or bowel function.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the right lumbar spine, decreased flexion and extension of the back, and a positive straight leg raise on the right. Vital signs are stable, with no fever and blood pressure within normal limits. X-rays demonstrate normal bony alignment without fracture. Lab results are unremarkable. The patient's heart and lungs are clear to auscultation.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with a lumbar strain, likely secondary to the recent injury. The prior discectomy does not appear to be contributing to the current presentation.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI to rule out any other pathology. I will prescribe meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily and ultram 50 milligrams every four hours as needed for pain. I am referring the patient to physical therapy for evaluation and treatment. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed. Continue the ibuprofen as needed, but discontinue once the pain is under control.
