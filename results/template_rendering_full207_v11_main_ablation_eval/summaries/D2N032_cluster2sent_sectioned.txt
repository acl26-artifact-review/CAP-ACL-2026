Chief Complaint
Right hip pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right hip pain since February 2020, which has been worsening with movement, particularly with pivoting and when standing and taking steps. The patient describes the pain as ranging from 2 to 7 out of 10. He denies fever or chills, tingling or numbness, or bowel or bladder problems. The patient reports decreased range of motion with exertion and pain with resisted right hip flexion, right hip flexion, pain when lifting the knee, pain when squeezing knees together, and pain when kicking leg out straight. The patient reports pain when leaning back.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no fever or chills, and the patient denies any neurological deficits.

Assessment
The clinician suspects right hip degenerative joint disease.

Plan
The clinician plans a low-impact exercise program, the use of NSAIDs, the use of a cane, and a cortisone injection into the right hip joint. The clinician explained the risks of injection, including needles, sterile and covid.
