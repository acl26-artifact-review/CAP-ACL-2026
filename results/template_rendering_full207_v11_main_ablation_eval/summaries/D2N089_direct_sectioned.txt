Chief Complaint
Annual physical examination and follow-up on chronic medical conditions.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 52-year-old female with a history of rheumatoid arthritis, atrial fibrillation, and reflux. She reports ongoing palpitations, particularly when stressed, and has been trying meditation and running with her dog to manage this. She has been taking methotrexate weekly and protonix daily and reports no issues with either medication. She has experienced a five-month remission of reflux symptoms. She reports nasal congestion attributed to allergies. The patient completed a review of systems sheet upon arrival, noting palpitations and nasal congestion.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a slight 2/6 systolic ejection murmur at the right elbow. The right elbow demonstrates edema and erythema with tenderness to palpation. Lung auscultation is clear bilaterally. The heart rate is controlled and regular. The event monitor demonstrates conversion pauses during episodes of atrial fibrillation. Autoimmune panel results are unremarkable, consistent with controlled rheumatoid arthritis.

Assessment
The patient's primary diagnoses include rheumatoid arthritis, atrial fibrillation, and allergic rhinitis.

Plan
Continue methotrexate 2.5 mg weekly. Order a cardiology referral for cardiac ablation to address atrial fibrillation. Prescribe protonix 40 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and address any ongoing concerns.
