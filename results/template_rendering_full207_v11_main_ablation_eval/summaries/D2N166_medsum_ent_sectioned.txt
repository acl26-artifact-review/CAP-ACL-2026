Chief Complaint
Low back pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports having persistent low back pain for the past two weeks, which began after lifting a box of books. He describes the pain as a combination of aching and sharp, primarily in his lower back. The pain initially wasn't radiating, but now he experiences radiating pain down his left leg. He admits to using ibuprofen and Tylenol with some relief, and reports that no one has discussed surgery with him. He is an Olympic weightlifter with a sedentary job. He has a history of playing soccer as a midfielder. He is a fan of Tottenham Hotspur.

Findings
Physical examination reveals midline tenderness at the L4-L5 disc space with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness. Range of motion is intact, with the ability to bend forward and backward. A straight leg raise test is positive on the left leg. Vital signs are normal: blood pressure 130/70 mmHg, respiratory rate 18, regular heart rate. The patient's lungs are clear to auscultation. Neurological exam reveals intact sensation and motor strength in the lower extremities. MRI findings show a disc desiccation, a disc bulge with paracentral disc herniation resulting in moderate right neuroforaminal stenosis.

Assessment
The patient presents with lumbar back pain at the L4-L5 level due to a herniated disc with radiculopathy, specifically moderate right neuroforaminal stenosis.

Plan
I recommend a spinal injection with a strong NSAID and corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and provide pain relief. This procedure will be performed under fluoroscopy to ensure accurate placement. I will schedule this injection today if you would like. Please review the provided paperwork for information on potential risks and benefits. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist, or sooner if they worsen. ,
