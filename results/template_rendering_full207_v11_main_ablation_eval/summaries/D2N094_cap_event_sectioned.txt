Chief Complaint
Pain in the right index finger following a skiing accident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his right index finger following a skiing accident five days ago. He fell and jammed his finger, describing it as a 'pop' and immediate pain. He took ibuprofen, but it did not provide significant relief. He rates the pain as 7/10. He denies any other injuries sustained during the fall. He reports chronic constipation managed with miralax.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the distal interphalangeal joint of the right index finger. Range of motion is limited due to pain. X-ray of the right hand reveals no fractures or dislocations; the right index finger appears normal.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and physical examination, a sprain of the distal interphalangeal joint of the right index finger is suspected.

Plan
I will apply a splint to the right index finger to immobilize the joint and promote healing. I will prescribe a strong anti-inflammatory medication, mobic, at a dose of 15 mg once daily. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess healing and determine if physical therapy is indicated.
