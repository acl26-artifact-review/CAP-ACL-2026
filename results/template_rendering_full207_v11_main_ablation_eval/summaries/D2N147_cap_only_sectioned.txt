Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left shoulder, describing it as a dull ache that has been present for approximately six weeks. The pain is exacerbated by overhead activities and when lying on the affected side. He denies any recent trauma or injury. The patient reports taking acetaminophen for the pain, which provides some relief. He also reports a headache and denies fever. He previously was a football player.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. The patient denies numbness in his fingers.

Assessment
The patient is experiencing shoulder pain and pain in the left hand. He needs to have this fracture healed.

Plan
The doctor recommends avoiding tackle football. Order shoulder radiographs. Initiate physical therapy. Continue acetaminophen as needed. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
