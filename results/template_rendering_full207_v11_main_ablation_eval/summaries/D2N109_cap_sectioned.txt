Chief Complaint
Ankle pain and a history of diabetes and high blood pressure.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a long spring and some snow and ice, and she slipped and her left ankle twisted underneath her two days ago. She reports significant pain and swelling, making it difficult to walk. She has been using ibuprofen, ice, and elevation, which has provided some relief. She reports stable blood sugar levels with her diet and regular monitoring. Her hemoglobin a1c last time i looked at epic was about six . eight. She is due for an eye exam in the next couple of weeks and needs a refill on her norvasc.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral malleolus and fifth metatarsal. There is some swelling and redness, but no bleeding. Lung sounds are clear. Heart sounds are normal. There is a two over six systolic ejection murmur. No sensation or pulses deficits are noted.

Assessment
The patient likely has an ankle sprain. She also has established high blood pressure and diabetes.

Plan
I'm going to order an x-ray of the ankle to rule out fracture. I'll place her in an air splint and provide crutches to avoid weightbearing. She will take naprosyn 500 milligrams twice a day for pain control. I'll order a hemoglobin a1c today. I'll schedule a follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist, and we'll arrange for an orthopedics consult if a fracture is confirmed. I'll also refill her norvasc medication.
