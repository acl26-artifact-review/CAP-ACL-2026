Chief Complaint
Ongoing management of rheumatoid arthritis, with worsening right knee swelling and pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports ongoing rheumatoid arthritis, with a history of a right knee steroid injection approximately two weeks ago. He states the injection provided temporary relief, but the knee has since become increasingly swollen and painful. The swelling has now spread to the entire leg, and he reports pain levels around 6 on a normal day. He has been wearing compression socks, but they have not been effective in reducing the swelling. He has been taking Xeljanz 11 mg daily and Celebrex 200 mg daily, reporting that the Celebrex has been helpful until the recent swelling. He admits to a high salt intake and frequently consuming salty snacks. He has not discussed this issue with his primary care physician.

Findings
Physical examination reveals diminished range of motion in the right knee, with pain upon straightening. There is pitting edema noted around the knee and ankle. Capillary refill is normal in the toes. The knee is warm to the touch, indicating inflammation. Laboratory results are pending.

Assessment
Clinical findings are consistent with rheumatoid arthritis exacerbation, characterized by right knee inflammation and edema.

Plan
Order a knee x-ray to evaluate the source of the swelling. Administer a steroid injection to the right knee to reduce inflammation. Schedule physical therapy for the right knee. Order CMP levels to monitor Xeljanz levels. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment. Advise the patient to reduce salt intake and elevate his feet as tolerated.
