S:
The patient reports intermittent facial pain for approximately two to three months, initially noticed gradually. He describes the pain as stabbing and shooting, particularly in the right cheekbone area, sometimes accompanied by heat. He mentions that certain temperatures and cold weather seem to trigger the pain. He denies sensory loss, weakness, or other neurological symptoms. He has tried ibuprofen and tiger balm with no relief. The patient reports occasional headaches, but not migraines, and takes occasional tylenol.

O:
Physical exam reveals symmetrical facial appearance. The patient follows the finger in both directions. Cotton tape test reveals numbness on the right side of the face, but not on the left. Needle prick reveals dullness on the left side of the face and sharpness on the right side. MRI reveals a small blood vessel abutting and pinching the trigeminal nerve.

A:
Based on the patient's reported symptoms and physical exam findings, a trigeminal neuralgia is suspected. The MRI confirms compression of the trigeminal nerve.

P:
I recommend starting with tegretol to manage the pain. I will also order genetic testing to assess for potential dermatologic side effects. Please schedule a follow-up in one month to assess the effectiveness of the medication and to discuss potential surgical options if the pain persists.
