Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath

History of Present Illness
Rachel is a 48-year-old female presenting with shortness of breath. She reports experiencing mild shortness of breath during mild exercise and walking up her driveway, occasionally accompanied by palpitations. She attributes this to possible back pain exacerbation. She has a history of depression and has been taking effexor for it. She also reports occasional low mood and feeling extremely low, particularly on certain days. She admits to smoking approximately one cigarette a day and previously smoked two packs per day. She has gained weight due to COVID-19 and is currently experiencing chronic back pain, which she manages with gabapentin neurontin. She reports that yoga has been attempted but not consistently maintained. She reports mild wheezes on auscultation. She denies any recent trauma.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild wheezes in all lung fields. The patient's diaphragm appears flattened on chest x-ray. Examination is otherwise unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with COPD and may have asthma. A pulmonary function test revealed possible COPD and asthma.

Plan
The doctor prescribes combivent (two puffs twice a day) and albuterol (two puffs as needed). A prednisone taper pack will also be ordered. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. The patient will continue taking effexor.
