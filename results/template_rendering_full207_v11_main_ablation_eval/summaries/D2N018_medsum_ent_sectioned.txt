Chief Complaint
Severe right upper arm pain following a volleyball injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing severe right upper arm pain following a volleyball injury. He describes spiking the ball, being kicked from under him, and landing on his arm. The pain began approximately 18 hours ago, following a fall during the game. He has been taking ibuprofen every six hours, but reports it is not providing adequate relief. He rates the pain as a 9 out of 10. He denies any numbness or tingling in the arm. He has a history of gallstones and takes pepcid for it, and he had a lumbar fusion six years ago.

Findings
Examination reveals swelling and erythema over the right shoulder, along with tenderness to palpation. Pulses are intact bilaterally. The patient is able to straighten the elbow but not move the shoulder, indicating a possible fracture. X-rays demonstrate a proximal humerus fracture.

Assessment
Proximal humerus fracture.

Plan
Prescribe lortab 500 mg (2 tablets every 6 hours as needed for pain), ordering 20 tablets. We will apply a long arm cast and sling to stabilize the fracture. Follow-up in two weeks to assess bone alignment and discuss potential surgical intervention if necessary. Refer the patient to physical therapy for rehabilitation.
