Chief Complaint
Severe acid reflux symptoms, worsening over the past few years.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a several-year history of acid reflux, with symptoms worsening over the past few years. He currently takes 40 milligrams of omeprazole twice daily, finding it ineffective at controlling symptoms on some days. He describes the sensation as burning in his chest and throat. He denies difficulty swallowing or abdominal pain. The symptoms are exacerbated by spicy foods and carbonated beverages. He has no history of gastrointestinal issues and reports no unintentional weight loss. He notes a family history of esophageal cancer on his mother's side and his mother had acid reflux and took medication to coat the stomach lining. He has had a childhood surgery for intestinal twisting.

Findings
Physical exam is unremarkable except for well-healed abdominal surgical scars. Vital signs are within normal limits. The patient denies dysphagia.

Assessment
The patient presents with a likely diagnosis of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) with a possible functional etiology.

Plan
I will prescribe nexium 40 milligrams twice daily to replace the omeprazole. I recommend an upper endoscopy to evaluate for potential causes of the GERD and rule out precancerous or cancerous conditions. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and discuss the results of the endoscopy. Please call my office to schedule the endoscopy.
