Chief Complaint
Left knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient presents with left knee pain that occurs occasionally, less than once a day, when walking. He describes a 'giving out' sensation and often needs to hold a grocery cart for support. The pain comes and goes quickly. He denies a specific injury, such as a fall or bump. He has not taken any medication for the pain as it does not last long. The patient reports no significant interference with his daily activities. He has a trip planned to Columbus, Georgia, in the next month, including line dancing, which he anticipates may be difficult due to the knee. He has been taking farxiga, amlodipine, lisinopril, and hydrochlorothiazide regularly. His blood pressure remains around 120/70 mmHg, and his blood sugars have been running slightly higher, around 130 in the morning. He is taking metformin with his blood pressure medication.

Findings
Vital signs are stable: temperature 98.2°F, pulse 72, respirations 16, blood pressure 120/70 mmHg. A slight two over six systolic ejection murmur is appreciated at the left base. Physical exam reveals no ecchymosis or edema, no effusion, and no pain to palpation of the left medial knee. Range of motion is full. No decreased range of motion was noted. The patient can bend and straighten his knee as expected. A quick physical exam was performed, including auscultation of the heart and lungs, which were clear. The patient's left knee x-ray showed no evidence of fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's left knee pain is most likely due to osteoarthritis, given the intermittent nature of the symptoms and the lack of acute injury.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily. Order an echocardiogram to evaluate the murmur. Order a hemoglobin A1C to assess diabetes management. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to monitor symptoms and adjust treatment as needed. Recommend physical therapy to strengthen the muscles around the knee and improve range of motion. Advise the patient to consider using a cane if needed when traveling to Columbus, Georgia, for the line dancing event.
