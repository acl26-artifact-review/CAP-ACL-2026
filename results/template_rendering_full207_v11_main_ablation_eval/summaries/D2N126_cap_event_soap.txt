S:
The patient reports sustaining a knee injury during skiing in Vermont last weekend, resulting in a fall and subsequent pain. He states the pain began immediately after the injury and has persisted since. He describes the pain as being most prominent on the outside of the knee and reports difficulty with weight-bearing. He reports some improvement with ibuprofen, but not complete relief. He denies any numbness or tingling in his toes and confirms sensation in his toes. He reports being a pretty active person, enjoying chasing his two four-year-old children, but currently not engaging in much running.

O:
Physical exam reveals edema and effusion over the right knee, pain to palpation on the lateral aspect, decreased flexion and extension, and a positive Lachman sign. Pulse is palpable. The right knee is warm to the touch. The patient has a brace on his right knee.

A:
The patient likely sustained an ACL injury based on the mechanism of injury and physical exam findings. Further investigation with a knee MRI is warranted to confirm the diagnosis and assess the extent of the injury.

P:
Order a knee MRI to confirm the ACL injury. Prescribe a brace for the right knee to provide support and stability. Recommend ibuprofen 600-800mg every 8 hours as needed for pain management. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 7-10 days to discuss MRI results and treatment options.
