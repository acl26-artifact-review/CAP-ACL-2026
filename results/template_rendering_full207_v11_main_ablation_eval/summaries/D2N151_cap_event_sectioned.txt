Chief Complaint
Elbow pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports elbow pain for approximately four days, exacerbated by pottery creation. The pain is located on the inside of the elbow and radiates down the arm. Ibuprofen has provided minimal relief. The patient denies any prior elbow injuries.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the medial epicondyle of the right elbow. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Pulse is regular and equal bilaterally. Blood pressure is 120/70 mmHg. Respiratory rate is 18 breaths per minute. Oxygen saturation is 99%.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with medial epicondylitis.

Plan
Order a sling for symptomatic relief. Prescribe 600 mg ibuprofen every six hours as needed for pain. Recommend ice packs for 20 minutes, three times daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress. Refer to physical therapy for evaluation and treatment. Advise patient to reduce pottery activities until pain subsides.
