Chief Complaint
Numbness and tingling in the right hand, primarily affecting the thumb, index, and middle fingers.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing numbness and tingling in his right hand for approximately six months, with intermittent symptoms. The symptoms have become more persistent over the past month. He reports pain primarily when typing on the keyboard and when moving his mouse. Ibuprofen provides some relief, and the pain worsens when wrist is pulled up towards you. He also notes pain on extension and when touching the fingertips. He denies any recent trauma. He reports that the numbness and tingling is constant now. He denies any sleep disturbances related to the symptoms.

Findings
Examination reveals decreased grip strength in the right hand. Tinel's sign is positive over the right wrist. Palpation of the wrist reveals tenderness. The patient grimaces when the wrist is tapped.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Order an EMG to confirm the diagnosis. Prescribe a wrist splint for nighttime use. Continue ibuprofen up to 600 mg four times daily. Schedule a follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
