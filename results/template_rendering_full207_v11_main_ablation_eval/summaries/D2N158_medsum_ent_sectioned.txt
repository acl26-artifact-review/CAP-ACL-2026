Chief Complaint
Abdominal pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing abdominal pain for one week. She has a longstanding history of diabetes type two and reports increased coffee consumption recently, speculating it may be a contributing factor to her current symptoms. She also reports intermittent nausea and occasional vomiting over the past week. The pain is increasing in severity and is exacerbated by exercise, specifically jogging. She denies fever. She has been taking metformin 500mg twice daily as prescribed and reports that it has been helping to manage her diabetes. She has been taking Flovent twice daily and albuterol as needed for her asthma, though she admits to occasionally forgetting to take the Flovent. She has not experienced any recent asthma attacks. She has been eating a lot of spicy food lately.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness in the epigastrium. Lung exam shows mild end-expiratory wheezes. Heart exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, unchanged from the prior exam. No tenderness in the right lower or upper quadrants. Blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 80 bpm.

Assessment
Acute gastritis is likely, possibly exacerbated by caffeine intake and a history of alcohol use. The patient's diabetes type two and asthma are present but not directly impacting the current acute presentation.

Plan
Prescribe zantac twice daily for symptomatic relief. Continue metformin 500mg twice daily, adjusting to 1000mg in the morning and 500mg in the evening. Recommend reducing caffeine and spicy food intake. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress. Advise the patient to continue taking Flovent twice daily and albuterol as needed for asthma. Arrange a urinalysis and urine pregnancy test and a comprehensive metabolic panel to rule out other potential causes.
