Chief Complaint
Headache

History of Present Illness
Carolyn reports a headache that began approximately one week ago, described as a dull pain in the back of her head. She experiences flushing in her ears and occasional dizziness with these headaches. She has taken Tylenol and Advil, but the headaches persist and return. The headache is worse in the evening and is associated with stress. She has a history of tension-type headaches. She has not been checking her blood sugars and has not been exercising regularly due to stress.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild posterior paraspinal muscular tenderness in the cervical spine and bilateral trapezius musculature. Extraocular movements are intact. The heart exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is unchanged from the prior exam. The funduscopic exam shows no papilledema. Blood pressure is 130/80 mmHg. Heart rate is 72 bpm. Temperature is 98.6°F.

Assessment
The patient has a tension-type headache, likely exacerbated by stress and musculoskeletal tension. Her diabetes mellitus type 2 remains under control with current metformin therapy.

Plan
Continue the patient on metformin 500 mg twice daily. Order a hemoglobin A1c and CBC/chem-12 to assess glycemic control and other relevant parameters. Prescribe flexeril 5 mg three times daily for muscle relaxation. Advise the patient to monitor for worsening headache symptoms or fever and to contact the clinic if these occur. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment. Encourage the patient to return to a regular exercise routine to improve blood sugar control and overall well-being. Recommend a visit to a golf course to improve mood and activity.
