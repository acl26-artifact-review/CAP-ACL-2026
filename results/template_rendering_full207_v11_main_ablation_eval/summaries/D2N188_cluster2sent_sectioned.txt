Chief Complaint
Right ankle injury following a mountain biking fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining an ankle injury during a mountain biking lesson after a fall. He describes hearing a crack and experiencing immediate pain. He has taken Tylenol for the pain and reports some tingling and numbness in his toes. He also reports a fever, which has now resolved.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 118/77 mmHg and heart rate is 77 beats per minute. Examination reveals edema and erythema on the lateral malleolus of the right ankle, with associated ecchymosis and excoriations. Palpation of the right lateral malleolus elicits pain. Range of motion is decreased in flexion and extension, and internal and external rotation of the right ankle. The right knee and foot are intact. An x-ray of the right ankle reveals no acute fracture or bony abnormality, but demonstrates soft tissue injury.

Assessment
The patient has an acute right ankle sprain.

Plan
I recommend an air cast, crutches, and meloxicam 15 mg once daily. Instruct the patient to ice the area for 20 minutes five times a day and to weight-bear as tolerated. I anticipate improvement within two weeks; return for follow-up if symptoms persist or if physical therapy is considered.
