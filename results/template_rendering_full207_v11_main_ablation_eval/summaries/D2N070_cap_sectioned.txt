Chief Complaint
Back pain

History of Present Illness
Logan is a 58-year-old male with a past medical history of diabetes type 2, hypertension, and osteoarthritis. He presents today with some back pain. Logan reports that he was helping his daughter with some heavy equipment and lifted some boxes a little too quickly, and they were a little too heavy. He thought he heard a pop when he moved and had to lie down for about an hour before the pain relieved. It's been a little stiff ever since. He's been taking Tylenol and ibuprofen, but they haven't seemed to help much. He reports tingling in his toes on his right foot until about Sunday afternoon, and that seemed to go away. He tends to lie on his right side at night to relieve the pain. He's been eating less than stellar due to being on the go and not having home-cooked meals in weeks, and he loves peanut butter cups. He reports a sweet tooth. He's also been forgetting to monitor his blood pressure when he's out of the house. He has had some early osteoarthritis in his knee, and it gets aggravated every once in a while if he runs too much or lifts boxes that are too heavy. The patient reports pain in his lower back.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slight 2 out of 6 systolic heart murmur. The patient's blood pressure is slightly elevated in the office. A lumbar spine palpation reveals pain with flexion and extension. The straight leg raise test is negative. Hemoglobin A1C is elevated at 8.0%. A normal x-ray of the back was obtained.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with lumbar strain. He has diabetes and hypertension, which require ongoing management.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for the back pain. Recommend applying icy heat to the spot. Refer to physical therapy. Increase the metformin dosage to 1000 mg twice a day. Increase the lisinopril dosage to 20 mg daily. Order a hemoglobin A1C test in six months. The nurse will come in to help the patient get checked out.
