Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately three weeks, initially unsure of the cause, but now linked to basement renovation work, particularly reaching and lifting. He denies a specific injury but reports difficulty with overhead movements and pain when lying on the affected shoulder. He has been taking two extra-strength Tylenol every six to eight hours, which provides some relief. He has experienced similar aches and pains in the past, but not severe enough to require other interventions.

Findings
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder, tenderness to palpation of the greater tuberosity, capillary refill less than three seconds, and intact light touch sensation. No signs of neurovascular compromise.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and physical exam findings, the most likely diagnosis is rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI of the left shoulder to further evaluate the rotator cuff tendons. I am also referring the patient to physical therapy for a six to eight week course to address the pain and improve shoulder function. The patient can continue taking extra-strength Tylenol as needed for pain relief. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
