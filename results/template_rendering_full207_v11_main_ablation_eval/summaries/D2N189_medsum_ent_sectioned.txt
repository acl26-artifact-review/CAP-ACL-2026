Chief Complaint
The patient reports struggling and feeling scared following a recent heart attack and stent placement.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing a heart attack and subsequent stent placement in the right coronary artery. He describes feeling thankful to be alive but struggling with the experience. He has been taking it easy and doing light work, primarily sitting and taking medication. He reports some soreness after the surgery, which has improved. He also reports experiencing shortness of breath when walking or going up and downstairs. He denies any further chest pain since the stent placement. He is currently taking brilinta and aspirin as prescribed.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slight systolic ejection murmur heard at the left base. Blood pressure is 127/70. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. No jugular venous distension or carotid bruits are noted. The right radial artery cardiac cath site is clean, dry, and intact with a palpable pulse. Mild mitral regurgitation is noted on echocardiogram.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is an inferior myocardial infarction with a stent in place. He has mild mitral regurgitation.

Plan
Continue the patient on brilinta and aspirin lifelong. Schedule a follow-up echocardiogram in six months. Recommend cardiac rehabilitation to improve confidence and provide dietary education. Advise the patient to resume swimming gradually, starting with a few laps. Order liver function studies and a lipid panel for follow-up. Schedule a follow-up appointment in six weeks.
