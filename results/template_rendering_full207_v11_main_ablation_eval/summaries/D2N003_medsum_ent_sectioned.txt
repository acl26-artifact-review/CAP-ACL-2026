Chief Complaint
Reports back pain, with some dizziness and blood in urine.

History of Present Illness
John is a 61-year-old male with a past medical history of kidney stones, migraines, and reflux. He presents with back pain, similar to what he experienced with previous kidney stones two years ago. The pain started on the right side and has shifted to the left. It's been present for the last four days, initially intermittent but now constant and quite severe. He reports feeling dizzy and lightheaded, particularly when exerting himself, such as climbing stairs. He also notes some blood in his urine, which he finds difficult to detect. He has been diligent with his migraine medication (imitrex) and reports no issues. He's been making dietary changes for his reflux, ordering take-out frequently. He's staying hydrated. He reports some body aches, attributing them to favoring his back due to the pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation on the right-hand side and in the right lower quadrant. The abdomen also feels tender. Heart sounds are clear, and the lungs are clear. Blood pressure is slightly elevated. Creatinine levels are mildly elevated. Abdominal x-rays are pending review. Dragon shows a blood pressure of 142/88 and a creatinine of 1.3.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms, particularly the back pain, tenderness, and elevated creatinine, suggest a recurrence of kidney stones. Further investigation is needed to confirm the diagnosis.

Plan
Order a CT scan without contrast of the abdomen and pelvis to evaluate for kidney stones. Prescribe ultram 50 mg every six hours as needed for pain. Continue the patient's protonix 40 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up in one week to review the CT scan results and assess treatment response. The nurse will assist with scheduling the CT scan and will provide further instructions.
