Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for several weeks, exacerbated by yard work activities. He describes persistent right shoulder pain, particularly with arm movement, and reports a history of similar right shoulder issues related to playing baseball in his youth. He has been using Advil and icing the affected areas. He denies numbness in the arm or hands. He reports foot and ankle soreness related to yard work, treated with ice and elevation.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the right shoulder, with tenderness to palpation over the greater tuberosity. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the right arm. Neurological exam is intact. Vitals are stable: blood pressure 116/84, heart rate 62, oxygen saturation 99%. Shoulder X-ray demonstrates no fracture.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy of the right shoulder.

Plan
I have ordered an MRI of the right shoulder to further evaluate the rotator cuff. I am also referring the patient to physical therapy for a course of six to eight weeks. Continue the use of acetaminophen as needed for symptom relief. If symptoms do not improve after six weeks, we will consider a corticosteroid injection to the shoulder joint.
