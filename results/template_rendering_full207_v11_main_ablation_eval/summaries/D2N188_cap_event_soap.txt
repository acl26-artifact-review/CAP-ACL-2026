S:
The patient reports an ankle injury sustained while mountain biking, describing it as a twisting outward and hearing a crack. He reports some tingling and numbness in his heart and other areas, and has experienced a fever reaching 102 degrees. He states he has taken Tylenol for the pain, which provided no relief. He admits to limping and reports hearing a crackle when the injury occurred.

O:
Physical exam reveals edema and erythema on the lateral malleolus with associated ecchymosis and excoriations. There is pain to palpation of the right lateral malleolus, and decreased flexion and extension of the right ankle with internal and external rotation limitations. Vital signs show a temperature of 98.6°F, blood pressure of 118/77 mmHg, and a heart rate of 77 bpm. The x-ray shows no acute fracture or bony abnormality, but does reveal soft tissue injury.

A:
The patient has an acute right ankle sprain.

P:
An air cast and crutches have been ordered. Meloxicam 15 mg once daily has been prescribed. Ice the area for 20 minutes five times a day. Weight-bear as tolerated. Follow up if symptoms do not improve in two weeks.
