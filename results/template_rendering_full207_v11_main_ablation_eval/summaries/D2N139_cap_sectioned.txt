Chief Complaint
High blood pressure and palpitations.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing high blood pressure and palpitations, initially attributed to caffeine consumption and a perceived panic attack in March. He has a family history of hypertension and reports that his blood pressure has been improving with a new medication started a month ago. He currently takes a combo medication, 40-25, and is taking amlodipine 10 milligrams daily. He checks his blood pressure every morning and reports readings between 146 and 155. He is attempting to reduce his salt intake and tries to walk on the treadmill regularly. He consumed several cups of coffee and no breakfast on the day of the event. He believes the event was likely a panic attack.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal heart and lung sounds. Examination of the ankles and feet does not reveal any signs of swelling. The patient reports chest pain.

Assessment
The patient presents with resistant hypertension and a family history of hypertension. He is currently on multiple medications with limited efficacy.

Plan
Spironolactone will be added to Mr. Roberts' regimen. A metabolic panel will be ordered in two weeks. The patient will be referred to the resistant hypertension clinic. Carvedilol is being considered. Follow up in two weeks to assess blood pressure and electrolytes. The doctor will provide information on the dash eating plan.
