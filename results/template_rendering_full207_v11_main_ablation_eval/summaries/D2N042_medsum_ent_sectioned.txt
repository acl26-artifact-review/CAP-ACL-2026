Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall yesterday while taking out the trash. He initially could not walk on it, but now can put some weight on it, though he is still limping. He iced and elevated his foot last night and took ibuprofen. He rates his pain as a 6/10 without ibuprofen and a 1/10 with ibuprofen. He has had many ankle injuries previously, but this is the first time he has injured this specific ankle. He also mentioned that he has played soccer in college and a little inner-marino soccer now, but has not been able to play since the injury.

Findings
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus and edema of the right ankle. Tenderness is noted to palpation of the anterior lateral soft tissue. There is no laxity on anterior drawer or inversion stress. Neurovascular exam demonstrates brisk capillary refill of less than three seconds and a strong dorsalis pedis pulse. X-ray of the right ankle shows no fracture.

Assessment
Right ankle sprain, likely involving the anterior talofibular ligament.

Plan
Prescribe an air cast to stabilize the ankle and crutches to limit weight-bearing. Recommend continued ibuprofen as needed for pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 14 days to assess healing progress. Advise the patient to continue icing and elevation.
