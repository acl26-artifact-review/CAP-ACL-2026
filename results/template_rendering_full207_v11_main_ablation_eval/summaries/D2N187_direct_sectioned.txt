Chief Complaint
Sudden visual distortion and blurring of vision.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sudden visual distortion and blurring of vision. He denies a personal history of similar episodes. He reports difficulty adjusting to dim lighting in restaurants, specifically at McDonald's in Arlington. He reports a history of smoking one pack of cigarettes per day and has attempted to quit but finds it challenging. He occasionally checks his blood pressure but does not consistently maintain a log. The technician noted a decrease in vision when reading and straight lines appearing crooked, and door frames appearing crooked to the patient.

Findings
The anterior segment examination reveals posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes. The cornea is clear. Cup to disc ratio is 0.2. Fundus examination reveals the retina attached without tears, holes, or pigment changes in the fovea. The right eye shows drusen, retinal pigment epithelium thickening, and subretinal fluid and exudates accumulation. The left eye shows the cornea is clear, cup to disc ratio is 0.3, and no pigment changes in the fovea. No hemorrhage or subretinal fluid is present. Diagnostic tests reveal drusen deposits in the retinal pigment epithelium and subretinal fluid accumulation. Fluorescein angiography reveals focal areas of leakage in the right eye consistent with neurovascular age-related macular degeneration.

Assessment
Neurovascular age-related macular degeneration, advanced stage.

Plan
Administer monthly injections of lucentis into the vitreous portion of the eye after numbing, administered regularly over time to potentially slow the rate of vision decline or prevent further vision loss. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
