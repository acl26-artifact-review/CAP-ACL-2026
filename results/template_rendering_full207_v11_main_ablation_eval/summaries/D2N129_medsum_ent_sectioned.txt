Chief Complaint
Yearly exam and concerns regarding recent dietary changes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of type 2 diabetes and hypertension, both of which she has been managing. She recently began working in a bakery, which has led to increased snacking and dessert consumption, impacting her blood sugar levels. Her blood sugars have been fluctuating due to this dietary change, despite continued adherence to her prescribed metformin twice daily. She reports no other concerning symptoms or changes in her medical history. She denies any recent trauma or other significant medical events. She reports sleeping well, with eight hours of sleep per night.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal vital signs, including blood pressure within the target range. Examination of the extremities is unremarkable. A grade 2 systolic ejection fraction murmur is present, as previously noted. Neurological examination is intact. No swelling or edema is observed. Hemoglobin A1c is elevated, indicating suboptimal blood sugar control.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is type 2 diabetes, currently exhibiting suboptimal control due to dietary changes. Secondary hypertension remains well-managed with current medication.

Plan
I recommend a trial of dietary modification to address the recent dietary changes and their impact on blood sugar control. I will order a fasting blood glucose to assess current blood sugar levels. Continue the patient on metformin twice daily as prescribed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately two months to reassess blood sugar control and adjust treatment as needed. I will also schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to review the fasting blood glucose results.
