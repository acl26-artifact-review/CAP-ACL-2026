Chief Complaint
High blood sugar

History of Present Illness
Hannah reports experiencing high blood sugar for several weeks, particularly exacerbated by holiday sweets and family gatherings. She describes lightheadedness and blurred vision, and initially attributed it to stress. She reports feeling dizzy and seeing spots, prompting her to seek follow-up. She's been taking protonix for reflux and has a history of atrial fibrillation managed with metoprolol. She reports feeling heart racing when anxious. She's been eating a lot of sweets recently and wants to make sure she's nipping this high blood sugar thing in the bud.

Findings
Physical exam reveals an enlarged thyroid, but no tenderness. The patient's heart rate is irregular and rhythm is irregular. Lungs are clear to auscultation. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. No edema in the lower extremities. Dragon shows elevated blood sugar and hemoglobin A1c levels. Dragon orders a complete metabolic panel.

Assessment
The patient's elevated blood sugar and hemoglobin A1c levels indicate a diagnosis of diabetes. Further investigation is needed to determine the type of diabetes and rule out other underlying causes.

Plan
Start metformin 500 mg twice daily. Order a complete metabolic panel. Order a refill on metoprolol 25 mg twice daily. The nurse will provide education on blood glucose monitoring and dietary modifications. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
