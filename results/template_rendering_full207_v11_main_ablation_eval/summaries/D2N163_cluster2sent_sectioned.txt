Chief Complaint
Newly diagnosed hypercholesterolemia.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being surprised by a recent elevated cholesterol finding during an annual checkup. He reports a family history of heart disease and sudden death in early years. He is concerned about the implications of his elevated cholesterol levels.

Findings
The patient's cholesterol level was measured at 210 mg/dL, which is elevated. Physical exam findings include normal blood pressure, clear lung sounds, and no palpable thyroid enlargement. No murmurs or gallops were appreciated on heart exam. No hepatomegaly or splenomegaly were noted on abdominal exam.

Assessment
The patient has been diagnosed with hypercholesterolemia.

Plan
The plan includes ordering a full cholesterol panel, glucose panel, complete metabolic panel, thyroid studies, and liver panels, as well as genetic testing to determine if the condition is familial or secondary. Follow-up will be scheduled in three to six months after meeting with a nutritionist and implementing dietary changes. The patient will continue to engage in regular physical activity, including bike riding. A referral to a nutritionist is arranged.
