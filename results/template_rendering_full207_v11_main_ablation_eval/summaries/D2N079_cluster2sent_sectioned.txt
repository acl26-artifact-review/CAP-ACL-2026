Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent left shoulder pain for approximately six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He recalls no specific injury and has been taking two extra-strength Tylenol tablets every six to eight hours for pain relief. He denies numbness or tingling in the arms or hands. He expresses a desire to establish a man cave and asks about the potential for the pain to resolve.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder, with tenderness to palpation of the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the left arm, and sensation is intact to light touch. Shoulder x-ray demonstrates no fractures.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI of the left shoulder to evaluate for other pathology. I am also referring the patient to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks, and will provide educational materials on the program. If symptoms do not improve, a steroid injection may be considered. The nurse will check the patient and provide educational materials regarding physical therapy. I will schedule a follow-up visit in four weeks to assess progress.
