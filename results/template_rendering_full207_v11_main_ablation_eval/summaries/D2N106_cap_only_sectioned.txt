Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath and difficulty breathing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing increased shortness of breath over the past week, particularly with exertion. He describes a feeling of tightness in his chest and reports difficulty breathing when walking short distances. He denies any recent illness or fever. He reports heavy drinking and reports having allergies. The patient's dog experiences eye watering. He denies lip or throat swelling.

Findings
The patient exhibits mild dyspnea on exertion. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. No wheezing or rhonchi are noted. Examination reveals eye watering.

Assessment
The patient has dyspnea and newly diagnosed asthma. The patient reports allergies.

Plan
Prescribe an albuterol inhaler for rescue use. Order allergy testing. Prescribe singulair ten milligrams. Schedule skin testing next week. ,
