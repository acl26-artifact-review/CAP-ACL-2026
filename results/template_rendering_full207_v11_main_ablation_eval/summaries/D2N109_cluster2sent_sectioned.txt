Chief Complaint
Left ankle pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a left ankle sprain following a slip and fall while walking to her car, involving inversion of the ankle. She experienced significant pain and swelling, which she managed with ibuprofen, ice, and elevation. She denies any bleeding or concerning symptoms. She reports a long spring and recent snow and ice.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral malleolus and fifth metatarsal, mild swelling and redness of the left ankle, and no palpable pulses or sensory deficits. Hemoglobin A1c is 6.8%, blood pressure is 120/70 mmHg, and the patient reports regular blood sugar monitoring. The patient's neck exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient has an ankle sprain with tenderness to palpation and swelling. She also has a history of diabetes and hypertension.

Plan
I am ordering an ankle x-ray to rule out fracture. I will place the patient in an air splint and provide crutches for weight-bearing restriction. I will prescribe naproxen 500 mg twice daily for pain control. I will order a repeat hemoglobin A1c and baseline labs. I will also refill the patient's norvasc prescription and schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess healing and adjust weight-bearing as tolerated. I will also remind the patient to maintain their diabetes management plan and attend their ophthalmology appointment.
