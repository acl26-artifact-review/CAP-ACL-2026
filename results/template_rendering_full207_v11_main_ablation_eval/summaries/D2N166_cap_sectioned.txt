Chief Complaint
Low back pain with radiating pain down the left leg.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports having low back pain for approximately two weeks, which began after lifting a box of books. He describes the pain as sharp and aching, with radiation down his left leg. He tried taking ibuprofen and Tylenol, which provided some relief. He denies any recent trauma. He reports that the pain has been worsening and is now affecting his ability to be as active as he was previously. He has a sedentary job and a history of back pain. His father also has back pain. He does not smoke and played soccer as a midfielder.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 130/70 mmHg, respiratory rate is 18, and heart sounds are regular and without murmurs. Lung exam is clear bilaterally. The musculoskeletal exam reveals tenderness to palpation at the L4-L5 disc space, with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness. A straight leg test is positive for radiating pain down the left leg. Reflexes are symmetrical. MRI findings show a disc desiccation, a disc bulge with paracentral disc herniation resulting in moderate right neuroforaminal stenosis.

Assessment
The patient presents with lumbar back pain at the L4-L5 level due to a herniated disc with radiculopathy. This is causing pain radiating down the left leg.

Plan
A spinal injection with a strong NSAID and corticosteroid is recommended under fluoroscopy. This will help reduce inflammation and provide pain relief. We will schedule this procedure today. The patient will receive paperwork outlining the procedure and potential risks. Please let me know if you have any questions.
