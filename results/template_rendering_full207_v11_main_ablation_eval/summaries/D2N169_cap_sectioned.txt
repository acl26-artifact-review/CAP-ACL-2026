Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath

History of Present Illness
Alan is a 61-year-old male presenting with complaints of shortness of breath. He reports that over the weekend, he took a very long bike ride, approximately three hours, and subsequently experienced lightheadedness, shortness of breath, and a feeling that he was short of breath afterwards. He states this was surprising as he typically bikes every morning and has never experienced this before. He denies any chest pain, but does recall feeling a little chest pain which eventually subsided after about an hour. He has no family history of similar issues. He reports a mild cough approximately a year and a half ago, which resolved. He denies nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. He is currently taking imitrex for migraines, which has been well-controlled by avoiding bright lights. He also reports managing his reflux with protonix. He underwent cataract surgery approximately four and a half months ago and reports good vision since then. He reports a long bike ride on the weekend.

Findings
The patient's oxygenation is normal. Deep breaths reveal no jugular venous distension. Heart rhythm is regular, with no murmurs appreciated. Lung auscultation reveals a slight expiratory wheeze bilaterally. Lower extremity edema is trace. Chest x-ray is normal. Pulmonary function tests are within normal limits. Labs are all within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with an asthma exacerbation triggered by exercise and potentially exacerbated by allergies.

Plan
I am prescribing an albuterol inhaler, two puffs every four hours as needed for wheezing or shortness of breath. I am ordering a pulmonary referral. I would like to see you in a couple of weeks to follow up on this. Please call if you experience any further shortness of breath.
