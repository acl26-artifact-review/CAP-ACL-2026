Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain, describing it as a sharp pain with a severity of 11. He states the pain is present with walking and is exacerbated by skiing. The patient reports a history of a knee injury two weeks ago while skiing, involving a pop sound. He denies any prior knee injuries. The patient reports mild swelling in the right knee. He also reports tenderness to palpation. The patient reports a high pain tolerance. He takes THC gummies. The patient had knee surgery involving reconstructive surgery fourteen years ago. The patient was a good gymnast. The patient had back surgery.

Findings
There is mild swelling in the right knee. The patient reports tenderness to palpation.

Assessment
The patient has right knee pain and right knee swelling. Clinical findings are consistent with a possible strain of the posterior cruciate ligament.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen eight hundred milligrams to be taken twice a day as needed and meloxicam fifteen milligrams to be taken once a day. Order brace. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
