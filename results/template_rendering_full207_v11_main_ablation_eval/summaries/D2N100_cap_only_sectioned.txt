Chief Complaint
Left hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his left hand for the past several weeks. He describes the pain as sharp and intermittent, and rates it as 6/10. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is worse with gripping and lifting objects. He uses Persa-gel daily on his face. He uses Elf facial cleanser in the morning and at night. He reports feeling greasy skin in the morning.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the volar aspect of the left wrist. Range of motion is limited by pain. Grip strength is decreased. No signs of inflammation.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Prescribe a carpal tunnel wrist splint to be worn at night. Recommend physical therapy for hand and wrist strengthening. Continue use of Persa-gel as directed. Follow up in 2 weeks to assess response to treatment.
