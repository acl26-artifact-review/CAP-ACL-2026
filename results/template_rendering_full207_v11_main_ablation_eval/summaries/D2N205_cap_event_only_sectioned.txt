Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient presents with progressive left shoulder pain over six weeks, worse with overhead activity and when lying on the affected side. He denies recent trauma. Acetaminophen provides partial relief. The patient reports nausea and fatigue.

Findings
Examination reveals reduced active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. The clinician observes erythema on the anterior aspect of the patient's chest on the left side. The clinician detects faint crackles in the patient's lungs bilaterally.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy and radiation pneumonitis secondary to recent lung cancer treatment.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs, initiate physical therapy, and continue acetaminophen as needed. Prescribe prednisone and lidocaine. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
