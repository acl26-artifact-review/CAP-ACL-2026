Chief Complaint
Left back pain, primarily reported by the patient.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing left back pain, which is often described as sharp or moving, and sometimes less defined. He indicates the pain is not consistently present, varying in intensity and character. He has also noted increased urine output and reports drinking more water than usual. He denies recent trauma. He has a history of recurrent strep infections, having experienced four in the last five months. He reports that his brother smokes, and while he has told him not to, the patient does not believe his brother is following his advice. He reports that school is going well, and that his appetite has remained unchanged. He has not noticed anyone else being sick at school. The patient reports that he is not recalling any recent tiredness.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no tenderness to palpation in the lumbar region. Range of motion is within normal limits. No rebound tenderness or guarding is present. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are normal. No murmurs are auscultated. Examination of the neck reveals no cervical lymphadenopathy. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient presents with left back pain, potentially related to musculoskeletal etiology, and is complicated by a history of recurrent strep infections. Further investigation is warranted to evaluate for glomerular nephritis.

Plan
I am ordering a CT scan to evaluate the patient's back pain. I am also referring the patient to an ENT specialist to evaluate the recurrent strep infections and consider a tonsillectomy. I am ordering a lipid panel, a UA, a CMP, a CBC, a vitamin D, and a TSH to assess overall health and rule out other potential causes. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to review the results and discuss further management strategies.
