Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports bilateral knee pain, with the right knee being more significantly affected. He describes the pain as having started over the weekend during box moving, with increased pain and stiffness following. He reports equal pain in both knees, but notes that the right knee is more tender to palpation and has decreased range of motion. He previously used Tylenol and icing, but found no relief. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports a history of kidney transplant, hypothyroidism, and arthritis. He is currently taking Synthroid and reports that his kidney transplant medications are stable. He denies fever, chills, or nausea.

Findings
Physical examination reveals edema and erythema in the right knee, with decreased range of motion and tenderness to palpation. The left knee appears less inflamed and has normal range of motion. The heart exam reveals a grade II/VI systolic ejection murmur, which is stable. Vitals are stable. The right knee x-ray demonstrates normal bony alignment without fracture. Lab results show a normal white blood cell count and stable kidney function.

Assessment
The patient's right knee pain is most likely an acute exacerbation of his underlying arthritis, given his history and examination findings. He has a history of kidney transplant, hypothyroidism, and arthritis, which may contribute to his symptoms. No acute findings suggest a new injury or infection.

Plan
Prescribe ultram 50 mg every six hours as needed for pain. Order an autoimmune panel to rule out inflammatory conditions. Advise the patient to take it easy and avoid activities that exacerbate his pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed. Refer to physical therapy for further evaluation and management.
