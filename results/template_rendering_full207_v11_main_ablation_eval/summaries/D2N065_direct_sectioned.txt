Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall last night while taking out the trash, describing a pop sensation and immediate pain. He was able to walk with assistance initially, but now limps. He iced the ankle and took ibuprofen last night, with minimal relief. He denies prior ankle injuries, though he played soccer in high school and remembers similar injuries in teammates. He has no numbness or tingling.

Findings
Physical examination reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus and swelling of the right ankle. Palpation elicits tenderness to the anterior and lateral soft tissue. Range of motion is limited. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the dorsalis pedis. The right ankle x-ray demonstrates no fractures or bony abnormalities. Blood pressure is 120/80 mmHg, heart rate 80 bpm, and oxygen saturation 98% on room air.

Assessment
Right ankle sprain, likely moderate severity based on examination and imaging.

Plan
Keep the leg elevated and continue icing the ankle five times a day for 20 minutes. Provide an air cast and crutches for one to two days. Instruct the patient to stay off the leg as tolerated. Continue ibuprofen as needed for pain. Follow up in two to three weeks, or sooner if symptoms persist or worsen. Consider MRI if symptoms do not improve after one week.
