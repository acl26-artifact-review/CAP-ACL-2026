He states the pain is exacerbated when applying pressure to the medial aspect of the knee. He is currently taking ibuprofen for swelling and reports a pain score of 4-5 out of 10 when mobile. He denies chest pain, belly pain, or shortness of breath. He reports needing help with his diet, specifically reducing sodium intake, and states he hasn't made significant changes since the pandemic.

O:
Physical exam reveals edema and effusion around the right knee. Palpation of the medial aspect of the right knee elicits tenderness. Knee flexion and extension are painful. The heart exam reveals a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur. Blood pressure is 150/70. Lungs are clear bilaterally with no wheezes or crackles. X-ray of the right knee shows no fractures or bony abnormalities.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a medial collateral ligament strain. His history of hypertension is relevant to his current management.

P:
Rescribe ibuprofen 800 mg twice daily for pain and swelling. Order a knee brace for support. Refer to nutrition for dietary counseling. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress. Advise the patient to elevate his leg and avoid activity if possible.
