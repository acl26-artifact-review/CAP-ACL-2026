Chief Complaint
Reports left back pain, primarily on the left side, which prompted a visit to evaluate the symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing back pain, primarily on the left side, for several days. The pain is described as varying in intensity, sometimes sharp, sometimes not, and occasionally moves. It is often present but not always. He reports that the pain is not consistently present and sometimes moves. He has not noticed any changes in his appetite or water consumption, though he has been drinking more water recently and experiencing increased nighttime urination. He denies recent trauma. The patient reports that school is going well and that his grades are good. He has not noticed any illness among his classmates or siblings. He reports a history of recurrent strep infections, having experienced four in the last five months, as diagnosed by his previous physician. He states that he follows his doctor's instructions regarding treatment for these infections.

Findings
Physical examination reveals no tenderness to palpation of the back, no rebound tenderness, and no guarding. Lung sounds are clear to auscultation bilaterally. Heart sounds are normal, with no murmurs or rubs. Neurological examination is unremarkable. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is concerning for possible glomerular nephritis, potentially related to recurrent strep infections. Further investigation is warranted to evaluate the extent of the renal involvement and rule out other potential causes.

Plan
I am ordering a comprehensive laboratory panel, including a lipid panel, urinalysis, comprehensive metabolic panel, CBC, vitamin D, and TSH, to assess renal function and iron levels. I am also referring the patient to an otolaryngology specialist for evaluation and consideration of tonsillectomy, given the history of recurrent strep infections. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to review the laboratory results and discuss further management strategies. The patient should contact the office if any new symptoms develop or if there is any change in his condition.
