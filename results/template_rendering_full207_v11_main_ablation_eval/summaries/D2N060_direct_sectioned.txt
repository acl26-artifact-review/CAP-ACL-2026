Chief Complaint
Left foot pain following a soccer injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left foot pain following a fall during a soccer game, resulting from entanglement with another player. He describes twisting his body and inadvertently landing on his foot. Since the fall, he has been elevating, icing, and taking ibuprofen. His pain level is 7-8 with ibuprofen and 3 without. He has no prior left foot injuries. He plays soccer since childhood and has played throughout high school.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bruising and swelling on the left foot. Tenderness to palpation is present at the midfoot. The piano key test is positive. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds on the dorsalis pedis. Motor and sensory function are intact. Radiographic review reveals a subtle dorsal displacement of the base of the second metatarsal with a 3 millimeter separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and the presence of a bony fragment in the lisfranc joint space.

Assessment
Lisfranc fracture with ligament injury.

Plan
An MRI is ordered to assess ligament injury. Surgical intervention is recommended to restore the bones in the lisfranc joint space and stabilize with plates and screws. The patient will be in a cast and will require crutches for six to eight weeks. Follow-up will be scheduled in the clinic in four weeks, depending on tolerance. The patient will be out of the season.
