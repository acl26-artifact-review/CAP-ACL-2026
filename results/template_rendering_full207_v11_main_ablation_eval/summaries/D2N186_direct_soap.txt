S:
The patient reports a history of lower left back pain following a microdiscectomy in November 2020 due to disk herniation and spinal thecal sac involvement. He describes nerve pain, rated as horrible, down his leg with the original surgery. He reports that stretching helps with the pain. He experiences pain with prolonged bending over and with physical activities. The pain is intermittent and random, sometimes occurring on the right side as well. He denies pain with flexion, but reports pain with extension and weightbearing. He reports no current leg pain. He denies any numbness or tingling. He reports no pain with palpation on the right side.

O:
Physical exam reveals normal dorsiflexion. No fractures are identified on the spinal bone via x-ray. Range of motion is within normal limits. Pain is elicited with palpation on the left side at L5. Blood pressure is mildly low. Heart rate is within normal limits. Neurological exam is nonfocal.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a muscle strain secondary to spinal misalignment following previous surgery. There is no evidence of acute instability or fracture.

P:
Rescribe physical therapy referral to the VA physical therapy department. Prescribe meloxicam 15mg twice daily for pain management. Refer to pain management for further evaluation and consideration of non-narcotic pain strategies.
