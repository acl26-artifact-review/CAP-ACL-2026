Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following surgery.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 44-year-old female presenting six weeks post-right medial unicompartmental arthroplasty. She reports no current problems, but continues to experience numbness in the area of the incision, which is expected to resolve as nerves heal. She expresses satisfaction with the surgery and reports no pain. She describes a gait that is antalgic with a slight limp and decreased velocity, and is not using an assistive device. The incision site appears well-healed.

Findings
Right knee focused exam revealed a healed surgical scar, mild swelling, and tenderness. Range of motion is 0 to 120 degrees. Radiographs demonstrate stable implant placement.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with post-operative right knee status following unicompartmental knee replacement.

Plan
Continue monitoring for any signs of loosening or early complications. Schedule follow-up in four weeks.
