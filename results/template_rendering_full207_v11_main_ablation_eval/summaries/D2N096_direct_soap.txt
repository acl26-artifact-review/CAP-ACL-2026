S:
The patient reports left shoulder pain for one week, exacerbated by specific movements following a gymnastics incident. He denies taking pain medications, previously using Tylenol for a few days without relief. He reports a past left arm surgery. He states he can not lift heavy weights or carry anything heavy for the next three to four weeks.

O:
The physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation with movement of the left shoulder in all ranges of motion. The x-ray shows no fracture or acute bony injury.

A:
The patient's left shoulder pain is likely due to musculoskeletal injury, possibly related to overuse or a specific movement pattern. The past surgery is not considered relevant to the current presentation.

P:
Rescribe ibuprofen 600 mg three times daily for one week. Advise ice or heat application as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to assess improvement or if pain persists. Advise patient to avoid heavy lifting or strenuous activity for at least three to four weeks. Consider repeat x-ray if symptoms do not improve after three weeks.
