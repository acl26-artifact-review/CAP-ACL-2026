Chief Complaint
Neck pain following multiple car accidents.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports neck pain stemming from multiple car accidents, beginning after the second crash and exacerbated by the third. He underwent physical therapy following the second accident and received surgery after the third. He describes the current pain as sharp and incapacitating, impacting his ability to work. He reports experiencing headaches, dizziness, and visual disturbances, and notes some numbness. He has been taking fentanyl prior to this visit. He has observed bruising and swelling in the neck area.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate range of motion in the neck, with increased pain upon forward flexion and extension. Palpation of the bony process and muscle reveals tenderness. No bruising or swelling is currently noted. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with a cervical sprain.

Plan
We will order an MRI to evaluate the extent of the injury. We will initiate treatment with robaxin, 1500 mg every 6-8 hours, and will monitor for effectiveness. We will provide ice packs for the affected area. We will refer the patient to physical therapy for rehabilitation and explore local injections if needed. We will continue to monitor the patient's pain levels and adjust medication as necessary. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
