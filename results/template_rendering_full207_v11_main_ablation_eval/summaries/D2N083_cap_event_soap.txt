S:
The patient reports elbow pain for approximately four days, primarily in the inside of his elbow, and describes it as a six on a pain scale. He states the pain keeps him from lifting and doing activities. He has tried ibuprofen with minimal relief.

O:
Physical exam reveals moderate tenderness to palpation of the medial epicondyle. Wrist pronation elicits pain, and wrist extension with resistance elicits pain. Vitals are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with medial epicondylitis.

P:
The doctor orders a sling to be worn at all times, instructs the patient to apply ice to the elbow three times daily, and prescribes ibuprofen 600 mg q6h with food for one week. The doctor recommends holding off on pottery work for two weeks and schedules a follow-up appointment in one week.
