Chief Complaint
The patient reports frequent infections and a recent diagnosis of type 2 diabetes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports frequent infections, particularly respiratory and abdominal infections, and a history of prolonged cold symptoms. He notes a family history of diabetes and reports being started on metformin by his primary care physician. He describes a tendency for his colds to linger. The patient reports no current diarrhea.

Findings
The patient's weight and vital signs are within normal limits. Physical examination reveals no fever or lymphadenopathy. Laboratory results confirm an IgA deficiency. The patient's abdominal exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is an IgA deficiency, contributing to recurrent infections and a recent diagnosis of type 2 diabetes.

Plan
Order additional blood work to evaluate other antibody levels. Initiate follow-up in four weeks to assess response to metformin and monitor for persistent infections or significant changes in abdominal symptoms. Advise the patient to inform healthcare providers of his IgA deficiency prior to receiving blood products.
