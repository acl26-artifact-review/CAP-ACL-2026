Chief Complaint
Persistent resistant hypertension.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a longstanding history of hypertension, initially diagnosed in his 40s and now classified as resistant hypertension. He states that despite multiple medications - including amlodipine, lisinopril, and hydrochlorothiazide - his blood pressure remains elevated. He reports experiencing symptoms consistent with resistant hypertension, including headaches and episodes of dizziness. He denies any recent changes in medication or lifestyle. His family history is significant for hypertension in multiple relatives. The patient reports secondary hypertension.

Findings
Blood pressure readings today are 162/98 mmHg in the left arm and 158/92 mmHg in the right arm. Physical exam reveals no edema or jugular venous distension. Neurological exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient presents with resistant hypertension, despite current medical therapy. Further investigation is warranted to determine the underlying cause and guide management.

Plan
A metabolic panel will be ordered to evaluate electrolytes and renal function. The doctor will consider renal denervation. Spironolactone will be added to Mr. Roberts' regimen. The doctor will prescribe spironolactone. A blood pressure check will be performed in two weeks. The patient will be referred to the resistant hypertension clinic.
