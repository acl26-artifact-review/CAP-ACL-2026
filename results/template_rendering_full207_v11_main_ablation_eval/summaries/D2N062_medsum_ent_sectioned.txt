Chief Complaint
Difficulty swallowing over a period of time, accompanied by chest pain when swallowing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty swallowing for several weeks, particularly with certain foods. He also experiences pain when swallowing, primarily in his chest. Over the past couple of months, he's been drinking more and eating foods like pizza and burgers, acknowledging this isn't ideal. He's recently moved from the West Coast to the East Coast, describing the move as stressful. He mentions his dog's travel arrangements are also a source of stress, fearing the impact on his pet during air travel and expressing concern about potential issues with cargo holds. He has also reported a weight gain of approximately ten pounds over the last month. He has not noticed any blood in his stool or dark, tarry stools. He occasionally experiences abdominal pain.

Findings
Blood pressure is 130/77 mmHg. Heart rate is 72 bpm. Oxygen saturation is 100%. Respiratory rate is 19 breaths per minute. Physical exam reveals no obvious ulcers or thrush in the mouth. Tonsils are midline. The neck is unremarkable, with no palpable lymphadenopathy. The abdomen is non-distended, with active bowel sounds. Palpation of the epigastric area elicits tenderness. Lung auscultation is clear bilaterally. No rash or lesions are observed. Eyes are equal and reactive to light. An upper endoscopy is scheduled for follow-up.

Assessment
Acute esophagitis is the primary diagnosis, likely secondary to esophageal irritation and inflammation.

Plan
Prescribe protonix (40 mg daily) and carafate (1 gram four times daily for one month). Schedule an upper endoscopy for next week to further evaluate the esophagus. Encourage the patient to change his diet, decreasing alcohol and caffeine intake, particularly after the move. Advise him to eat slowly and chew food thoroughly. Advise him to avoid citrus foods, fruits, and spicy foods until symptoms improve. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week.
