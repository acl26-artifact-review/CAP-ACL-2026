Chief Complaint
Persistent asthma symptoms and difficulty managing them.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a long-standing history of asthma, characterized by intermittent symptoms, particularly exacerbated by colds and exposure to cat dander. He describes frequent coughing and wheezing, with symptoms occurring almost daily and requiring frequent use of his rescue inhaler (Ventolin). He has experienced multiple urgent care visits and emergency department visits over the past six months, often requiring antibiotics or steroids. He specifically notes a flare-up after visiting his aunt, attributed to her cat. He uses two inhalers: a blue inhaler (Ventolin) which he believes provides relief, and another inhaler which he finds ineffective. He has been hospitalized on several occasions, typically requiring prednisone taper packs. He enjoys outdoor activities like hiking and is eager to resume these pursuits.

Findings
Physical examination revealed normal vital signs, including blood pressure of 128/82 mmHg, respiratory rate of 16 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 99% on room air. Heart sounds were regular and without murmurs. Lung sounds demonstrated diminished breath sounds with occasional expiratory wheeze bilaterally. Neck examination was normal. Pulses were strong and bilaterally equal. Capillary refill was brisk. Pulmonary function test results were consistent with asthma.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and pulmonary function test results are consistent with moderate, intermittent asthma.

Plan
I am initiating a regimen of Flovent 100 mcg, one puff twice daily to provide long-term asthma control. Continue using Ventolin as needed, up to two puffs every four to six hours. I am also creating an asthma action plan to help guide management based on symptom severity. Follow up in one month or sooner if symptoms worsen.
