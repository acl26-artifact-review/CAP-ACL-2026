Chief Complaint
The patient reports an injury to their knee sustained during skiing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that they sustained a knee injury while skiing in Vermont last weekend, resulting in a fall and subsequent pain. They were unable to bear weight immediately after the injury and were taken down by ski patrol. The patient reports that the pain is most pronounced on the outside of the knee and that they experience a feeling of instability. They report difficulty bearing weight and that the pain worsens with flexion and extension. The patient denies numbness and reports that ibuprofen provides some relief. The patient reports having twins and is an active mom who enjoys running, though currently is not very active.

Findings
Examination reveals edema and effusion over the right knee. Palpation elicits pain. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Lachman's test is positive, indicating ACL instability. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable. The patient's heart sounds are regular and rhythmic. Lungs are clear to auscultation. The patient reports no leg edema.

Assessment
The patient presents with a likely ACL rupture, evidenced by the positive Lachman's test and reported instability. The examination findings support a significant knee injury.

Plan
The doctor orders a knee MRI to confirm the suspected ACL rupture and evaluate for other potential injuries. The doctor recommends a brace to provide support and stability to the knee. The doctor prescribes ibuprofen at a dose of 600 to 800 milligrams every eight hours as needed. The doctor plans to follow up in approximately two days to discuss the MRI results and formulate a long-term management plan.
