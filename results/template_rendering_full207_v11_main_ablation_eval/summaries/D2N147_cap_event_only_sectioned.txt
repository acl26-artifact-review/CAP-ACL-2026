Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left shoulder, describing it as a dull ache that has been present for approximately six weeks. He states the pain is exacerbated by overhead activities and when lying on his affected side. The patient denies any recent trauma or injury. He reports using acetaminophen for partial relief of the pain. He also reports experiencing a headache and denies fever.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. Vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs to evaluate for rotator cuff pathology. Initiate physical therapy to address the shoulder pain and improve range of motion. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
