Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right ankle pain following a fall yesterday while taking out the trash. He initially reported a popping sensation and inability to walk on the ankle. Today, he can tolerate a small amount of weight-bearing but is still limping. He iced and elevated the ankle last night and took ibuprofen. His pain is rated as 4/10 without medication and 1/10 with ibuprofen. He has no prior ankle injuries but has a history of playing soccer and inner-marrow soccer. He has not participated in sports since the injury.

Findings
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus and edema of the right ankle. Palpation elicits tenderness to the anterior and lateral soft tissue. Range of motion is limited. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong and intact. Capillary refill is brisk. Neurological exam is intact. X-ray shows no fracture.

Assessment
Right ankle sprain, likely involving the anterior talofibular ligament.

Plan
Prescribe an air cast to stabilize the ankle. Recommend ice application and elevation. Order crutches for weight-bearing. Prescribe ibuprofen for pain management. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 14 days to assess healing progress.
