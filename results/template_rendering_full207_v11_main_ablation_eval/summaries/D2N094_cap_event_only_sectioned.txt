Chief Complaint
Right hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his right hand that began approximately two weeks ago following a fall. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, exacerbated by gripping and lifting objects. He denies any recent trauma beyond the fall. He reports mild constipation and currently takes miralax daily.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the dorsum of the right hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No swelling or erythema is noted. Vital signs are stable.

Assessment
Right hand sprain, likely involving the wrist and metacarpal bones.

Plan
Order X-rays of the right hand to rule out fracture. Recommend a splint for support and immobilization. Advise the patient to avoid heavy lifting and gripping. Follow up in two weeks to assess healing and adjust treatment as needed.
