Chief Complaint
Sudden visual distortions and blurring of vision.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sudden visual distortions and blurring of vision, which began approximately one week ago. He describes difficulty reading and seeing straight lines, noting that door frames appear crooked. He reports occasional difficulty adjusting to dim lighting, particularly in restaurants, and mentions a fondness for McDonald's parmas. He reports smoking a pack of cigarettes daily and occasionally attempts to quit. He sporadically monitors his blood pressure and reports difficulty remembering to do so. He denies recent trauma. The technician noted a decrease in vision when reading and straight lines, and door frames appearing crooked.

Findings
The anterior segment examination reveals posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes. The cornea is clear. Cup to disc ratio is 0.2 in the right eye and 0.3 in the left eye. Fundus examination reveals drusen in the macula, retinal pigment epithelium thickening, subretinal fluid and exudates accumulation, and hemorrhage in the left eye. The right eye shows a drusen deposit in the retinal pigment epithelium, subretinal fluid accumulation, and a choroidal neovascular membrane visualized via fluorescein angiography with focal areas of leakage.

Assessment
The diagnostic tests and eye exam are indicative of neovascular age-related macular degeneration, advanced to a stage that usually leads to more severe vision loss.

Plan
Administer monthly injections of Lucentis into the vitreous portion of the eye after numbing, scheduled to be repeated monthly. Arrange follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Continue to monitor blood pressure regularly and encourage adherence to medication.
