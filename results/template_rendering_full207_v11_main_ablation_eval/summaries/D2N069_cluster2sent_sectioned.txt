Chief Complaint
Right knee pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 43-year-old female presenting with right knee pain. She reports applying ice and taking ibuprofen, but finds little relief. She also reports a history of atrial fibrillation and is currently taking digoxin. She has had a previous rhinoplasty and denies any prior surgeries other than the nose job. She describes tenderness primarily when valgus stressing her leg and reports some calf tenderness. The patient reports a recommendation for a knee brace and temporary use of crutches.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness over the medial portion of the right knee, specifically over the medial meniscus area. Sensation is intact. Radiographs demonstrate no fractures or dislocations, but reveal some fluid accumulation.

Assessment
The patient likely has an acute medial meniscus sprain or strain.

Plan
Order a follow-up examination in approximately one week to assess improvement. If symptoms do not improve, an MRI will be obtained. Recommend a knee brace and temporary use of crutches. Prescribe 800 milligrams of ibuprofen.
