Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a trampoline injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports injuring his right knee four days ago while jumping on a child's trampoline. He describes a popping sensation at the time of injury followed by significant swelling. He attempted to manage the pain with ibuprofen and ice. He reports difficulty with weight-bearing and limited range of motion, particularly with flexion and extension. He admits to struggling to consistently monitor his diet and occasionally enjoying sugary foods like ice cream. He reports that his diabetes management has been 'okay' but he sometimes forgets to check his sugars. The patient reports that his primary care physician is treating his diabetes.

Findings
Physical examination reveals significant swelling in the right knee. Palpation elicits tenderness to the medial aspect of the patella. Range of motion is limited with pain on flexion and extension. The McMurray's test is negative. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are normal with no murmurs. There is no evidence of effusion.

Assessment
The patient presents with a likely diagnosis of a torn or injured medial collateral ligament (MCL) of the right knee, based on the mechanism of injury, reported pop, and associated swelling and pain.

Plan
I am placing the patient in a straight leg brace. I am prescribing a course of oral mobic for pain relief and to help reduce swelling. I am ordering an MRI to evaluate the extent of the injury. I will also order a hemoglobin A1c to assess his diabetes control. I will refill his metformin prescription to maintain his current dosage. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess his progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.
