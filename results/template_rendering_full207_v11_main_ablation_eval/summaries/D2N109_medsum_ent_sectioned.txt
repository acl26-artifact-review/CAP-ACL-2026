Chief Complaint
Ankle pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing ankle pain and swelling following a slip and fall two days prior, where she felt her ankle 'turn underneath' her. She describes the pain as being primarily on the outside of her ankle and reports difficulty walking. She has attempted to manage the pain and swelling with ibuprofen and ice elevation, which provided some relief. She has a history of diabetes and hypertension, well-managed with metformin and norvasc, respectively. Her blood sugars have been stable, and her hemoglobin A1c was recently 6.8%. She is due for an ophthalmology checkup in the coming weeks.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral malleolus and fifth metatarsal. There is some swelling and redness observed. Sensation and pulses are intact bilaterally. The patient's neck exam is unremarkable, with no bruits and clear lung sounds. A two over six systolic ejection murmur is present but unchanged from previous examinations. Blood pressure is 120/70 mmHg.

Assessment
The patient likely has an ankle sprain, with possible fracture concern. Her diabetes and hypertension are well-controlled.

Plan
I have ordered an x-ray of the ankle to evaluate for fracture. I am placing the patient in an air splint and providing crutches to limit weightbearing. We will continue naprosyn 500mg twice daily for pain control. I will also order a repeat hemoglobin A1c to monitor diabetes control. Please schedule an ophthalmology checkup in the next couple of weeks. Follow up in one week if symptoms do not improve, or if there is any concern regarding fracture.
