Chief Complaint
Abdominal pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports abdominal pain for one week, with increasing severity. She attributes this to a longstanding history of diabetes and recent increased coffee consumption. She describes experiencing intermittent nausea and has vomited twice this week. The pain is located on both sides of her upper abdomen and worsens with exercise, such as jogging. She reports that the pain increases when she works out. She denies fever. She has been eating a lot of spicy food lately. The patient reports drinking too much coffee lately.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the epigastrium. The patient's heart exam is notable for a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, which is unchanged from her previous exam. Lung exam reveals mild end-expiratory wheezes. Otherwise, the physical exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with acute gastritis, likely exacerbated by caffeine intake and possibly spicy foods. Her underlying diabetes and asthma remain well-controlled.

Plan
I am prescribing zantac for symptomatic relief. I will increase the metformin dosage to 1000 mg in the morning and 500 mg in the evening. I recommend cutting back on spicy foods and caffeine. I will order a urinalysis, pregnancy test, and CBC with CMP to rule out other causes. Please follow up with me in four weeks, and let me know if you have any questions or concerns.
