S:
The patient reports constant pain in his left shoulder and elbow for approximately five months, following a fall five months ago while going up stairs. He describes the pain as constant and often severe, particularly when lifting weights. He has reduced his gym routine to two days a week, limiting weights to 15-20 pounds due to pain. He reports stopping exercise and icing the area has provided some relief. He denies numbness or tingling. He continues to walk approximately 30 minutes daily with his wife since retiring last year. He has not taken any pain medication.

O:
Left shoulder and elbow are tender to palpation. Positive Hawkins-Kennedy and Neer's tests. Proximal and distal upper extremity strength is normal. Intact median, radial, and ulnar sensation and abduction to 90 degrees. Normal empty tension test.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with impingement syndrome of the left shoulder, likely secondary to the prior fall. There is no evidence of acute wrist injury.

P:
Order a referral to Dr. Martha Rivera for physical therapy, two sessions per week. Recommend initiating physical therapy and icing the left shoulder as needed. Follow up if symptoms worsen or do not improve with physical therapy or if the patient experiences any new symptoms.
