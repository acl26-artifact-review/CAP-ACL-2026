Chief Complaint
Right knee numbness and post-operative follow-up.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports six weeks post-right medial unicompartmental arthroplasty. She experiences persistent numbness on the right side, which she attributes to nerve healing. She reports no current pain related to the surgery and is satisfied with her recovery progress. She also reports occasional aches and pains, typically lasting three months. The patient works as an ups driver and is currently unable to perform the job due to lifting restrictions. She is seeking a note to excuse her from work for another six weeks. She is not using an assistive device.

Findings
Examination reveals a healed surgical scar, mild swelling, and tenderness to palpation over the incision site. Range of motion is 0 to 120 degrees. Radiographs demonstrate stable implant placement with no loosening or early complications. Gait is antalgic with a slight limp and decreased velocity. The patient is not using an assistive device.

Assessment
The patient's primary concern is persistent right knee numbness following unicompartmental arthroplasty. There is no evidence of implant loosening or acute complications.

Plan
Schedule a telemedicine follow-up visit in six weeks to assess ongoing numbness and functional status. Provide a note to the patient's employer excusing her from work for another six weeks, pending further evaluation. Advise the patient to continue physical therapy as prescribed. Arrange follow-up in six weeks to discuss job restrictions and return-to-work considerations. Order reflex time testing at the follow-up visit.
