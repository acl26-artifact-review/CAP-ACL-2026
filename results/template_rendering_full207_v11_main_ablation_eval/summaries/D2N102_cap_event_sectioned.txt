Chief Complaint
Hematuria

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of hematuria, initially observed six weeks ago, with intermittent episodes of bright red urine, resembling 'cherry kool-aid.' He describes these episodes as occurring after strenuous activity and accompanied by significant pain, rated as an 8 on a 0-10 scale. He reports passing two small stones approximately one week prior to this appointment. Over the past six weeks, he has experienced frequent urination and associated discomfort. He currently takes Alavert 10mg daily and reports no drug allergies. He admits to drinking more Mountain Dew than water.

Findings
Renal ultrasound revealed a 10 cm x 5 cm right kidney and a 10.8 cm x 5 cm left kidney. A 6 mm left renal stone was identified, non-obstructing. Pre-void volume was 533 ml, and post-void volume was 0 ml. The exam revealed no flank pain or tenderness to palpation.

Assessment
The patient's hematuria is attributed to a left renal stone, which is currently non-obstructive. No surgical intervention is recommended at this time.

Plan
The doctor recommends limiting animal protein intake and avoiding food rich in sodium oxalate. The patient should consume less than 1000 mg of sodium daily and reduce the amount of beef, poultry, fish, and pork. A handout detailing dietary recommendations has been provided.
