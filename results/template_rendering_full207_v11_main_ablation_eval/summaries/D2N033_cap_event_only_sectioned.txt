Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right knee pain for the past three months, worsening with activity. He denies a specific injury. He describes the pain as a deep ache, with occasional sharp twinges. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with limited relief. He rates the pain as a 6/10 at rest and 8/10 with exertion.

Findings
The patient demonstrates mild right knee effusion. Range of motion is limited by pain. McMurray's test is negative. Patellar grind test is positive.

Assessment
Right knee osteoarthritis

Plan
Prescribe naproxen 500mg twice daily. Recommend physical therapy referral for strengthening and range-of-motion exercises. Schedule follow-up in 4 weeks to assess response to treatment.
