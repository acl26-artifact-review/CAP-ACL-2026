Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right knee pain for the past three months, initially following a twisting injury during recreational soccer. He denies any further trauma. He describes the pain as aching and intermittent, with some improvement with rest and ice. He reports difficulty with stairs and prolonged walking. He denies any fever or warmth. He currently takes ibuprofen as needed for pain relief.

Findings
The right knee demonstrates mild tenderness to palpation over the medial joint line. Range of motion is limited by pain, particularly with flexion. Lachman's test is negative. McMurray's test is negative. No effusion is palpable. Vitals are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a medial meniscus injury.

Plan
Order MRI of the right knee to evaluate for meniscal tears. Recommend physical therapy to improve range of motion and strength. Advise the patient to avoid activities that exacerbate the pain. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to review imaging results and assess progress.
