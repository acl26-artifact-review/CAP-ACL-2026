Chief Complaint
Difficulty urinating

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty urinating, which began approximately six months ago and has progressively worsened. He describes a weak urinary stream and sometimes feels like he doesn't fully empty his bladder, although this isn't always the case. He reports waking up three to four times per night to urinate. He denies any burning during urination. He reports occasional diarrhea last week. He has no history of urinary retention or catheterization. He was diagnosed with coronary artery disease and stent placement in 2018 and is currently taking lipitor, aspirin, and metoprolol. He has no chest pain or shortness of breath. He is a former college football player and enjoys watching games, particularly the Georgia Bulldogs.

Findings
Physical exam reveals an enlarged prostate. A slight systolic ejection murmur is heard at the left base. Lung exam is clear bilaterally. The abdomen is non-tender and non-distended. Hemoglobin A1c will be ordered to assess diabetes control.

Assessment
The patient presents with difficulty urinating, likely related to prostate enlargement. Further evaluation is needed to determine the underlying cause and guide treatment.

Plan
I am ordering routine labs including a PSA to assess for prostate cancer, and a urinalysis and urine culture. I am also prescribing flomax 0.4 mg once daily at night, as it can cause dizziness. I will refer the patient to a urologist for further evaluation and management. I will order an echocardiogram to follow up on his heart murmur. I will continue the patient on lipitor 40 mg daily, aspirin, and metoprolol. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in three to four weeks to assess his progress.
