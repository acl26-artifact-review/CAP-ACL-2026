Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports progressive left shoulder pain over the past six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies any recent trauma. He reports that acetaminophen provides some relief. He also reports that he previously wore compression socks.

Findings
Physical exam reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. The patient reports previously wearing compression socks.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
I will order shoulder radiographs to evaluate for rotator cuff pathology. I will also initiate physical therapy to address the patient's shoulder pain and weakness. The doctor will check the patient's CMP levels every couple of months. The patient should prop their feet up.
