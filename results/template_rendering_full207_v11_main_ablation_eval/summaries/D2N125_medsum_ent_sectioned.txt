Chief Complaint
Facial pain

History of Present Illness
The patient presents with facial pain, described as stabbing and shooting, for approximately two to three months. The pain is primarily located in the right cheekbone area and sometimes becomes super hot. The patient reports that the pain started slowly and has been worsening. He has tried ibuprofen and Motrin, but they have not provided relief. He also tried rubbing tiger balm, but it did not help. The patient reports no sensory loss, but mentions that certain temperatures and cold weather seem to trigger the pain. He has no history of multiple sclerosis or brain tumors. He occasionally experiences headaches, but they are not migraines and he takes Tylenol when needed. The patient's family doctor recommended seeing a neurosurgeon.

Findings
Physical exam revealed normal facial symmetry and equal smile bilaterally. The patient reported no weakness in his facial muscles. Cotton tape testing revealed numbness on the right side of the face and a sharp sensation on the left side. An MRI revealed a small blood vessel abutting and pinching the trigeminal nerve.

Assessment
Based on the patient's reported symptoms, physical exam findings, and MRI results, the patient likely has trigeminal neuralgia.

Plan
I recommend starting with Tegretol to manage the pain. I will also order genetic testing to assess for potential side effects, particularly dermatologic reactions. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to assess the effectiveness of the medication and continue to monitor the patient's symptoms. If the pain persists despite medication, we will discuss surgical options to decompress the nerve. ,
