Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports injuring his right knee approximately two and a half weeks ago after stepping off a curb. He states he 'sorta turned over his ankle' and thought his ankle was the thing that would hurt most. However, after the ankle soreness subsided, he noticed pain in the outside of his knee. He describes a 'clicking' sensation when walking, particularly when squatting deeply, and reports feeling 'locked' when standing on one foot for an extended period. He has been taking ibuprofen, but finds it only provides limited relief. He also reports numbness in his right foot, dependent on the shoes he wears. The patient has a history of three toe injuries over the past ten years. He rides a bike for exercise and enjoys music-themed rides.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild edema in the right knee with a slight effusion. Palpation of the lateral aspect of the knee elicits no pain. Range of motion is full, with a negative Lachman's test. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable. The patient demonstrates a positive varus test.

Assessment
Based on the clinical presentation and examination findings, the patient likely has a lateral collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily. I am referring you to physical therapy to strengthen the surrounding muscles and prevent future injuries. Please continue icing the knee as needed, particularly during the day. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist or worsen.
