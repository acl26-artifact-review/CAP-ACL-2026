S:
The patient reports feeling depressed and experiencing severe pain and bleeding related to her newly diagnosed ovarian cancer. She also reports constipation and weight loss. She denies vomiting. She takes oral hormone replacement therapy and has a history of endometriosis. She reports no family history of gynecological cancers.

O:
Physical exam reveals a three centimeter left ovarian mass with associated localized lymph node involvement. There is slight tenderness to palpation of the left lower quadrant. The vaginal exam shows no external lesions, a pink cervix without lesions, and a left adnexal mass. Abdominal CT shows no evidence of gross peritoneal or metastatic disease.

A:
The patient is most likely diagnosed with stage three ovarian cancer based on the CT scan and lymph node involvement. Genetic counseling and testing are recommended to assess for a genetic predisposition to ovarian cancer.

P:
I order blood tests including CA-125, HCG, AFP, and LDH. I recommend genetic counseling and testing. I order a hysterectomy with bilateral oophorectomy and lymph node dissection. I recommend adjuvant chemotherapy with cisplatin and taxol, and consider intraperitoneal chemotherapy if appropriate based on surgical findings. I schedule a pathology review of all specimens.
