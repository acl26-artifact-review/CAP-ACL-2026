S:
The patient reports a history of injury to his right knee following a fall on a trampoline a week ago, describing a 'pop' and immediate pain. He states the pain is localized to the lateral aspect of the knee. He reports using Tylenol for the pain and elevating the knee. He admits to not regularly exercising but enjoys recreational trampoline park visits with his wife. He describes a slight limp when walking. The patient reports that he does not like to come to the doctors.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild ecchymosis and edema on the lateral aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Gait is slightly altered with a limp. Blood pressure is slightly low. Heart rate is regular and within normal limits. Lungs are clear bilaterally. No murmurs are auscultated.

A:
The patient's right knee injury is likely a lateral ligament strain. He has hypertension and type 2 diabetes. Further investigation is warranted to assess the extent of the injury and optimize management of his chronic conditions.

P:
I am ordering an X-ray of the right knee to rule out fracture. I am prescribing meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. I am referring the patient to physical therapy for rehabilitation. I am ordering an echo to assess his hypertension. I am ordering an A1C to assess his diabetes control. I am ordering a lipid panel.
