Chief Complaint
Right finger pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right finger pain following a skiing injury over the weekend, sustained while skiing moguls. He describes getting his strap caught on his finger, resulting in a bend of the finger. He has been applying ice and taking ibuprofen, but the pain persists. He reports constipation recently and takes miralax.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness over the distal phalanx of the right index finger and the distal interphalangeal joint. Range of motion is limited with flexion. Radiographs demonstrate no fractures.

Assessment
Right hand index finger contusion.

Plan
Prescribe an aluminum foam splint and order ibuprofen 600 milligrams every six hours for one week. Schedule follow-up if symptoms do not improve.
