Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately three weeks, initially attributed to basement renovation work. He denies a specific injury but recalls strenuous activity. He has been taking two extra-strength acetaminophen tablets every six to eight hours, which provides some relief. He reports difficulty reaching overhead and pain when applying pressure to the affected area, particularly when lying on his shoulder.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder, with associated pain. Tenderness is noted over the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the left hand. Sensation is intact to light touch. Shoulder x-ray demonstrates no fractures.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
An MRI of the left shoulder is ordered to evaluate for additional pathology. Referral to physical therapy is arranged for approximately six to eight weeks. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms do not improve, or consider a corticosteroid injection if symptoms persist.
