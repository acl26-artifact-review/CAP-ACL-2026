S:
The patient reports a dull headache starting approximately one week ago, accompanied by flushing in the ears and occasional dizziness. He describes the pain as localized to the back of his head and notes it worsens in the evening. He has taken Tylenol and Advil, but the headaches persist. He reports feeling stressed and has not been exercising regularly.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation of the cervical and trapezius muscles. Extraocular movements are intact. Heart sounds are normal with a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur. Blood pressure is slightly low. Neurological exam is unremarkable.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a tension-type headache, likely exacerbated by stress and musculoskeletal tension.

P:
Rescribe flexeril 5 mg three times daily for muscle relaxation. Order a hemoglobin A1c and CBC/chem-12 to assess diabetes control. Continue metformin 500 mg twice daily. Advise the patient to monitor blood sugars daily and to increase physical activity. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to assess response to treatment and address any worsening symptoms.
