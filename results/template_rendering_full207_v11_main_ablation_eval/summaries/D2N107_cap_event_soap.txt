The patient reports back pain following raking leaves, with pins and needles in his right foot. He describes the pain as tightening and exacerbated by sitting and standing, but relieved by a hot shower. He reports no prior history of this type of pain, except for a previous episode three years ago. He denies taking any medications for the pain and reports his husband's pain tolerance is high. O:
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the L5 vertebra. Dorsiflexion is normal. Pulses are equal bilaterally. Neurologic exam shows tingling sensation in the right leg. X-ray of the low back is unremarkable.

A:
The patient is likely experiencing a low back sprain.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam. Refer to physical therapy. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess improvement or consider an MRI if symptoms persist.
