Chief Complaint
Right foot pain following a tennis injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right foot pain that began approximately two days ago following a fall while playing tennis. He describes attempting a double volley and falling on his foot. He initially wrapped the foot and iced it, but experienced pain and difficulty bearing weight. Ibuprofen provided some relief, but the pain persists. He denies prior injuries to this foot and reports a previous injury to his left leg. He was unable to continue playing tennis after the injury and was assisted off the field. He reports no numbness or tingling. He enjoys playing tennis with friends and is eager to return to the sport, but is concerned about the impact on his playing ability. He is right-handed.

Findings
Physical exam reveals bruising to the plantar and dorsal aspects of the right foot. The foot is swollen and warm to the touch. Palpation elicits tenderness at the midfoot. The Lisfranc joint exam reveals a positive piano key test at the first and second metatarsal levels. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the right foot's dorsalis pedis pulse. Neurological exam is intact, with no sensory deficits. Vitals are stable: BP 120/75, HR 60, RR 18, Temp 97.1°F, SpO2 100%. X-ray confirms a 3mm separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and a bony fragment in the Lisfranc joint space.

Assessment
Right foot pain consistent with a Lisfranc fracture, involving a disruption of the Lisfranc joint.

Plan
I am recommending surgical intervention to stabilize the Lisfranc joint and promote bone healing. An MRI will be ordered to assess ligamentous injury. The patient will be placed on a cast and crutches and will follow up in one week. Physical therapy will be initiated post-operatively to strengthen the foot and ankle musculature. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in six to eight weeks to assess healing and progress. I will provide the patient with documentation regarding the risks and benefits of surgery and the potential complications.
