Chief Complaint
Follow-up on poorly controlled blood sugars and medication adjustments.

History of Present Illness
Maria Martin presents for follow-up regarding her blood sugar control. She reports that her sugars are not very well controlled and that she has been taking two milligrams of glimepiride twice a day. She states she has not been eating much pasta or bread, and she prefers club soda. She reports foot pain and reports that her feet feel like hell. She is currently taking metoprolol 50mg daily, lisinopril/hydrochlorothiazide 20/25mg daily, and amlodipine 10mg daily. She is also on atorvastatin. She reports that she is still smoking and drinks club soda. She reports that she has been coughing lately because she has been congested. She reports urinary urgency and has been peeing several times per day. She reports decreased sensation in the tips of the first and second toes. She reports that her feet feel like hell and that she has foot pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals carotids are normal, pulse is regular, lungs are clear, extremities are without edema, pulses are palpable, thickened nails, and mildly decreased sensation in the tips of the first and second toes. Blood pressure is 140/60. She reports foot pain.

Assessment
The patient's A1c was 9.9, indicating poorly controlled diabetes. She has peripheral neuropathy, evidenced by decreased sensation in her toes. She is experiencing foot pain and reports that her feet feel like hell. She is currently taking multiple medications for hypertension, diabetes, and cholesterol.

Plan
Increase glimepiride to four milligrams twice a day. Continue metformin. Order blood work to check kidney function. Schedule follow-up in four months. Recommend keeping the feet moisturized. Continue atorvastatin. Advise the patient to quit smoking. Order shoulder radiographs. Initiate physical therapy. Continue acetaminophen as needed.
