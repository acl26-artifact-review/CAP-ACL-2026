Chief Complaint
Logan presents with an upper respiratory infection and reports a cold and congestion.

History of Present Illness
Logan is a 57-year-old male with a past medical history of coronary artery bypass grafting, hyperlipidemia, and OCD. He reports that approximately one week ago, he started to experience a cold, which progressed into his chest over the last few days. He notes some post-nasal drip at the beginning of the week, but it has since resolved. He reports a slight fever, but denies significant symptoms. He has been vaccinated against COVID and reports both stages. He traveled over the last week or so, which impacted his diet. He is currently taking Crestor 20 milligrams daily and reports that it has been tolerating well, with no muscle aches. He reports no chest pain, but does experience some left hand pain. He has been exercising regularly prior to becoming ill. He has received both stages of the COVID vaccine.

Findings
The patient's oxygenation is 96%. Cervical lymphadenopathy was noted on exam. Bilateral ronchi and wheezing were also observed. Lipid panel results show good cholesterol levels. The patient's COVID test is positive.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with a COVID infection and a cold. He has an upper respiratory infection.

Plan
The doctor advises the patient to quarantine for 10 days. The doctor orders a lipid panel in six months and orders a stress test in six months. The patient should continue taking Crestor 20 milligrams a day. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
