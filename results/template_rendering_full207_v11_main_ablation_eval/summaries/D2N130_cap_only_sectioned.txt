Chief Complaint
Left elbow pain and weakness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left elbow pain and weakness for approximately one week. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, exacerbated by gripping and lifting. He denies any recent injury. He reports feeling down and stressed and states he has a past medical history of epilepsy and depression. He also reports nasal congestion. He is currently taking keppra, lexapro, and protonix. He previously took protonix and meloxicam. He reports that typing is difficult.

Findings
Examination reveals swelling in the left elbow. The patient reports weakness in the arm. Palpation reveals tenderness over the olecranon process.

Assessment
The patient is experiencing elbow pain and weakness, likely secondary to olecranon bursitis. He also has a history of epilepsy, depression, and a past medical history of peptic ulcer disease and gastritis.

Plan
Order a refill of keppra. Prescribe meloxicam. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment. Advise the patient to continue with physical therapy as instructed.
