Chief Complaint
Constant pain in his left shoulder and left elbow.

History of Present Illness
Mr. James reports constant pain in his left shoulder and left elbow for approximately five months, stemming from a fall five months ago while going up a flight of stairs. The pain was exacerbated by lifting weights, and he has reduced his gym routine to 15-20 pounds due to the pain. He continues to walk approximately 30 minutes daily with his wife since retiring last year. He denies any numbness or tingling. He has tried ice but no other pain medications. He states he enjoys his exercise routine but the pain has made it difficult to continue.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the left elbow and shoulder, with no warmth, erythema, or deformity. Hawkins-Kennedy and Neer's tests are positive. Proximal and distal upper extremity strength is normal. Median, radial, and ulnar sensation are intact. Active abduction is limited to 90 degrees. Emptying the trapezius test is normal.

Assessment
Impingement syndrome of the left shoulder is the most likely diagnosis.

Plan
I have requested a referral to Dr. Martha Rivera for physical therapy sessions twice per week. We will also consider a cortisone injection if physical therapy does not improve symptoms. Please continue icing the affected shoulder and rest as much as possible. I recommend that you contact us if your symptoms worsen or do not improve with the therapy.
