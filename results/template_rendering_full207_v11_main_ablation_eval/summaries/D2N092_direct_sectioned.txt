Chief Complaint
Pain in the distal radius of the right arm following a tennis injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his right arm following a tennis injury, specifically after hitting a backhand shot. He describes hitting the net pole with his arm, resulting in a jar. He reports some swelling, but not significant. He also reports mild pain with wrist movement and no numbness. He has a history of allergies and takes flonase. He also fell while working on his rake.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal radius of the right arm. Pain is elicited with resisted movements of the wrist and thumb. Range of motion is limited by pain. The x-ray demonstrates no fractures or other abnormalities.

Assessment
Distal radius strain and contusion.

Plan
Prescribe ice packs for pain and swelling. Recommend motrin 800 milligrams three times daily with food for 7 days. Follow up in one week if symptoms do not improve. Dragon, please order the medications and procedures as discussed.
