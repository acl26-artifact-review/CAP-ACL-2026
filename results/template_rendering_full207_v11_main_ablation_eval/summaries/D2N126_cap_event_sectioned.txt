Chief Complaint
Right knee injury following a skiing accident in Vermont.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining a knee injury while skiing in Vermont last weekend, resulting in a fall and subsequent pop sensation. He was unable to bear weight immediately after the injury and required ski patrol assistance for transport. He describes the most significant pain as being on the lateral aspect of the knee and reports feeling like it 'won't hold his weight.' He reports some difficulty with flexion and extension, with some improvement after the injury but still pain with straightening. He has been taking ibuprofen for the pain, which provides some relief. He is a very active mom who enjoys chasing her two four-year-old children, but currently isn't very active due to the injury. He denies any other significant medical history or current medications beyond ibuprofen.

Findings
Physical exam reveals edema and effusion over the right knee, with pain to palpation on the lateral aspect. Active abduction is limited, and Lachman's test is positive. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable. The knee demonstrates decreased flexion and extension, and the patient reports pain with straightening. The heart and lungs are clear to auscultation. No leg edema is noted in the ankle.

Assessment
Based on the history and physical examination, a ruptured ACL is suspected, along with possible anterior knee instability. Further evaluation with a knee MRI is warranted to confirm the diagnosis and assess for any other associated injuries.

Plan
I have ordered a knee MRI to confirm the diagnosis of ACL rupture and evaluate for any other associated injuries. A knee brace will be prescribed to provide support and stability. The patient will continue taking ibuprofen as needed for pain relief. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately 7-10 days to discuss the MRI results and develop a comprehensive rehabilitation plan, including physical therapy to restore range of motion and strength.
