Chief Complaint
Patient reports ongoing shortness of breath with exertion and a history of recent myocardial infarction.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing chest pain following a recent myocardial infarction and subsequent stent placement in the right coronary artery. He was hospitalized for 48 hours. Since returning home, he has been advised to take it easy and has been doing light work. He reports some soreness at the cardiac catheterization site, which has improved. He denies further chest pain since the stent placement. He is currently taking brilinta twice daily and aspirin daily. He reports difficulty with salt intake, particularly with pizza. He expresses a desire to resume swimming.

Findings
Blood pressure is 127/60 mmHg. Jugular venous distension is not present. Carotid bruits are absent. A slight systolic ejection murmur of 3/6 is heard at the left base. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. The right radial artery cardiac cath site is clean, dry, and intact with a palpable pulse. Echocardiogram reveals mild mitral regurgitation. Ejection fraction is 50%, considered low-normal.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is post-myocardial infarction with evidence of mild mitral regurgitation. He is managing well with current medications.

Plan
Continue aspirin and brilinta as prescribed. Initiate a follow-up echocardiogram in six months. Recommend cardiac rehabilitation to improve confidence and provide dietary and exercise counseling. Advise resumption of swimming with a few laps initially. Order liver function studies and a lipid panel for follow-up. Schedule a follow-up appointment in six weeks.
