Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath for approximately one week. He states this symptom occurred six months ago and was managed with a lasix dose adjustment. He has a history of coronary artery disease with a stent placed in the right coronary artery and a prior ejection fraction of 35%. He admits to occasional salt intake due to family gatherings over the holidays. He reports swelling in his legs and difficulty lying flat at night, necessitating the use of two pillows. He is currently taking 40 mg of lasix daily and reports urinating less frequently. He has experienced foot pain recently and took motrin to alleviate it. He is not currently taking tylenol. He has not discussed potassium levels with his healthcare provider.

Findings
Vital signs reveal blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg. Jugular venous distention is present to a height of 9 cm. Bibasilar crackles are heard on lung auscultation with occasional rhonchi that resolve with coughing. One plus pitting edema is noted bilaterally. A three out of six systolic ejection murmur is heard at the left base.

Assessment
Clinical presentation suggests an acute heart failure exacerbation, likely secondary to underlying coronary artery disease and chronic kidney disease.

Plan
Increase lasix dosage to 80 mg daily, starting in the morning. Order a chest x-ray to evaluate for other potential causes of shortness of breath. Order an echocardiogram to assess cardiac function and the murmur at the left base. Schedule a follow-up call in two days to assess response to increased diuretic therapy. Refer a dietitian to assist with dietary modifications to manage potassium levels. Continue to avoid motrin and advil. Consider an alternative medication for pain management.
