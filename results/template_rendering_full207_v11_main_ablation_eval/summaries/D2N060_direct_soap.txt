S:
The patient reports foot pain following a soccer injury, specifically after a fall during a game. He states she fell onto his right foot and he felt pain. He describes the pain as a three without ibuprofen and a seven-eight with ibuprofen. He has no numbness or tingling. He reports no prior left foot injuries. He has been elevating and icing the foot and taking ibuprofen. He has been playing soccer since he was approximately five years old, starting locally and then playing in high school teams.

O:
Physical exam reveals bruising on the left foot and right foot, swelling, tenderness to palpation of the midfoot, and a piano key test positive. Capillary refill is less than three seconds on the dorsalis pedis. Motor and sensory function are intact. The patient's vitals are within normal limits: blood pressure 118/72, heart rate 58, respiratory rate 14, oxygen saturation 99% on room air, and temperature afebrile. A subtle dorsal displacement of the base of the second metatarsal with a three millimeter separation of the first and second metatarsal bases is noted on the x-ray. A bony fragment is present in the listerfranc joint space.

A:
The patient's right foot pain is due to a Lisfranc fracture. The x-ray and exam findings indicate a need for surgical intervention to stabilize the bones and ligaments in the foot. Surgery is recommended to prevent complications such as loss of arch and development of arthritis.

P:
Order an MRI to assess ligament injury. Recommend surgical fixation of the Lisfranc fracture with plates and screws. The patient will undergo a day-surgery procedure and be discharged the same day. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week. Apply a cast and instruct the patient to use crutches for six to eight weeks. Advise the patient to gradually increase weight-bearing as tolerated after the initial immobilization period. Counsel the patient regarding the potential for long-term complications if the fracture is not treated appropriately.
