Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing increasing shortness of breath, particularly when resting, and swelling in his legs. He also reports feeling lethargic and has been managing his type 1 diabetes with an insulin pump. He is currently taking omeprazole for reflux and reports that his depression is stable. He denies chest pain, fever, chills, or cough. He reports difficulty lying flat in bed. The patient reports that he is not ready to try any medications just yet.

Findings
The patient's vital signs are stable. A physical exam reveals 1+ pitting edema in the lower extremities and a slight 3/6 systolic ejection murmur. Chest x-ray demonstrates no pneumonia or airspace disease. Laboratory results show a normal white blood cell count.

Assessment
The patient is experiencing an acute heart failure exacerbation secondary to his underlying congestive heart failure and type 1 diabetes.

Plan
I am ordering intravenous furosemide (Lasix) at 40 milligrams daily. I will continue the patient on omeprazole 20 milligrams daily. I will order a hemoglobin A1c in one month to assess diabetes management. I am referring the patient to psychiatry for ongoing depression management. I will follow up with the patient in one week to assess response to treatment and address any further concerns. I advise the patient to call if he experiences worsening shortness of breath or any other concerning symptoms.
