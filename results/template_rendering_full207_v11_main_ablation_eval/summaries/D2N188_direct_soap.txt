S:
The patient reports an ankle injury sustained during mountain biking, describing it as a fall resulting in pain and an outward twist. He reports tingling and numbness in his toes and heart. He reports a fever reaching 200 degrees Fahrenheit. He reports hearing a crack and crackle during the injury. He has been mountain biking for approximately three years and enjoys riding in Colorado, but does not recall his favorite location. He reports feeling giggly.

O:
Temperature is 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit. Blood pressure is 118/77 mmHg. Heart rate is 77 beats per minute. Examination reveals edema and erythema on the lateral malleolus of the right ankle with associated ecchymosis and excoriations. Palpation of the right lateral malleolus elicits pain. Range of motion is limited with decreased flexion and extension and internal and external rotation of the right ankle. No apparent injury to the knee or foot.

A:
The patient has an acute right ankle sprain. The patient's symptoms are consistent with a soft tissue injury.

P:
I am placing the patient in an air cast. I am prescribing crutches for ambulation. I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg once daily. I am instructing the patient to ice the area for 20 minutes five times daily, as tolerated. I am advising the patient to weight-bear as tolerated. I will follow up with the patient in two weeks, or sooner if symptoms do not improve.
