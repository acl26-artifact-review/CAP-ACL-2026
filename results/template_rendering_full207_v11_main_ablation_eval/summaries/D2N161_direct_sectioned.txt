Chief Complaint
Hospital follow-up after an acute on chronic heart failure exacerbation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of hypertension and stage three chronic kidney disease. He states he has been trying to follow a dietary plan but experienced ankle swelling and shortness of breath, requiring emergency room transport and supplemental oxygen. He admits to enjoying pizza and chicken wings frequently and acknowledges difficulty adhering to dietary restrictions due to social events. He reports being on different blood pressure medications since his recent hospital admission and feels significantly better with current medications.

Findings
The patient's vital signs reveal a blood pressure of 128/72 mmHg, heart rate of 79 bpm, respiratory rate of 26 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 99%. Physical examination reveals no jugular venous distension. A grade II/6 systolic ejection murmur is present. Lower extremities demonstrate trace edema. The echocardiogram shows a preserved ejection fraction of 55%, abnormal diastolic filling, and mild to moderate mitral regurgitation. Weight is monitored daily, with a goal of no more than three pounds gained in two days.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with chronic heart failure, complicated by hypertension and chronic kidney disease. Management requires continued optimization of blood pressure control, diuretic therapy, and monitoring of renal function.

Plan
Continue bumetanate 2 mg daily. Continue cozaar 100 mg daily and norvasc 5 mg daily. Order a renal artery ultrasound. Refer the patient to a registered dietitian for dietary counseling. Monitor weight daily, reporting any gain exceeding three pounds in two days. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months.
