Chief Complaint
Left elbow pain and stiffness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left elbow pain that has been bothering him for several weeks, particularly after prolonged typing at his desk. He describes difficulty opening and closing his left elbow, noting stiffness and pain. He reports some weakness in his arm, but denies numbness or tingling. He reports feeling down and stressed, but denies any thoughts of self-harm. He reports being better with his ulcer and has not had any issues since the procedure. He is currently taking Keppra for epilepsy and protonix for his ulcer. He reports taking Keppra twice daily and protonix once daily before meals. He has not had any recent seizures.

Findings
Physical examination reveals erythema and edema in the left elbow. Palpation elicits tenderness. Range of motion is limited due to stiffness. Radial pulse is present. The patient reports nasal congestion.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with olecranon bursitis. He has a history of epilepsy and depression.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain. Continue Keppra 500 mg twice daily. Order a refill of Keppra. Prescribe lexapro 10 mg once daily. Schedule follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist. The nurse will provide instructions on medication administration and ice application.
