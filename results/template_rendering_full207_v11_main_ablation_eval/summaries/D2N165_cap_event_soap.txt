S:
The patient reports knee pain for approximately six months, initially with no known injury. He describes the pain as deep and achy, particularly when getting from a seated position to standing, and when going up and down stairs. He reports the pain worsens after watching TV. The patient reports leg pain and left hand pain. He also reports experiencing pain after watching TV.

O:
The doctor heard a regular heart rate and rhythm with no murmurs. The patient has normal gait. Examination reveals no erythema or warmth over the knees. Palpation of the bilateral retinacular retinaculum is tender. The patient's strength is 3/5 for abduction of the legs. The patient's neuro exam is unremarkable.

A:
The patient has patellofemoral pain syndrome. The examination findings are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome.

P:
The patient will receive physical therapy. The patient will take ibuprofen as needed. The doctor recommends a low-impact exercise program to improve lower extremity strength and mobility. The doctor recommends a follow-up visit in two weeks to assess progress.
