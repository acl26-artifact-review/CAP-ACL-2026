S:
The patient reports pain on the inside of his knee following a basketball injury seven days ago, describing it as exacerbated by movement. He states he fell to the ground and was unable to continue playing. He reports ice and an ace wrap were applied immediately after the injury. He denies hearing or feeling a pop in his knee. He is currently using exercises and an ace wrap for pain management.

O:
The right knee demonstrates ecchymosis and swelling on the medial aspect. Palpation reveals fluid effusion. Range of motion is decreased. The negative varus and valgus stress test was noted. There is no evidence of fractures on the x-ray.

A:
The patient likely has a medial collateral ligament strain. The examination findings suggest moderate knee effusion and decreased range of motion.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain and swelling. Order a right knee brace for 3 days. Schedule physical therapy follow-up in 7 days. Advise the patient to continue exercising with the brace. Instruct the patient to discontinue nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory medications. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 7 days to reassess the knee and consider further imaging if needed. Refer to primary care physician for continued diabetes management.
