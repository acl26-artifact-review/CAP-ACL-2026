Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain that has been present for approximately three weeks. He is unable to recall a specific injury and has been attempting to perform a significant amount of work in the basement, which he suspects may have contributed to the pain. He reports difficulty reaching overhead and pain when applying pressure to the affected area, particularly when lying on his side. He has been taking two extra strength acetaminophen every six to eight hours, which provides some relief but does not eliminate the pain. He denies any prior shoulder injuries or numbness/tingling.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited range of motion in the left shoulder, with decreased active and passive abduction. Tenderness is noted over the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Neurovascular exam reveals capillary refill less than three seconds. The patient reports no numbness or tingling. Shoulder X-ray demonstrates no fractures.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI of the left shoulder to further evaluate the rotator cuff tendons. I am also referring the patient to physical therapy for a six to eight week course of treatment, focusing on strengthening exercises. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust the treatment plan if necessary.
