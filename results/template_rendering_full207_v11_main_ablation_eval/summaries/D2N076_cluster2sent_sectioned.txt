Chief Complaint
The patient reports concerns regarding a suspected genetic syndrome.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 22-month-old former 34 and 3-week-old twin with Smith-Magenis syndrome. He has a history of feeding difficulties, including episodes of apnea during feeding, and has been evaluated by an allergist and neurologist, who diagnosed the syndrome due to delayed walking and lack of independent sitting at one year of age. He receives therapies including feeding therapy, occupational therapy, and physical therapy. He has mild hypotonia of the lower extremities and arms. He has autism. He reports running around without problems. The patient's parents report that he is slightly shorter and chunkier, which they attribute to the syndrome.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slightly fussy female, who is shorter and chunkier. Several organ systems can be affected by this chromosomal deletion syndrome, including congenital anomalies of the kidney and urinary tract. The patient has reflex abnormalities. A geneticist has reported that individuals with this syndrome may experience kidney problems and leg issues, leading to an orthopedics evaluation.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with Smith-Magenis syndrome, presenting with associated physical and neurological manifestations.

Plan
Order a kidney ultrasound to evaluate for congenital anomalies of the kidney and urinary tract. The nurse will provide instructions and paperwork for the ultrasound. Follow up with the patient in four weeks to assess ultrasound results and adjust management as needed. Inquire about family history of kidney failure, dialysis, or transplant.
