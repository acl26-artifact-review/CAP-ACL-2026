Chief Complaint
A non-healing foot ulcer.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a foot ulcer that has been present for approximately two months and is recently becoming red and draining. He states he may have acquired it from walking barefoot on the beach. He reports having taken medication for it previously but did not pick up the prescription. He recalls the name of the medication but does not recall the specific details. He reports experiencing occasional throbbing pain and reports the ulcer is red and draining. The patient's blood sugar levels are not regularly checked, and he is unsure how to manage them. He previously had an A1C of around ten.

Findings
A physical examination reveals a one by two inch circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot, proximal to the fifth metatarsal head. The wound is red and swollen, with drainage present. There is some edema around the wound. No signs of necrosis or bone exposure were observed. The patient denies any odor. An x-ray confirmed no evidence of osteomyelitis.

Assessment
The patient has a diabetic foot ulcer and an infection.

Plan
An ABI will be ordered to assess blood supply. A debridement will be performed. The patient will be prescribed clindamycin 400mg twice daily for seven days. A surgical shoe will be provided for wound care and protection. Follow-up will be scheduled in two weeks for reassessment and potential further debridement. The patient is advised to call the office if any concerns arise or if an emergency occurs.
