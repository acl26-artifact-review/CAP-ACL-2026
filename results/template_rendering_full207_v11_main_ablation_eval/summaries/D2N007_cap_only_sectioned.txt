Chief Complaint
Chronic lower back pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports chronic lower back pain for approximately six months, exacerbated by prolonged sitting and bending. He describes the pain as a dull ache, with occasional sharp shooting pains. He denies any recent injuries or trauma. He reports difficulty with daily activities, including lifting and prolonged standing. He reports feeling depressed and struggling with sleep. He currently takes prozac 20 milligrams a day.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild lumbar stiffness and limited range of motion. Neurological exam is intact, with no reported sensory or motor deficits. There are no signs of acute inflammation.

Assessment
The patient is experiencing chronic lower back pain with possible depressive symptoms.

Plan
Increase the dosage of prozac to 40 milligrams. Schedule physical therapy to address lumbar stiffness and improve range of motion. Follow up in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
