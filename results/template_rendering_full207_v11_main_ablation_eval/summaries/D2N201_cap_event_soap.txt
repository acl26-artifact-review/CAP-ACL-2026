S:
The patient reports back pain for approximately two years, initially following a basketball injury. He describes the pain as sharp and throbbing, radiating down his left leg and causing occasional numbness in his toes. He also reports experiencing weakness when standing for prolonged periods.

O:
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation at L4-L5, positive straight leg raise test, and decreased sensation to light touch at the L4 dermatome. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a lumbar herniated disc with radiculopathy.

P:
A spinal injection with a corticosteroid is ordered. A prescription for pain medication is ordered. Follow up is scheduled if symptoms do not improve.
