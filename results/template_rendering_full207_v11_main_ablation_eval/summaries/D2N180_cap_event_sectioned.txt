Chief Complaint
Bilateral hand numbness and tingling.

History of Present Illness
Ms. Taylor reports bilateral hand numbness and tingling for approximately six weeks, initially following a car accident last December. She describes the symptoms as worsening over the past month, particularly with use. She reports mild neck stiffness following the accident and has been taking ibuprofen for it. She has a history of pre-diabetes, which she controlled through diet changes. She is currently taking carvedilol 25mg twice daily for hypertension and lexapro 10mg daily for anxiety. She reports an allergy to sulfa drugs. She denies any recent trauma. She notes fatigue and weight gain over the past month.

Findings
Physical exam reveals diminished pinprick sensation to the third and fourth digits of the right hand and the fourth and fifth digits of the left hand. Reflexes are 1+ and symmetric bilaterally. Tinel's signs are positive at the wrists and elbows bilaterally. No other significant findings on physical exam.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome and possibly cubital tunnel syndrome. Further evaluation with EMG/NCV is warranted to assess the extent of nerve compression.

Plan
Schedule an EMG/NCV study to evaluate the extent of nerve compression. Continue current medications (carvedilol and lexapro). Advise the patient on splint use and ergonomic adjustments to minimize hand use. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to discuss the results of the EMG/NCV and assess treatment response.
