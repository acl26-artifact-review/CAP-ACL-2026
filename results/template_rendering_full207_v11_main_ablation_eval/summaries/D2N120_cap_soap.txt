S:
The patient reports a primary care doctor referral for back pain that has been present for approximately two years. He states he has tried numerous treatments with limited success. The patient describes the pain as sharp and stabbing, radiating down his right leg to his big toe. He also reports numbness and tingling in his right leg and foot. He describes the pain as being worse when sitting and reports difficulty getting comfortable. The patient reports weight gain over the past years and mentions he used to enjoy playing golf, but this has been set back by his back pain. He reports experiencing pain with lumbar flexion. The patient reports numbness and tingling.

O:
Physical exam reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness. Straight leg raise test is positive on the right. Neurological exam shows decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at L4 dermatome. Patellar and Achilles reflexes are symmetrical. Vitals are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with an L4-L5 herniated disc with radiculopathy. The patient has chronic lumbar back pain.

P:
A spinal injection with a corticosteroid is recommended. A corticosteroid will be injected. The patient will be monitored. Follow up if symptoms worsen or do not improve.
