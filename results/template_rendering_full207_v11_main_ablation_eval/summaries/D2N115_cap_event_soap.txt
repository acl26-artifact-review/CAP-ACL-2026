S:
The patient reports a car accident approximately one week ago, resulting in neck pain. He describes the pain as a 7/10, constant but exacerbated by movement, and primarily localized to the back of his neck. He reports some headaches recently and states the pain worsens with neck flexion and rotation. He reports difficulty touching his ear to his shoulder. He has a history of playing football years ago. He did not go to the emergency room after the accident.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the cervical soft tissues. Flexion and rotation elicit pain. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Neurological exam is unremarkable. X-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormalities.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a cervical strain, likely secondary to the car accident. The history of football and the mechanism of injury support this diagnosis.

P:
I am prescribing ibuprofen 600 mg every 6-8 hours as needed for pain relief. I am also prescribing Flexeril 10 mg every 12 hours as needed for muscle relaxation. I have ordered an MRI to rule out concussion or other serious injury. I am referring the patient to physical rehabilitation or a chiropractor for further evaluation and treatment after the MRI results are available.
