S:
The patient reports feeling swollen and experiencing some pain in his knee. He describes the injury as occurring during skiing, involving a cartwheel maneuver and a pop sensation. He has been taking Advil and icing the knee intermittently. His shoulder feels bruised. He reports a little tenderness when raising it up.

O:
Vitals are stable: temperature 97.9°F, heart rate 64 bpm, respirations 16 breaths per minute, blood pressure 110/74 mmHg, and oxygen saturation 98%. A physical exam reveals edema and ecchymosis around the right knee. Range of motion is somewhat limited, with pain on flexion and extension. There is no fracture appreciated on the x-ray. The patient reports a slight bruise to his shoulder.

A:
The patient has a knee contusion with associated pain and swelling. The shoulder bruise is likely secondary to the fall. Further evaluation is warranted to assess the extent of the knee injury and rule out other potential issues.

P:
I recommend rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE) for the knee. Prescribe ibuprofen 625mg every 6-8 hours as needed for pain. Order a right shoulder x-ray to rule out further injury. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 7 days to assess healing and range of motion. Advise the patient to return sooner if symptoms worsen or do not improve.
