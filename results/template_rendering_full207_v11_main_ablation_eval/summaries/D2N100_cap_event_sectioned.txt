Chief Complaint
Acne on the face, chest, and back.

History of Present Illness
Kayla Ward presents with a two-year history of acne on her face, with minimal involvement on the chest and back. She has been using over-the-counter products like washes and persa-gel, which provide some improvement. She describes a flare-up following the start of basketball practice due to sweating. She uses an elf facial cleanser and a noncomedogenic moisturizer daily, followed by persa-gel. She reports no associated symptoms like itching, bleeding, or pain. Her periods are regular. She has been diligent with her skincare regimen, but feels it hasn't provided sufficient improvement.

Findings
Examination reveals multiple benign nevi on the trunk and extremities. The skin shows scattered skin-colored papules, open and closed comedones, and erythremaous papules primarily on the forehead and central cheeks and chin. The chest and back are relatively spared. The remainder of the physical exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
Mild to moderate acne, primarily comedonal with a small inflammatory component, is present on the face.

Plan
Start topical therapy with a noncomedogenic moisturizer and sunscreen SPF 30 or higher daily. Apply adapalene 0.1% cream to affected areas three times per week, gradually increasing to nightly. Use the elf cleanser and noncomedogenic moisturizer daily. Follow up in six months to assess progress. Advise patient to wear sunscreen when outside due to retinoid use.
