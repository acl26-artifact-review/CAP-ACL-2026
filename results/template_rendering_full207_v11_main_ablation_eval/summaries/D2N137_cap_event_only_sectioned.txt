Chief Complaint
Left hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand for approximately two years, initially related to a carpal tunnel release. He states the release has been doing well, but now reports increased pain and tingling. He denies any recent injuries. He reports the pain is worse with gripping and repetitive movements. He denies numbness in the fingers.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the wrist and dorsal aspect of the hand. Grip strength is reduced bilaterally. Tinel's sign is negative. Phalen's maneuver is negative.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with recurrent carpal tunnel syndrome.

Plan
Continue current medication regimen of gabapentin 300mg twice daily. Refer to occupational therapy for ergonomic assessment and training. Schedule follow-up in 6 weeks to assess response to treatment.
