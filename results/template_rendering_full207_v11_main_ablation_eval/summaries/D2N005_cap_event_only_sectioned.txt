Chief Complaint
Right middle finger pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports significant pain in his right middle finger following a fall yesterday. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, with some tenderness to palpation. He states he is unable to grip objects firmly. He denies any other injuries. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain, which provides minimal relief.

Findings
Examination reveals moderate tenderness to palpation over the right middle finger. Range of motion is limited due to pain. There is no swelling or bruising visualized.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with a distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger.

Plan
Order finger splint to immobilize the affected area. Prescribe tramadol 50 mg every 6 hours as needed for pain for two days. Schedule a follow-up x-ray in two weeks to assess healing.
