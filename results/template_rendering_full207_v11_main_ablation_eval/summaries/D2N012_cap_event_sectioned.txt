Chief Complaint
Routine follow-up of chronic problems, including depression, asthma, and diabetes.

History of Present Illness
Joseph reports managing his depression with Prozac 20mg daily, having weaned himself off recently. He notes that his asthma has been exacerbated by pollen season, using his inhaler approximately 85% of the time on cooler, dry days. He reports keeping his diabetes under control, but admits to occasional dietary slip-ups when traveling for sports tournaments. He describes feeling lightheaded when running up stairs and reports some knee pain from running and lifting boxes. He has experienced occasional battles with depression.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no cervical lymphadenopathy or thyroid enlargement. Lung auscultation reveals bilateral expiratory wheezing, faint in character. The heart rhythm is regular at 72 bpm. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. Lower extremities are not swollen. A pft revealed normal results. Chest x-ray was unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient presents with chronic depression, asthma, and diabetes mellitus. His hemoglobin A1c is elevated.

Plan
Increase metformin to 1000 mg twice daily. Initiate Zoloft at 25 mg daily. Continue albuterol inhaler (2 puffs) every 4-6 hours as needed and add symbicort (2 puffs) twice daily during the summer months. Schedule a follow-up in three months to repeat the hemoglobin A1c and assess response to medications. The nurse will assist with medication administration and scheduling.
