Chief Complaint
Left hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent ache in his left hand, describing it as achy and occasionally sharp. He states the pain started approximately two weeks ago and has been gradually worsening. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is exacerbated by gripping and fine motor tasks, such as writing. He mentions that the pain is not interfering with his ability to perform daily activities, but it does make it difficult to use his hands for extended periods. He is currently taking metformin as prescribed by his physician.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the distal phalanges of the left hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No swelling or erythema is noted. Neurological exam is intact.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with peripheral neuropathy, likely secondary to diabetes mellitus.

Plan
Continue current metformin regimen. Order a comprehensive metabolic panel and nerve conduction studies to evaluate for peripheral neuropathy. Provide patient education on proper hand care and strategies to manage pain.
