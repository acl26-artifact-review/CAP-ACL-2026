Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent, dull chest pain for the past month, described as a tightness rather than sharp. The pain is exacerbated by exertion and relieved by rest. He denies shortness of breath, palpitations, or syncope. He reports a family history of coronary artery disease.

Findings
Blood pressure 138/82 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, regular rhythm. Auscultation reveals normal heart sounds. No murmurs appreciated.

Assessment
Possible angina pectoris. Rule out acute coronary syndrome.

Plan
Order serial cardiac enzymes, obtain a 12-lead ECG, and initiate aspirin 81 mg daily. Schedule follow-up with cardiology in 24 hours.
