S:
The patient reports headaches for years, initially linked to menstruation, now occurring with stress and more frequently and severely recently. He notes headaches are mostly on the right side, like behind the eye, with a pulsing sensation. He has been trying to exercise more to lose weight, but experiences worsening headaches with exercise. The patient reports experiencing ringing in his ears for several months and occasional blurred vision. He also reports numbness or tingling in his hands or feet or face, but denies experiencing any. He used to run a lot, preferring New Balance and Asics shoes, and currently uses Asics. He previously used Nike and then transitioned to Nike. The patient reports a history of tumor growth but moved over to Nike. He states he has had a recent MRI of his brain but a more recent one has not been obtained.

O:
The patient's blood pressure is within normal limits. Heart rate is within normal limits. Neurological examination reveals cranial nerves one through twelve are intact with normal reflexes. Funduscopic exam shows papilledema. Gait is normal. The examination of the heart reveals normal sinus rhythm with no murmurs, rubs, or gallops.

A:
The patient's symptoms are concerning for idiopathic intracranial hypertension. The papilledema and reported symptoms are consistent with this diagnosis. Further investigation is warranted to confirm.

P:
Arrange for an MRI of the brain to evaluate for increased intracranial pressure. Order a spinal tap to assess cerebrospinal fluid pressure and rule out other causes of symptoms. Refer the patient to a neuro-ophthalmologist for a comprehensive eye exam. Initiate medication management for idiopathic intracranial hypertension as appropriate, pending MRI and spinal tap results. Schedule follow-up in one week to discuss results and further management plan.
