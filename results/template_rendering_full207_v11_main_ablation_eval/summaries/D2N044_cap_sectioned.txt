Chief Complaint
Reports knee pain for six months.

History of Present Illness
Julie reports that she has been experiencing knee pain for approximately six months, with no specific injury recalled. She describes the pain as a deep, achy sensation, particularly exacerbated by sitting and standing, and when going up and down stairs. She has tried ibuprofen and aleve, which provide some relief but not complete pain freedom. She reports that she runs five to six miles a day, but the pain has limited her ability to run to half a mile. She works from home and her office is upstairs, requiring her to go downstairs to get to it. She enjoys working from home and her commute, and she loves biking. She notes that the pain is not relieved by rest. She denies any prior injuries or fractures.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 120/70, heart rate is 60, and respiratory rate is 14. The physical exam reveals normal gait, strength is good except for a three out of five abduction of the legs bilaterally, and patellar grind test is positive. Neurological exam is normal. X-rays show no fractures or osteoarthritis. There is no redness, swelling, or warmth in the knees.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome.

Plan
The doctor recommends physical therapy to address lower extremity strength and running mechanics. The patient is to continue taking ibuprofen or aleve as needed for pain relief. A follow-up appointment is scheduled in four weeks to assess progress.
