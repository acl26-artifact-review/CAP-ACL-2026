Chief Complaint
Ashley reports stomach pain and burning when she urinates.

History of Present Illness
Ashley James, a 14-year-old female, presents with a history of abdominal pain and urinary burning. The patient reported stomach pain and difficulty starting urination, accompanied by vomiting, approximately two weeks ago. Initially, her mother suspected appendicitis and treated her with a stomach virus medication. However, the symptoms persisted and worsened over the next day, prompting them to seek medical attention. Subsequent evaluations by a primary care physician and a pediatric specialist revealed a small kidney on the right side and cysts in the ovaries and lymph nodes. The patient's grandfather had obstructive neuropathy and died of a heart attack at age 47. Her mother had breast cancer. A genetic panel was performed, which did not reveal clinically significant mutations. Ashley has diabetes and hypertension. She drinks Gatorade and water. She complains of stomach pain and burning when she eats certain foods.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slightly smaller right kidney compared to the left. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are regular. No signs of acute distress. Lymph nodes are palpable but not significantly enlarged.

Assessment
The patient presents with a complex clinical picture, including a small right kidney, cysts in the ovaries and lymph nodes, and a family history of obstructive neuropathy and breast cancer. Her symptoms of abdominal pain and urinary burning suggest possible renal involvement and require further investigation.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs to evaluate for rotator cuff tendinopathy. Initiate physical therapy to address muscle weakness and improve range of motion. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as necessary. Advise the patient to stay hydrated and avoid carbonated beverages.
