Chief Complaint
Left lower back pain following previous surgery.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent left lower back pain, exacerbated by flexion and extension, following previous lumbar surgery. He describes severe, debilitating nerve pain and reports a history of high-dose gabapentin use, abruptly discontinued. He previously used ibuprofen 800 mg daily. He inquires about potential opioid options, including Vicodin and Demerol. He denies other current medical problems.

Findings
Physical examination reveals pain with palpation over the L5-S1 region, with pain reproduced upon left lateral flexion and extension. Dorsiflexion is normal, demonstrating full range of motion. No fractures are identified on radiographic examination. There is no nerve pain appreciated upon examination.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with a lumbar muscle strain secondary to spinal misalignment, complicated by chronic pain and previous surgical history.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam for pain management. I am also referring you to pain management for further evaluation and discussion regarding non-narcotic pain control strategies. I will prescribe demerol as well.
