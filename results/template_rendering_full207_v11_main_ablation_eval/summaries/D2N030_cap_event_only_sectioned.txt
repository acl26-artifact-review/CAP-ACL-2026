Chief Complaint
Patient reports breast tenderness and breast lumps.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports breast tenderness and breast lumps, which have been present for approximately two weeks. She denies fever, chills, or recent trauma. She reports the lumps are palpable in the upper outer quadrant of her right breast. She states, 'I'm concerned about these changes.' She denies any prior breast cancer history in her family. She reports occasional breast pain, rated as 3/10. She has not taken any medications for these symptoms.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a right breast mass, approximately 2 cm in diameter, with irregular borders, palpable in the upper outer quadrant. No skin changes or nipple retraction are noted. The breasts are warm and non-tender to palpation. No palpable lymph nodes in the axilla.

Assessment
The patient's report of breast tenderness and palpable breast lumps raises concern for possible malignancy. Further investigation is warranted.

Plan
Order a mammogram and breast ultrasound to evaluate the identified mass. Counsel the patient on the importance of early detection and discuss potential treatment options if malignancy is identified. Schedule follow-up in one week to discuss imaging results.
