Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a trampoline injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports injuring his right knee while at a trampoline park, experiencing an immediate snap and pain. He has been taking Tylenol for the pain, which provided some relief over the past week. He is currently taking lisinopril 20mg daily and metformin. He reports difficulty walking with a slight limp and reports difficulty bearing weight.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slight gait, ecchymosis and edema on the lateral aspect of the right knee, and a range of motion with some pain. Blood pressure is elevated at 180/80 mmHg. The patient denies effusion.

Assessment
The patient has a lateral ligament strain of the right knee and hypertension.

Plan
I am ordering an x-ray of the right knee and an A1C test. I am also ordering a lipid panel. I will prescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. I am referring the patient to physical therapy. I will continue lisinopril 20mg daily and order an echocardiogram. I will schedule a follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
