Chief Complaint
Severe right elbow pain, particularly with flexion and extension.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a new onset of severe right elbow pain approximately one and a half years ago, primarily exacerbated by use of his hands and arms. He describes the pain as being most prominent with flexion and extension, and it worsens with use. He denies any specific injury or trauma. He has been using ibuprofen 800 mg three times daily for pain relief, but reports it provides only minimal relief. He has not tried icing. The patient reports no prior sports injuries, but has been using heavy weights in his workouts. He has not been pitching, and has not been golfing recently. He is expecting a newborn soon.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited range of motion on extension of the right elbow, with pain upon testing. Palpation of the medial aspect of the right elbow elicits pain. Sensation is intact to light touch in all fingers. Pulses are equal bilaterally. Pronation and supination are unremarkable on the right elbow. X-rays demonstrate no fractures or bony misalignment. The left elbow demonstrates similar findings, with slightly less pain on flexion and extension, and limited range of motion on extension.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and physical examination, the most likely diagnosis is medial epicondylitis, commonly known as golfer's elbow.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI of the right elbow to further evaluate the findings. We will schedule this as soon as possible. Additionally, I am recommending a whole blood transfusion to aid in the healing process, which we can schedule for the next couple of weeks. We will follow up in two weeks to assess progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.
