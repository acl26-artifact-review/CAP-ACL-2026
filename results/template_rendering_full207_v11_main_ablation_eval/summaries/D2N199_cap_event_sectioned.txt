Chief Complaint
Cough and feeling unwell

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a cough that started approximately one week ago, accompanied by yellow sputum production. He describes feeling generally unwell for the past two days. He denies fever but feels warm. He reports some achiness and no chills. He also reports slight shortness of breath and congestion with nasal drainage and sinus pressure. He reports having twisted his right knee earlier today after falling down the stairs. He is not taking any medications for his cough or cold symptoms, only over-the-counter dayquil. He does not monitor his blood sugar levels or take his metformin.

Findings
Physical exam reveals bilateral cervical lymphadenopathy. Examination of the face shows pain to palpation of the bilateral maxillary sinuses. The right knee demonstrates pain to palpation and decreased flexion and extension with associated ecchymosis. Lung exam reveals coarse rhonchi bilaterally. Vital signs: temperature 98.4°F, blood pressure 120/62 mmHg, heart rate 80 bpm, oxygen saturation 97% on room air.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with a viral upper respiratory infection with possible bronchitis and a right medial collateral ligament strain.

Plan
Prescribe guaifenesin 600 mg twice daily for cough. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for right knee pain. Order right knee radiographs. Order hemoglobin A1C. Schedule physical therapy referral for right knee. Advise patient to monitor blood sugars and report to clinic. Schedule follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
