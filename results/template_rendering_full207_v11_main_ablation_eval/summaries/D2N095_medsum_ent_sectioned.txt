Chief Complaint
Back pain, reported as a lumbar strain possibly related to recent exercise and walking.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing back pain approximately six days ago following increased walking and recent weightlifting. The pain initially presented as a sudden spasm, described as feeling like a clenched muscle. He has been taking ibuprofen and applying heat, but the pain persists. He denies any recent trauma or numbness/tingling in his legs or feet. He reports some nasal congestion related to seasonal allergies. The patient's boyfriend is assisting with his diet to help manage his blood pressure.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation on the right lateral aspect of the lumbar spine with pain on flexion and extension. Straight leg raise is negative. Vital signs are stable, with blood pressure well controlled. A slight, grade 2 systolic ejection murmur is present on heart exam, which is stable. X-rays of the lumbar spine demonstrate normal bony alignment and no fracture. Lab results show no elevated white blood cell count or signs of infection. Dragon's assessment indicates good lung sounds and heart sounds.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a lumbar strain, likely exacerbated by recent activity. There is no evidence of acute pathology or infection.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain management. Refer to physical therapy for strengthening exercises to improve back stability. Continue lisinopril 10 mg daily. Advise the patient to monitor blood pressure readings and report any increases. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
