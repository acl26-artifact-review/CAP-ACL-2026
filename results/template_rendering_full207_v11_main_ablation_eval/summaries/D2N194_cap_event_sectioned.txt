Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies any specific injury or trauma. He describes being very active and recently involved in basement renovations. He has been taking acetaminophen (Tylenol) every six to eight hours for pain relief, which provides some relief. He reports difficulty sleeping on his left side due to the pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and tenderness to palpation over the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Neurovascular exam demonstrates brisk capillary refill in the left hand and intact sensation to light touch. Radiographs of the left shoulder do not reveal any fracture or bony abnormalities.

Assessment
Based on the patient's symptoms, physical examination findings, and radiographic results, the most likely diagnosis is rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI of the left shoulder to further evaluate the rotator cuff tendons. I am also referring the patient to physical therapy for a course of approximately six to eight weeks. The patient should continue taking acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. I will follow up with the patient in approximately two weeks to assess progress.
