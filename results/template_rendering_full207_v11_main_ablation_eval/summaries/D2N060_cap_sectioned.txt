Chief Complaint
Foot pain following a soccer injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his left foot following a soccer injury yesterday, sustained while attempting to steal the ball. He describes twisting his body around another player and feeling a sudden pain in his foot. He reports no prior injuries to this foot. Since the injury, he has been elevating the foot, icing it, and taking ibuprofen. He rates his pain as a 3 without ibuprofen and a 7-8 with ibuprofen. He has played soccer since he was approximately five years old, starting in his local town and continuing through high school. He reports no numbness or tingling sensation in the foot.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bruising and swelling on the left foot. Palpation elicits tenderness at the midfoot. The WAD test is positive. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds on the dorsalis pedis. Motor and sensory function are intact. Radiographic review reveals a subtle dorsal displacement of the base of the second metatarsal with a 3 millimeter separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and the presence of a bony fragment in the listerfranc joint space.

Assessment
The patient has a Lisfranc fracture with ligamentous injury to the foot.

Plan
I am recommending surgery to place the bones back in their proper position and using plates and screws to hold them there while they heal. This is a day surgery and the patient will be able to go home the same day. You will be in a cast and will need to use crutches for approximately six to eight weeks. After that, you will gradually start walking on your foot based on how you tolerate it and we will see how you do at that point. I will see you on Monday morning to get your foot taken care of.
