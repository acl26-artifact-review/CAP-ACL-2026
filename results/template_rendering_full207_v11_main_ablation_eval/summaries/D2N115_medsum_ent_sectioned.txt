Chief Complaint
The patient reports neck pain following a car accident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports neck pain following a car accident approximately one week ago. He describes hitting the back of his car, resulting in forward movement of his neck and head. He has been experiencing pain since the accident, which is rated as 7/10 in severity. The pain is not constant but worsens with head movement. He has tried ibuprofen, which provided mild relief, approximately 5/10. He denies any swelling or bruising. He reports headaches early on, which have persisted. He has a history of playing football years ago, but denies any prior neck injuries. He did not go to the emergency room after the accident, citing the cost of a $500 visit.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the cervical soft tissues. Range of motion is limited, particularly with flexion and extension, with pain reported. Rotation is also painful. There is no evidence of fracture on X-ray. Vital signs are within normal limits. No reported visual or hearing disturbances.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and physical examination, the most likely diagnosis is whiplash-associated neck pain, secondary to the recent motor vehicle collision.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI to rule out any other complications. I will prescribe motrin 600 mg every 6-8 hours and flexeril 10 mg every 12 hours as needed. I recommend conservative management, including relaxation techniques to avoid further neck strain. I am referring the patient to physical therapy or a chiropractor for evaluation and treatment once the MRI results are available. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
