Chief Complaint
Persistent cough

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent cough for approximately three days, initially mild but now more pronounced. He describes the cough as 'hacking' and notes the presence of green phlegm. He reports some sore throat and nasal congestion. He has not experienced fever or shortness of breath. He has been taking over-the-counter cough medicine without significant relief. He mentions being around a family member with similar symptoms a few days prior to the onset of his cough. He reports no history of seasonal allergies. He denies nausea or vomiting. He has been checking his blood pressure at home and notes it has been slightly elevated, around 140/90. He's been taking hydrochlorothiazide daily. He was on vacation last week and reports no illness during that time. He has been trying to limit salty foods.

Findings
Physical exam reveals bilateral maxillary sinus tenderness upon palpation. A systolic ejection murmur is auscultated at the left base. Lung exam is clear to auscultation. Lower extremities are unremarkable. Blood pressure is 140/90 mmHg.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with an upper respiratory infection, likely viral in origin, given the recent exposure to a similar illness. The elevated blood pressure warrants further investigation and management.

Plan
I recommend coricidin hbp for symptomatic relief. I will also increase the hydrochlorothiazide dose to 25mg daily. I will order a COVID-19 test to rule out a positive result. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed. I will also recommend physical therapy to address any potential muscle weakness or stiffness.
