Chief Complaint
Ankle injury following a basketball-related incident.

History of Present Illness
Jerry, a 45-year-old male, reports an ankle injury sustained while playing basketball. He describes a fall where he landed awkwardly, resulting in immediate pain and swelling. He's been experiencing difficulty walking and a sense of instability in the ankle. He's been trying to maintain his regular workout routine and is concerned about disrupting his progress. He also volunteers as a coach, starting in April, and is worried about the impact on his coaching responsibilities. He reports the pain is exacerbated by weight-bearing.

Findings
Physical examination reveals significant tenderness to palpation over the lateral ankle, with crepitance. The ankle demonstrates marked swelling and bruising. Capillary refill is normal. The patient reports difficulty standing on the ankle and a feeling of instability. An AP lateral and oblique x-ray demonstrates a displaced lateral malleolus at the Weber C level, without evidence of medial or posterior malleolar fractures.

Assessment
The patient presents with a lateral malleolar fracture. The instability and associated symptoms indicate a need for surgical intervention.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam for pain and inflammation. Instruct the patient to ice and elevate the ankle regularly. Order surgical consultation for evaluation and potential fixation with a plate and screws. Provide crutches for weight-bearing and mobility. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess healing and progress, and to discuss resuming coaching activities.
