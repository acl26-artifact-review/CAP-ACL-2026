Chief Complaint
Right leg pain following a bowling injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right leg pain following a fall while bowling. He describes hitting the leg with a bowling ball during a swing, resulting in immediate pain. He denies significant swelling but reports some bruising on the back of his right leg. He was able to walk with some difficulty but reports significant pain when going upstairs or downstairs. He has a history of atopic eczema and currently uses fluocinonide as needed. He has had a previous colectomy due to diverticulitis.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to the lateral aspect of the right upper leg. Range of motion is intact. No signs of acute instability. X-ray demonstrates no fracture or significant swelling, appearing normal.

Assessment
The patient likely sustained a contusion of the right upper leg.

Plan
I am initiating an anti-inflammatory medication, prescribing mobic 15 milligrams once daily. I recommend applying ice to the affected area. Follow up in one week if symptoms do not improve, or if they worsen. Dragon, please document the orders and procedures as described.
