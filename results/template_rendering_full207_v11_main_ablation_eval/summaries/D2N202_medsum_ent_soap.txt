S:
The patient reports having some shoulder pain, which started approximately three weeks ago while painting a house. He reports falling while reaching out and bracing himself. He initially took Advil for the pain and rated his pain level as a 6 without medication, but it decreases to a 4 with medication. He denies numbness or tingling. He has had previous shoulder pain, but this is the first time. He is able to move his arm, but experiences pain when lifting overhead or raising it overhead. He reports intermittent pain, not constant.

O:
A right shoulder x-ray reveals no fracture or bony abnormality. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds. Neurological exam reveals equal and bilateral response to sensation and touch. Limited active and passive range of motion is noted on the right shoulder. Tenderness to the greater tuberosity of the humerus is present. No tenderness at the sternoclavicular joint.

A:
I believe your symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy, likely sustained during the remodeling project. An MRI is ordered to rule out other pathology. Referral to physical therapy is arranged for approximately 6-8 weeks.

P:
Continue to take an NSAID if tolerated. Consider Tylenol for pain relief. Arrange follow-up in two weeks to evaluate progress and adjust treatment plan as needed.
