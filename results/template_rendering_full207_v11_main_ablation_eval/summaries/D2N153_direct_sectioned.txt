Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports injuring his right knee approximately two and a half weeks ago after stepping off a curb. He denies a significant fall but describes turning over his ankle. Initially, he experienced ankle pain, but the ankle soreness resolved, and he noticed knee pain, characterized by clicking when walking. He has used ibuprofen intermittently, finding it helpful at night but not significantly impacting his activity during the day. He reports numbness in his right foot, related to footwear. He has a history of three prior right foot toe fractures.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild edema and effusion in the right knee. Palpation is unremarkable. Range of motion is full, with a positive varus test and no Lachman sign. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable. X-ray demonstrates no acute fracture or bony abnormality. The patient reports increased clicking with full squatting.

Assessment
Based on the clinical presentation and examination findings, the patient likely has a lateral collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily. Refer the patient to physical therapy for strengthening exercises and rehabilitation. Advise the patient to continue icing the knee as tolerated, approximately 20 minutes on, 20 minutes off, particularly after activity. Follow up in four weeks, or sooner if symptoms worsen or if an MRI is indicated.
