Chief Complaint
Numbness and tingling in the fingers and pain in the wrist.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a gradual onset of numbness and tingling in his fingers, primarily on the left hand, for approximately three months. He describes this sensation as occurring most frequently at night and often necessitates shaking his hands to alleviate it. He also reports wrist pain, which he's experienced for several months. The patient notes that the pain and numbness are exacerbated by nighttime sleep. He describes the pain as a dull ache. The patient reports that he drops things more often due to decreased sensation. He denies any recent injuries or trauma. He reports drinking a beer or two on Fridays and Saturdays. He denies any history of rheumatoid arthritis. The patient's wrist and hand has been bothering him for several months. He notices pain mostly at night. The patient reports weakness in the left hand.

Findings
Physical examination reveals diminished left hand grip strength. Tinel's sign is positive bilaterally. Palpation of the left wrist reveals tenderness. The patient reports dropping things more often due to decreased sensation.

Assessment
The patient's clinical presentation and physical exam findings are consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome, likely affecting the left hand.

Plan
I would like to order an EMG to confirm the diagnosis. I'm also going to give you a wrist splint to wear. Start ibuprofen 600 milligrams every six hours. Follow up with me in two weeks to assess your progress. I'd like you to consider modifying your landscaping activities to reduce repetitive motions. I may have to keep that in mind because I do want to do some backyard landscaping remodel work. I will have a nurse in to discharge you.
