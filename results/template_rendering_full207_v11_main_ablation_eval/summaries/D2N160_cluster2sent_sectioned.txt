Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain following a lacrosse injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in his right elbow following a collision with a lacrosse stick. He describes the pain as sharp and radiating down his forearm. He has attempted to take ibuprofen but found it only provided minimal relief. He reports no other symptoms such as abdominal pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the posterior elbow, mild pain with flexion and extension, and normal range of motion. The patient's blood pressure was 150/90 mmHg. An x-ray revealed no fracture or bony abnormality. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. The heart exam reveals a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur.

Assessment
The patient has a contusion of the right elbow with associated swelling and pain.

Plan
Prescribe 800 mg ibuprofen twice daily for pain and inflammation. Recommend icing the elbow three times daily for 20 minutes. Schedule an A1C test to assess diabetic control. Continue the patient on 500 mg metformin daily and 20 mg lisinopril daily. Follow up in two weeks to assess healing and adjust treatment as needed.
