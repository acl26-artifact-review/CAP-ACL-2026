Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the outside of their knee, along with a feeling of instability. He reports difficulty bearing weight with his knee. The patient also reports pain in their right knee. The patient denies numbness. The patient is an active skier and has two four-year-old children. The patient experienced an injury approximately one month ago. The patient suspects an ACL rupture.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the right knee. Range of motion is limited. No effusion is present.

Assessment
The patient has sustained a knee injury and is experiencing right knee pain and instability.

Plan
The doctor will put the patient in a brace. The doctor plans to order a knee MRI. The doctor will prescribe ibuprofen at a dose of 600 to 800 milligrams every eight hours as needed. The doctor recommends a brace.
