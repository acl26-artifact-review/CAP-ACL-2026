S:
The patient reports anxiety regarding recent hepatitis C blood work results, stating they were surprised by the diagnosis despite feeling well. He reports a history of past IV drug use, now clean for over fifteen years. He currently drinks approximately one beer per day, with slightly more on weekends. He reports smoking one to two cigarettes per day, having significantly reduced from a pack and a half. The patient reports feeling tired at the end of the day, particularly after work, and notes that work has been busy lately. He reports having hypertension, diabetes, and depression in his family history. He states he has been feeling pretty good otherwise and had no other issues during his recent physical exam.

O:
Blood pressure was within normal limits. Heart rate was regular and within the normal range. The patient appeared in no distress and without jaundice on skin exam. Heart sounds were normal with a two out of six, six systolic murmur, unchanged from last year. Lung sounds were clear with no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi. The abdomen was soft with no hepatosplenomegaly and bowel sounds were present. Musculoskeletal exam revealed no gait disturbance or edema. Liver panel showed an elevated AST of 39, with normal albumin and total bilirubin. Hepatitis C antibody test was positive.

A:
The patient has a positive hepatitis C antibody test, indicating exposure to the virus. The elevated AST suggests possible liver inflammation. The patient's history of substance use and family history of hypertension, diabetes, and depression are relevant to his overall risk profile.

P:
Order hepatitis C RNA testing to confirm the diagnosis. Order a liver ultrasound to assess for fibrosis. Schedule follow-up in three weeks to review results and discuss treatment options. Counsel the patient on the importance of continued abstinence from alcohol and tobacco. Offer referral to a diabetes educator and mental health specialist. Arrange for family members to undergo hepatitis C screening.
