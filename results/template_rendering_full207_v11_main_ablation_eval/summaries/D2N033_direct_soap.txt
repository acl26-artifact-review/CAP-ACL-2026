S:
The patient reports a right knee injury sustained from a trampoline fall four days ago, characterized by a popping sensation and subsequent swelling. He reports some pain with weight-bearing but can tolerate some weight. He admits to enjoying ice cream regularly. He reports difficulty checking his blood sugar levels frequently. The patient reports that his diet has been good lately.

O:
The right knee demonstrates significant swelling. Pain is elicited with palpation on the medial aspect of the knee. Limited range of motion is noted with pain on flexion and extension. The McBurney's test is negative. Heart and lung sounds are clear. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's presentation is consistent with a possible medial knee ligament injury, likely secondary to the trampoline incident. The patient's diabetes management is suboptimal.

P:
Rescribe a straight leg brace for the right knee. Prescribe Mobic for pain relief. Instruct the patient to ice the knee for 15 minutes every hour. Order an MRI of the right knee to evaluate for ligamentous injury. Order a hemoglobin A1c to assess diabetes control. Refill metformin 500 mg. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress and MRI results.
