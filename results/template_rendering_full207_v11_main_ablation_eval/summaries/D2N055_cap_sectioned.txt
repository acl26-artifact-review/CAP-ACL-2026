Chief Complaint
Evaluation of a new right eye twitch.

History of Present Illness
Karen Nelson, a 3-year-old female, presents with a new right eye twitch that her father noticed approximately two weeks ago. The patient reports that the side of her face moves when it happens, and she feels an urge to blink her eye and then feels better afterwards. She reports that she sometimes feels the urge to move her face and then it moves and she feels better. She enjoys playing soccer and reports that she is sometimes kicked and pulled on her hair by another girl named Isabella. She also enjoys going to McDonald's and eating McNuggets. She denies any family history of seizures or Tourette's syndrome. She reports that she has been eating well and playing with her friends and has been engaging in her normal activities. She denies fever, coughing, or headache. She reports experiencing tics.

Findings
Physical exam reveals reflexes to be two plus and symmetric. Neurological exam shows no focal neurologic findings. The patient's sensation is intact. The patient denies fever, coughing, or headache.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with a transient tic disorder. No seizure activity was observed.

Plan
I am going to order radiographs of the right eye to rule out structural abnormalities. I am also going to recommend physical therapy to help manage the tics. We will continue to monitor Karen's symptoms and follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. I will advise the patient's father to avoid drawing attention to the tic and to ignore it.
