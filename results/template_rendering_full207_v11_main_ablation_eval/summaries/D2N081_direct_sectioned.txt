Chief Complaint
Follow-up for stage three nonsmall cell lung cancer and management of chemotherapy side effects.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 53-year-old female with a recent diagnosis of stage three nonsmall cell lung cancer. She reports experiencing a minimal cough and sore throat, with some fatigue. She has tolerated the chemotherapy regimen of cisplatine and etoposide well, reporting no nausea but some fatigue. She denies significant shortness of breath. She reports that she has been able to maintain adequate oral intake, though has experienced some difficulty swallowing at times. She has been receiving supportive care from the nursing staff and appreciates the office support.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no cervical or supraclavicular lymphadenopathy. Oxygen saturation is adequate. Lung exam demonstrates bilateral crackles, suggestive of mild radiation pneumonitis. Skin exam reveals erythema on the anterior chest wall on the left side, likely related to radiation therapy. Vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
Stage three nonsmall cell lung cancer. Radiation pneumonitis.

Plan
Continue current chemotherapy regimen of cisplatine and etoposide. Continue radiation therapy at 45 Gy. Prescribe low-dose prednisone, 40 mg daily for five days, to manage radiation pneumonitis. Prescribe lidocaine swish and swallow, to be used four times daily to aid with swallowing. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess tumor response and monitor for complications.
