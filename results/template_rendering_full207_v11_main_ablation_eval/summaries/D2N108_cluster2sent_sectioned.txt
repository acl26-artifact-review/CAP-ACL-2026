Chief Complaint
Gregory reports a two-month history of a non-healing foot ulcer and concerns about its recent drainage and redness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a two-month history of a red, draining foot ulcer. The ulcer appeared suddenly and has been slow to heal. He occasionally experiences throbbing in the affected area and reports a lack of specific pain. He admits to inconsistent medication adherence and infrequent blood sugar checks. He received initial medication from his PCP but has not yet obtained it. He previously saw his PCP for this issue.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a one by two inch circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot, proximal to the fifth metatarsal head. The wound is erythematous and draining. There is mild edema around the wound. No signs of necrosis, odor, or bony exposure were observed. Vital signs are normal. An x-ray showed no evidence of osteomyelitis. The ankle-brachial index was not yet obtained.

Assessment
The patient presents with a complicated diabetic foot ulcer requiring further evaluation and management.

Plan
An ankle-brachial index will be ordered to assess blood flow to the foot. A debridement will be performed to remove necrotic tissue. The patient will be prescribed clindamycin 400 mg twice daily for seven days to treat the infection. A diabetic shoe will be provided for wound care and protection. Follow-up will be scheduled in two weeks to assess healing and consider further debridement.
