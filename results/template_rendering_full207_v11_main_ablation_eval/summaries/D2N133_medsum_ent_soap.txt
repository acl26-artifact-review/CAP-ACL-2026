S:
The patient reports really bad knee pain, particularly after swimming, specifically using the breaststroke. He describes this pain as starting about a month ago after increasing his swimming frequency. He denies any injury or remembering a specific incident causing the pain. The patient's chronic back pain has been doing well recently, with no pain radiating down his legs. He reports stable mood and adherence to his medication and therapy regimen. He's sleeping well and eating regularly. He denies any feelings of hurting himself or others.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation of the right knee, particularly with patellar inhibition. Range of motion is limited due to pain. X-ray of the right knee is normal, showing no swelling. Heart and lungs are clear. No adenopathy is palpable. Grip strength is equal bilaterally. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's primary problem is chondromalacia of the patella, likely secondary to overuse and the breaststroke swimming technique. There is no evidence of acute injury.

P:
Rescribe ibuprofen 600 mg four times daily with food for pain management. Recommend straight leg raising exercises to strengthen the quadriceps. Advise the patient to avoid lunges and the breaststroke swimming technique for now. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks if symptoms do not improve. Continue current antidepressant medication and therapy.
