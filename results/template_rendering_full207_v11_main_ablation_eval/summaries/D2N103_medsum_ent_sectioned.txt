Chief Complaint
Status post mitral valve repair, experiencing intermittent chest pain and shortness of breath.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being seen today for follow-up after her mitral valve repair on 8/3/2020. She states she's doing well overall, but has been experiencing intermittent chest pain and shortness of breath, particularly with exertion. She describes the chest pain as 'weird' and intermittent, lasting only a few minutes, and she's learning to slow down when she feels it. She's also noticed increased shortness of breath, describing it as 'shallow.' She's been taking her medications regularly and reports only mild irritability from the atenolol, which has lessened over time. She's taking coumadin 4mg daily, lasix 4mg daily, and atenolol 50mg daily.

Findings
Physical exam reveals irregular irregular rhythm, trace peripheral edema on the extremities, and auscultation reveals reduced breath sounds bilaterally. The patient denies any fever, chills, or other acute symptoms. Examination of the head and neck reveals no jugular venous distension.

Assessment
The patient's primary concern is intermittent chest pain and shortness of breath, likely related to atrial fibrillation and residual cardiac issues post-mitral valve repair. She remains stable on current medications.

Plan
Continue current medications: coumadin 4mg daily, lasix 4mg daily, and atenolol 50mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in six to nine months to reassess her condition and adjust medications as needed. Advise the patient to continue monitoring her symptoms and slowing down during episodes of chest pain or shortness of breath. Refer to physical therapy for further evaluation and management of her symptoms.
