Chief Complaint
Right ankle pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that he slipped on ice last night while taking out the trash, resulting in a pop in his ankle. He was unable to walk immediately and his friend helped him inside. Now, he can put a small amount of weight on it, but is still limping. He describes the pain as constant and throbbing, and it feels warm to the touch. He took ibuprofen last night and this morning, but it hasn't helped much. He has had previous ankle injuries, but not this particular one. He denies numbness or tingling in his foot.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the anterior and lateral ankle, with ecchymosis visible over the lateral malleolus. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the right foot. Dorsalis pedis pulse is strong. The patient's blood pressure is 120/80 mmHg, and his heart rate is 80 bpm with an SpO2 of 98%.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with a right ankle sprain.

Plan
I'm ordering an air cast to stabilize the ankle and crutches for offloading. I'm recommending icing the ankle five times a day for 20 minutes. I'll also write a note for your employer regarding your inability to work. We'll schedule a follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist, or sooner if needed.
