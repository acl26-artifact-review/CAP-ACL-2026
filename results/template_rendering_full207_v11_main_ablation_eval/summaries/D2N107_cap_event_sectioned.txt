Chief Complaint
Back pain with right foot tingling and pins and needles.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain that began after raking leaves, exacerbated by standing, and relieved by sitting. He describes pins and needles in his right foot. He previously experienced similar back pain three years ago, which resolved after a day. He has not tried any treatments and takes two Advils, which did not help. His partner reports similar pain when standing. He is concerned about a long-term injury and his partner's pain tolerance. He has been largely inactive and reports his partner's activity level.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal dorsiflexion, equal pulses in all extremities. There is tenderness to palpation around the L5 vertebra. Right foot tingling is noted.

Assessment
Sprained low back.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam, order physical therapy, and schedule a follow-up in two weeks if symptoms persist. Consider an MRI if symptoms do not improve after two weeks.
