S:
The patient reports a left knee injury that occurred when running with his niece, describing a twisting injury. He states the pain started as a four, but has been worsening and now feels like a throbbing pain. He reports limping since the injury and has been taking Tylenol, which provides temporary relief but has a limited time frame. He also reports that he cannot take ibuprofen. The patient reports that he took an ulcer medication recently.

O:
The physical exam reveals moderate edema and effusion in the left knee. Range of motion is limited, with pain on flexion and extension. Lachman's and valgus/varus tests are negative. The x-ray was normal, showing no fractures or bony abnormalities. The patient's skin is intact and dry.

A:
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with a medial collateral ligament strain or MCL injury. There are no signs of fracture or other structural abnormalities.

P:
I will prescribe tramadol 50mg twice daily for breakthrough pain and Tylenol as needed. I will also prescribe a knee immobilizer to wear for one week and advise the patient to ice the knee three to four times daily for 20-30 minutes. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately one month to assess healing and function.
