S:
The patient reports left shoulder pain for one week, exacerbated by specific movements, following a gymnastics incident. He previously took Tylenol for two to three days without relief. He reports a history of left arm surgery. He denies taking any other pain medications. He states he can move up a little bit but that also hurts. He also reports that any kind of movement on his left shoulder actually hurts. He did have past surgery on his left arm. He wants to know if he can carry any heavy weights or anything like that.

O:
The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. The physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation with movement of the left shoulder. The x-ray did not reveal any broken bones or fractures.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to a musculoskeletal injury, possibly involving the shoulder joint. The past surgery is not directly related to the current pain.

P:
I suggest continuing with 600 mg of ibuprofen three times daily for one week. Recommend ice or heat pad application. Advise the patient to avoid heavy lifting or strenuous activity with the left arm for at least three to four weeks. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to assess improvement or if pain persists. Order medications and procedures as discussed. Advise the patient to try Advil or Aleve if the ibuprofen is not effective.
