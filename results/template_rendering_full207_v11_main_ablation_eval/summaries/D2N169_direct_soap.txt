S:
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath following a three-hour bike ride over the weekend, which he found surprising as he regularly cycles every morning. He described feeling lightheaded and noted some chest discomfort that resolved within an hour. He denies fever, chest pain, nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. He reports a mild cough approximately one year and a half ago, which resolved. He reports stable migraine control with medication and avoidance of bright lights. He reports stable reflux management with protonix. He reports cataract surgery approximately four and a half months ago with good vision.

O:
Xygenation is normal. Heart rate is within normal limits. Neck is supple, no jugular venous distension. Heart rhythm is regular, no murmurs auscultated. Lungs reveal slight expiratory wheezing bilaterally. Lower extremity edema is trace. Chest x-ray is normal, no pulmonary edema or pneumonia. Pulmonary function tests are normal. Labs are unremarkable.

A:
The patient's symptoms suggest a possible asthma exacerbation triggered by exercise and potentially exacerbated by allergies. The lightheadedness and wheezing are concerning. Migraine and reflux are currently controlled.

P:
Rescribe albuterol inhaler, two puffs every four hours as needed for wheezing or shortness of breath. Order a pulmonary referral. Continue imitrex as needed for migraine. Follow up in two weeks to assess response to treatment and address ongoing shortness of breath. Advise patient to call immediately if shortness of breath recurs.
