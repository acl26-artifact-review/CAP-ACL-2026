Chief Complaint
Hospital follow-up after an acute on chronic CHF exacerbation.

History of Present Illness
The patient is an 82-year-old male with past medical history significant for hypertension and stage three chronic kidney disease who presents for hospital follow-up after an acute on chronic CHF exacerbation. He reports feeling better since being hospitalized, attributing it to medication and diuretics, but mentions struggling with dietary indiscretions, particularly enjoying pizza and chicken wings, and occasionally missing medication doses. He describes swelling in his ankles and experiencing shortness of breath, leading to a recent 911 call. He reports that he has been watching his diet and taking his medications more consistently since being discharged.

Findings
The patient's vital signs reveal a blood pressure of 128/72 mmHg, heart rate of 79 bpm, respiratory rate of 26 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 99% on room air. Physical exam reveals no jugular venous distension. Lung auscultation is clear bilaterally. A grade II/6 systolic ejection murmur is heard. Lower extremities demonstrate trace edema. The echocardiogram shows a preserved ejection fraction of 55%, abnormal diastolic filling, and mild to moderate mitral regurgitation. The patient's weight has been monitored daily, with a reported gain of approximately three pounds over two days.

Assessment
The patient presents with chronic kidney disease, hypertension, and congestive heart failure. The current exacerbation is likely related to dietary indiscretion and suboptimal medication adherence.

Plan
Continue bumex 2 mg daily, monitor weight daily and report any gain of three pounds in two days, order a renal artery ultrasound, continue cozaar 100 mg daily and norvasc 5 mg daily, and schedule a nutritional consultation. Follow up in three months.
