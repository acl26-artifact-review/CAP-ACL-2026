Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath and intermittent respiratory symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing increasing shortness of breath over the past several months, particularly noticeable during periods of increased outdoor activity and exposure to cats. He describes episodes of sudden shortness of breath, sometimes accompanied by wheezing. He reports experiencing eye irritation, which he attributes to allergies. He has used an albuterol inhaler intermittently when experiencing symptoms and has taken Singulair 10mg daily. He notes occasional lip and throat swelling, and reports diarrhea after heavy drinking. He denies any prior history of allergies or asthma, and has not had previous allergy testing. He reports experiencing symptoms in the house, outside, and with his cat.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild expiratory wheezing bilaterally in all lung fields. Pulse oximetry is within normal limits. No rash is observed. No stridor is audible. Vital signs are stable. Chest x-ray and pulmonary function tests were previously normal.

Assessment
Newly diagnosed allergic asthma. Possible allergic triggers, including cat dander and outdoor allergens.

Plan
Prescribe Singulair 10mg daily. Prescribe albuterol inhaler for intermittent use as needed for shortness of breath. Order allergy testing (skin testing). Schedule follow-up appointment in one week to discuss allergy test results and further management. Advise patient to avoid cat exposure and outdoor activity when possible.
