Chief Complaint
The patient is anxious about their recent blood work results, specifically regarding hepatitis C.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling surprised to learn they have hepatitis C, as they had never been told previously. They state they used to party and even use intravenous drugs years ago, but have been clean for over fifteen years. Currently, they drink approximately one beer a day, with slightly more on weekends. The patient also admits to smoking, having reduced it to one to two cigarettes a day. They report feeling tired at the end of the day, particularly after work, although they've been busy lately. They previously felt fine but now experience fatigue. The patient's family history includes hypertension, diabetes, and depression.

Findings
A physical exam reveals a regular rate and rhythm heart, with a two out of six systolic murmur. Lung sounds are clear with no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi. The abdomen is soft with no hepatosplenomegaly. Bowel sounds are present. The patient appears in no distress and has no jaundice on skin exam. The patient's AST is elevated at 39.

Assessment
The patient's recent blood work results are consistent with hepatitis C. The patient has hepatitis C.

Plan
I am going to order a cervical MRI and Hepc RNA testing to confirm the diagnosis and determine the severity of liver disease. I will also order HCV genotype testing. I would like to schedule a follow-up in three weeks.
