Chief Complaint
Low back pain with radiating leg pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent low back pain for approximately two weeks, initiated after lifting a heavy object, characterized by a sharp pain followed by a dull ache. He describes the pain as both achy and sharp, and reports radiating pain down his left leg. He has attempted to manage the pain with ibuprofen and acetaminophen, with limited relief. He denies any recent trauma. The patient's father has a history of back pain. He reports no loss of bowel or bladder control or weakness.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness at the L4-L5 level, positive straight leg raise test on the left, and midline tenderness. Vital signs are within normal limits. MRI findings demonstrate a disk desiccation, disk bulge with paracentral disk herniation resulting in moderate right neuroforaminal stenosis.

Assessment
The patient presents with lumbar radiculopathy secondary to a herniated L4-L5 disc, resulting in radicular symptoms.

Plan
I recommend a spinal injection with a strong NSAID and corticosteroid under fluoroscopy to reduce inflammation and provide symptomatic relief. We will provide you with paperwork explaining the procedure and potential risks. Follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist, or sooner if they worsen.
