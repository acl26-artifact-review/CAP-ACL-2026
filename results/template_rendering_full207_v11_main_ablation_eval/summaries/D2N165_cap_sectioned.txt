Chief Complaint
The patient reports knee pain that has been present for approximately six months.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that the initial cause of the knee pain is unknown, describing it as a deep, achy pain that he feels behind his kneecaps. He describes the pain as being exacerbated by getting from a seated position to standing and after watching television. The patient reports that the pain is also worsened by going up and down stairs. He mentions that ice and ace bandages were previously used. He takes ibuprofen or aleve occasionally to manage the pain. The patient reports a history of running five to six miles daily, but now can only manage half a mile before experiencing leg pain. He prefers running outdoors, particularly on senior routes in Boston. The patient denies any prior fractures or bone breaks, except for a broken arm in sixth grade.

Findings
The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. Examination reveals normal gait. Strength testing shows abduction of the legs at 3 out of 5. Palpation of the knees reveals tenderness to palpation of the retinacular retinaculum. The patient demonstrates a positive patellar grind test. There is no erythema, swelling, or warmth appreciated. Neurovascular exam is unremarkable. X-rays show no fractures or osteoarthritis.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome.

Plan
The patient will receive physical therapy to address lower extremity strength and mobility. The patient will continue to take ibuprofen or aleve as needed for pain management. The doctor recommends treadmill running and outdoor running. Follow-up will be scheduled in four weeks to assess progress. ,
