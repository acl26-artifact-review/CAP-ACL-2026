Chief Complaint
Runny nose and congestion, with associated ear pulling and irritability.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports onset of runny nose and congestion approximately 24 hours ago. He also reports ear pulling, which he states has been present since the onset of nasal symptoms. He denies fever, but reports feeling irritable and difficulty sleeping. He has been drinking approximately 7 ounces of formula this morning and only 3 ounces of formula this morning. He has been using saline mist, baby Vick's on his feet, and a humidifier. His older sister has been sick. He has been using a cradle cap brush with aveno shampoo. He reports that his appetite has been poor, and he has been fighting to go to sleep. He has been using zarbee's for the stuffy nose.

Findings
The patient's temperature was 100.3°F rectally. Examination reveals clear nasal mucus. Examination of the ears reveals no acute findings. Vitals are within normal limits. No physical exam findings beyond those described in the history.

Assessment
Viral upper respiratory infection is the most likely diagnosis. The patient's symptoms are consistent with a common cold.

Plan
Continue saline mist and humidifier. Administer half a teaspoon of the patient's sister's medication if a barky cough develops. Follow up in one week if symptoms do not improve or worsen. Order a follow-up appointment in one week. Provide instructions to the parents regarding the use of zarbee's and the importance of avoiding honey until one year of age. Provide instructions regarding the use of the cradle cap brush and aveno shampoo. Provide instructions regarding the use of head & shoulder's, selsun blue, or nizoral ad for the cradle cap.
