Chief Complaint
Check blood pressure and discuss high blood pressure concerns.

History of Present Illness
Susan reports struggling with high blood pressure for years, with it seeming to get increasingly out of control lately. She's been on norvasc 10mg daily, but feels it's not adequately controlling her blood pressure. She's also experienced occasional headaches, which she attributes to stress at work or the high blood pressure. She has a history of a kidney transplant approximately three years ago, which has been stable. She takes all immunosuppressant medications as prescribed. She denies any chest pain or shortness of breath. She reports her home blood pressure readings have been consistently above 150.

Findings
Physical exam reveals regular heart rate and rhythm, with a slight systolic ejection murmur at the left sternal border. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. No lower extremity edema is noted. The scar from her kidney transplant is healed and visible. Her blood pressure measured 162/92. Her kidney function appears excellent on lab results. Her EKG is normal.

Assessment
The patient presents with uncontrolled hypertension, likely requiring additional therapeutic intervention. Her kidney transplant is stable.

Plan
Prescribe Coreg 25mg twice daily to help lower blood pressure. Order a lipid panel and a complete metabolic panel. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess blood pressure response to the new medication. Refer the patient to physical therapy for further management of shoulder pain.
