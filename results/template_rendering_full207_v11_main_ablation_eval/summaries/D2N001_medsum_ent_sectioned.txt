Chief Complaint
Annual exam and discussion of current health status.

History of Present Illness
Martha is a 50-year-old female with a past medical history significant for congestive heart failure, depression and hypertension who presents for her annual exam. She reports traveling a lot recently, particularly hiking, including a trip to the Northern Cascades last weekend. She reports doing well with her diet, resisting tater tots and salty foods, and has been consistently taking her blood pressure medications. She reports that therapy has been helpful over the past year. She admits to occasionally forgetting to take her blood pressure medications, particularly when work is stressful. She denies any current feelings of self-harm or harming others. She reports nasal congestion from fall pollen allergies.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a systolic ejection murmur of three out of six, and one plus pitting edema in the lower extremities. The echocardiogram from last year showed a low-ish pumping function of the heart at 45% and mitral regurgitation. The lipid panel from last year showed a slightly elevated cholesterol level. Blood pressure readings today are elevated.

Assessment
The patient presents with congestive heart failure, hypertension, and depression. She is currently managing these conditions with medication and therapy.

Plan
Continue the patient on current medications, including increasing lisinopril to 40 milligrams daily and starting lasix at 20 milligrams daily. Order an echocardiogram for repeat evaluation. Schedule a mammogram for the patient's annual exam. Instruct the patient to monitor and record her blood pressures daily and report them to the provider. Arrange follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and address any ongoing concerns.
