Chief Complaint
Sharp pain in the right abdomen below the ribs.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing sharp pain in the right abdomen below the ribs for the past several days. The pain is described as moving to the lower abdomen and groin and is quite severe, making it difficult to find a comfortable position. He notes some burning pain when urinating. He has previously had kidney stones, but this episode is prolonged. He has taken Tylenol for the pain, but it hasn't provided significant relief. He experiences nausea but not vomiting. He denies fever or chills. His family history includes kidney stones.

Findings
Physical examination reveals positive CVA tenderness on the right flank, indicating potential inflammation over the right kidney. The CT scan confirms a 0.5 cm kidney stone in the proximal right ureter, with no evidence of hydronephrosis.

Assessment
The patient presents with acute nephrolithiasis (kidney stone) with a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter.

Plan
I recommend increasing fluid intake to facilitate stone passage. I am prescribing oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours as needed for pain, alternating with Tylenol. I will provide a strainer for urine to monitor stone passage. A basic metabolic panel, urinalysis, and urine culture will be ordered. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks. If symptoms persist, we will discuss lithotripsy or ureteroscopy.
