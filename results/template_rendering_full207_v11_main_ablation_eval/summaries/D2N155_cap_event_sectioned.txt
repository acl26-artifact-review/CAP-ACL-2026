Chief Complaint
Non-healing foot ulcer.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a non-healing foot ulcer that has been present for approximately one year. It started as a small blister and became infected, leading to the use of antibiotics, which provided temporary relief. The patient reports no pain but also notes a lack of feeling in their foot. They have been wearing regular shoes and have not received a surgical shoe. The patient's last A1c was about ten. They report feeling tired.

Findings
Examination reveals a one by two centimeter circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot, proximal to the fifth metatarsal head. The wound exhibits yellow slough and minimal granulation tissue. There is no surrounding erythema or cellulitis, and no evidence of fluid collection or necrosis. No odor is present, and there is no bony exposure. An ankle brachial index will be ordered.

Assessment
The patient has a diabetic foot ulcer requiring ongoing management.

Plan
Order an ankle brachial index. Prescribe colagenase ointment for topical application to the wound once daily, covered with a sterile dressing. Arrange for a surgical shoe to provide pressure relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess the ulcer's progress.
