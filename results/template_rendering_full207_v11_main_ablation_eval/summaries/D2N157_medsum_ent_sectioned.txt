Chief Complaint
Concerned about all medications and dizziness upon standing quickly.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being concerned about the number of medications he is taking and experiencing dizziness when standing up quickly. He takes aspirin and brilinta and fish oil. He reports muscle aches related to crestor. He is also concerned about the longevity of his stents. The patient reports a history of diabetes and has had an A1c of approximately 6%. He reports feeling down since his heart attack. He reports needing a referral for diabetic education and is considering a referral to psychiatry. He reports one plus nonpitting edema in his lower extremities.

Findings
Examination reveals a slight 3/6 systolic ejection murmur heard at the left base and one plus nonpitting edema in the lower extremities. Kidney function is stable, with a creatinine of 0.9. The patient's blood pressure is 127/78. The patient's hemoglobin is 8.2.

Assessment
The patient presents with coronary artery disease, stage 3 chronic kidney disease, and poorly controlled diabetes mellitus. He also exhibits symptoms consistent with medication side effects, specifically dizziness upon standing.

Plan
I am ordering a hemoglobin A1c and will order a referral to a dietitian for diabetes education. I will also refer you to psychiatry for support with adjusting after your heart attack. I will arrange a follow-up appointment with your nephrologist. I will refill your metoprolol, crestor, and aspirin. I will continue to monitor your kidney function and blood pressure. I recommend cardiac rehabilitation to improve your exercise capacity and confidence.
