S:
The patient reports a history of basketball injury, specifically a fall resulting in pain on the inside of his right knee. He describes the pain as sharp and persistent, exacerbated by movement. He states he was unable to continue playing after the injury. The patient reports using ice and an ace wrap for the injury and taking meloxicam for pain. He checks his blood sugars daily.

O:
Examination reveals moderate right knee effusion and mild tenderness to palpation on the medial aspect of the knee. Range of motion is decreased. The x-ray shows no fractures but demonstrates the effusion. The patient's blood sugars are within the target range.

A:
The patient has a medial collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. Order a right knee brace for support. Schedule physical therapy for rehabilitation. Advise the patient to follow up in one week for re-evaluation and to monitor blood sugars closely. Recommend avoiding basketball activity until cleared by physical therapy.
