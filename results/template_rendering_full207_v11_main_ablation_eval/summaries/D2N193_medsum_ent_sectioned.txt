Chief Complaint
A hacking cough for approximately three days accompanied by sore throat and nasal congestion.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports the onset of a hacking cough three days ago, initially mild but now producing green phlegm. He also describes a sore throat and nasal congestion. He reports drinking plenty of water and taking over-the-counter cough medication, but finds it ineffective. He mentions experiencing seasonal allergies around springtime, characterized by sneezing and runny nose. He denies fever or shortness of breath. He reports being around a family member who was experiencing similar symptoms. He has been monitoring his blood sugars, which have been in the range of 120, and reports difficulty with sugar intake. He is currently taking metformin 500mg daily and reports no problems with it. He denies diarrhea. He notes his blood pressure has been slightly elevated, around 140/72, and he is taking hydrochlorothiazide 25mg daily.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bilateral maxillary sinus tenderness upon gentle palpation. There is also some anterior cervical adenopathy. Lung exam is clear, with no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi. The patient's blood pressure was measured at 140/72 mmHg. The patient's heart rate was regular and at 72 bpm. The patient's oxygen saturation was 98% on room air.

Assessment
The patient presents with an upper respiratory infection, likely viral in etiology, given his exposure to a symptomatic family member. His symptoms are consistent with a common cold. His blood pressure is elevated, and he is taking a low dose of hydrochlorothiazide.

Plan
I recommend a coricidin HBP for symptomatic cough relief. I will order a COVID test to rule out infection. Continue monitoring blood pressure and encourage regular monitoring at home. We will check his hemoglobin A1c at his next visit in three months. I will increase the hydrochlorothiazide to 25mg daily. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Advise the patient to rest and drink plenty of fluids.
