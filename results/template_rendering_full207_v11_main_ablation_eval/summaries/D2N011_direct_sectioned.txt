Chief Complaint
Roger reports episode of chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports an episode of chest discomfort during yard work over the weekend, associated with shortness of breath and mild chest pain lasting approximately one hour. He denies prior episodes of chest pain and reports no other symptoms. He had knee surgery approximately two months ago and reports feeling well since then. His blood pressure was elevated at 180/95 in the emergency room, and he felt it had regulated after the event. He has a family history of heart disease on his cousin's side.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a slightly enlarged thyroid gland, non-tender. Carotid bruit is present on the right side. Lung sounds are clear. Heart rate is regular and rhythmic, with a systolic ejection murmur of 3/6. The abdomen is soft. The healed scar from his prior right knee surgery is noted. Blood pressure is 180/95 mmHg. An EKG shows normal sinus rhythm. Echocardiogram reveals mild low left ventricular ejection fraction.

Assessment
The patient presents with an episode of chest pain, potentially related to hypertension or underlying cardiac disease.

Plan
Change the blood pressure regimen to add carvedilol 25 mg twice daily, in addition to continuing lisinopril 10 mg daily. Order a cardiac catheterization to evaluate for coronary artery disease. Follow up closely on blood pressure response to the new regimen. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess symptom resolution and adjust management as needed.
