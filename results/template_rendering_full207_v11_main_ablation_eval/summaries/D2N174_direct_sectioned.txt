Chief Complaint
Right knee pain and swelling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports increasing right knee pain and swelling over the past few days, exacerbated by activity, particularly cycling. He reports doubling his mileage on the bike recently. The pain is present in the morning and improves throughout the day. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports no fever or other systemic symptoms. He is currently taking crestor and aspirin as prescribed.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bilateral knee edema and mild erythema. There is a palpable effusion in the right knee and mild tenderness to palpation. Range of motion is decreased bilaterally. The heart exam reveals a two out of six systolic ejection murmur. The lungs are clear to auscultation. Right knee X-ray demonstrates no acute bony abnormalities. EKG shows normal sinus rhythm. Dragon reports vital signs are stable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a possible flare of lupus or mechanical knee pain secondary to overuse. Coronary artery disease remains stable.

Plan
Order an autoimmune panel to evaluate for lupus flare. Order a lipid panel and echocardiogram to assess cardiovascular risk. Order a right knee ultrasound to evaluate for effusion and rule out structural abnormalities. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and adjust plan as needed. The nurse will schedule a physical therapy consultation.
