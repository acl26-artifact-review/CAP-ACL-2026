Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath and chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports shortness of breath and chest pain. He describes the chest pain as a pressure sensation, rated 6/10 in severity, and reports it radiating to his left arm. The shortness of breath is intermittent and worsens with exertion. He denies any recent trauma. He reports a history of knee surgery approximately two months ago and subsequent rehab and recovery with no problems reported. He denies any other symptoms. He reports performing yard work this weekend and experiencing heat. He denies fever, chills, or cough.

Findings
Blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 88 bpm, respiratory rate 18 breaths per minute. Physical exam reveals clear lung sounds bilaterally. No edema noted. Palpation of the chest reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the left anterior chest wall.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms of shortness of breath and chest pain are concerning for possible cardiac etiology. Clinical history is notable for recent knee surgery and current medications.

Plan
Order a cardiac catheterization. Prescribe carvedilol 25mg twice daily and lisinopril 10mg daily. Schedule follow-up in one week to assess response to medications and further investigate cardiac status.
