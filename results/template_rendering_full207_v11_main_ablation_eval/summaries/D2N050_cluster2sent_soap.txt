S:
The patient reports experiencing right flank pain approximately one week ago, initially attributed to heavy lifting but later confirmed by a CT scan to be a kidney stone. He reports nausea and sweating during the initial episode. The patient states he has never had a kidney stone before, though his father has a history of them. He also reports noticing blood in his urine.

O:
The patient's vital signs are stable, with blood pressure and heart rate within normal limits. Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the right flank. CT scan demonstrates a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter with no evidence of hydronephrosis. Urinalysis is pending.

A:
The patient has a confirmed kidney stone in the right ureter, likely causing his flank pain and hematuria. There is no evidence of significant obstruction or kidney damage.

P:
Recommend increasing fluid intake to facilitate stone passage. Prescribe oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours for pain. Continue Tylenol as needed for breakthrough pain. Order BMP and urinalysis/urine culture. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks to assess stone passage and consider further management if necessary, including potential lithotripsy or other interventions.
