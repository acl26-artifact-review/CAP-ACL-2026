Chief Complaint
High blood pressure check and management.

History of Present Illness
Susan reports a long-standing struggle with high blood pressure, which has recently become more uncontrolled. She reports experiencing headaches possibly related to the high blood pressure. She has a history of kidney transplant five years ago, which is currently stable and managed with immunosuppressants. She is currently taking norvasc 10mg daily and reports that her home blood pressures have been consistently above 150. She denies any chest pain or shortness of breath.

Findings
Physical exam reveals regular heart rate and rhythm, a slight systolic ejection murmur, clear lung sounds, and no lower extremity edema. The scar from her kidney transplant is healed. Creatinine is normal, and the EKG is normal. Blood pressure measured at 162/92.

Assessment
The patient presents with uncontrolled hypertension. Her kidney transplant is stable.

Plan
Prescribe Coreg 25mg twice daily to manage blood pressure. Order Carvedilol 25mg twice daily. Order a lipid panel and a complete metabolic panel. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to reassess blood pressure and monitor medication effectiveness. The nurse will schedule a follow-up appointment.
