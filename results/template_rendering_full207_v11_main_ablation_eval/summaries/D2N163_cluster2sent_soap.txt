S:
The patient reports being surprised by a high cholesterol reading at her annual checkup and expresses concern about this finding. She enjoys cycling and maintains an active lifestyle. She denies any prior history of hypercholesterolemia or family history of heart disease or sudden death.

O:
Blood pressure is within normal limits. Lung exam is clear. Thyroid exam reveals no thyroid enlargement. Abdominal exam is unremarkable.

A:
The patient has been diagnosed with newly diagnosed hypercholesterolemia. Further investigation is warranted to determine the cause of the elevated cholesterol levels.

P:
Order a full cholesterol panel, complete metabolic panel, thyroid studies, and liver panels. Order genetic testing to evaluate for familial or secondary causes of hypercholesterolemia. Schedule a follow-up appointment with a registered dietitian to discuss dietary modifications. Schedule a repeat cholesterol panel in 3-6 months after dietary changes are implemented. Recommend continued participation in regular physical activity, including cycling.
