Chief Complaint
Follow-up for adult hydrocephalus and related symptoms.

History of Present Illness
Mr. Watson is a 86-year-old male in follow-up for adult hydrocephalus, initially diagnosed in June 2020 following shunt surgery on August 1st, 2020. Prior to surgery, he experienced progressive gait impairment, cognitive impairment, and decreased bladder control. He reports no recent hospitalizations related to the shunt. The patient reports improved walking ability with assistance, but still requires assistance with ambulation. He reports improved cognitive function, stating he can now engage in normal conversations. He reports wearing diapers due to urinary incontinence. He denies headaches or pain at the shunt site. He has not experienced any sores related to diaper use. He reports no recent falls or hallucinations.

Findings
Blood pressure is 124/80, heart rate 64, respirations 18, weight 174 pounds. The shunt site is clean, dry, and intact with a setting of 2.0. CT scan from 10/15/2020 reveals a tiny amount of blood in the right parietal region with a tiny subdermal collection. MMSE score with spelling was 26/30, and with calculations was 25/30. Gait testing using the Tinetti assessment tool yielded a score of 6-8/12 for gait and 12/16 for balance, resulting in a total score of 18-20/28. Cranial nerves are intact. Pupils are equal and reactive. Motor strength is normal. Coordination is slow.

Assessment
Adult hydrocephalus, gait impairment, urinary incontinence, and cognitive impairment.

Plan
Maintain shunt setting at 2.0 for the next two to three months. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three months to reassess symptoms and consider further management. Recommend driver safety program if improvement in mobility is achieved. Continue monitoring for any signs of infection or complications.
