Chief Complaint
Right knee pain related to running.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain that began during running, exacerbated by activity and worse towards the end of the day. He describes ice and ibuprofen providing some relief. He has experienced this for several years and was previously evaluated by Dr. Wood, who referred him to this visit. He denies any family history of arthritis. He has been running regularly, including 5k and marathon training, and has experienced pain limiting his distance.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild effusion in the right knee, tenderness to palpation on the outside, and no signs of redness or swelling. Pedal pulse is present and intact. Knee range of motion is within normal limits with mild pain on extension. Blood pressure is 120/60 mmHg, heart rate 58 bpm, and respirations 14. X-rays show no fracture or evidence of arthritis.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a knee sprain secondary to overuse, likely exacerbated by running. There is no evidence of structural damage or arthritis.

Plan
Prescribe two doses of 500 mg acetaminophen and two doses of 200 mg ibuprofen every 6 hours as needed for pain. Order physical therapy for the right knee to address strength and balance. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed. Advise the patient to limit running for two weeks and focus on low-impact activities like weight training, as recommended by the physical therapist.
