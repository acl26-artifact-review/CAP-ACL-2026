Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath and painful swallowing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath over the weekend, initially noticing it while walking. Prior to this, she was active, regularly walking her dog for mile-long walks. She describes the difficulty as being unable to walk down the street without assistance. She also reports experiencing pain when swallowing, describing it as pain deep in her chest rather than a typical cold sensation. She has been taking ensure shakes to maintain hydration due to nausea and fatigue experienced during chemotherapy. She reports nausea and fatigue with the chemotherapy regimen, but zofran helped. She has been on radiation therapy for a left upper lobe lung cancer nodule.

Findings
Physical exam reveals crackles in both lung bases bilaterally. Examination reveals erythema on the anterior chest wall of the left side, likely due to radiation. No cervical or subclavicular lymphadenopathy is palpated. The heart exam is regular rate and rhythm with no murmurs. Extremities are clear of edema. Chest x-ray shows mild radiation pneumonitis.

Assessment
Lung cancer, stage three; radiation pneumonitis.

Plan
Prescribe prednisone 40 mg daily for five days to manage radiation pneumonitis. Prescribe lidocaine swabs for oral use four times daily to alleviate painful swallowing. Continue current chemotherapy regimen and radiation therapy. Schedule follow-up in two weeks.
