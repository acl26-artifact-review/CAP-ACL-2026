S:
The patient reports foot pain following a soccer injury, describing the pain as a 3 without ibuprofen and 8 with ibuprofen. He states the pain is due to bruising and swelling, and reports tenderness to palpation. He denies any numbness or tingling. He has not experienced any prior foot injuries.

O:
Physical exam reveals bruising and swelling on the left foot. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds. The piano key test is positive. Neuromuscular exam is intact. The MRI reveals a dorsal displacement of the base of the second metatarsal with a 3mm separation of the first and second metatarsal bases and a bony fragment in the Lisfranc joint space.

A:
The patient has a Lisfranc fracture, a fracture to one of the second metatarsal bones at the top of the foot, requiring surgical intervention to stabilize the bones and ligaments.

P:
An MRI is ordered to assess ligament injury. Surgery is recommended to place plates and screws to stabilize the bones and ligaments. The patient will be in a cast and use crutches for 6-8 weeks. Follow-up will be scheduled in the clinic to assess healing and gradual weight-bearing.
