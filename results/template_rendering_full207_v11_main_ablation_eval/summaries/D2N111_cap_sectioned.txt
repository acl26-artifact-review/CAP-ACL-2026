Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a trampoline injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining right knee pain following a fall while using a trampoline. He describes hearing a 'pop' at the time of the injury and immediately experiencing pain. He states this is the first time he has ever attempted this activity. He reports only elevating the knee and taking acetaminophen for the pain, which provided minimal relief. He has not sought medical attention for this injury previously. He reports difficulty walking with a slight limp. He reports trying to watch what he eats but not counting calories or macros.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the lateral aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited due to pain. There is mild ecchymosis and edema noted. The patient's heart rate is 78 bpm and blood pressure is 178/84. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. The patient reports no murmur. The right knee x-ray shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a lateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and swelling. I am ordering an a1c test and lipid panel. I am referring the patient to physical therapy for strengthening exercises and rehabilitation. I will follow up with the patient in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
