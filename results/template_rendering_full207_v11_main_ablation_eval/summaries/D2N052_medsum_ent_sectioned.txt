Chief Complaint
Right-sided back pain and hematuria.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right-sided back pain for approximately one week, initially mild but progressively worsening. He describes the pain as constant and rated it as 6/10. He also reports noticing blood in his urine over the past few days. He denies any fever, chills, nausea, or vomiting. He took ibuprofen, which provided some relief but not complete resolution of the pain. The patient reports a family history of kidney stones in his father. He states he did not participate in the Easter egg hunt due to the pain. He reports feeling slightly nauseated.

Findings
Vitals are stable. Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the right flank. Examination of the abdomen is unremarkable. There is no rebound tenderness or guarding. A CT scan of the right flank confirms a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter, with no evidence of hydronephrosis.

Assessment
The patient presents with a confirmed kidney stone in the proximal right ureter, causing right flank pain and hematuria.

Plan
I am prescribing ibuprofen 800 mg to take as needed for pain. I am also ordering a urine culture and urinalysis to assess for infection. I will order a BMP to evaluate kidney function. I recommend increasing fluid intake to facilitate stone passage. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in one to two weeks to assess progress and discuss further management options, including lithotripsy if necessary. My nurse will provide the prescription and schedule the follow-up appointment.
