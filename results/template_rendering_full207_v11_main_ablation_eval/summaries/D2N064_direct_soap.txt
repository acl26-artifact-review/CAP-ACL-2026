O:
There is ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus and swelling of the left ankle. Palpation reveals tenderness to the lateral soft tissue. Capillary refill is brisk bilaterally. Dorsalis pedis pulses are strong. Neuromuscular exam reveals intact sensation and motor function bilaterally. The x-ray showed no fracture or bony abnormalities.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with a lateral ankle sprain of the ligament complex.

P:
I am ordering an air cast for the left ankle to stabilize it. Prescribing crutches for weight-bearing as tolerated. Instructing the patient to remain off the affected leg for several days. Recommending continued NSAID use for pain and swelling. Scheduling a follow-up appointment in two weeks to monitor progress. Advise the patient to contact the office if symptoms worsen or do not improve.
