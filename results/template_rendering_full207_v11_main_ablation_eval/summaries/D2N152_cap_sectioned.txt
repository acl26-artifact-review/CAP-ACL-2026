Chief Complaint
Back pain for approximately one week.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing back pain for approximately one week. He initially attributed it to a possible injury sustained while twisting, but the pain has persisted. He has tried over-the-counter medications, but they have not provided significant relief. The pain is located primarily in the lower back, with occasional radiation to the left side, and is exacerbated by sudden movements, particularly to the left. He also reports some irritation in his left eye, which he believes is related to the back pain. He has a history of skiing and occasionally visits the bakery owned by his family.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the lumbar region, particularly at the center of the lower back. Range of motion is limited. The patient reports pain with lifting his right leg and pain with lifting his left leg. Lung sounds are clear. Heart sounds are regular. No edema or tenderness in the lower extremities. Reflexes are intact bilaterally. The patient's face appears grimaced.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical examination findings are consistent with a musculoskeletal low back strain. Further evaluation with a lumbar x-ray is warranted to rule out other pathology.

Plan
I am ordering a low back x-ray to assess for any structural abnormalities. I am also ordering physical therapy to help with strengthening and pain management. I am prescribing ibuprofen 400mg every 4-6 hours as needed for pain, and flexeril 375mg at bedtime as needed for muscle relaxation. Please continue with your current diet and exercise regimen. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess your progress.
