Chief Complaint
A cough that started about a week ago, accompanied by yellow sputum and feeling unwell.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a cough that began approximately one week ago, accompanied by yellow sputum production and a general feeling of malaise. He states that his condition has worsened over the past two days. He denies fever or chills, but reports mild aches. He reports no shortness of breath, but does experience nasal congestion and sinus pressure. He reports no sick contacts in his household. The patient works as a teacher and is a Brownie fan. He reports twisting his right knee while coughing and falling down four or five stairs. He describes the injury as causing pain to palpation of the right medial knee and decreased flexion and extension. He is currently taking over-the-counter cough and cold medication, but not other medications. He is not monitoring his blood sugars and is taking metformin 1000 mg twice daily as prescribed by his wife. He is not watching his salt intake.

Findings
Physical examination reveals coarse rhonchi bilaterally, erythema and edema of the nasal turbinates bilaterally with associated yellow discharge, bilateral cervical lymphadenopathy, and pain to palpation of the bilateral maxillary sinuses. The patient's temperature is 98.4°F, blood pressure is 125/62 mmHg, heart rate is 88 bpm, and oxygen saturation is 97% on room air. A slight limp is noted. Examination of the right knee reveals pain to palpation and decreased flexion and extension, with ecchymosis to the right medial knee.

Assessment
The patient presents with a likely viral syndrome with mild bronchitis, evidenced by the cough and rhonchi. The right knee injury is consistent with a medial collateral ligament strain.

Plan
I recommend guaifenesin 600 mg twice daily to help with sputum clearance. I will prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for the right knee pain and order a right knee x-ray to evaluate the injury. I will also order a lipid panel and a hemoglobin A1c to monitor cardiovascular and metabolic health. I will refer the patient to physical therapy for rehabilitation of the right knee. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist. I will also instruct the patient to monitor his blood sugars and report them to me through the patient portal.
