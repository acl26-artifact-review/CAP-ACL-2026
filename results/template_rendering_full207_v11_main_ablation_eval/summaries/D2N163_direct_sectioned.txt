Chief Complaint
Newly diagnosed hypercholesterolemia.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being concerned about a recent annual checkup finding of elevated cholesterol. He states that a finger-prick test revealed an elevated level, prompting a clinic visit. He reports enjoying cycling and riding trails, and consumes a diet that includes cookies. He recalls his grandparents mentioning similar issues in their youth, but they did not have it checked at a young age. He reports feeling generally healthy and active.

Findings
Physical exam findings include normal blood pressure, clear lung sounds, normal thyroid exam, and no hepatosplenomegaly. Cholesterol level was elevated at 210 mg/dL. Glucose, complete metabolic panel, thyroid studies, and liver panels are pending. Genetic testing is also ordered.

Assessment
Elevated cholesterol.

Plan
Order a full cholesterol panel, glucose panel, complete metabolic panel, thyroid studies, liver panels, and genetic testing. Refer to a nutritionist for dietary counseling. Schedule a follow-up cholesterol level in three to six months after dietary changes are implemented. Continue encouraging regular physical activity, including cycling.
