Chief Complaint
Patient reports elevated blood pressure and concerns regarding potential kidney involvement.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing elevated blood pressure, which has been increasing since starting new medications prescribed by his PCP. He has been consistently monitoring his blood pressure at home, noting readings as high as 170/90 mmHg. He reports taking his prescribed medications as directed and experiencing some mild headaches when his blood pressure is elevated. He also reports enjoying watching football games and consuming a significant amount of pizza, wings, and subs during football season. He occasionally takes acetaminophen for minor pain. He reports a family history of hypertension and kidney disease. He is currently taking norvasc 10mg daily and carvedilol 25mg twice daily, and previously took lisinopril.

Findings
Vital signs reveal a blood pressure of 169/74 mmHg, heart rate of 88 bpm, and oxygen saturation of 98%. Physical exam reveals no jugular venous distension or carotid bruits. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. A small amount of 1+ pitting edema is noted bilaterally. The patient reports no shortness of breath with exertion.

Assessment
Uncontrolled hypertension. Possible multifactorial etiology, including medication adjustments and lifestyle factors.

Plan
Order a renal artery ultrasound to evaluate for vascular obstruction. Order morning aldosterone and 24-hour urine collections to assess adrenal gland function. Prescribe cardura 4mg daily. Refer to a registered dietitian for dietary counseling regarding sodium reduction and portion control. Counsel the patient to reduce alcohol consumption to no more than two drinks per week. Instruct the patient to maintain a log of blood pressure readings taken at home, recording the time and reading for each measurement, and bring the log to the next follow-up appointment. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to assess response to treatment.
