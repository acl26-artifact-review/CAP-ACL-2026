Chief Complaint
Follow-up for hypertension and discussion of alcohol use.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports struggling with not drinking, having last had a drink on Monday and drinking four to five times a week hard alcohol. She reports taking half of the lisinopril due to feeling lethargic and tired after taking it, and reports no headaches or swooshing after taking lisinopril. She reports taking the full dose of hydrochlorothiazide and denies chest tightness or pressure or shortness of breath. She checks her blood pressure at home, reporting numbers ranging from 130 to 145 or 150 over 95 to 100 in the morning and 130 to 145 or 150 over 95 to 100 in the evening. She takes her blood pressure medications in the morning. She denies fever, cough, shortness of breath, or sore throat and reports no recent travel or contact with the coronavirus. She received the coronavirus vaccine on February 9th. She reports swelling in her ankles and feet.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no acute distress. Auscultation of the heart is regular with no murmurs. The patient's extremities are not swollen. Blood pressure readings at home range from 130 to 145 or 150 over 95 to 100 in the morning and 130 to 145 or 150 over 95 to 100 in the evening.

Assessment
Alcohol use disorder. Hypertension.

Plan
We will initiate naltrexone, which blocks brain receptors that alcohol stimulates and helps with both urges and constant thinking about alcohol, starting with a monthly injection. The potential benefit of alcohol cessation would be highly beneficial to her for many reasons. We will schedule a follow-up in June to assess her progress. I recommend upgrading influenza vaccination and shingrix. Furthermore, we will continue her current medications, lisinopril 10 milligrams daily and hydrochlorothiazide 12.5 milligrams daily, and monitor her blood pressure regularly. We will also follow up with a repeat blood pressure check at home.
