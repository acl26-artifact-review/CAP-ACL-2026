S:
The patient reports a history of knee injury from skiing two weeks ago, describing a pop and instability in his right knee. He states that his left knee feels unstable but doesn't hurt as much. He reports pain on the inside of his right knee with palpation and pain with range of motion, particularly with flexion. He describes a mild limp and reports increased pain with prolonged walking. He uses a brace and has been using THC cream for the pain. He denies any other medications besides THC gummies.

O:
Physical exam reveals mild swelling and bruising on the right knee. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Gait reveals a slight limp. Neurological exam is unremarkable. X-rays show evidence of mild arthritis and no fracture.

A:
The patient has a strained posterior cruciate ligament in his right knee, likely exacerbated by previous injury and contributing to the current pain and instability. He also has mild arthritis.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for right knee pain and ibuprofen 800mg twice daily as needed for right knee. Recommend physical therapy three times per week to improve strength and stability. Advise against skiing or gymnastics for now. Schedule follow-up in two weeks to assess progress.
