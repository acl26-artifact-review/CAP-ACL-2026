Chief Complaint
Right back pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the right back that began approximately one week ago. He describes the pain as sudden onset while working in the barn moving hay. He initially thought it was from the hay but experienced nausea and sweating, prompting him to seek medical attention. He has a family history of kidney stones, with his father having experienced them in the past. He has not had a kidney stone himself. He reports the pain radiating down to his groin, but not all the way. The pain has subsided since the initial episode, but he continues to experience it intermittently. He has been straining his urine and has been using a strainer to monitor it. He denies seeing blood in his urine.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation on the right side of the abdomen. No rebound tenderness or guarding is present. Vital signs are within normal limits. The patient reports nausea and sweating.

Assessment
The patient has a kidney stone. Examination findings are consistent with a kidney stone.

Plan
The doctor ordered a urine culture and a BMP. The doctor prescribed oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours for pain and Tylenol as needed. The doctor scheduled a follow-up appointment in one to two weeks to monitor the stone's passage. The doctor recommended continuing to use the urine strainer.
