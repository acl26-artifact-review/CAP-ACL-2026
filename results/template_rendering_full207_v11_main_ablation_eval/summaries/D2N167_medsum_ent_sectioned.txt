Chief Complaint
Left knee pain following a fall while running with her niece.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that she injured her left knee while running with her niece, who was learning to ride a bike. She fell and believes she twisted her knee in an unusual way approximately one week ago. Since the injury, she has experienced limping and has been unable to bear weight fully on the left leg. The pain initially rated as a 'four' but has progressed to a 'throbbing' sensation, now rated as a 'four' and potentially worsening. She has been taking Tylenol for the pain, but it provides only temporary relief. She also took an ulcer medication recently, which precludes the use of ibuprofen. She reports using kinesiology tape for support. The patient describes the pain as being more pronounced on the medial aspect of the knee and less so on the lateral aspect.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate edema and effusion in the left knee. Range of motion is limited, with pain on flexion and extension. Lachman's and valgus/varus tests are negative. A chest x-ray was performed and revealed no fractures or bony abnormalities. The patient's vital signs are stable.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and physical examination, she likely has an MCL strain.

Plan
I am prescribing tramadol 50mg twice daily as needed for breakthrough pain, along with Tylenol as needed. I am also recommending a knee immobilizer for one week to reduce inflammation and promote healing. The patient should ice the knee three to four times a day for 20-30 minutes. I advise against running for approximately three weeks to allow the ligament to heal. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately one month to reassess her condition and determine if further imaging, such as an MRI, is necessary.
