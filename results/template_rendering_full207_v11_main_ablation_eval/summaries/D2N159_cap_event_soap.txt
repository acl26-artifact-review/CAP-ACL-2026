S:
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his right hand, primarily affecting the thumb, index, and middle fingers, for approximately six months with increasing persistence over the past month. He reports some pain with wrist extension and when pulling his wrist up towards him. The patient reports pain when typing on the keyboard and when moving his mouse. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain.

O:
The doctor identifies a positive Tinel's sign. The patient reports decreased grip strength on the right hand. The doctor observes grimacing when touching the wrist.

A:
The doctor diagnoses carpal tunnel syndrome.

P:
The doctor orders a wrist splint to be worn at night. The doctor prescribes ibuprofen up to 600 milligrams four times a day. A nerve conduction study will be ordered.
