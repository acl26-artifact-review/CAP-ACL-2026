S:
The patient reports feeling better now, but last week he was very sick with cough, mucus, and shortness of breath with a low-grade fever. He reports frequent pneumonia episodes, approximately monthly or every other month over the past six months. He notes symptoms start when unloading hay. He denies any personal or family history of allergies. He does not smoke and does not live with someone who smokes. He has no personal history of allergies. He reports ten siblings. He is the only one who seems to be getting sick.

O:
The patient's respiratory rate is 20 breaths per minute with oxygen saturation of 99% on room air. Physical exam reveals fine rales in the lungs but no wheezes. There is no clubbing of the fingers. Chest x-ray reveals round glass opacities.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with hypersensitivity pneumonitis, also known as farmer's lung, likely triggered by exposure to hay. The round glass opacities on the chest x-ray support this diagnosis.

P:
Rescribe a course of oral steroids to reduce inflammation in the lungs. Order a CT scan of the lungs to confirm the diagnosis and assess the severity of the lung damage. Order a pulmonary function test to assess respiratory impairment. Advise the patient to eliminate or prevent exposure to hay, or if that is not possible, to wear a respirator when moving hay. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
