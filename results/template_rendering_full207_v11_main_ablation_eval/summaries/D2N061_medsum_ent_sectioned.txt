Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a basketball injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a basketball injury where he fell to the ground after being bumped by another player. He states he landed on his side and was unable to continue playing. He reports ice and an ace wrap were applied immediately following the injury. The pain has persisted for seven days. He takes metformin 500mg twice daily and checks his blood sugars every morning. He denies any prior knee injuries or surgeries.

Findings
Physical examination reveals right knee swelling and effusion. Palpation of the medial aspect of the right knee elicits tenderness. Range of motion is decreased, particularly with flexion. Negative varus and valgus stress tests. No palpable pop on examination.

Assessment
Right knee medial collateral ligament strain.

Plan
Prescribe a right knee brace and meloxicam 15mg daily. Refer to physical therapy for rehabilitation. Advise patient to continue monitoring blood sugars and to follow up in seven days for re-evaluation. Instruct patient to discontinue nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory medications. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week.
