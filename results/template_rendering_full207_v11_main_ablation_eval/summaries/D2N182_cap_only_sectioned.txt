Chief Complaint
Left hand pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand. The patient reports that the pain is worsening and that it is affecting his ability to perform daily activities. The patient reports that the pain started approximately one week ago and has been getting worse over time. The patient denies any recent trauma. The patient reports that he has tried over-the-counter pain relievers, but they have not provided any relief. The patient also reports feeling some muscle spasms in his left hand.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation in the distal left hand. Range of motion is limited. There is no swelling or erythema. Blood pressure is 142/88. Pulse is 78. Respiratory rate is 16. Temperature is 98.6°F. White blood cell count is elevated at 13,000/mm3.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are concerning for inflammation and possible musculoskeletal issues. The elevated white blood cell count warrants further investigation to rule out infection.

Plan
Order complete blood work to evaluate the elevated white blood cell count. Recommend physical therapy to address the musculoskeletal issues. Advise the patient to continue over-the-counter pain relievers as needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to monitor progress.
