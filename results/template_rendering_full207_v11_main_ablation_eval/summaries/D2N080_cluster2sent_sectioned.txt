Chief Complaint
Right knee pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing right knee pain, with a history of a pop sound at the time of injury. He denies any specific trauma and reports no pain with palpation of the medial knee. The patient uses acetaminophen for pain relief and has considered using a cane for support. He is currently taking farxiga, amlodipine, lisinopril, hydrochlorothiazide, metformin, and reports taking these medications as directed.

Findings
Physical examination reveals no ecchymosis or edema, no effusion, and no pain to palpation of the left medial knee. Range of motion is decreased, but within acceptable limits. The patient reports feeling able to fully move the knee as they should. The patient's vital signs are stable, including a temperature of 98.2°F, pulse of 72, respirations of 16, and blood pressure of 120/70. A slight two over six systolic ejection murmur is appreciated at the left base. The left knee x-ray showed no evidence of fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's right knee pain is likely due to arthritis.

Plan
I will prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily and order physical therapy to strengthen the muscles around the knee. I will also order an echocardiogram to evaluate the murmur and continue the lisinopril at 20 mg daily. I will order a hemoglobin A1c to assess diabetes management and a lipid panel. I will have my nurse schedule the echocardiogram and arrange for its completion.
