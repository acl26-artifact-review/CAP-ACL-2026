S:
The patient reports numbness and tingling in his right hand, primarily affecting the thumb, index, and middle fingers, for approximately six months with increasing persistence over the past month. He states the pain is most prominent when typing and using a mouse, and it is exacerbated by wrist extension and flexion. He reports some pain but mostly when typing and using the mouse. He denies any pain or symptoms at night. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain.

O:
The physician observes decreased grip strength in the right hand. A positive Tinel's sign is identified at the wrist. The patient's right hand demonstrates tenderness to palpation with wrist flexion and extension. The patient's right wrist shows mild warmth.

A:
The primary problem is carpal tunnel syndrome. The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with this diagnosis.

P:
The doctor orders an EMG to evaluate nerve conduction. The doctor orders a wrist splint to be worn at night. The doctor prescribes ibuprofen up to 600 milligrams four times a day. The doctor recommends physical therapy if the EMG results are positive or if symptoms do not improve with conservative management.
