Chief Complaint
Left knee pain following a skiing injury two weeks ago.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left knee pain that began two weeks ago after a skiing injury. He describes falling while skiing and hitting a tree, resulting in a twisting injury. He reports hearing a pop at the time of the injury. The patient states that he regularly skis three times a week and enjoys skiing. He describes a high pain tolerance. He reports that his left knee feels unstable and reports mild swelling. He denies any recent trauma. He reports using THC cream for pain relief. He has had previous reconstructive knee surgery fourteen years ago due to a gymnastics injury. He also had back surgery as a teenager. He currently takes THC gummies.

Findings
Examination reveals mild swelling and bruising on the right knee. The patient demonstrates a slight limp. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Palpation of the medial aspect of the right knee elicits tenderness. Dorsiflexion causes pain, while plantar flexion does not. The patient reports a shooting pain radiating down to the ankle with knee flexion. There is mild swelling and tenderness noted on the right knee.

Assessment
The patient has a strain of the posterior cruciate ligament in the right knee. Clinical findings are consistent with a history of prior knee surgery and a high pain tolerance.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam fifteen milligrams once daily and ibuprofen eight hundred milligrams twice daily as needed. Recommend physical therapy to improve strength and range of motion. Advise the patient to avoid skiing and gymnastics for now. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress. Wear a brace to provide support.
