Chief Complaint
Ongoing management of psoriatic arthritis.

History of Present Illness
Judy Gomez presents for ongoing management of psoriatic arthritis. She reports decreasing her prednisone dose, currently on one pill daily, which appears to be effective. She occasionally takes an extra dose when experiencing increased pain, particularly in her feet, often related to periods of increased activity. She reports experiencing COVID symptoms a month ago, including significant pain, and was advised to delay the second vaccine dose until a week later. She experienced significant pain with the first dose and sought assistance from a hotline, who recommended continuing with the second dose. She has been taking methotrexate, which has helped reduce joint pain. She has recently noticed bruising, which she attributes to the prednisone. She reports these bruises appear without her noticing them.

Findings
The patient reports a good range of motion in her toes and nails, with visible psoriatic plaques noted on her nails. She reports mild joint pain in her feet and left shoulder, particularly with overhead activity. Examination reveals no acute distress.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is psoriatic arthritis, with associated musculoskeletal symptoms and recent bruising.

Plan
Continue methotrexate therapy. Discontinue prednisone as planned, monitoring for symptom recurrence. Schedule blood work for follow-up. Arrange an appointment for the patient to return in three months for a follow-up visit.
