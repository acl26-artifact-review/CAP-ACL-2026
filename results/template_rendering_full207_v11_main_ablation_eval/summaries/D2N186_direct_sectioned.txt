Chief Complaint
Left back pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent left lower back pain, which he attributes to a microdiscectomy performed in November 2020 where his disc broke off and wrapped around his spinal thecal sac. He describes the pain as nerve-like, with severe pain radiating down his leg. He was highly dosed on gabapentin, which he found difficult to withdraw from. The pain is intermittent, sometimes occurring randomly on either side, and is exacerbated by bending over. He has tried stretching, which provides relief, and ibuprofen 800 mg daily. He denies any recent trauma. He has previously received physical therapy at the VA.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal dorsiflexion and extension of the feet. There is no pain with palpation on the right side of the spine around the L5 level. No pain with palpation on the left side. The patient reports no nerve pain. An X-ray demonstrates no fractures or abnormalities in the spinal bone, consistent with healing from the previous surgery.

Assessment
The patient has a musculoskeletal strain secondary to spinal misalignment.

Plan
Prescribe physical therapy at the VA for musculoskeletal management. Prescribe meloxicam instead of ibuprofen for pain management. Order a referral to pain management for further evaluation and consideration of non-narcotic pain management strategies.
