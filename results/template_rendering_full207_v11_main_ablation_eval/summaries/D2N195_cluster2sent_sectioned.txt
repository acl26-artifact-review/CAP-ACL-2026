Chief Complaint
Patient reports fatigue and knee pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue for the past two months and knee pain after playing basketball. The patient describes the knee pain as achy and states the swelling has decreased overnight. The patient tried tylenol for the pain and reports a pain level of four on a scale of one to ten. The patient denies blood in stool.

Findings
The clinician performed a physical exam revealing pain to palpation of the medial aspect of the right knee, edema, and ecchymosis. The x-ray of the right knee was normal. The patient's blood pressures are typically in the one forties to one fifties range. The patient's blood sugars are typically around one fifty when taken.

Assessment
The patient has an MCL strain due to twisting the knee during basketball.

Plan
The clinician prescribed tylenol fifteen milligrams and a knee immobilizer. The clinician is prescribing meloxicam fifteen milligrams. The clinician is increasing norvasc to five milligrams. The clinician wants the patient to start taking blood sugars daily. The clinician wants to consult with a nutritionist.
