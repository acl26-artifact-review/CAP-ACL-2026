Chief Complaint
The patient reports symptoms of vaginal discomfort, discharge, and odor, and seeks evaluation for sexually transmitted infections.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing vaginal discomfort, a change in vaginal discharge, and an odor in her vaginal region over the past week. She also reports pain on the right side of her vagina and denies fever or chills. She recently learned that her partner has been involved in sexual encounters with multiple partners. She also reports an abnormal pap smear in the past and is concerned about potential STIs.

Findings
A physical exam revealed no external lesions. Swabs were obtained for testing for gonorrhea, chlamydia, and trichomoniasis. Blood samples were drawn for syphilis, HIV, and hepatitis C testing. Pelvic exam findings were unremarkable, with no cervical motion tenderness or adnexal tenderness or masses noted.

Assessment
Screening for sexually transmitted infections is indicated.

Plan
I discussed with the patient her high risk status due to a prior abnormal pap smear and her partner's history of multiple sexual partners. I ordered testing for gonorrhea, chlamydia, trichomoniasis, syphilis, HIV, and hepatitis C. I will follow up on the results of these tests and provide appropriate treatment as indicated.
