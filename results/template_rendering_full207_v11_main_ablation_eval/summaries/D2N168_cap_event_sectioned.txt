Chief Complaint
Follow-up for high blood pressure and related symptoms.

History of Present Illness
Diane is a 28-year-old female with a history of depression and hypertension who presents for follow-up after experiencing episodes of high blood pressure, particularly when traveling. She reports that her blood pressure has been running high approximately once a week, often coinciding with periods of travel. She's been monitoring her blood pressure at home but admits to not consistently doing so when traveling. She also reports experiencing headaches and lightheadedness related to these episodes. She's been attending weekly therapy sessions and finds them helpful. Her support system is strong, including her boyfriend, mother, and brother. She's been taking lisinopril as prescribed.

Findings
Physical exam reveals blood pressure of 192/110 mmHg. A grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur is auscultated. Trace pitting edema is noted bilaterally. An EKG shows changes consistent with chronic hypertension. An echocardiogram reveals a slightly leaky heart valve and stable heart murmur. No carotid bruits are present. Nasal congestion is noted.

Assessment
The patient presents with uncontrolled hypertension and associated symptoms, including headaches and lightheadedness. She has a history of depression, currently managed with therapy, and a slightly leaky heart valve.

Plan
Increase lisinopril to 40 mg daily. Continue monitoring blood pressure through the patient portal. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to medication and adjust as needed. Refer to physical therapy for musculoskeletal support.
