Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain on the outside of his arm, which began approximately six weeks ago following various yard projects. He describes the pain as persistent and exacerbated by overhead activities and when lying on his affected side. He reports that he had a similar injury to his right shoulder when playing baseball in his youth. He has been taking Advil and icing the area, which provides some relief. He denies any numbness. He also reports foot and ankle pain, attributing it to the yard work, and has been trying Epsom salt soaks to alleviate it.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder. Tenderness is noted at the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Neuvascular exam shows capillary refill less than 3 seconds and intact sensation. Shoulder x-ray is negative for fracture. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits: blood pressure 116/84, heart rate 62, respiratory rate 16, oxygen saturation 99%.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy of the left shoulder.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI to further evaluate the shoulder and rule out other pathology. I am also referring the patient to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. I recommend continuing the use of acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. If symptoms do not improve, we can consider a steroid injection to the shoulder. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
