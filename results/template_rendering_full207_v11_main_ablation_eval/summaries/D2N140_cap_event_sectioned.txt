Chief Complaint
Irritable bowel syndrome with functional abdominal pain and nausea.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of IBS, diagnosed in 2019, with flares triggered by spicy foods, fatty foods, and alcohol. She describes experiencing cramping and nausea, often accompanied by diarrhea, particularly after eating out. She has been using bentyl as needed, and zofran when needed. She has been trying to avoid alcohol and spicy foods, but reports that it is difficult to avoid them when socializing. She has been using citrucel twice daily, but reports that it is not always effective. She has been drinking water instead of sodas, and reports that it has helped.

Findings
The patient is alert and cooperative. The abdomen is soft, non-tender, and has normal active bowel sounds. No masses or organomegaly are palpated. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
Irritable bowel syndrome, likely exacerbated by dietary triggers.

Plan
Start elavil 25mg nightly. Continue bentyl PRN as needed, up to four times daily. Encourage dietary modifications, avoiding high-fat and spicy foods and alcohol. Follow up in one month via mychart to assess response to treatment and adjust medications as needed. A repeat prescription for bentyl has been sent to the pharmacy.
