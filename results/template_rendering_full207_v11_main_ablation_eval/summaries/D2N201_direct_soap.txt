S:
The patient reports experiencing back pain for approximately two years, initially following a basketball injury involving a fall. He has tried various over-the-counter pain medications and physical therapy, but reports limited relief. The pain is described as sharp and throbbing intermittently. He reports that it radiates down his left leg and occasionally causes numbness in his toes. He states that lying on his side provides some relief, while standing exacerbates the pain. He reports experiencing urinary incontinence once, associated with prolonged standing.

O:
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation at L4-L5, with moderate right paraspinal tenderness. Straight leg raise testing is positive on the right. Neurological examination demonstrates decreased sensation to light touch at the L4 dermatome. Vital signs are within normal limits. MRI findings demonstrate a disk desiccation, disk bulge with paracentral disk herniation resulting in moderate right for neural foraminal stenosis.

A:
Based on the patient's history, physical examination findings, and MRI results, the patient's primary problem is chronic lumbar back pain secondary to a herniated disk at L4-L5 with radiculopathy. The patient's symptoms are consistent with nerve root compression.

P:
I recommend a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and provide symptomatic relief. I will prescribe a medication to help with anxiety if needed. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment. I will recommend physical therapy to improve core strength and stability.
