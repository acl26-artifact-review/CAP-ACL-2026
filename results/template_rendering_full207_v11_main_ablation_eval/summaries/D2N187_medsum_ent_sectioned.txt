Chief Complaint
Sudden visual distortions and blurring of vision.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sudden visual distortions and blurring of vision. He states he has been experiencing a decrease in vision when reading and straight lines have appeared crooked. He reports difficulty adjusting to dim lighting in restaurants, specifically mentioning trouble at a favorite restaurant, McDonald's in Arlington. The patient reports smoking a pack of cigarettes daily and has attempted to quit but has struggled. He reports irregularly checking his blood pressure and taking medication as remembered.

Findings
The anterior segment examination reveals posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes. The cornea is clear. Cup to disc ratio is .2. Fundus examination of the right eye shows the retina attached, 360 degrees, without tears or holes. The fovea is clear. Fundus examination of the left eye shows the retina attached, 360 degrees, without tears or holes. The fovea is clear. OCT reveals drusen deposit and retinal pigment epithelium subretinal fluid accumulation. Fluorescein angiography reveals focal areas of leakage in the right eye consistent with neurovascular age-related macular degeneration. Blood pressure was not readily available.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with neurovascular age-related macular degeneration.

Plan
Administer an injection of Lucentis once a month into the vitreous portion of the eye after numbing. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment and monitor for any further vision loss. Advise the patient to continue monitoring blood pressure regularly and to maintain a log of readings. Refer to a physical therapist for evaluation and treatment of shoulder pain.
