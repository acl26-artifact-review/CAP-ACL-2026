S:
The patient reports being surprised by a positive hepatitis C diagnosis after a routine screening. He states he has been feeling fine and that he used to party and did use intravenous drugs but has been clean for over fifteen years. He admits to occasional beer consumption and smoking one to two cigarettes per day. The patient reports having high blood pressure, diabetes, and depression in his family history. He denies any other medical conditions.

O:
Vitals are within normal limits. Physical exam reveals a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur, clear lung sounds without wheezes or rales, and normal bowel sounds. Liver panel shows an elevated AST of 39, with normal albumin and bilirubin. The patient appears in no distress and has no jaundice.

A:
The patient has a confirmed diagnosis of hepatitis C. He is surprised by the positive result given his history and lack of prior knowledge. He has a history of intravenous drug use and family history of hypertension, diabetes, and depression.

P:
Order hepatitis C RNA and HCV genotype testing. Order an ultrasound elastography of the liver. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to discuss treatment options based on the results. Refer the patient to his primary care physician to coordinate care and schedule follow-up appointments.
