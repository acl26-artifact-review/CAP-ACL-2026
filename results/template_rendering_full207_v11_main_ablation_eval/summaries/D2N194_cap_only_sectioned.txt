Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain that waxes and wanes, particularly when lying on their left side. He reports a pain level of 7 without any tylenol and a pain level of 2-3 with tylenol. The patient reports pain in the left shoulder and left hand. The patient has had aches and pains. The patient denies any trauma over the past three weeks. The patient has been renovating their basement over the past few weeks.

Findings
Examination reveals reduced active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
A cervical MRI is planned. The patient should take tylenol p.r.n. The patient takes extra strength tylenol every six to eight hours. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
