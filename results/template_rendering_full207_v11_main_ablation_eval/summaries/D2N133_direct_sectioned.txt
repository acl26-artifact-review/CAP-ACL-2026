Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following swimming.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain that began approximately one month ago following an increase in swimming frequency. He describes using the breaststroke, which he believes contributes to the pain. The pain is exacerbated after approximately two laps of swimming and has progressed to the point where he can only swim ten laps before experiencing discomfort. He denies any injury or trauma. He reports that his chronic back pain has been well-controlled with current medication and therapy, and his depression has been stable with medication and therapy and outside activity. He reports sleeping well and eating well. His carpal tunnel release has been stable with full range of motion and no flare-ups.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild patellar tenderness to palpation and pain with patellar inhibition. The patient's heart rhythm is regular, lungs are clear to auscultation, and there is no palpable adenopathy. Grip strength is equal bilaterally. X-ray of the right knee demonstrates normal findings. No joint effusion is noted.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with chondromalacia of the patella secondary to overuse and swimming technique.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 600 milligrams four times daily with food for symptomatic relief. Recommend straight leg raising exercises to strengthen the quadriceps. Advise avoidance of lunges and the breaststroke swimming technique. Suggest alternative swimming strokes with straight knees. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks if symptoms do not improve. Continue current antidepressant medication and therapy.
