S:
The patient reports difficulty urinating over the past six months, with occasional feeling of incomplete bladder emptying. He reports waking up three to four times per night to urinate. He denies any burning with urination. He reports occasional diarrhea last week. He has no prior history of urinary retention.

O:
A slight 3/6 systolic ejection murmur is appreciated at the left base. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. Abdomen is nontender and nondistended. Prostate exam reveals an enlarged prostate without masses.

A:
The patient presents with difficulty urinating, potentially related to an enlarged prostate. Further evaluation by a urologist is warranted.

P:
Order PSA. Start flomax 0.4 mg daily. Refer to urology for evaluation. Order routine blood tests including a urinalysis and urine culture. Order an echocardiogram to follow up on the murmur. Continue current medications: Lipitor 40 mg daily, Aspirin, and Metoprolol. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 3-4 weeks.
