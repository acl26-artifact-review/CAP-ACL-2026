The patient reports fatigue and occasional feverish chills, particularly with activity, and has noted cold intolerance and frequent chilling sensations. She also reports worsening anxiety and depression symptoms. She states she has been anemic since childhood and that her previous doctors suspect internal bleeding. She had a hysterectomy in 1996 and reports no heavy periods since. She last had a colonoscopy approximately five to six years ago, which was unremarkable. She denies any history of sedation issues or significant problems with sedation in the past. She drinks alcohol occasionally, approximately twice a year. She is currently taking ferrous sulfate 25 mg twice daily with vitamin C from orange juice, and over-the-counter vitamin B12.

O:
Blood pressure is within normal limits. Heart rate is 78 bpm. Physical exam reveals no tenderness on palpation of the abdomen. Neurological exam is unremarkable. Laboratory results previously showed low hemoglobin, stable hematocrit, and normal iron labs with low ferritin.

A:
The patient's symptoms and history are consistent with persistent iron deficiency anemia, potentially complicated by underlying gastrointestinal bleeding. Further investigation is warranted to determine the source of blood loss.

P:
Order an upper endoscopy and colonoscopy. Send a referral to Dr. Flores, a hematologist, for evaluation and potential intravenous iron supplementation if needed. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 8-12 weeks after iron supplementation to reassess CBC, iron, ferritin, B12, and folate levels. Provide instructions for bowel preparation prior to endoscopy. Advise patient to take ferrous sulfate with orange juice to enhance iron absorption.
