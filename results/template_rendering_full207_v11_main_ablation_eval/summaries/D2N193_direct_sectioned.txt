Chief Complaint
Cough

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a hacking cough for approximately three days, initially mild and now producing green phlegm. He states he was around his sister a few days ago who presented with similar symptoms, leading him to suspect exposure. He has been taking over-the-counter cough medication without significant relief. He denies fever, shortness of breath, nausea, or vomiting. He reports seasonal allergies around spring with sneezing and runny nose. He has no history of body aches. He has been monitoring his blood sugars with values around 120 mg/dL on the metformin 500 mg daily.

Findings
Blood pressure was elevated at 140/72 mmHg. The patient reports a top number in the forties. The patient is tolerating the hydrochlorothiazide. Lung exam reveals clear lung sounds with no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi. Bilateral maxillary sinus tenderness was noted to percussion. A two out of six systolic ejection murmur was auscultated at the left base. Extremities are clear of edema. The patient's temperature is not recorded.

Assessment
Upper respiratory infection, likely viral in etiology, given exposure history. Type 2 diabetes mellitus, currently managed with metformin.

Plan
Continue observation for viral resolution. Recommend Coricidin HBP for symptomatic relief. Increase hydrochlorothiazide to 25 mg daily. Schedule follow-up in four weeks for hemoglobin A1c monitoring and continued diabetes management. Consider COVID-19 testing if clinically indicated.
