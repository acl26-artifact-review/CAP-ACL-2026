Chief Complaint
Headache

History of Present Illness
Donna is a 37-year-old female presenting with a complaint of headache. She reports that the headaches have been worsening lately, occurring more frequently and with increased intensity. She notes that her blood pressure has been running high, and she has been taking it approximately once a week over the past three weeks. She admits to occasionally forgetting to take her lisinopril medication, particularly on weekends. Her blood sugar has been running at 120 and 100. She describes the headaches as a seven out of ten initially, which decreases to a four or five after taking Tylenol. She has had headaches before, but not like this one. She has not experienced congestion or runny nose along with the headache.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure readings were 160/87 and 175/92. Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the forehead and cheekbones, but no tenderness in the frontal or maxillary sinuses. Lymph nodes are not palpable. The patient reports persistent headaches.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with a headache disorder, likely exacerbated by hypertension and potentially related to underlying lifestyle factors.

Plan
I am going to increase the patient's lisinopril dose to 40 milligrams once daily. I will order some blood tests, including a complete blood count, a hemoglobin A1c, and other laboratory tests. I will also provide diabetes education to the patient. Please follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. The patient can continue taking Tylenol as needed. Please continue walking.
