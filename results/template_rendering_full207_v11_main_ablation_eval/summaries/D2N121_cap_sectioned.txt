Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a skiing injury five days ago. He describes a twisting injury while landing and subsequent swelling. He was able to get out of the water but it was very sore to climb up and any kind of squatting or bending is really pretty sore. He denies any popping sensation but felt a stretch and swelling. He has taken Aleve twice a day and applied a cold pack. He has no prior knee injuries. He is a frequent water skier and enjoys going every weekend when he can. He is not a baseball fan but his husband makes him watch the games all the time. The patient is taking lisinopril 20 milligrams a day and reports no side effects. He checks his blood pressure at home and it's been about 120/80 mmHg most days.

Findings
Physical exam reveals ecchymosis and edema in the right knee. Range of motion is decreased. Lachman's test and varus/valgus stress tests are negative. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable. The patient's blood pressure is 120/80 mmHg. Lung exam is clear bilaterally. Heart exam is normal. Neck exam is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's clinical presentation is most consistent with a medial collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
Order an x-ray of the right knee to rule out fracture. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily. Recommend physical therapy. Schedule a follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Order a lipid panel.
