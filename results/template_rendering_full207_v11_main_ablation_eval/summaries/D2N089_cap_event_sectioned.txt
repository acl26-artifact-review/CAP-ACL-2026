Chief Complaint
Annual exam and management of chronic conditions.

History of Present Illness
Andrea is a 52-year-old female with a past medical history of rheumatoid arthritis, atrial fibrillation, and reflux. She reports that her rheumatoid arthritis has been well-controlled with methotrexate, and she has been managing her atrial fibrillation with medication and lifestyle modifications. She has experienced occasional palpitations related to stress and reports that meditation and running with her dog have provided some relief. Her reflux has been well-controlled with protonix and dietary modifications, with no recent flare-ups. She reports nasal congestion related to allergies. She has been consistently taking her methotrexate weekly and has no issues with it. She has been doing regular exercise and yoga to manage her rheumatoid arthritis.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a systolic ejection murmur of 2/6, regular rate and rhythm, clear lung sounds, and edema and erythema of the right elbow with mild tenderness to palpation. The event monitor showed conversion pauses into atrial fibrillation. The autoimmune panel was within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's primary concerns are rheumatoid arthritis, atrial fibrillation, and related symptoms. Her rheumatoid arthritis is currently well-controlled. Her atrial fibrillation is intermittent and requires further evaluation and management.

Plan
Continue methotrexate 2.5 mg weekly. Refer to cardiology for cardiac ablation to address the atrial fibrillation. Order shoulder radiographs. Continue protonix 40 mg daily. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Schedule a follow-up appointment with the rheumatologist as needed.
