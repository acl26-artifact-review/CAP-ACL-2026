Chief Complaint
Chronic lumbar back pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of lumbar back pain for approximately two years, initially following furniture moving. The pain is described as sharp and stabbing, radiating down his right leg to his toe. He experiences numbness and tingling in his right leg and foot. He denies weakness and bowel or bladder dysfunction. He has tried physical therapy previously and was prescribed strong pain medications, but these provided only temporary relief. He was previously discussed with surgical options.

Findings
Physical examination reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness. Straight leg test is positive on the right. Neurological examination demonstrates decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at L4 dermatome. Knee and ankle reflexes are symmetrical. MRI reveals a disk herniation with nerve impingement. Vitals are stable and unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with an L4-L5 herniated disk with radiculopathy.

Plan
I am recommending a spinal injection with a corticosteroid to reduce inflammation and provide symptomatic relief. We will monitor the patient's response to the injection and adjust the treatment plan as needed. I have ordered a spinal injection with a corticosteroid and fluoroscopic guidance. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess the patient's progress.
