Chief Complaint
Low back pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports low back pain for approximately one week, initially attributed to a possible twisting injury. He tried over-the-counter medications but found no relief. He describes the pain as intermittent, with exacerbation during sudden movements, particularly to the left. He also reports occasional pain radiating to the left eye, and mentions increased pain when performing activities like skiing. The patient takes metformin twice daily for diabetes management and reports that his blood sugar levels have been trending higher recently. He exercises occasionally. He reports visiting the bakery frequently and consuming sweets.

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation in the lumbar region, with increased pain upon flexion and rotation of the lumbar spine. Pain is more pronounced in the center of the lower back and less so on the right side. Range of motion is limited. Lung sounds are clear. Heart rhythm is regular. Neurological exam reveals no sensory deficits or motor weakness in the lower extremities. The patient's feet are examined and show no signs of numbness or tingling. The patient's face appears gritty.

Assessment
Clinical findings are consistent with a musculoskeletal low back strain. Further investigation with imaging is warranted.

Plan
Ordered a low back x-ray. Prescribed ibuprofen 400mg every 6-8 hours as needed and acetaminophen 500mg every 4-6 hours as needed. Prescribed flexeril 375mg at bedtime. Ordered physical therapy to address muscle strengthening and improve range of motion. Advised the patient to continue with diet and exercise. Scheduled a follow-up appointment in four weeks if symptoms persist. Advised the patient to monitor blood sugar levels regularly and to continue taking metformin as prescribed.
