S:
The patient reports sharp pain in the right lower abdomen for several days, radiating to his groin, and describes it as constant with intermittent exacerbations. He reports nausea but denies vomiting. He states that the pain worsens with certain positions and that he has had previous kidney stones, but this one is taking longer to pass. He reports urine dark at times and some burning with urination. He has taken Tylenol for the pain without significant relief.

O:
Physical exam reveals positive CVA tenderness on the right flank. Abdomen is non-tender to palpation with no rebound or guarding. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with nephrolithiasis (kidney stone).

P:
I recommend increasing fluid intake to facilitate stone passage. I am prescribing oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours as needed for pain, along with a strainer to monitor stone passage. I have ordered a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks and discuss further treatment options, including lithotripsy or ureteroscopy, if symptoms persist.
