S:
The patient reports frequent lower respiratory infections, occurring approximately monthly or every other month over the past six months, often leading to visits to his primary care physician. He describes symptoms including cough, mucus production, and shortness of breath. He attributes these symptoms to exposure to hay, specifically when unloading hay for his brother. He reports a history of significant mucus production and low-grade fever last week. He denies a personal history of allergies and reports no family history of seasonal allergies.

O:
The patient's respiratory rate is 20 breaths per minute with oxygen saturation at 99% on room air. Lung exam reveals fine rales but no wheezes. Chest x-ray shows round glass opacities. The patient's blood pressure is within normal limits.

A:
The patient's symptoms are consistent with hypersensitivity pneumonitis, also known as farmer's lung, likely triggered by exposure to mold and bacteria in hay. The recurrent infections are concerning and require further investigation.

P:
I am prescribing a course of oral steroids to reduce inflammation in the lungs. I am ordering a CT scan of the lungs to confirm the diagnosis and a pulmonary function test to assess respiratory impairment. I recommend wearing a respirator when moving hay to prevent further lung damage. I will prescribe antibiotics to treat the current infection. Follow up in 4 weeks to assess response to treatment and adjust as needed.
