Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath primarily during mild exercise and walking up her driveway. She attributes this to potential exacerbation of her chronic back pain, though she's unsure of the direct link. She has recently increased her exercise regimen to combat weight gain following COVID-19. The patient reports occasional episodes of low mood, particularly on inclement days, and has been taking effexor for this. She continues to smoke approximately one cigarette per day and reports that she is taking gabapentin for chronic back pain, which provides some relief. She wishes she could commit to a yoga practice but hasn't established a habit.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild wheezes in all lung fields. The patient's diaphragm appears slightly flattened. Pulmonary function tests indicate a possible diagnosis of asthma or COPD, consistent with a history of smoking. Examination is otherwise unremarkable.

Assessment
Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

Plan
Prescribe combivent (two puffs twice daily) and albuterol (as needed for shortness of breath). Order a prednisone taper pack. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment. Advise the patient to continue taking effexor as prescribed.
