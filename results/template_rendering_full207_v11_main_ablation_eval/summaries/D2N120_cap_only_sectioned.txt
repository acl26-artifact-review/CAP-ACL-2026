Chief Complaint
Left leg pain and numbness

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a gradual onset of left leg pain and numbness over the past month. He describes the pain as sharp and stabbing, primarily in the calf, and notes numbness in his foot. The symptoms are exacerbated by prolonged standing or walking. He denies any recent trauma. He reports a history of l4 l5 herniated disk with radiculopathy and chronic lumbar back pain. He has previously taken strong pain medications. He used to enjoy playing golf and reports weight gain over the past years. He reports back pain with lumbar flexion. The patient reports decreased sensation to light touch. He reports tingling. The patient reports numbness. The patient reports pain.

Findings
Examination reveals decreased sensation to light touch in the left leg and foot. The patient reports numbness to the foot and reports tingling. The patient reports sharp, stabbing back pain.

Assessment
The patient has an l4 l5 herniated disk with radiculopathy and chronic lumbar back pain. The patient's symptoms are consistent with an L4-L5 herniated disk with radiculopathy.

Plan
A spinal injection with a strong inflammatory medication called a corticosteroid is recommended. The patient will be monitored. A spinal injection is ordered. The patient has previously tried physical therapy. The patient has previously been offered surgery. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
