Chief Complaint
Difficulty urinating.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty urinating over the past six months, with a perceived weakening of his urinary stream. He states that urination often feels incomplete, requiring extended periods of standing to fully empty his bladder. He reports waking up three to four times per night to urinate. He denies experiencing any burning during urination. He reports occasional diarrhea last week. He previously experienced urinary retention approximately six months ago but did not seek treatment at that time. He has had a stent placed in his right coronary artery in 2018 and reports no chest pain or shortness of breath. He is a former college football fan and is excited about the Georgia victory. He is currently managing his diabetes with metformin and taking lipitor and aspirin.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slight systolic ejection murmur at the left base, lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally, the abdomen is nontender and nondistended, and the prostate is enlarged. Hemoglobin A1c is pending.

Assessment
The patient presents with difficulty urinating, likely secondary to an enlarged prostate. He also has a history of coronary artery disease and well-controlled diabetes.

Plan
Order PSA, flomax 0.4 mg once daily, and refer to urology for further evaluation. Order an echocardiogram to follow up on the heart murmur. Continue metformin 1000 mg twice daily and lipitor 40 mg daily. Order a routine blood panel and urinalysis. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three to four weeks, or instruct the patient to contact the office with any concerns.
