S:
The patient reports back pain, taking ibuprofen and occasionally tylenol, and denies chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting. He reports no fever and no leg pain, numbness, or tingling. He states he had a previous lumbar discectomy.

O:
The patient's vital signs are stable, with blood pressure within normal limits. The back x-ray shows no fracture. Lab results are unremarkable. Physical exam reveals pain to palpation of the right lumbar spine, decreased flexion and extension of the back, and a positive straight leg raise on the right. Heart sounds are regular and rhythmic.

A:
The patient's presentation is consistent with a lumbar strain, likely related to his prior discectomy.

P:
I am prescribing meloxicam 15 milligrams once daily and ultram 50 milligrams every four hours as needed. I am ordering an MRI to evaluate the lumbar spine. I am referring the patient to physical therapy after the MRI results are available. Continue taking tylenol and ibuprofen as needed.
