S:
The patient reports a two-month history of a red, draining foot ulcer and intermittent throbbing, with no specific pain. He admits to not checking his blood sugar regularly and reports taking medication but not having picked it up from the pharmacy yet. He states the ulcer began suddenly and is concerned about its healing.

O:
The patient's right foot exhibits a one by two inch circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral foot, with redness, drainage, and mild edema. The x-ray showed no evidence of osteomyelitis. Vital signs are normal.

A:
The patient has a diabetic foot ulcer with signs of infection. The ulcer is likely related to his diabetes and poor glycemic control. A surgical shoe is recommended to protect the wound.

P:
Order an ABI to assess blood flow to the foot. Perform debridement of the wound. Prescribe clindamycin 400 mg twice daily for seven days. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess healing and consider further debridement. Arrange for a diabetic shoe to protect the foot during the healing process. Advise the patient to call the office with any concerns or emergencies.
