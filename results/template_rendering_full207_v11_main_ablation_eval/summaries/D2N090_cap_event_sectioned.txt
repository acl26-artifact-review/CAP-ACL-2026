Chief Complaint
Patient reports feeling woozy and elevated blood sugar levels.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 62-year-old male with a past medical history of depression, type 2 diabetes, and a kidney transplant. He presents today following an ER visit where he felt 'woozy' and reported elevated blood sugar readings. He admits to not consistently monitoring his blood sugar levels, particularly over the weekends when he's been busy with social activities and grabbing meals. He reports feeling better after returning home from the ER visit. He's been diligent about taking his immunotherapy medications as prescribed. He's been holding off on medical therapy and prefers to continue meditation for relaxation. He reports no chest pain or dizziness.

Findings
Physical exam reveals edema in the lower extremities. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, oxygen saturation 98% on room air. Glucose level is 162 mg/dL. Hemoglobin A1c is 8%.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is hyperglycemia, likely related to inconsistent blood sugar monitoring and lifestyle factors. He also has a history of type 2 diabetes and a kidney transplant.

Plan
The doctor wants to increase the patient's lantus to 20 units at night. The doctor orders a hemoglobin a1c in a couple of months. The patient should find time to relax in the afternoon. The patient wants to continue meditation. The nurse will be in soon to check the patient out.
