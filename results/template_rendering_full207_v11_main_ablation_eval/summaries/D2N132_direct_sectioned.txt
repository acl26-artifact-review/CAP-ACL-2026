Chief Complaint
Back pain following a fall at a surprise birthday party.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain following a fall while hiding during a surprise birthday party. He describes twisting his back while attempting to stand up and hitting the staircase railing. He denies radicular symptoms or leg pain. He reports some stiffness with rotation and increased pain with prolonged lying flat. He has a history of lumbar fusion performed previously. He currently takes ibuprofen for pain and takes medication for anxiety.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation of the lumbar paraspinal muscles. Range of motion is limited by pain with rotation. Neurological examination is unremarkable, with intact sensation and motor strength in the lower extremities. The x-ray demonstrates healing from a previous lumbar fusion at L4, without evidence of acute fractures or compression fractures.

Assessment
Low back strain secondary to acute injury.

Plan
Prescribe ibuprofen 800 mg three times daily with food. Recommend ice and heat alternating on the lumbar region. Encourage ambulation. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week if symptoms do not improve or worsen.
