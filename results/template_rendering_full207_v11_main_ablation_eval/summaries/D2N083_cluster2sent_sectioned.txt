Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right elbow pain that began approximately one week ago, coinciding with an increase in pottery making. The pain is exacerbated by wrist flexion and resisted movement. He denies any recent trauma. He has tried ibuprofen with minimal relief. The patient reports difficulty lifting and performing activities due to the pain, and it occasionally disrupts sleep. He has not previously injured this elbow.

Findings
Physical examination reveals moderate tenderness to palpation of the medial epicondyle. Pain is elicited with resisted wrist flexion and pronation. Range of motion is limited by pain. Vitals are stable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are consistent with medial epicondylitis.

Plan
Order a sling to be provided by the nurse, instruct the patient to apply ice packs to the elbow for 20 minutes, three times daily, and to take ibuprofen 600 mg every 6 hours as needed. Follow up in one week.
