Chief Complaint
Follow-up for hypertension.

History of Present Illness
The patient is a 72-year-old woman presenting for follow-up regarding hypertension. Her history includes significant alcohol use disorder and a prior myocardial infarction. At her last visit on April 16th, her blood pressure was 130/90. After presenting at 150/100, she was prescribed hydrochlorothiazide 12.5mg daily and lisinopril 20mg daily. CMP and CBC were essentially unremarkable. She reports struggling with not drinking and desires to discuss this further. She has been drinking four to five times a week to excess and has tried naltrexone previously. She only takes half the lisinopril due to feeling lethargic and experiencing headaches and swooshing in her left ear. She is currently taking her blood pressure medications in the morning.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no fever, cough, or shortness of breath. The patient is alert, pleasant, and in no acute distress. She has no tremor or CVA tenderness. Chest is clear to percussion and auscultation. Heart rhythm is regular with no murmur or gallop. JVP is flat at 90 degrees. Skin is moist and trigger is good. No signs of anxiety or depression. Blood pressure readings at home range from 130-150/95-100 in the morning and 130-145/70-80 in the evening.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with alcohol use disorder and hypertension.

Plan
Schedule a follow-up appointment for May 17th to administer the first dose of naltrexone. Recommend influenza vaccination and shingrix. Continue current medications: hydrochlorothiazide 12.5mg daily and lisinopril 20mg daily. Monitor blood pressure at home and at follow-up visits. Explore further discussion regarding alcohol cessation strategies.
