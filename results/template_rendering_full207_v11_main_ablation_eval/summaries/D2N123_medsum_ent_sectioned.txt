Chief Complaint
The patient reports difficulty swallowing solids over the past four to five weeks, accompanied by occasional pain and a sensation of needing assistance to push down solid foods.

History of Present Illness
The patient describes a gradual onset of difficulty swallowing, primarily with solids, beginning approximately four to five weeks ago. He reports feeling that solids 'just need a little help pushing it down' and experiences some pain when attempting to swallow. He denies coughing or choking episodes, and reports no blood in his stools or vomit. He denies any weight loss or chest pain or shortness of breath. The patient reports feeling generally well regarding his diabetes management, checking his blood sugars approximately 80% of the time and states he is taking his metformin as prescribed. He reports his blood pressure is typically around 120/50 mmHg at the office, but slightly higher at home. He is currently taking norvasc.

Findings
Physical examination reveals no visible swelling in the neck, a normal thyroid, and no palpable masses or lymphadenopathy. The throat appears normal with no redness or carotid bruits. Lung exam is clear, and the heart exam is normal with no murmurs. Abdominal exam reveals some epigastric tenderness. Neurological exam is unremarkable. Vital signs are not reported in this note.

Assessment
The patient's reported difficulty swallowing raises concern for a potential structural abnormality in the upper aerodigestive tract, possibly involving a narrowing or stricture of the esophagus. A gastroenterology consultation and esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EGD) are warranted to evaluate the cause of the swallowing difficulties.

Plan
I am ordering a repeat hemoglobin A1c, additional blood work including kidney function, and an ophthalmology referral for a dilated eye exam. I will also initiate a prescription for Prilosec 20 mg once daily to address potential reflux contributing to the swallowing difficulties. I recommend frequent chewing of food, particularly when consuming solid foods, and will provide instructions on recognizing and reporting potential emergencies. I am scheduling a follow-up appointment in one month to review the test results and assess treatment response. I will also send a refill request to the pharmacy for the patient's norvasc medication.
