S:
The patient reports an ankle injury sustained during basketball, describing a fall and twisting of his ankle. He states he's been having trouble walking and feels the ankle is unstable. He also reports being back into working out and not wanting to disrupt his routine. He mentions volunteering to coach starting in April.

O:
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lateral ankle, with brisk capillary refill. The x-ray demonstrates a displaced lateral malleolus fracture at the Weber C level, with no medial or posterior malleolar fractures.

A:
The patient has a lateral malleolar fracture requiring surgical intervention.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam for pain and swelling. Advise ice and elevation of the ankle. Recommend crutches for approximately three months. Schedule follow-up in three months to assess healing and facilitate return to activity, including coaching.
