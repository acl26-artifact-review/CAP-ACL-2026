Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing episodes of dyspnea, particularly with mild exertion and walking up inclines, accompanied by palpitations. She attributes this to possible exacerbation of chronic back pain. The patient reports that symptoms have increased in frequency recently. She has been taking effexor for depression and gabapentin for chronic back pain. She quit smoking approximately one month ago and reports using nicotine replacement therapy. She reports exercising more to combat weight gain from the COVID pandemic.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild wheezing in all lung fields. Chest x-ray demonstrates diaphragmatic flattening, consistent with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease. Pulmonary function tests indicate possible asthma or COPD.

Assessment
Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease

Plan
Prescribe combivent inhaler, two puffs twice daily. Prescribe albuterol inhaler, as needed. Prescribe prednisone, taper pack. Order follow-up in four weeks.
