S:
The patient reports a history of right knee pain for approximately two and a half weeks following a fall. He describes clicking and locking sensations, particularly with deep squats, and reports some pain with prolonged standing. He denies significant pain at rest and reports no pain with weight bearing. He reports taking ibuprofen but finds it only provides temporary relief. He also reports numbness in his right foot, attributed to footwear. The patient's primary complaint is the clicking and locking sensation during activity, leading to fear of injury. He reports symptoms worsening with prolonged standing or exertion, such as yard work.

O:
Physical exam reveals slight edema and effusion in the right knee. Palpation elicits no pain. Range of motion is full, though with some discomfort during deep flexion. Lachman's test is negative. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable. Knee x-ray demonstrates no acute fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
Based on the patient's history and physical exam findings, I suspect a lateral collateral ligament strain. The patient's report of clicking and locking, combined with the negative Lachman's test, suggests a strain rather than a complete tear. The absence of acute fracture on x-ray supports this assessment.

P:
I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain management. I am referring the patient to physical therapy to strengthen the knee and prevent future injuries. Advise the patient to continue icing the knee as tolerated. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress.
