Chief Complaint
A one-by-two centimeter circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot that is not healing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that the wound began approximately one year ago after a minor injury and initially improved with antibiotics, but has not healed. He reports no recent trauma. He has been taking keflex for five months. He describes increased drainage and fatigue. He reports decreased sensation in his foot.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a one-by-two centimeter circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot. The wound is covered with yellow slough and minimal granulation tissue. There is no surrounding erythema or cellulitis. No fluid collection or necrosis is present. There is no odor or bony exposure. The x-ray showed no evidence of osteomyelitis. Vitals are stable. Right foot ABI is pending.

Assessment
The patient has a chronic, non-healing diabetic foot ulcer requiring further evaluation and management.

Plan
I am ordering an ankle brachial index to assess blood flow to the foot. I will perform a debridement of the ulcer in the office today. I will prescribe a collagenase ointment for daily application to the wound and instruct the patient to cover it with a sterile dressing. A surgical shoe will be provided to minimize pressure on the wound. Follow-up will be in two weeks for reassessment and potential further debridement.
