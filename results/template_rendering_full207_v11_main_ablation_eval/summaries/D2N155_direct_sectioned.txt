Chief Complaint
A one-year-old non-healing foot ulcer.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a foot ulcer that began approximately one year ago, initially appearing as a blister that became infected and subsequently failed to heal despite prior antibiotic treatment. The patient reports feeling no pain but also notes decreased sensation in the foot. He has been on Keflex for approximately five months. The patient reports feeling more tired lately and has experienced increased drainage from the ulcer. He plans to walk around at the zoo this weekend and eat out.

Findings
Examination reveals a one by two centimeter circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot, proximal to the fifth metatarsal head. The wound is covered with yellow slough and minimal granulation tissue. There is no surrounding erythema or cellulitis. No fluid collection or necrosis is present. There is no odor or bony exposure. A right foot x-ray showed no evidence of osteomyelitis. Vitals are stable. The patient's blood supply is pending ABI.

Assessment
The patient has a chronic, non-healing diabetic foot ulcer requiring further evaluation and management.

Plan
Order an ankle brachial index to assess blood supply. Perform debridement of the ulcer in the office. Prescribe collagenase ointment for daily wound care and cover with a sterile dressing. Provide a surgical shoe for offloading and pressure relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks for reassessment and potential further debridement.
