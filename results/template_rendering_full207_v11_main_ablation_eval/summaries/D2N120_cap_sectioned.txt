Chief Complaint
Larry reports chronic lumbar back pain and associated leg symptoms.

History of Present Illness
Larry reports a history of chronic lumbar back pain for approximately two years, initially attributed to furniture moving. He has tried various treatments, including physical therapy and strong pain medications, with limited long-term relief. He was previously offered surgery. He describes the pain as sharp and stabbing, radiating down his right leg to his big toe. He also experiences numbness and tingling in his right leg and foot. He reports difficulty getting comfortable, particularly when sitting. He has gained weight over the past years and enjoys playing golf, but this pain has significantly impacted his ability to participate.

Findings
Physical exam reveals midline tenderness at L4-L5 with right-sided lumbar paravertebral tenderness. Lumbar flexion elicits pain. Straight leg raise test is positive on the right. Neurological exam demonstrates decreased sensation to light touch at the right lateral thigh at L4 dermatome. Patellar and Achilles reflexes are symmetrical. Vitals are stable and unremarkable. MRI reveals a disk herniation with nerve impingement at L4-L5.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with an L4-L5 herniated disc with radiculopathy, causing nerve impingement and resulting in leg pain, numbness, and tingling.

Plan
A spinal injection with a corticosteroid is recommended to reduce inflammation and alleviate pain. The injection will be performed under fluoroscopy to ensure accurate placement. Follow-up will be scheduled to monitor response and adjust treatment as needed. The patient will be monitored for any adverse reactions to the medication. A follow-up appointment is scheduled in four weeks if symptoms persist.
