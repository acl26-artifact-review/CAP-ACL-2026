Chief Complaint
Left side low back pain with reports of muscle pull and misalignment in the spine.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left side low back pain, exacerbated by bending over and certain physical activities. He describes a history of microdiscectomy in November 2020 due to a disk break that wrapped around and connected to the spinal thecal sac. He was highly dosed on gabapentin for nerve pain, which he found difficult to withdraw from. He previously tried ibuprofen eight hundred milligrams daily with limited relief. He has been attending physical therapy at the VA, and reports that stretching helps. He denies any recent trauma. He reports the pain is often random, sometimes on the right side and sometimes on the left, depending on the day.

Findings
Physical examination reveals pain with palpation to the left side around the L5 region. Range of motion is normal with flexion, extension, and dorsiflexion. No fractures were identified on the spinal bone via x-ray. Palpation to the right side reveals no pain. The patient can push back up on the examiner's hands.

Assessment
The patient presents with a muscle pull secondary to a misalignment in the spine, likely contributing to the reported left side low back pain.

Plan
Prescribe physical therapy at the VA for continued management. Prescribe meloxicam instead of ibuprofen for pain management. A referral to pain management will be arranged to explore alternative pain management strategies, potentially avoiding narcotics. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
