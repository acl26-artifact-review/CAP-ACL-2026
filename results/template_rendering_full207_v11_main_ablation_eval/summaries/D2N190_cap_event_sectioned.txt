Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a bicycle fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports falling off his tandem bicycle with his partner approximately two weeks ago. He states his knee has been hurting ever since. He took ibuprofen and a bandage, but the pain persists. He describes the pain as lingering and worsening with incline, particularly when walking up hills. He has been rocking babies at the children's hospital as a professional. He also enjoys walking his dog. He denies any prior knee problems and reports no previous injuries. He has been taking ibuprofen for the pain, but it hasn't provided sustained relief. He is concerned about potential surgery and a possible leg amputation. He has not been swimming since the injury. He is currently taking ibuprofen twice daily.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild posterior drawer instability and a slight limp with ambulation. Palpation of the medial joint line elicits tenderness. Range of motion is full, but with pain on extension. The patient reports clicking and crepitus. Examination reveals abrasions on the lateral aspect of the knee. X-rays show no fractures or dislocations. The patient's left knee range of motion is normal.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and physical examination, a sprain of the posterior cruciate ligament is suspected. Further evaluation with an MRI is warranted to rule out other injuries, such as a meniscal tear.

Plan
Order an MRI of the right knee to evaluate for ligamentous and meniscal injuries. Refer the patient to physical therapy for rehabilitation. Prescribe a knee brace for support and stability. Continue ibuprofen as needed, up to three times daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four to six weeks to assess progress and determine the need for further intervention.
