Chief Complaint
Back pain and leg pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports having back pain for a long time, with a significant exacerbation approximately one month ago as noted in the PCP's report. He describes the pain as an electrical toothache, radiating down his left leg and into his foot. The pain is exacerbated by standing, walking, and coughing/sneezing. He reports difficulty tying shoes and mentions a previous fall 30 years ago, resulting in a suspected nerve injury. He has tried NSAIDs without significant relief and has been bedridden due to the pain. He expresses frustration with patients who focus on irrelevant historical details and struggles to elicit a clear chief complaint.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation at the L4-L5 vertebral level. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Strength is 4/5 in the left leg and 5/5 in the right. Reflexes are brisk and symmetric. The patient's gait is affected, with a noticeable drag of the left foot. Vital signs are stable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a lumbar disc herniation at L4-L5 with nerve root impingement, causing radiculopathy.

Plan
I recommend an epidural steroid injection today to reduce inflammation and provide symptomatic relief. I will also refer you back to physical therapy to strengthen the muscles and improve your range of motion. We will schedule a follow-up appointment in approximately one month to assess your progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.
