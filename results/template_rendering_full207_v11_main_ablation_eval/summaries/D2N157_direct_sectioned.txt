Chief Complaint
Patient reports concern regarding multiple medications and reports some dizziness when standing quickly.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being on multiple medications, including aspirin, brilinta, and crestor, due to a previous myocardial infarction six months ago. He reports feeling dizzy when standing up quickly, and reports taking fish oil. He reports that the crestor may have been causing muscle aches. He has a history of diabetes and reports an A1c around 60, with fasting glucose around 130. He reports feeling down since his heart attack and lives with his dog. He had a recent kidney function test and his creatinine was stable.

Findings
Vital signs reveal blood pressure of 127/78 mmHg. Physical exam reveals no jugular venous distension or carotid bruits. A slight 3/6 systolic ejection murmur is heard at the left base. There is one plus nonpitting edema in the lower extremities. The patient's A1c was approximately 60.

Assessment
Coronary artery disease, stage 3 chronic kidney disease, and type 2 diabetes mellitus.

Plan
Prescribe refills of metoprolol 40 mg daily and crestor 40 mg daily. Order a referral for cardiac rehabilitation. Order a referral to a nephrologist for follow-up. Order a referral to psychiatry for evaluation of mood symptoms. Order a hemoglobin A1c and schedule a follow-up appointment. Arrange for a dietitian consultation to address dietary needs related to diabetes and coronary artery disease.
