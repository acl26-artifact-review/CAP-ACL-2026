Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient presents with a two-day history of sharp, stabbing chest pain. The pain is located in the center of the chest and radiates to the left arm. The patient reports that the pain started after strenuous exercise. He denies any fever, shortness of breath, or nausea. He has a history of smoking and reports smoking 1 pack per day for 20 years. He also reports a family history of coronary artery disease.

Findings
Physical exam reveals elevated blood pressure and a rapid heart rate. The lungs are clear to auscultation. The heart rhythm is regular.

Assessment
Acute coronary syndrome

Plan
STAT EKG, cardiac enzymes, and cardiac catheterization.
