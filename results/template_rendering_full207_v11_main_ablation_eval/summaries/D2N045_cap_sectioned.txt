Chief Complaint
Knee pain during running.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports knee pain that is isolated to the right, particularly when running. He describes the pain as worsening towards the end of the day, especially after prolonged activity. He has been using ice and ibuprofen to manage the pain, which provides some relief. He reports this has been ongoing for several years. He previously saw Dr. Wood, who referred him to this appointment. The patient reports a history of running, including 5k and marathon training.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a small effusion in the right knee joint. Palpation elicits tenderness on the outside of the knee. Pedal pulses are intact. No sensory or motor deficits are noted. Blood pressure is 120/60 mmHg, heart rate is 58 bpm, and respirations are 14 breaths per minute. The right knee demonstrates no redness or swelling.

Assessment
The patient has knee pain, likely due to overuse and a sprain. Radiographic findings are unremarkable, ruling out fracture or significant arthritis.

Plan
I am ordering physical therapy for your right knee to strengthen the lower extremities and recommend appropriate running exercises. I am also prescribing two tablets of Tylenol 500 mg every four hours and two tablets of Ibuprofen 200 mg every four hours. Please follow up in two weeks to assess your progress. Avoid running for two weeks and consider light weight training and squats, as recommended by the physical therapist.
