Chief Complaint
Right knee pain and clicking sensation.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain exacerbated by prolonged weight-bearing and when standing on one foot. He describes a feeling of being 'locked' in the knee and reports a clicking sensation, particularly when squatting. The patient is fearful that the clicking is an injury. He has a history of multiple toe injuries. He reports numbness in his right foot, dependent on the shoes he wears. The patient reports pain in the left hand and numbness. He reports a clicking sensation during squatting.

Findings
Examination reveals right knee pain with palpation and range of motion limitations. The patient reports a clicking sensation upon examination. The patient reports pain in the left hand.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with patellofemoral pain syndrome and possible toe numbness.

Plan
Order physical therapy, prescribe meloxicam 15mg daily, and schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress. Advise the patient to avoid activities that exacerbate the pain.
