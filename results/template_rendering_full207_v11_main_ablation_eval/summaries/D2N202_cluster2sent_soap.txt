S:
The patient reports right shoulder pain that began three weeks ago during home remodeling, with intermittent pain and some constant pain. He describes difficulty lifting overhead and raising his arm overhead. He reports icing the arm and taking Advil for the pain. He denies numbness or tingling in his arm or hand. The patient rates his pain as 3 without medication and 6 with medication. He previously rated pain as 0 when initially injured, but now reports no numbness or tingling.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the right shoulder, tenderness to the greater tuberosity of the humerus, brisk capillary refill, and equal and bilateral response to sensation and touch. The x-ray of the right shoulder shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy, likely sustained during remodeling activities. No other acute findings are present.

P:
I am ordering an MRI of the right shoulder to rule out other pathology. I am referring the patient to physical therapy for a course of 6-8 weeks. I recommend continuing the patient's use of an NSAID if tolerated, and advise them to take Tylenol for pain relief. I will follow up with the patient in 4 weeks to assess progress.
