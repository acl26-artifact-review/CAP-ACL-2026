Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for the past several weeks, with increasing intensity. He is a 44-year-old female who presents to evaluate the pain. She denies recent trauma and reports no prior surgeries related to the arm. She has tried Tylenol for a few days with minimal relief and has not taken any other pain medications. She suspects a joint injury and has attempted to use ice and heat pads to relieve the pain.

Findings
The last x-ray did not reveal any broken bones or fractures.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is consistent with musculoskeletal shoulder pain.

Plan
I suggest continuing with 600 mg of ibuprofen three times daily for one week. Order medications and procedures as discussed. Advise returning for follow-up in three weeks if pain persists despite rest and regular pain relievers. Encourage the patient to consider Advil if needed.
