Chief Complaint
Headache

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing headaches lately, which have been getting worse and more frequent. She notes that her blood pressure has been running high, as she's been monitoring it weekly, with readings of 160/87 and 175/92. She has been taking acetaminophen, which provides some relief but the headaches persist. She reports she has been taking lisinopril as prescribed, but occasionally forgets to take it, particularly on weekends. She has diabetes and has been checking her blood sugar, reporting values around 120-100. She denies any recent trauma or other significant medical events. She has not experienced congestion, cough, or runny nose.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no tenderness to palpation of the frontal or maxillary sinuses. No lymphadenopathy is palpable. Blood pressure readings were 160/87 and 175/92. The patient reports headaches rated 7/10 initially, decreasing to 4-5 with acetaminophen.

Assessment
The patient presents with a persistent headache, accompanied by elevated blood pressure and a history of diabetes.

Plan

I also want to schedule a diabetic education session for you to review. Please continue monitoring your blood pressure regularly. Please continue taking your acetaminophen as needed for your headaches. Please continue with your walks. I'll see you back in four weeks for follow-up.
