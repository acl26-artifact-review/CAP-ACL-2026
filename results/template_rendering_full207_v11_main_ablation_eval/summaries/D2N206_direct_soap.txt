S:
The patient reports pain in the right flank radiating to the groin for the past two weeks, which is under control but has bouts of exacerbation. He states the pain is related to activity and fluid intake. He denies nausea, vomiting, chills, or fever. He reports no urinary symptoms, but did note a testicular pain episode that resolved. He has a family history of kidney stones.

O:
Blood pressure is slightly elevated. Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation in the right flank. CT scan demonstrates a 0.5 cm stone in the proximal right ureter without evidence of obstruction or nephrosis. No other abnormalities are noted on physical exam.

A:
The patient has a history of kidney stones. The current presentation is consistent with a new kidney stone event.

P:
Continue ibuprofen as needed for pain. Prescribe tramadol 50 mg as needed for breakthrough pain. Order BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture. Follow up in 1-2 weeks if symptoms persist or if the stone is not passed. Advise patient to continue using a strainer when urinating.
