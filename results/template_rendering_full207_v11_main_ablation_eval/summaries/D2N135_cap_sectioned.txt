Chief Complaint
Evaluation of high blood sugar and follow-up of diabetes management.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports high blood sugar, initially noticing symptoms approximately two weeks ago, coinciding with increased holiday sweets consumption. He describes feeling lightheaded and experiencing visual disturbances, or 'spots,' particularly when fatigued. He reports a family history of diabetes and acknowledges a challenging environment with frequent access to sweets at work and during family gatherings. He's been taking protonix for reflux, which has been well-controlled. He also reports occasional heart racing when anxious, and occasional joint pain. He denies fever, chills, chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, or urinary symptoms. He has a history of osteoarthritis and atrial fibrillation.

Findings
Physical exam reveals an enlarged thyroid, but no tenderness. The patient's heart rate is irregular and rhythm is abnormal. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. Lower extremities are not swollen. Blood glucose levels are elevated, and hemoglobin A1c is also elevated.

Assessment
The patient has been diagnosed with diabetes. Further investigation is needed to determine the type of diabetes and rule out other underlying causes.

Plan
Order a complete metabolic panel and metformin, 500 milligrams twice daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and continue monitoring blood glucose levels. The nurse will provide instruction on glucometer use and blood sugar monitoring. Order a refill of metoprolol, 25 milligrams twice daily.
