Chief Complaint
Right knee injury following a soccer game.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a pop in his right knee three days ago while playing pickup soccer, resulting in significant swelling and pain. He describes decreased functional mobility and reports difficulty straightening and bending the knee fully. He has been using ibuprofen with some relief, but continues to experience pain and swelling. He reports a six to seven pain scale.

Findings
Examination reveals significant right knee effusion and mild ecchymosis. Palpation of the medial joint line elicits tenderness. Range of motion is limited; passive range of motion is reduced to 115 degrees flexion and 20-30 degrees extension. Lachman's test is positive. Neurovascular examination of the bilateral lower extremities is intact. Muscle strength is a five out of five. Gait is marked with a right leg limp.

Assessment
Right knee injury is suggestive of an anterior cruciate ligament tear.

Plan
Order an MRI to evaluate the integrity of the ACL. Initiate meloxicam 50 mg daily for pain and swelling. Instruct the patient to ice the knee as needed. Prescribe a knee immobilizer and crutches for weight-bearing. Recommend moderate activity modification, limiting work activities that involve prolonged sitting. Advise the patient to avoid jogging until further evaluation. Schedule an MRI and follow up in one week to discuss results.
