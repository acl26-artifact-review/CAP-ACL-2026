Chief Complaint
Abdominal pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports one-week history of abdominal pain, attributing it to increased coffee consumption and possibly related to recent workouts. He describes intermittent nausea and occasional vomiting over the past week. The pain is primarily in the upper abdomen and worsens with exercise. He denies fever. He has been drinking more coffee lately and has decreased his alcohol intake to less than three drinks per week. He has been taking the flovent twice daily and albuterol as needed for asthma, but occasionally forgets to take the flovent.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation in the epigastrium. Lung exam demonstrates mild end-expiratory wheezes. Heart exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, unchanged from prior exam.

Assessment
Acute gastritis is likely related to caffeine intake and possibly alcohol use. The patient's asthma is currently well-controlled.

Plan
Prescribe zantac for twice daily administration. Increase metformin dosage to 1000 mg in the morning and 500 mg in the evening. Order urinalysis, urine pregnancy test, and CBC with CMP. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks.
