Chief Complaint
Left hand pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left hand. He states the pain is constant and has been present for the past week. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports that the pain is exacerbated by gripping and lifting objects. He denies any numbness or tingling. He reports taking ibuprofen for the pain, which provides minimal relief. The patient states he has been unable to perform daily activities due to the pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the distal volar aspect of the left hand. Range of motion is limited due to pain. There is no swelling or erythema. Reflexes are 2+ bilaterally. Capillary refill is normal. The patient's grip strength is decreased.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with a diagnosis of lateral carpal ligament compression syndrome.

Plan
Prescribe a splint for the left hand to be worn at night. Recommend ice packs to the affected area. Advise the patient to avoid activities that aggravate the pain. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess progress. Consider a corticosteroid injection if symptoms do not improve with conservative management.
