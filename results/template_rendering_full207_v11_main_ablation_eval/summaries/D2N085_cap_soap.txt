S:
The patient reports sharp pain in the right abdomen below the ribs for several days, moving to his lower abdomen and groin, and feeling it intensely. He describes a burning sensation when urinating. He reports nausea but no vomiting and denies fever or chills. He states he has had kidney stones before but this is taking longer to pass. His father had kidney stones. He has been taking Tylenol for the pain, which hasn't helped.

O:
Abdomen is non-tender to palpation. No rebound or guarding. No peritoneal signs. Positive CVA tenderness on the right flank. Urinalysis shows dark urine.

A:
Primary problem is nephrolithiasis (kidney stone). Secondary problem is ureterolithiasis (stone in the ureter). Risk factors include prior history of kidney stones and family history.

P:
Recommend increasing fluid intake to facilitate stone passage. Prescribe oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours as needed for pain. Provide a strainer for urine to monitor stone passage. Order a BMP, urinalysis, and urine culture. Schedule follow-up in 1-2 weeks. Discuss lithotripsy or ureteroscopy if symptoms persist.
