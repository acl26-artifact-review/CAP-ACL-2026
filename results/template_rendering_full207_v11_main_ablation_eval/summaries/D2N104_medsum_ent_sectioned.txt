Chief Complaint
Ongoing management of psoriatic arthritis and concerns regarding recent bruising.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports ongoing management of psoriatic arthritis and reports decreasing her prednisone dose. She experienced occasional extra prednisone doses due to foot pain when engaging in desk work. She also reports receiving a COVID vaccine, which resulted in significant pain and required a second dose a week later. The patient recently noticed new bruises appearing without apparent trauma and inquired about their cause. She reports that prednisone may be contributing to increased bruising. She reports that methotrexate has helped with joint pain. The patient's nails also show ridges consistent with psoriasis.

Findings
Examination reveals good range of motion in the toes and nails. No acute physical exam findings were noted.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with psoriatic arthritis and is experiencing related symptoms including bruising and nail changes.

Plan
We will continue the current methotrexate regimen and discontinue the prednisone. I have scheduled an appointment for blood work to monitor the patient's response to treatment. I will also schedule a follow-up appointment in three months to assess her progress.
