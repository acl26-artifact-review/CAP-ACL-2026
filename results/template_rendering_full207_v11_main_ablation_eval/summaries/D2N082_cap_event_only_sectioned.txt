Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent ache in his left shoulder for the past six weeks. The pain is exacerbated by overhead activities and when lying on his affected side. He denies any recent injuries or trauma. He has tried acetaminophen for relief, but it only provides partial comfort. He also mentions a family history of diabetes and a donut shop.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain upon impingement testing. Vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are most consistent with a rotator cuff tendinopathy. Given the family history of diabetes, we must monitor for potential complications.

Plan
I am ordering shoulder radiographs to evaluate the extent of the injury. I will also refer the patient to physical therapy for targeted exercises and pain management. We will continue the patient on acetaminophen as needed, and schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
