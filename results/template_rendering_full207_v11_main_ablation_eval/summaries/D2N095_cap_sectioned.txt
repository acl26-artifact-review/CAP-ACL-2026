Chief Complaint
Low back pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports low back pain, which began approximately six days ago after walking. He describes the pain as a spasm-like sensation, exacerbated by flexion and extension. He has been taking ibuprofen and applying heat, but the pain persists. He reports lifting more recently, possibly contributing to the issue. He denies any trauma. The patient also reports nasal congestion due to seasonal allergies. His blood pressure readings at home have been well-controlled, and he is taking lisinopril.

Findings
Physical examination reveals pain to palpation on the right lateral aspect of the lumbar spine, with pain on flexion and extension. There is no leg weakness or numbness. The patient's vital signs are stable. X-rays show normal bony alignment. Lab results are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with a lumbar strain.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily. Refer to physical therapy for strengthening exercises. Continue lisinopril 10 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
