S:
The patient reports persistent left shoulder pain, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected shoulder. He describes the pain as constant and worsening with pressure. He has been taking two extra-strength Tylenol tablets every six to eight hours for pain relief. He denies numbness or tingling in his arms or hands. He has no prior shoulder pain.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder with tenderness to palpation of the greater tuberosity. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the left hand. The x-ray of the left shoulder shows no fractures.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy. Further imaging is indicated to rule out other pathology.

P:
I am ordering an MRI of the left shoulder. I am also referring the patient to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. The nurse will provide educational materials on physical therapy and schedule a follow-up visit after the physical therapy course. If symptoms do not improve, a steroid injection may be considered.
