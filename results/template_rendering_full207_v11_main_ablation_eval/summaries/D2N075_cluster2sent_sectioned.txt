Chief Complaint
The patient reports chest pain that occurred four days ago and has since resolved.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports chest pain, initially occurring four days ago, which resolved spontaneously. The pain was sharp and felt like a heart attack. The patient denies heartburn or indigestion and reports that swallowing has improved since the initial episode three months ago. The patient reports a stressful job transition around the time of the chest pain. There is no family history of gastrointestinal problems or abdominal surgeries. The patient reports mild tenderness to palpation in the upper abdominal quadrants. The patient has a past medical history of uterine fibroids and anemia.

Findings
An EKG and blood tests were normal. Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to light palpation in the upper abdominal quadrants. The patient reports no pain with lower body palpation.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are concerning for potential cardiac etiology, but were ruled out in the emergency room. Other possible etiologies include anxiety, esophagitis, and musculoskeletal issues.

Plan
The patient has been referred from the emergency room for esophagitis. The patient will self-monitor for any additional symptoms and call the office if they experience any recurrence or change. Follow up with me as needed for any other gastrointestinal complaints.
