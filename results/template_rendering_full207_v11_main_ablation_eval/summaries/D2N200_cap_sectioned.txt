Chief Complaint
Headache and high blood pressure.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing headaches for approximately one month, described as throbbing on the top of his head, with periods of remission. He also states that his blood pressure has been consistently elevated for a long time, and he has been taking chlorthalidone, though he finds it causes a stomachache, so he doesn't consistently take it. He reports occasional dizziness and blurred vision. He has been taking tylenol for the headaches, which provides some relief. The patient admits to eating a lot of McDonald's, including Big Macs with large fries, and frequently forgets to take his medications, typically taking them every other day. He also reports eating a lot of Easter candy over the weekend, which may have contributed to his elevated blood sugar levels.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure was recorded at 172/98 mmHg this morning. The patient reports mild edema in the bilateral upper extremities and notes a one plus pitting edema in his legs. Examination reveals a systolic ejection murmur of II/VI at the left lower sternal border. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are normal. Oxygen saturation is within normal limits. The patient's blood sugar was measured at 170 this morning, which is above the desired range.

Assessment
The patient presents with uncontrolled hypertension and elevated blood pressure and blood sugar levels. He also reports experiencing headaches and dizziness.

Plan
The doctor will continue the patient's current medication regimen, including chlorthalidone. The doctor will monitor the patient's blood pressure three times a week. The doctor plans to increase the patient's metformin dosage to 1500 mg. The doctor will schedule a one-month follow-up appointment to assess the patient's progress. A nutritionist will be consulted to help the patient develop a low sodium diet plan. The doctor recommends that the patient start using a blood pressure monitor at home to track his blood pressure regularly.
