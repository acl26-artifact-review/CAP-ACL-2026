Chief Complaint
Annual exam and ongoing depression and chronic back pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient presents for her annual exam and reports struggling with depression over the past year due to remote work. She states that her depression has been 'on and off' and reports feeling worse recently. She reports ongoing chronic back pain, which is exacerbated by prolonged sitting, particularly at her desk. The patient describes the pain as intermittent, but bothersome when sitting for extended periods. She reports some numbing sensations in her legs, but no tingling or pain. She denies weakness in her legs. She reports having had a congenital artery at birth, requiring a coronary artery bypass grafting at a young age, but her heart and arteries have been stable. She is currently taking prozac 20 milligrams daily and reports that it has helped in the past. She is able to perform activities of daily living, but experiences discomfort when standing or sitting for extended periods. She reports that the symptoms improve when she changes position.

Findings
Physical exam reveals no distress. The neck is unremarkable, with no thyroid enlargement. Heart sounds are normal, with a systolic ejection murmur of moderate intensity, stable from the last exam. Lungs are clear to auscultation. The abdomen is soft and non-tender. Lumbar spine palpation reveals tenderness. Range of motion is decreased in the lumbar spine. Lower extremity strength is normal. There is no edema. An ECG shows stable findings compared to the previous year. A lumbar spine X-ray is stable. A back X-ray is stable.

Assessment
1. Major depressive disorder, recurrent. 2. Chronic low back pain. 3. Stable cardiovascular history following coronary artery bypass grafting.

Plan
Increase the patient's prozac dosage from 20 milligrams to 40 milligrams daily, one tablet once daily. Order a physical therapy referral to address the chronic back pain. Order an echocardiogram to monitor cardiovascular health. Prescribe aspirin 81 milligrams daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and address any ongoing concerns. The nurse will coordinate medication refills and schedule the echocardiogram.
