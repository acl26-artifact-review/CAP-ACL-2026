S:
The patient reported falling off his tandem bike with his partner approximately two weeks ago, resulting in right knee pain. He states the pain was initially sore and he took ibuprofen and a bandage, but it has persisted as a lingering ache. He describes the pain as holding on and mentions it worsens with incline activities like walking up hills. He reports no prior knee problems and never had a normal knee before this injury. He has been swimming since the injury and is unsure if he should continue. He has been taking ibuprofen up to three times a day. He reports feeling weak when walking up hills.

O:
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation on the medial joint line of the right knee. The patient reports a one plus posterior drawer and a negative Lachman test. The patient's right knee range of motion is full extension and full flexion. There is no palpable crepitance. The patient has approximately a dime-size abrasion over the medial patella. The lateral knee is unremarkable. The popliteal space is nontender. The patient's left knee range of motion is full and symmetric. The patient has no deformities. X-rays show no acute bony abnormalities or fractures. The patient's knee appears stable.

A:
The patient's primary problem is right knee pain following a fall. Suspicion for a posterior cruciate ligament sprain or injury is high, potentially accompanied by minor cartilage damage. A possible mechanism of injury is a tibial bone displacement.

P:
Order an MRI of the right knee to evaluate for ligamentous and cartilaginous injuries. Prescribe a brace for the right knee. Refer to physical therapy for evaluation and treatment. Recommend continued ibuprofen as needed, up to three times daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four to six weeks to reassess symptoms and treatment response. Consider a prescription for a lower-dose alternative medication if ibuprofen is not effective.
