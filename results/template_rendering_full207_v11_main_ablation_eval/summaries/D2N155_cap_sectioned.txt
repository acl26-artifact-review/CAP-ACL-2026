Chief Complaint
A non-healing foot ulcer and type 2 diabetes.

History of Present Illness
Christina reports a non-healing foot ulcer that has been present for approximately one year. She describes the ulcer as starting as a small blister that became infected and was treated with antibiotics approximately five months ago. The ulcer has not resolved, and she reports feeling more tired and experiencing increased drainage from the wound. She also reports some foot pain but denies significant pain. She has type 2 diabetes, which she reports is currently managed, but her A1c was recently around ten. She was previously on Keflex for this issue. She plans to be at the zoo this weekend.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a one by two centimeter circular wound on the dorsal aspect of the lateral right foot, proximal to the fifth metatarsal prominence. The wound is covered with yellow slough and minimal granulation tissue. There is no surrounding erythema or cellulitis. No evidence of fluid collection or necrosis is observed. There is no odor, and no bony exposure. Neurological exam reveals diminished sensation in the foot. The patient's vitals are stable.

Assessment
The patient has a diabetic foot ulcer and type 2 diabetes mellitus. Neuropathy is present.

Plan
Order an ankle brachial index to assess blood supply. Perform debridement of the ulcer in the office. Prescribe colagenase ointment for topical application. Provide a surgical shoe for pressure relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks for re-evaluation and potential further debridement. ,
