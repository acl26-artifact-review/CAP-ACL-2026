Chief Complaint
Patient reports feeling fine most days, but reports high blood pressure and occasional headaches.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports feeling generally fine most days, though they note feeling busier at work. They report experiencing mild headaches associated with high blood pressure, but not regularly. They have noticed a decrease in blood pressure readings since the pandemic. The patient reports no chest pain or shortness of breath. Family history includes both parents with high blood pressure and one with kidney disease. The patient is currently taking ten milligrams of norvasc daily and carvedilol twenty five milligrams twice a day, previously taking lisinopril. The patient occasionally uses over-the-counter medications like Advil for knee or back pain.

Findings
The patient reports feeling fine most days, with occasional headaches associated with high blood pressure. Vital signs reveal a blood pressure of 169/74 mmHg, heart rate of 88 bpm, and oxygen saturation of 98%. Physical exam reveals no jugular venous distension or carotid bruits. Lung sounds are clear. A two over six systolic ejection murmur is heard. One plus pitting edema is present bilaterally. The patient reports noticing some swelling in their lower extremities with prolonged standing.

Assessment
The patient presents with uncontrolled hypertension, evidenced by elevated blood pressure readings and peripheral edema. Further investigation is warranted to determine the underlying etiology.

Plan
I am ordering a renal artery ultrasound to rule out vascular causes of hypertension. I am also ordering morning aldosterone and renin levels and a 24-hour urine collection. I will prescribe cardura 4 milligrams once daily. I recommend a referral to a registered dietitian for assistance with dietary modifications to reduce salt intake. I will also prescribe tylenol for pain. I would like to schedule a follow-up in three weeks to review blood pressure readings and discuss further management strategies. Please continue to monitor your blood pressure at home and record readings daily, bringing them to the office for review.
