Chief Complaint
Severe right elbow pain.

History of Present Illness
Dylan reports experiencing significant pain in both elbows for approximately one and a half years, with the right elbow being the most affected. He attributes the pain to heavy weightlifting, primarily focusing on biceps and triceps development. He reports that the pain has been gradually increasing over time, particularly with activities involving gripping and twisting. He has tried ibuprofen 800mg three times daily, but finds it provides only minimal relief. He denies any recent injuries or trauma. He also mentions that he has not been pitching recently. He is expecting a newborn soon.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited range of motion in extension of the right elbow, with pain upon palpation of the medial epicondyle. Sensation is intact to the touch. Pulses are equal bilaterally. There is no pain with palpation of the outside of the elbow. Range of motion is limited in pronation and supination of the right elbow. The patient reports pain with torsion.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with medial epicondylitis (golfer's elbow).

Plan
A cervical MRI has been ordered to further evaluate the elbow. The doctor recommends icing. We will consider a whole blood transfusion to aid in healing. A follow-up appointment will be scheduled in approximately two weeks, pending the MRI results.
