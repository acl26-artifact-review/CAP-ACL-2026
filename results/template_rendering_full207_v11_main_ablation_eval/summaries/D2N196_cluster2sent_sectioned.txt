Chief Complaint
Elevated and fluctuating blood pressures.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fluctuating blood pressures over the past four weeks, with occasional readings over 180 mm Hg, accompanied by lightheadedness and slight headaches. He has experienced these symptoms primarily when blood pressure readings are higher. He denies chest pain or sustained respiratory difficulty. He has been taking lisinopril 20 mg daily and atorvastatin. He continues to engage in golf and walking.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, trace lower extremity edema, and excellent air movement. Blood pressure readings have been fluctuating, ranging from the low 120s to over 180 mm Hg. The patient reports feeling lightheaded when blood pressure is elevated.

Assessment
The patient's blood pressure is out of control, requiring further management.

Plan
Increase lisinopril to 60 mg twice daily and add hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily. Order a cholesterol panel, CBC with differential, comprehensive metabolic panel, and urinalysis. Continue atorvastatin. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess blood pressure control and adjust medications as needed.
