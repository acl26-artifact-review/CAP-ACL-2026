Chief Complaint
Back pain following a fall at a surprise birthday party.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports back pain following a fall at a surprise birthday party. He describes being crunched behind others while hiding, and then falling down a staircase railing. He states the pain started immediately after the fall and was exacerbated by twisting motions and lying flat. He denies any radicular symptoms or numbness in his legs. He has a history of lumbar fusion performed previously. He is currently taking ibuprofen for the pain and also takes medication for anxiety. He reports no other significant medical history.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lumbar region, particularly with rotation. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Neurological examination is unremarkable, with no reported numbness or tingling in the lower extremities. Examination does not reveal any signs of acute instability or nerve compression.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with a lumbar strain secondary to trauma. The history of the fall and reported pain with rotation are concerning for muscle strain. The prior lumbar fusion is relevant to the overall clinical picture.

Plan
Continue ibuprofen 800mg three times daily with food. Recommend ice and heat alternating on the back. Encourage gentle movement and activity as tolerated. Follow up in one week if symptoms persist or worsen. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
