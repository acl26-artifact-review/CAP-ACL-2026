Chief Complaint
Fatigue and cough.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue for the past two months, with no specific triggers identified. He also reports a cough for approximately three months, which began around the time he was loading bills. The patient describes the cough as preceded by a cold. He reports his joints feel okay with activity and occasional hand problems, and states he stopped smoking seven years ago, reporting feeling better after cessation. The patient denies any pain or shortness of breath.

Findings
Physical exam reveals blood pressure of 124/76 and heart rate of 70. The patient's oxygen saturation is 98% on room air. Auscultation reveals clear and equal bilateral lung sounds, a regular rate and rhythm heart, and no clicks, rubs, or murmurs. The patient has some swelling to his knuckles on the third digit of each hand. A CT scan shows a solitary two centimeter nodule in the right upper lobe, which is smooth and shows no emphysematous disease.

Assessment
The patient presents with fatigue and a persistent cough, along with a solitary pulmonary nodule. Clinical suspicion is raised for potential malignancy.

Plan
Order a pulmonary function test (PFT) and a PET CT. The clinician suggests video assisted thoracoscopy removal of the nodule, with the patient to be under general anesthesia during the procedure, expected to take approximately one hour and a half, and requiring one night's admission.
