Chief Complaint
Routine yearly exam and evaluation of blood pressure control.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of hypertension and depression, with a previous carpal tunnel release performed approximately two years ago. She reports that her depression has been well-managed with medication and weekly therapy. However, she has experienced fluctuating blood pressure readings over the past couple of weeks, often elevated or low, and reports difficulty maintaining a consistent reading. She attributes this to increased work demands and related stress, leading to inconsistent meal preparation and reliance on frozen meals. She denies headaches or swelling. She reports no current thoughts of self-harm.

Findings
Physical exam revealed normal lung sounds, clear to auscultation bilaterally, no palpable lymphadenopathy, regular heart rhythm without murmurs, and equal grips. Neurological exam was intact. Blood pressure measured 142/88 mmHg in the office today. Laboratory results were within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient presents with hypertension, managed with medication, and reports fluctuating blood pressure readings. Her depression is currently well-controlled.

Plan
Increase lisinopril to 10 mg daily, and schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to assess blood pressure control. Continue current antidepressant medication and psychotherapy. Advise the patient to maintain a consistent diet and schedule regular blood pressure monitoring.
