Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the right knee, describing it as sharp and intermittent. He states the pain began after a weekend injury and has been present continuously. He reports pain with both flexion and extension of the knee. The patient also reports tenderness to palpation and reports bruising in the shoulder. He denies any fever or chills.

Findings
The patient's right knee demonstrates tenderness to palpation. Range of motion is limited by pain. No effusion is palpable. The patient reports shoulder bruising.

Assessment
The patient has a knee contusion and right knee pain.

Plan
The patient is icing the knee. The patient should apply ice. A cervical x-ray is planned. The patient should rest. The patient should take ibuprofen as needed.
