Chief Complaint
Left leg pain and right leg pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient presents with left leg pain and reports right leg pain. She describes the pain as soreness to walk on and reports it looks like she had a history of atopic eczema in the past, for which she takes fluocinonide. She has been using ibuprofen and tylenol for pain management. She was prescribed mobic 15 milligrams once daily.

Findings
Physical examination reveals significant tenderness to the lateral aspect of the right upper leg. The x-ray demonstrates no evidence of fracture or swelling, and appears normal.

Assessment
The patient likely has a contusion of the right upper leg.

Plan
I am starting an anti-inflammatory medication.
