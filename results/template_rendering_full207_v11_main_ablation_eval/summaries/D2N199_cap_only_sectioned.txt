Chief Complaint
Right knee pain and difficulty with stairs.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain, which began after a fall down four or five stairs today. He describes the pain as sharp and localized. He also reports a slight limp and difficulty with stairs. The patient denies any prior knee injuries. He reports feeling unwell and has experienced some congestion and drainage down his throat. He has taken over-the-counter Dayquil for his symptoms. The patient reports not watching his sugar intake and not taking his blood sugars. He is currently taking metformin 1000 milligrams twice a day and lisinopril. He reports feeling achy at times.

Findings
The doctor observes decreased flexion and extension of the right knee and ecchymosis on the right medial knee. Bilateral cervical lymphadenopathy is noted. Coarse rhonchi are auscultated bilaterally. A lipid panel and hemoglobin A1C will be ordered. The patient reports a limp.

Assessment
The patient has a right medial collateral ligament strain, a twisted knee, and a history of hypertension. He also has a viral syndrome with mild bronchitis.

Plan
The doctor orders a lipid panel and a hemoglobin A1C. The doctor prescribes meloxicam. The patient should buy a blood pressure cuff. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Arrange follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
