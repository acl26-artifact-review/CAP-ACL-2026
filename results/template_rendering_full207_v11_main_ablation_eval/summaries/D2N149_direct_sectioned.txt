Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath and hematemesis.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a two-week history of increasing shortness of breath, exacerbated by exertion. He describes waking with shortness of breath and reports coughing up blood, approximately three to four cups per day. He states this began approximately three weeks ago. He has a history of diabetes mellitus, hypertension, and asthma. He reports his blood sugar has been running high and low, with spikes over 200 after meals and around 150 in the morning. He wears a Dexcom and notes spikes after meals. He has been taking norvasc but occasionally skips doses due to low blood pressure. He had a knee problem some time ago but denies current knee symptoms. He reports no recent travel history or fever.

Findings
Oxygen saturation 94% on clinic pulse oximetry. Pulse 78, regular rate and rhythm. Blood pressure 120/70 mmHg, occasionally dipping to the low nineties. No JVD, carotid bruits, or lymphadenopathy. Lung exam reveals mild bilateral crackles and rhonchi, more prominent on the right. Heart exam reveals a two+ ejection murmur. Extremities reveal one+ nonpitting edema bilaterally. No calf tenderness or negative Hogan's sign. No findings of bleeding from teeth or gums.

Assessment
Acute respiratory distress with suspected pulmonary etiology. Type 2 diabetes mellitus with suboptimal glycemic control. Hypertension.

Plan
Order a chest radiograph and CT angiogram of the chest to evaluate for pulmonary embolism and pneumonia. Send blood for complete blood count, comprehensive metabolic panel, and coagulation studies. Increase metformin dosage to 750 mg twice daily. Continue norvasc as needed for blood pressure management. Schedule a follow-up appointment in three weeks to assess response to treatment and further evaluate diabetes control. Refer to the emergency department for further evaluation and management.
