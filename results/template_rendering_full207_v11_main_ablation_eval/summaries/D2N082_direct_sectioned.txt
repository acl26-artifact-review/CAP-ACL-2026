Chief Complaint
The patient was referred for evaluation of low immunoglobulin A levels and recurrent infections.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports frequent infections, particularly colds and prolonged respiratory symptoms. He describes these infections as more common than usual, especially during the winter months. He denies any family history of immune deficiencies. He was recently diagnosed with type 2 diabetes and is taking metformin. He reports traveling to Zambia recently. He denies any prior blood transfusions.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal vital signs, including a temperature of 98.6°F and blood pressure of 128/82 mmHg. The heart exam is normal, with no murmurs. Lung exam is clear to auscultation. The abdominal exam is benign, with no tenderness or distention. Laboratory results confirm a low immunoglobulin A level.

Assessment
The patient's low immunoglobulin A level is the primary concern. This likely contributes to his recurrent infections.

Plan
Order additional blood work to evaluate other antibody levels. Counsel the patient on the importance of reporting any infections that do not resolve promptly. Advise the patient to monitor for abdominal symptoms or signs of severe diarrhea. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment and further investigate antibody levels.
