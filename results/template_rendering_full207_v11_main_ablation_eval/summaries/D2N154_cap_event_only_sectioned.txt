Chief Complaint
Right knee pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent right knee pain for the past three months, worsening with activity and stairs. He denies any specific injury. He describes the pain as a deep ache, with occasional sharp twinges. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with minimal relief. He rates his pain as a 6/10 at rest and 8/10 with exertion.

Findings
The patient demonstrates mild right knee effusion. Range of motion is limited by pain. Lachman's test is negative. McMurray's test is negative. Palpation reveals tenderness over the medial joint line.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with medial meniscus injury.

Plan
Order MRI of the right knee to evaluate for meniscal tears. Recommend physical therapy to improve range of motion and strengthen surrounding muscles. Advise the patient to avoid high-impact activities until further evaluation.
