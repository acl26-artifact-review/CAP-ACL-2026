S:
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately four weeks, exacerbated by yard work projects. He describes the pain as tenderness on the outside of the shoulder with arm elevation, similar to a previous baseball injury. He denies numbness or tingling. He has been taking Advil and icing the shoulder. He reports some relief with these measures. The patient also reports soreness in his feet and ankles due to yard work, and mentions his wife's concern about his hydration levels.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder, with tenderness to palpation over the greater tuberosity. Right hand grip strength is normal. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds in the right arm. Neurological exam is intact. Shoulder x-ray shows no fracture. Vital signs are stable: blood pressure 160/104, heart rate 62, respiratory rate 16, oxygen saturation 99%.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy. Further evaluation is warranted to rule out other causes.

P:
Order an MRI of the left shoulder to evaluate for rotator cuff pathology. Refer the patient to physical therapy for 6-8 weeks. Continue Tylenol as needed for pain. Consider a corticosteroid injection if symptoms do not improve after physical therapy.
