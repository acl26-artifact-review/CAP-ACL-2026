Chief Complaint
Annual exam and management of hypertension and depression.

History of Present Illness
Ralph is a 62-year-old male with a past medical history of depression and prior lobectomy, who presents for his annual exam. He reports a tough spring with his daughter moving into college and some stress related to that. He states he's been managing his depression with prozac and reports no incidents. He also reports difficulty managing his hypertension, struggling with it, particularly on weekends when he's traveling. He takes norvasc at home but sometimes forgets to take it when he's away. He also reports some shortness of breath with exertion, particularly in humid weather, and occasional chest pain that subsides. He exercises regularly, running 4-5 miles daily.

Findings
Physical exam reveals blood pressure of 120/80 mmHg. A grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur is auscultated. Lung sounds are clear. No edema is present. Sinuses are mildly tender to palpation. An EKG shows no abnormalities. A chest x-ray is normal.

Assessment
The patient's primary problem is hypertension, currently well-controlled. He also continues to experience symptoms consistent with depression, which is being managed with medication. No acute findings are present.

Plan
Order an echocardiogram to evaluate the murmur. Order a refill of prozac 20mg daily. Follow up with the results of the echocardiogram and routine blood work. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks.
