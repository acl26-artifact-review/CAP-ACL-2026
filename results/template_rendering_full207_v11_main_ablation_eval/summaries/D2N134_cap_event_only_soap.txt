S:
The patient reports a new rash on his legs, describing it as red and slightly itchy. He states it appeared three days ago. He denies fever, chills, or swollen lymph nodes. The patient also reports experiencing fatigue for the past two weeks.

O:
Physical exam reveals a diffuse maculopapular rash on the lower extremities, most prominent on the shins. The rash is warm to the touch but not erythematous. Lymph nodes are not palpable. Blood pressure is 122/78, heart rate is 78 bpm, and temperature is 98.6°F.

A:
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are consistent with Lyme disease.

P:
Rescribe doxycycline 100mg twice daily for 14 days. Schedule follow-up in 2 weeks to assess response to treatment. Advise patient to monitor for any worsening symptoms.
