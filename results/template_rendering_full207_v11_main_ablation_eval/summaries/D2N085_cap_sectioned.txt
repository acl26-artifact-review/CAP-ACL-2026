Chief Complaint
Sharp pain in the right abdomen below the ribs.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing sharp pain in the right abdomen, located below the ribs, for the past several days. The pain is described as moving and intensifying into the lower abdomen and groin. The patient reports the pain is constant and severe, making it difficult to find a comfortable position. The patient also notes a burning sensation when urinating. He has previously experienced kidney stones, which he was able to pass, but this current episode is prolonged. He has been taking Tylenol for the pain, but it has not provided significant relief. He reports experiencing nausea but denies vomiting. The patient's father had kidney stones, and the patient has a family history of the condition.

Findings
Physical examination revealed positive CVA tenderness on the right flank, indicating inflammation over the right kidney. The CT scan confirmed a 0.5 cm kidney stone in the proximal right ureter, with no evidence of hydronephrosis.

Assessment
The patient presents with acute nephrolithiasis, characterized by a 0.5 cm kidney stone in the proximal right ureter.

Plan
I recommend increasing fluid intake to facilitate urination and stone passage. I am prescribing oxycodone 5 mg every 6-8 hours as needed for pain, alternating with Tylenol. A strainer will be provided to monitor stone passage. A basic metabolic panel, urinalysis, and urine culture will be ordered. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in 1-2 weeks. If symptoms persist, we will discuss further treatment options, including shockwave lithotripsy or ureteroscopy.
