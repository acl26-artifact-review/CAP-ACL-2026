Chief Complaint
Knee pain, bilateral, exacerbated by activity.

History of Present Illness
James reports bilateral knee pain that has worsened over the past few days, particularly with increased cycling mileage. He describes the pain as aching and stiff, with some inflammation noted in the mornings. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He reports taking aspirin daily and Crestor as prescribed. He has a history of coronary artery disease and lupus, with a stent placed last year and good kidney function. He has not had significant lupus activity in the past year. He denies chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, nausea, or fever.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bilateral knee edema and mild erythema. Range of motion is decreased bilaterally. A slight effusion is noted on the right knee. The heart exam reveals a two out of six systolic ejection murmur. The lungs are clear. An EKG demonstrates normal sinus rhythm. Right knee X-ray shows no bony abnormalities or fracture. The echocardiogram is pending.

Assessment
The patient's primary concern is bilateral knee pain, likely exacerbated by increased activity and potentially related to a lupus flare. His coronary artery disease and lupus require ongoing management.

Plan
Order an autoimmune panel to evaluate for lupus activity. Order a lipid panel and echocardiogram to assess cardiovascular risk. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to review the autoimmune panel results and assess response to conservative management. Advise the patient to continue aspirin as prescribed and to modify his exercise regimen to reduce knee stress. Refer to physical therapy for evaluation and treatment.
