Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies recent trauma and states that acetaminophen provides partial relief. He reports a gradual onset of pain and increasing difficulty with activities. The patient states, "It's getting worse and harder to lift things."

Findings
Physical exam reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. Vitals are within normal limits.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs to evaluate for rotator cuff pathology. Initiate physical therapy for targeted strengthening and range of motion. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
