Chief Complaint
Low back pain following lifting boxes.

History of Present Illness
Logan is a 58-year-old male with a history of diabetes type 2, hypertension, and osteoarthritis. He reports that he was helping his daughter with some heavy equipment and lifted some boxes a little too quickly, and they were a little too heavy. He thought he heard a pop when he moved and had to lie down for about an hour before the pain relieved. The pain has been stiff ever since. He has taken Tylenol and ibuprofen, but it hasn't helped much. He reports tingling in his toes on his right foot until Sunday afternoon, and that resolved. He tends to lie on his right side to sleep to relieve the pain. He's been eating a lot of peanut butter cups and has not had a home-cooked meal in weeks. He is monitoring his blood pressure at home but sometimes forgets when he's out of the house. He has some knee pain that gets aggravated with activity.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a slight systolic heart murmur. The patient's blood pressure is elevated in the office. A lumbar spine palpation reveals pain to palpation of the lumbar spine and pain with flexion and extension of the back. Straight leg raise is negative. The patient reports pain in the left hand.

Assessment
The patient's low back pain is most likely due to a lumbar strain from lifting heavy objects. He has diabetes and hypertension, which require ongoing management.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain. Increase metformin to 1000 mg twice a day for diabetes management. Increase lisinopril to 20 mg daily for hypertension management. Refer to physical therapy for evaluation and treatment. Schedule a follow-up in four weeks if symptoms persist. Order a hemoglobin A1C repeat in six months.
