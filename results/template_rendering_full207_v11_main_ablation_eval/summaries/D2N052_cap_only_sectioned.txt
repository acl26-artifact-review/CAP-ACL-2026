Chief Complaint
Right lower flank pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right lower flank pain that began approximately 24 hours ago. He describes the pain as constant and rated it 7 out of 10. He denies fever, chills, nausea, or vomiting. He reports the pain is exacerbated by movement. He states he has a history of kidney stones and was treated with lithotripsy 3 years ago. He denies any recent trauma. He reports taking ibuprofen as needed for the pain.

Findings
The patient's vital signs are stable. The right flank is tender to palpation. Urinalysis reveals hematuria. The patient's abdomen is soft and non-distended.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is concerning for possible nephrolithiasis. Differential diagnoses include urinary tract infection and musculoskeletal strain.

Plan
Order a urine culture and sensitivity. Order a BMP. Consider lithotripsy. Advise patient to increase fluid intake. Follow up in 24-48 hours.
