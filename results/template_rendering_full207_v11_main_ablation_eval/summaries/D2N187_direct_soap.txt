S:
The patient reports sudden visual distortions and blurring of vision, stating it is worse when reading and when adjusting to dim lighting in restaurants. He reports difficulty adjusting to dim lights at restaurants, specifically mentioning McDonald's with an egg plant parmajon. He reports a history of smoking one pack of cigarettes per day and has attempted to quit but finds it difficult. He admits to inconsistently monitoring his blood pressure.

O:
The technician noted decreased vision when reading and straight lines, with door frames appearing crooked. The anterior segment examination reveals posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes. The cornea is clear in both eyes. Cup to disc ratio is 0.2 in the right eye and 0.3 in the left eye. Fundus examination reveals drusen in the macula, retinal thickening, subretinal fluid and exudates, and hemorrhage in the right eye. The left eye shows no hemorrhage or subretinal fluid. OCT shows drusen deposits and retinal pigment epithelium subretinal fluid accumulation. Fluorescein angiography reveals focal areas of leakage in the right eye consistent with neurovascular age-related macular degeneration.

A:
The patient is diagnosed with advanced neurovascular age-related macular degeneration. The findings are indicative of wet AMD with neovascularization and leakage.

P:
Order monthly intravitreal injections of Lucentis into the vitreous after numbing. Schedule follow-up in one month to assess treatment response and monitor for further vision loss. Advise patient to continue monitoring blood pressure regularly and to keep a log of readings. Counsel patient on the potential for vision loss despite treatment and the importance of adherence to the medication regimen.
