Chief Complaint
Right middle finger pain following a motor vehicle accident.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right middle finger pain that began approximately five days after a motor vehicle accident where he was rear-ended. The accident occurred on Saturday. He describes the pain as significant, particularly with palpation at the tip of the finger. He reports no other injuries. The patient states he was stopped at a four-way stop when the other vehicle struck him. He admits to flipping the bird to the other driver, describing the action as 'a little too enthusiastic.' He denies any other injuries. He is currently taking digoxin for atrial fibrillation.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal phalanx of the right middle finger. Radiographs demonstrate a distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger.

Assessment
Distal phalanx fracture of the middle finger.

Plan
Prescribe tramadol 50 mg every six hours as needed for pain for two days, dispensing eight tablets. Order a finger splint. Schedule a follow-up x-ray in two weeks. Advise the patient to continue digoxin for atrial fibrillation.
