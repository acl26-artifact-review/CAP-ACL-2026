Chief Complaint
Foot pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right foot pain, which he describes as sharp and constant. He states the pain began approximately two weeks ago following a fall. He denies any recent trauma. He reports difficulty with weight-bearing and ambulation. The patient also reports a prior injury to his left leg approximately five years ago. He denies numbness or tingling. He is concerned about being unable to drive due to the foot pain.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the dorsum of the right foot. Range of motion is limited due to pain. The patient demonstrates foot instability. The patient reports stridor.

Assessment
The patient has a Lisfranc fracture and foot instability. There are risks associated with surgical procedures.

Plan
The patient will wear a cast for six to eight weeks. The patient will use crutches for six to eight weeks. An MRI is ordered. The patient will follow up with the doctor in a week. The patient will take ibuprofen.
