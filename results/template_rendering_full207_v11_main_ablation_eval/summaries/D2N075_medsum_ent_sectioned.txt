Chief Complaint
Patient reports three-month history of intermittent chest pain and difficulty swallowing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing intermittent chest pain approximately three months ago, initially in October 2020, which resolved after four days. Since then, she has not experienced any recurrence. The pain was described as sharp and felt like a heart attack. She reports experiencing occasional heartburn or indigestion, though she is unsure if she has ever had heartburn. The difficulty swallowing also occurred around the same time and has since resolved. She attributes the chest pain and swallowing issues to a stressful job transition. She denies any recent trauma. She reports trying to lose weight.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to light palpation in the upper abdominal quadrants, but no pain on palpation of the lower abdomen. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. Examination does not reveal any abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms, including chest pain and difficulty swallowing, raise concern for potential esophageal issues, such as esophagitis or gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). Musculoskeletal etiologies and anxiety are also considerations given the reported stress and the initial presentation resembling a cardiac event. Further investigation is warranted to determine the underlying cause.

Plan
We will discuss an upper endoscopy to evaluate the esophagus and stomach. Given the resolution of symptoms over the past three months, we will initially monitor for any additional symptoms. The patient has been advised to call the office if she experiences any further episodes of pain or difficulty swallowing. Follow-up will be scheduled in four weeks if symptoms persist, or sooner if warranted. ,
