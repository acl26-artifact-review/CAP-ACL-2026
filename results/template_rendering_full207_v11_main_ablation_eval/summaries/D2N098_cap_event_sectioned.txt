Chief Complaint
Left shoulder and elbow pain, onset five months ago following a fall.

History of Present Illness
Mr. James reports constant pain in his left shoulder and elbow for approximately five months, stemming from a fall five months ago while going up stairs. The pain was present immediately after the fall and has persisted. He reports difficulty with lifting, particularly weights (approximately 15-20 pounds), and has reduced his gym sessions to two days per week due to the pain. He continues to walk approximately 30 minutes daily with his wife. He denies any numbness or tingling. He has not taken any pain medication and has only iced the area.

Findings
Left shoulder and elbow tenderness to palpation. Positive Hawkins-Kennedy and Neer's tests. Normal proximal and distal upper extremity strength. Intact median, radial, and ulnar sensation and abduction to 90 degrees. Normal empty tension test. No warmth or erythema or deformity observed.

Assessment
The patient's presentation and physical exam findings are most consistent with impingement syndrome of the left shoulder.

Plan
A referral has been requested for Dr. Martha Rivera to initiate physical therapy sessions two days per week. The doctor suggests a cortisone injection if physical therapy does not provide sufficient relief. The patient is advised to continue icing the affected area and resting. A follow-up appointment is scheduled in four weeks to assess progress.
