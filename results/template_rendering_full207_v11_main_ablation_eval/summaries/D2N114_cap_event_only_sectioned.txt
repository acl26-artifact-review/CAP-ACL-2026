Chief Complaint
Headache

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a persistent, dull headache primarily located over the right temporal region for the past three weeks. He describes it as a constant pressure sensation, not sharp or throbbing. He rates the pain as 5/10 on a numerical rating scale. He denies fever, stiff neck, or visual disturbances. He has tried ibuprofen with minimal relief. He reports increased stress levels at work.

Findings
Neurological exam is unremarkable. Blood pressure 130/80 mmHg. Temperature 98.6°F. No nuchal rigidity noted.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most consistent with tension-type headache.

Plan
Recommend continued use of ibuprofen as needed. Suggest stress management techniques, such as deep breathing exercises or yoga. Schedule follow-up in two weeks if symptoms persist or worsen.
