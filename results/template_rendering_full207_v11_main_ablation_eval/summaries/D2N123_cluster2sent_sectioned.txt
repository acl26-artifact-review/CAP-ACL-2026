Chief Complaint
Difficulty swallowing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty swallowing for four to five weeks, accompanied by a feeling of something stuck in their throat when swallowing solid foods, particularly sandwiches. He denies coughing or vomiting blood, and reports no chest pain or shortness of breath. The patient reports feeling 'pretty good' regarding their diabetes and checks his blood sugars approximately 80% of the time, with levels within the target range. He reports his blood pressure is occasionally higher at home. The patient denies weight loss.

Findings
The doctor observes no swelling or masses in the neck, and no redness in the throat. A carotid bruit was not auscultated. Epigastric tenderness was noted.

Assessment
The patient likely has a narrowing in the throat, possibly a stricture.

Plan
The doctor recommends a referral to gastroenterology for a scope. The doctor recommends prescribing Prilosec 20mg once daily and recommends frequent chewing of food and chewing hard foods thoroughly. The doctor orders a hemoglobin A1c repeat and additional blood work including kidney function tests. The doctor orders an eye referral to ophthalmology. The doctor continues the patient on metformin and will schedule a follow-up appointment in one month.
