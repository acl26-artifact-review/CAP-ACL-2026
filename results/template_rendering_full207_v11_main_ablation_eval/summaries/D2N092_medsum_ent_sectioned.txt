Chief Complaint
Pain in the distal radius of the right arm following a tennis injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the distal radius of his right arm following a tennis injury. He describes hitting a backhand shot, missing the ball, and contacting the net pole with his arm, resulting in immediate pain. He notes some swelling, but not significant. He denies numbness in his hand, but reports some mild pain with wrist movement. He reports a previous fall while doing lawn work, resulting in a stab wound to his arm. He takes flonase for allergies. He has no other surgeries reported.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal radius of the right arm. Range of motion is limited due to pain. No fracture is palpated. X-ray demonstrates no fractures or significant abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient presents with a strained and contused right distal radius, likely secondary to a traumatic injury.

Plan
We recommend ice packs to reduce swelling, and 800 milligrams of motrin three times a day with food. Follow up in one week if symptoms do not improve. Schedule physical therapy as appropriate.
