Chief Complaint
Patient reports fatigue and chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports fatigue for the past two months, describing it as pervasive and impacting daily activities. He denies fever or chills. The patient reports chest pain when walking, describing it as a burning sensation, and experiences it approximately three times per week. He denies recent trauma. The patient reports endorsing nasal congestion due to allergies. He denies blood in stool.

Findings
The clinician hears a slight three out of six systolic ejection murmur. The echocardiogram shows a slightly low pumping function of the heart at 45% and mitral regurgitation. The ECG shows stable findings reflecting prior coronary artery disease.

Assessment
The patient presents with fatigue and chest pain, concerning for potential cardiac etiology. Clinical findings are consistent with mitral regurgitation and reduced ejection fraction.

Plan
The clinician plans a cervical MRI. The clinician plans to continue the patient on aspirin. The clinician plans to start the patient on toprol. The clinician orders a lipid panel. The clinician orders a cardiac stress test.
