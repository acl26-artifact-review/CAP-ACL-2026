Chief Complaint
Reports of lower back pain following a back injury sustained while moving boxes.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports lower back pain that began approximately four months ago after moving boxes. He describes the pain as exacerbated by movement and when positioned on his side, with a sensation of 'taping over' and inability to support his upper body weight. He initially felt numbness and tingling in his right toes, which has since resolved. He tried physical therapy at his gym but found it ineffective. He has been taking ibuprofen and acetaminophen with limited relief. He denies any bladder or bowel symptoms.

Findings
Physical examination reveals decreased flexion and extension of the lumbar spine and a positive straight leg raise. The patient demonstrates some spasm of the paraspinal muscles. Vital signs are within normal limits: blood pressure 117/75 mmHg, heart rate 68 bpm, oxygen saturation 95% on room air. Lumbar spine x-ray shows no acute bony abnormality. Right knee is a total knee replacement from 2020.

Assessment
Acute disc herniation of the lumbar spine is suspected.

Plan
Order a lumbar spine MRI to further evaluate the findings. Refer the patient to a physical therapist through the health system. Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily. Discuss the potential for an epidural steroid injection if symptoms persist.
