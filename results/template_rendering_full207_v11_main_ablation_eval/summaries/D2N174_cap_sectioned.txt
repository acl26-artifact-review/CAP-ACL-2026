Chief Complaint
Knee pain related to repetitive motion.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports knee pain that has increased over the past few days, particularly with cycling. He describes stiffness in the mornings and soreness by the afternoon. The pain is bilateral, but more pronounced on the right knee. He reports no injury and denies any other systemic symptoms such as fever or fatigue. He has been increasing his cycling mileage recently, doubling his usual distance. He is currently taking aspirin daily and crestor without issues. He reports no chest pain or shortness of breath. He denies nausea or vomiting. He has a history of lupus and coronary artery disease, but his lupus symptoms have been well-controlled and his heart function is good.

Findings
Physical examination reveals bilateral knee swelling and mild erythema. Palpation of the right knee elicits tenderness. Range of motion is limited bilaterally. A mild effusion is noted on the right knee. The patient's heart and lungs are clear to auscultation. EKG shows normal sinus rhythm. Blood pressure is 130/80 mmHg, and heart rate is 72 bpm. Right knee x-ray shows no bony abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient's knee pain is likely related to overuse and inflammation, possibly exacerbated by increased activity. Given his history of lupus, an autoimmune workup is warranted to rule out a flare.

Plan
Order an autoimmune panel to evaluate for lupus flare. Order a lipid panel and echocardiogram to monitor coronary artery disease. Schedule physical therapy for the patient's knees. Advise the patient to reduce his cycling mileage and use ice packs to manage inflammation. Follow up in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
