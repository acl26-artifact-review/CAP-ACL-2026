Chief Complaint
Right wrist pain and tingling.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right wrist pain, which started approximately one week ago following a minor fall. He describes the pain as sharp and constant, exacerbated by movement. He also reports tingling in his right wrist, which he describes as 'pins and needles.' He reports that ibuprofen provides minimal relief. The patient denies any recent trauma or other medical issues. He states, 'My wrist just hurts all the time now.'

Findings
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation over the dorsum of the right wrist. Range of motion is limited due to pain. Tinel's sign is positive. Phalen's maneuver is positive.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome or focal wrist tendinitis.

Plan
Order wrist X-rays to rule out fracture. Prescribe a wrist brace for six weeks. Recommend physical therapy for evaluation and treatment. Provide instructions on wrist splinting and activity modification. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
