The patient reports intermittent left knee pain that occurs occasionally, less than once a day, when walking. It feels like a 'giveout' and he often catches himself from falling. He frequently uses a grocery cart to help stabilize his knee. He describes the pain as coming and going quickly. He denies any specific injury or recollection of a fall. He reports no pain with palpation of the knee. He states the pain is not interfering with his activities significantly. He has been experiencing slightly elevated blood sugars, around 130-140 in the morning, and reports that his blood pressures have been running around 120/75. He takes farxiga, amlodipine, lisinopril, and hydrochlorothiazide regularly, eight thirty AM and eight thirty PM. He is taking his metformin as well.

O:
Temperature is 98.2°F. Pulse is 72 bpm. Respiratory rate is 16 breaths per minute. Blood pressure is 120/75 mmHg. A slight two over six systolic ejection murmur is appreciated at the left base. Physical exam reveals no ecchymosis or edema, no effusion, and no pain to palpation of the left medial knee. Range of motion is full and unconstrained. X-ray of the left knee shows no evidence of fracture or bony abnormality.

A:
The patient's left knee pain is likely due to arthritis. He has a history of no significant knee injuries. His blood pressure is slightly low, and his blood sugars are elevated. The murmur is benign.

P:
Rescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily. Order an echocardiogram. Order a hemoglobin A1c. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Schedule follow-up in one month to assess response to treatment and address blood sugar control. Advise patient to continue with regular medication adherence. Counsel patient on the importance of physical therapy to strengthen the muscles around the knee and prevent further problems. Arrange follow-up with a diabetes educator.
