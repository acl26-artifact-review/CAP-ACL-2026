Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a six-week history of progressive left shoulder pain, exacerbated by overhead activities and lying on the affected side. He denies recent trauma. He reports partial relief with acetaminophen. He denies fever, chills, or other systemic symptoms.

Findings
Physical exam reveals limited active abduction of the left shoulder and pain with impingement testing. Range of motion is reduced. No signs of acute inflammation.

Assessment
Clinical presentation is most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
Order shoulder radiographs to evaluate for rotator cuff pathology. Initiate physical therapy for targeted strengthening and range of motion. Continue acetaminophen as needed for pain relief. Schedule follow-up in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
