Chief Complaint
Reports increased shortness of breath and difficulty breathing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports increased shortness of breath and difficulty breathing for approximately two years following a recent move to a large property with numerous deer. He describes episodes of sudden shortness of breath, often accompanied by eye watering, which he attributes to allergies. He reports using a nebulizer prescribed at an urgent care visit for similar symptoms. He also reports heavy drinking and occasional diarrhea. He denies lip or throat swelling or past allergies. The patient's dog also experiences eye watering. He reports experiencing symptoms in the house, outside, and when his cat is present.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal vital signs, including pulse ox at 98%. Examination does not reveal any rash. Lungs exhibit faint expiratory wheezing bilaterally. No stridor is audible. The patient denies lip or throat swelling.

Assessment
Newly diagnosed asthma with possible allergic component. Dyspnea is present.

Plan
Prescribe an albuterol inhaler for immediate relief of shortness of breath. Prescribe singulair ten milligrams daily to reduce asthma exacerbations. Order allergy testing via skin testing. Schedule follow-up in one week to complete allergy testing.
