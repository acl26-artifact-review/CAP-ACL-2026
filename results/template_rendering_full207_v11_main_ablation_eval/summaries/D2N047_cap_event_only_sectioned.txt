Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports persistent left shoulder pain for the past month, exacerbated by overhead activities and prolonged arm elevation. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with minimal relief. He describes the pain as a dull ache, with occasional sharp spikes. He rates the pain as a 6/10 on a numerical pain scale.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild tenderness to palpation over the anterior shoulder, limited range of motion in abduction and external rotation, and positive impingement signs. Neurological exam is intact, with full strength and sensation in the affected extremity.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is most consistent with subacromial impingement syndrome.

Plan
Prescribe a course of NSAIDs for pain management. Refer to physical therapy for targeted exercises to improve range of motion and strengthen rotator cuff muscles. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 4 weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
