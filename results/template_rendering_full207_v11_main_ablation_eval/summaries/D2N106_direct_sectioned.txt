Chief Complaint
New onset shortness of breath and intermittent respiratory symptoms.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing new onset shortness of breath over the past several months, particularly noticeable when near his cat or outdoors. He describes episodes of sudden shortness of breath, sometimes accompanied by eye irritation, which he attributes to allergies. He reports using an inhaler previously prescribed after an urgent care visit for similar symptoms. He also notes intermittent diarrhea following periods of heavy drinking. He denies any prior history of allergies or asthma. He reports no current symptoms of nausea, vomiting, or lip/throat swelling.

Findings
Physical examination reveals normal vital signs, including a pulse oxygen saturation of 98% on room air. Lung auscultation reveals faint expiratory wheezing bilaterally. Examination does not reveal any rash or angioedema. No stridor is appreciated.

Assessment
Newly diagnosed allergic asthma.

Plan
Prescribe albuterol inhaler for rescue use as needed. Prescribe singulair 10 mg daily to reduce asthma exacerbations. Order allergy testing including skin testing. Schedule follow-up in one week to complete allergy testing and discuss results. Advise patient to avoid cat exposure and to discuss this with his veterinarian.
