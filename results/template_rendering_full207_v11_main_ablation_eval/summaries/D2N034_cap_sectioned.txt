Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain for the last three weeks.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for the last three weeks, stating it's not getting better. He recalls no specific injury or trauma. He describes the pain as constant and reports it's worse with overhead activity and when lying on the affected side. He has been doing a lot of work in his basement, including putting in a new ceiling, and speculates it might be related. He hasn't tried icing the shoulder recently. He hasn't taken any medication for the pain. He reports limited range of motion and difficulty lifting his hands overhead. He mentions numbness or tingling in the arm or fingers, but denies experiencing it currently.

Findings
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder. Palpation of the greater tuberosity elicits tenderness. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the left hand. Sensory examination is intact to light touch. The x-ray of the shoulder was normal, showing no fractures or bony abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to a rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
The doctor orders an MRI of the left shoulder to rule out other pathology. The doctor is referring the patient to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks to strengthen the shoulder muscles. The doctor may consider a steroid injection of the shoulder if the patient's symptoms do not improve. The patient can continue to take acetaminophen as needed.
