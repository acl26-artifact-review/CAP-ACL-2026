Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a trampoline injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports sustaining a knee injury while using a trampoline, describing a 'pop' and immediate pain. He states this was the first time attempting this activity. He has not taken any medication for the pain beyond elevating the knee. The pain has persisted for approximately one week, and he has been managing it with elevation. He reports a slight limp when walking.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild ecchymosis and edema on the lateral aspect of the right knee. Range of motion is limited, with pain on flexion and extension. Gait is slightly altered with a limp. Vital signs are stable. An x-ray of the right knee revealed no evidence of fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is consistent with a lateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg daily for pain and swelling. I am referring the patient to physical therapy for strengthening and rehabilitation. I will order an A1C and lipid panel to monitor diabetes and cardiovascular risk. I will also order an echo to assess hypertension and adjust medication as needed. Follow up in four weeks to assess progress.
