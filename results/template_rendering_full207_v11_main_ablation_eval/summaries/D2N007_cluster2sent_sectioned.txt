Chief Complaint
Increased depressive symptoms and chronic back pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports increased depressive symptoms over the past year, attributed to remote work and social isolation. He has been taking Prozac 20 milligrams daily. He also experiences chronic back pain, with occasional numbness and tingling in his legs, which improves with position changes. He has a history of coronary artery bypass grafting at age 27, which he reports has been proceeding well. He denies recent trauma. The patient's abdomen is soft with some tenderness to palpation of the lumbar spine, and he reports decreased flexion of his back.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a grade 3/6 systolic ejection murmur, no edema in the lower extremities, and normal strength. The thyroid is not enlarged. The patient appears comfortable and denies distress.

Assessment
The patient presents with chronic depression and chronic back pain.

Plan
Increase Prozac to 40 milligrams daily and order a physical therapy referral. Order an echocardiogram to monitor cardiac function. Order aspirin 81 milligrams daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress.
