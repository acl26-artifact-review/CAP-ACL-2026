Chief Complaint
Worsening headaches over the past few months.

History of Present Illness
Mr. Ward presented to Dr. Howard with worsening headaches over the past few months, initially described as three out of 10 in severity, gradually worsening to six out of 10. The headaches are located behind his eyes and last for several hours, with no alleviating factors. He denies any prior headaches, illness, fever, rash, neck stiffness, numbness, tingling, or passing out. He has tried acetaminophen with partial relief. He recently noticed he's bumping into door frames, which prompted his concern. He was adopted and has no family history of relevant diseases. He works as a computer programmer and is married with one child. He denies alcohol, tobacco, or recreational drug use.

Findings
Examination reveals alert and oriented patient with appropriate affect and fluent speech. Cranial nerve examination is grossly intact. Visual acuity and eye movements are normal. Pupils are equal and reactive. Visual field testing reveals bitemporal hemianopia. Color vision is normal. No focal motor or sensory deficits in the upper or lower extremities.

Assessment
Mr. Ward is a 52-year-old male with a benign pituitary adenoma, incidentally discovered during workup for worsening headaches. He is symptomatic with clinical and radiographic evidence of optic chiasmal compression, necessitating surgical intervention to excise and decompress the pituitary fossa.

Plan
Refer the patient to the ophthalmology department for a comprehensive visual field assessment prior to surgery. Schedule a consultation with the surgical scheduler, Deborah, to arrange for surgical evaluation and scheduling. Monitor visual field changes post-operatively.
