Chief Complaint
Right elbow pain with radiation to the neck.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports onset of right elbow pain approximately one day ago, with no specific injury. He describes the pain as aching and heavy, extending up the arm to the neck. He denies recent trauma. He reports taking motrin for the pain, which provides minimal relief. He mowed the grass yesterday and mentions some discomfort, but no specific injury. He has been doing some household chores. He denies any numbness or weakness. He reports no prior similar episodes.

Findings
Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the distal right elbow and upper arm. Range of motion is limited by pain. Blood pressure is 150/90 mmHg. Heart rate is 72 bpm, regular. A slight murmur is auscultated. Glucose level is pending. EKG is ordered.

Assessment
Right elbow pain with radiation to the neck, potentially musculoskeletal in origin. Rule out cardiac etiology given chest pain and associated arm pain.

Plan
Order an electrocardiogram to evaluate for cardiac ischemia. Administer aspirin and nitroglycerin as ordered. Obtain a glucose test. Continue metformin at 500 mg twice daily. Schedule follow-up in 24-48 hours to assess response to treatment and further evaluate. Refer to cardiology for further evaluation if EKG is abnormal.
