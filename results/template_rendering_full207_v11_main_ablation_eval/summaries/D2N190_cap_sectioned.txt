Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a bicycle fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports falling off a tandem bicycle with his partner approximately two weeks ago. He states he saw a squirrel and lost control, subsequently impacting the curb. He took ibuprofen and applied a band-aid to the knee, but the pain persists daily. He has been taking ibuprofen, but it's not providing adequate relief. He describes the pain as lingering and worsening, particularly with inclines. He reports feeling weak and mentions difficulty walking up hills. He previously played sports in high school and is still working hard. He states he needs to work harder. The patient took ibuprofen.

Findings
Examination reveals tenderness to palpation on the medial joint line of the right knee. The patient reports a dime-size abrasion over the medial patella. Range of motion is full, with mild laxity noted on valgus stress and a one-plus posterior drawer test. Lachman's test is negative. Patellar tracking is full and without crepitus. The patient reports clicking in the knee. There is no tenderness to palpation of the posterior aspect of the knee. The lateral and popliteal spaces are nontender. The left knee examination reveals full range of motion and symmetric flexion and extension. The left knee has no instability.

Assessment
Right knee injury, likely involving a sprain of the posterior cruciate ligament, with possible associated medial collateral ligament injury. The patient's presentation suggests a potential for rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
A brace will be fitted to the patient today. A prescription for ibuprofen will be sent to the patient. The patient will follow up with physical therapy in 4-6 weeks. A referral to physical therapy will be arranged. The doctor recommends leg removal. The doctor recommends the patient work harder.
