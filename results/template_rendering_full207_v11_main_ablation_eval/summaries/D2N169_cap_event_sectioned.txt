Chief Complaint
Shortness of breath and lightheadedness following a long bike ride.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing shortness of breath and lightheadedness approximately one hour after a three-hour bike ride over the weekend. He describes this as a surprise, as he typically experiences no issues with his usual morning bike rides. He also recalls a mild chest pain during the episode, which resolved within an hour. He has a history of migraines, managed with imitrex, and reflux, controlled with protonix. He also had cataract surgery four and a half months ago, which has resulted in good vision. He experienced a mild cough with his first cold a year and a half ago, which resolved. He denies fever, nausea, or abdominal pain.

Findings
Physical exam reveals normal oxygenation, neck mobility is normal, heart rhythm is regular, no murmurs are auscultated. Lungs exhibit trace lower extremity edema. Pulmonary function tests are normal. Chest x-ray is normal. Labs are unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient's presentation suggests a possible asthma exacerbation triggered by exercise and potentially exacerbated by allergies. Given the history of mild cough and wheezing, this is a reasonable clinical impression.

Plan
Prescribe an albuterol inhaler (two puffs every four hours as needed for wheezing or shortness of breath). Order a pulmonary referral. Continue current imitrex regimen as needed for migraines. Follow up in two weeks to assess response to treatment and symptoms. Advise the patient to call immediately if shortness of breath recurs.
