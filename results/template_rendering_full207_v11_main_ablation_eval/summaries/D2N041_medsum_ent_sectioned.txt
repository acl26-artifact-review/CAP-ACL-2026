Chief Complaint
The patient reports a sore throat for four days accompanied by fever, chills, and difficulty swallowing.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing a sore throat for approximately four days, accompanied by fever, chills, and difficulty swallowing. He reports experiencing fever last night and chills last night, along with significant difficulty swallowing. He also reports sweating last night. He denies recent trauma. He reports that he drinks water and wine primarily. He notes a stuffy nose and reports swollen tonsils. He indicates that his household has children with runny noses and coughs. He recalls having strep throat as a child. He works as a cashier at a supermarket. He received a COVID vaccine and experienced side effects. He is currently taking lisinopril 20 mg daily with multivitamin and vitamin D, and metformin 1000 mg twice daily. He reports watching his salt intake and does not enjoy sports. He does not like sports.

Findings
Physical examination reveals a low-grade fever of 100.4°F. Vital signs are blood pressure 130/80 mmHg and heart rate 84 bpm. Examination of the face reveals no tenderness to palpation of the frontal or maxillary sinuses. Nasal examination reveals edema and erythema of the nasal turbinates with clear discharge. The throat demonstrates erythema and edema of the peritonsillar space with exudates. Cervical lymphadenopathy is present on the right side. Lung auscultation reveals coarse rhonchi at the bases, which clear with cough. The heart rhythm is regular and without murmurs.

Assessment
The patient presents with signs of an upper respiratory infection, with concern for possible streptococcal pharyngitis based on clinical findings and examination.

Plan
A rapid strep test will be performed to assess for streptococcal infection. If positive, amoxicillin 500 mg three times daily for ten days will be prescribed, along with lidocaine for swishing and swallowing to alleviate throat pain. Ibuprofen may be taken as needed for pain relief. Lisinopril will be increased to 40 mg daily to better control blood pressure. A lipid panel and hemoglobin A1c will be ordered to monitor cardiovascular and diabetic status. Follow-up will be scheduled in four weeks if symptoms persist.
