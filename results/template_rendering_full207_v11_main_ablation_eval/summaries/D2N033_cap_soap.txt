S:
The patient reports a history of pain in his right knee following a fall on a trampoline four days ago. He describes feeling a pop at the time of the injury and reports significant swelling afterward. He reports difficulty with weight-bearing and limited range of motion in the knee, particularly with flexion and extension. He reports pain with palpation on the inside of the knee and with extension. He reports swelling in the right knee following the injury. The patient reports taking ibuprofen and applying ice to the knee, which provided some relief.

O:
The examination reveals significant swelling in the right knee. Palpation elicits tenderness on the medial aspect of the knee. Range of motion is limited, with decreased flexion and extension. The McMurray's test is negative. Vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient's presentation is consistent with an injury to the medial collateral ligament (MCL) of the right knee. Further evaluation with an MRI is warranted to assess the extent of the injury.

P:
The doctor orders a straight leg brace to immobilize the knee and promote healing. The doctor prescribes mobic for pain relief and to reduce inflammation. The doctor orders an MRI to evaluate the extent of the MCL injury. The doctor recommends applying ice to the knee for 15 minutes every hour. The doctor orders a refill on metformin. The doctor orders a hemoglobin A1c test.
