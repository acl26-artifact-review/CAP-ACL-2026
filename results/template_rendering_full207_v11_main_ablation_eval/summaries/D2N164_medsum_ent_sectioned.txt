Chief Complaint
The patient reports chest pain when walking.

History of Present Illness
The patient describes chest pain as dull, occurring with walking, and lasting approximately three months. The pain is exacerbated by walking and relieved by stopping. The patient reports difficulty keeping up with walking due to the pain. He reports elevated blood sugars due to decreased activity levels and is trying to improve his diet. He reports no nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. He has an ophthalmology appointment scheduled in two weeks. The patient reports a history of high blood pressure, but does not recall being told about it previously.

Findings
Physical examination reveals no fever, no shortness of breath, no diaphoresis. The patient's EKG demonstrates no acute ischemic changes. Examination reveals a two out of six systolic ejection murmur. The patient exhibits mild lower extremity edema, 1+ non-pitting. Lung sounds are clear with no crackles or rhonchi. Heart sounds are regular rate and rhythm. Neurologic exam is unremarkable. Hemoglobin A1c was 7.0.

Assessment
The patient's presentation is concerning for unstable angina, likely exacerbated by underlying diabetes and suspected high blood pressure. Clinical suspicion is for coronary artery disease.

Plan
I am ordering aspirin 81 mg daily and nitroglycerin for use as needed for chest pain. I am also ordering a hemoglobin A1c test to be repeated in two weeks. I am prescribing hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily to manage blood pressure and edema. I am referring the patient to cardiology for a stress test to evaluate for coronary artery disease. I have scheduled a follow-up appointment in two weeks to monitor blood pressure and assess response to medications. I have instructed the patient to monitor for chest pain and to seek immediate medical attention if it occurs.
