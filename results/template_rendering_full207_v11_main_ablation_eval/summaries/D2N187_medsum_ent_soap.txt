S:
The patient reports sudden visual distortions and blurring of vision, which began approximately one week ago. He describes difficulty reading and seeing straight lines. He also reports trouble adjusting to dim lighting in restaurants, specifically at McDonald's in Arlington. The patient admits to smoking a pack of cigarettes daily and has attempted to quit several times. He reports inconsistently keeping track of his blood pressure readings.

O:
The anterior segment examination reveals posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes. The cornea is clear in both eyes. Cup-to-disc ratio is 0.2 in the right eye and 0.3 in the left eye. Fundus examination reveals drusen in the macula, thickening of the retina, subretinal fluid and exudates accumulation, and a hemorrhage in the left eye. Fluorescein angiography reveals focal areas of leakage in the right eye.

A:
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with neovascular age-related macular degeneration, likely advanced to a stage that typically leads to more severe vision loss.

P:
Order a monthly injection of Lucentis into the vitreous portion of the eye, administered after numbing the surface. Schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to assess treatment response and monitor for any changes in vision. Counsel the patient on the potential for vision loss despite treatment and the importance of adherence to the medication schedule.
