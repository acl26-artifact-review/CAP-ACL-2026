Chief Complaint
Worsening headaches for the past few months.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports worsening headaches over the past few months, initially described as three out of ten in severity, which have gradually increased to six out of ten. He describes the pain as a dull ache located behind his eyes, and reports it is worse in the morning and lasts for a few hours. He denies fever, rash, neck stiffness, numbness, tingling, or passing out. He reports bumping into door frames recently and denies any medical problems or medications, having previously tried tylonol for the headaches. He reports no family history of disease and works as a computer programmer. He denies alcohol, smoking, or recreational drug use. The patient notes the onset of vision loss, specifically bitemporal hemianopia, approximately three months ago.

Findings
Examination reveals the patient to be alert, oriented, and with appropriate affect. Cranial nerve examination is grossly intact. Visual acuity and eye movements are normal. Pupils are equal and reactive. Visual field testing reveals bitemporal hemianopia, and color vision is normal. The MRI demonstrates a well-defined pituitary adenoma compressing the optic chiasm.

Assessment
The patient is diagnosed with a pituitary adenoma, evidenced by incidental discovery on MRI and clinical presentation with optic chiasm compression and bitemporal hemianopia.

Plan
The patient will be referred to an ophthalmologist for a comprehensive visual field evaluation prior to surgical consideration. A transsphenoidal approach to surgical excision of the adenoma is planned. The patient will be scheduled with the surgical scheduler for further evaluation and scheduling of the procedure. Follow-up will be arranged to monitor visual function post-operatively.
