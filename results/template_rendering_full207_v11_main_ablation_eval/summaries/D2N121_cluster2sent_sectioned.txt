Chief Complaint
Right knee pain following a skiing injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right knee pain following a ski accident where he twisted his knee. He took over-the-counter ibuprofen and ice packs for pain relief. He denies any numbness or tingling in his foot and reports no prior surgeries. He has been taking lisinopril 20mg daily for hypertension and reports no side effects.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure is 120/77 mmHg. Examination reveals mild edema and pain to palpation of the right medial knee with no lymphadenopathy. The heart and lungs are clear to auscultation. Range of motion is limited, with negative varus and valgus tests and a negative Lachman sign. Pulses are palpable. X-ray pending.

Assessment
The patient has a medial collateral ligament strain of the right knee.

Plan
Order a right knee x-ray to rule out fracture. Prescribe meloxicam 15mg daily for pain and inflammation. Refer to physical therapy for rehabilitation. Advise the patient to rest the knee and avoid skiing until further evaluation. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess healing and adjust treatment as needed. Discuss the potential for skiing tomorrow, but emphasize the need for continued rest and physical therapy before resuming activity.
