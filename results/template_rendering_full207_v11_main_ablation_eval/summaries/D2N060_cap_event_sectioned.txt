Chief Complaint
Foot pain following a soccer injury.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports foot pain following a soccer injury where she fell and twisted her right foot. She describes the injury as occurring during a game when attempting to steal the ball, resulting in a fall and entanglement with another player. She was immediately helped off the field. Since the fall, she has been keeping the foot elevated, icing it, and taking ibuprofen. Her pain level without ibuprofen is 3, and with ibuprofen, it's 8. She has played soccer since she was 5 and has never had a foot injury before. She denies any numbness or tingling in the left foot.

Findings
Physical exam reveals bruising and swelling on the right foot, tenderness to palpation, and a positive piano key test. Capillary refill is less than 3 seconds on the dorsalis pedis. Motor and sensory function are intact. The right foot is stable.

Assessment
The patient has a Lisfranc fracture, a fracture to one of the second metatarsal bones at the top of the foot, requiring surgical intervention to stabilize the bones and ligaments with plates and screws.

Plan
An MRI will be ordered to assess ligament injuries. Surgery is recommended to restore bone alignment and prevent long-term complications such as arch loss and arthritis. The patient will be in a cast and use crutches for 6-8 weeks, with gradual weight-bearing as tolerated. Follow-up will be scheduled in four weeks, and the patient will return for further evaluation and rehabilitation.
