Chief Complaint
Recurrent chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing recurrent chest pain, approximately three times per week, which he describes as a burning sensation. The pain occurs when walking and subsides with rest. He denies nausea, vomiting, or shortness of breath. He reports the pain began approximately one month ago. The patient reports congestion due to allergies. He has been doing well with his back pain following a recent steroid injection and carpal tunnel release.

Findings
Physical exam reveals a systolic ejection murmur of 3/6 in the right sternal border. Blood pressure is well controlled. Oxygen saturation is normal. No lower extremity edema is noted. The echocardiogram shows mild mitral regurgitation and a pumping function of 45%. The ECG demonstrates stable findings consistent with prior coronary artery disease.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with recurrent angina pectoris, likely related to underlying coronary artery disease.

Plan
Continue aspirin and toprol. Order a stress test to evaluate for coronary artery blockages. Order a lipid panel and initiate Crestor 20 mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to treatment.
