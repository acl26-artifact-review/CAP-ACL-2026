Chief Complaint
Yearly follow-up for asthma, allergic rhinitis, and food allergy.

History of Present Illness
The patient is being seen for a yearly follow-up for asthma, allergic rhinitis, and food allergy. She was switched from symbicort to wixela due to insurance changes since her last office appointment in April 2020. She uses zyrtec in the spring, summer, and fall for her seasonal allergy symptoms, which tend to work well. She has known peanut allergy, she tolerates almonds and cashews. She reports that she has been trying to use her daily inhaler less and less because she is trying to go into the military soon. She wants to be able to operate at a certain fitness level without an inhaler. She has been taking it less and less. She rarely uses her albuterol inhaler, only before strenuous activity. She has not had any urgent care or ER visits for breathing issues over the past year. She has been vaping and smoking, but has been trying to quit. She completed a breathing test a couple of years ago and her numbers were lower at that time.

Findings
Examination reveals clear ears with a little wax on both sides. The mouth is clear. The lungs sound terrific. The patient's heart rate is normal.

Assessment
Asthma, allergic rhinitis, and peanut allergy.

Plan
Order a follow-up breathing test in four months. Continue wixela twice daily. Continue zyrtec as needed during allergy season. Refill the albuterol inhaler and epipen. Schedule a follow-up appointment in 25 minutes.
