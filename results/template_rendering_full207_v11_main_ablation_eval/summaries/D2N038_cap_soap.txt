S:
The patient reports he was surprised to find positive lab work for hepatitis C, as he had not experienced any symptoms. He states he used to party a lot and even used intravenous drugs, but he has been clean for over fifteen years. The patient also reports smoking one to two cigarettes per day and having a hard time kicking that habit. He admits to having a beer here and there every day. The patient reports having high blood pressure and depression. He also reports having diabetes.

O:
A grade two out of six systolic ejection murmur is appreciated on physical exam. The lungs are clear to auscultation. The patient's skin is clear of jaundice. The abdomen is soft with no hepatosplenomegaly. The patient's vital signs are within normal limits.

A:
The patient has hepatitis C. The patient's history includes intravenous drug use, smoking, and family history of high blood pressure, diabetes, and depression.

P:
The doctor will have the nurse place orders for further blood work, including hep C RNA and HCV genotype testing. The doctor will also order an ultrasound elastography with the liver to assess fibrosis. The doctor recommends scheduling a follow-up appointment in three weeks to discuss the results and determine the next steps for treatment.
