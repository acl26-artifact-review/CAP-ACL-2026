S:
The patient reports left shoulder pain for the past three weeks, exacerbated by reaching and overhead activities, and relieved by Tylenol. He denies numbness or tingling in his arm or fingers. He has not previously experienced shoulder pain. He has not tried physical therapy before. He has not used ice packs.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder with tenderness to palpation. Capillary refill is less than three seconds in the left arm. Neurological exam is intact. X-ray of the shoulder is normal.

A:
The patient's symptoms are most likely due to rotator cuff tendinopathy. There are no acute findings suggesting other causes of shoulder pain.

P:
I am referring the patient to physical therapy for approximately six to eight weeks. The patient can continue taking Tylenol as needed. I will consider a steroid injection if symptoms do not improve.
