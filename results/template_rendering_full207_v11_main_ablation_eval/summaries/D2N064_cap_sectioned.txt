Chief Complaint
Left ankle pain following a fall.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports pain in the left ankle following a fall yesterday while walking. He describes feeling an 'extended and stretched' sensation at the time of the fall and has experienced persistent pain for the past few days. He reports elevating and icing the ankle and taking ibuprofen. He denies any prior ankle injuries. He and his wife have been walking on new trails at the new Rex Center, and he plans to check them out. He has been working from home since COVID and has been taking walks regularly.

Findings
Physical examination reveals ecchymosis over the lateral malleolus and swelling of the soft tissue. Capillary refill is brisk bilaterally. Dorsalis pedis pulses are strong and equal bilaterally. Neurological exam is intact. X-rays demonstrate no fracture or bony abnormalities.

Assessment
The patient has an ankle sprain of the lateral ligament complex, likely secondary to the recent fall.

Plan
I am going to prescribe an air cast and crutches. The patient should elevate and ice the ankle. I would like to schedule a follow-up visit in two weeks to monitor progress. Continue taking NSAIDs as needed. Please contact the office if symptoms persist or worsen. ,
