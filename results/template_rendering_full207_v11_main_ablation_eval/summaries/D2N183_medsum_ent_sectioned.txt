Chief Complaint
Follow-up for acne.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports an acne follow-up visit. He reports that he has been using clindamycin lotion, tretinoin cream (4-5%), and benzoyl peroxide wash (5-10%) as prescribed on 3/3/21. He reports no redness, peeling, or dryness from the medications. He states he washes his chest and back with the higher strength benzoyl peroxide wash but does not consistently do so. He reports that the acne on his face has improved, but there is a mild flare on his back. He reports that he has not refilled his tretinoin cream.

Findings
Physical exam reveals scattered inflammatory papules and pustules across the upper back and posterior shoulders. There are less than 5 inflammatory papules on the forehead, cheeks, and chin. Acne vulgaris is improved and well controlled on the face, but there is a mild flare on the back. Vital signs are within normal limits.

Assessment
Acne vulgaris, mild flare on the back.

Plan
Continue current treatment plan for the face. Initiate treatment with PanOxyl 10% foaming wash to the back in the shower daily. Order a one-year prescription for clindamycin with 11 refills and a refill for the tretinoin cream with three refills. Send patients home with samples and coupons for PanOxyl 10%.
