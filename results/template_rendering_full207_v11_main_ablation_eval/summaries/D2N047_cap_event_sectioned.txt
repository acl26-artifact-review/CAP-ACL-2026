Chief Complaint
Difficulty urinating

History of Present Illness
The patient reports difficulty urinating for approximately six months, with a perceived weakening of the urinary stream. He describes occasional difficulty emptying his bladder fully, sometimes requiring prolonged effort. He reports waking up three to four times per night to urinate. He denies any burning during urination. He has had intermittent diarrhea over the past week. He has never had urinary retention or seen a urologist. He was diagnosed with coronary artery disease and stent placement in 2018 and is currently taking lipitor, aspirin, and metoprolol.

Findings
Physical exam reveals an enlarged prostate and a slight systolic ejection murmur at the left base. The abdomen is non-tender and non-distended. Lung exam is clear bilaterally.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with prostate issues, including possible prostate enlargement and urinary retention. Further investigation with PSA testing and referral to a urologist is warranted.

Plan
Order PSA, flomax (0.4 mg daily), and schedule a referral to a urologist for further evaluation. Continue current medications: lipitor 40 mg daily, aspirin, and metoprolol. Order a hemoglobin A1c and routine blood tests. Follow up in 3-4 weeks.
