Chief Complaint
Chest pain

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing persistent, dull chest pain for the past three weeks. The pain is described as a tightness and is not sharp. It is worse with exertion and relieved by rest. He denies shortness of breath, palpitations, or fever. He reports a family history of coronary artery disease.

Findings
Blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 72 bpm, regular rhythm. Auscultation reveals normal heart sounds. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally.

Assessment
Chest pain, etiology uncertain. Differential includes musculoskeletal pain, angina, and anxiety.

Plan
Order ECG, troponin, and chest X-ray. Counsel patient on risk factors for coronary artery disease and the importance of lifestyle modifications. Schedule follow-up in 24-48 hours to review results and assess symptom progression.
