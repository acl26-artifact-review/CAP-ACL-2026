The patient reports a fear of injury following a twisting fall two and a half weeks ago. He describes a clicking sensation in his right knee, particularly when squatting, and reports feeling 'locked' when standing on one foot for an extended period. The patient also reports numbness in his right foot, dependent on the shoe he wears. He takes ibuprofen but isn't a big pill taker and reports it doesn't significantly impact his symptoms during the day. He reports foot pain and toe numbness. The patient rides a bike and enjoys music rides.

O:
A slight edema is noted on the right knee with mild effusion. Palpation reveals no pain. Range of motion is full. Lachman's test is negative. Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses are palpable. The patient's skin is intact and dry. The patient's right foot shows signs of previous toe injuries.

A:
The primary problem is a lateral collateral ligament strain of the right knee. The patient's symptoms are consistent with this diagnosis. The patient's toe numbness is likely related to previous injuries.

P:
I am prescribing meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain management. I am ordering physical therapy to strengthen the knee and prevent future injuries. I will follow up with the patient in two weeks to assess progress.
