Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain that has been present for approximately six weeks. He describes the pain as waxing and waning, particularly when lying on his left side. He denies any specific injury or trauma, although he notes that he has been actively renovating his basement, involving a lot of lifting and reaching. He has previously taken acetaminophen for the pain, which provides some relief. He reports that the pain is exacerbated when raising his arm overhead. He is unable to lie on his left side due to the pain.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited range of motion in the left shoulder, particularly with abduction and external rotation. Tenderness is noted over the greater tuberosity of the humerus. Neurovascular examination reveals brisk capillary refill in the left hand. Cervical examination is unremarkable. X-ray of the left shoulder shows no fracture or bony abnormality.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and physical exam findings are most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy.

Plan
I am ordering an MRI of the left shoulder to further evaluate the rotator cuff tendons. I am also referring the patient to physical therapy for a six to eight week course. The patient should continue taking acetaminophen PRN for pain relief. I will follow up with the patient in approximately two weeks to assess progress.
