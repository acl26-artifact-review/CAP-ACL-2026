Chief Complaint
Severe right upper arm pain following a suspected fracture.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports severe right upper arm pain, accompanied by swelling and erythema over the right shoulder. He has been taking ibuprofen every six hours, but reports minimal relief. He also reports having gallstones and a previous lumbar fusion six years ago. He denies numbness or tingling in the right arm and reports no medication allergies. He states he has been taking Pepcid. The patient requests assistance with getting a long arm cast and sling.

Findings
Examination reveals normal pulses and generally normal physical exam findings. The x-ray demonstrates a proximal humerus fracture.

Assessment
Proximal humerus fracture.

Plan
Prescribe lortab 500 mg, one to two tablets every six hours as needed for pain. Order a long arm cast and sling. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess bone realignment and discuss potential surgical intervention if necessary. Dragon, please order the prescribed medications and procedures.
