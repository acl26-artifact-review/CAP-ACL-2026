The patient reports right hip pain since February 2020, which is worse with movement, particularly when pivoting and standing. He rates the pain as 2 to 7 out of 10. He denies fever, chills, tingling, numbness, or bowel/bladder problems. He reports decreased range of motion with exertion.

O:
Physical exam reveals limited range of motion in the right hip with resisted flexion and palpation. Hip flexion elicits pain.

A:
The patient's symptoms and exam findings are consistent with right hip degenerative joint disease.

P:
The clinician plans a low-impact exercise program, the use of NSAIDs, a cane, and a cortisone injection into the right hip joint. The clinician explained the risks of injection, including needles, sterile technique, and COVID precautions.
