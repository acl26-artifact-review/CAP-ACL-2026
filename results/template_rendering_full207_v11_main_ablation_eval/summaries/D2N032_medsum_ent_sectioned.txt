Chief Complaint
Right hip pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports right hip pain since February 2020, which has been worsening with movement. He describes the pain as present when pivoting and whenever he moves, rating it 2-7 on a scale. The pain started after walking around the infusion room and has now become present with any movement. He reports feeling better when still, but does not want to stay still. He has had multiple surgeries, including PCL, infertility, and gallbladder removal, and his family history includes high blood pressure, diabetes, thyroid disease, heart disease, kidney disease, and gastric ulcers. He is allergic to percocet, vicodin, and regulin and has experienced intentional weight loss of 110 pounds, achieved through Weight Watchers, which he has been participating in for over a year.

Findings
Physical examination revealed no ankle edema or calf tenderness. Vital signs were blood pressure 115/75, pulse 67, and no fever or chills. Neurological examination was unremarkable, with intact sensory and motor function and normal deep tendon reflexes. Range of motion in the right hip was decreased with pain during internal and external rotation and with resisted hip flexion.

Assessment
Based on the patient's history and physical examination findings, the most likely diagnosis is right hip degenerative joint disease.

Plan
We will provide a handout with low-impact exercise recommendations. The patient will be prescribed NSAIDs as needed for pain relief and instructed to use a cane to offload the right side. We will proceed with a cortisone injection into the right hip joint today, explaining the risks of injection, sterile technique, and COVID precautions. Follow-up will be scheduled in three months for repeat injection if needed.
