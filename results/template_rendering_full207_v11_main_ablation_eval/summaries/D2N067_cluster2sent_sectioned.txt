Chief Complaint
Right knee pain and instability.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of knee injury sustained while skiing two weeks ago, resulting from colliding with a tree. The left knee previously underwent surgery following a back injury. He describes persistent pain, stiffness, and swelling in the right knee, particularly in the morning, accompanied by a shooting pain radiating to his ankle. He has been using a brace and applying THC cream to the affected area. He reports no other medications besides THC gummies. The patient's pain is rated 7/10 and lasts for several hours. He denies any recent hospitalizations or surgeries other than the previous knee surgery.

Findings
Physical examination reveals mild swelling and bruising in the right knee. Range of motion is limited due to pain, with decreased dorsiflexion and plantar flexion. Palpation of the medial aspect of the right knee elicits tenderness. X-ray findings demonstrate mild arthritis but no fracture.

Assessment
The patient presents with a strained posterior cruciate ligament in the right knee, accompanied by osteoarthritis.

Plan
Prescribe meloxicam 15 mg daily for right knee pain and ibuprofen 800 mg twice daily as needed. Recommend a knee brace for support. Order physical therapy to address strength and stability. Advise against skiing and gymnastics for now. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess progress and adjust treatment as needed.
