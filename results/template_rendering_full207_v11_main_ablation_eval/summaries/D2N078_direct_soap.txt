S:
The patient reports a right knee injury following a fall while working on his property one week ago. He describes pain with flexion and extension, particularly with pressure applied to the knee. He reports some swelling and a palpable pop sensation. He rates his pain as 4-5 out of 10 when mobile. He is currently taking ibuprofen for swelling.

O:
Physical exam reveals tenderness to palpation of the medial aspect of the right knee, pain with flexion and extension, and edema with effusion around the knee. The right knee x-ray was negative for fractures or bony abnormalities. Blood pressure was 150/70 mmHg. Heart rate was 78 bpm. Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally. Grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur is present.

A:
The patient has a medial collateral ligament strain of the right knee. The examination findings are consistent with this diagnosis.

P:
Rescribe ibuprofen 800 mg twice daily for pain and inflammation. Order a knee brace for support. Recommend icing the knee 20 minutes, three to four times daily. Refer to nutrition counseling to address dietary sodium intake. Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily. Schedule follow-up in one week to assess healing and adjust treatment as needed.
