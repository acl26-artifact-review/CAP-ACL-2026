Chief Complaint
Left shoulder pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports left shoulder pain for approximately six weeks, exacerbated by overhead activities and when lying on his affected side. He initially experienced soreness after working in the backyard, including projects such as installing an outdoor shower, extending a patio, and building flower boxes. He describes the pain as a tenderness on the outside of the shoulder, particularly when raising his arm. He has a history of a baseball injury to the same shoulder and has previously experienced similar issues. He has been managing the pain with over-the-counter Advil and icing. He denies numbness in his arm or hands. He also reports soreness in his feet and ankles due to yard work, which he manages with Epsom salt soaks and elevation.

Findings
Physical examination reveals limited active and passive range of motion in the left shoulder. Tenderness is noted over the greater tuberosity. Neurological exam shows capillary refill less than 3 seconds and intact sensation. Vitals are within normal limits: blood pressure 116/84, heart rate 62, oxygen saturation 99%.
