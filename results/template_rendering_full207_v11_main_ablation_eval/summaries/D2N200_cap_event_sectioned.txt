Chief Complaint
Headache and high blood pressure.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a history of high blood pressure, managed with chlorthalidone, though he admits to inconsistent adherence. He also experiences intermittent headaches, primarily at the top of his head, which he manages with over-the-counter Tylenol. These headaches are throbbing and come and go. He reports occasional dizziness and blurred vision associated with the headaches. He has been experiencing these symptoms for approximately one month. He also reports taking metformin every other day for his diabetes.

Findings
The patient's blood pressure was recorded at 172/98 mmHg this morning. The patient reports slight edema in his upper extremities and pitting edema in his lower extremities (1+ bilaterally). Examination reveals a systolic ejection murmur of II/VI. Oxygen saturation is within normal limits. Lung sounds are clear bilaterally. Heart sounds are clear. No jugular venous distention or carotid bruits were noted.

Assessment
The patient presents with uncontrolled hypertension and associated symptoms including headaches and dizziness. His blood glucose levels are slightly elevated.

Plan
I will continue the patient's current medication regimen. I will monitor his blood pressure three times per week. I will recommend a referral to a nutritionist to discuss dietary modifications, particularly regarding sodium intake. I will also schedule a follow-up appointment in one month to assess his progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed. I will increase his metformin dosage to 1500mg.
