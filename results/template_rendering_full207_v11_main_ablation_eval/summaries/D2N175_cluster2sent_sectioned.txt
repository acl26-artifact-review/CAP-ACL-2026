Chief Complaint
Abnormal lab results and concerns regarding diabetes management.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports being shocked by elevated blood sugar readings and has been managing diabetes with metformin 1000 mg daily. He denies chest pain, shortness of breath, headaches, or belly pain. He reports a prior ACL repair five years ago and stable mobility. He reports no difficulty urinating or burning when he urinates. He is currently stable on lisinopril for hypertension. He reports doing well with his high blood pressure medication.

Findings
The patient's hemoglobin A1c is 8.1%, which is elevated. Vital signs include blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, oxygen saturation 98%, and temperature 98.6°F. A systolic ejection murmur of approximately 3/6 is heard. Physical exam reveals no swelling in the legs or extremities. Dragon ordered a CBC, CMP, and lipid panel.

Assessment
The patient has a history of high blood pressure, diabetes mellitus, and an ACL repair. He presents with an elevated hemoglobin A1c, indicating suboptimal glycemic control.

Plan
Prescribe Jardiance 10 mg daily. Continue lisinopril. Order a CBC, CMP, and lipid panel. Schedule a follow-up appointment in four weeks to assess response to Jardiance and monitor A1c.
