Chief Complaint
The patient reports experiencing dizziness and lightheadedness, particularly when sitting.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports experiencing dizziness and lightheadedness, particularly when sitting. He reports some nausea, especially when sitting in a car. He denies recent trauma and reports a minor cut from a knife while chopping onions. He has experienced a slight decrease in appetite but denies weight loss or night sweats. He reports having some tenderness to the right lower quadrant upon abdominal palpation. He has a history of possible lung cancer, which was ruled out with biopsy several years ago. He reports his knee replacement is functioning well and he is walking without problems. He admits to occasional nausea, particularly when sitting.

Findings
The patient is a 56-year-old male with a hemoglobin of 8.2 g/dL. The endoscopy revealed gastritis and a slight polyp, which was biopsied. The patient appears comfortable and has no edema in his lower extremities.

Assessment
The patient presents with anemia and gastritis, as evidenced by his low hemoglobin and endoscopic findings.

Plan
Start proton pump inhibitor, protonix 40 milligrams once daily. Order a complete blood count to further evaluate the anemia. Follow up in four weeks if symptoms persist.
