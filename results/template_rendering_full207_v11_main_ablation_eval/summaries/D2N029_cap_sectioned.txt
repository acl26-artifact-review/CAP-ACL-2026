Chief Complaint
Surgical consult for a biopsy of a suspicious skin lesion.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports that his mother and sister both had melanoma. He noticed a skin spot on his back approximately one month ago and sought evaluation. He denies any prior skin cancers or suspicious lesions. He reports occasional smoking and has had gall bladder and appendix removed. He is currently not taking any medications and has no other medical problems. He reports feeling well.

Findings
Examination reveals a one centimeter shave biopsy site with no residual pigmentation. There is a soft, nontender lymph node in the right groin. Otherwise, the examination is unremarkable.

Assessment
The patient has been diagnosed with melanoma. A biopsy revealed an intermediate thickness melanoma. There is a right inguinal lymph node with slightly atypical consistency.

Plan
An ultrasound is recommended to rule out metastatic disease. A wide local excision may be needed. Follow-up is required. The patient should continue to apply sunscreen and perform regular skin self-exams.
