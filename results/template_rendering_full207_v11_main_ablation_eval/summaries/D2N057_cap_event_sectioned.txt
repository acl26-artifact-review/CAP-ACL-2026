Chief Complaint
Right big toe pain and redness.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a two-week history of intermittent right big toe pain, initially mild but now significantly painful. Last week, the toe became red and warm to the touch, particularly when walking. He describes the pain as throbbing, occasionally sharp, and exacerbated by movement or prolonged weight-bearing. He reports difficulty walking the halls at a recent trade show convention and mentions needing to stand still. He denies any recent injury or trauma. He has been taking ibuprofen twice daily with limited relief. He has never had problems with his stomach. He uses regular shoes and a regular pedal and has never had any problems with his stomach. He enjoys fishing and catching blue croppies and he wants to continue working and doing his regular activities.

Findings
Examination reveals a large spur and significant cartilage loss in the right big toe joint. There is evidence of arthritis, characterized by pain with palpation and movement, particularly plantar flexion and pushing the toe into the adjacent toe. Bone spurs are palpable. The patient reports pain with pressure on the outside of the toe and also on the inside of the toe. The joint moves approximately 20 degrees with pain.

Assessment
The patient's symptoms and examination findings are consistent with hallux rigidus with significant arthritis and cartilage loss in the right big toe joint.

Plan
Prescribe an orthotic insert for the patient's shoe. Start meloxicam, 1mg daily. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess treatment response. If symptoms do not improve, consider a shot or surgical intervention to clean out the joint. Encourage the patient to continue working and doing his regular activities and to test the orthotics and medication. Schedule a follow-up in two weeks to assess treatment response.
