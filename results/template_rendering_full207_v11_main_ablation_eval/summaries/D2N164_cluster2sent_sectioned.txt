Chief Complaint
Patient reports chest pain.

History of Present Illness
The patient reports chest pain when walking, described as dull, and lasting approximately three months without radiation. The patient denies blood in stool, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. The patient reports fatigue for the past two months. The patient is unaware of having high blood pressure, as it was previously documented by other clinicians.

Findings
The patient reports no current chest pain or shortness of breath.

Assessment
The clinician suspects unstable angina and cervical radiculopathy. The patient's reported symptoms are concerning for possible cardiac ischemia.

Plan
The clinician orders a hemoglobin A1c test, an ophthalmology exam, and prescribes aspirin daily. The clinician prescribes nitroglycerin for chest pain, instructing the patient to take it if chest pain persists and wait five minutes to assess its effect. The clinician orders a cervical MRI. The clinician prescribes metformin and hydrochlorothiazide.
